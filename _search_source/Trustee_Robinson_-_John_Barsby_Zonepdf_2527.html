Trustee Robinson – Report for December 20, 2017 This has been a short month busy with Christmas activities. The highlights for me so far this month are described below:      I have had lunch with teachers at each of my elementary schools. Everyone seemed happy with the way the year is unfolding and all of the principals have been extremely complimentary about the work their staff members are doing. We have begun to pilot “The Best Me I Can Be” project I dreamt up a couple of months ago. The first steps were taken with Grate 1-2 and a 4-5 classes at Bayview and two Grade 1 classes at Georgia. I am also expecting at least one class at Park Avenue to join us. The participating teachers invited me to describe the project to their students. The response from both students and teachers left me buzzing for the rest of the day! I attended a Spuptitul meeting with Elders who agreed to help us identify stories to be told in Hulquminum this year. During this meeting we agreed to create an Indigenous Language Symposium with all districts on the Island invited. It will be held the day after the Spuptitul. Participants will be invited to attend the Spuptitul as well. Anne Tenning agreed to take the lead on this project and several of us committed to support her. I attended the Barsby PAC meeting. The most important action point for me was the need for something to be done with the school sign, which has been unusable since October. I have spoken to Scott Saywell and Carrie McVeigh about this. They have agreed to look into solving this problem. I’ll follow up in the New Year. I have begun to work with Deb Marshall on our fourth Human Library project. I have committed once again to arrange for 30 to 40 business and community leaders to meet with Barsby students who are interested in learning more about their various occupations and other areas of expertise.
