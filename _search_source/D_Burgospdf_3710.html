School Calendar Consultation Report to Board April 22, 2015 Local School Calendar Consultation • The 30-day consultation began on Friday, March 20, 2015 • Online survey • Notice to parents • SD68 News • Social media • The consultation was closed on Sunday, April 19, 2015 • 917 online survey participants • 15 emails received Local School Calendar Consultation • The consultation consisted of five agree/do not agree questions • Respondents were given the opportunity to provide feedback for each question Local School Calendar Consultation Q1: To extend the three-year local school calendar to the 2017-18 school year. 86.64% - Agree 13.36% - Did not agree Local School Calendar Consultation Q2: To implement elementary school local schedule guidelines. Consistent early dismissal on Mondays – Consistent for students Monday to Friday bell schedules – Consistent short (morning) recess and long recess schedules. 62.30% - Agree 37.70% - Did not agree Local School Calendar Consultation Q3: To implement secondary school local schedule guidelines. Consistent late start time (9:15 a.m.) on Mondays to allow for PLC time. 64.22% - Agree 35.78% - Did not agree Local School Calendar Consultation Q4: To increase spring break to two weeks within the duration of a three year calendar plan. 67.22% - Agree 32.78% - Did not agree Local School Calendar Consultation Q5: To remove a non-instructional day, and replace with an instructional day to allow additional hours to support PLCs. 84.92% - Agree 15.08% - Did not agree Local School Calendar Consultation QUESTIONS?
