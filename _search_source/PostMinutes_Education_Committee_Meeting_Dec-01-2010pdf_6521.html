CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY EDUCATIONAL UPDATE Report from Ms. Frisson and Ms. Southwick Daily Physical Activity - DPA Ms. J. Poulin, Principal Forest Park Elementary Ms. Poulin spoke to the Education Committee about daily physical activity in elementary schools. The Ministry of Education has mandated that all students in Kindergarten to Grade 7 have 30 minutes a day of physical activity. Ms. Poulin said that elementary schools meet this requirement in a variety of ways, including schoolwide walks and runs; the use of Action Schools materials in individual classrooms; and regular activity breaks throughout the day. She said that elementary principals told her that the implementation of daily physical activity has had a positive impact on many students. Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held December 1, 2010 Ms. Joanne Bays and Ms. Crystal Pedersen Farm to School Program 3 Ms. Joanne Bays of the Farm to School Program, said that the concept of the Farm to School program is simple – the farm produces food for the school, and students have a chance to eat fresh vegetables and fruit. Students visit the farms and the farmers visit the schools. There are 24 schools participating in the program in B.C. Three of those schools are in School District 68 – Fairview, Georgia Avenue and South Wellington. Ms. Stacy Bestard, the district’s school meals coordinator, and Ms. Crystal Pedersen, of Nanaimo Foodshare, described the way that a number of initiatives, including the Farm to School Program, support nutrition education. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Murchie IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Dale Folio E10/12/01-03 We Day That the Education Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that we further endorse the Farm to School Program. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Ms. Arlene McGuff, a teacher from NDSS, showed the We Day Presentation to the committee. She said that it was an extraordinary day for all of the students who attended. We Day is a day-long event that ignites a yearlong program for change called Schools in Action. Sponsored by Free the Children Foundation, We Day is a celebration, inspiration and vehicle for young people to change the world. About 300 students from Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools attended this year’s We Day in Rogers Arena in Vancouver in October and each school makes a continued commitment to follow through with special projects all year.
