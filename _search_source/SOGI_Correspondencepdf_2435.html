The bottom line for us, as Christians, is that the Bible clearly teaches humans are, by God’s design and purpose, heterosexual. At the same time, we believe that bullying is wrong, and that all people, regardless of their preferences, must be treated respectfully. Isolating SOGI bullying from other types of bullying seems to imply that bullying over SOGI issues is worse than other types of bullying. This is not true. Children must be taught never to bully, whatever the reason. It is unfortunate and unnecessary that SOGI 123 goes beyond teaching tolerance to presenting LGBTQoptions as healthy alternatives to heterosexuality. Using the privileged position of a teacher to promote this thinking is a betrayal of the trust we place in the school system. We appeal to you, Minister of Education, Deputy Minister of Education, Superintendent of Schools and the members of our own School Board, to prevent further unhappiness and division over this program, halting its implementation until or unless signi?cant changes have been made. It is our hope that the Ministry of Education can be persuaded to return to an approach which respects parental concerns and values. Thank you for taking the time to read this letter.
