Motion Carried Page 2|2 PRC DEPARTMENT UPDATES PROGRAM AREA SUMMARY Health, Wellness, Fitness & Sport For January, participation numbers in the Fitness Centre, drop-in fitness, and sports classes were just over 4,870. Participation numbers increased slightly for February to 4,890. Drop-in pickleball, badminton, soccer, and basketball continue to be very popular among youth, adults, and the older adult populations. January also marked the start of the 2024 Winter registered programs. The Winter season runs from January to March. An additional 34 programs and 290 instructional hours are offered during this season. A wide variety of these registered programs and classes are offered including spin, yoga, run/walk clinic, dance, and strength can conditioning. Aquatics Drop-in aquatic participant numbers remain steady with close to 3,163 users in January and 2,875 in February. Aquatic fitness programs and classes continue to be popular, with high levels of participation in each class. An additional 334 instructional hours were offered through registered swim programs, including swimming lessons, Bronze Medallion and Bronze Cross. Recreation – Preschool/Children/Youth/General Recreation Preschool, Children, and Youth programs continue to gain traction. Program highlights include Square Dancing, Stop Motion, and Beginner Watercolours classes. The Adventure Zone Daycamp program is always a hit and was offered during SD68 Pro-D Days. Activities included swimming, sports/physical activities, arts, and walking to local parks and playgrounds. Participation numbers in the Pre-Teen Program offered on Wednesdays after school continue to be popular, with numbers climbing for Friday Night Rec Room. Friday Night Hoops continues to be a huge success with 40+ children and youth learning to play and advancing their basketball skills. SPECIAL EVENTS, PROJECTS & COMMUNITY Family Day On Monday, February 19, the department hosted two free Family Day Swims and Gym activities at the Frank Jameson Community Centre. Session #1 was from 12:00-1:30 pm and session #2 was from 2:00-3:30 pm. Special family-oriented activities and games were facilitated in the pools and gymnasium, focusing on family, inclusivity, physical activity, and physical literacy. Healthy to-go snacks were provided at the end of the event. The Town received $1,000 from the BCRPA to help support these 2024 BC Family Day activities. Partners included Ladysmith Arts and PacificSport Vancouver Island. Healthy Start to Learning On Thursday, February 22, the department through the Ladysmith Early Years Partnership (LEYP) invited the community to attend the Healthy Start to Learning Event from 9:30-12:00pm at Aggie Hall. The event was focused on children, families, and the early years and is a great opportunity to engage with community programs and services. Partner organizations provided an engaging child-friendly activity for the children to do while their parents had the opportunity to learn more about local resources. Ladysmith Lions Club made homemade pizza for participants.
