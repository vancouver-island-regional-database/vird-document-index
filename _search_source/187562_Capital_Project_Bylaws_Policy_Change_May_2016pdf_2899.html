May 6, 2016 Ref: 187562 To: All Superintendents All Secretary-Treasurers Re: Capital Project Bylaws Policy Change As part of our Capital Program review, we are looking for ways to streamline the project development, approval and delivery cycle. One area that many of you identified for streamlining pertains to the requirement for capital bylaws for each approved capital project. We reviewed Division 6, Sections 142 and 143 of the School Act and determined Bylaws are not required for each approved capital project providing the following: 1. The School Board prepares and submits an Annual Capital Plan approved by resolution by the Board prior to submission. 2. The Ministry receives and approves, with or without modification, the Capital Plan. 3. The Board passes a Bylaw adopting the Ministry’s approved plan. We have are hopeful this change will reduce workload across the sector and streamline the project approval and project agreement process. As such, effective immediately, we are eliminating the requirement for Boards of Education to pass Bylaws for each individual capital project - providing Boards pass an Annual Bylaw adopting the supported projects identified by the Ministry. Please see the Ministry of Education’s Capital Plan Project Bylaws policy on our website at: http://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/education-training/administration/resourcemanagement/capital-planning/capital-bylaws Also, in an effort to better align school district capital planning with government's fiscal cycle; we have accelerated the Annual Capital Plan submission deadline from November 2016, to August 31st, 2016.
