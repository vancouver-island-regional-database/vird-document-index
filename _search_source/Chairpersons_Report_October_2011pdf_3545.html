SD 68 Chairperson’s Report Sharon Welch October, 2011 In October, five of the Trustees attended the Vancouver Island School Trustees Association (VISTA) conference in Parksville. BCPSEA representatives updated us on the Provincial negotiations, and then we had fulsome discussions about the benefits and challenges of 21st Century Learning and its successful implementation. The highlight of this past month was the opportunity to attend the Vancouver Island Economic Alliance’s (VIEA’s) Summit. In addition to hearing the keynote speaker’s address on the economic realities being experienced in the world, Canada, BC, and Vancouver Island today, I had the pleasure of participating in three very informative and inspiring break-out sessions. First, I learned about the 70% of Vancouver Island entrepreneurs who would be retiring over the next 5-10 years and the importance of strong business planning and succession planning to ensure that many of these businesses transition and continue to be viable. Then I heard four speakers share their successes in creating strong public-private partnerships to accomplish significant projects such as the Sooke Conference Centre, the Deep Bay Marine Field Station, and the collaborative and innovate approach to a transitioning economy in Campbell River. Finally, I was inspired by five Vancouver Island entrepreneurs who built smaller homegrown businesses and larger corporations that are thriving today. They shared challenges and benefits of conducting their businesses on the Island, and they emphasized the importance of relationships, adaptability, value-added products and services, and creative positioning in the marketplace. I was surprised to learn that I was the only attendee connected to the K-12 public education system, so this presented opportunities for me to ask questions about how we can work with other organizations to communicate both current and future opportunities to students, so that they will be attracted to living and learning in Nanaimo. Other attendees picked up on this, and we are hoping that the VIEA executive may create opportunities for some of our secondary students to attend in future years. Last night, I attended the Opening Doors VII discussion held by the Chamber of Commerce at VIU. Trustees D. and N. Allen and Murchie also attended. Susan Cudahy, the leader of the new Nanaimo Economic Development Corporation, discussed the importance of ‘selling’ Nanaimo to potential tourists residents, and business investors in order to build our economic stability. Ms. Cudahy emphasized the importance of focusing on our strengths, rather than weaknesses, and marketing Nanaimo so that young people will see opportunities and choose to study and work here. The participants were divided into three groups to discuss issues around current and future labour market challenges, VIU’s role in the community, and opportunities for business development. Many of the conversations revolved around the need to engage young people and ensuring that we are giving them the tools for success. I am encouraged with the increasing numbers of opportunities for SD68 to participate in these economic discussions. As the city’s largest employer, and as the team who prepare our young people for future success in the business world, we need to have a strong voice. Thanks to those who are making these opportunities possible.
