Key Roles • Role of staff in capital planning • Role of Board of Trustees in securing capital monies for school construction Proposal That the district start the public consultation process on the NDSS replacement options and have the wider community take part in this important discussion. Issues to be considered… • J. Lloyd Crowe Secondary in Trail was built in 1952. It was replaced 55 years later, with a new school, at a cost of $39.4 million. (current enrolment 825) • NDSS was also built in 1952, 65 years ago. This is an old building and it needs to be replaced as soon as possible. We in the community need to come together to support an application to government to speed up the funding for the NDSS project and provide a modern learning environment for our students. Issues to be considered… • Clarification of the role of the city and VIU in the project. • A more detailed explanation of the intent and significance of the city’s purchase of district land at the NDSS site. Administration & Operations Centre Issues to be considered… • Establish, with input from the public, a firmer timeline for the relocation of the district administration centre and operations centre. • With the sale of the lands on Old Victoria Rd., options to relocate the operations centre are narrowing. 430 Selby St. A proposal in the five year capital plan calls for the demolition of 430 Selby St., the old CRC/Learning Services Building. Issues to be considered… • Previous boards completed studies for the possible uses of the Selby St. property, those options are still viable and need to be part of the wider public discussion. To leave this valuable piece of district owned property out of the wider conversation would be a major error. Mixed Use Development? One option for the Selby St. property is to have it developed by public and private monies for a new district administration centre along with commercial components, and a learning area for First Nations studies if desired. The property is zoned for public use as part of the city strategy of locating public government services in this area. Thank you
