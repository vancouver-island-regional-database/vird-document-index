CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Organization of Classes in SD68 Final Report from the Superintendent of Schools for the 2010-2011 School Year Mr. Munro reviewed his report on the organization of classes in schools in the district as of September 30th with Board members. He said the report is required by the School Act, to ensure that the district is meeting the class size provisions in the School Act. The report shows that there are 146 intermediate class divisions in the district, and none of those divisions has more than 30 students. The 30student limit in intermediate classes can only be exceeded with the agreement of the teacher. Kindergarten and primary classes are not Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held October 13, 2010 2 included in the report because those class size limits of 22 and 24 cannot be exceeded in any situation. At the secondary level, of the 851 instructional blocks this semester, 84 have more than 30 students. Of those, 19 have been organized to meet the needs of specific programs, such as band and fine arts. The other 65 have been organized in order that schools can offer a more complete array of courses. The Board discussed the organizational problems created by late student registrations and transfers and asked the Education Committee to look at ways to encourage more parents to register students earlier. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Dale IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan Folio SB10/10/13-02 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) asks the Education Committee to have discussions that could look at strategies to ensure registrations and transfers at schools are done within acceptable time lines.
