Nanaimo Foodshare Society “Local Food Matters” School Food: Programs and Possibilities Nanaimo Foodshare We have many programs in partnership with the schools This means programs that: • Increase Food Literacy • Increase Access to Food for students in need, • Build capacity for student academic success School Meals/Summer Lunch Munch Programs School Food Workshops for teachers and stakeholders Nanaimo Food Security Roundtable Programs increasing healthy food access: Summer Lunch Munch School Meals Good Food Box BC Farmers Market Nutrition Coupon Program Farm to School Programs Cont’d Programs supporting food literacy: Kids Get Cooking classes for kids & youth Community Roots School Gardens Programs supporting local food Seedy Sunday (at NDSS) Five Acre Farm (collaboration with Growing Opportunities Coop) Nanaimo Foodshare and SD 68: School Gardens Five Acre Farm Five Acre Farm In partnership with Growing Opportunities we are farming an Urban Farm site, close to Park Ave School Training site for garden facilitators Community education site, including site visits from school groups https://youtu.be/JyNmoRFNHzw Nanaimo School Gardens • With support from Farm 2 School and a teacher advisory, Foodshare developed the SOIL Lit Kit, a teacher resource kit connecting curriculum to class based activities for school gardens (grades k-6) • Foodshare School Garden Facilitators are mentoring teachers and school stakeholders to use school gardens and resources to incorporate garden learning in their class activities • Foodshare continues to expand the number of schools supported to develop and implement school gardens • The Five Acre Farm is available as a teacher summer institute for teachers to gain garden skills Why Support Healthy Food and Food Literacy at school? Because Mental Health is directly associated with food insecurity and Healthy Eating 35-47% of a students daily nutrition is eaten at school Healthy Foods lead to positive school outcomes • • • • • • Increased academic performance (short and longterm) Increased Mental Health Healthy immune system  more days at school and not sick Healthy energy levels (not falling asleep in school, hyperactive) Appropriate classroom behavior Social Inclusion Because Mental Health is directly associated with food insecurity (access) and Healthy Eating From the Food Costing in BC 2017 report, BCCDC Source: Food Costing in BC 2017: Assessing the affordability of healthy eating – BC Centre for Disease Control Because it is mandated by the province “The guidelines are mandated policy for all B.C. public schools” SD 68- Friendly Food Literacy Challenge What the Board can do: 1. Appoint a Board Trustee (and/or staff person) to sit on the Nanaimo School Food Advisory Committee, as a liason to the School District 2. Develop communication protocol for community partners, such as Foodshare, to share information and resources district wide that support pre-approved projects with school stakeholders 3. Review provincial Guidelines for Food and Beverage Sales in BC Schools and develop a strategic plan in response to the policy Supports for nutrition access: What the board can do • Write a letter to Minister of Education to reinstate Community LINK funding that is targeted specifically to supporting student access to healthy food and food literacy. And Put the ‘N’ back in LINK. • Continue Supporting Community School Coordinators in the Districtthey are critical to facilitating Foodshare’s ability to bring programs to district schools • Consider Reinstating a SD 68 staff position with a food mandate with a possible role to coordinate food supports and healthy eating programs throughout the district Supports for healthy school: Community partnerships Nanaimo Foodshare Lots of possibilities to partner –Foodshare brings to the Table: • Food access and Food Literacy program collaborations • Train the trainer school garden facilitation, nutrition • Knowledge sharing • indigenous foods, • healthy eating school policies, • guidelines for health promoting fundraising • Healthy food supports for mental health and behavior issues……..
