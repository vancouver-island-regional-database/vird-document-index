BCSTA Update: 1 SUMMER WORK BCPSEA Advisory Committee: We got word in early August that the BCPSEA would be creating an advisory committee made up of management partner groups. While this group is not a decision making body, it is still a step in the right direction towards rebuilding the BCPSEA Board of Directors. The expression of interest went out in mid-August for interested trustees. Through a process of negotiations we were able to negotiate 7 trustee spots on the committee, up from the original 5. There is also 1 BCSSA rep and 1 BCASBO rep. Collectively the entire province is covered and trustees make up a majority of the group. The trustees are not there to represent their regions, rather they are there to represent the interests of the BCSTA as a whole. Submission and presentation to Selection Standing Committee on Finance: Collectively the board worked on a submission to the Select Standing Committee on Finance. Teresa presented the submission on Wednesday, September 16. I have attached a copy of the presentation to this report. The presenters are given a ten minute time slot for the entire presentation, including questions. Teresa gave a brief presentation in hopes of leaving time for questions. There were so many questions that the chair eventually had to cut off the questions and our presentation lasted for over the allotted time. Questions seemed to really focus on the issues surrounding capacity utilization around the province (and the complexities and nuances involved across the province), transportation issues and funding surrounding this, the difficulty in sustaining specialty programs, implementation of the new curriculum. There was no push back this year, which Teresa has felt in the past. The questions seemed to focus on information gathering rather than questioning the details and information in the submission. Ministry Meetings: The board met with the Ministry on June 19. Present were the Board of Directors of the BCSTA and the Deputy Minister and most of the ADM’s of the Ministry. The purpose of the meeting was to begin joint strategic planning for the upcoming year. We discussed areas of overlap, areas of concern and priorities and next steps for the two organizations. Changes at the Ministry: Of course everyone heard the announcement regarding the new Minister of Education. Teresa has met with Minister Bernier. He has committed to open communication between the two organizations and so far has lived up to that promise and has been very available for questions and discussions. The change in minister meant a lot of clerical staff changes at the Ministry, however the MOE staff, will remain the same. Funding for training teachers for roll out of new curriculum: There was an announcement made on September 28 that the ministry would provide funding for training teachers on the new curriculum. There will be 30 hours of training provided for teachers and $1 million of funding along with what has already been provided. This involves 10 hours this year and 5 hours plus one pro-d for the following two years. The way districts decide to utilize this time will be worked out jointly with the district and local teachers’ association. This is much needed training for our staff and teachers and many partner groups worked together to advocate for this time. 2 UPCOMING EVENTS: September 29: Joint Strategic Planning Sessions with the Ministry of Education: This meeting is a continuation of the June 19th meeting and will involve the same people. It will be co-chaired by Teresa Rezansoff and Dave Byng. The focus is the operationalization of the MOU. We will also be discussing any emergent issues and future events. October 21: First Annual Liaison Meeting: BCSTA pushed for this event based on feedback and communication from our members stating that they would like more contact with Ministry staff and, in particular, the Minister of Education. Board chairs, Superintendents and Secretary Treasurers will meet with the Ministry to discuss three issues: 1. Introduction to the new curriculum and pro-d implementation 2. The Accountability Framework 3. District Auditing Process. The format will be interactive, case studies, round table discussions etc. October 22: Board Chair Professional Development: The BCSTA is taking advantage of the Board Chairs all being gathered in one spot the day before for the Liaison Meeting. This is an opportunity to provide professional development to Board Chairs opportunities that chairs have been asking for as well as an opportunity for chairs to meet and network amongst themselves. Topics being covered are: 1. Media and Communications Strategies 2. Conflict Resolution 3. Board Governance: Roles and Responsibilities October 23: Meeting with FNESC: The Board of the BCSTA will be meeting with the Board of FNESC on Friday October 23 for two hours. The purpose is to discuss areas where the two organizations can support each other’s goals and work more closely together.
