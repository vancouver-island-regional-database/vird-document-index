Appendix B Le tte r from the Office of the Supe rinte nde nt August 30, 2024 Dear caregivers, As Premier David Eby shared this week, all schools in BC will have restrictions on personal digital devices when classes resume next week. In May, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools adopted a policy to comply with the province’s requirement for all public schools in BC to have language restricting personal digital devices use in schools. It is easier for students to focus on school without distractions from cell phones and other personal digital devices. Helping all our learners manage how they use their digital devices at school can help everyone have a better learning experience. Our staff is committed to a collaborative approach to helping students understand the new rules around personal digital devices in classrooms when they return to classes next week. Elementary Personal digital devices are now restricted throughout the instructional day (start of day bell to dismissal bell.) This includes class time, recess, and lunch. For some of our schools, this may include supervision time on school grounds before the welcome bell. Devices must be turned off or placed on airplane mode and kept in a backpack or school bag for the entire school day. Secondary In secondary schools, personal digital devices must be turned off or placed on airplane mode before students enter the classroom and during instructional time. Personal digital devices are not permitted for use during class/instructional time unless an educator has given permission. In all schools, there will be consideration given to: • use of personal digital devices for appropriate educational purposes; • accessibility and accommodation needs; • medical and health needs; • equity to support learning environments. For more information visit www.gov.bc.ca/phonesandschools Kind regards, Laura Tait Interim Superintendent Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Questions and Answers Is it okay to send my child to school with a personal digital device (cellphone) to school next week? Cellphones do not need to be left at home. However, before heading to school, students should be aware there will be restrictions on using their phones for noneducational purposes. When are students NOT allowed to use their personal digital devices? For elementary students, from the start of day bell to dismissal bell. This includes class time, recess, and lunch. For some schools, this may include supervised time before the start of day bell. Between these bells, all personal digital devices must be turned off or placed on airplane mode and kept in a backpack or school bag entire school day. In secondary schools, personal digital devices must be turned off or placed on airplane mode before students enter the classroom and during instructional time. Personal digital devices are not permitted for use during class/instructional time unless an educator has given permission. We kindly ask everyone to join us in encouraging students to develop digital literacy skills and use technology solely for educational purposes while at school.
