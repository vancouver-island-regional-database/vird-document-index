BACKGROUND: The 2016-17 school year was the second year of the Becoming Sustainable budget planning process. The goal of the two-year plan was to establish operating budgets that didn’t require the use of surplus money, which would allow the district to effectively function within the “operational budget”. This process allowed the district to focus capital and grant funding into student support and educational programming, facilities renewal, and vulnerable students. There were three major components in the Becoming Sustainable plan: 1) The 2015 budget was to be balanced without the use of surplus funds. The need to use surplus dollars from previous years did not allow the district to maintain a sustainable budget. This meant that significant cuts had to be made to ensure that the district did not need to “dip into the cupboard” to support existing programs and staffing that had been protected in the past. 2) The unrestricted surplus was significantly higher than in previous years due to job action. Staff recommended that the money be directed to strategic projects that enabled the district to maintain long-term budget sustainability while aligning with the Board goals. Once achieved, NLPS was able to plan for the improvement of student learning and the Updated Facilities Plan. With the end of year two of Becoming Sustainable approaching, district staff is able to confirm that all but three budget strategy initiatives were completed. This year’s budget continues to support the Primary Learning functions in the district with a focus on assessment and curriculum.
