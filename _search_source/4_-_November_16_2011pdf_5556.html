NEW BUSINESS Budget/Actual Forecast Update #1 as at October 31, 2011 Mr. Turin summarized some of the significant financial changes since the district’s preliminary budget was set last spring: • Enrolment has increased by 117 full-time equivalent students over what was projected in the budget last spring. • The number of students designated in a funded special needs category has decreased by 118 students from the projections for this year’s budget and by 258 students from last year. • Staffing levels in the district are close to the levels projected in the budget, with the exception of education assistant positions. • The district’s funding protection grant has increased from about $4.4 million that was projected in the preliminary budget to $5.9 million, primarily as a result of the decrease in Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held November 16, 2011 3 the number of students with funded special needs designations. Mr. Turin said that currently the district is projecting a surplus of about $900,000 for this school year. He said that without funding protection the district would be in serious financial difficulties. Mr. Turin said It is clear that the funding protection program cannot continue in its current format and that the Board should begin planning for this eventuality and look at a potential surplus this year as an opportunity to offset some of the impact of next year’s budget reductions.
