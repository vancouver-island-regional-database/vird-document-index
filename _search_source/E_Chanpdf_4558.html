1 Good evening Mr Chairman, Trustees, Mr. Blain, District staff & community members. My name is Ed Chan; I’m the proud parent of two graduates from our school district. I’d like to thank the School District and our District Staff for hosting these presentations and for this district consultation. I’d like to use my remarks to talk about my kids’ alma mater: John Barsby Community School. When our family moved here in 2008, we checked out our neighbourhood high school, John Barsby. And, in 2008, we heard about Barsby’s reputation. And, after doing our due diligence, we sent our kids there. Now, it’s 2015 and our family still hears about Barsby’s reputation. 2 So, I’d like to provide some substance to Barsby’s reputation tonight having experienced John Barsby for 5 years. One of my kids spent only grades 11 & 12 at Barsby; the other was there from grades 8-12. One of my kids leaned heavily towards the Arts, the other just as heavily towards Science. Both found a wide range of academics and electives at Barsby, though course scheduling was more challenging when the school’s population was in the 600 range. Our artsy one found, aside from her core academics, took history, psychology, theatre and leadership...our science-focused daughter was able to take all her grade 12 sciences, Pre-Calculus & Calculus 12 at Barsby. While both of our kids were and are very strong academic students, both had classmates who were just as strong academically in their classes. There were then & there are today strong academic students at John Barsby School. 3 Both of my kids found easy access to the VIU Dual Credit program. One took 3 VIU courses in her grade 12 year. She didn’t find the 15-20 minute walk to campus taxing, and she was able to score 2 A+’s & an A in her first university courses, while maintaining an A average at Barsby. Both also found an excellent range of extracurriculars that Ms Audrey Bouvet, the Barsby PAC president, has already outlined tonight. And perhaps most importantly, they found a great circle of friends, friends that my wife & I were & are happy to welcome into our home & into lives. Today, Barsby students are thriving; they are living positively, volunteering within the school community and the larger community; they’re trying their best in their respective areas of study. I won’t deny that our school has its challenges, but similar challenges can be found in all of our schools. Poverty is not restricted to one school, it’s a district wide phenomenon. 4 I asked a 2012 grad yesterday if he knew what other Barsby grads were up to: the grad listed a 2011 grad who completed an Honours Science degree at UBC and has gone on to study Pharmacology; another 2011 grad is taking a Masters degree in Chemistry; another went to the University of Calgary and played football. And not to be outdone that grad is finishing his history degree in Leiscester, England. Other Barsby grads are taking trade apprenticeships, working full time or part time, paying taxes, volunteering to make our community better. Still other Barsby graduates have gone on to sit on our City Council, and another is a Member of Parliament in the newly elected federal government. John Barsby students and grads are more than alright, they are fantastic. Lastly, I’d like to extend an invitation to any parent or youth who still holds concerns or fears of Barsby, to visit the school. I’d be happy to accompany you. I 5 know that our principal, Deb Marshall, and our community would welcome you. We would just ask that you keep an open mind, and get to know us.
