Page 6 of 107 7.1 NANAIMO DISTRICT SECONDARY SCHOOL HIGH SCHOOL FIELD LACROSSE ACADEMY: A PROGRAM OF CHOICE TO: School District Education Committee FROM: Mr. Joel Smith, Teacher, Nanaimo District Secondary School Mr. Tim Davie, Principal, Nanaimo District Secondary School and District Learning Alternative Programs DATE: February 4, 2015 SUBJECT: Implementation of a Field Lacrosse Academy at Nanaimo District Secondary School BACKGROUND The idea for a Field Lacrosse Academy in Nanaimo has existed for many years. Throughout the various organizations in Nanaimo there has always been a desire to see the lacrosse community expand into the high school system. For Nanaimo Lacrosse, the time is now. Both minor associations, the Nanaimo District Lacrosse Association (NDLA) and the Nanaimo Junior Varsity Raiders Field Lacrosse (NJVFL) continue to experience yearly growth in terms of registration. In 2014, NDLA had 438 players registered. This made NDLA the fourth largest association in British Columbia. The Nanaimo Raiders had over 130 players registered. The proposal to bring a field lacrosse academy to Nanaimo has unified and brought together support from all lacrosse organizations in Nanaimo. The consultation process has found partnerships with the NDLA, NJVFL, Senior ‘B’ Nanaimo Timbermen, Senior ‘A’ and Junior ‘A’ Nanaimo Timbermen, and the Nanaimo Masters Lacrosse Club. Through this un-paralleled support the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy will have access to guest coaches, mentorship opportunities, youth community involvement and much more. It speaks to the desire of the lacrosse community to come together and support this project. Initial feedback for the proposed field lacrosse academy has been nothing but positive. The academy will focus on the development of the overall student athlete. Within the academy model, academic success, character and leadership development are paramount to the success of the athletes. It is our goal to have our student athletes experience success through their academic and athletic endeavors while enrolled in the Nanaimo District Secondary School Field Lacrosse Academy. Page 7 of 107 7.1 In addition to the general goals of the program, the Field Lacrosse Academy has the opportunity to showcase potential student athletes to Colleges and Universities throughout Canada and the United States. Over the past ten years, on Vancouver Island alone, there have been over 40 student athletes who have gone on to Division One and Two NCAA Universities directly from Field Lacrosse Academies. The potential for scholarships and post secondary education can only be further developed through the development of the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy. IMPLEMENTATION ISSUES Some of the key issues to first be discussed were:    Why is NDSS an ideal location for the Field Lacrosse Academy: How many students might choose to participate in this program, including students from other secondary catchment areas, and other school districts: How many students are needed to offer this program: I am committed to seeing this project through and establishing a Field Lacrosse Academy at NDSS. The administration team is in full support of the academy as it represents a fantastic opportunity to add to the developing culture and dynamic makeup of the NDSS site. Within the Nanaimo School District we feel that the NDSS site is ideal to house a Field Lacrosse Academy. At NDSS a number of out of catchment students throughout Nanaimo attend everyday as part of the French Immersion program. A large number of these students rely on the city bus to get to and from school. The close proximity to Vancouver Island University and the Third Street Connector make attending NDSS a viable option for students coming from out of catchment. The precedence has been set for NDSS to host students from across Nanaimo. NDSS is located within a burgeoning Sports Corridor within the City of Nanaimo. Located in walking distance to the NDSS campus are the Nanaimo Ice Center, Nanaimo Aquatic Center, Rotary Bowl, Serauxmen Stadium, and the Mariner Sports Fields. The long-term plan for the area calls for a complete redevelopment of the sporting infrastructure on the NDSS site, which includes an all sport turf field. A turf field on the NDSS site would be of great value to the Field Lacrosse Academy. The addition of a Field Lacrosse Academy within this region speaks to the overall long-term development of the area. Beginning Semester 2, NDSS will be offering ‘Fundamentals of Lacrosse’ within the timetable. The introduction of this class into the NDSS timetable has been met with enthusiasm and excitement. At this time there are 20 students registered ranging from grades 9-11. The class has students enrolled from both streams within the NDSS site. The Fundamentals of Lacrosse Page 8 of 107 7.1 class has acted as bridge between the sites and has given more programming options to students. As second semester draws near, interest within the school has risen, but also at surrounding high schools. A number of out of catchment students have expressed interest in attending NDSS for this initial class, in hopes of the Academy becoming a reality. When semester 2 begins we strongly believe we will have more than 25 students enrolled. In consultation with the various lacrosse organizations, Nanaimo District Lacrosse Association, Nanaimo Junior Varsity Field Lacrosse, and conversation with parents and athletes, the level of interest for potential participants in the first year ranges between 25 and 60. Through the NDLA and NJVFL membership, we have sent out a letter of interest that offers a synopsis of what we are planning at NDSS and gives the Nanaimo lacrosse community a chance to offer insight. At this time we are still gathering data, but feedback again has been positive and a number of parents have indicated interest in putting their son or daughter in the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy. The Field Lacrosse Academy at Spectrum Secondary School currently has 24 students registered in their program for the 2014-15 year. Last year they had 20 enrolled. It would be feasible to offer this program in the first year for up to a total of 30 students at the grade 9-12 level (based on 1 block per semester). At the low end of the spectrum it would be possible to offer this program to a minimum of 20-25 students at the grade 9-12 level (based on 1 block per semester). Based upon expressions of interest, and precedence set by other local high school academies, and the experience at the Spectrum Field Lacrosse Academy, it is considered likely that up to 30 students would, in fact, register for such a program in the first year in which it was offered. We strongly believe this number would only continue to grow as the academy is established. At this time, the program has been designed to include grade 9-12. STAFFING AND HUMAN RESOURCES I am a fully qualified Physical Education teacher. I hold a Bachelor of Arts degree with Major in History and Minor in Physical Education from Vancouver Island University. I also have a Physical Education Diploma from Vancouver Island University. I hold a variety of NCCP coaching certificates, including lacrosse. I have been involved in minor lacrosse in Nanaimo since I was 5 years old and have coached with the NDLA and NJVFL. I am also an active member of the Nanaimo Masters Lacrosse Club. Page 9 of 107 7.1 We do not foresee staffing of the program as a problem. The lacrosse community will act as an invaluable resource in regards to guest coaching and mentorship. A variety of individuals connected with lacrosse across Nanaimo have stepped up and agreed to appear as guest coaches and mentors. We have verbal commitments from the Nanaimo Masters Lacrosse Club, Nanaimo Senior B Club, and Nanaimo Senior A and Junior A organization to support the program. DIRECT STAFFING IMPACTS Within the NDSS site, based on one block per semester, the addition of the Field Lacrosse Academy will not significantly affect staffing. When the program demand calls for two blocks per semester, staffing will increase. As the program numbers increase, the demand for other courses will rise resulting in an overall increase of FTE status at the school. At this time we have initiated a consultation process with community Minor Lacrosse, through the NDLA and NJVFL, to gather data on the long-term sustainability of the academy. Through a variety of social media outlets and direct membership email correspondence, we have asked the lacrosse public to express their interest in the program. We have put a soft deadline for this consultation as February 25th. As we assess the data a clearer picture will develop as to possible impacts on future staffing issues at NDSS and in turn other local high schools. Our plan has been submitted to the NDSS Staff Committee for review. FACILITIES IMPACT With the focus of the academy on Field Lacrosse, the facility impact at NDSS would be minimal. One of the biggest attractions to have this academy established at NDSS is the accessibility of a variety of facilities. For the majority of the training, students will be outside on the school field or the neighbouring Rotary Bowl field. Close proximity of the Nanaimo Ice Centre means a dry floor surface is accessible if needed. Facility use will have little to no impact on regular Physical Education classes at NDSS. In working with the Physical Education department at NDSS, the upcoming Fundamentals of Lacrosse class has been placed in the regular facilities rotation for semester 2. This has been Page 10 of 107 7.1 established to allow the ‘fundamentals’ class access to the weight room and gymnasium on a regular basis. This rotation and partnership would continue under the Academy model. PROGRAM DEVELOMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION The Field Lacrosse Academy would be offered for 1 block for each semester. It is expected that the course will fill at 30 students. If we have a larger number of students applying, we would need to re-evaluate the program blocks to accommodate additional students. As the program is designed to be offered in 1 block, in each of grade 9-12, the creation of a timetable with appropriate course pairings is necessary. This will pose a challenge to the schedule; however it has been proven successful in the other academies in Nanaimo. The following program schedule is based on one block per semester. If numbers permitted another section, the same guideline would apply to two blocks. Based on initial estimates, students would be required to pay approximately $600 to enroll in the Academy. This fee would cover the entire school year. The registration fee would cover, but not limited to:        Team equipment (balls, cones, goals etc.…) Equipment maintenance Referee fees Possible tournament Fees Basic travel costs Indoor floor rental Annual Team Kit o Helmet (first year only) o Gloves o Shorts o Dry-Fit Shirt o Reversible Pinnies In addition to the yearly fee, fundraising and corporate sponsorship will also be employed. A number of payment options exist in terms of payment schedule. Perhaps the most feasible option would be to allow families to pay monthly over the ten-month term. In discussion with the Spectrum Field Lacrosse Academy, they have attested to the benefits of allowing families to pay monthly with post-dated cheques. Based on our initial estimate of $600 per year, the cost would be $60 per month. EQUIPMENT Players would provide their own equipment including appropriate cleats, protective gear, sticks, mouth guards, and water bottles. As described in the fee allotment, the Academy will provide all players with ‘NDSS’ team helmets (first year only), gloves and training gear. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE Should the Board of Education recommend the approval of the Field Lacrosse Academy at NDSS, information would be provided to the students immediately. An information session will Page 13 of 107 7.1 be held for all parents and prospective participants of the Academy. Information regarding registration guidelines, enrollment dates and Academy specific details will be advertised through various media outlets. We will also continue to work with the NDLA and NJVFL to contact all registered and eligible players via membership email. As the 2015-2016 school year is fast approaching we will work quickly to incorporate the academy into the NDSS timetable in order to alleviate any scheduling conflicts that could occur. We will be ready to begin the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy in September 2015. EFFECTS UPON OTHER SCHOOLS While we do anticipate movement from out of catchment schools we do not feel the impact on other schools throughout the district will be substantial. We will be attracting students to NDSS who have the desire and dedication to become better lacrosse players and better citizens. All students who participate in the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy will be eligible to participate in other school-based sports. Generally, the students taking part in this program will be highly motivated and skilled individuals who thrive for sport. In turn, the respective schools these students leave to attend NDSS will see a drop in prospective members of their athletic departments. Given the nature of the Academy program and the likelihood that many of the students will continue to play community Box and Field Lacrosse, it is difficult to know how many will compete for NDSS in other sports. SUMMARY If the board decides to approve the program, the Field Lacrosse Academy will work diligently to promote academic excellence and build the relationship between character development and leadership skills. The program is designed to develop our student athletes into better lacrosse players and ultimately better citizens. We will work diligently to enhance the student experience at NDSS and further develop the unique culture within the NDSS site. We are committed to lacrosse in Nanaimo and through partnerships with our community lacrosse organizations we have a great opportunity to further establish Nanaimo as one of the leaders in lacrosse on Vancouver Island. At a DBS student’s house there is a common but an invasive tree called the Tree of Heaven. The Tree of Heaven was originally from China, but it has been brought to North America in 1784. The tree creates a chemical that prevents other plants from growing. Our eco problem is that the student’s family doesn’t know what to do about the tree, either to cut it down, trim the branches or leave it as is. Mrs. Creighton’s class went on a walking field trip to a walking trail park called Woodstream Park. Once they got to the student’s house the class noticed right away that the Tree of Heaven was big! The class learned that the public does not like the Tree of Heaven, and many say to cut all of them down. Later in the day the class was split into nine groups of three and was given a collaborative project about the Tree of Heaven. Their job was to decide as a group either to cut the tree, trims the branches or leave it alone. When they were done, each group shared their opinion to the class. In total, six groups wanted the Tree of Heaven to be cut down and three of them wanted the tree’s branches trimmed. We don’t know what is going to happen to the Tree of Heaven but for now the student’s family is going to decide for now and leaving it as is. Our Vision To become a school with an environmental focus that produces students who are environmentally literate by using a hands-on, interdisciplinary, placed-based approach to provide students with meaningful and imaginative learning opportunities where students are constantly engaged in the natural world around our facility. Our Goals  To develop students’ awareness, knowledge, attitudes, skills and actions in regards to environmental content.  Inspire our families to develop an interest in becoming involved in science and sustainability (in our local community). How?  Connect students more directly with the world outside the school by interacting with the community surrounding our facility.  Pursue increased aboriginal understandings through place-based activities on traditional Snuneymuxw Territory.  Continue to focus on core skills but have freedom to pursue individual interests within a topic and place emphasis on key competencies like inquiry, self-reliance, critical thinking, creativity, problemsolving, teamwork, collaboration and technological literacy.  Increase the focus on learning these skills across topic areas. Students are required to meet core curriculum outcomes.  Cover additional outcomes that reflect an emphasis of our ecology and place-based theme. There is a heavy focus on inquiry based, interdisciplinary, hands-on learning. Program Overview Nature Kindergarten/Primary Nature Kindergarten/Primary provides young children with the opportunity to spend larger amounts of time in an outdoor setting. Children have the chance to play, explore and experience natural ecosystems and the materials within. Children spend part of their day outside engaged with their body, mind and spirit in a natural setting, and come back to school where there would be opportunities for indoor learning. The program will meet the needs of the prescribed learning outcomes for Kindergarten as well as the goals of the Primary Program and Early Learning Framework. Children will explore their local, natural environment and learn to know and understand their home place, developing confidence in being outside while meeting the objectives of the curriculum.
