APRIL 2025 LADYSMITH LINKS DEENA BEESTON SWORN-IN AS MAYOR OF LADYSMITH Tricia McKay, who had been acting mayor since October following the resignation of Aaron Stone in September, will resume her duties as councillor. The Town of Ladysmith officially has a new mayor. Deena Beeston was sworn-in as the Town of Ladysmith’s newest mayor at a ceremony held during the Inaugural Meeting of Council at the Ladysmith Seniors Centre on Tuesday, March 18. Beeston, who received the highest number of votes in the 2025 mayoral by-election, becomes the 23rd mayor in Ladysmith’s history and only the second woman to hold the position. “It is truly an honour and a privilege to be Ladysmith’s mayor. I am committed to working tirelessly to meet our community’s needs and build a future that reflects our shared values and aspirations,” says Mayor Deena Beeston. Beeston will also serve as the Town of Ladysmith’s Director on the Cowichan Valley Regional District board for the 2025 term after being appointed by Council during Tuesday’s meeting. FIRESMART REBATES NOW AVAILABLE FOR LADYSMITH, DUNCAN AND CVRD ELECTORAL AREAS Emergency Management Cowichan (EMC), on behalf of the CVRD, the Town of Ladysmith and the City of Duncan, will be launching a FireSmart Rebate program to support homeowners in performing Firesmart activities on residential properties. The Cowichan Regional FireSmart Resiliency Program is designed to help homeowners and communities reduce the risk of wildfire damage. Wildfires are a natural occurrence, but they can also be devastating to homes, businesses, and communities. A Wildfire Home Assessment, available free from EMC, will help home owners identify easy steps and actions to reduce their property’s risk to wildfires. In 2023-2024, more than 200 homes were assessed in the Cowichan region. Recommendations in FireSmart home assessments can now be funded for eligible applicants under this rebate program. “The frequency and intensity of wildfires have increased, making it more important than ever to take steps to protect our community,” said Chris Geiger, Town of Ladysmith Fire Chief. “This rebate program will provide funding to families who want to take steps to protect their home from wildfire, making us more resilient in the future.” Applications for rebates of up to $4,000 opened on March 19, 2025 at a limited amount per community. To be eligible for a rebate, homeowners in the Town of Ladysmith, the City of Duncan and the Electoral Areas of the CVRD must have a free FireSmart Home Assessment completed, and be willing to complete approved FireSmart work within 90 days of the application approval. All information regarding the rebate program and home assessments, including Terms and Conditions, how to apply, and FAQs can be found online at https:// emcowichan.ca/programsresources/fire-smart/. Water Main Flushing Program - Each year, the Town of Ladysmith flushes water supply and distribution water mains. Regular water main flushing is an important part of a comprehensive water management program. Potable water is flushed at a high velocity through the water mains to ensure all piping is refreshed and minor sediment within the pipe is removed. This is one of the methods relied upon by the Town to help keep our drinking water safe and clean. During this flushing period, water users may experience some discoloration of the water supply. Should this occur, running a cold water tap will help to restore water quality back to normal. Residents who are dependent on medical equipment that uses water, or who have any questions regarding the flushing are invited to call Infrastructure Services 250.245.6445 for specific flushing dates.
