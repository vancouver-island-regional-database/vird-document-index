LADYSMITH TAKES STEPS FOR SUSTAINABLE GROWTH “To address our goals for sustainable growth, we have made - and will continue to make -- signiﬁcant investments in our water and wastewater systems. These investments in our award-winning infrastructure will ensure that we are well positioned for decades to come,” said Mayor Stone. The Town of Ladysmith continues to be discovered as a very desirable community to live, work and play and as a result has been cited as one of the fastest growing communities in British Columbia for last year. A recent BC Stats report on population estimates shows Ladysmith welcomed approximately 324 new residents in 2018. This 3.6% year-over-year increase makes our town the fastest growing municipality among those with a population of at least 5,000 residents. “The continued investments in making our community an attractive place to live and do business are paying dividends,” said Mayor Aaron Stone. “We have become one of the premiere destinations for new residents and businesses in one of the most in-demand areas of Canada.” The Province estimates year-over-year population growth by using the health client registry and residential hydro hook-ups. Ladysmith’s population is currently projected to be 9,417 and the community has grown an estimated 2.9% annually “We are also committed to our environmental goals, working to ensure that new development holds up our sustainability vision, reducing negative inputs into our environment and improving outcomes for all.” Ladysmith’s Economic Development Strategy supports attracting residential development and sustainable population growth to create a larger market for local businesses, while also expanding the local workforce. The Town similarly continues to support local not-forproﬁt organizations and to lobby our provincial and federal government partners on affordable housing opportunities and future housing needs for seniors and people with limited incomes. DCC BYLAW REVIEW UPDATE The Town of Ladysmith is finalizing the updated Development Cost Charges (DCC) Bylaw after several indepth Council workshops and a consultation process with the development community as well as other engaged citizens. Development Cost Charges or DCCs are collected from developers or builders to help pay for the capital cost of installing growth-related infrastructure. Ladysmith Council has seized opportunities to support, engage and grow local business with its “open for business” strategy priority. DCCs are collected for water, sanitary sewer, storm drainage, roads and park acquisition and parkland improvement. DCCs are payable by anyone obtaining subdivision approval for the creation of new residential parcels or a building permit for the construction or expansion of commercial, industrial, institutional or multi-family residential projects or for a single family residential building permit when a DCC has not previously been paid. The Town is also grateful for the ongoing efforts of our small business champions, the Ladysmith Chamber of Commerce and the Ladysmith Downtown Business Association. The current DCC Bylaw was adopted in 2011. Regular review is important to ensure that the projects, associated costs and growth estimates are up-to-date. We also continue to strengthen our partnership our with Stz’uminus First Nation as we move forward on the remediation of Ladysmith Harbour and implementation of the Waterfront Area Plan – a major economic driver of our communities for future generations. The timeframe for a DCC program is typically 20 years The review of the current program started in 2016. Trail and District (Lower Columbia) took home the category award at the gala in Vancouver on Feb. 21, but the Town is proud to have been recognized as a small business champion. Council received feedback from the DCC Open House at a meeting in March and approved the first three readings of the new Bylaw at its meeting on April 1. The Open for Business Awards recognize and celebrate communities that best demonstrate they are promoting business friendly initiatives within their region. TABLETS AND PAPERLESS AGENDAS In keeping with Ladysmith’s commitment to sustainability, Town Council will save more than 50,000 sheets of paper each year with its switch to paperless agendas. Ladysmith joins the local governments across Canada that are replacing often lengthy paper-based meeting documents with digital versions which can be accessed at a Council meeting through a secure Microsoft Cloudbased platform called eScribe. If you’re attending a Council meeting or watching it from the comfort of your home, you’ll see Council members referencing their tablets for agenda material. Councillors will also be able to add comments and follow up notes using their tablets in the same way they would with a hard copy version. A limited quantity of printed agenda packages will still be made available to members of the public at Council and Municipal Services Committee meetings. To view the agendas online, visit: https://www.ladysmith. ca/city-hall/mayor-council/council-minutes-and-agendas.
