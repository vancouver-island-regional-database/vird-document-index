Gale, DASG Rep Meeting No. B07 CALL TO ORDER The Chair called the meeting to order at 6:03 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. ADDITIONS TO THE AGENDA There were none. DELETIONS TO THE AGENDA There were none. CHANGE IN ORDER There were none. APPROVAL OF THE AGENDA Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held April 11, 2018 Folio BC18/04/11-01 That the Agenda be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY APPROVAL OF THE MINUTES Minutes - March 7, 2018 Folio BC18/04/11-02 That the minutes from the Business Committee meeting held March 7, 2018, be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS There were none. SENIOR STAFF REPORTS Secretary-Treasurer McVeigh and Director of Planning and Operations Sabo Re: Appointment of Architect Ecole Hammond Bay Elementary Expansion Folio BC18/04/11-03 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Higginson IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) direct staff to award the contract to KMBR Architects Planners Inc. for the design and construction supervision for the expansion of Ecole Hammond Bay Elementary subject to the successful negotiation of a client/architect agreement. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Secretary-Treasurer McVeigh Re: Special Education Audit Report Folio BC18/04/11-04 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Higginson That the Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive the 2017/18 Special Education Audit Report. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held April 11, 2018 CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Secretary-Treasurer McVeigh Re: Operating Surplus/Deficit Policy Folio BC18/04/11-05 Superintendent Blain Re: Review of Budget Strategies, Requested Presentations, Q&A Folio BC18/04/11-06 Secretary-Treasurer McVeigh presented a draft Operating Surplus and Deficit Policy. The draft policy is attached to the Agenda. Trustees will review the draft policy and forward any comments/concerns to Executive Assistant Matthews. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Rae IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan That the Business Committee receive the first draft Operating Surplus/Deficit Policy and that Trustees review this draft policy and bring it back to the next Business Committee meeting for further discussion. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Assistant Superintendent Esliger presented on the Focus School Model and Community LINK. Goeff Steel, Kevin Brand and John Cunnian presented on behalf of NSAA. Presentations are attached to the Agenda. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Higginson IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brzovic That the Business Committee recommend that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) write a letter to the Minister of Mental Health and Addictions to seek funding for the new position of Mental Health and Addictions Coordinator, as a pilot model for possible expansion to other districts. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brzovic Folio BC18/04/11-07 That the Business Committee recommend that Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve, in principle, the 2018 / 2019 Operating Budget on the following basis: 1. Budget initiatives totaling approximately 4.3M dollars, as presented by Superintendent Blain during the 2018 / 2019 Budget Process, are included and; 2. Four Community School Coordinators be retained for a one-year period and funded through the District’s Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held April 11, 2018 Accumulated Surplus and; 3. No other substantive changes (eliminations or additions) will be reflective in the Annual Operating Budget Bylaw (2018 / 2019) unless added or eliminated by the Board or added or eliminated after partner groups and the community have had an opportunity for feedback. Opposed by Trustee Higginson CARRIED Director of Planning and Operations Sabo Re: Rutherford/FJN Transportation Folio BC18/04/11-08 CORRESPONDENCE REFERRED FROM REGULAR BOARD MEETING Director of Planning and Operations Sabo presented an Information Sheet on Bus Routes from Rutherford Elementary to/from Frank J. Ney Elementary. The presentation is attached to the Agenda. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Rae IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan That the Business Committee recommend that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopt Option 2: Use Spare Bus and Driver, for a one year period. This route is subject to review pursuant to the guidelines of the Transportation Policy and Procedure. Opposed by Opposed by Trustee Higginson CARRIED There was none.
