CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY CHAIRPERSON'S REPORT Chair Welch told the board that she had visited a number of schools. Her goals, this year, is to visit all of the schools in the district. She said that Trustees had attended a social at the NDTA Office earlier this month. She noted with thankfulness for the safe return of Woodlands and Wellington students from Japan and took a moment to remember all of the people who have lost friends, relatives, and their possessions in this terrible tragedy. COMMITTEE REPORTS From the Education Committee School Registrations and Transfers The Education Committee reported to the Board that it considered and discussed strategies to ensure registrations and transfers at schools are done within acceptable time lines. It was suggested that we look at our communications procedures in relations to advising parents, students and schools of the timelines involved in registrations and transfers. It was hoped that in the review of the French Immersion programs that they Committee will take a look at the registration process. VISTA - Personalized Learning The Education Committee reported to the Board of Education that it was engaged in a meaningful dialogue at the Committee level and with a set purpose of arriving at a useful vision of personalized learning for School District 68. UNFINISHED BUSINESS Low Barrier Housing - Motion Referred from the February 28th Board Meeting That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) write to the City of Nanaimo expressing our concerns regarding the safety of students at Quarterway School because of the proposed construction of two low barrier Page 8 of 9 8 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held March 30, 2011 houses in close proximity Elementary School. to Quarterway IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Nelson Allen IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Donna Allen Folio R11/03/30-15 Motion to Amend Folio R11/03/30-16 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) write to the City of Nanaimo expressing our concerns regarding the safety of students at Quarterway School because of the proposed construction of two low barrier houses in close proximity to Quarterway Elementary School. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Bonkowski That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) writes to the City of Nanaimo expressing its concerns regarding the potential for conflict and/or negative impact, on SD68 properties and their occupants, associated with locating social/low barrier housing developments in close proximity to schools, and further, expressing its expectation that the District will be recognized and included as a principal partner with the City, in the establishment of Good Neighbour Agreements pertain to any such developments. Trustee Murchie said he thought this was a motion of substitution rather than an amendment. Chair Welch agreed and stated she would accept it as a Motion of Substitution. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Trustee Bonkowski left the meeting at 7:58 pm.
