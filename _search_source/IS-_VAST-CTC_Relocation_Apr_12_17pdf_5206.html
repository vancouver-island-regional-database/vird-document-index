Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION BUSINESS COMMITTEE INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: April 12, 2017 Board of Education Assistant Superintendent, Scott Saywell Relocation of VAST & Career Technical Centre to John Barsby Community School BACKGROUND: At the March 15, 2017 Regular Board meeting, Superintendent John Blain informed the Board of Education the proposed relocation of both The Vocational Adult Secondary Training (VAST) and The Career Technical Centre (CTC) from Nanaimo District Secondary School (NDSS) to John Barsby Community School. District staff were to explore the feasibility of relocating the district programs and report to the Board with the findings at the April 12, 2017 Business Committee meeting. Rationale as stated by Superintendent Blain: “Due to the implications associated with the Supreme Court of Canada (SCC) decision to restore the 2002 language in the Collective Agreement between the British Columbia Public School Employers Association/BCTF, and further complicated by the Nanaimo District Teachers’ Association local language, the construction of a new synthetic turf field as well as the increased enrolment at NDSS, it is necessary to consider the relocation of VAST and CTC presently situated at NDSS.” “The SSC decision will dictate smaller class size thereby requiring the use of more classrooms at NDSS. The construction of the synthetic turf field will require that the two VAST portables be moved to another location. Enrolment at NDSS continues to grow putting further pressure on the need for more space.” Superintendent Blain further explained that the next step would be to undertake a financial feasibility study as well as consult with stakeholder groups for input. CONSULTATION: Staff have concluded the consultation process and report the following:  District staff met with parents, teachers, support staff, and community partners (DPAC, CUPE, NDTA and Snuneymuxw First Nation).  The majority of those consulted were in favour of the relocation of VAST and CTC to John Barsby.  The majority of respondents appreciate the need to move VAST and CTC and recognize some positive implications. The majority of students that attend VAST live in the south end of Nanaimo (Fig. 1) and many are of Aboriginal descent.(Fig. 2)  Some staff and parents at John Barsby are concerned about the public perception moving VAST to John Barsby. They fear it will add to an already negative perception that the school presently endures.  Some parents and staff also wondered if the district had considered other locations for VAST, in particular, Cedar Community Secondary School. Due to implications of the restored language to the NDTA/NLPS Collective Agreement, the Principal at NDSS predicts that should the Board decide to keep VAST and CTC at NDSS, he will require at least four portables to be added to the property to accommodate the extra teaching staff. The cost to relocate a portable is approximately $40,000 (see fig. 3) thereby generating a cost of approximately $160,000 to relocate four portable. There exists a further challenge in that the only space available in which to locate portables is on the front lawn of NDSS between the school and Wakesiah Avenue. Figure 3. To keep those students within the neighbourhood in which they live, John Barsby is the best location for VAST. While all other secondary schools were considered for the CTC and VAST relocation, only John Barsby has the physical, space to accommodate both programs as well a location that is central to Nanaimo, Ladysmith and the Cedar community. The CTC is a school that can provide a great educational opportunity for students in grade twelve interested in a trades career and will greatly benefit the students at John Barsby.
