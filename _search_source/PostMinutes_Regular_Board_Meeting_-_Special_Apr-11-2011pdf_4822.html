CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Budget Presentation - Staff Mr. Turin made a short presentation outlining the background of the school district’s budget process and the proposed budget reductions. Detailed information, including Mr. Turin’s presentation, is available on the school district’s website www.sd68.bc.ca. PRESENTATIONS Paula Kuffler, Elementary School Counsellor Paula Kuffler spoke on behalf of elementary school counselors, opposing the proposal to eliminate the services of child, youth and family support workers in elementary schools Derek DeGear, President, NDTA Derek DeGear, president of the NDTA, spoke on behalf of teachers and all employees in the B.C. education system. He outlined the impacts of Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2011 2 budget reductions over the past ten years and urged the Board to take action to ensure education receives the appropriate level of funding. Greg Paterson Greg Paterson, of the Maintenance Department, said that the district’s facilities are deteriorating because budget reductions over the years have meant that district tradespeople are unable to carry out preventative maintenance on schools. Kirstin Verhoven, Nanaimo Music Teachers Association Kirstin Verhoeven, of the Nanaimo Association of Music Educators, outlined the benefits of music programs and advocated for quality music programs in District 68 schools, including dedicated space in elementary schools for music teachers. Rob Zver, President, CUPE Local 606 Rob Zver, president of CUPE Local 606, said that the proposed budget suggestions place the majority of the reductions on support staff employees. He suggested the Board say no to further cuts. Robin Smith Robin Smith, a child, youth and family support worker, outlined the way that these employees liaise with families and communities to support students. Jim MacGregor Jim MacGregor, community school coordinator at NDSS, outlined the community school programs and the way those programs support students and families. Camilla McGuire Camilla McGuire and Louise McArter, child, youth and family support workers, outlined their concerns about the proposal that the service be removed from elementary schools. They gave an overview of the supports they offer to students. Sherry Blake Sherry Blake, a parent from Woodlands Secondary, spoke about the importance of music programs and the need for music courses to be scheduled outside of the regular timetable. Tabitha Smith Tabitha Smith, a child, youth and family support worker, said that the workers are a link for the most vulnerable students and families in the Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2011 3 community. She outlined some of the programs and supports provided by child, youth and family support workers. Jodi Lucas Jodi Lucas, an education assistant, talked about the important role of the education assistants in classrooms and schools. She also spoke on behalf of child, youth and family support workers, community school workers and custodial staff. Susan Reno Susan Reno, a child, youth and family support worker, outlined the ways in which the workers provide supports to vulnerable students and the reasons that the district needs the services of child, youth and family support workers. Teacher-Librarians Teacher-librarians said that they are concerned about proposed changes in staffing in secondary school libraries. They said that studies have shown that teacher-librarians make a difference in students’ academic achievement and replacing some teacher-librarians time with library clerks is not a solution to the budget shortfall. Jaye MacFarlane In a written presentation, Jaye MacFarlane, a Woodlands parent, advocated for the retention of teacher staffing for courses outside the regular timetable, such as band. Ronni Pakosz, North Cedar Intermediate & Woodbank Primary PAC Ronni Pakosz, from the North Cedar and Woodbank PAC, talked about the cycle of budget reductions and declining enrolment. She said that she does not support reductions to education assistants and child, youth and family support workers and suggested the Board consider submitting a deficit budget. Sandra Walsh Sandra Walsh, a child, youth and family support worker, said that having connections with students and families is paramount in students’ academic success and that child, youth and family support workers support the most marginalized students. Students Kyla, Aubrey, Quinnishia and Solace Students Kyla, Aubrey, Quinnishia and Solace told the Board about the ways that child, youth and family support workers have helped them personally and other students at difficult times in 4 Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2011 their lives. Barb Kovich Barb Kovich, a Woodlands parent, spoke about the secondary teacher staffing for courses outside the timetable. She said that music programs keep many students going to school. Sharon Fitzgerald Sharon Fitzgerald, chair of the Uplands Park Elementary PAC, commented on the year-afteryear budget reductions that erode the education system. She asked the Board to review its priorities when making budget reductions. Alita Dancy Alita Dancy, a third year student at VIU and former NDSS student spoke about community schools and the value of the work of community school coordinators. Josephine Staddon Josephine Staddon, a teacher in the district, talked about vulnerable students and the challenges they face and outlined the value of the child, youth and family support workers. Sandi Coones, Harewood Neighborhood Association Sandi Coones, of the Harewood Neighbourhood Association, said the Board should follow up on the review of community schools before making any decisions about the community school coordinator positions. Ron Farino Ron Farino, from CUPE Local 606, read letters from students and teachers supporting child, school and family support workers.
