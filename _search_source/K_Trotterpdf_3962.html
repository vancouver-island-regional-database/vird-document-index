April 14, 2014 Dear Trustees: I am writing to you as a parent of elementary aged children in Cedar. I recently attended the information session hosted by our school PAC regarding the plans for the new Cedar Elementary. I can say without hesitation that I left feeling great dismay for the future of my young children’s education. This new school is nothing like the school you told us we were getting in our community. Three members of senior district staff arrived at the meeting and even after they tried to calm the fears of parents in the room, I still left with a knot in my stomach. When you voted on this plan you thought there would be 475 students at the school. You thought the school would be an arts focussed magnet school. You thought it would enhance the education of the small children who would attend the school. When you voted on the plan did you know that the fields were unusable all year round? All the fields, not just two of the three fields. There was a phys-ed teacher from Cedar at the meeting who has taught at the school since it opened and she told us that the two fields closest to the school are not usable at all during the school year and the one field that we have been told we have to make do with is actually not usable from December 1-April 1. Did you know this when you voted on the plan? Did you know that the bathroom fixtures would not be lowered? That the lockers would be left in the school? I have to assume that you did not know these details, because if you did you would not have thought that this was going to be a school that would enhance the education of the children attending it. My understanding of this plan is that one of the main reasons for it is to provide enrichment opportunities for the students. Currently, Woodbank has an outdoor kindergarten program, a very successful “run club” and a triathlon club that sees numerous students compete in the Parksville Triathlon each year. All of these programs create connectedness amongst the students, particularly kinetic and active students who might normally struggle in a typical school environment. However, with the plan to move students to Cedar Elementary, these programs will have to fold. You might reply by saying that you hope that vacuum will be filled by another program, initiated by another teacher, but hoping in this case is not good enough. And furthermore, these particular programs serve a group of students who will be most impacted by the plans for Cedar Elementary as there will be no outdoor playfields for these highly active students to run their energy out on. It is a known and proven fact that a lack of outdoor play space results is agitated and distracted students in the classroom. With this plan, the smallest students of Cedar will actually be getting much less than what they currently have at Woodbank. The current renovation plans say that they are “scaled back” due to the new enrolment numbers, however they are still costing $2million and the students are getting much less than was originally promised to them. Finally, using Annual Facilities Grant money to tear apart the NEWEST high school in the district to create a substandard elementary facility (even substandard to what the students currently have) is not only highly questionable, it is outright irresponsible. So much has changed from when you voted for the creation of Cedar Elementary. I recently read the remarks made by our zone trustee, Nancy Curley about how the decision has been made about this school and the district is moving forward with the repurposing of this school. I worked in the private sector for many years until I transitioned to sustainable small farming. When I was in the private sector we had to make alterations to plans as new information arose. I understand why you voted the way you did with the information you had at the time. However, the parameters have changed significantly. It would be foolish to continue with this plan without acknowledging that it no longer makes sense. What are the alternatives? Woodbank is a far superior facility to NCI. With about half of the proposed budget you could easily add a wing onto Woodbank to house the extra students. Or you could simply use a four classroom portable and drop it on the school grounds at Woodbank, pretty up the outside of said portable and make it the grade 6-7 wing of the school. Having Woodbank sit boarded up 2.5 kilometers away from Cedar Elementary while the students are stuck inside, in an oversized, unfriendly environment does not make educational or economic sense, especially when the whole point of this plan was to provide enhanced facilities for the students. You might be asking how having an empty Cedar Secondary only 2.5 kilometers away from Woodbank makes sense. Well, we all know the answer to that conundrum. If you don’t want to keep the high school as a high school then rent it out to the RDN as a recreation centre. Give them a good deal on the rent and use the money to save for the Cedar Elementary that you envisioned when you voted on this plan. Everybody wins in that scenario. There is no community that has been hit harder than Cedar when it comes to this Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan. This community deserves the same amount of consultation, if not more than other communities in this district. Cedar is part of SD68 too, and so are my children. It often feels like you have forgotten that in your effort to make decisions that are good for the entire district and these latest plans for Cedar Elementary do nothing to convince me otherwise. Sincerely, Kevin Trotter.
