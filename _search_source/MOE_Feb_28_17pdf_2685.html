February 28, 2017 Ref: 192960 To: All British Columbia Board Chairs Re: Substance Use and Overdose Prevention/Intervention for Schools British Columbia has seen a significant rise in opioid-related overdose deaths, with 922 deaths last year (an 80.8 percent increase over 2015). In October 2016, the Provincial Health Officer, Dr. Perry Kendall wrote school district superintendents and independent school administrators regarding the rise of illicit drug overdoses this year. Dr. Kendall indicated the Province’s focus for youth aged 10–18 continues to be on prevention through better decision-making, increasing awareness of naloxone, and safer drug practices. In addition, Dr. Kendall stated that schools are not currently designated as high-risk environments. Recently, the BC Coroners Service concluded an inquest into the death of Brandon Jansen. Following this inquest, one of the recommendations made was for naloxone kits to be made available in the school system with trained personnel on site. Following this recommendation, Dr. Kendall has reiterated his guidance in a letter dated February 24th that:    Schools are not designated as a high-risk environment at this time as no overdoses have occurred in a school. If a school administrator knows that a particular school has a high-risk population or is aware of students using drugs on or near school grounds, in addition to the referral and support protocols that the school may have, it is recommended they obtain a naloxone kit for the school and ensure a person is available who is comfortable administering it. Naloxone kits can be purchased behind the counter in many pharmacies for about $50, and pharmacists can provide overdose prevention and response training to ensure safe administration. Letters from Dr. Kendall with this updated information are being sent out to all school district superintendents and independent school administrators. I encourage you to contact your local Medical Health Officer if you have any questions.
