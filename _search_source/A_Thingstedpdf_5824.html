Anette Thingsted PO Box96 Lantzville, BC VOR 2H0 April 26, 2016 Board of Education School District #68 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, BC VQR 3K6 Dear School District Trustees and Superintendent, l am writing to voice my concernregarding proposed budgetary changes with respect to the elimination of courtesy busing, sewing the children in our community. l object to the proposed transportation changes, and do not feel that the Board is making budget decisions that best serves the needs of students in the long term. The Seaview Elementary School catchment is large, and encompasses both sides of Highway 19 and and extends down to the Snaw—Naw-AsFirst Nation. Our community does not have sidewalks and there are narrow or non-existent shoulders on many of the roads. Currently Seaview Elementary has approximately 85 students that regularly take the bus and nearly all are defined as courtesy riders. There are a number of singleparent families living in upper Lantzville and the Snaw-Naw-As First Nation Reserve that do not have access to a vehicle. Bussing children to the school is their only option. There are huge safety concerns with your proposal to eliminate bus service to our children. Children should not be crossing a main highway, with vehicles traveling in excess of 100km/hr, to attend school. There are at least six lanes on the highway with no access to overpasses or underpasses to facilitate safe crossing. The current bus service is not a courtesy, it is needed for the safety of our children. The community of Lantzville does not have any alternative transportation options. The transit service is very limited in the area, and does not offer any transportation to either Seaview Elementary or Dover Secondary school. l respect that your board has difficult financial decisions to make however your recent budget decisions have been biased to one community within School District 68. As a board member you have been elected to represent the entire School District. l would suggest you look at other options to cut costs, such as following through on the recommendations that were outlined in the 10 Year Facilities Plan. Sincerely yours, Anette Thingsted
