THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 ACTION SHEET TO: The Board of Education FROM: Chris Southwick, Assistant Superintendent DATE: July 12, 2010 SUBJECT: 2010-2011 District Achievement Contract By July 15, 2010 all school districts in British Columbia are required to submit a district plan for student achievement to the Minister of Education. Improving student achievement is the top priority of the Ministry of Education and the intent of the SD68 2010-2011 District Achievement Contract is to focus district attention and resources on increasing success for each student. Connection with School Plans: The information in the Draft 2010-2011 Annual School Plans for all schools in the district was used in the development of the District Achievement Contract 2010-2011. Draft School Plans were submitted by school principals on behalf of their School Planning Councils in May, 2010. In October, Zone Trustees will be provided plans to review for each school in their zone and invited to discuss these plans with the senior staff member also assigned to the zone schools. District staff will meet with each school principal in their assigned zone to listen, discuss, make suggestions for improvement as needed, and provide feedback on the plan. Utilization of Data: Data from the Ministry of Education Satisfaction Surveys; Foundation Skills Assessments (FSA); classroom assessment of student work; school-wide writes; school marks; BC Performance Standards assessment of Literacy, Numeracy and Social Responsibility; Provincial Exam results; and data collected for the Aboriginal Education Enhancement Agreement (AEEA) have all been considered in developing the Achievement Contract for 2010-2011 and in establishing student achievement targets for next year and beyond. The Process: Data was reviewed in the spring; additional data was submitted at the end of June and received from the Ministry of Education over the past few months – all of which has been reviewed and considered. Some of the 2009-2010 data will not be available until later this fall, and will be used once available. Senior Staff, Management Committee and other district staff have provided input and suggestions for the District Achievement Contract. 1 The key elements of this plan, established several years ago, remain broadly the same as in previous years. We have made some changes to the goal areas to better reflect the work currently being done in the district focusing on student achievement. Some of the changes and additions to this year’s Achievement Contract are in response to the guidelines stated by the Ministry of Education. The format has changed to reflect two goals. The first goal is to have every student complete grade twelve. The objectives to support this goal are a) to improve graduation and completion rates, b) to have every child meet expectations in grade four reading, writing and numeracy, and c) to improve the pass rates in required provincial exams. In response to the need for updated policies and procedures around critical incidents, the second goal continues to focus on students demonstrating safe, caring, orderly behaviours and conduct. Summary: The District Achievement Contract 2010-2011 is designed to be clear and easily read. Targets for improvement have been stated for each goal area for all of our learners and in addition, we continue to track the performance of our Aboriginal learners as well as special needs students. Action plans in this Achievement Contract address strategies and structures to support attaining our goals. A key strategy for the first goal and its objectives is the further refinement of assessment instruments to more accurately assess the performance of students and provide assessment information that can support student learning. School District 68 is committed to improving the achievement of each student. Our District Achievement Contract is informed by - and aligned with - school-based priorities. The plan reflects our growing skills at utilizing evidence for data-based decision making and states clear goals for intended student achievement. Ultimately, it is a plan that is a living document and one that is current to the information, participation and decisionmaking processes available at this time. The District Achievement Contract is presented to the Board of Education for consideration and approval prior to submission to the Minister of Education. RECOMMENDATION: The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves the 2010-2011 District Achievement Contract and (allowing for minor editorial revisions) forwards it to the Minister of Education by July 15, 2010. 2
