As presented at the SD68 Board of Education Special Board Meeting September 2, 2015. Good afternoon, my name is Tine McLennan. I want to thank you for the opportunity to speak to you today. I am here representing Rutherford Open and Thriving. We are a group of Rutherford families and concerned community members. The group was formed in 2007 in response to School District 68’s Facilities Review Process. We continue to support and promote the thriving Rutherford Elementary Community. Today I will present to you some of our concerns with the Updated Facilities Plan presented June10, 2015 with the understanding that some of the material may be presented to you again today by staff. The elementary school population in our area is growing and our neighbourhoods are thriving. Without Rutherford Elementary School every neighbouring school would be put over capacity immediately. As presented in June 2015, Rutherford students could be dispersed to surrounding schools as early as September 2016 or 2017. The resulting over capacity at neighbouring elementary schools is unacceptable and reminds us that Rutherford Elementary is needed. (Enrollment numbers taken from June 10, 2015 Updated Facilities Plan 2015-2021) Randerson Ridge Elementary would operate at 104% capacity, which is 14 students over functional capacity. This would result in the likely need for at least one portable McGirr Elementary would operate at 127% capacity, which is 97 students over functional capacity. This would result in the need for at least 4-5 portables. Frank J Ney Elementary (Rutherford students only) would operate at 150% capacity, which is 105 kids over functional capacity. This would result in the need for a least 5 portables. Frank J Ney Elementary (Rutherford and Dept. Bay students) would operate at 182% capacity, which is 169 kids over functional capacity. This would result in at least 8 portables at a site where the School District already rents Harry Wiper Park from the City of Nanaimo to provide outdoor play areas for the students due to insufficient outdoor play area for the current population. This means 11-16 portables would be needed. The BC Ministry of Education Area Standards Document published in May 2012 was used to calculate the number of portables needed at each site to accommodate students. (BC Ministry of Education Area Standards Document, page 11 section 2.4.1. The average elementary classroom capacity for grades 1-8 schools is 23.29) Where would these 11-16 portables coming from? The school district owns portables but many of these portables have sat boarded up for years so one would have to question the health and safety of using them particularly in a facilities renewal plan. Portables take away physical space at school sites resulting in smaller play areas, smaller fields. Portables are not an acceptable long term solution for housing our students. Every student in an overcrowded school will have less access to computer labs, library, gym time, washroom facilities, gym equipment, playground equipment and outdoor play areas if this plan goes ahead this would become the norm in all schools in our area. Rutherford Elementary is needed to ensure all students have adequate access to all school amenities. There is significant development in the area that further justifies the need for Rutherford Elementary. The Linley Valley or other developments in the area need to be taken into consideration and be part of the planning for this area. The City of Nanaimo has provided us with the preliminary numbers for the Linley Valley developments and they total close to 600 units. Some child yield estimate projection calculations use a multiplication factor of 0.50 children per household to estimate the number of children a new development would yield. If we applied that factor to the Linley Valley developments we are looking at an additional 300 kids in the area within the foreseeable future. Some of these houses may be in the in the Uplands Elementary catchment, a school already projected to be at 97% capacity in 2016. Rutherford is needed to help absorb these students into schools in their neighbourhoods and prevent our schools from exceeding optimal elementary size. We understand the proposal includes a possible addition at Frank J Ney. While this may potentially address some of the capacity issues at Frank J Ney it could be years away and subject to Ministry Funding approval etc. This also doesn’t address the long term capacity issues that would occur at the other neighbouring schools or the new developments in the Frank J Ney catchment bringing additional students to an overcrowded, less than ideal site that is renting field space from the city of Nanaimo. The idea behind having a plan is to prepare for the future. We need to ensure that adequate educational space remains for future generations. The closure and subsequent disposal of school site property is short sighted. Past facilities plans listed the School District property at 6780 Dickenson Rd known as Pioneer Park as a future elementary school site in the Dover Bay feeder schools area. In 2014 this property was signed over to the City of Nanaimo as part of a land swap. We need to realize that the school district no longer owns a Ministry approved surplus school site in the area to allow for future growth and expansion. We have established that elementary enrolment is increasing and with a plan to push our remaining schools significantly over capacity we will have no ability accommodate the school age children resulting from future developments. Rutherford Open and Thriving has been suggesting since 2008 that catchment boundaries be reviewed. Rutherford’s catchment is one of the smallest by area in the District. Pieces of the catchment have been assigned to neighbouring schools to support their growth resulting in a smaller catchment for Rutherford. The District has the opportunity to mitigate the effects of out of catchment students by encouraging students to attend their in catchment school, not adding additional classes to accept out of catchment students in schools at or near capacity. Rutherford Elementary School is needed and plays an important role in the North End schools being able to absorb the children moving into our neighbourhoods and new developments. By continuing with the plan to rebuild Rutherford Elementary, the School District will be well positioned to handle the growth and development in the area. A rebuilt Rutherford Elementary and re alignment of the catchment boundaries would allow for better distribution of students in the area. This would allow for optimal capacity utilization in schools of optimal size, limit the use of portables and provide better learning environments for all. Rutherford Elementary School is vital to ensuring that elementary schools in our area continue to thrive and provide a quality education for our students. The School District 68 Strategic Plan states “Our Core Business: Providing Quality Education in Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools”. Quality education does not happen in overcrowded schools.
