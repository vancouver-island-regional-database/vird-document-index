Presentation to Education Committee of School District #68 Presenter: Derek DeGear ❖ Member of District Assessment Review Committee (2014) ❖ Member of Task Force on Assessment and Accountability (2009-2011) ❖ Facilitator - Assessment for Learning SD68/NDTA January 7, 2015 ❖ BCTF Facilitator - Assessment for Learning (2008-2010) ❖ Development and Marking of FSAs (20012003) ❖ Development of assessment exemplars for Mountain View Elementary (20042005) Below are selections taken from district communications on assessment in our district: DATE: June 17, 2011 TO: The Board of Education SUBJECT: Assessment and Accountability Task Force Recommendations “Following extensive conversations, the Task Force has agreed upon the following statements of belief: ● The purpose of the assessment must be clear, transparent and respected; ● Both formative and summative assessment practices are necessary to support student learning; ● The summative structures of the BC Performance Standards and report cards are key indicators at the school and district level and should be utilized to assess achievement and progress of students and specific programs and initiatives.” From: Cathy Kelt Sent: Monday, May 27, 2013 10:02 AM Subject: Notice of Motion - Policy 1.8 “The attached draft Policy – 1.8 The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment was approved to be sent out as a Notice of Motion at last week’s Board Meeting. Please distribute this to your staff. If there are any comments and/or suggestions please forward them to me by June 23rd. This policy will be on the June 26th Board Agenda.” November 12, 2013 From: David Hutchinson To All District Staff “In August of 2013, the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education adopted a district policy on the Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment.” Full documents and additional communications can be provided on request. March 3, 2014 From: Darlene Crane To: Member of District Assessment Review Committee “The Department of Learning Services and Superintendent both have validated our work and our recommendations.” “ For grades four through nine, the assessment review committee recommends that the district design a common formative assessment as opposed to adopting either the RAD or the DART.” November 27, 2014 From: David Hutchinson To: All Teaching Staff “Last year, the Board approved a policy on The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment.” “To clarify, all K-3 teachers are expected to administer the Primary Years Literacy Assessment, and all Grades 4-9 teachers are expected to administer either the RAD or DART literacy assessments, as per the attached 2014-2015 school year schedule.” Board Governance Policy 1.7 Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment (1.0) “Results of provincial assessments should be communicated to participating learners in order to facilitate personal growth and goal setting.” Full documents and additional communications can be provided on request.
