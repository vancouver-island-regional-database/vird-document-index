Budget and Bylaw due to Ministry of Education Page 12 of 74 8.3 BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET Date: January 17, 2018 To: Business Committee From: Carrie McVeigh, Secretary-Treasurer RE: Appointment of Audit Firm RECOMMENDATION That the Business Committee recommend that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) appoint KPMG as auditors for School District No. 68 for a term of 3 years, with an option for two one-year renewals. The first audit year will be fiscal year ending June 30, 2018. Background In accordance with Section 158 of the School Act, the Board of each school district is required to appoint an auditor to audit the accounts and financial transactions of the school district and to issue an audit opinion on its annual financial statements. MNP has been the auditor for School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) since 2010. In March 2017, when MNP was appointed for an additional year, staff advised they would be issuing an RFP for the following year. The Audit RFP was issued on Nov 29th, 2017. The District received 3 proposals. All three firms were either regional or national. Page 13 of 74 8.3 The stated assessment criteria/weighting, on which the proposals were evaluated, were: 40% Capability of proponent and audit team 40% Proposed audit strategy 20% Fee Staff recommend appointing KPMG, Victoria team, led by Partner, Lenora Lee. The fee quoted for 2017/18 is $24,000. This is a reduction from the 2016/17 fiscal year, which was $34,125. The fee structure presented by KPMG in their submission is: Year 1 $24,000 Year 2 $24,000 Year 3 $24,500 Year 4 $24,500 [optional renewal year] Year 5 $25,000 [optional renewal year] In additional to KPMG presenting the lowest annual fee, staff reasons for the recommendation include KPMG’s significant experience with school districts. In addition, they are providing a seminar on Enterprise Risk Management, free of charge. This is an initiative that staff want to move on and was identified in the Ministry of Finance review (2016 /2017) as an area that the school district should focus on. Of significant interest, is KPMG’s strategic commitment to First Nations communities – Inspire Awards and Mentorship Program; participation in Aboriginal Career Fairs; participation in Martin Aboriginal Education Initiative Accounting Mentorship Program. Reference and Resources School Act of BC [Excerpt] – Current at January 3, 2018 [RSBC 1996] CHAPTER 412 Part 8 — Finance Division 1 — Provincial Funding Appointment of auditor 158 (1) Unless the Auditor General is appointed in accordance with the Auditor General Act as the auditor of the board of a school district, the board of the school district must appoint an auditor to audit the accounts of the board. (2) The auditor appointed by the board must be a person who is a member or a partnership whose partners are members in good standing of the Chartered Professional Accountants of Canada or the Organization of Chartered Professional Accountants of British Columbia. 2 Page 14 of 74 8.3 (3) If a board fails or neglects to appoint an auditor and the Auditor General is not appointed in accordance with the Auditor General Act, the minister may on one month's notice to the board appoint an auditor. (4) The board must pay the auditor's remuneration.
