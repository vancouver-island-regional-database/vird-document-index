From the Board of Education Email: BoardOfEducation@sd68.bc.ca November 10, 2015 Honourable Mike Bernier: On behalf of the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education, we bring to your attention the district’s perspective on the difficulties it has experienced since the implementation of MyEducation BC. These challenges created thousands of dollars in overtime costs. While we understand the need for the software upgrade, the Board agrees that the Ministry of Education should compensate the district for these unexpected costs in labour. To manage the MyEducation BC software, along with other daily duties, the district employs application specialists, system/support specialists and a project secretary. The MyEducation BC implementation resulted in a total of 404.67 overtime hours at a cost of $16,398 (from July 1, 2015 to October 16, 2015). One temporary position was created to help support our schools during the transition to MyEducation BC. Due to the ongoing problems during the implementation, the position was extended to March 2016, for an additional cost of $21,396. Again, keep in mind, these are Board level costs and do not include the numerous hours added to our school staff. In an email I had recently written to Alex Attal, Executive Vice-President of Fujitsu America, I had indicated that this change has been more than an inconvenience. The transition process has been filled with frustration, lost time for many users and it has been a complete failure that has had a significant effect on our front line staff, teachers, administrators and most importantly, our students. At recent meetings with the Ministry of Education and the BCSTA, the topic of compensation was discussed and it was asked if the Ministry would be seeking compensation from Fujitsu. We recommend that the Ministry take this matter very seriously and that it begin the process to be compensated for the increased wage expenses for all BC school districts and ensure that districts are the recipients of the reparations made available. I can be reached by email at steve.rae@sd68.bc.ca or by phone 250 729-1246. I look forward to your response. Sincerely, Steve Rae Board Chair cc. BCSTA NDTA CUPE Superintendent’s Office IS Department
