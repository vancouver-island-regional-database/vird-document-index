THEREFORE the Parties agree as follows: 9/11/2014 Page 3 of 22 1.0 INTERPRETATION For the purposes of this agreement, the following definitions shall apply: "Additional Funding" means funding other than core funding and supplementary funding. "Block Grant" means the funds received by the Board from the Province of British Columbia for the education of students in the School District through the funding allocation. "Board" means the Board of Education, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (SD68) "Educational Program" means an organized set of learning activities that, in the opinion of the SFN and the Board, is designed to enable Snuneymuxw First Nation students to develop their individual potential and acquire the knowledge, skills, and attitudes needed to achieve a quality academic and culturally relevant education. " SFN " means the Snuneymuxw First Nation as defined in the Indian Act (Canada). "SFN /NLPS LEA Committee” means the Committee consisting of Snuneymuxw First Nation and School District representatives which monitors the Local Education Agreement. " SFN Education Staff" means the Education Coordinator and Support Worker established by SFN to develop, monitor, and administer SFN education programs. “Early School Leaver” refers to any student leaving school prior to completion of Grade 12. “Snuneymuxw First Nation student" means a student who is a Snuneymuxw First Nations member or who is affiliated and associated with the Snuneymuxw First Nation and who resides on the Snuneymuxw First Nation Reserves and who is enrolled in a school operated by the Board. "Full Time Equivalent (FTE) First Nation student" means a student who is: a) of school age as defined in the School Act, or b) an adult learner in full time attendance in an educational program recognized by the Board, the Ministry, and SFN "LEA" means this Local Education Agreement. "Ministry" means the Ministry of Education. 9/11/2014 Page 4 of 22 "Nominal Roll" means a list of those First Nation students in an Educational Program as at September 30 of a school year even if a student withdraws from, or is enrolled in, an Educational Program after September 30. "Programs" means the regular program, alternate program, ABE program, Pathfinder program, E.S.L. program, Resource Room program, Learning Assistance program and any other programs established under the School Act during the term of this agreement. "School District" means the area constituted under the School Act as Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (SD68) "School Year" means a 12-month period commencing on July 1st and ending on June 30th of the following calendar year. "Supplementary Funding" means the targeted funding provided to school districts by the Ministry for Aboriginal Education Programs. 2.0 PREAMBLE Subject to the provisions of Section 104 of the School Act of British Columbia, SFN and the Board wish to recognize that a Local Education Agreement will afford the SFN an opportunity for greater participation in and control of, the education of their children. SFN and the School Board agree that the unique educational needs of First Nations students are best met through recognition and respect for their culture, language and heritage. The following agreement provides the details of a joint educational venture between SFN and the Board. Both parties wish to make it clear that underlying the provisions is a sincere determination to reach a greater level of understanding, communication and cooperation with respect to education of the SFN children. 3.0 STATEMENT OF SHARED INTENT During the term of this agreement SFN and the Board intend to work together to achieve the following objectives: 3.1 To work diligently to assess the educational needs of SFN students with their families and arrange the delivery of services that will meet these needs. 3.2 To enhance and affirm a strong identity, a sense of belonging, pride in heritage, and healthy self-esteem in SFN students. 3.3 To develop policies, programs, and procedures that increases the awareness and respect of all educational staff for cultural and linguistic differences. To facilitate and increase the percentage of Snuneymuxw First Nation graduates 3.4 9/11/2014 Page 5 of 22 who demonstrate skills of self-sufficiency and responsibility such that they are able to secure employment or gain entrance into post-secondary institutions. 3.5 The Board and the SFN will work collaboratively to define the appropriate steps for the SFN involvement in the Board’s hiring process and once developed will be added as an appendix to this Agreement. 3.6 To actively involve, in collaboration with SFN, Elders of SFN and other First Nations resource people in the school system. 3.7 To collaborate with SFN education personnel to provide opportunities in training and professional development activities of the school district. 3.8 To endorse the Federal and Provincial policies on employment equity. 3.9 To work together to increase awareness of First Nation culture among all students and, subject to funding being available, to provide for the integration of Snuneymuxw cultural values and information about the Snuneymuxw First Nation in appropriate curriculum areas. 3.10 To work together, subject to the Provincial Curriculum guidelines, to provide a curriculum that reflects the linguistic and cultural differences that exist between SFN and other students and designed to enhance the learning experience of both. 3.11 To review regularly the education needs of SFN students and arrange for the delivery of services that will meet these needs. Priority will be placed on early intervention. 3.12 The parties will undertake such measures as are required and reasonable to give effect to the spirit of this agreement. 3.13 To establish a SFN /SD68 LEA Committee. 3.13.1 to ensure regular and ongoing consultation, services, and discussions regarding the education of SFN students; 3.13.2 to collaborate on the development of SFN programs which enhance academic and vocational skills, while promoting personal, social, and cultural growth; and 3.13.3 to monitor this agreement. The composition, mandate, and schedule of meetings of this Committee will be agreed upon by both parties. This includes: 4.4.1 Taking actions to support keeping SFN students in school such as immediate notification to parents/guardians of difficulties a student may be experiencing; working together with parents/guardians to reach feasible and productive solutions that stimulate the interest of the students. 4.4.2 Providing culturally and academically appropriate alternative educational services to potential “early school leavers.” 4.4.3 Accessing the services of support personnel employed by the SFN who are available to assist in the schools. 4.5 The Board agrees to provide equal access and opportunity to SFN students in a manner consistent with Board practices for all students enrolled in educational programs. 4.6 The Board agrees to appoint SFN /SD68 LEA Committee members and support the Agreement. 4.7 The Board agrees to reiterate and monitor its commitment to increasing the number of qualified First Nations personnel working in schools where SFN students are enrolled. 4.8 The Board agrees to support SFN in its initiatives to increase the number of qualified SFN education personnel associated with the delivery of educational programs to SFN students in SD 68. The Board agrees to involve SFN in the planning for and direction of the Supplementary Funding provided by the Province to the Board relating to Aboriginal students participating in Aboriginal education programs and services. 4.9 4.10 The Board agrees to provide, by June 30th of each year, notice to SFN of any planned changes in student grade placements or timetable systems planned by the Board for the September school opening of that year and by March 31st. 9/11/2014 Page 7 of 22 Any planned changes in instructional offerings at schools will be discussed at the February LEA Committee meeting. 4.11 The Board agrees to provide notification of non-instructional days to SFN as soon as these dates are known. 4.12 The Board remains committed to collaborating and consulting with SFN regarding the provision of transportation to SFN students in accordance with Board policy. 4.13 The Board agrees to prepare the following information on the provision of Education Programs to Snuneymuxw First Nation students under this Agreement and to present this information at the October Meeting of the SFN /SD68 LEA Committee. The information shall include details as at September 30 of each school year in accordance with the Freedom of Information Act, concerning: 4.13.1 the number of SFN students covered by this Agreement and their programs; 4.13.2 comparative aggregate data pertaining to attendance, graduation and student retention rates for SFN students in comparison to the general population at their schools; 4.13.3 financial statements related to this Agreement which are prepared by the Board in the regular course of its operation; 4.13.4 measures of participation and success rates of SFN students, suitable to provide a reliable benchmark and a means of tracking program performance. 4.13.5 other issues agreed upon by Board and the SFN. 4.14 The Board agrees to ensure that the annual report related to article 4.13 is prepared and distributed to the SFN on or before the October advisory committee meeting of each year. Therefore, the Board will monitor the progress of Snuneymuxw First Nation students and will strive to ensure that Snuneymuxw First Nation students are placed in appropriate programs. 7.2 Placement of SFN students requiring psycho-educational assessment will occur only after the following steps have been completed: 7.2.1 9/11/2014 informed consent has been obtained from the child’s parent or guardian prior to the assessment and/or placement; Page 10 of 22 7.2.2 an appropriate assessment has been completed, or made available if a recent assessment has been completed, and results made available to the parent, the school-based team, and to SFN Education; 7.2.3 SFN Education Staff has obtained parent or guardian consent to receive information about Snuneymuxw First Nation student assessment and placement; 7.2.4 a written report stating the reason for the placement, the options considered and the educational opportunities gained and lost by the placement, has been received by the parents or guardian and SFN Education; and, 7.2.5 an appeal regarding the assessment and placement must be placed by the parent or legal guardian. in good faith without referring the matter to the Dispute Resolution Committee. 16.2 In the event that SFN Education and Board personnel are unable to resolve a dispute, a letter will be written to the Superintendent of the Board and the SFN Administrator outlining the details of the dispute and the steps that have been taken to resolve the dispute. Within 10 working days of receipt of this letter, the Superintendent and the SFN Administrator will meet to discuss and attempt to resolve the issue. 16.3 Failure of the Superintendent and the SFN Administrator to resolve a dispute will result in the dispute being elevated to the Board of School Trustees and SFN 9/11/2014 Page 15 of 22 Council through written request. Within 10 working days of receipt of a written request, the Board of School Trustees and the SFN Council will meet in an attempt to resolve the dispute. Failure of the Board of School Trustees and the SFN Council to resolve the dispute will result in the Dispute Resolution Committee, as outlined in articles 15.4 to 15.6, dealing with the issue if either party requests in writing that the issue be elevated to the Dispute Resolution Committee. 16.4 Dispute Resolution Committee If a dispute in respect of any interpretation of this Agreement arises between SFN and the Board which is not settled, the parties shall establish a panel consisting of three members. The panel shall be called the Dispute Resolution Committee. The purpose of the Dispute Resolution Committee shall be to resolve as expeditiously as possible any dispute arising under this Agreement so as not to impair progress in the implementation of the provisions of this Agreement. SFN and the Board will each appoint one member of the Dispute Resolution Committee and the two appointees shall agree upon the appointment of the third member, who shall be the Chair of the Dispute Resolution Committee. The Dispute Resolution Committee will convene within ten business days, or such longer period of time as may be reasonably required to appoint the third member of the Dispute Resolution Committee and, to consider and resolve the dispute. 16.5 Proceedings All proceedings before the Dispute Resolution Committee shall be informal. When a dispute is under consideration by the Dispute Resolution Committee, the Dispute Resolution Committee shall determine the manner in which the parties shall proceed to carry out their respective obligations under this Agreement until the dispute is resolved. No formal rules of evidence shall apply to proceedings of the Dispute Resolution Committee, however, established legal procedure and evidentiary rules may be used as a guide in conducting the proceedings. Proceedings of the Dispute Resolution Committee shall not be open to the public. 16.6 Decision The Dispute Resolution Committee shall render a written decision to the parties as soon as possible after the conclusion of its proceedings; the Dispute Resolution Committee shall be required to provide reasons for its decision. The Dispute Resolution Committee shall decide the extent to which each of the parties shall bear the reasonable costs of resolving any particular dispute. Any decision of the Dispute Resolution Committee may be submitted by either of the parties to binding arbitration as provided for in Article 16 if the decision is not acceptable to a party. 17.
