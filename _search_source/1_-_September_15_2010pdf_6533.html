CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY EDUCATIONAL UPDATE School Start-Up Ms. Kathy Bergman, principal of Pleasant Valley told Committee members about the activities during the first week of school. She said the activities were part of an ongoing project at the school that focuses on connecting cultures while supporting student learning. She said the activities were planned by a committee of Pleasant Valley teachers and school district Aboriginal Education staff. The activities included literacy and social responsibility activities, centered around the Circle of Courage. Students participated in multi-age groupings as members of one of four clans – Eagle, Wolf, Bear and Killer Whale. Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held September 15, 2010 NEW BUSINESS John Abbott - Philosophy of Education Mr. Munro provided the Education Committee with an overview of discussions that have been underway among educators in Canada about new ways of thinking about learning. He showed a video sponsored by the New Brunswick government. It was intended to provoke discussions about education. The video can be viewed on the New Brunswick government website at http://www.gnb.ca/0000/indexe.asp. IT WAS MOVED BY TRUSTEE Brennan That the Education Committee recommends to the Board of Education that staff be directed to establish a Nanaimo Secondary Schools Program Revitalization Committee. This group will focus on possible configurations for course and program delivery among Nanaimo Secondary Schools. It may include the consideration of different models such as: distributed learning a campus model utilizing specializations academies a different model for administration.
