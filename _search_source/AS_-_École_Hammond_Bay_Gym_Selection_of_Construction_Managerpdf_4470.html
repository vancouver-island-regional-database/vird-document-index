Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: February 21, 2018 TO: The Board of Education FROM: Carrie McVeigh, Secretary - Treasurer Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Capital Project Development Appointment of Construction Manager – École Hammond Bay Elementary Gym Expansion Recommendation: That the Board of Education direct staff to award a construction management contract to Unitech Construction Management Inc., subject to the successful negotiation of a Construction Management Agreement for the École Hammond Bay Elementary gymnasium expansion project. Background: At the meeting of February 21, 2018, the Board is considering the following motion as recommended by the Business Committee during its meeting on February 14, 2018: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) direct staff to move forward with plans to complete the new gymnasium at École Hammond Bay as Phase 1 of the Facility Plan. Should this motion pass, staff recommend that Unitech Construction Management Inc. be awarded the construction management contract. Funding for the new gymnasium is to come from local capital as discussed in the Business Committee meeting of February 14, 2018. Staff are now preparing to continue with the design in preparation for the procurement related to the expansion of the gym (see Appointment for Architect Action Sheet, dated February 21, 2018). Notwithstanding the necessary approval to proceed with KMBR Architects Planners Inc., the Board has indicated a desire for an accelerated construction timeframe. Preliminary design is complete and the remaining stages include design development (tender documents), procurement and construction. Discussion: Considering the timelines and District Administrative Procedure 513(Purchasing), staff recommends a variance to the tendering requirements for the sole sourcing of a Construction Manager (CM) by engaging Unitech Construction Management Inc. (Unitech) to provide construction management services for the École Hammond Bay gym project. Unitech was chosen for seismic Wellington upgrade, following a selection process that included a request for proposal, a site visit, review and rating of the proposals, shortlisting and interviews. Staff have a strong, established working relationship with Unitech resulting from their excellent work on both the Wellington and Frank J. Ney projects and expect that the immediate selection of Unitech will greatly benefit the École Hammond Bay gym expansion project. By engaging a CM so early in the process, the benefits expected by staff include project cost savings, reduced project risk, a compressed construction schedule and the immediate availability of Unitech staff who are already on site at Frank J. Ney. A CM acts as the owner’s agent and tenders and oversees the work of subcontractors offering flexibility in construction and accommodating the occupants. The early engagement of the services of a CM have the additional benefit of offering the design team contractor-based advice when considering construction methods and materials. This affords a greater opportunity to advance the schedule (giving better assurance of completion in keeping with the goal), more opportunity to adjust the budget as the project proceeds and greater flexibility in considering value added items to the project. The CM approach will also see approximately 90% of the construction work bid on a competitive basis.
