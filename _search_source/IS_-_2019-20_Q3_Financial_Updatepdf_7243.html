pandemic, the district’s operating expenses for the month of June are very difficult to forecast with certainty. As such, the projected surplus may differ from actual year-end results. However, at this time, and with all known information taken into account, the district looks to wrap up operations with a $4.6 million dollar surplus at year-end. The overall surplus, as presented on the financial statements, will be subject to internal restrictions for multiple items. These would include, but not be limited to items such as the Aboriginal Education target funding, year-end school operating balances as those will roll over to the next school year, and any other planned expenditures that we do not take delivery of by June 30th. Current projections put these restrictions at approximately one million dollars; however, this may vary depending on year-end delivery of particular items, as well as service and spending levels during the month of June in support of the school re-start plan and in continued response to COVID-19. The remaining balance of operating surplus will be allocated per the district’s Operating Surplus/Deficit Policy (2.16) and Administrative Procedure (AP524), and presented to the Board of Education. Summary of Expenses: At May 31, the district is 92% through the fiscal year; the percentage of expenses consumed to date, as compared to the third quarter results reported at May 31, 2019, are summarized as follows: The most notable decreases include the substitutes, services and supply figures, which are directly reflective of the Ministry’s mandate to continue paying regular employees during the pandemic but not being required to compensate substitute or casual staff. Additionally, the closure of schools directly affected spending levels for student and operational supports. 3|Page Forecast and Year-End results: The district is anticipating a loss of approximately $517,000 of revenue due to both the global pandemic, and a decrease in International Student revenue compared to budgeted figures. Cafeteria revenue, rentals and leases and investment income were adjusted to account for lower than expected interest rates, inactive cafeterias and the loss of revenue associated with the community use of our facilities. In addition to the loss of revenue, our costs for supplies and services as well as replacement staff and associated benefits were reduced due to COVID-19. These savings are in addition to those derived from situations of unfilled positions due to recruitment and retention, and failure to fill absences. The combined result of decreased revenue and cost savings makes up the estimated $4.6 million dollar year-end surplus. Important to note that the forecast includes approximately $200,000 of additional expenses to account for the 9 extra custodial staff, additional cleaning supplies necessary to ensure the district complies with the standards set by Ministry, and increased technology, all in response to COVID-19. Staff will continue to monitor expense levels throughout June and respond to further pressures due to the pandemic and the re-start of schools, as current surplus levels may be required to support unanticipated costs. Year-end results, inclusive of any associated surplus balances, will be presented with the Financial Statements in September. 4|Page
