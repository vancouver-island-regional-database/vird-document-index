TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Chair Charlene McKay Name of School(s) Visited: Fairview Elementary, Georgia Avenue Elementary, Bayview Elementary Activities happening at school location: a. Related to school goals/school plan: School Name  Fairview: The holiday concert was an enjoyable performance by students and staff. The sense of community at Fairview was highlighted in the performance. The collaborative approach to cross grade performances with some classes working together to showcase their holiday spirit was a delight, and some staff members provided musical accompaniments. b. Related to Board goals/Strategic Plan School Name  Georgia Avenue: I was able to visit the Skills for Life Program at Georgia Avenue accompanied by Vice-Chair Barron. We spent an hour chatting with staff and students who were involved in a variety of activities. Skills for Life is a program that meets the needs of some of our unique learners, and students there are fully supported by a caring and generous staff.  Bayview Elementary: We were invited to join in a morning breakfast with staff before being given an introduction to a Hul’q’umin’um’ language lesson by teacher Adam Manson. Revitalizing the languages of our indigenous students and offering these lessons in schools speaks to actions being taken toward the Board’s goal of reconciliation. Highlights of Visit – School Name  Georgia Avenue: Sharing stories with a student who was able to share his favourite hockey teams and ask me about my family and pets utilizing a personalized iPad to assist in his communication beyond sign language. Highlights of Visit – School Name  Bayview: Enjoying time spent with staff as we shared a community meal before everyone went to their classrooms for the day. Possible areas of support from the Board for future planning/budgeting  Georgia Avenue: Continued communication with teaching staff in the Skills for Life program around playground expansion plans. Other Items to Report Meeting with Tillicum Lelum Board members to learn more about the services offered by one of our indigenous partner groups, and an opportunity to continue to build a positive relationship. Meeting with provincial candidates in the Nanaimo by-election to discuss facilities for learning, and building positive partnerships with MLAs. Attended BCSTA/BCPSEA trustee academy to learn more about the role of trustees as employers.
