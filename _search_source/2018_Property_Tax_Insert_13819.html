YOU CHOOSE HOW TO PAY: • In person at City Hall (cash, cheque or debit card) • In the after-hours drop box at City Hall (beside the front doors) LADYSMITH’S 2018 BUDGET HIGHLIGHTS In 2018, Ladysmith residents will experience a slight increase in property taxes as we continue to make significant, long-term investments that set a solid foundation for the future of our community. This year, taxes will increase by just over 2 per cent based on the assessed value of your property as determined by BC Assessment. For an average single family dwelling, the municipal share of property tax you pay will rise slightly compared to last year. For example, a single family residential property with an assessed value of $373,500 in 2018, up approximately 16.6 per cent in market value over 2017, will pay $32.59 more in 2018 in assessment based municipal property taxes. Ladysmith’s total municipal taxation for 2018 is $7.961 million. This helps to pay for the ongoing costs of maintaining parks, roads and other infrastructure, keeping our community safe, running the Frank Jameson Community Centre, supporting many local community organizations, and more. These include: • • • • • • Maintaining 900,000 square metres of park land and 26 kilometres of trails Caring for 38 kilometres of Ladysmith sidewalks Filling 50-100 potholes per year Placing approximately 250-300 tons of salt and 200 tons of sand on roads each winter Cleaning and repairing 68 kilometres of sanitary sewer lines Supplying 1,407,000,000 litres of clean, safe drinking water to residents in Ladysmith, the Diamond, Saltair and Stz’uminus First Nation • Maintaining 67.5 kilometres of roads and 17 municipally owned buildings The chart below shows how one dollar of your property taxes is spent. In addition to the amounts we’ve listed, property owners also pay water and sewer parcel taxes to help fund upgrades to our water supply system, the award winning waste water treatment plant, the new bio-solids composting facility, and our soon-to-be constructed water filtration plant.
