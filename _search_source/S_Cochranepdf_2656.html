Ff“ M -m«— _<:M‘ ~‘ -ct“ - ,4 To Whom It May Concern: I hope you will consider taking the time to read this letter on behalf of Big Brothers Big Sisters of Central Vancouver Island in regards to how their |n—School mentoring program has directly benefited the students of Fairview Community School. For the past few years Fairview Community School has partnered with Big Brothers Big Sisters with their In- School mentoring program to help the students who attend Fairview to feel a sense of belonging, and ability to strive in all areas (socially, academically, physically, emotionally) while being at school. The students that we match with the teens from the paired high school have been chosen to take part for varies reasons, some of which include: friendship challenges, living in isolation, poverty, challenging home lives, behavioral challenges, and self-esteem issues. The students that attend Fairview that struggle with these challenges have a difficult time with their academic connection to their peers and school community. Having the inlearning due to the lack of social/emotional School mentoring program at Fairview has made a huge difference in our student's lives. The teens that come to spend time with our students have been healthy, fun, and safe role models for our children to trust and look forward to spending time with once a week. These children have developed a stronger sense of self-worth, and love for themselves as well as being able to show it to others. It has also made a difference when it comes to their academic learning. Teachers are reporting that they have noticed their students focusing better, and are more engaged in their learning. It has made a world of a difference for these students to know that someone is comingjust to spend time with them once a week over a set period of time. Throughout my time working alongside Stephen Cochrane to coordinate the |n—School mentoring program I have seen major growth in the students as well as the youth that mentor. Having a relationship with a positive and healthy role model that these children can relate and bond to makes them excited about learning and being at school. Any support financially to help keep this program specifically in schools would be well worth it and greatly appreciated! These children need this support to help guide them to be a positive person to someone else that may need it one day. Big Brothers Big Sisters of Central Vancouver island has made a difference in the Harewood Community and hope it continues to do so for years to come. Thank you for yourtime and consideration. Sincerely, Samantha Brockman Child, Youth and Family Support Worker School District 68
