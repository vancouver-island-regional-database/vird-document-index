Thomas Nanaimo District Secondary School October 8, 2025 8:18:24 AM From: Dave Thomas <> Sent: October 5, 2025 10:10 AM To: District Administration Centre Manager Subject: Nanaimo District Secondary School CAUTION: External Message Good Morning, My name is David Thomas, and I am the parent of 2 children currently enrolled within schools of SD68. My daughter is currently enrolled at Nanaimo District Secondary School with my son slated to attend there beginning the 2026/27 school year. I myself attended NDSS as part of the 2002 graduating class and during my time at NDSS and I was present for the rebuild of the library tower that had been previously shuttered for asbestos, while currently my daughter speaks of the condition of the school (often referring to rats amongst other items of disrepair) I've been reminded of prior engagements regarding the school since watching the latest story on CBC. The reason I am writing is that I am reminded of approximately 8-10 years ago parents were requested to engage in SD68 strategic planning for feedback, and one specific item for input was the age/replacement of NDSS. At that time Woodlands Secondary was slated for closure/or had closed and the district was considering selling the property. I engaged and made the suggestion for SD68 to rebuild Woodlands Secondary school for operation, with the intent to allow students to be diverted from NDSS which would later allow for that school to also be re-constructed. A member representative of the school district would not even entertain the suggestion, as she stated that Woodlands was a done deal and not in consideration for long term strategic plan. I remember being very upset at the response at the time as I found her to be very ignorant. I wanted to reflect on this and bring it to the attention of the SD68 board, because since this property was closed for instruction, it has now been re-opened to host alternate programs/occupants, while zero progress has been made on NDSS long term strategy. As my children have grown up in this community, I have watched first hand multiple SD68 properties closed to instruction, only to be utilized for alternative uses where I can only assume the district collects rental income. Example being my children attended a daycare at the closed Mount Benson Elementary school, which then moved to the closed Dufferin Elementary school property. This leaves me to question how much tax payer funded budget the district is allocating to maintaining properties not being utilized for public instruction purposes, but rather maintaining rental properties which is not the intent of this public infrastructure. I still stand by my suggestion that Woodlands should have been reconstructed, and still should be. While it does not have the size/capacity to replace NDSS, utilization in a long term strategy could support the the district overall for years to come as this property is central to the Nanaimo area making it a sustainable property for multiple uses in the future. Students could be diverted (Gabriola students, French Immersion programming, or as a middle school etc etc) and allow for construction at other facilities to begin with reduced student population. Thank you for your time and consideration, David Thomas
