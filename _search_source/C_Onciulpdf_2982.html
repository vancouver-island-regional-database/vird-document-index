Kelsey Toxe Subject: FW:brief email to the Trustees Hi Kelsey, On the eve of the Board's decision about whether or not to approve staff's recommendation to move ahead with the introduction of French Immersion at LadysmithSecondary School, I just want to send the brief note of encouragement below. Please circulate to the Trustees in advance of tomorrow's meeting: Dear School Board of Education Trustees: Ijust want to send this quick note to thank each of you for your consideration of staff's recommendation to introduce French Immersion at Ladysmith Secondary School in September of 2016. As you heard in the presentation Carrie Segreto and I delivered at the Education Committee meeting earlier this month, the benefits to students, the district,and the community are many, and as the Superintendent's proposal confirms, the costs to implement this enriched educational opportunity are relatively small. I just wanted to share with you a little story as you make your decision. Yesterday I was at the local grocery store in Ladysmith and I ran into a mom whose children used to go to Ecole Davis Road (and whose home is just blocksfrom that school.) I told her about our efforts to encourage SD 68 to introduce French Immersion to LSS(which she had heard about) and inquired about her children. She shared with me that her two kids, in grade 6 and 5 I believe, are now in their second year at Chemainus Elementary and are loving it. So much so, that her son recently stated his intention to continue on to Chemainus High School when he enters grade 8 in 2017. She told me that there are 45 students from Ladysmith at Chemainus Elementary this year. I haven't checked with St. Joseph's recently, but when last I did, I was told that 75% of their student body lived in Ladysmith at that time. So, as you deliberate whether or not to make a relatively modest investment in an enhanced educational opportunity for Ladysmith students, please keep the big picture in mind. Ladysmith families are leaving School District 68 in search of better educational opportunities for their children and with no investment in enriched programming in Ladysmith schools for many years, it's easy to understand why. Ifthe $36,000 required for full implementation of the pilot keeps even 5 students from leaving for School District 79 or neighbouring private schools (or even better, bringssome back), our District will be ahead. — Please take this opportunity to improve the educational opportunities of Ladysmith students. You vision in doing so today will reap rewards for our District for many years to come. Sincerely, Cheryl Onciul Cheryl.onciul@shaw,ca 250-245-2908
