KelseyTole Subject: FW: SD68 Board of Trustees From: George Hanson [mailto:george@clayton-consultingcom] Sent: December 7, 2015 4:25 PM To: ‘BillRobinson‘ Subject: SD68 Board of Trustees HiBill, Please pass this along to your colleagues on the School Board. I think my patience pot has finally boiled over! Driving my car the other day delivering my son to Sylvan learning where he is tutored twice a week at considerable expense to fill the educational gaps resulting from the inferior education he has received in the public system, i happened to hear the SD68 Board Chair interviewed on the radio. The utter audacity and hypocrisy of what he said and of what the board is doing is outrageous! He essentially said that because of financial constraints and the desire for adequate educational programming, schools such as Rutherford needed to close and that (get this!) ‘we are confident that these children with thrive in other schools!’ How is it OKfor a school board, now controlled by disgruntled parents who were adamantly against the planned closure of their neighbourhood schools, to make such blatantly hypocritical statements? How is it OK for this school board to ignore a professionally assembled facility plan that was focused on improving educational programming throughout the system so as to re—jigthe entire plan to suit their personal interests? How is it OK for this school board to put numerous school cultures and hundreds of families through prolonged argument and chaos so that the personal preferences of trustees can be satisfied? How is it OK for this school board to waste hundreds of thousands of dollars in facilities conversion, reconversion, and consulting fees—not to mention several lost years of planning and now wrangling--to satisfy their neighbourhood preferences——especia||y when it is patently obvious that SD68 has serious financial challenges and must close some schools? And presuming that the board’s focus should be primarily on the quality of education rather than the location of that education, how is it OK for the board to put the entire district through all of this when achieving their objective results in reopening the Cedar Secondary School for a mere 200 students! Are you kidding me! A full third of the former Cedar students don’t even want to return! I also heard the Board Chair on the radio several weeks ago voicing his assurances that ‘we are confident that over time these student numbers will increase.’ Really? Increase to what? 300? 400? And over how many years? Wow! And then you'll only be about 600 students short of being able to offer a full secondary school curricu|uml!!! Not to mention that peeling these 200 students away from Barsby, a school that might otherwise actually have sufficient numbers to warrant a full curriculum, means that that school also will be under—served.Brilliant! A friend of mine has a daughter in 11”‘grade somewhere in SD68. She told me that because there are not enough students to make up a second math class, her daughter now has to study math on her own through the ‘school at home’ program. We're not talking about an elective course. We're not talking about a new student who has only recently entered the system or the school. We're talking about a student who has been in this district since kindergarten who cannot access core curriculum for 11”‘grade! It is absolutely unacceptable! Would we all like to have perfect schools with exceptional programs and great facilities within easy walkingdistance of our homes? Of course. But this isn't nirvana. Ifthere is a choice between quality of education and convenient location, it should be a slam dunk for quality. When our two children entered elementary school in Nanaimo, we chose the French Immersion program at Pauline Haarer even though we lived one block from another school. It was less convenient but it was important to us that our children be in the best learning environment available. When it became clear to us that our daughter was no longer being adequately served by SD68, we enrolled her at QMS in Duncan where she thrived for five years at considerable expense and significant inconvenience even though we live only blocks away from an SD68 secondary school where our son is now a student. The fact that the board of trustees is willing to put the community through this chaos; willing to risk disrupting the French Immersion program which is perhaps the only success story in the district; willing to waste significant time and money to achieve a result that actually further compromises the district's ability to provide a full curriculum for its secondary students is, in a word, ridiculous! I had no issue with Cedar parents advocating for their schools and children when the previous facilities plan was underway just as I have no issue with Rutherford parents or NDSSparents resisting the present (ill—advised) direction. I have serious issue with these same Cedar parents now running the school district using their personal bias to railroad an unprofessional and unjustified change of direction. As advocates outside of school district governance?—-have at ‘er! As Trustees charged with stewarding the available resources for educational outcomes in our community?——shameon these Trusteesl Their personal interests are in absolute conflict with the best interests of the school district. The very word ‘trustee’ implies a person who has been entrusted with something. The Board of Trustees has been entrusted with the public welfare in education in our community. This trust is being abused in this absurd process. I suggest that the School Board cease its pursuit of this indefensible direction regarding facilities; return to the previous sound and professional plan; and get on with the vital business of improving educational outcomes in School District 68. Academic achievement and high school graduation rates in this district are deplorable. While Trustees manipulate facilities plans to suit their personal agendas, the failure rates in our district continue to mount with virtually no meaningful discussion about programming pathways for improvement. Wake upl George Hanson
