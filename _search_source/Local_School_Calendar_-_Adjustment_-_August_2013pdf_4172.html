BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: August 28, 2013 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Learning Services RE: Proposed Change to the Local School Calendar 2013-14 Rationale: During the past school year (2012-2013) senior management of Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) provided all staff with two workshops: 1. “Success for All” that took place at the Nanaimo Convention Centre and, 2. “Celebrating Success for All” that took place at the Port Theatre. Through community sponsorship the Port Theatre offers their venue free of charge to the school district. Both sessions focused, predominantly, on the improvement of learning for all students. To meet the NLPS Board of Education’s goals of “Meeting Each Student’s Unique Needs,” and focusing on the “Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment” the Learning Services Department would like to continue offering these professional development opportunities for staff. Background: Upon review of the two “Success for All” days it was agreed that this type of staff development/inservice should continue. In May of 2013, the Board approved a recommendation from the Learning Services Department to continue the workshop series for 2013-14. The (2013-2014) “Success for All” dates have been scheduled for Tuesday, September 3 and Friday, January 31. Unfortunately, the Port Theatre is not available on either of those dates. Proposal: That the “Success for All” date originally scheduled for Friday, January 31, 2014 be rescheduled to Tuesday, May 6, 2014. Discussion: The NDTA Professional Development Day occurs on Monday, May 5, 2014. We have tentatively reserved the Port Theatre venue for Tuesday, May 6, 2014. 1 Ministry: This Amendment to the Local School Calendar is permitted according to the School Calendar Regulation Section 6 (3) copied below. Amending a school calendar 6 (1) For the purposes of section 87.02 [school calendar – amendment] of the Act, a board must make publicly available proposed amendments to a school calendar at least one month before making the amendments. (2) A board must, as soon as practicable and, in any event, within 30 days of amending a school calendar under section 87.02 of the Act, provide the minister with the amended school calendar. (3) Subsections (1) and (2) do not apply to a board that reschedules a non-instructional day previously scheduled in a school calendar if the board gives notice of the change to (a) the parents of the students enrolled in the school to which the proposed change is to apply, and (b) the representatives of employees of the board assigned to the school. Recommendation: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) change the “Success for All” date from Friday, January 31, 2014 to Tuesday, May 6, 2014 and that the parents of students and the representatives of employees of the Board be advised as required by the School Calendar Regulation. 2
