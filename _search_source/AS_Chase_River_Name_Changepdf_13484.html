NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 4, 2026 Strategic Direc�ons Commitee Jacquie Poulin, Assistant Superintendent Chase River Elementary School Recommendation The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) engage Policy 307 – Naming of School Facilities and assign an ad hoc Committee to rename Chase River Elementary School. Background As the school community prepares for the opening of the school addition, conversations about the name of the school have naturally emerged. This reflection is timely, as the expansion has encouraged many to revisit the history, identity, and meaning connected to the school’s name. Discussion When Chase River Elementary opened in 1951 to replace the former Southfield School, it was named after the nearby river, reflecting the local landscape and community at that time. Further research indicates that the name “Chase River” is connected to events that took place in 1853. After a Hudson’s Bay Company employee was reported shot, colonial authorities pursued a young Snuneymuxw man named Siamsit. His village was burned, and he was later located close to a nearby river, brought into custody, and executed. The river associated with this pursuit became known as Chase River. This event represents one of many historical interactions that have contributed to generational trauma still experienced by families and community members today. As a district, we are committed to working in partnership with Snuneymuxw to support healing, strengthen relationships, and ensure that schools are places of belonging, respect, and safety for all children. Given this history, we have concerns about whether the current school name allows all students— particularly those from Snuneymuxw—to feel welcomed, honoured, and represented in their learning environment. As our district continues its work toward Truth and Reconciliation, we believe it is both appropriate and necessary to consider whether the current school name reflects the values we hold today. Action Sheet: Chase River Elementary School Page 2 The Board’s naming policy specifically contemplates the renaming of facilities. The purpose statement of Policy 307 – Naming of Schools or Parts of Facilities is as follows: As per the Syeyutsus Reconciliation Framework, the Board recognizes that naming a school is a respected process critical to creating a sense of community, a sense of belonging, and creating an opportunity to honour the land in which it is located. A school name also fosters a unique identity for families, students, and staff. In addition to naming facilities, the policy clearly outlines circumstances in which renaming may be considered: 1. The Board will consider the renaming of a facility or part of a facility in limited circumstances that may include: a. When a local municipality or regional district renames a geographic reference associated with the school; or b. When the name of the facility or part of the facility does not align with Board policies; or c. When the Board receives a request from the community that aligns with the Board’s naming policy. 2. When the Board considers renaming a school or facility, they will receive a report regarding any potential cost implications for consideration in their decision-making process.
