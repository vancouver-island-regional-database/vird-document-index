CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY 4 Page 7 of 9 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 5, 2019 Education Committee Jessica Stanley, Education Committee Chair Climate Crisis Recommendation: That the Education Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) will: Integrate environmental considerations and sustainable practices into administrative, capital planning, and programming decisions; Review programs and practices to limit the nature and scale of negative environmental impact and to increase sustainable practices; Create measureable targets and objectives to reduce our use of resources, greenhouse gas emissions, and carbon footprint consistent with CleanBC targets of reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 40% by the year 2030 and further by 90% by the year 2050 at minimum; Allocate resources for the effective implementation and maintenance of environmental stewardship practices; Communicate our attempts to increase our sustainability, reduce our greenhouse gas emissions and carbon footprint, and to become environmental stewards in our communities; and Seek financial support and other resources from the provincial government for these endeavors to the extent that they align with the CleanBC plan. Background: With awareness of scientific consensus and referencing the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change’s Synthesis Report (2014), The Board of Education of School District #68 (Nanaimo Ladysmith) note the following scientific findings: Page 8 of 9 1. Human influence on our climate system and climate change is undeniable. 2. Climate changes have broad and significant impact on human and natural systems. 3. These changes include warming of the climate system including our oceans, rising sea levels, ocean acidification, and more extreme weather events. These changes are expected to worsen. 4. Greenhouse gas emissions have increased and are viewed as a dominant cause of global warming. 5. Climate change is expected to undermine food security, exacerbate health problems, impact water availability, slow the economy, trigger economic loss, increase migration, and create conditions that increase the likelihood of violence. 6. The severity of abrupt or irreversible changes increases as the magnitude of the warming increases. 7. Substantial emission reductions are possible and can reduce climate risk, reduce the cost of future mitigation, and increase the likelihood of successful adaptation. 8. If we do not act now, we face “severe, widespread and irreversible impacts globally”. Without remediation, climate change will interfere with our capacity to achieve our mandate of educating the youth of NLPS and will undermine the wellbeing of our communities. The Board of Education of School District #68 (Nanaimo Ladysmith) is committed to sustainability and environmental stewardship. Further, as our Syeyutus Policy states, “Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools respects and cherishes the land our schools and offices are situated upon. The land and its resources touch every aspect of our life, and is the cornerstone of our connectivity.” As such, The Board of Education of School District #68 (Nanaimo Ladysmith) believe it important to be an active part of the solution to our current climate crisis.
