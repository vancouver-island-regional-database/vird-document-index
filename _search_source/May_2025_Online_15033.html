EMPLOYMENT 02 STZ’UMINUS ARTIST UNVEILS HUMMINGBIRD CARVING AT NANAIMO AIRPORT **NANAIMO, April 9** — A small crowd gathered at Nanaimo Airport on Tuesday to witness the unveiling of a striking new art installation: a five-foot-wide hummingbird panel carved by local Stz’uminus artist Jason Harris. The piece, commissioned by the Nanaimo Airport Commission, now hangs prominently near the security checkpoint, offering a warm symbol of welcome and good fortune to travelers. The intricate carving, which took over five months to complete, features a traditional Coast Salish design that Harris says is meant to bring good luck. “My late mother used to say that a hummingbird is God’s messenger— it brings good news and good luck,” he shared during the event. “The hummingbird meant a lot to my mom, and while I wish she were here today to see the unveiling, I know she’s with me in spirit.” 03 Harris, who resides in the Stz’uminus First Nation, has been carving for many years. He was first inspired by his father, late Joe Harris Sr., and further honed his skills under the guidance of renowned carvers such as the late Simon Charlie and fellow Stz’uminus artist Luke Marston. Marston attended the unveiling, where Harris honored him with a blanket and a small hummingbird carving. “This is my first large commission,” Harris noted, “and it’s opened so many doors for me. I’ve learned a lot from this experience.” Indeed, Harris’s artistic journey is gaining momentum. At the ceremony, he also revealed that one of his designs has been selected for integration into the structural elements of the Pattullo Bridge Replacement Project in Vancouver. The bridge crosses the Fraser River, where the Cowichan Nation Alliance—including the Stz’uminus, Cowichan Tribes, Halalt, Lyackson, and Penelakut—once had a village site known as Tl’uqtinus. Jason Harris continues to grow as an artist, embracing both traditional carving and modern design techniques, including digital art. In this role, Candace will report to the Social Development Manager. As a Brighter Futures Program Worker, she will play a vital role in supporting mental health through community-based programs, organize engaging activities for boys’ and girls’ groups (including Parent Break and Mom & Tots groups), and facilitate culturally sensitive parenting workshops and support groups. She will also assist in contributing to child development initiatives of the nation by ensuring nurturing environments, support healthy pregnancies through prenatal education, and prevent injuries with safety workshops. About Candace Hill Candace brings over three decades of professional experience that began in 1990 when she embarked on her healing journey, cultivating a wealth of skills as a wholistic nutritionist, family counselor, health activist, writer, and spiritual healer. Her education, including a Certificate in Family Community Counseling from Native Education College, a Certificate in Wholistic Nutrition, and certifications in programs like Bringing Tradition Home, Reclaiming Connections, and Circle of Security, equips her to address intergenerational trauma, addiction, grief, and the importance of healthy living. With a robust background in community-based health promotion—such as providing natural herbs and traditional foods to Indigenous families and facilitating culturally sensitive parenting and mental health programs, Candace is a seasoned advocate for wellness and growth. On a personal level, Candace is an honored mother and Kokum (grandmother), whose life path was shaped by overcoming significant health challenges at 19. This experience ignited her passion for healing herself and others, blending traditional knowledge from her elders and ancestors with modern tools. She finds joy in teaching, drumming, singing, hiking, picking medicines, and attending ceremony—practices that reflect her deep connection to nature and spirit. Candace’s background is rooted in a spiritual commitment to making lives brighter, a mission she has pursued since her early years and now shares as part of her “Give Away” in this season of life. She is humbled and excited to serve the Stz’uminus First Nation, eager to share her knowledge, learn from the community, and plant positive seeds for families, children, and youth. Please join us in welcoming Candace and wishing her success in her new role. She can be reached at Candace.Hill@stzuminus.com. Welcome to the Team! About Anna Lekomsteva Anna brings a deep passion for mental health, recovery, and personal growth, rooted in her empathetic approach and intuitive understanding of her clients’ needs. She is committed to creating a safe, supportive space where clients can reconnect with themselves, rebuild confidence, and develop lasting resilience through inner strength, self-worth, and purpose. With over a decade of experience in the medical field since the age of 17, Anna has worked extensively with individuals facing physical, mental, and addiction-related challenges, emphasizing the power of empathy in fostering healing and growth. In her most recent role, she worked for the Vancouver Island Health Authority as a mental health professional, where she honed her skills in supporting diverse populations. Anna immigrated to Canada in 2002 and has since made island her home, where she lives with her 13-year-old son and beloved tabby cat. Her personal journey prior to immigration has shaped her resilience and gratitude for the opportunity to live and work on this land. Throughout her journey, she has felt a deep connection with Indigenous culture, especially through the wisdom and experiences shared by the elder’s she have met. Spiritually, she is drawn to nature, meaningful human connections, and the concept of healing through music. Anna is honored to work on the traditional Coast Salish Territory, acknowledging the Stz’uminus peoples, the Snuneymuxw, and Halalt peoples, and is committed to making a meaningful difference in the lives of those she serves. Please join us in welcoming Anna and wishing her success in her new role. She can be reached at Anna.Lekomsteva@stzuminus.com. Welcome to the Team!
