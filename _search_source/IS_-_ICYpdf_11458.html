NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 10, 2024 Education Committee Kirstin Funke Robinson, Director of Instruction Integrated Child and Youth Teams Overview Board of Education Strategic Plan – Board Goal Safe, caring, and healthy learning and working environment that is inclusive of the diversity of our entire learning community. Objective: • Increasing awareness and capacity in addressing mental health and wellness. Background In June 2019, the provincial government announced A Pathway to Hope, which laid out a 10-year vision for mental health and substance use care that provides people with the services they need in order to tackle problems early and support their wellbeing. It identifies the priority actions government will take to help people immediately and reduce demand for services down the road. The Ministry of Mental Health and Addictions (MMHA) worked in partnership with the Ministries of Children and Family Development (MCFD), Health and Education and Child Care, as well as with First Nations, Métis, and Inuit partner organizations to develop a suite of initiatives that target existing gaps in child, youth, and young adult mental health and substance use service delivery. One of the initiatives detailed in A Pathway to Hope is Integrated Child and Youth (ICY) Teams. The province is currently working to establish ICY teams in 20 school districts. These multidisciplinary teams will deliver wraparound mental health and substance use services and supports for children, youth, and their families/caregivers. In 2021, 5 districts began to implement ICY teams, with 7 additional districts added in 2023. Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools is part of this second wave of implementation. Aside from Richmond, which was in the first wave, our community has received the largest allocation of ICY teams thus far, with 4 teams allocated. ICY teams serve children and youth 0-19 years of age in the Nanaimo Ladysmith area (this includes non-NLPS children and youth) who have identified mild, moderate, or severe mental health and/or substance use needs as defined by impairment or disruption in key areas of human development, and for whom existing community or school-based supports are not adequate. For youth who are already receiving youth mental health and/or substance use services from an ICY team, services may be available to age 21, based on unique needs, developmental considerations, transition plan, and best fit. 1|Page ICY in NLPS Targeted funding for ICY was provided for the 2023-24 school year. An initial gathering of local community organizations who support children and youth, including our 5 Indigenous partner groups, took place in the spring of 2023 to consider ICY in Nanaimo-Ladysmith. On an ongoing basis, involved ministries meet at local employers’ table (LET) meetings, as well as provincial meetings that include all districts in the second wave of implementation. Kirstin Funke Robinson, Director of Instruction-Inclusive Education and Lynn Brown, Assistant Director-Complex Mental Health, represent NLPS at the LET and provincial ICY tables. 2|Page NLPS has hired 4 Registered Clinical Counsellors (RCC) who will serve as part of the ICY teams. Clinical counsellors are distinct from school counsellors in that they provide direct clinical counselling support and therapy through an outreach/outbound service delivery model to clients. The NLPS RCCs are excluded staff who work 12 months per year at variable hours and supervise a Peer Support Worker (CUPE). Early in January 2024, we will be hiring 4 Peer Support Workers. These staff will have lived experience as a young person with mental health and/or substance use challenges. They will be responsible for non-clinical work to connect with children, youth, and their families/caregivers. Although the service delivery model is flexible and outbound, a central office space for ICY team members, including those from multiple ministries, is being created at the Dufferin Crescent site. At the present time, the referral pathway via multiple ministries is being created between LET members. In NLPS, initial student referrals are being collected centrally, through the Learning Services Support Team, as well as the Assistant Director of Complex Mental Health. ICY teams are expected to begin intake for referrals in mid-late January, 2024. For further details on ICY, visit: gov.bc.ca/icyteams 3|Page
