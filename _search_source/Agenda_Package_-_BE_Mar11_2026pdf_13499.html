Hul’q’umi’num traditionally, and to this day, unites the Snaw Naw As, Snuneymuxw and Stz’uminus peoples as one family and on whose territories we stand today. Director of Communications, Privacy & Community Engagement 2026-2027 Community Budget Feedback Background The Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education is required by the School Act to pass a balanced budget for the upcoming school year by June 30. As part of the annual budget process, the district solicits feedback from our community on spending priorities. Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools’ annual budget priorities are guided by the district’s Strategic Plan. The Strategic Plan lays out three goals for the school district to focus on from 2024 to 2028. These goals were established following a comprehensive engagement process over several months that gathered feedback from students, staff, caregivers, parents and the community. For each goal, there are two specific actions for district staff to focus on. Student Success • • Implement Literacy and Numeracy Frameworks to support students with math, reading and writing across all grades. Strengthen and align assessment and data practices- focusing on collecting and using data to understand how our students are doing in relation to previous years and provincial averages. Student and Employee Wellness • • Create and implement Student Wellness Framework Create and implement an Employee Wellness Framework Truth and Reconciliation • • Deepen Nation and Indigenous relationships with district/schools Build a culturally responsive mindset with the goal of cultural safety throughout the district through a system wide professional learning approach. The Board of Education’s role is to ensure that the necessary resources and funding are in place to achieve the goals of the Strategic Plan in a manner that is sustainable over time.
