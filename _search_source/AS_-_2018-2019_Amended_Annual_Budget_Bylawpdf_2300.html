Background: At the February 19, 2019, Business Committee meeting, the Business Committee passed the following motions: The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) give all three readings of the 2018/2019 Amended Annual Budget Bylaw during the February 27, 2019, Open Board meeting; and That the Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopt the 2018/2019 Amended Annual Budget Bylaw during the February 27, 2019, Open Board meeting. Information: In June 2018, the Board approved the 2018/2019 Annual Budget and passed the associated Bylaw. Since that time, the Board has received a first quarter report on the Annual Budget with necessary adjustments. The purpose of this update is to provide the Board of Education with the Amended Annual Budget Bylaw, current budget figures in comparison to the Annual Budget as well as provides year-to-date revenues and expenses to January 30, 2019, with explanation of actuals to budget variances. The information is intended to summarize the Budget Bylaw document as well as highlight the changes that have been made to accommodate increased enrolment, the allocation of deferred revenue, the addition of restricted surplus as approved by the Board of Education, and other revisions necessary to support School and District Operations. At January 30, 2019, the district was 58% through the fiscal year 2018/2019 with total Operating expenses at approximately 49%. The Amended Annual Budget presentation is being provided in the same format as the Ministry Budget document, which is to be read from the back, starting at Schedule 4 through to Statement 2. Schedule 4 – Capital Revenue and Expense This schedule discloses the budgeted revenues and expenses for the Capital Fund. Revenue amounts include capital funding that the district will recognize, or account for, to support capital operations over the course of the fiscal year, as well as interest earned on Local Capital balances. Budgeted expenses include the interest we pay on capital leases and the amount we must expense for the amortization of all our tangible capital assets which includes items such as buildings, equipment and technology. The amount of expense generally exceeds the amount of revenue that will be recognized for the fiscal year which puts the fund in an acceptable deficit. The Schedule also includes the amount of funds transferred from the Operating Fund to Local Capital for the purchase of tangible capital assets such as technology and capital lease payments. It may also include a portion set aside to support capital projects not funded by the Ministry of Education, but necessary to ensure our facilities are maintained and upgraded as necessary. The Fund balance, as presented on Schedule 4, budgets a surplus of $1,521,475 due to the difference between the revenue we anticipate will be recognized, the expense of capital asset amortization and the increased transfer to Local Capital for future projects. Schedule 1 and Statement 4 Schedule 1 - Changes in Accumulated Surplus (deficit) by Fund is only required for the Amended Annual Budget as it contains prior year surplus balances that are not known at the time the Annual Budget is submitted to Ministry. This schedule is used to present opening surplus/deficit balances in all funds, any transfers between funds, and the resulting budgeted surplus/deficit balances; actuals will be presented in the Audited Financial Statements. The Amended Annual Budget shows the Operating Fund’s opening surplus balance of $7.2 million less the amount of surplus appropriated to balance the Operating Budget and transfer $4.3 million to Local Capital leaving a balance of $2.6 million surplus. The Capital Fund’s opening balance of $5.7 million is reduced by the difference between the amortization of capital revenue and the amortization of capital assets on Schedule 4, then increased by the transfer from the Operating Fund, leaving a closing surplus balance of $7.2 Million Statement 4 - This statement reflects the Districts budgeted Change in Net Financial Assets (debt) by showing how expenditures acquired for the period were met by revenues recognized. This statement pertains to the Audited Financial Statements which will be presented in September 2019. Statement 2 – Amended Annual Budget – Revenue and Expense and Budget Bylaw Statement 2 consolidates the revenue and expenses from the Operating, Special Purpose and Capital Funds as well as provides the Ministry Operating Grant funded FTE. The Amended Annual Budget has a net revenue (expense) of ($3,033,408), as presented in Schedule 1, which includes the difference in Operating revenue and expenses of ($219,883) and capital revenue and expenses of ($2,813,525). The budgeted allocation of restricted surplus is then added, leaving the budgeted surplus (deficit), for the year, in the amount of $1,521,475 which resides solely in the Capital Fund, available for future capital needs. Budget Bylaw Amount The Budget Bylaw amount is presented at the end of Statement 2 and includes all expenses reported by the District.
