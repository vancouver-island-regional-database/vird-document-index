V9R 3K6 Attention: Jamie Brennan, Natasha Bob, Tanya Brzovic, Scott Kimler,Stephanie Higginson, Steve Rae, BillRobinson,Noah Routley, Jeff Solomon Dea r Trustees, With September upon us, students have returned to school and most are settling into their new school routines. Unfortunately, my daughter and her classmates returned to school prepared for learning, but have no teacher to teach them. So in this classroom, the students have had three substitute teachers in the ?rst week of classes. Therefore, no routines are being established and no learning is taking place for this group of students. These students will have a substitute teacher for several weeks, as the position has recently been posted, andso must still close, resumes reviewed and interviews be held. The pool of teachers applying for positions this time of year will be disappointingly small, as those teachers with experience and the required quali?cations are already teaching students. Unfortunately, this is the second time my daughter's class has had a substitute at the beginning of the school year, while the students waited for a teacher to be hired. My other daughter had a substitute to start the kindergarten a few years ago. In the eight years that I have had a child enrolled in a School District 68 school, first Ecole Davis Road and then at North Oyster, there has always been at least one class in the school with a substitute teacher to start the school year, while the class waited for a teacher to be hired. in all cases, there was no signi?cant change in enrolment which would result in the requirement to increase the number of teachers. It was simply that a teacher from the school had posted to another position within the district and no replacement was yet hired. Unfortunately, this scenario is undoubtedly repeated at all the schools in School District 68. I am sure you can provide me with reasons why not all classrooms have teachers at the beginning of the school year, but it simply comes down to a systemic failure in the hiring process. This practice is unacceptable and Is contributing to the loss of enrolment, and funding, in School District 68. It is time that School District 68 Identi?es the root causes which contribute to too few teachers for the students enrolled, and develops solutions which will prevent this from recurring. Certainly other school districts have faced this issue and have taken measures to ensure all students have a teacher at the beginning of the school year. It is time that School District 68 follows their lead and addresses this objectionable practice. Sincerely, Jaw Lorena Mueller
