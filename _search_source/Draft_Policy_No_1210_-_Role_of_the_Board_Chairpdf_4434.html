SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1210 Role of the Board Chair The Chairperson of the Board of Education is primarily responsible for safeguarding the integrity of the Board’s processes. The Chairperson represents the Board of Education in the broader community. The Chairperson is elected at the inaugural meeting following a general election. In December of each consecutive year, or as required, the Board of Education will elect one member to act as Chairperson. Specific Areas of Responsibility 1. Meetings The Chairperson of the Board of Education shall: 2. 1.1 Preside over all Board of Education meetings. 1.2 Ensure that Board of Education meetings are conducted in accordance with the School Act. 1.3 Ensure that Roberts Rules of Order are followed. 1.4 Ensure that Board of Education Policy and procedures are followed. 1.5 Act as an ex-officio member of all committees appointed by the Board of Education. The Agenda The Chairperson of the Board shall: 2.1 Prior to each Board of Education meeting confer with the Vice-Chair and the Superintendent regarding the items on the Agenda. 2.2 Ensure that each Trustee has a full and fair opportunity to be heard. 2.3 Ensure that all Trustees present vote on all Agenda items. -1 - DAF Draft Policy No. 1210 is out for Notice of Motion until June 1, 2011 – If you have any comments regarding this Policy please email ckelt@sd68.bc.ca or call Cathy Kelt at 250-741-5238. SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1210 3. Chairperson/Superintendent The Chairperson of the Board of Education shall: 4. 3.1 Keep the Superintendent and the Board of Education informed on all matters that might affect the School District. 3.2 Convey directly to the Superintendent any concerns which may affect the administration of the School District. 3.3 Be in regular contact with the Superintendent to maintain a working knowledge of current issues and events. Corporate Responsibilities The Chairperson of the Board of Education shall: 4.1 Bring all matters requiring a corporate decision to the Board of Education. 4.2 Act as a Signing Officer for the School District. 4.3 Serve as an officer of the Board of Education authorized to witness the use of the Board’s Corporate Seal. 4.4 Act as chief spokesperson for the Board of Education by stating positions consistent with Board direction and policies. -2 -
