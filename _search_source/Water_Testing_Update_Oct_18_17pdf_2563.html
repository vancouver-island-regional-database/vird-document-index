Department of Facilities Update - Testing for Lead Content in Drinking Water October 18, 2017 Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background On February 24, 2016 the Ministry of Education directed School Districts in British Columbia to begin a program of testing for lead content in all facilities built prior to 1990 Testing and remediation of 1/3 of the schools (facilities built prior to 1990) completed April 2017 Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background Following recommendations from both Environmental and Mechanical Engineering consultants, approximately $85,000 (reallocated SEP funding) was spent remediating the tested schools. Physical remediation included: • 28 auto flush valves • 6 Fountains c/w auto flush valves • 3 Faucets • Signage was confirmed at all pre-1990 facilities. • Flushing instructions Communications included: • Communication with Principals • Communication with PAC’s • Communication with Parents • Bulletin on water testing Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background Learning from Year 1: • • • • • Phase testing results vs waiting for a final report Timeliness of stakeholder communication Staff time budgeted to attend PAC meetings Engineering solutions took more time that anticipated Contract execution took more time than anticipated Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background Testing above requirement: • 8 hour min vs leaving over the weekend. • Extends the testing period Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background On May 13, 2017 correspondence was received from DPAC SD68: • That DPAC advocate for the School District to install at least one water filtration system (Water Fill Station) for all schools that do not currently have one • Request that all schools, including those built after 1990, are included for lead content water testing Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background Staff responded to the DPAC request as follows (in part): • Staff currently working on Year 2 of both Ministry of Education and Health with respect to Lead Content in Drinking water of School Facilities • A number of fountains have been installed prior to the Ministry policy and more were installed during Year 1 remediation • Staff continue to consider how it may be possible to integrate the DPAC requests with the mandatory testing and remediation Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background On September 28, 2017 correspondence was received from J.Ross with the following specific questions: • Has the District applied for SEP (School Enhancement Program) Funding? • When are you going to apply for funding? Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Next Steps – Funding Staff reviewed the letter from J. Ross and the Lead Content in Drinking Water of School Facilities Program Subsequently a request was submitted to Ministry for consideration - includes $360,000 for water testing and remediation (Year 2 and 3), including one bottle fill in each school which does not have one (recommendation from DPAC) Ministry indicated request will be considered upon receipt of a detailed remediation plan.
