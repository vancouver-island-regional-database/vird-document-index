Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: January 14, 2015 TO: Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO (Acting) RE: Cedar Site Background: The Nanaimo Ladysmith School Board requested a stoppage of work at the construction site of Cedar Elementary School by implementing the following motion: That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education to enact a 30-day hold on the construction and awarding of contracts at Cedar Elementary, but permit CUPE work that must be completed whether the Board decides the school is best suited as a K-7 facility or a 8-12 facility. During that 30-day period the Board asked staff to answer a number of questions. These questions and answers are included in the agenda of the January 14, 2015 Business Committee Meeting. Considerations to altering the current plan: The Board has the authority within the School Act to direct staff to return Cedar to a secondary school for September 2015, as the School Act does not require a consultation process to open a school. Staff does not believe such action is plausible due to the following points. Policy Policy No. 4005 (Building Program and Site Acquisition Planning)    By opening a school, the District is acquiring a school, so the Board is required to follow this policy. Policy requires consultation, which has not been done since the decision to open as an elementary school. The procedure attached to this policy, sets up a committee to facilitate the design of the school. Policy No. 3020 (School Boundaries/Catchment Areas)  This policy also requires consultation on changes to school boundaries. 1 Logistical items  Notwithstanding the time delays created with the required consultations under District Policy, the practicalities of opening the school as a secondary school are extremely limited, due to the following: o o o o time needed to review current plans to determine what changes may be required; new plans would need to be created (Architect); potential requirements for new building permits; and procurement timelines Operational funding implications  The current plan creates financial efficiencies for the District. The potential impact of reversing the previous Board’s decision has not been fully discussed. Long-term implications  Because the district has a structural deficit further efficiencies would need to be found. In addition, the district would also need to consider capital cost pressures. In light of the above, it is prudent for the Board to consult with the broader community if it considers altering a decision which has been made after extensive consultation. Therefore, staff presents the following two scenarios for the Board’s consideration. Scenario #1: Status Quo: The Board takes no further action (no further consultation required) This scenario would reactivate all construction at the site and enable staff to open the new Cedar Elementary School for September, 2015. This scenario also includes the closures of Woodbank Primary and North Cedar Intermediate schools on June 30, 2015. Scenario #2: The Board moves to a district consultation process on the future use of the Cedar site. This scenario would continue to halt all construction at the site, enabling staff to begin to plan a consultation process with the community, stakeholders, and Board.   This scenario would result in the delay in opening; and Closure bylaws for Woodbank and/or North Cedar Intermediate may be rescinded. 2
