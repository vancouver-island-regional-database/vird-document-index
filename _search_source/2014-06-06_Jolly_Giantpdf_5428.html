Jolly Giant Childcare Ltd. Administration Office #201 – 1861 Dufferin Crescent Nanaimo, BC, V9S 5N2 Ph: 250.591.8899 Fx: 250.591.8877 June 11, 2014 PRESENTATION TO THE BOARD OF EDUCATION SD68 Introduction At the beginning of May our BC Government announced that Capital Funding is available for creating new daycare spaces. The government recognizes the need for childcare in our province and they have provided extra incentive for the creation of childcare spaces within school districts. We request the board’s consideration regarding School District 68 working collaboratively with Jolly Giant Childcare in creating new daycare spaces on School District property. We plan to utilize the Capital Funding Program and this project will be at no cost to the district. About Us A Brief History: John Wilson acquired his early childhood educator’s license to practice and opened his first daycare centre in 1999 with a vision of offering high quality childcare to children and families. His wife, Cheryl, recommended that he call his dream Jolly Giant Childcare which is very fitting if you have ever met him. He regrets not being able to be here tonight as he is away on a one week family camp with his wife and children. You might ask why choose to work with us? Jolly Giant Childcare’s relationship with School District 68 officially began in 2005 when we opened a centre at VAST. A month later we opened another centre at Princess Royal and in 2007 we opened another centre at Selby, the old student services building. We have seen more than 40 VAST students use our daycare services over the years! This is all to say that we have a longstanding relationship with the school district that we are proud of. We are a well-established company with thoroughly documented policies and procedures and 15 years of experience. In addition to our four locations in Nanaimo we have one in Victoria. We are a private for-profit company, and to clarify our structure is very similar to a non-profit in that we have a director, and we have managers and educators at each centre. What we do not have is a board of director’s, and access to all government funding as the government restricts some of the funding to not-for-profit organizations. However the current Capital Funding has been made available to us. Where we excel: We continue to operate at capacity offering high quality educational childcare to both typical and vulnerable families from our community. We are proud of our track record in serving children and families, assisting parents and equipping their children with the necessary skills for their future success through the school system and life in general. Year after year our children graduate and venture off into kindergarten and we are proud to say that they are better equipped for success in school than they would have been had they not graduated from our program. We provide much more than childcare to school district students, we also serve as a resource for parenting and lend an ear when someone just needs to talk. www.jollygiant.ca Proposal Our proposal is to work collaboratively with School District 68 in creating new childcare spaces on School District property to offer high quality educational childcare to infants, toddlers, and preschoolers and their families in our community. With direction from you, the board, we can work collaboratively with School District 68 staff in developing a completely detailed proposal with the project being funded by the Capital Funding Program. Cost: The intention is to apply for the Capital Funding Program announced by the Ministry for Children and Family Development. With Capital Funding the government will be injecting up to $250,000 towards the project. This funding could provide up to 49 childcare spaces per project. There will be no cost to the district. As we currently do with our other daycares, Jolly Giant Childcare will be paying rent which will provide a source of revenue to the school district. Where: We have identified a desperate need for new childcare spaces in North Nanaimo that is primarily due to the lack of suitable daycare locations in that area. We have printed two maps from the government website that shows all of the licensed group childcare centres in Nanaimo. You can see on the maps that north of Northfield Road only has one licensed group childcare centre. We have had many requests from our clients over the years to open locations in the North but have not been able to locate any potential space that would work for a daycare site. That is why a working relationship with School District 68 is so vital because school sites have the space to meet the daycare licensing regulations. Priority of Enrollment: We have always offered School District 68 students a priority of enrollment meaning they bypass all waiting lists which helps them get childcare established quickly so they can return to school themselves. Moving forward we would like to extend the priority of enrollment to all school district employees as well. Benefits of Jolly Giant Childcare at a Secondary School Site: • • • Vulnerable students will be able to access childcare and return to school Vulnerable students will be able to access parenting resources Vulnerable children will develop necessary social, emotional, and cognitive skills while in our daycare setting them up for success as they enter kindergarten Benefits of Jolly Giant Childcare at an Elementary School Site: • • Extension of services right down to infant and toddlers meaning that parents can start bringing their children to the site when their children are babies and they can continue at that site right through grade 7. It is recognized in the field education that continuity of care is important. Vulnerable children will develop necessary social, emotional, and cognitive skills while in our daycare setting them up for success as they enter kindergarten Next Steps We request the board’s consideration regarding School District 68 working collaboratively with Jolly Giant Childcare in creating new daycare spaces on School District property.
