NEW BUSINESS Update on the Wellington Seismic Project Mr. Dalrymple, Energy and Capital Projects Manager, told the Committee that the Wellington Seismic project is proceeding in stages and said this will allow the school to continue to operate while the project is underway. Mr. Dalrymple said that work is currently underway on Blocks G and F, and that the work on those two blocks is expected to be completed by June 2015. As soon as those two blocks are completed, Block A will be demolished and a new entrance to the school will be built. Mr. Dalrmplye said some of the major work that was carried out this summer included the use of a giant crane to remove the roof and supports in Block F. Update on the Cedar Field Project Mr. Brent Spohn, Facilities Planning Supervisor, outlined the site and playfield work that was done during the summer as part of the conversion of Cedar Secondary into an elementary school. Mr. Spohn said that the work is on time and on budget. The playfield has now been seeded, and the project is expected to be complete by the end of October. Update on Summer Facilities' Work Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations, said that picketing during the summer affected a Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held September 10, 2014 number of planned maintenance projects. Mr. Sabo said the Facilities Department is working with the Learning Services Department to develop a priority list for the order in which projects will be completed once the strike is resolved. One of the impacts is that the district is spending its Annual Facilities Grant for about 10 FTE, without any work on the AFG projects being accomplished. June 2014 letter from Stephanie Higginson referred to the Business Committee Meeting The Business Committee reviewed Ms. Higginson's June correspondence to the Board, in which she asked the Board to consider alternatives to the project to convert the closed Cedar Secondary building into an elementary school. Trustees asked staff a number of questions regarding the current plan. Staff outlined why the plan approved last year remains the most suitable plan for Cedar schools.
