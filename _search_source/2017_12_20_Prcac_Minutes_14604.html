Minutes of the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee Wednesday, December 20, 2017 at 6pm COMMITTEE MEMBERS PRESENT: Tim Richards, Chair Roger Kimmerly Gord Horth Tim Godau Bruce Mason REGRETS: Tara Pollock STAFF PRESENT: Clayton Postings Julie Tierney Councillor Duck Paterson Jim Allair AGENDA Moved and seconded: 2017-17: That Parks, Recreation and Culture Advisory Committee approve the agenda for the meeting with the following additions: • Saltair Recreation Update; • Canada 150 Canoe; • Arts & Culture Update. Motion carried. MINUTES 2017-18: That Parks, Recreation and Culture Advisory Committee approve the minutes of the September 20, 2017 meeting. REFERRED BY COUNCIL Mid Isle Soccer and Rotary Club of Ladysmith Forrest Field Enhancement Proposal Committee members discussed proposal at length considering needs of field users, future needs and uses of the property, and facility planning for the future. Move and seconded: 2017-19: THAT the Parks, Recreation and Culture Advisory Committee support the proposal in principle and recommend that Council consider approving the proposed: grandstand improvements, player bench improvements and facility improvements; AND THAT Council consider engaging with past members of the Field Steering Committee and current field user groups executives to complete a comprehensive review of the existing draft site plan and take into account possible changes to trends, demographics and community needs to best determine the location and design concept for the proposed clubhouse/concession and present to Council for consideration. UNFINISHED BUSINESS NEW BUSINESS Nil. The consultants will use this information when drafting their proposed plan for the area, which will be provided at the next meeting. UPDATES Saltair Recreation Update – T. Godau advised the Committee that the Saltair Community Centre project has been a success with 85% of available rooms rented or leased to organizations; the roof and furnace have been repaired. Arts & Culture Update – Waterfront Gallery is hosting a Fine Arts Show in February; they receive applications from all over Vancouver Island and the Gulf Islands. They anticipate over 1800 visitors to the show and that overall they have had over 2000 more visitors to the Art Gallery this year. The Ladysmith Theater is presenting “Kitchen Witches” this month and is doing very well. Waterfront Update – Meeting to take place in late January.
