DPAC SD68 February 21, 2017 Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) RE: International Baccalaureate Application and Program Dear Trustees: The District Parent Advisory Council would like to take this opportunity to share our current perspective on the possible International Baccalaureate program implementation for School District 68 Nanaimo-Ladysmith. The IB program has many potential benefits for students who attend an IB school. The program includes ideas such as teacher/student collaboration, learning critical thinking skills, developing a sense of social responsibility in relation to local community and the world, and includes specific professional development to support teaching staff. Many of these benefits can also be found in the BC redesigned curriculum. Core competencies that include critical thinking, personal and social, and communication skills are all designed to be woven through the curricular content for each grade level. It appears that many of the positive things about IB are things that we are discovering in the updated curriculum. The District PAC is in the process of identifying the budget priorities we plan to bring forward for the 2017/2018 budget. The cost of the International Baccalaureate program will not be included in those priorities. We feel that the current learning strategies, including the NLPS Assessment, and curriculum/reporting implementation, need the full-time support of district staff and financial support of the Board without any pressure to implement a new program concurrently. Allowing more time for these to be fully implemented will benefit our students, and lead to higher literacy and graduation rates. Of concern is the actual cost of accreditation, and associated fees that would be required to be paid for by SD 68. The decision to move this program forward needs to be looked with a long-range view. Based on the proposal presented February 1, 2017 the cost for implementation over the course of the next 7 years is $489,000; all of which would be spent specifically in one school location. This causes us to pause and ask the questions: Is this an equitable decision for all of our students? Is there an area of learning where we would prefer to see this amount of money spent to benefit a larger number of students? A secondary concern, but nonetheless important, would be the possibility of parents either needing to pay a fee or participate in fundraising in order to access the program. We firmly believe in the public education system and feel that charging fees for a district program will create inequity in a district that already has a significant level of childhood poverty. Should the program be reviewed again in future years, our opinion may in fact change. However, at this juncture we do not believe the staff or monetary resources would be best spent on the International Baccalaureate Primary Years Programme. Respectfully submitted, Charlene McKay DPAC SD 68 - President
