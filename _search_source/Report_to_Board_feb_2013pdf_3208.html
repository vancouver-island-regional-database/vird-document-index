Report to the Board of Education, Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools From trustee D. Neary Social Planning Advisory Committee The work of the Social Planning Advisory Committee furthers the goals of the Social Equity Pillar of the City of Nanaimo’s Strategic Plan. - - The priority focus of SPAC for this year will be ‘food security infrastructure’. SPAC recommended, and City Council recently allocated, $60,000 from Social Development Grant fund, $3,600 from the 2012 Social Development Grant fund, and $1,410 from the 2012 Community Service Grant fund to Loaves & Fishes Community Food Bank for the expansion of its Food 4U Recovery Program. Work is presently underway on the organization of the ‘Excellence in Social Development Awards’, which recognize exceptional commitment and leadership by individuals and organizations in working to improve the quality of life for Nanaimo residents. Nomination forms available online at: www.nanaimo.ca or from City Hall and must be submitted by March 29. Council will hold an awards ceremony in May 2013. Footnotes: ‘Excellence in Social Development Awards’, examples of 2012 award recipients: - Administrator (individual) of the Semi-independent Living Progam The First Unitarian Fellowship of Nanaimo The Nanaimo Men’s Resource Centre Columbian Centre Society Nanaimo Community Hospice Society The Food 4U Program is a partnership between Loaves & Fishes Community Food Bank, local grocery stores, food wholesalers, restaurants and trucking companies. - - Perishable food items are picked up from participating grocery stores, seven days a week. Food is sorted to ensure only food that meets Food Banks Canada guidelines is distributed for consumption. Recovered food is distributed to Loaves & Fishes clients and other social service agencies, for example the Salvation Army, 7-10 Club, Nanaimo Foodshare, John Barsby Secondary School Breakfast Program, Boys and Girls Club, Nanaimo’s Women’s Resource Centre Any food that is not suitable for distribution is composted. Since September 2012, more than 40,000 kilograms / $215,000 worth of food has been diverted from the waste stream into the hands of needy people in the community. The operation is of mutual benefit to grocery store participants, who realize a reduction in the cost to recycle packaging and dispose of the products.
