February 13, 2014 School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Aveneue, Nanaimo, B.C. V9R 3K6 Attention: Phil Turin Secretary-Treasurer Dear Sir: Re: Serauxman Stadium I am writing on behalf of the Nanaimo Minor Baseball Association (the “NMBA”). I am writing to request that we be added to the Agenda for the February 26, 2014 Board meeting. I will be the NMBA spokesperson. I can be reached via phone at home: 250 754 3375 or via email at: scott.leaf.nmba@gmail.com We understand that the school district is studying the future utilization of the NDSS site including the stadium. As a partner and stakeholder the NMBA would be pleased to assist the School district with this process. We would be pleased to meet with you, or to attend a Board or Committee meeting in this regard. The NMBA has worked in partnership with the school district on the construction and maintenance of the Serauxman Stadium for nearly 40 years. Serauxman Stadium was built through a joint effort by the School District, the NMBA, the Serauxman service club and many members of the community. The Stadium was officially opened in 1976 with the great Yankee Mickey Mantle in attendance. In 1980 the School District and the NMBA entered into a 25-year lease. The NMBA has been maintaining and operating the Stadium since that time. The NMBA has been organizing youth baseball in Nanaimo since 1956. There are normally over 500 registered players, most of whom are students in the district. We have worked with the School District, the City and other stakeholders in the community for many years in order develop and maintain baseball fields and infrastructure. We have raised hundreds of thousands of dollars and our volunteers have committed untold hours in order to deliver baseball to the youth of this community. The Stadium is presently used to its maximum capacity. It is the home of the Nanaimo Pirates of the B.C. Premier Baseball League (ages under 18); the Nanaimo Junior Pirates of the B.C. Junior Premier League (ages 14-17); the NMBA Midget division (ages 18-15); the Nanaimo Coalminers of the B.C. Men's League and the Vancouver Island Baseball Institute Mariners of the Canadian College Baseball Conference. In addition, the stadium will once again host the BC Summer Games and the Toronto BlueJays Super Camp this summer. The Stadium could see additional use and attract National Championships to Nanaimo if a series of upgrades and enhancements were undertaken. In closing, we look forward to meeting with you to discuss the future of Serauxman Stadium. Regards, Scott Leaf NMBA President Nanaimo Minor Baseball Association, Box 327, Nanaimo B.C, V9R 5L3
