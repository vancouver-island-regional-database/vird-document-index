QUESTION PERIOD There were two questions this evening: received as TL.Saunders: Do you use a computer program to schedule bus routes? The $25,000 cost savings for amalgamating route 5 and 6, does that mean the bus sits idle or is it used elsewhere? P. Sabo Responded: The District investigated the purchase of a program and after going through the detailed investigation, we determined the overhead cost of the program and the overhead to populate the data annually was going to be greater than any anticipated savings we would receive from the efficiencies. With respect to the bus, it was utilized elsewhere. C.McKay: Conservative estimates by parents suggest that PACs provide approximately $600,000 in funding annually to schools to support field trips, extracurricular, supplies for classrooms and libraries, to mention a few. This would be in addition to the amounts received for the McGirr playground last year. Could you provide and/or highlight this anecdotal information in future reports? Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held September 26, 2018 C. McVeigh Responded: We have had a wholesome conversation and it is a good opportunity to tweak the report going forward. Director of Finance Jedha Holmes and I did have a conversation with you around PAC monies and how they’re reflected in terms of money that comes into the school district as well. I think those are conversations that we look forward to continue having and somehow highlight. Trustee Kimler Responded: It would be nice to highlight the money that PACs bring in each year. I know it’s a big part of the cost of education and that’s sort of downloaded on parents for their fundraising. The fundraising comes from the pockets of the parents whose kids are in school. I want to add that there’s also school generated funds of grants and things like that that are not a part of our operation budget and it would be good to include those numbers as well and we’d get a much better picture of what the district is costing us every year.
