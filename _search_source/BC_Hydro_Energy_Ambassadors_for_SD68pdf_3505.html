BC Hydro Energy Ambassadors The BC Hydro Energy Ambassadors program is a partnership between school teams, school districts and BC Hydro. Students in grades nine to 12 are empowered to lead real change by engaging with teacher sponsors and district facilities mentors to investigate energy use and deliver outreach presentations encouraging energy conservation in the school and throughout the district. In Nanaimo Ladysmith SD68, the Energy Ambassadors teams consisted of students from Dover Bay Secondary, Ladysmith Secondary and Woodlands Secondary. They participated in two workshops facilitated by BC Hydro, completed energy audits, conducted behaviour studies, made presentations to both secondary and elementary schools, and finally delivered a presentation to the School Board at the end of May. Through their participation in the program they have developed teamwork, communication and leadership skills. The school teams had an opportunity to apply for the new BC Hydro Energy Ambassadors Conservation Grant. In order to receive the grant, the students completed the program activities and put forth a compelling energy conservation project proposal. With support from their sponsor teachers and the school district Energy Manager, Brian Kingsley, the students wrote strong proposals and each of the three teams has been awarded a $1000 grant from BC Hydro.  Dover Bay Secondary: Workout into Watts: Electricity generating bicycle  Ladysmith Secondary: Install landfill waste/compost/recycling bins  Woodlands Secondary: Install vending misers, door seals, light sensors
