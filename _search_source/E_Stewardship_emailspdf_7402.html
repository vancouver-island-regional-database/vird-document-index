From: To: Subject: Date: Jennifer Archer Consultation Environmental Stewardship Policy - Comments July 10, 2020 7:55:03 PM CAUTION: External Message To SD68 Board Members, I fully support this new Environmental Stewardship Policy and applaud your leadership to integrate our responsibility to our environment into all aspects of education and administration. I fully agree with everything in the policy itself and a few comments came to mind. One is that I strongly encourage the board to implement land-based educational programming at all grade levels through outdoor education including educational gardens. School gardens offer incredible opportunity for children to learn valuable life skills as well as learn science, math, art, cooking, ethics and culture. Outdoor education can also build self esteem and lead to healthier lifestyles. On a more practical level - kids will be the primary beneficiary of healthy food that they have grown. This is not a "nice to have" program, it is about teaching fundamental skills and habits to last a lifetime. As an aside - in the era of COVID - I would be especially grateful to know that the kids are spending more time outside of the classroom and away from screens. Let's take the classroom outside. Secondly - I noted that "targets" are being set but also encourage reference to regular annual evaluation, tracking progress against these targets and efforts communicate learning. How do we know if this policy is successful? What outcomes are we trying to achieve? How do we know if they are working? Resources for evaluation are an important part of the Board's learning and accountability measures. Evaluations also provide the precedents that other school boards and organizations need to see to validate their own efforts. Your leadership and willingness to innovate is an important provincial initiative. Please profile and highlight your successes and your key learnings to be an example for other districts. District to district learning will be very important to scale up this initiative across BC. Finally, I encourage the Board to identify existing non-profit resources, tools and potential partnerships to expand educational opportunities to include environmental education. For example, see https://cwra.org/en/affiliates-programs/project-wet/ or https://tucanada.org/water-edu-kits/ or https://waterlution.org/ or https://waterlution.org/gwc/). There are many opportunities to partner with non-profits that are engaging youth in environmental stewardship and opportunities to support youth leadership training. Thank you for your willingness to innovate and your efforts to promote environmental stewardship and education. Jennifer L. Environmental Stewardship The Nanaimo-Ladysmith Board of Education of School District #68 (the “Board”) honours and respects the land as an important source of knowledge and a determining factor of our overall health. We acknowledge and have experienced the interconnection and holism of all things in our ecosystem. The success of our schools and offices are ever so dependent on this sacred relationship. Accordingly, we will use restoration as a means to reconnecting with these great lands, and to reaffirm the sacredness of each site. The Board realizes that climate change is a result of human activity and that our actions impact the land and our ecosystem. The Board also understands that, without remediation, climate change will interfere with our capacity to achieve our mandate of educating the NLPS youth and will undermine the wellbeing of our communities. As such, the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Board of Education of School District #68 commits to, guided by the notions of reciprocity and responsibility; to be an active part of the solution to the current climate crisis and to ongoing environmental stewardship: • • • • • • • Integrating environmental considerations and sustainable practices into administrative, capital planning, and programming decisions; Reviewing programs and practices to limit the nature and scale of negative environmental impact and to increase sustainable practices and environmental stewardship; Creating an environmental stewardship action plan that includes measurable targets and objectives to reduce our carbon footprint, use of resources, waste, and greenhouse gas emissions in a manner consistent with current climate science; Supporting educational and professional development opportunities to foster ecological literacy, environmental stewardship, and connection to the land among our students and staff; Advocating for and allocating resources for the effective implementation and maintenance of environmental stewardship practices and the environmental stewardship action plan; Encouraging intergovernmental and community partnerships to achieve and enhance our environmental stewardship goals; and Communicating our attempts to increase our sustainability, reduce our greenhouse gas emissions and carbon footprint, and to become environmental stewards in our communities.
