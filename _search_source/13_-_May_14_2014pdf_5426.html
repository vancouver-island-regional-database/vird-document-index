CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Tyler Heisterman, Nanaimo Track & Field Club Lighting Proposal Mr. Tyler Heisterman, of the Nanaimo Track and Field Club outlined the group’s proposal to install lighting at the Rotary Bowl. Mr. Heisterman said that the Rotary Bowl, which is owned by the school district, is used on a regular basis by the Track and Field Club and a number of other community organizations. New lighting would allow athletes to train at night, especially during the winter months. He said that the Track and Field Club has raised the required $27,000 for the lighting, including the cost to have it installed by school district workers. UNFINISHED BUSINESS Wellington Seismic Update Mr. Dalrymple, Energy Manager, gave the Business Committee an update on the design for F block as part of the Wellington Secondary seismic project. The F block is the central circular section of the school which is being completely stripped as part of the seismic work and then will be rebuilt with a new look featuring contemporary learning spaces such as a learning commons, learning labs and student collaboration spaces. The seismic project at Wellington is being staged over several years so that the school can continue operating while work is underway. By the time all of the work is complete in January 2016, much of the school will be newly renovated. NEW BUSINESS Cedar Playgrounds/Playfields Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations, gave the Business Committee an update on the planning in process for the outdoor areas at the new Cedar Elementary School. Woodbank Primary and North Cedar Intermediate students will move into the newly renovated Cedar Elementary School in September 2015. Mr. Sabo said that parents had expressed concern about the state of the playfield at the new elementary school. At the March Board Meeting had asked Facilities Department staff to review the possibility of providing an upgraded playfield, and to report back to the Business Committee in June. Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held May 14, 2014 Mr. Sabo said that since then, staff have reviewed the budget for the renovation of the school and the AFG program, and have identified some opportunities for additional funds that may be sufficient for the playfield. He said that in order to determine the cost of the playfield and to allow for the possibility of installing the playfield this summer, the district will advertise for tenders this month and will likely be in a position to provide the Board with some options at the June 25 meeting. Forecast 2013-14 as of April 30, 2014 Mr. Turin provided the Business Committee with a financial update for the current school year, as well as a look ahead at the financial picture for the district for the next several years. He said that current projections are for the district to end the school year with about a $2.1 million unrestricted surplus. Mr. Turin is forecasting that the district will be facing an approximate $3.5 million shortfall in 2015-16. He said that current projections forecast the elimination of the Ministry’s funding protection grant after the 2015-16 year.
