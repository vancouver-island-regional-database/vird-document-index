Program Overview Nature Kindergarten/Primary Nature Kindergarten/Primary provides young children with the opportunity to spend larger amounts of time in an outdoor setting. Children have the chance to play, explore and experience natural ecosystems and the materials within. Children spend part of their day outside engaged with their body, mind and spirit in a natural setting, and come back to school where there would be opportunities for indoor learning. The program will meet the needs of the prescribed learning outcomes for Kindergarten as well as the goals of the Primary Program and Early Learning Framework. Children will explore their local, natural environment and learn to know and understand their home place, developing confidence in being outside while meeting the objectives of the curriculum. Working as a group of learners, they can develop confidence in their own abilities to investigate and to collaborate. All of this play-based outdoor learning will support indoor learning activities that often call for a quieter and calmer presence. 3 Eco-School Academy at Departure Bay - Proposal In order to ensure an optimal and safe environment for the children we will have a teacher and an education assistant to teach and support the students. Cost to Student: A yearly fee of $500 to cover the cost of an Education Assistant to accompany classes on the outdoor portion of the day. Grades 4-7 Program Students in our grades 4-7 program will also spend more time outdoors as compared to traditional classrooms. Our intermediate students will be engaged in citizen science projects where they are working alongside community members conducting meaningful work while learning. We will continue to focus on extending the learning in our four theme areas. Emphasis will be on experiential learning opportunities including inquiry-based and project-based learning. Students will also be involved in a variety communitybased work and community supported/developed projects.
