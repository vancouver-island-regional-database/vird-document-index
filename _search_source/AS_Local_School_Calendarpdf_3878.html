BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: September 24, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Learning Services RE: Adjustment to Local School Calendar: Local Calendar Recommendations Rationale: Due to the recent strike action, the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) local school calendar for 2014-15 requires some adjustments. Two specific issues have arisen: 1. The Monday, September 29 School-Based Professional Development Day is too close to this week’s school start-up date; 2. There is a resulting imbalance between Semester 1 and 2 for secondary schools. Background The Board approved the 2014-15 Local School Calendar in the spring, and took it to consultation for approval. As outlined in School Calendar Regulation 314/12, the Board may approve amendments to the local school calendar. Proposal 1) Due to the strike, the Ministry of Education has given school districts some flexibility this year by moving the first semester exams into the first week of February (from the last week of January). This allows the school district to balance the number of instructional days in each semester. 2) By moving the September PD day from Monday, September 29 to Monday, February 24 it will create more instructional time when students first return to school in September, and create further balancing of the number of instructional days in both semesters. Ministry This change to the Local School Calendar requires a 30-day notice period as stipulated within School Calendar Regulation 314/12. Both the NDTA and CUPE executive have been made aware of this recommendation and are supportive. Recommendation: That the Board of Education moves that the Local School Calendar be adjusted by moving the end of Semester 1 to Friday, February 6; and the Monday, September 29 PD day be moved to Monday, February 24. A 30-day notice period will be provided, as required.
