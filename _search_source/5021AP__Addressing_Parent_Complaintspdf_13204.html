Appendix A Section 500 – Learners and Their Families 502.1AP – Addressing Parent Complaints Purpose The Board of Education believes that education needs to be transparent and responsive to the needs of our students. Concerns regarding the operation of the school district may arise, and it’s important to provide an avenue for parents to raise concerns. Objective This Administrative Procedure is intended to guide parents and employees on raising and addressing complaints in a transparent, responsive, safe and respectful manner. Procedure The expectations for a parent raising a complaint are as follows: 1. Take the matter directly to the staff member. Discuss the concern regarding the action, policy, procedure or practice. If a parent is uncomfortable, they may request a meeting directly with the school principal to discuss their complaint. 2. If not resolved, the parent should request a meeting with the principal of the school. The principal shall determine if it is appropriate to involve the staff member in this meeting. 3. If still not resolved, the parent may request a meeting with the appropriate director of instruction. The director of instruction shall determine if it is appropriate to involve the principal in this meeting. Complaints raised directly to the Superintendent, Assistant Superintendents or Board of Education shall be redirected to follow this Administrative Procedure. Complaints that may result in employee discipline are subject to collective agreement requirements and are not subject to this Administrative Procedure.
