Regards, XX—Therapeutic Swim Teacher—Ph: 250-741- xxxx email: Common Questions and Answers Regarding the Therapeutic Swim Program 2013-2014 What is the purpose of the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Therapeutic Swim Program? The PRIMARY purpose of the swim program is to improve and/or maintain the physical, mental, and emotional functioning of our students with physical disabilities and mobility issues. Can a student without a physical disability take part in the Swim Program? Yes—We are able to accommodate all of our students with physical disabilities and still have spots available for other students; screening for placement is done through Learning Services in the fall. How can I help my student get the most from the swim? (Follow the Therapeutic Process): 1. Have a clear assessment of the student’s abilities 2. Establish goals—both short term and long term based on the assessment 3. As a team design a written plan to achieve goals and put that plan into action 4. Evaluate progress and revise your plan as necessary What are some of the goals other students are working on?  Improving body awareness  Increasing movement repertoire  Improving flexibility, symmetry  Improving balance, equilibrium and recovery skills  Increasing movement repertoire  Improving strength especially in trunk & respiratory muscles  Improving balance, equilibrium and  Improving cardiovascular endurance recovery skills  Improving speech, language, and communication skills  Improving body awareness  Improving weight bearing, weight shift, mobility, and gait  Improving flexibility, symmetry skills Where does free play fit into the swim program? The first 20 minutes are designated for physical activity not free play. Physical activity may be joining in on the aqua-size lesson, pool walking or running, swimming, an active game, or exercises on the pool deck. You know your student’s needs and your student’s IEP try to shape the activity around this information. The last 40 minutes of the swim can be used to continue to work toward your student’s identified goals with a structured activity or through water play. Do I need to be in the water with my student? Yes--EAs are required to be an arm’s length from the student or students they are working with at all times At some times during the swim the pool deck will make more sense as it may allow for better supervision, instruction and/or interaction with games, toys and balls) Can I go in the hot tub? At the end of the session, (about 10 minutes before getting changed), intermediate students may go into the hot tub accompanied by the EA. Therapeutic Swim Teacher Department of Learning Services
