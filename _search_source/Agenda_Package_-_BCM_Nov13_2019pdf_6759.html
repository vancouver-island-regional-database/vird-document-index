Data Collection: 1701 – Student Enrolment Background - The Ministry of Education requires that all School Districts in British Columbia provide a three year projected enrolment estimate, due each year in mid-February, in order to facilitate the budgeting and allocation of provincial funding. The District’s approach to enrolment projections is to consider all available and relevant information, to be as conservative as possible in order to avoid preliminary overfunding but to ensure that the calculated estimates provide sufficient supports to students and District operations. Once all relevant information has been compiled and analyzed a final enrolment projection is submitted to the Ministry of Education, who then provides our “estimated” Operating Grant allocation, by March 15th, for the coming school year. The District’s preliminary Annual Budget is based upon this allocation which is then adjusted in December after the September 30 th student data collection has been verified. The information collected on Form 1701 is used to calculate the Districts number of Full Time Equivalent (FTE) students. Total FTE received by Ministry is then added to the Funding Allocation System (FAS), along with the determined allocation per student category. This recalculation is then communicated to Districts in mid-December and confirms the actual Operating Grant. At that time, funding levels may be increased or decreased depending on the accuracy of our estimated enrolment projections, which is then presented in our Amended Annual Budget each February. As well, Ministry may or may not make changes to the per pupil allocation levels as seen in the table below, that will also be communicated by Ministry in December. Page 28 of 30 Section 1 – Board Governance 2.7 – Policy Development Policy Development The Board believes that to fulfill its democratic mandate in representing the public interest it must provide direction in all stages of policy development and implementation. The Board shall establish and maintain written policies and where appropriate, accompanying Board procedures, to guide and monitor progress in meeting the expectations for student success. These policies provide a framework for Board and District’s accountability to families, the community, and the Ministry of Education. Board policies shall establish a balance between the responsibilities of the Board to develop the broad direction that guide the District and the Superintendent to exercise professional knowledge in the administration of the District. Criteria for Exemplary Policy 1. Policies prioritize enhancing student learning. 2. Policies are consistent with the Board’s Mandate, Vision, Values, and Strategic Plan 3. Policies are developed in response to an identified need and intended to achieve an implementable, common approach. 4. Policies should be based on reliable and valid evidence and be the result of a comprehensive consultative process. 5. Policies are clearly written and are easily understood by those who reference them. 6. Policies are precise enough to give guidance and broad enough to allow appropriate discretionary action. 7. Policies are current and regularly reviewed. 8. Policies are readily available and accessible. 9. Policies, when read together, should demonstrate alignment and consistency. 10. Policies convey the Board’s scope and areas of oversight for the school district. 1.0 Development process The Board, in cooperation with the Superintendent, shall assess the need for a policy, as a result of its own monitoring activities or on the suggestion of others, and identify the critical components of each policy to be developed. The Board Policy Committee may develop the policy Policy 2.7 – Policy Development Page 1 of 2 Page 29 of 30 2.7 – Policy Development itself or delegate the responsibility for development to the Superintendent. The Board may establish procedures in respect to Board policy planning, development, implementation and evaluation. The policy development process shall ensure that trustees act on adequate and sufficient information and may seek the guidance of external expertise. Policies should be consistent with the above stated “Criteria for Exemplary Policy” and relevant legislation or provincial direction. Administration should research each policy proposal to determine its education impact, budgetary effect, and the relationship of the proposal with existing plans, policies, procedures, legislation, and case law. The policy formulation and revision process should be inclusive and consultative. Educational stakeholders significantly affected by policies in the development or review process must be provided an opportunity to share their input during the formulation process either directly or through appropriate representation. Following this process, the policy committee will review the draft policy for recommendation to send the policy to the Board for feedback and approval. All Board policies shall be circulated as notice of motion for 30 days as a method of soliciting community feedback. All feedback submissions will be reviewed by the Board – Committee of the Whole prior to final approval. 2.0 Implementation The Board is responsible implementing policies and procedures governing its own processes. The Board and Superintendent share the responsibility for implementing policies relating to the Board-Superintendent relationship. The Superintendent enacts the Board’s vision by implementing policies related to the daily operations of the district. The Superintendent is also responsible for developing, implementing, approving, and evaluating Administrative Procedures 3.0 Evaluation The Board shall monitor and evaluate each policy in a timely manner to determine whether the policy achieves the intended purpose. The Board must also be informed about and evaluate Administrative Procedures to determine their capacity to carry out the intention and spirit of the relevant policy.
