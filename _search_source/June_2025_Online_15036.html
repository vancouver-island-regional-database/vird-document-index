But overlooking your gutters can lead to expensive and damaging consequences for your home. Clean gutters do more than just look tidy—they play a crucial role in protecting your home’s foundation, roof, and overall structural integrity. Here’s why keeping your gutters clean should be a top priority for every homeowner: --- 1. Prevent Water Damage to Your Home Gutters are designed to direct rainwater away from your house. When they become clogged with leaves, twigs, and debris, water can overflow and seep into your roof, siding, and foundation. Over time, this excess moisture can cause wood rot, mold growth, and structural damage that’s costly to repair. --- 2. Protect Your Roof Clogged gutters trap water and debris against the edge of your roof. This can lead to sagging gutters, damaged shingles, and even roof leaks. In colder climates, backed-up gutters can contribute to ice dams, which prevent proper drainage and can force melting snow into your home. --- 3. Avoid Foundation Issues Water that isn’t properly diverted away from your home can pool around the foundation. Over time, this water can cause the soil to expand and contract, leading to cracks in the foundation or even basement flooding. Clean gutters help keep water flowing away from the house, preserving your home’s stability. --- 4. Keep Pests Away Debris-filled gutters can become a breeding ground for pests 08 like mosquitoes, ants, birds, and even rodents. These pests can find their way into your home or cause damage from the outside. Clean gutters eliminate these nesting spots and help keep your home pest-free. --- 5. Extend the Life of Your Gutters Regular cleaning and maintenance prevent heavy debris from building up and stressing the gutter system. This helps avoid rusting, sagging, and detachment from your house, saving you from the expense of premature replacements or repairs. --- 6. Boost Your Curb Appeal Clogged, overflowing gutters aren’t just a functional issue— they can also be an eyesore. Water stains, sagging lines, and plant growth in gutters can take away from the overall beauty of your home. Clean gutters help maintain a well-kept appearance. --- How Often Should You Clean Your Gutters? For most homes, gutters should be cleaned at least twice a year—typically in the spring and fall. However, if your home is surrounded by trees or you live in an area with frequent storms, more frequent checks and cleanings may be necessary. --- Final Thoughts Clean gutters are essential to a healthy home. A small investment in time or hiring a professional to clean them regularly can prevent thousands of dollars in damage. Don’t wait for a costly issue to remind you—make gutter cleaning part of your regular home maintenance routine. 09 10 STZ’UMINUS SECONDARY SCHOOL CLASS OF 2025 TUESDAY, JUNE 24, 2025, AT STZ’UMINUS COMMUNITY SCHOOL DOORS OPEN AT 10:00 A.M. These ticks are often found in and near areas with trees, shrubs, tall grass or piles of leaves. The best way to protect your pets against Lyme disease is by avoiding tick bites: R Check your pets for ticks after being outdoors. Ticks often attach to the head, neck and ears of dogs and cats. R Carefully remove attached ticks immediately with clean fine-point tweezers and wash the bite area with soap and water, or alcohol-based sanitizer. R Talk with your veterinarian about tick-prevention products for pets and Lyme disease vaccines for dogs. While it’s essential for students to unwind after a busy school year, summer also offers a golden opportunity—the chance to gain real-world work experience. For teenagers and young adults, applying for a summer job can be one of the most rewarding and growthoriented steps they take during their formative years. Here’s why it’s important for youth to start applying for summer jobs: 1. Build Responsibility and Independence One of the biggest benefits of a summer job is learning responsibility. Managing a work schedule, showing up on time, and following through on commitments help teens build a strong work ethic. These experiences foster independence and prepare young people for the responsibilities of adulthood. 2. Gain Real-World Experience No matter the job—whether it’s scooping ice cream, working retail, babysitting, or assisting at a local office—every role teaches valuable skills like communication, time management, problemsolving, and teamwork. These “soft skills” are highly sought after by future employers and are difficult to learn in a classroom alone. 3. Develop Financial Literacy Earning a paycheck for the first time can be both exciting and educational. Teens learn how to budget, save, and make smart financial decisions. Managing money early on helps build habits that can last a lifetime and encourages young workers to value their time and effort. 4. Boost College and Job Applications Having a summer job looks great on college applications and future resumes. It shows initiative, maturity, and a willingness to work hard. Even a simple part-time job demonstrates that a young 20 person is proactive and capable of handling responsibilities. 5. Explore Interests and Career Paths Summer jobs allow teens to explore different industries and discover what they enjoy—or don’t enjoy— doing. For example, someone might find they love working with kids while babysitting, or realize they have a knack for customer service. This early exposure can help guide future career choices or areas of study. 6. Build Confidence and Social Skills Interacting with coworkers, customers, and supervisors boosts communication skills and builds confidence. Many teens come out of their first jobs feeling more capable, self-assured, and prepared for the “real world.” 7. Make Connections and Network Even summer jobs can lead to important networking opportunities. A positive reference from a summer employer can go a long way when applying for future jobs or internships. And sometimes, a summer job can even turn into a long-term opportunity. Start Early – Opportunities Fill Up Fast! Many summer jobs get filled quickly, especially in popular industries like retail, food service, and camp counseling. Starting early gives teens a better chance of landing a good position—and shows potential employers that they’re motivated and serious. Final Thoughts While summer should still include fun and relaxation, a job can provide structure, growth, and invaluable life lessons. For young people, starting to work—even a few hours a week—can set the stage for future success, both personally and professionally. So if you’re a student or a parent of a teen, don’t wait too long. Now is the perfect time to apply for that summer job. The experience could shape more than just your summer—it could shape your future. 21 Sample Interview questions for practice General Questions Tell me a little about yourself.
