DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET February 14, 2023 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer and Taunia Sutton, Associate Secretary Treasurer Three-Year Enrolment Projections (2024-25 to 2026-27) Background The following information sheets provides the districts three-year enrolment projections which are required by the Ministry of Education and Child Care, due each year in mid-February. Year one of the projection is used to calculate our “estimated” Operating Grant allocation for the upcoming school year. The funding generated from this estimate via the Ministry of Education and Child Care’s Funding Allocation System (FAS) is announced in mid-March and provides the largest source of revenue in support of our Preliminary Annual Budget. Year two and three estimates are used to facilitate provincial, and district based budgeting and financial planning. The preliminary enrolment estimates and associated Operating Grant funding amounts are then adjusted in December, after the September 1701 Student Data Collection has been submitted and actual enrolment figures are verified by Ministry. At that time, funding levels may be increased or decreased depending on the accuracy of our enrolment projections, and due to any changes in per student funding rates or categories. Any changes to the preliminary funding amounts are incorporated and presented in the districts Amended Annual Budget each February, along with any necessary expense changes to balance the budget. Subsequent to the Amened Annual Budget, the February and May Data Collections take place and any variances of enrolment and funding from preliminary projections are accounted for. Discussion In order to comply with section 106.3 (2) of the School Act, enrolment must be estimated to include the number of students who may be enrolled in educational programs provided by the board, at three distinct intervals, and for specific student categories, for the coming school year: due each year on or before February 15th. Specifically, the intervals and categories are as follows: • • • September Enrolment Count – includes School-Age, Adults, Alternate and Continuing Education, Online Learning, Special Needs, Indigenous Education and English Language Learning students. February Enrolment Count – includes Continuing Education, Online Learning, Special Needs enrolment growth and Newcomer Refugee Students. May Enrolment Count - includes Continuing Education and Online Learning students. As the district is required to project what enrolment will be well in advance of the associated school year, the district is essentially receiving an advance of funding that supports all preliminary budgeted district expenses. Once those future estimated enrolments are confirmed the preliminary funding is adjusted up or down, so it is important to estimate in a somewhat conservative manner. Information Sheet: 2024-25 to 2026-27 Three Year Enrolment Projections Page 2 Enrolment estimates are calculated using a variety of district metrics which includes historic enrolment trends, current student cohorts’, the application of a secondary headcount to funded student full time equivalent (FTE) conversion factor pursuant to individual student course loads, and other local knowledge. As well, the district utilizes a software solution called Baragar Systems which uses our 1701 Student Data Collection data in conjunction with demographic trends, school migration trends, birth rates, student participation, and a number of other minor factors to produce our districts annual enrolment projection. For 2024-25 we are seeing numbers at Elementary remain reasonably level. When comparing 1701 Student Data Collections on September 29th year over year, Elementary increased by 32 funded students in Sept 2022 and 54 in Sept 2023. We are anticipating a marginal increase in September 2024 between incoming kindergarten cohorts and in-out migration of 18 funded students FTE. (18 headcount) Secondary numbers have been increasing over the last few years as larger elementary cohorts have been moving through to graduation. When comparing 1701 Student Data Collections on September 29th year over year, Secondary increased by 202 funded students in Sept 2022 and 62 in Sept 2023. We are anticipating a substantial increase in September 2024 as a small grade 12 cohort will graduate in June 2023 and large Grade 8 cohort moves in, resulting in an additional 255 funded student FTE at the Secondary level. (217 headcount) For our Alternate, Distributed Learning, CTC, and Continuing Education Programs we are anticipating relatively stable funded student full time equivalent (FTE) enrolment figures for September 2024-25, recording a slight decrease of -12 for September 2024. We did however note that our funded student FTE estimates for the February and May counts related to Distributed Learning were a bit high, still leveling out following COVID and the discontinuation of offering online educational services to out of district (OOD) learners. The total adjustment to the Feb and May funded student FTE category for 2024-25 is -29 FTE. The total estimated change to the districts Operating Fund for 2024-25, as compared to the enrolment recorded on September 2023 which our current 2023-24 Amended Annual Budgeted is resourced on, is 233 funded student FTE (rounded up) With regards to supplemental funding categories which includes Level 1, 2 and 3 Special Needs, English Language Learners, and Indigenous Education, estimates are based on the current student cohorts with Grade 12 students moved out and a projected number of incoming kindergarten students based on historic trends and current knowledge. As noted in the Amended Annual Budget presentation, the district saw a significant increase in the Level 2 Special Needs Category that is not anticipated to be an ongoing trend, incoming future enrolments in this category are expected to revert to more historic levels which the 2024-25 projections account for, in conjunction with the current student cohort. Summary Overall, on a very preliminary basis the district is in an enviable position as we prepare for the 2024-25 budget. Due to the projected domestic enrolment increase, the International Student Program maintaining record levels of enrolment, and assuming that the Ministry of Education and Child Care fully funds general wage increases, the district is projected to be in a balanced budgeted position. Despite the positive enrolment situation staff are not estimating significant additional resources to invest in programing. This is due to increased cost in wages and benefits, the ongoing inflated cost of sick leave replacement staffing and general inflationary costs (materials, hydro etc). Further, staff continues to be aware that the reopening of Rutherford will result in a considerable ongoing operational cost beginning in 2025-26 and additional available operating funding, if any, should not be allocated to ongoing programming in 2024-25 as it would likely create a deficit for the 2025-26 school year. Information Sheet: 2024-25 to 2026-27 Three Year Enrolment Projections Page 3 It should also be recognized that while District staff have been less conservative in our projections over the last few years, it is important that we do not over project. It is far easier to invest additional dollars in students that we did not project (as is evident by our amended annual budget) than it is to remove resources once allocated. Therefore, it is likely that our actual enrolment will exceed projections. Following this presentation are three Appendices. Appendix A provides a summary of the districts three-year enrolment projections, along with the projections and actuals for 2023-24 for information purposes. Appendix B is the official Ministry of Education and Child Care enrolment projection document to be submitted by February 15th. Appendix C presents the estimated financial impact of our projected enrolment figures as compared to the current 2023-24 Amended Annual Budget, subject to the rates set out by the Ministry of Education and Child Care for the 2024-25 Operating Grant, which will be announced in mid-March 2024.
