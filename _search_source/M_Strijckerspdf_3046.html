Kelsex Tole Subject: FW: Bussing for children to Barsby from Cinnabar Importance: High From: Marnie Strijckers [maiito:mstr1i@§@shaw~.<;a] Sent: Friday, October 02, 2015 11:55 AM To: John Blain Cc: Jamie Brennan; Tania Brzovic;Scott Kimler;BillRobinson; Jeff Solomon;Natasha Bob; Stephanie Higginson; Steve Rae; Noah Routley Subject: Bussing for children to Barsby from Cinnabar High Importance: Good morning Mr. Blain and Trustees; I wanted to voice my opinion on the bussing situation that seems to be circulating for the children who want to attend John Barsby next year and who do not want to be shuffled off to Cedarjust because we are in Cinnabar Valley and that is now the ”catchment school” again. My daughter is in Grade 9 this year and loves Barsby and would like to continue on there until she graduates. I have her sister who will want to go to the same school in 2 years so she is in the same school as her older sister. As a parent it is hard enough to split myself between 2 schools right now, I can't imagine when my son starts Cinnabar in 2 years, and have 3 schools to flip back and forth to. The children need consistency and they should not have to worry about moving schools because decisions are changing due to budgeting interests and not the best interests of children. Allis I hear is cutting costs, to what extent are we willing to go? Why are we not looking at implementing a bus fee for students? They did this in District 69 for years. If my child wants to go to an ”out of catchment” area school and is accepted, then i would be prepared to pay a bus fee for this ($120$140/yr or more). If they are bussing to their catchment school then make it a lower amount like $50-$75/yr. Have it as an upfront cost due at the end of each school year and kids get a bus pass. Look at subsidy for families who can't afford it and if they have more than 1 child bussing then there is a discount. It is all about funds and children should not have to deal with or be burdened with what I would call an ‘adult issue”. Children should not have to worry about being displaced, they should be worrying about studying and getting good grades. Just saying ”No bussing will be provided next year” is really not a great option. Driving my daughter to Barsby next year is not feasible for our family, and I don't think it is fair to uproot her to a school that will not have the same opportunities. I know other parents are In the same situation so please, look at how this will affect the children and their families and consider charging for bussing or find the money from somewhere may be a fundraiser. We need to be working together to find a solution. — I have asked in the past if we are that short of funds why aren't we considering a telethon or a lottery of some kind to make up the difference. I am tired ofthe finger pointing and waiting for someone to budge. Mygoodness if the Variety Show of Hearts telethon can raise millions in a day can you imagine what we could raise, these children are our future and we need to give them the best education possible!!! Lookat may be talking to the Lottery Corp and do $2.00 education scratch tickets. If this is even a possibility I am happy to try and get something going. PS — Thanks Marnie Strijckers 250-591-8090 —
