THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: November 26, 2014 TO: The Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Department of Learning Services SUBJECT: Board Motion R14/06/25-43 (Elementary Hockey Academy) BACKGROUND On June 25, 2014 Mr. Jamie Davis, President Nanaimo Minor Hockey Association (NMHA), made a presentation to the Board of Education, School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith). The Nanaimo Minor Hockey Association asked the district to consider offering a hockey academy for Grades 6 and 7 students. They indicated that the current secondary hockey academy at Woodlands has been a big success. Subsequently, at the June 25 Board meeting, the following Motion was passed: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) refers the matter of the Elementary Hockey Academy to staff with a report back to the Board in the fall of 2014. INFORMATION UPDATE In consultation with Mr. Jamie Davis, President, NMHA, it has been determined that, while NMHA is interested in developing a community hockey academy it has no interest in developing an academy to be “contained” within or associated with an existing elementary school. The NMHA plan is to develop a community hockey academy that will attract hockey players district-wide, who are in Grades six and seven. While the academy is directed at elite (rep) players, no player that is interested in the academy will be turned away. NHMA intends to offer these sessions on Mondays and Wednesdays from 2:15 to 3:15 pm, and Thursdays from 2 to 3pm at the Nanaimo Ice Centre (NIC) 2, beginning January 5, 2015. These dates and times were selected by NHMA to take advantage of the available ice time. NMHA has indicated that it has a list of 24 families (representing 10 Nanaimo-Ladysmith public schools) who will be interested in this opportunity. There is no further action required by the Board, at this time.
