Additional Information  A Bus Driver’s typical day consists of:  Working a split shift at a minimum 4 hours per day i.e. 7 to 9 am & 2 to 4 pm.  Between bus routes, drivers gain additional hours/pay by performing charter/field trip, bus cleaning, bus seat repairs etc.  To gain up to 8 hours pay, it is not unusual for a driver to be at work for up to 12 hours.  Bus route lengths are from 4 hours to 7.5 hours per day, on school days only. Bus Driver Information  The Transportation Department supports our schools by providing bus service for approximately 2,000 charter/field trips per year.  The charter/field trips are from short in town trips (swimming, hockey academy, etc.) to longer trips to Victoria, Mt. Washington and Bamfield.  Costs for these trips are billed back to the requesting school. SD68 Policy Requirements  Courtesy Riders  A courtesy rider is a student that utilizes current bus service and resides within 4.0 kilometers and 4.8 kilometers walk limits of their catchment school or attends a school outside of their attendance area.  Courtesy riders must use existing bus service and stops, bus routes are not altered and new bus stops are not implemented for courtesy riders.  Once a courtesy rider begins utilizing the system, it becomes increasingly difficult to revoke riding privileges. Courtesy Riders  We have 29 school busses:  4 wheel chair equipped busses on regular bus routes and 2 spare busses.  16 regular busses (70 to 84 passenger) on bus routes and 7 spare busses. Regular busses transport 56 secondary students and up to 84 elementary students.  Spare busses are used as replacements when busses are being serviced/repaired or are going out of District for a charter/field trip.  Presently we have 9 spare busses, which is more than we usually have. This was due to the possibility of needing more busses temporarily due to school closures. We will be eliminating at least one bus from our fleet at the end of this school year. Bus Information  Busses are replaced as per Ministry of Education criteria: - 84 passenger busses: 15 years or 450,000 KM - 34-76 passenger busses: 12 years or 325,000 KM Note: SD 68 has been purchasing 48 passenger busses that are equipped with wheel chair lifts and tie downs.  Once a bus has been replaced, it serves as a spare bus and it cannot be utilized permanently on a regular bus route as per the Ministry of Education.  Each year our district submits a bus replacement plan to the Ministry of Education.  Bus Purchase Costs: - 84 passenger: $140,000 - 48 passenger (Wheel chair equipped): $112,000 Note: Bus prices fluctuate from year to year.
