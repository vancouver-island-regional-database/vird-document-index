District Facilities Plan Process Change Bylaw Page 26 of 35 Agenda Item Number 14.3 BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 DISTRICT FACILITIES PLAN CHANGE PROCESS BYLAW A BYLAW by the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) (hereinafter called the "Board") to adopt a process to effect changes to the District Facilities Plan (hereinafter called the “Plan”). WHEREAS THE Board has adopted the Plan after an extensive public consultation process; and, WHEREAS THE Board in adopting the Plan considered certain facilities planning documents and policies, including, but not limited to guiding principles and a capital planning framework; and, WHEREAS THE Board is desirous of establishing a process whereby changes to the Plan will be made in accordance with the following process; NOW THEREFORE the Board agrees to the following process: 1. Change(s) to the Plan may only be initiated by the Board. Proposals for change may generate from: a) a standing committee of the Board b) management committee c) other Board committees. d) consistent with Board Policy and Procedure related to Policy Development/Implementation and Committee responsibilities. Any proposed changes will be considered by the Board in an in camera meeting and if accepted for consideration will be referred to a public meeting of the Board for approval of the commencement of a consultation process as set out in this Bylaw. 2. A written report (“the Report”) with rationale for the proposed change(s) and the implications to the district’s long term capital planning direction will be prepared by the Facilities Planning Committee, approved by the Board and made available for the public consultation process. 3. Upon approval by the Board, there will be at least a 30 day public consultation process which will include a. notification to all affected persons and entities and all stakeholder groups; b. posting of the proposed changes including the Report on the district website; and District Facilities Plan Process Change Bylaw Page 27 of 35 Agenda Item Number 14.3 c. provision for written submissions, oral submissions, and the use of surveys for feedback, as determined by the Board for each process. 4. Following the public consultation process, the Facilities Planning Committee will review the input from the public consultation process and will prepare a second report (“Second Report”) for Board consideration at a public meeting. The Second Report will summarize the public input and will include a recommendation(s) for the Board consideration. 5. There shall be a minimum of 60 days between the acceptance for consideration of the Report and the Board deliberation and decision on the Second Report. 6. The Second Report will be posted on the district website for a minimum of 10 days before the public Board meeting at which the Second Report will be considered for approval.
