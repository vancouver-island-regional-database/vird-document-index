Fire Ban now in effect The hot, dry weather is creating significant forest fire hazards with the fire danger for our area currently rated as extreme. Therefore, campfires and cooking fires will not be permitted across BC in order to reduce risk. This fire ban will be in effect until October 15, 2021. https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/safety/wildfire-status/fire-bans-and-restrictions Celebrate our Present. Embrace our Future. Honour our Past. Salish Wind restoration The Salish Wind canoe at Transfer Beach Park has been removed in order to complete important restoration work over the coming months. The project is being completed in collaboration with Stz’uminus First Nation, Kinsmen Club of Ladysmith and the Town. We’re grateful for the work of Stz’uminus master carver Manny Sampson, brother Elmer and artist Edward Joe who all had a hand in creating the Salish Wind and look forward to the preservation of this important representation of Coast Salish cultural heritage. A new shelter that is part of the Transfer Beach improvements through the Tourism Dependent Community Fund will now be constructed to protect the canoe from the elements. Ongoing Water Restrictions The Town of Ladysmith is observing a high rate of water consumption among property owners early in the summer. We encourage you to find opportunities to conserve water during these hot and dry months. Your help ensures our reservoir levels remain high in case of a fire. For information on current water restrictions, visit https://www.ladysmith.ca/our-services/water. Official Community Plan Review public engagement The Town of Ladysmith is updating its Official Community Plan (OCP) to help manage growth and change in a way that helps our community meet its goals. An OCP is a policy document that influences how we live, gather, work, learn, recreate, shop, and move around the community. At its heart, an OCP is about managing land use and the physical growth of the town. Over the coming months we will complete the public engagement phase of the OCP review and be asking for your input. Right now you can get involved by completing our OCP Launch Survey and our ‘Show ‘n Tell while Oot and Aboot’ visual mapping survey. What’s your big idea for Ladysmith? What makes our community special? Where do you see our Town in the next 20 years? Your input throughout the OCP Review process will help shape the final plan. TOWN NEWS YOU CAN USE Graffiti Removal Rebate Program - Council has approved a new Graffiti Removal Rebate Program that runs now until September 30, 2021. A one-time rebate of $50 is available to all property owners to cover the cost of removing graffiti. Please complete a form available at City Hall or my emailing info@ladysmith.ca and submit it with photos of the work to be completed. Holland Lake Intake at Chicken Ladder - Town crews completed the dredging of the Holland Lake intake at Chicken Ladder over three days in late June. The work ensures the intake basin is clear of sediment buildup - particularly fine sand - which causes added maintenance at our Water Filtration Plant. Art Donation - Council approved a public art donation request from Duck and Tracy Paterson for 13 signed, original, limited edition Roy Henry Vickers art prints that are currently on display in the City Hall Council Chamber. Vickers is a well-known BC artist who is beloved for his colourful landscapes. Thank you Duck and Tracy for your generousity. Pet Responsibility - We encourage community members to be responsible pet owners and please pick up after your animal(s) when out walking on sidewalks, trails and visiting parks. Properly disposing of your waste bags by using the nearest available trash is helping to keep our public spaces enjoyable for everyone. We appreciate your cooperation. Yard Waste Pilot Project - The Town’s Yard Waste Pilot Project took place over two weeks in the spring and was highly successful. A report will be brought to Council in the fall with recommendations for potential future initiatives. In the meantime, you can always bring your yard waste free of charge to the CVRD’s Peerless Recycling Depot. Aggie Hall being used as a COVID-19 Vaccination Site - The Town of Ladysmith continues to support Island Health in facilitating a COVID-19 immunization clinic at Aggie Hall. All appointments for vaccinations must be pre-booked.
