For details on these events and others, visit the Ladysmith Chamber of Commerce website: https://www. ladysmithcofc.com/ calendar NEW EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS WEBSITE The fall and early winter months bring the potential for inclement weather conditions and all Ladysmith residents are encouraged to be prepared. Recently, the Cowichan Valley Regional District and Emergency Management Cowichan (EMC) launched a new website to provide information and tools to mitigate, prepare, respond, and recover from a range of hazards and emergencies. EMC is a shared emergency management service that works together the Town and with other local governments in the region. The new comprehensive and user-friendly website launched over the summer comes as communities in the region are seeking efficient and reliable information, resources, as well as solutions to mitigate and prepare for incidents of climate-influenced crises such as flooding, extreme weather, wildfire, and drought. This innovative platform is the result of collaboration between emergency response experts, community safety advocates and EMC’s partners who include the First Nations of the Cowichan area, on whose territory EMC is grateful to do its work, and first responders: fire departments, the RCMP, BC Ambulance, ground and marine search and rescue groups and community service organizations. Autumn brings with it falling leaves, cooler temperatures and little ghosts and goblins in search of candy. With Halloween around the corner, here are a few tips for ensuring your children can have fun and remain safe while trick or treating: The Family Fireworks Permit Application fee is $10. Separate permit classes, fees and insurance requirements apply for display fireworks and pyrotechnics. The Family Fireworks Permit replaces the permit process previously overseen by the Cowichan Valley Regional District. Permit approvals are subject to the current Coastal Fire Ban and any corresponding restrictions. Application forms can be obtained at Ladysmith City Hall, 410 Esplanade during business hours, Monday to Friday: 8:30 a.m. – 4:00 p.m., by emailing info@ladysmith. ca or visiting our website. Please note that all fireworks permit applications are due at least 3 business days before the event to be approved by the Fire Chief. • Wear a light-coloured or bright costume, reflective tape or arm bands to heighten visibility. • Wear costumes that are properly fitted to reduce tripping hazards. • Select costumes constructed from flame-retardant materials. • Make sure your vision is not restricted. Consider wearing make-up instead of masks. • Create a fun necklace with string and glow sticks or reflective tape to keep kids visible to drivers. Or accessorize with a flashlight! • Stay on the sidewalk and never jaywalk. • Trick or treat in pairs or groups. • Make sure your kids ask you to check their candy before they eat it. BUDGET MEETINGS SCHEDULE The Stocking Lake Dam is an earth-fill dam constructed from natural materials and maintained to Provincial standards through an agreement between the municipality and regional district. Council reviews the budget at meetings in the fall and winter before adopting the Financial Plan bylaws by May 15, 2024. Citizens are invited to provide input into the budget by attending Council meetings at which the budget is discussed, or by writing to Council at any time. Water drawn from Stocking Lake is the drinking water source for the residents of Ladysmith in the winter months, as well as the CVRD Area G (Saltair).
