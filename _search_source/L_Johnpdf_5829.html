I object to the proposed transportation changes. I am a concerned member of my community, a community that prides itself on taking care of all of our children. As you know, the Seaview Elementary catchment is quite large, encompassing both sides on a main highway and many rural areas. Our catchment also includes the Snaw-Naw—AsFirst Nation. Currently Seaview Elementary has approximately 85 students that regularly take the bus and nearly all are defined as courtesy riders. There are safety concerns with your proposed budget that I have to assume you are not aware of or this proposal would not be up for consideration. Children should not be crossing a main highway, with vehicles travelling in excess of 100krn/hr, to attend school. There are at least six lanes on the highway with no access to overpasses or underpasses to facilitate safe crossing. This is not about convenience, this is about safety. I would encourage you all to walk the routes that our children will be expected to walk to get an idea of what you would be imposing on our children. I would be happy to arrange for members of the board to walk home with children in our community at your convenience. I respect that your board has dif?cult ?nancial decisions to make however your decisions regarding transportation are not balanced to include all stakeholders. The decision to provide out of catchment bussing to LSS for the 165 students from Cedar is being paid for by the children in my community. 450 courtesy riders will be eliminated to allow 165 students to go to school out of catchment. You were elected to represent us all and if I can stand up for safety of the kids in my community, you certainly should. You were elected to do so. Sincerely, Laura John
