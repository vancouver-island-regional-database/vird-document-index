NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 7, 2022 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Pete Sabo, Executive Director Planning & Operations Brian Hackwood, Assistant Director Planning & Operations Kelsey Bakewell, Facilities Planning Manager Phil Barkhouse, Maintenance Manager École North Oyster Update Background: The purpose of this Information Sheet is to provide the Board with a status update on various activities relating to École North Oyster, including the Active School Travel (AST) Project, Building Envelope Project (BEP), the Water Use Agreement, and water quality. Discussion: Active School Travel Update: The École North Oyster “Active School Travel (AST) Project” is addressing some of the issues that ENO has been experiencing both on and off school property. These issues include poor accommodation on school site for daily transportation demands, insufficient parking, vehicle speeds in school zones, ineffective signage, unsafe crossing, insufficient lighting, unsafe roadway for users, and shortage of road safety awareness and training for students and families. A School Travel Action Plan was completed in 2019 and a Stakeholder Committee was put together, including District Staff, Ministry of Transportation & Infrastructure (MOTI), ICBC and District Facilities Staff to make recommendations based on the Plan. Information sheet: École North Oyster Update Page 2 Recommendations are as follows in priority order: To date, NLPS has completed: • Crossing guard in place • #2 – Installation of dynamic speed sign on school property • #3 – Installing an asphalt pathway from the crosswalk to the front entrance of school • #5 – Installing barriers to protect parking entrances • Lease renewal at NOAHS - use of their parking lot for Drop-off and Pick-up in exchange for water supply/snow clearing MOTI installed pedestrian indicating lighting at the x-walk connecting the North Oyster Community Center parking lot. As the school did not support the addition of staff parking, Priority #1 was not completed. To date, NLPS has funded approximately $38,000 on improvements and $12,000 per year in Crossing Guard expenses on the ENO Active School Travel Project. The school principal commented that: • The district has provided funding for an adult crossing guard from 8:00-8:30 am and 2:00-2:30 pm to help students/parents cross Cedar Road in a safe and orderly manner and that this is working well. • The speed signs installed by the district have been seen to be a help in reducing the speed of vehicles passing through the school zone. • Parents parking along Cedar Road to drop off their kids continues to be a problem, however the availability of the North Oyster Community Center for drop off in the AM and pick up in the PM has greatly reduced the number of cars that chose to park along the street. • The school sends frequent reminders to parents about the availability of the community lot and address unsafe unloading/loading of children in front of the school with individual drivers as needed. Information sheet: École North Oyster Update • Page 3 The number of parents pulling into the staff parking lot to drop off their kids has almost completely stopped since the North Oyster Community Center parking became and approved/legitimate option. This has been well received by staff. BEP Project Update: Construction activities on the BEP Project are expected to continue into the first quarter of 2023, as the contractor has been experiencing supply chain issues and material delivery delays. Measures have been put in place to reduce the likelihood of water ingress into the school, following the flooding that took place in September. All but one of the displaced classrooms from the flooding incident have been remediated, and students are back in their classrooms. Staff are working closely with our insurance adjuster to ensure that the best interests of NLPS are met. Water Use Agreement: An official Water Use Agreement has been recently signed between the District and NOAHS (North Oyster & Area Historical Society), effective on October 1, 2022 (see attached Appendix A). This agreement is for a term of three years with an expiration date of September 30, 2025. By means of this agreement, NLPS is continuing to grant NOAHS use of the well water from the NLPS water source. Additionally, NOAHS has agreed to provide NLPS access to the parking lot during school operating hours for parent drop off and pick up. NLPS will provide snow clearing as operationally required, to the portions of North Oyster Community Centre parking lot on the days snow clearing is being provided to Ecole North Oyster’s parking lot. This will facilitate the use of the parking by parents. Water Quality Update: Monitoring: NLPS follows the established bacteriological monitoring program in consultation with the Drinking Water Officer. At a minimum, monthly bacteriological samples must be submitted for bacteriological analysis to a laboratory approved by the Provincial Health Officer. Based on the results of the monitoring program, the monitoring requirements may be extended or altered by the Drinking Water Officer. The raw water supply is chemically sampled every five years at a minimum and includes those parameters specified by the Drinking Water Officer. The water quality must meet or be lower than the maximum acceptable concentration (MAC) limits as listed in the most recent edition of the Guidelines for Canadian Drinking Water Quality (GCDWQ), prepared by the Environmental Health Directorate, Health Canada. Information sheet: École North Oyster Update Page 4 NLPS maintains two other well water sites: Mountain View Elementary and Gabriola Elementary. Post Fire: The water supply is monitored quarterly for contaminants due to the Schnitzer recycling metal fire and sent to Island Health to protect the quality.
