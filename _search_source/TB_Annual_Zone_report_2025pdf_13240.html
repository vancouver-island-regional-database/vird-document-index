ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Tania Brzovic Date: November 18, 2025 School Names/Zone: • • • • • • Wellington Secondary Ciliaire Elementary Departure Bay Elementary Rock City Elementary syuẁén’ct Elementary Uplands Elementary Annual Highlights: Overall, I am impressed by the quality of communication that our school leaders send out to families. Everyone sends out a regular (weekly or bi weekly) update to parents about what is happening at the school, often including updates not just about fun events like hot lunches, but also areas of focus that the kids are learning about, whether it be related to academics or social emotional learning. I also saw a tremendous amount of enthusiasm from everyone about this year’s Terry Fox run. Wellington: I spent a great deal of time at Wellington this past year. I was given the opportunity to support a grade 12 student with the completion of her Capstone project. The project entailed interviewing grade 9 students about their experiences in adjusting to high school, and finding out areas where the school can better support them… even though we didn’t have our ‘Belong, Grow, Thrive,’ tagline at the time, the questions that the student devised really gave insight as to the ways students did & didn’t feel they had a sense of belonging in the school. In our preparations for the interviews, we had terrific conversations about the importance of feeling a sense of belonging & connection in order to achieve to one’s highest potential. There was some sadness at Wellington in late 2024, due to the passing of vice principal Stacy Marusic. The staff came together, and with support from the district, the kids were able to mourn & honour his memory. I attended the year end assembly & student leaders were chosen to thank outgoing staff, and the acknowledgement of Stacy was beautiful. The school is getting an addition with the new modular section that is currently being built. It will really help with the overcrowding issues. Everyone is excited for the project to be complete. New principal Dary Hoff told me that there is some great work happening between Wellington & some of their feeder schools… one project he finds really exciting is Senior Textiles and our Careers 10 courses partnering with Rock City for Big Buddies. And prior to that, in the 2024-25 school year, the school did a canoe building project which brought students from both Wellington & their feeder schools together to build a canoe. I was able to attend the ceremony where the canoe was officially revealed & it was a very moving experience. Page 2 Cilaire: One of things we often talk about is that learning can happen in multiple ways. I saw a great example of that at Cilaire, as I attended a concert where the primary students (kindergarten & gr 1, if I recall correctly) performed songs that demonstrated their learnings about letter sounds. It was a great deal of fun, and the students were a terrific audience for their younger peers. I noticed that the school works hard to create leadership students for the older grades, which is so important. I must also note that the renovations that were done at the school have made it much easier for me as a person with mobility issues to be able to visit the school. The changes to the parking & the new elevator are fantastic. Departure Bay: I have not spent as much time at this school as I would have liked. I do receive their weekly parent memo & I love that it always includes samples of great photos, be it the Remembrance Day school wide poppy mural, or photos from Cross Country. I also love that it always features a Hulqiminum word. Rock City: They had a great year; they have a vibrant PAC who puts on a tremendous number of events that help to create a great environment at the school. Tis heir Christmas gingerbread event was very creative, and their fun fair was fantastic. The school has a new principal, Trina Norgan, and she has been warmly welcomed by families & staff. I was at the Open House & I was impressed by the spirit I felt in the building. It’s a very inviting environment. They are really focused on school goals that integrate the strategic plan, such as • • • Intentional, targeted response to literacy data. This looks like UFLI in all primary classes, Six Minute Solution in Intermediate classes, PRESS intervention based on identified need, lunch and learn series with our literacy coordinator. Improving literacy is a school goal as well. Monthly Indigenous Culture Nights in partnership with Nuu-chah-nulth Nations. All families are welcome to join for dancing, singing, drumming, and a meal. Connected both to student wellness and Truth and Reconciliation. Intentional partnerships and collaboration with Wellington Secondary (their skills for life program will access our playground, Big Buddies with an ELL class, student helpers from Career Explorations 11, volunteers from Wellington to support Indigenous Culture Nights, et al.) Supports student wellness syuẁén’ct: I haven’t been able to spend as much time at this school as I’d like. One thing that stands out for me is their commitment to environmental stewardship. They have worked with Nanaimo & Area Land Trust (NALT) to support the health of the Northfield marsh. It’s a great way for students to truly learn both about & from the land.
