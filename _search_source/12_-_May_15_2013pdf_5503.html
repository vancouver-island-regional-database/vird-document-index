UNFINISHED BUSINESS Custodial Review Update Mr. Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations gave an update on the steps the district has taken in response to the Custodial Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held May 15, 2013 Services Review that was tabled in June 2012. Mr. Sabo said that each recommendation in the review has been addressed, although some recommendations will require ongoing attention and review. He said that implementation of the recommendations has had a positive effect on the operations of the department. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Bard Folio B13/05/15-06 Energy Management Update The Business Committee receives the Custodial Review Update. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Chad Dalrymple, Energy Manager, gave the Business Committee an update on energy management initiatives in the district. • He said that the district spends more than $1 million annually on natural gas and $900,000 on electricity. Since 2003 district has saved $5 million in energy costs because of management initiatives. • Dover Bay Secondary and Cedar Secondary have electrical monitoring systems that show daily electrical usage. • Five schools are participating in the BC Hydro Community Champions Program.  The district’s first electric vehicle is expected to be delivered in the next month. Recharging stations have been installed – two at NDSS and one in the Facilities Department. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Saunders Folio B13/05/15-07 That the Business Committee receives the Energy Management Update. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held May 15, 2013 NEW BUSINESS Financial Forecast as at April 30, 2013 Mr. Phil Turin presented the Business Committee with a financial forecast to April 30th. He said that additional revenues, including the May distance learning enrolment funding and additional international students, combined with some additional expenses, are expected to result in an increase in the district’s projected surplus by about $300,000. The Board has earmarked the surplus from this year to reduce the reductions required in next year’s budget. BCASBO Procurement Working Group Mr. Turin updated the Committee on the BCASBO Procurement Working Group. He said that Graham Roberts, Assistant SecretaryTreasurer, was a member of the provincial committee and that he would ask Mr. Roberts to update the Business Committee in the Fall of the work the committee has done. BMO - P-Card Rebate Mr. Turin reviewed the district’s p-card program. He said that the program has brought more than $140,000 in rebates to the district in the past four years.
