These are listed and discussed below. 1 THE REPORT CLAIMS THERE IS AN INABILITYTO EXPAND ON THE EXISITNG PROPERTY (NOT POSSIBLE TO PLACE PORTABLES) The existing property is huge so it is very difficultto understand why there would be any significant expansion problems. In the past portables have been located on site, at the front of the school where the paved parking is currently located. It would seem to be a fairly minor issue to put portables in a portion of the paved parking area (where they were previously located) and move some of the parking to the lot at the rear of the school. This really seems to be a non-issue. 2 DECLINING ENROLLMENT IS LISTED AS ANOTHER REASONS FOR CLOSING THE SCHOOL This is another puzzler as the district's own numbers included on Page 5 of the report show the numbers steadily increasing between now and 2026. The 2013 population is shown as 184 currently and increasing to 231 by 2026. 3 FACILITY CONDITION The original Facilities Report in 2013 indicated that the school was in reasonably good condition while the updated report shows it as being in relatively poor condition (but still in better condition than some of the other schools that are to remain open). It would be beneficial to carry out a further assessment of the actual condition of the building, clarifying what will have to be done in the near future. It should be of concern that some students willbe relocated from this school with a fairly good seismic rating to Cilaire which has a much poorer seismic rating. The plan also suggests that improvements at Cilaire won't be taking place anytime soon so it seems students will be left in a seismically risky situation indefinitely. Why not move all the students to the safer Departure Bay School until Cilaire can be upgraded. 4 NEGATIVE STUDENT FLOW Not sure what this means but could be the same as declining enrollment discussed above. 5 LOCATION WITHINZONE IS LISTED AS ANOTHER REASON FOR THE CLOSURE The report talks of moving students to Frank Ney and the need to expend capital funds to build a new wing to accommodate them. It isn't clear why we are spending money to build a new wing when the existing Departure Bay School could continue to be used. Some other issues that need to be considered if Departure Bay students move to Frank Ney include (1) This will move students from the Wellington High school catchment area to Dover Bay Catchment. Dover Bay is already over capacity so this will only aggravate that condition. (2) The separation between Departure Bay School and Frank Ney is significant. As measured along Hammond Bay Road it is about 6.5 km. This could result in significant additional transportation costs for families and possibly additional bussing costs for the school district. None of this has been discussed in the report. The proximity of Departure Bay School to Cilaire and Rock City does provide some flexibilityin moving students to those locations. However there are also some negative consequences of doing so. Rock City will become the largest elementary school in the district and is shown operating at 135% capacity once it absorbs the Departure Bay students. There is no mention in the report as to how this problem will be dealt with. Hopefully the unwritten plan is not to have students in portables throughout their elementary school carriers as this would be contrary to the goals the School District has set for itself. Alan Davidson
