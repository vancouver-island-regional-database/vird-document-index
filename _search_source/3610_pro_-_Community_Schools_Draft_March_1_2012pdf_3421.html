SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3610P COMMUNITY SCHOOLS Definitions - for the purposes of this policy the following definitions apply. 1. Community Education Community Education is an educational endeavor which involves an entire community in the process of addressing lifelong educational issues for the betterment of individuals, families, and the community as a whole. The definition extends the role of the school from one of the more traditional roles of instructing students to one of identifying the needs, challenges, and resources of the community and acting as a catalyst in the use and development of facilities, programs, and leadership in support of community improvement. 2. Community School A Community School is designated as such by the Board of Education and is established to facilitate the advancement of community education by providing a variety of programs and services, either directly or in cooperation with community members, organizations, and agencies and municipalities. A Community Schoolâ€™s role is extended to identifying the needs, challenges, and resources of the community and acting in partnership with other community agencies and organizations to facilitate the development of educational, social, cultural, recreational programs and leadership. 3. Community School Coordinator The Community School Coordinator is an employee of the District who is assigned to a Community School or to a family of Community Schools and who is responsible for providing leadership in promoting integrated services, community development and parent and community involvement within the school and for other neighbouring schools. 4. Family of Community Schools A group of individual Community Schools may constitute a Family of Community Schools that works in a cooperative manner to recognize and support the unique educational, cultural, social, and recreational needs of a particular area of the School District.
