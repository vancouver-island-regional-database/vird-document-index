ADJOURNMENT Page 3 of 25 4.1 Kelsey Toye Subject: Rutherford FW: Rutherford Elementary Elementary Dear Trustees, I am writing to request that you withdraw the bylaw to close Rutherford Elementary. I currently have a son in grade two at Rutherford and a daughter ready to start kindergarten this fall. We moved to this neighbourhood shortly before my son started kindergarten and the fact that there was an elementary and high school within walking distance to our house was one of the primary reasons we chose the house we did. At the time we purchased our house, Rutherford was slated for a rebuild. It has been almost exactly 1 year since the initial letter went home from the school board advising us Rutherford was now under consideration for closure. The last year has been an extremely difficult one for my family because ofthe closure announcement. After a of summer of unrest, we spent countless hours in the fall attending meetings and writing letters only to feel like our voices were falling on deaf ears. The whole consultation process left us and many others in our community feeling like the outcome was predetermined. After the vote in December, we have now spent 6 agonizing months waiting to hear if the ministry would approve the funds for the expansion. Our school has tried very hard to keep the spirit that makes Rutherford so special alive but you can see the stress wearing on parents and children. Our PAC has done little fundraising because many feel ”what's the point”. The money we had already raised for new playground equipment lays dormant. When the ministry announces new funding for general maintenance such as boilers, we don't even make the list for consideration. Being left in limbo essentially leaves our school to die a slow death. To hear Graham Roberts suggest in a recent meeting that the funding request should be left in place indefinitely is absolutely heart wrenching and unfair! My husband and I worry about the stress this potential closure has caused our son. We've seen a lot of tears and many nights where he has had difficulty going to sleep because he is worried about having to go to another school and which friends he is going to lose along the way (maybe all ofthem if we fall into the McGirr catchment). He's just a kid and besides family, school and friends are probably the most important things in his life. He shouldn't be left ”indefinite|y” to lose sleep over these things. I also worry about before/afterschool care, which is something we depend on. Currently all north end schools have long waitlists, how much longer will those lists be if we close a school? Will we now be on the bottom of that list? Also, what about overcrowding a few years from now? The school district can tell us the north end enrollment is set to decline over the next 10 years all they want but those of us that live here see nothing but new developments. Rutherford already has at least a dozen more kids enrolled than we did in Sept. Are we just going to start jamming kids in portables until we realize we should have just kept Rutherford open or rebuilt it in the first place? These are things I lose sleep over at night.
