Survey of Parents of Preschoolers Regarding the Proposed Closure of Departure Bay School JUNE 2015 Information gathered by Taryn Silver 2920 Haliday Cres. Nanaimo BC V9T1B1 (250) 758-1644 tarynmsilver@gmail.com Formatted by Michelle Fairbanks 2882 Biscayne Bay, Nanaimo BC V9T3C4 (250) 585-7655 mfairbanks@shaw.ca Survey of Parents of Preschoolers Regarding the Proposed Closure of Departure Bay School I came to my attention recently that Departure Bay Eco School was being considered for closure in June of 2016. As a parent of a 2 year old boy and 3 year old girl living just up the road from the school it had always been our plan to enrol our children there and the Eco School Program having just been approved this spring is a really good fit for our children’s learning styles. I immediately wrote letters to the school board, MLA and Minister of Education. One of my concerns was the data regarding to projected enrollment. Because the federal census no longer collects information on where children of different ages are living, and because anecdotally I’ve heard many parents talking about the Eco-school initiative from both within catchment and outside of the area, I wanted to conduct a brief ad hoc survey to poll these parents and feel like their opinions can be counted. I was honestly overwhelmed with the number of responses I got without any real significant effort. - Taryn Silver -1- This report is a compilation of survey results from 56 parents representing 95 preschool aged children living in the school district 68 region. Six basic questions were asked. The parents volunteered their responses through Facebook messages, and e-mails between June 15th and June 19th, 2015. Some of the parents chose to remain anonymous while others agreed to have their names shared. All of their comments are included in the last section of this report. SOME THE THEMES THAT EMERGED FROM THE DATA ARE: • 50% of the parents surveyed live within the Departure Bay catchment and 50% live outside of the Departure Bay catchment, but 95% of parents surveyed were planning or seriously considering sending their children to Departure Bay • 95% had heard about the Eco program and felt it influenced their education choices • 62% would not send their children to Cilaire if the Eco program was moved there, and 20% were undecided and only 18% would move to Cilaire if the Eco Program moved.
