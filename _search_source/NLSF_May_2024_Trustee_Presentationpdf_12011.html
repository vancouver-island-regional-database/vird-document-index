Together…Making a Difference Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools Presentation May 22, 2024 Dan Morris, NLFS Board Chair Crystal Dennison, NLSF Exec Director Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation Through partnerships and collaboration, we continue to provide resources, programs and support to NLPS students  Raise awareness to increase support from Community  Develop & implement efficient, direct-to-student supports  Quick and efficient response to requests  Allocate funds to meet student needs  Provide confidential support for students  Encourage graduating students to pursue post-secondary education and training  Provide supports to schools where funds are not otherwise available Diverse Programs - Supporting Students  Scholarships, Bursaries, Awards  Student Support Fund  Food4Schools  Music Initiative grants  Enhanced Learning Initiatives Scholarships Bursaries & Awards Community sponsored awards to honour graduating students in: Academics Field of Study Service/Leadership Affiliation Adversity Financial Need Incentive for Students to graduate and acquire additional education/training Working to increase the value of scholarship funds to keep up with tuition rates Host the annual District Awards Night at the Port Theatre Scholarships Bursaries & Awards 392 $425,000 128 Student Applications Scholarships, Awards & Bursaries District Authority Scholarships $165,000 District Awards Night – June 11 Port Theatre Student Support Fund Administrators, teachers, CYFSWs and other staff request funds to directly support students Examples of needs: food | shoes | clothing | eye care | dental care | medical| school supplies | fees for field trips | band instrumental rental | student fees for grad, athletics Student Support Fund 875 Students received school supplies $86,500 Supports provided Increased requests for emergency supports, fees (athletic/grad) and certifications Food4Schools Providing equitable and sustainable food support for all students. Food4Schools Feeding Futures and New Possibilities * Equitable Access, Resources, & Delivery * Sustainable systems & partnerships to increase local food * Collaboration with District, community organizations & broader community Goals & Next Steps: * Support creation of school food framework * Support schools with creating stigma free meals programs/food literacy * Expand menu to increase variety, nutrition and culturally safe foods * Build community of support for lasting impact Food4Schools Supported the District in acquiring a FIP Grant to create a designated food prep space Food4Schools Delivery Van (sponsored by Tom Harris Community Foundation) Proud to work with NLPS and numerous Community partners to ensure students have access to health meals and snacks at school. Food4Schools at a Glance 350 pre-made breakfast and 500 lunches every day to 28 elementary schools 34 Schools receive weekly grocery orders 65,000+ pre-made meals provided so far in 2023/2024 Fall 2023 and Spring 2024 Provincial Feeding Futures Gatherings Regional School District’s Community of Practice “CoP” BC Chapter – Coalition for Healthy School Food Gabriola Elementary Music Provided funds for the purchase of instruments for the school music program.
