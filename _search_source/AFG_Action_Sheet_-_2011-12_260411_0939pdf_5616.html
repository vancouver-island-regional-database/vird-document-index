THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET TO: Business Committee FROM: Facilities Planning Committee DATE: May 3, 2011 SUBJECT: 2011/12 Annual Facility Grant (AFG) REFERENCE:  2011-12 AFG Plan - High Priorities (7 pages attached).  2011-12 AFG Programs – Planning Background (6 pages attached).  Ministry of Education letter dated March 30, 2011 from Mr. Doug Stewart, Director, Capital Management Branch.  Schedule A – MOE Annual Facility Grant Allocation (1 page attached). BACKGROUND: Each year the Ministry of Education announces the amount of the Annual Facility Grant, to be used by the Districts for projects that meet Ministry of Education Policy 1.1.100. The Annual Facility Grant was introduced in 1988/89 as a sharable capital allowance supplementing the School Board’s operating budget and funding projects required to extend facility life, especially re-roofing. Eight years ago, the Ministry changed the AFG process and increased funding to $2.56 million. Projects up to $1.5 million became eligible. Subsequently, the Ministry approved annual targeted funding for non-structural seismic mitigation in the value of approximately $165,000. DISCUSSION: The Ministry of Education News Release dated March 30, 2011 announced the Annual Facility Grant for 2011. School District 68 has been allocated a total of $2,613,085. The AFG Allocation Schedule A indicates that SD 68 will receive a capital portion amounting to $1,662,872 and an operating portion amounting to $950,213. The operating portion will be reduced by a contribution of $71,266 toward the continuing implementation of Capital Asset Management Services. SD 68 has also been advised that all school districts must provide the Ministry with 2011-12 AFG plan and cash flow before any funding is allocated. Page 2 School Districts were also advised that:  Existing AFG balances must be fully utilized.  Once the operating portion of the allocation is expended a Certificate of Approval for the capital portion of the allocation will be granted.  A capital bylaw will be required before the Certificate of Approval is issued. A plan for the expenditure of this AFG funding has been developed considering the following:  High priority District wide annual programs, including re-roofing, flooring and energy management.  Completion of high priority AFG health and safety projects, particularly washroom renovations.  Other selected high priority projects.  Funding in support of initiatives to reduce vandalism.  Emergent high priority projects, including those supporting educational program changes.  Contingency funding allowing for unforeseen emergent projects and program changes. RECOMMENDATION: That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that the 2011-12 Annual Facility Grant Plan in the value of $2,613,085 be approved.
