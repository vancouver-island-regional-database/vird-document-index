ADJOURNMENT Page 1 of 3 3.1 Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: May 5, 2015 TO: Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent SUBJECT: 2015-16 Projected Annual Budget Background: Staff believe that NLPS needs to become sustainable and live within its operating budget. By doing so, it will allow the district to focus its capital and grant money into efficiency saving programs such as facilities renewal, educational programing, student support programs, and the vulnerable population of students in Nanaimo. Over the past 3 years the budget has been supplemented and supported by funding protection and surplus rollover: 2012-13 1.5 million budgeted from surplus and received 4.0 million budgeted from funding protection Totalling 5.5 million subsidizing the operational budget 2013-14 2 million budgeted from surplus used 2.5 million from funding protection Totalling 4.5 million subsidizing the operational budget 2014-15 2.29 million from surplus 1.96 million from funding protection totalling 4.25 million subsidizing the operation budget Page 2 of 3 3.1 Rationale: Staff believe that in order to become sustainable financially, we need to project and combine school consolidations and closures into the budget process. This allows the district to precede strategically aligning district efficiencies and savings with the facilities plan to develop a financially sustainable operating budget for September 2016. To accomplish this goal the staff recommends the following Strategic guidelines: 1) Firstly, the 2015 budget must be sustainable on its own. The need to use surplus dollars from previous years will not allow us to prepare the system to maintain a sustainable budget. 2) Secondly, the unrestricted surplus is directed to strategic projects that enables us to support the district in maintaining long term budget sustainability and the Boards three primary goals. 3) That the district use the unrestricted surplus and the school closure savings to create efficiencies and savings to support next year’s budget. Recommendation: After hearing Trustees’ concerns from the April 30, 2015 presentation, the following modifications have been incorporated into the 2015/16 draft budget: 1) $500,000.00 be moved from Cedar Secondary reserve to custodial FTE for the year 2015-16. 2) A review of Senior Management/Excluded Staff organization for efficiencies to be completed by February, 2016. 3) That any district surplus for the year 2014-15 up to $500,000 be returned to the Cedar reserve fund. Staff recommends that the Board adopt the 2015-16 budget as presented. Board Motion: The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that it adopt the 2015-16 budget as presented. Page 3 of 3
