NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 10, 2020 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Administrative Procedure 501 – Selection of Architect and Administrative Procedure 513 - Purchasing Background As discussed at the March Board of Education Meeting, the Facilities department has faced difficulties in the past when applying Administrative Procedure 501 – Selection of Architect (AP 501) in the selection of Architects. Therefore, instead of returning to the Board of Education to request approvals for exemptions from the procedure on a regular basis, staff have proposed that AP 501 be amended in order to better meet the operational needs of the Facilities department in order to increase efficiencies. While bringing forward the amendments to AP 501, we would like to take advantage of the opportunity to also propose amendments to Administrative Procedure 513 – Purchasing (AP 513). Over the last year there has been extensive staff consultation regarding the purchasing administrative procedures and thresholds and it has been determined that the current version of AP 513 is very out of date (last revised in 1986) which is leading to operational challenges, inefficiencies and frequent requests for exemptions in order to meet required timelines. The update to AP 513 will bring the procurement thresholds in line with other local districts as well as adjust for inflation. Information Highlights of the significant amendments to AP 501 and AP 513 are as follows: AP 501  Adjustment to the name of the AP to “Appointment of Capital Project Consultants” to reflect the new wording throughout the document to accommodate other consultants that are engaged in relation to capital projects, rather than just Architects.  Removal of the use of the $500,000 threshold to determine if a project will require a formal evaluation process and request for proposal before appointing a consultant. Instead this is suggested to occur for projects that are deemed to be a “project of significance” by the Executive Director of Planning and Operations and SecretaryTreasurer.  Increase the flexibility and efficiency of the facilities department by providing the option to appoint either: o o a consultant that has been previously engaged in the construction or alterations of the facility, or a capital project consultant from a list of pre-qualified consultants. AP 513  Added definitions to improve the understandability of the AP, reorganized information, and made minor content updates.  Addition of a spending authority schedule which expands beyond tenders/contracts to include regular purchasing and will allow staff to better understand their spending/approval authority and provide Finance with the means to ensure appropriate authorization is followed.  Reduced the frequency in which we would tender banking services to accommodate for the significant operational burden that would exist upon a change to our banking provider.  Revised procurement thresholds to consider the rate of inflation since 1986 (106.36%) and to be more consistent with other local districts in order to better support our operational needs and improve efficiencies.  Incorporation of environmental considerations during the procurement process.  Modified the parties with the authority to approve sole source purchasing to include the Associate Secretary-Treasurer, Secretary-Treasurer, and/or Superintendent.  Increased the threshold for requiring bid/performance bonds from $10,000 to $75,000.  Altered the surplus material policy to ensure accordance with the spirit and intent of the Government of BC’s Core Policy & Procedures Manual, policy 6.3.4.d. The changes to AP 501 and AP 513 will be effective as of July 1, 2020, the start of the new fiscal year.
