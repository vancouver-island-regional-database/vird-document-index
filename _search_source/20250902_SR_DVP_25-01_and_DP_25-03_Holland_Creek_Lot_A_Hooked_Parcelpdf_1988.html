INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: Subject property The subject property is an 18.57-hectare hooked parcel located in the Holland Creek neighbourhood. A hooked parcel is a legal parcel that is divided by other tracts of land but is registered at the Land Titles Office as a single legal parcel. Typically, these parcels are created when park or road is dedicated from within a contiguous parcel of land, thereby creating a noncontiguous one. (The term “hooked” refers to the hooked symbols used on survey plans to show that what appears to be separate parcels of land are actually the same parcel). This large property is covered by multiple OCP and zoning designations. The property is currently designated as Neighbourhood Residential and Parks and Open Spaces under the OCP and zoned Low Density Residential (R-3-A), Comprehensive Development 8 Holland Creek Mixed Use Residential (CD-8), Park and Recreation (P-2) and Nature Park (P-3). The subject property also falls within the following development permit areas:  Commercial Development Permit Area (DPA 3)  Multi-Unit Residential Development Permit Area (DPA 4)  Riparian Development Permit Area (DPA 6)  Hazard Lands Development Permit Area (DPA 7)  Arbutus Hump Environmentally Sensitive Area Development Permit Area (DPA 11) The property is currently undeveloped, but some large portions were cleared by previous owners. Adjacent land uses are predominantly single dwelling residential. A map of the subject property is provided in Attachment C. PROPOSAL: The proposal is for variances to facilitate the subdivision of the hooked parcel into three separate parcels (proposed Lots A, B, and C) and defer typical subdivision requirements to later stages of development when more infrastructure will be in place. The subdivision supports future development plans for the proposed mixed-use and residential development and would result in the dedication of 7.18 hectares of parkland. This includes 5.68 hectares of land on and adjacent to Arbutus Hump where a sensitive ecosystem is located. To facilitate the proposed subdivision, a development variance permit is required to vary section 17.8.3(c) of the Zoning Bylaw to reduce the frontage requirement for parcels (for proposed Lot C) from 12.19 metres to 0.0 metres. Additionally, sections 4.05 (c), (e), (f), (g), and (h) of the Subdivision and Development Servicing Bylaw must be varied to allow the creation of three parcels by subdivision without utilities (i.e., electrical, telephone, cablevision, and gas utility) and connections to the Town servicing systems (i.e., water, sanitary sewer, and drainage systems). The proposed permits would only authorize the subdivision of the hooked parcel into three parcels and would not authorize any land alteration or construction. A covenant is in place on the property requiring infrastructure to be built prior to each phase of development and the proposed DVP contains a condition requiring road frontage and servicing requirements to be met prior to any further development of each lot. Essentially, the proposed variances delay—rather than exempt—the developer from meeting the required frontage and servicing requirements. The subject property falls within DPA 3, 4, 6, 7 8 and 11. Subdivision is exempt from the development permit requirements in DPA 3 and DPA 4. DPA 6 (Riparian) requires a development permit and biologist’s report prior to approval of subdivision. However, riparian development permits are staff-issuable and staff are waiting for provincial approval of the biologist report. For DPA’s 7, 8 and 11, none of the guidelines apply because no land alteration or development will occur to execute the proposed subdivision. Subsequently, DP 25-03 is an “empty” development permit, issued to meet the Local Government Act requirement for a development permit, but containing only basic conditions. Attachment D includes a letter of rationale from the applicant and Attachment A shows the proposed subdivision. ANALYSIS: For the reasons outlined below, staff are recommending approval of the proposal. The proposed variance and development permit facilitates the subdivision of the hooked parcel, will lead to the acquisition of large parkland areas, and will bring this project closer to the development phase. Once development begins, frontage and servicing requirements will be met. As noted above, the development permit guidelines for DPA 7, 8, and 11 are not applicable to the proposed subdivision. Additional development permits will be required prior to any development of the site and riparian protection requirements will be met through a staff-issuable development permit. The proposed permits simply allow lines on the subdivision plan to be changed. There is no impact to approving the proposed DVP or DP. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1.
