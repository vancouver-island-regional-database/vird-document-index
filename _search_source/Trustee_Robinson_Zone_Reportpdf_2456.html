April 2018 Trustee Robinson Zone Report Best Me I Can Be –    The pilot is going well. We have one more presentation to do in two classes and two more in one. One story I like came from the PAC parent at Park Avenue. She said her son came home very excited and hung his certificate in his bedroom. It stayed there until his dad got home … That’s when it was moved to the living room!! T’uxusthampsh (Formerly Spuptitul)   Planning is progressing well. We are expecting more teams than previous years and all the older elementary students as well as the high school students will be doing plays this year. For the first time we will be organizing a language symposium on May 30 following the T’uxusthampsh event at LSS on the 29th. The symposium will be held at VIU. We are expecting 200 participants from across the Island. Congrats to Anne Tenning who is doing an outstanding job organizing the symposium. Literacy Central Vancouver Island  LCVI has connected with the school board in an effort to strengthen the BEARS (Be enthusiastic about reading success) and BEAMS (Be enthusiastic about math success) programs.  Thanks to a $7,000 donations four elementary schools will each spend half a day in the Big House in Cedar learning about Hul’qumi’numn culture and language. This will happen on April 29th and 30th. The schools are Bayview, Fairview, Georgia and Quam Quam. Bayview Elementary Basketball  The school finished in second place in this years Small Schools Basketball Tournament. Although they lost to a very strong Departure Bay team, the referee was so impressed with the team’s sportsmanship that he donated his payment to the coach to buy pizza for the boys. Huge congratulations are due to the boys and also, perhaps especially, to their coach, Sean Lowrence. Georgia Accessible Playground Project April 11, 2018 I met with Georgia PAC members Barbara and Corinna today at Georgia. These are my conclusions from the meeting:  Georgia PAC has three members. Therefore, they are happy to leave this accessible playground fundraising and building project in the hands of staff. They simply do not have the time or energy required to take on the kind of major fundraising project required to build an accessible playground. That said, they will provide advice and suggestions as requested.  The PACs main interest is in bringing the “lower playground” up to standard. Their plan sees them beginning to fundraise for this project next year. They are hoping, and expecting, that some of the “upper playground” equipment will be moved to the “lower playground” as staff develops the accessible playground project.  I would hope that the district will make every effort possible to help them bring the “lower playground” up to standard as the accessible playground project is brought to fruition. It certainly needs upgrading in large measure for safety reasons. My suggestions    That Pete and his staff add a “cherry on the top” addition to their accessible playground plans to be left for the combined Georgia/McGirr PACs to do the required fundraising. They will also work to fund those improvements the district can’t provide for the “lower playground.” The ideas is that this fundraising will take place over the next year or two or more if required. That the Georgia and McGirr PACs continue to meet as required to take advantage of the McGirr PAC’s fundraising contacts and knowledge, knowledge they are enthusiastic about sharing. I have agreed to write all required proposals with support from Twyla Spoke (McGirr PAC), Basia Henoch and Lisa Baron (Community School Coordinators). It would also be very helpful if we do hire a full-time fundraising proposal writer to have that support.
