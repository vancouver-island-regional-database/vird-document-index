CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS Budget 2013-14 Trustee Allen asked that the Postponed Motion regarding the saved monies, approximately $400,000, that the extended Spring break for 2013-14 would create from last week be put back on the table. Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 29, 2013 Mr. Turin asked Mr. Nixon to update the committee on the NDTA position regarding the proposed calendar changes. Mr. Nixon stated that the NDTA has rejected the Board’s final offer regarding the local school calendar for 2013-14 that included a two-week spring break. He said that the NDTA would continue to negotiate with the Board. Mr. Turin, clarified, that the Board had made their final offer to the NDTA. As a result, the Board will revert to a one-week spring break next year and will not be able to achieve the $400,000 in proposed budgetary savings.
