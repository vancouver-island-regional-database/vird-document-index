Discussion An SSAC is a development charge that is assessed on developments that create new residential units to ensure there are funds available for amenity needs as neighbourhoods develop. The SSAC is intended to represent approximately 1/3rd the cost of the acquisition of green field (undeveloped land) or brown field (previously developed adjacent to schools) land for expansions. In our district we continue to anticipate the need for a future elementary school to the south of the highway in Lantzville as well as potential expansions at a number of sites dependent on in fill growth. We note that growth has slowed in the district, however, SSACs are intended to be long term in nature. Our district has a balance of $687,290.86. The majority of these funds have been collected in the City of Nanaimo. Moreover, we have split the fund between the north $635,629.99 and the south $51,660.87. We note that in the south we do not project an immediate need for a school site. However, a district cannot apply an SSAC to only a part of a district unless there is a significant geographic separation between communities – which is not the case here. As previously noted, we remain alert for strategic acquisitions to support future growth, but no acquisitions have occurred since the expansion of the Bayview site in 2023.
