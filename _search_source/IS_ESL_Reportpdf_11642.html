NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 28, 2024 Board of Education Laura Tait, Acting Superintendent Enhancing Student Learning Report (ESL Report) Background The Framework for Enhancing Student Learning (FESL) Policy and accompanying Enhancing Student Learning Reporting Order (M302/20) came into effect September 1, 2020. The Enhancing Student Learning Reporting Order requires Boards to submit to the Ministry of Education and Child Care an annual report in accordance with the requirements in the Order. In January 2024, a provincial review team reported back to all 60 districts around their respective ESL submissions from the previous school year. Feedback was provided to Superintendents, along with a brief written report (attached). Discussion Enhancing Student Learning Reporting Order Authority: School Act, sections 81 and 168 (2) (t) The Framework for Enhancing Student Learning (ESL) MUST including information and evidence of the following: 1. Intellectual Development • Students will meet or exceed literacy expectation for each grade level • Students will meet or exceed numeracy expectation for each grade level • Number and percentage of students who are completing grade to grade transitions on time 2. Human and Social Development • Students will feel welcome, safe and connected to their school 3. Career Development • Students will graduate 4. Establish categories for each of the following populations and report on those categories separately: Indigenous students ON/OFF reserve, Children and Youth In Care, Students with Disabilities or Diverse Abilities 5. Board’s approach to continuous improvement of student achievement Information Sheet: Enhancing Student Learning Report Page 2 As part of the Framework for Enhancing Student Learning’s Annual Review, the provincial review team pays particular attention to 5 focus areas: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. Approach to continuous improvement Data and evidence (quanitative data) Ongoing engagement (qualitative evidence) Alignment and adaptations Improving equity of learning outcomes The review team then builds consensus on strengths and considerations for each district in these focus areas. Our NLPS report has both strengths and areas of growth. The process the Board is embarking on currently, around the Strategic Plan is timely as it can be directly aligned with the FESL requirements. Moving forward, areas of focus that should be considered are: articulating our continuous improvement cycle which is based on the metrics cited in the Strategic Plan; continuing our phased approach to evidence/data informed decision making and planning; highlighting and growing the connection between school plans and the Strategic Plan; and an extrapolated approach to identified demographics.
