NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 14, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Funding Update Background This Information Sheet provides the Board with an update on the District’s confirmed Operating Grant as announced in December by the Ministry, along with the variance from the information presented in October. As a reminder, each February the District is required to estimate the coming school year’s anticipated enrolment for each of the three 1701 Student Data Collections set by the Ministry of Education and Child Care which take place in September, February, and May per year. Estimates must be calculated for students in regular K-12 programing, distributed (online) learning, alternative programs, continuing education, as well as many additional categories such as students with unique needs, those who require language support, Indigenous ancestry, and others. Final figures are submitted to the Ministry of Education and Child Care and are used to calculate the District’s preliminary annual Operating Grant. Following the September 1701 Student Data Collection, the Board is provided with an update on the District’s actual enrolment as compared to preliminary estimates, as well as the revised Operating Grant amount based on the enrolment variances and the anticipated funding levels. However, at the time of the update to the Board in early October each year the 1701 Data Collection is not officially confirmed by Ministry, further, annual Operating Grants are not finalized and announced by the Ministry of Education and Child Care until the end of December. Discussion In October, preliminary results indicated the District was up 95.858 funded student FTE which provided for an additional $3.28 million in funding. Pursuant to the Ministry of Education and Child Care’s funding announcement in late December, there was a slight reduction to our regular student funded FTE along with some other minor changes in enrolment. Additionally, the supplemental funding categories were confirmed which resulted in an overall variance from initial October estimates of $386K. The majority of this funding relates to our Salary Differential Supplemental category which increased by $491K; this funding is to account for our higher-than-average teacher salaries as compared to other districts in the province. Variances to these grants would generally be to account for factors such as general wage increases or significant enrolment changes. 3|Page Information Sheet: Funding Update Page 4 With regards to the Classroom Enhancement Fund, the District requested an additional 14 FTE Teacher Staffing over 2022/23 which had a value of $1,997,135. The Ministry of Education and Child Care did provide the requested FTE, however, the funding received was $1,841,392 which was $155,743 less than requested. A funding shortfall for teacher staffing was noted across the province, when questioned, Ministry indicated they had frontloaded it by way of the Labour Settlement Grant that was provided in April 2023 to support the Annual Budget and the associated costs of general wage increases for all employee groups. However, there was no indication a portion of that funding was for the pending Classroom Enhancement Fund needs and costs that would be calculated and provided in the Fall of 2023. Accordingly, districts utilized the Labour Settlement Fund for their Operating Fund costs, fully absorbing the funding and not isolating any for future CEF costs which has resulted in the current shortfall. We are still accounting for this shortfall as we finalize the Amended Annual Budget and will provide additional information in February. One additional note with regards to the CEF funding. Ministry provides districts with an Overhead allocation which is meant to cover the costs associated with administering LOU17, for our district this includes Payroll, HR, and Custodial staffing and a contractual Pro-D obligation. Districts are also required to use the Overhead funding for the replacement costs associated with the additional Teacher Staffing. Note the District’s Overhead funding over the last seven years. As previously indicated the District’s Overhead Fund supports a variety of staffing, notably those costs increase year-over-year in line with negotiated general wage increases, however, the Overhead Fund has not incremented to account for those cost escalations. The Fund did increase in 2023/24 by 5.5% but general wage increases over the last two years are in excess of 10%. Further, as the District’s replacement costs have increased substantially due to both the global pandemic and the revision to the Employment Standards Act, the Overhead Fund which must support replacement costs, has clearly not increased for this rising cost. The District must therefore use its Operating Fund to supplement the replacement costs associated with the staffing under the CEF, which Ministry committed to fully funding. Aside from the Operating Grant and CEF adjustments, we also received an additional $24,793 under our Community-LINK Fund, which we assume is to account for the cost of general wage increases.
