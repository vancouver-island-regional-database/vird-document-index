CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY 6 Page 8 of 26 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 17, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Deletion of AP 421 – Contract Labour Background This Information Sheet is related to the ongoing process and review of district Administrative Procedures to ensure relevancy and alignment with current district practice and procedures. Staff intend to delete Administrative Procedure 421 – Contract Labour (the AP), and this Information Sheet will outline the rational behind the decision. Discussion The AP originated from the early 1980’s and district staff assume it was previously in Board Policy moved to an Administrative Procedure. The AP requires the District to hire unionized labour when contracting out. It is important to recognize that the AP is related to the contracting out of services only and has no impact on our CUPE 606 partners. Staff cannot find an instance where such preference has been sought in Request for Proposals (RFP) or Tenders and are unaware of how long it has been since a requirement was included. Rather than activate the AP, the District intends to delete it and apply our purchasing AP 513 which governs decisions associated with contracts and purchasing. We also note that the deletion of the AP allows us to follow government procurement best practices which requires competition and fair, open, and nondiscriminatory procurement processes. Finally, it should be noted the District works hard to have our own employees perform work as much as possible and the deletion of AP 421 has no impact on this practice. We have met with CUPE in advance of bringing this item forward to ensure that they are aware that the deletion of the AP has no impact on the District’s philosophy with respect to “in-housing” work, particularly associated with facilities. Appendix A: AP 421 – Contract Labour Page 9 of 26 Appendix A Administrative Procedure 421 – Contract Labour AP 421 – Contract Labour Purpose Specifications for work contracted to outside sources shall stipulate that Union Labour be employed wherever possible. Adopted: October 12, 1983 Amended: January 11, 1984 AP 421 – Contract Labour Page 1 of 1 Page 10 of 26 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 17, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Print Strategy – Update Background: The District recently adopted a Print Strategy as ‘paper and GHG reduction strategies’ under the District’s Environment Stewardship Action Plan (ESAP) requirement to “Reduce our impact on the land and implement restorative efforts”. The Print Strategy was developed through a comprehensive review of the print and copy environment throughout the organization. Staff in Finance and IT worked with an external consultant as well as school and district staff at all schools and sites to examine options to limit paper and electricity use associated with printing and copying taking place in the District, while also addressing our aging fleet of copiers and the inflating costs of paper. Specifically, the strategy envisions three priorities to lessen paper and electricity use as follows: 1. A significant reduction of inefficient, single use “standalone” printers in the District replaced by larger multifunction copier/printers (MFP) dispersed through schools to ensure appropriate geographic placement for staff to ensure convenience; 2. The use of a print management software program that requires all printing to be released at source by individual users with individual passwords for access and that provides visibility and reporting on printer and copier usage data; and 3. Communication about the strategy's importance to promote less paper usage and address concerns about the impact of the removal of single use machines. An overview of the proposed Print Strategy was presented to the Business Committee on November 09, 2022. This information sheet provides a summary of the implementation status of the Print Strategy across the District along with observations on its initial impact. Discussion: Implementation and Status Following the adoption of the Print Strategy, a comprehensive implementation plan was developed with the goal of completing the rollout at every district school and administrative site by September 2023. Information packages outlining the present and proposed print/copy environment, details on the new devices, cost information, estimated environmental benefits, reductions of standalone printer devices, support and training plans were communicated with all school and site administrators providing an opportunity for concerns, questions, and change requests to be addressed. Following acceptance of the implementation plan, the rollout began in March 2023. As of September 2023, the new printing environment based on the Print Strategy was completed. Page 11 of 26 Information Sheet: Print Strategy – Update Page 2 Summary, Impact and Preliminary Results Reduction of Standalone Printers • • Standalone printers are associated with higher overall environmental costs vs MFPs. As of September 2023, we have reduced our fleet count from 382 to 146 standalone printers, a reduction of over 60%. Printers removed were handed over to our vendor for environmentally responsible recycling and disposal. Our MFP fleet was increased slightly from (85 to 93) units to accommodate printing workloads redirected from removed standalone printers. The fleet size is now actively managed, where requests for new or replacement printers are now being reviewed and approved only where solid rationale exists (e.g., to address accessibility needs). Staff are currently developing Internal Procedures to streamline and formalize this process. Impact: o An assumed positive energy and financial impact will have resulted from the removal of 60% of our standalone printer fleet, where printing workloads have been diverted to more efficient MFP devices. Part of the Print Strategy is to actively manage this fleet size and continue to make further reductions where they are possible and make sense. o An anticipated negative impact from staff ‘missing’ their classroom or desktop printer has not materialized to a significant, or lasting degree. This may be attributed to attention made towards thoughtful and strategic device count/distribution planning where equity was factored in, stakeholders were consulted, and concerns addressed prior to implementation. Software-Managed Printing, Monitoring and Reporting A new print-management software tool, a key component of the Print Strategy, is now fully deployed in our district. The software provides important features to reduce the energy and environmental costs of printing and copying and to provide valuable and insightful printing and copying statistics. • “Find-Me Printing”: This is a new printing method where a user sends a print job from their computer to a virtual ‘queue’, and then releases their printout at any MFP device they choose to walk up to by entering their unique PIN code at the printer. This feature serves to reduce abandoned or accidental print jobs and increases the security and privacy of confidential or sensitive print content as documents are only released to the MFP in the user’s presence at the printing device. Impact: o Find-Me Printing is the most prominent change experienced by users in the District, as it replaces the traditional methods of remote printing. While the requirement on users to release their print job only when present at the MFP is expected to result in a reduction in wasted printing (from abandoned and accidental print jobs), it’s a new method for users to learn and get used to, and for some, an extra step that is seen as a loss of previously enjoyed convenience. Acceptance has been generally positive for the most part, and significant or widespread push-back has not been observed. Training was provided at every site during implementation and support resources are made easily available to all users. The rationale behind the Print Strategy – its direct connection to the District 's Environmental Stewardship Action Plan – has arguably gone a long way towards user acceptance of the changes. o The new technical environment implementation has been more complex and resource intensive than anticipated, with a small number of unpredictable but significant challenges initially experienced during the rollout. These challenges are mostly resolved and expected to be overcome in the near future. Page 12 of 26 Information Sheet: Print Strategy – Update • Page 3 Energy-efficient Print/Copier options: The print management software has provided the District the ability to reliably apply and manage energy-efficient print and copy configurations across every printing device, such as black-and white and double-sided printing. Realtime data and reports are being made readily available to school, site and department administrators on printer usage and the environmental impact. Individual users have access to their own print and copy usage levels. Impact: o Actionable data and intelligence made readily available to managers and individuals on our print/copy costs, consumption levels and trends are expected to provide the greatest driver towards continued and lasting shifts to changes in behaviour related to our environmentally costly dependence on printing and copying in our district. Countless opportunities for energy and paper reduction initiatives and innovations can be construed at the District, school and workgroup level, with metric goals and objectives now available in real time via the software to measure and celebrate the immediate and long-term impacts of such initiatives. Staff are currently working on the rollout of reporting features of the software and the first goal of creating a set of monthly usage reports that will automatically be distributed to Managers/Principals. The reports will highlight top printers, top users, number of copies broken out by color/grayscale, type of print job, and environmental impact. Page 16 of 26 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 17, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Funding Update Background This Information Sheet provides the Board with an update on the District’s confirmed Operating Grant as announced in December by the Ministry, along with the variance from the information presented in October. As a reminder, each February the District is required to estimate the coming school year’s anticipated enrolment for each of the three 1701 Student Data Collections set by the Ministry of Education and Child Care which take place in September, February, and May per year. Estimates must be calculated for students in regular K-12 programing, distributed (online) learning, alternative programs, continuing education, as well as many additional categories such as students with unique needs, those who require language support, Indigenous ancestry, and others. Final figures are submitted to the Ministry of Education and Child Care and are used to calculate the District’s preliminary annual Operating Grant. Following the September 1701 Student Data Collection, the Board is provided with an update on the District’s actual enrolment as compared to preliminary estimates, as well as the revised Operating Grant amount based on the enrolment variances and the anticipated funding levels. However, at the time of the update to the Board in early October each year the 1701 Data Collection is not officially confirmed by Ministry, further, annual Operating Grants are not finalized and announced by the Ministry of Education and Child Care until the end of December. Discussion In October, preliminary results indicated the District was up 95.858 funded student FTE which provided for an additional $3.28 million in funding. Pursuant to the Ministry of Education and Child Care’s funding announcement in late December, there was a slight reduction to our regular student funded FTE along with some other minor changes in enrolment. Additionally, the supplemental funding categories were confirmed which resulted in an overall variance from initial October estimates of $386K. The majority of this funding relates to our Salary Differential Supplemental category which increased by $491K; this funding is to account for our higher-than-average teacher salaries as compared to other districts in the province.
