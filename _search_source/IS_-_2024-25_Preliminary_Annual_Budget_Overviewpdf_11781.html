DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET April 10, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer and Taunia Sutton, Associate Secretary Treasurer 2024-25 Preliminary Annual Budget Overview Background This information sheet provides an overview as to how the 2024/25 Preliminary Annual Budget has been created, and how it compares to the 2023/24 Amended Annual Budget. The overview provides enrolment, funding, staffing and expense adjustments and assumptions as well as highlights any gaps in information that may remain that could have an impact on the actual preliminary budget. It should be noted at this time that although the district anticipates increased enrolment and we have received funding based on those assumptions, the 2024/25 funding levels provided by Ministry only covered general wage increases and inflationary costs for ongoing required expenses such as benefits, replacement costs, utilities, and contractual obligations. Funding levels did not allow for departmental inflationary increases which means that ongoing resource allocations will need to be realigned for the coming year. Nor did funding provide any resources that allow for a reinvestment of any kind; however, staff anticipate a balanced budget without the need to consider reductions which is a favorable position for the Board to be in. Discussion The 2024/25 Preliminary Annual Budget, which is substantially complete, has been created by taking the 2023/24 Amended Annual Budget that was presented and approved by the Board in February and adjusting it for the coming year. This process includes updating the estimated student enrolments, the revenues that those enrolments generate, making assumptions and estimates on the total revenues and expenses applicable to the coming fiscal year, and updating salary and benefit costs to account for general wage increases. The most notable changes for 2024/25 include: • • • • • • • • • • All items within the 2023-24 Amended Annual budget that were funded by Restricted or Unrestricted Surplus have been removed. The Ministry of Education and Child Care Operating Grant has been updated per projected student enrolment. Any one-time Grants have been removed, along with their associated expenditures. Rental, Lease, and Investment income levels have been adjusted per 2024-25 estimates. International Student Tuition has been adjusted per anticipated enrolment for 2024-25 General Wage Increases have been incorporated (2% in 24/25 with a 1% Cost of Living Adjustment that was confirmed but not yet funded or accounted for). Average Teacher Salaries have also been increased per estimated incremental costs in 24/25. Adjustments to account for estimated benefit and replacement cost have been incorporated. All staffing has been adjusted to remove one-time supports where applicable, and to add the staffing necessary to support the anticipated number of students in 2024/25. Contractual obligations, inflationary cost pressures, and other miscellaneous expenses and revenues have all have been audited and adjusted where necessary. 2024-25 Preliminary Annual Budget Overview Page 2 Operating Grant and District Enrolment: As a reminder to the Board, the district is required to estimate enrolment in advance of the school year in order to allow the Ministry of Education and Child Care to use those estimates to calculate our preliminary Operating Grant. The total estimated change in student enrolment, as compared to the enrolment recorded on September 2023 which our current 2023-24 Amended Annual Budgeted is resourced on, is 233 funded student FTE (rounded up). Our 2024/25 Preliminary Operating Grant has therefore been increased by the 233 FTE, as well as by the per student funding amounts, and supplemental funding categories. General Wage Increases (2%) – Consolidated wage increases which include the 2% negotiated lift, as well as the cost of teacher increments (movement from one salary grid level to another per experience under the BCTF/NDTA contractual language) results in a total financial impact estimated at $2.8 million. As previously noted, this does not yet include the additional 1% Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) that has been announced as we have not received the special grant to cover the additional expense. Once the grant is received, salaries will be adjusted once again and reflected in either the Preliminary Annual Budget if received soon, or the Amended Annual Budget. Teacher Staffing – At the secondary level a significant amount of staffing flowed to schools due to the incoming grade 8 cohort in September 2024 being substantially larger than the exiting grade 12 cohort in June 2024. An additional 13.56 FTE has been added to budget to support the increased secondary enrolment, over the 2023/24 staffing levels. At the Elementary level, estimated student enrolment provided for additional staffing, but in 2023/24 we added 6 co-teaching scenarios to schools due to a lack of space to add official divisions. The complexity here is that we retain the CEF funding, but until we can justify the use of those particular one-time 6 FTE, we must remove them and hold them in reserve. However, due to the space constraint in 2023/24 we identified 2 schools we could place portables on which were Fairview and Mountain View. This has allowed us to add a division to each school for 24/25, and as both divisions are a direct result of classroom composition adherence, we have utilized 2 of the 6 FTE’s held in reserve. The total adjustment at the elementary level is therefore (3.507), but again, these were one-time Co-Teaching positions that were not intended to continue. If we identify similar scenarios in 2024/25, we have the 4FTE in reserve under the CEF and could allocate them to schools in early September if justifiable under the LOU. There were a few other minor shifts and changes that resulted in an additional adjustment of (.209) FTE for a total increase of 9.844 Teacher FTE for the 2024/25 school year. Education Assistant Staffing EA Staffing for the 24/25 school year looks to be reduced by 7.767 FTE, however, as noted on the Summary of Expense Changes, we added $166K for EA staffing adjustments. There are a number of factors that contribute to this anomaly. First, in 23/24 we added 10+FTE of EA Staffing using Restricted Surplus which had to be removed from the 24/25 Preliminary Annual Budget. However, we only reduced our EA Staffing allocation in 2024/25 by 52.5 hours (2 positions), the cost that was previously covered by the restricted surplus has been converted into an ongoing operating cost as the significant increase in student designations is estimated to continue, only slightly reduced in our student enrolment projections. Second, in late 2022/23 we were advised that a significant number of EA’s were being moved to an alternate job description and put through the JJEC review. The outcome of this review moved many EA’s to a higher rate of pay plus benefits, which was not able to be realigned in the budget due to the substantial work involved, this flowed into 2023/24 but has now been corrected for 2024/25 where all the current positions are in budget with the correct rate, and the 72 hours that were added to account for the wage differential have now been removed. Although this was mostly cost neutral, the impact looks to reduce the EA pool by 2.229 FTE, however that is not the case as those hours were never part of the EA Staffing pool. Finally, as the Board is aware the district applied for a Grant through the City of Nanaimo, referred to as the Youth Resilience Grant. The grant flows to the district based on the City of Nanaimo’s fiscal accounting year, which is not aligned with the districts. This, added to the terms of the contract have resulted in a temporary removal of the staffing, in budget only, until the 24/25 funding is 2024-25 Preliminary Annual Budget Overview Page 6 confirmed and can be added in, along with the reinstatement of the staffing. This complexity looks to remove 1.886 FTE, however again this is not a removal, the staff remain, it is a temporary budgeting scenario only. The decrease per the estimated enrolment in 24/25 was only 1.966 FTE and this will likely be added back in the fall if student designations exceed our very careful estimates. In addition, in 2023/24 there was a Pilot program that utilized a 1.0 of Speech Language Pathologist FTE that was unable to be filled, it was converted to 2 Inclusion Support Workers, but this was a one-time program that has now reverted. A couple other shifts and changes pertain to Special Purpose Funds where the CYFSW under the Mental Health Grant has been removed due to program shifts, a new School Age Child Care Space Expansion Grant has been provided to the district for the next two years which we have added some EA/RA FTE into in anticipation of future costs, however, we are still in the planning stages in regards to this Fund so the EA FTE may or may not be applicable. We did increase our district’s child care EA time to align with current operations, and we increased the Feeding Futures Fund program by adding a cafeteria at Wellington, along with some Cafeteria Attendant time. The other staffing categories saw little change, which is summarized below: Under the Administrators category, due to enrolment estimates, at the elementary level there are two less Vice Principals in 2024/25. Enrolment in the two schools dropped below the trigger for a VP in the 23/24 school year, however we have historically left the VP in place for the following year to allow for transition and to ensure the school enrolment is lower on a consistent basis which was the case. The two schools are North Oyster Elementary and Syuwen’ct, note that the VP teaching time reverts to the NDTA Teaching pool. An additional adjustment was to add a VP into Rock City for the 2024/25 school year in anticipation of the reopening of Rutherford Elementary, in order to allow the newly designated Principal the time necessary to support the opening within the community as well as to allow for succession planning at Rock City. Under the Excluded staffing category there was only one change to note, and this relates to the new School Age Child Care Space Expansion Grant where preliminary planning has accounted for a Business Manager, a CUPE Finance Assistant and the aforementioned additional EA/RA time to cover district expenses that may not be covered by total fees collected. Besides the additional Finance Assistant that we believe is necessary to support the childcare space expansion, the other notable change in Support Staff is the transfer of the BCTEA Transportation Routes staffing and expenses into the Operating Fund on a temporary basis, until the 24/25 BCTEA funding is confirmed and approved where at such time the staffing and expense will be transferred back to the BCTEA Special Purpose Fund. First, the district is in the process of filling the 2024/25 Leadership role for the Indigenous Ed Program. Once new leadership has been established any changes if any, will be brought forward for discussion. 2024-25 Preliminary Annual Budget Overview Page 8 The second relates to Bill 40 which came into effect in November of 2023; referred to as the School Amendment Act. Under the Act, districts across the province are to establish and maintain an Indigenous Education Council (IEC) which acts in an advisory role in regards to the Indigenous Education Target including financial oversight and educational programming. To support IEC’s the Ministry of Education and Child Care has added a funding supplement within our Operating Grant which is specifically targeted to the function of the Council. As this funding is new and our current Learning for Leaders Partner Group will need to shift to the new structure, the budget for the IEC has been placed into secretariate related expense accounts such as Mileage, Travel, and Professional Services, etc. Adjustments can be made in alignment with the Councils structure as expenses are identified. Pending Although the Preliminary Annual Budget is substantially complete with regards to estimated revenues and expenses, our District Learning Services Team is still finalizing their 2024/25 Operational Plan. Any net-zero reorganizations will be incorporated into the final Preliminary Annual Budget Presentation. The 1% Cost of Living Adjustment may or may not be incorporated into the Preliminary Annual Budget, we are awaiting funding from the Ministry, once received all staffing will be updated accordingly. Summary Overall, on a very preliminary basis the district is in a favorable Budget position for the coming 2024/25 school year. Although funding covered the districts basic operational and obligatory inflationary costs, it did not provide additional resources to address departmental cost pressures which means allocations are status quo for the coming year, and budget managers will need to realign their resources and be fiscally prudent. Finally, although the budget is balanced and the district is not faced with making any cuts to controls costs, there is no opportunity to reinvest. Due to continued growth however, we are able to maintain all the staffing and programming in support of our students.
