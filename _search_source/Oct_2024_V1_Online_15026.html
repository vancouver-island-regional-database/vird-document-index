When to Call 911: *9-1-1 is for police, fire or medical emergencies when immediate action is required: someone’s health, safety or property is in jeopardy or a crime is in progress. Examples of When to Call 9-1-1: • Events that involve an immediate threat to a person or property: screams, attacks, gunshots, fires, car accidents with injuries or any other medical emergency • A crime in progress. This includes fights, break and enters (if there is a suspect on scene) or a report of an impaired driver • A serious crime that has just occurred (examples: sexual assault or robbery) • A suspicious circumstance that may indicate an imminent criminal act (examples: prowler, vandal) • You need a Police Officer right now! When to call the RCMP Non-Emergency line: #250-245-2215 • An incident which is not an immediate threat to safety of a person, or an incident which has been discovered after the fact: • Examples: theft, theft from vehicle, traffic complaints, damage to property, fraud complaints, drug complaints etc…. • All complaints should be called in right away when they are occurring so the police can properly follow up and address the issue while it is happening. • Calling in right away to police creates a record of the incident which assists police, property managers and Chief & Council Community Liaison When to contact the RCMP Community Liaison or Band Office: • Report all incidents to the police first, when they are occurring. • RCMP Community Liaison is not meant to replace calls for responsive or investigative matters. Some calls need immediate response and shouldn’t wait until the Liaison Officer is available. Your Liaison is sometimes away from the office for periods of time and we do not want a serious matter to go unreported. • If you need police in the community, call 9-1-1 or the general complaint number at #250-245-2215, if the Liaison is on duty, he will be advised. • It is a good idea to follow up after any incident and inform the RCMP Liaison and the Band Office staff to ensure they are advised and aware of your issue. Updated September 24, 2024 PAGE 15 MID-ISLE soccer club Schedule PAGE 16 Tim Harris Joins the SD68 School Board of Trustees Tim Harris has secured a seat on the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School Board following a byelection held on Saturday, September 14. Harris garnered 594 votes, while his opponent, Joan Brown, received 224. According to the district, less than one percent of eligible voters participated in the election. At 50, Harris brings extensive experience as a former education assistant, teacher, and principal. He also served as an elected council member for the Stz’uminus First Nation for 17 years. Expressing his surprise at the outcome, Harris said, “It’s my first time running in something like this, so I guess I was surprised because you never know until the votes and numbers come in.” The seat became vacant in May when Charlene McKay resigned, citing harassment and passive-aggressiveness within the board. Harris expressed his excitement at the opportunity to collaborate with the current board, stating, “I’m eager to roll up my sleeves, get in the trenches with this team. It’s a strong group, and I’m ready for the challenge.” Harris will be sworn in as the ninth member of the school board later this week. Likewise, the benefits are also evident in participation in the arts and culture world. The KL Foundation is committed to helping offset the costs of registration in a sport, recreation or a culture program to ensure children from our Member Nations can participate. The grant is to a maximum of $1000 per child. You may apply for multiple children in the same household. Distribution of awards Due to increasing interest in this grant priority may be given to first time applicants. Depending on funds available, we will review remaining grants. Wherever possible, awards will be distributed by way of a direct payment from the Foundation to the organization providing the service. If the costs have already been paid by the applicant, the Foundation may refund the applicant directly upon proof of payment and confirmation of enrollment. Awards will not be paid to the recipients directly without adequate proof of enrollment and attendance. Eligibility In order to be eligible, you must be a member of one of our 9 Member Nations ( Halalt, Lyackson, Málexeł, Penelakut, Qualicum, Snaw-naw-as, Snuneymuxw, Stz’uminus and Ts’uubaa-asatx).
