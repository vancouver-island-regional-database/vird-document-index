QUESTION PERIOD There were 5 questions this evening: Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held September 12, 2018 G. Keller: What is the total cost of the Frank Ney Addition and specifically how did the School District fund the construction of the addition? Specifically, I would like to know the value of the request made to MOE for the capital project, the amount of new capital funding approved by MOE, and where the balance of the funding came. Response by Chair Kimler: A portion of your question was answered by P. Sabo's presentation. The original cost was forecast to be approximately $5 million. The PIR (Project Identification Report) that was submitted to the Ministry of Education would have been for the full estimated cost of construction. In essence, the "ask" is always for the full amount and then the MoE decides how much (if any at all) they are willing to contribute - after vetting the business case (which in this case would have been, "We need X amount of money to refurbish Rutherford Elementary, or we need $5 million to build a new wing at FJN"). The ministry approved the business case but had already spent its capital funding allocation. It is my understanding that the MoE funding of FJN was unusual in that they reallocated contingency funding left over from the Wellington Secondary Seismic upgrade project and channeled it directly to the FJN expansion. While I was disappointed that they didn't fund a greater share, I understood they wanted to, but could not. I took it as a positive sign that they approved the business plan and cut through red tape to work with our school district. The district funded the remainder of the project from Local Capital (e.g., money from the sale of the Rotary Bowl & Serauxmen Stadium is put into Local Capital and used for expansion, retrofits, maintenance or other capital projects as recommended and voted on by the school board). For clarity, it is our understanding that had we not put the ask forward to the MoE, the left over funds from the Wellington Seismic upgrade would have gone back to the MoE and distributed to another districts. In our view that was the MoE's contribution to the project. G. TL Saunders: Has anyone researched the rationale for the Trustee Remuneration siting that every September the Board was to receive an increase based on the Vancouver CPI? What was the result? Who was consulted? Secretary-Treasurer McVeigh responded: It was last year, 2017, that an in depth report was provided to the Board of Education providing some comparisons to other school districts and revisiting whether or not the Board wanted to compare themselves to other school districts in terms of compensation using an average of like sized districts or to continue with the policy to increase by the CPI. In an open Board Meeting, made the decision to stay with the CPI. Trustee Higginson further explained that this Board of Education felt that the increase happening regularly was not transparent and it was voted at a Board meeting to make it a transparent process each year.
