NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 2, 2021 Education Committee Superintendent, Scott Saywell Secondary Semester System for 2021/2022 Background During the 2019-2020 school year, our secondary schools were organized in a semester system. Students would take 4 courses from September through January, then another 4 courses from February through June. For the 2020-2021 school year, secondary schools organized in a quarter system, with students taking 2 courses for 10 weeks, for four “quarters” between September and June. The quarter system was necessary to comply with guidelines established by the Ministry of Education’s Restart Plan. In February, parents, students, and NLPS staff were invited to respond to a survey about the quarter system. School staff also engaged in conversations about the benefits and challenges of teaching and learning in a quarter system. Both positive and negative themes emerged from the surveys. Some of the challenges identified were related to the limitations related to scheduling students into “learning group” cohorts of 120 or less. This limited course selections and peer interactions. The quarter system required students to complete each course within a 10-week period. This shortened time frame, accompanied by longer classes every day were identified by students, staff, and parents as being problematic. On the other hand, the longer class blocks provided opportunities for students and staff to build stronger relationships while offering more intense and sustained engagement with the curriculum. Throughout February, March, April, and the beginning of May, school staff prepared to continue with the quarter system for 2021-2022. The Ministry guidelines had not changed, quarter system timetables were built, and administration provided tentative teaching assignments to staff. Information Sheet: Secondary Semester System for 2021/2022 Page 2 Schedule changes for 2021-2022 On Tuesday, May 18th, with encouraging announcements regarding the provincial vaccine program, the superintendent met with secondary principals and vice principals and gave the goahead for schools to move away from organizing timetables in learning groups. Secondary school administration worked collaboratively with school staff committees to create a timetable system for 2021-2022 that would address the concerns raised regarding the quarter system, while acknowledging the very short timeline available to implement any changes for the coming school year. Consideration was given to unique school community needs, issues related to human resources, and student programming needs, both within secondary schools and for districtbased programs. For 2021-2022 secondary schools will be using a semester system, with students taking four courses from September to January, and another four courses from February to June. Cedar Community School and Ladysmith Secondary School will be reverting to timetable schedules that they have used in previous years. For John Barsby Community School, Nanaimo District Secondary School, Wellington Secondary School, and Dover Bay Secondary School, a common weekly schedule will be implemented. This timetable will see students attending four classes per day on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. On Tuesday and Thursday, students will attend two extended “double-block” classes. The combination of shorter blocks and longer blocks during the 2021-2022 year will allow teachers and students to further explore the longer learning periods, without the limitations of the learning group model, or the other limiting factors associated with the COVID-19 restrictions. Future Considerations Each secondary school will use the 2021-2022 school year to engage their school community in a fulsome review of timetable issues so that they will be able to create a timetable for the 2022-2023 year that further meets the unique needs of their learning community.
