NEW BUSINESS Update on the Wellington Seismic Project Mr. Dalrymple, Energy and Capital Projects Manager, told the Committee that the Wellington Seismic project is proceeding in stages and said this will allow the school to continue to operate while the project is underway. Mr. Dalrymple said that work is currently underway on Blocks G and F, and that the work on those two blocks is expected to be completed by June 2015. As soon as those two blocks are completed, Block A will be demolished and a new entrance to the school will be built. Mr. Dalrmplye said some of the major work that was carried out this summer included the use of a giant crane to remove the roof and supports in Block F. Update on the Cedar Field Project Mr. Brent Spohn, Facilities Planning Supervisor, outlined the site and playfield work that was done during the summer as part of the conversion of Cedar Secondary into an elementary school. Mr. Spohn said that the work is on time and on budget. The playfield has now been seeded, and the project is expected to be complete by the end of October. Update on Summer Facilities' Work Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations, said that picketing during the summer affected a Page 4 of 37 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held September 10, 2014 number of planned maintenance projects. Mr. Sabo said the Facilities Department is working with the Learning Services Department to develop a priority list for the order in which projects will be completed once the strike is resolved. One of the impacts is that the district is spending its Annual Facilities Grant for about 10 FTE, without any work on the AFG projects being accomplished. June 2014 letter from Stephanie Higginson referred to the Business Committee Meeting The Business Committee reviewed Ms. Higginson's June correspondence to the Board, in which she asked the Board to consider alternatives to the project to convert the closed Cedar Secondary building into an elementary school. Trustees asked staff a number of questions regarding the current plan. Staff outlined why the plan approved last year remains the most suitable plan for Cedar schools. Sincerely, Jason Reid, CPA, CA Executive Director JR/mr Enclosures Copy: David Hutchinson, Superintendent of Schools Dot Neary, Chair, Board of Education Page 33 of 37 11.4 SCHOOL DISTRICT NO.68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) School District Budget Preparation and Monitoring and Control of Expenditures Appendix 1: Audit Synopsis Audit Purpose and Scope To examine the development of budgets, the monitoring and control of expenditures in a sample of school districts, and related oversight and monitoring by the Ministry of Education. The audit will examine the existence and design of these processes and controls, but will not conclude on their overall effectiveness. Four school districts were selected as samples, in addition to the Ministry of Education:     SD08 – Kootenay Lake; SD38 – Richmond; SD61 – Greater Victoria; and SD68 – Nanaimo- Ladysmith. Since budgeting processes vary by district size and complexity, the sample reflects districts with a range of budget sizes and geographical areas. A key objective is to develop and report on good practice principles that all school districts can benefit from learning about. Audit Objectives and Criteria The following objectives and criteria will be examined in the audit. Objective 1 – Does the school district follow good practice in the development and communication of its strategic plan? Criteria 1.1 The school district monitors the needs of the community and changes in the environment. 1.2 The school district identifies its opportunities and challenges. 1.3 The school district develops and documents its strategies to achieve its goals. 1.4 The school district communicates its strategic plan to stakeholders. 1.5 The school district approves and adopts its strategic plan. Page 34 of 37 11.4 SCHOOL DISTRICT NO.68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) School District Budget Preparation and Monitoring and Control of Expenditures Appendix 1: Audit Synopsis Objective 2 – Does the school district follow good practice in the development of its budget? Criteria 2.1 There is a formalized and documented process, with clear responsibilities, for preparing and adopting the annual budget. 2.2 Budgets are supported by operational plans, which include evaluation of significant cost drivers, financial options, and assumptions. 2.3 The school district communicates significant decisions to key stakeholders in a timely way. 2.4 The approved school district budget reconciles to all approved responsibility center budgets and there are appropriate processes and controls in place to enable the creation of a budget that incorporates realistic assumptions and is accurate and complete. Objective 3 – Does the school district regularly monitor its actual and forecasted costs to identify significant risks, required adjustments, and prevent unapproved budget deviations? Criteria 3.1 Responsibilities for budget compliance, monitoring and reporting are clear and align with component (responsibility centre) budgets. 3.2 Managers and finance staff receive appropriate training, and their performance is monitored. 3.3 Timely information on actual costs compared to budget is available to responsibility center managers and executive management. 3.4 Information on actual costs compared to budget is monitored regularly by responsibility centre managers and executive management. 3.5 For each responsibility centre, actual and forecasted costs, along with explanations for expected or actual variances and identification of risks and recommended adjustments, are reported regularly to executive management. 3.6 Consolidated actual and forecasted costs, along with explanations for variances and identification of risks and recommended adjustments, are reported regularly to the Board of Education. 3.7 Required adjustments to operational plans are identified and approved. Significant adjustments are approved by the Board of Education. 3.8 Significant decisions are communicated to stakeholders. 3.9 Financial reporting requirements to the Ministry of Education are met (including budgets, quarterly reporting, and financial statements). Ministry reporting is reviewed and approved, and is consistent with school district internal reporting. Page 35 of 37 11.4 SCHOOL DISTRICT NO.68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) School District Budget Preparation and Monitoring and Control of Expenditures Appendix 1: Audit Synopsis Objective 4 – Are there appropriate controls in place to manage financial operations in accordance with the approved budget? Criteria 4.1 Expected processes and controls are in place to enable the creation of accurate and complete reporting of actual and forecasted results (as detailed under objective 3). 4.2 Appropriate controls exist over events with significant budgetary impact. 4.3 Appropriate controls exist over expenditure processing. 4.4 Systems or other controls appropriately limit expenditure authorities. 4.5 There are adequate segregation of duties. Objective 5 – Does the Ministry of Education provide clear expectations, guidance, and monitor school district performance? Criteria 5.1 The Ministry provides school districts with clear expectations with respect to budgeting and reporting on financial performance. 5.2 The Ministry has appropriate monitoring and enforcement processes in place to achieve its objective of ensuring school districts comply with budget requirements. Audit Approach Evidence Gathering Evidence used to support our conclusions against the objectives and criteria will include interviews with Ministry and school districts’ staff. We will also review documentation requested during the audit, which includes, but is not limited to: relevant policies and procedures; internal and external communications; and financial records and information.
