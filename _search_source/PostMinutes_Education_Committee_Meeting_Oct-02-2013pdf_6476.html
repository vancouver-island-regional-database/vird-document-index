Scott Saywell, Assistant Superintendent replaced John Blain this evening. He gave the Committee a brief update on the September 30th Pro D event. Jacquie Poulin, Principal, Forest Park, gave a presentation about comprehensive school health. She said that concept of comprehensive school health is based on the understanding that healthy young people learn better and that schools can directly influence students’ health and behavior. The pillars of comprehensive school health are: • Social and physical environment • Teaching and learning • Partnerships and services • Healthy school policy In partnership with Pacific Sport, the district offers an after-school sport and art program to provide opportunities for students facing barriers to sport or art participation. Pacific Sport received a grant, along with support from the school district, to offer the Physical Literacy Mentorship Project in the spring of 2013 and the project will be offered again this school year. The City of Nanaimo has applied for a grant for the Swim to Survive program for grade 3 students. Vancouver Island Symphony Grade 4 Project Peter Hewitt, Principal of Ecole Quarterway Elementary, made a presentation about the Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held October 2, 2013 Vancouver Island Symphony Grade 4 project. The Symphony will present the Education Concert Series to all Grade 4 students in January. The Education Concert Series is a three-year partnership with the Vancouver Island Symphony. NEW BUSINESS Canada Learning Bond Trustee Robinson told the Committee that the $500 Canada Learning Bond is offered by the Government of Canada to help parents start saving for their child’s education after high school. In addition to the initial $500, parents can receive $100 every year until the child turns 15 years old to a maximum of $2,000. To be eligible, the child’s family must receive the National Child Benefit Supplement and open a RESP for the child.
