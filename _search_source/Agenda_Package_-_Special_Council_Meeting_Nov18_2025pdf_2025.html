EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Following the direction from Council at the November 4, 2025 budget meeting, staff contacted GFL to negotiate the contract back to the 77L garbage bin size. The contractor had already started moving forward with organizing personnel and equipment based on the 125L bins and has concerns with compliance with the smaller bins. As a result, the Contractor proposes keeping the first year at the quoted amount of $695,607 and reducing it to $462,837 in the following 4 years if the Town supplies bins for automated collection. Page 3 of 5 INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: The contractor’s new proposal is to collect existing bins up to 125L and 20kg until the end of 2026 at an annual cost of $695,607. The Town would purchase 3 new 125L bins for each residence during 2026: one each for garbage, organics and recycling. The new bins would be wheeled 125L units and compatible with automated collection trucks. On January 1, 2027 the contractor would start automated collection, and the annual cost would decrease to $462,837. To aid in the decision-making process staff have prepared a summary of considerations below: Advantages of Proposal:  Customers not required to downsize existing bins in 2026  Customers enjoy increased capacity for garbage, organic waste and recycling bins in 2027  Automated collection beginning in 2027, at a time when many neighbouring jurisdictions have gone to automated collection  All customers will have wheeled bins which are easier to move  All customers will have the same bins, making collection more equitable  Automated bins are estimated to have 15-year lifespans, so the Town can continue with same bins for two subsequent contracts from 2031-2035 and 2036-2040. Disadvantages of Proposal:  Collection volumes will increase  Costs increase from 2025 rates. Based on the above considerations, staff recommend acceptance of the contractor’s latest proposal. ALTERNATIVES: There are very few alternatives as the existing contract expires December 31, 2025. 1. Council could direct staff to include a $6.75 increase for the manual 125L bins as proposed at the November 4, 2025 meeting; or 2. The next lowest bid was $812,286, which would result in a $23.50 monthly charge. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: As presented in the budget meeting on November 4, 2025, the cost to recoup the revised $695,607 contract would see an increase of $6.75 per month, resulting in a monthly charge of $20.75 ($249 annually) to the solid waste, organics and recycling charges. Even based on the original base bid, the monthly charge would have increased at a minimum of $1.25 per month, resulting in a monthly charge of $15.25 ($183 annually). As the counteroffer by the Contractor retains the first year at the $695,607 amount and requires the Town to purchase bins, the cost will be greater than $15.25. There is roughly $1.29 million in the solid waste reserve earmarked for the eventual purchase of automated bins. There is still another $1.11 million required to fund the Page 4 of 5 remainder of the purchase. Included in the 2025 budget were funds of $965,570 for the borrowing on a new City Hall building. This funding could instead be used towards the capital purchase of bins, leaving $144,000 of funding from other internal purposes. Staff are recommending an increase of $3.50, resulting in a monthly charge of $17.50 ($210 annually). The first year would see a loss of approximately $135,000, which would be subsidized by other reserves (or taxation), though the savings over the remaining 4 years would pay back the first-year loss as well as the shortfall for the capital purchase.
