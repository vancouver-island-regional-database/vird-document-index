Reaction to Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Ecole Hammond Bay School PAC Jennifer Therriault, PAC Chair November 4, 2015 Overcapacity with Undersized Gym 158% capacity utilization for 2015-2016 school year (354 students) Gym still has maximum capacity of 312 Gym remains inadequate and unsafe Before proceeding with new building in the district, we ask the board to honour the commitments made to our students at EHB. Future Planning PAC is looking to fundraise to make improvements to the playgrounds (also terribly inadequate for the size of school) and we need to make decisions around locating those items. Thank you for the commitment of $25 000 to further study the site this year. We are anticipating the follow-through on this commitment. We also appreciate the inclusion of Hammond Bay in the 5-year Capital Plan and look forward to working with the district to see this to fruition. Considerations re: moving the district French Immersion program from NDSS transit - the program is currently accessible from all parts of the district via well-established city bus routes that are timely with minimal transfers and still many students are in transit to and from school for 1.5 to 2.5 hours a day retention - will the difficulties around transit create a retention issue? staffing - the district has a hard time attracting qualified French Immersion teachers and there is concern that attrition might result in the fantastic teachers we have losing their positions Considerations re: moving the district French Immersion program from NDSS (continued) loss of funding to the district should attrition from the program occur lost opportunities for French Immersion students to participate in Sports Academies if the programs are not housed in the same location Suggestions for consideration re: declining enrollment and French Immersion Programming People are looking for alternatives to traditional programming as evidenced by the success of the Ecoschool and the growth in private schools, school districts need to be proactive in attracting and retaining students. Remove caps on elementary enrollment in French Immersion Ensure stability and growth of the program Quote from the Ministry of Education Recruitment, integration and retention of students are essential to meeting the overall objectives of the Protocol. Given projections for further decline in the school age population, the greatest challenge will be to sustain, and hopefully increase, the number of students in both French minority and French second language programs by 2017–2018. - Ministry of Education, French Funding Guide Reasons for considering the move of district programs We have been told that there is room at NDSS to house the current programs as well as any influx of students from a potential closure of Woodlands. The board needs to address capacity underutilization at Barsby but we feel strongly that the underutilization has not been measured accurately as only projected data has been used, not real data - especially in terms of enrollment for 2016. If the real-life data show a capacity issue at Barsby, perhaps the discussion needs to be one of future planning, not a reactionary approach to a potential problem. Additionally, there can be a redistribution of district programming from any closed schools to help alleviate the capacity underutilization. Misconceptions around French Immersion French Immersion is not an elite program. In fact, over the past couple of years, the District has acted to equalize access to the program by creating the lottery system for registration. We have students in every Immersion school from all parts of the district and from all backgrounds. The one thing that I can guarantee is that French Immersion parents have a passion for their children’s best interests and education. The overwhelming majority of parents I have spoken with agree that this is not an issue of Barsby vs NDSS. It is an issue of the necessity of uprooting a well-established, successful program with adequate transportation options when there may be other solutions to the capacity issue. Closing Remarks Given the declining facilities, underutilization issues, and budget shortfalls, we understand the need to examine all possible scenarios to reduce those issues. The secondary French Immersion program does not exist in a void. The students are fully integrated into every part of NDSS - from their English courses and electives to their participation on sports teams and other extracurricular activities. Questioning the merit and validity of the concerns of the French Immersion community differently than any other group undermines the entire consultation process and I ask the Board not to fall into this behaviour.
