Ed Chief Stz’uminus First Nation 05 Notice of nomination meeting Notice is hereby given that a meeting of the Electors of the Stz’uminus First Nation will be held at the Stz’uminus First Nation Elders Centre, 3945A Shell Beach Road, Ladysmith, B.C. on Thursday, March 2, 2023, beginning at 3:00 PM and lasting for at least three hours, for the purpose of nominating candidates for the positions of Chief and Councillors on the Band Council of the Stz’uminus First Nation for the next ensuing term. There are nine (9) Councillor positions and one (1) position for Chief available. The election will be held at the Stz’uminus First Nation Community Centre, 3949 Shell Beach Road, Ladysmith, B.C. on Wednesday, April 5, 2023 Please note that any Elector may nominate candidates by using a mail-in nomination form. Electors residing off-reserve may nominate or run as a candidate for the position of Chief and Council. You can either deliver or mail-in written nomination forms and completed, signed and witnessed voter declaration forms to the electoral officer before the time set for the nomination meeting OR you may nominate or second candidates orally at the nomination meeting. Mailed nominations not received by the electoral officer before the time set for the nomination meeting are void. Also note that any voter may vote by mail-in ballot. We have staff/students with allergies and asthma. 08 library partnership Stz’uminus Community Schools Library partners with Vancouver Island Regional Library We are partnering with our local public library, Vancouver Island Regional Library, to get our students free public library cards. With a free public library card, students can access a wide collection of books, DVDs, video games, eBooks and Audiobooks. Students over the age of 13 can sign up on their own through the school library. For students 12 and under, a parent or guardian can complete the library card registration form. Return the completed form to your child’s teacher. Public library cards will then be handed out to students by the school library. To make it even easier for students to access the public library, public library books for students will be delivered directly to the school! Students with a public library card can place holds on items through the VIRL website, www.virl.bc.ca, and then pick them up at the school library. These items can also be returned to the school library, which will deliver them back to the public library. Books will be exchanged between the school library and the public library every two weeks. There are no late fees on public library books, so students can return books to the school library without worrying that the books will be overdue. If this system works well, we hope in the future to enable community members to also pick up public library books at the school. The staff of the Indigenous Employment Hub looks forward to connecting with you soon to support you in your next steps in Lifelong Learning! 12 Updates from the resource team Uy skweyul mukw lhwet! The Resource Team is super busy these days. We’ve been doing a lot of grant writing, interim reports, and new applications. We are all happy when these are complete, as it lets us get back to doing what we really love, working with the language! Our tech teams continues’ to work on translating and recording lessons 1-8 of the First Nations Language Essentials ASKME Book One. These lessons include a lot of TPR (total physical response), which increases language understanding primarily in the JK/K immersion room. Roxy also uses a lot of TPR in her language classes as well. We are also continuing on with our weekly translation meetings with the elders. They are supporting the work being done with the Hulq’umi’num Vocabulary Level 2 Curriculum. We have also been spending some time lately talking about cultural themes and lessons associated with the cultural calendar. We plan on further developing these words, phrases, and lesson ideas for next year. Here are a few words and phrases for February: February – mim’ne’ (Baby Month) Valentine’s Day - mulintu (pull) weeds – squl’wey’ bee – sum’suma’yu herring roe – ts’umush gathering – ‘aluxut This is me and my friend. - “e’et te i nu sii ye yu”. “Can I have a hug?” - tthixwum kwunustul? We are always excited to see more elders coming into our buildings.
