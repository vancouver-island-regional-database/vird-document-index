NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 19, 2019 Board of Education Carrie McVeigh, Secretary Treasurer Strategic Plan Recommendations: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), refer the amendments to Board Policy, Section 1: Foundation and Direction - 1.1 Mandate & 1.2 Strategic Plan and Section 2: Governance and Management - 2.1 Role of the Board to the Policy Committee for recommendations. That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), pursuant to Section 1: Board Governance - 2.7 Policy Development, engage in a public process of consultation to obtain feedback on the new draft Strategic Plan. Background: At the June 12, 2019, Business Committee meeting, the Business Committee passed the following motions: That the Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), refer the amendments to Board Policy, Section 1: Foundation and Direction - 1.1 Mandate & 1.2 Strategic Plan and Section 2: Governance and Management - 2.1 Role of the Board to the Policy Committee for recommendations. That the Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), pursuant to Section 1: Board Governance 2.7 Policy Development, engage in a public process of consultation to obtain feedback on the new draft Strategic Plan On November 28, 2018, the Board of Education agreed to update the district’s Strategic Plan. The primary purpose of the strategic plan is to create a blueprint for the district that provides direction to the system, maintains its exemplary qualities and supports the continuous improvement of student learning. An updated Strategic Plan serves to organize and ground the district around a few significant goals and ensures that the necessary resources are in place to successfully implement the goals in a manner that is sustainable over time. The Strategic Plan assists the Superintendent in establishing an Operational Plan that will include strategies and action items aligned to the district’s overall direction. The Operational plan will be put in place by September for the start of the school year and will be periodically reported out to the Board of Education and shared with the public to demonstrate progress against the goals and objectives that are outlined in this plan. In essence, the Operational Plan operationalizes and implements the strategic direction set by the Board of Education through its Strategic Plan. The plan was developed by completing an Environmental Scan, SWOT analysis and extensive discussion with Trustees and Senior Staff, including a number of strategic planning workshops, during the period from January – April 2019. In addition, public input was sought through a Board hosted Open House and Thought Exchange survey. The Board of Education has committed publicly to releasing the draft Strategic Plan for public feedback before finalizing the plan. Board policy changes are consequently required to remove references to the previous Board’s strategic plan; to establish governance expectations through the establishment of a new Strategic Plan Policy; and to clarify the Roles and Responsibilities of the Board of Education and the Superintendent to develop, implement and monitor the Strategic Plan.
