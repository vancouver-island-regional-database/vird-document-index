NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 4, 2020 Business Committee Pete Sabo, Executive Director Planning and Operations Brian Hackwood, Assistant Director Planning and Operations NLPS Snow Removal Background    Yearly beginning late September and ending late March, NLPS Facilities staff plan for the possibility of snow events Approximately 15 pieces of equipment, $7,000 of road salt and $27,000 of sidewalk deicer are prepared and purchased for what is, on average, 10 event days per year (CON report 26 August 2019) o Funding for these supplies are diverted from Operating supplies Labour for 2019/20 snow events and black ice approximately $50,000 o Labour is diverted from Operating ie Carpentry, Plumbing, Electrical, Grounds, etc. Discussion       Senior management with assistance from the Transportation Manager and Maintenance Manager determine school closures due to inclement weather For 2019/20, all maintenance and custodial staff deemed essential service for the purpose of snow clearing Priority of snow removal is as follows: 1. Plowing, salting/sanding of bus drop off, staff/student parking 2. Removal of snow from interior sidewalks, ramps, main entrances and exits 3. City sidewalks Crews are dispatched as soon as a snow event begins and continue to concentrate on removal in priority order until the event ends Commencing July 1, 2020, custodian staff now participate as essential service for snow removal. o Staff split into Day 1 and Day 2 o Additional staff has allowed for earlier start to City sidewalk clearing Minimal complaints received following the 2 day snow event (Jan 14,15)
