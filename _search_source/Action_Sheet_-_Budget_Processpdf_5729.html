Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: March 11, 2015 TO: Board of Education FROM: A/Secretary-Treasurer, Graham Roberts SUBJECT: Budget Process Background: Budget Process Currently, Your Voice 2015 is collecting community input to help the Board with budget allocation decisions. Your Voice 2015 will reflect the communities’ values which will be connected with the Board’s goals. The Board, to the best extent possible, will align these goals to allocate its limited resources with its 2015/16 budget. Board Goals/Direction (Per Policy) 1.2 Vision Success for All 1.3 Mission Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools, together with families and communities, enable all students to become life-long learners and productive, caring citizens by providing inclusive, challenging and engaging educational opportunities through the continuous improvement of instruction and supports for learning. 1.4 Values In the pursuit of each student’s learning success and well-being Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools value:  High ethical standards and mutual respect.  Collaborative planning, committed action, accountability for results, and celebration of success.  Diversity of opinion, creativity and innovation.  Student-centered, principled and informed decision-making.  Compassionate, inclusive, safe and caring schools.    Engaging learning environments that are responsive to unique student strengths and capabilities. The courage and conviction to achieve our goals. Fair and inclusive education policies, instructional practices and allocation of resources. 1.6 District Goals 1. Meet Each Student’s Unique Needs - Possible Critical Outcomes         Implementation of the Response to Intervention model (RTI) throughout the district. A greater understanding of universal tier one instructional strategies oriented to the development of self-regulated learning. Personalized learning (with an emphasis on flexibility and choice). Higher Aboriginal student academic achievement and graduation rates. Curriculum and instructional improvements reflective of indigenous pedagogy; Engaged parents who support their children’s learning. Learning empowered by technology. A high school renewal plan that takes into account such ideas as a campus model, interdisciplinary project-based learning, community apprenticeships, and other related 21st century learning strategies. 2. The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment – Possible Critical Outcomes       Board of Education and Senior Administration accountability for achieving District goals through the establishment of a performance management model. An organizational culture focused on and committed to Board Values, strategic priorities, and learning excellence. System-wide goals for student achievement addressed in the Achievement Contract and monitored annually. Comprehensive, accurate, District-wide data used fundamentally to strengthen each student’s achievement. A K-12 literacy improvement initiative. A leadership development model that supports, and helps create a culture around, high academic achievement and performance management. 3. Enhanced Facilities for Learning - Possible Critical Outcomes    Innovative school models, with a focus on shared facilities, community partnerships, and on-line learning. Determination of the optimal size for elementary and secondary schools from an educational standpoint in order to provide equity of access to high-quality programs and services across the district –including the regular instructional program as well as French Immersion, and Aboriginal programs. A ten year plan to provide school facilities of the optimal size that reflects the responsible stewardship of District assets and also helps address facilities condition improvements. Further Action Plan Budget Time Line (See Appendix A – Budget Schedule for the Preparation of the 2015/2016 Budget) Budget Meeting 1 (March 31, 2015) The intent of this meeting is to summarize information from Your Voice 2015, as it relates to the 2015/2016 Budget. Followed by this, will be information on our non-staff costs and how they compare to similar districts. This will help clarify if we are allocating our resources based on District goals. Outcomes will help the Budget Committee develop recommendations. Budget Meeting 2 (April 7, 2015) This meeting will look at our staff costs and how they compare to similar districts with the intent to determine if we are allocating our resources based on District goals. Outcomes will help the Budget Committee develop recommendations. Budget Meeting 3 (April 14, 2015) This meeting will summarize areas where we would like to invest more resources and where reductions will be considered. This will also be an opportunity for the Board to provided direction to staff as they develop a balanced budget. Budget Draft 1 (April 21, 2015) High-level descriptions of how budgeted items align with Board Goals. Narratives will describe what goal is being supported, how we plan to do it and how success will be measured. Information will be structured by FTE and release days and $ for professional fees, travel/meals, and supplies to provide a high level understanding of the budget. This budget will be structured as a new starting point, where budgets are built with Board Goals in mind, not cuts from the status quo. Budget Draft 2 (April 28, 2015) Full budget will be presented to the Board. Options/recommendation may be presented and further decisions may need to be made. Budget Final (April 30, 2015) Balanced budget presented to be adopted May 5, 2015.
