CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Higginson: JBCS  Parent Prom: Prom hosted by parents raised enough extra money for the students to present $500 to Southside Minor Football, the Child Development Centre and Loaves and Fishes.  Nathanael Durkan has been awarded a scholarship at SFU for football (it's more that a full ride scholarship as Canadian schools full ride is tuition only). SFU plays sports in the NCAA which means that they can offer an all-inclusive (up to $18,000 scholarship). His scholarship is expected to increase each year to where his senior year, like most seniors would have a fully paid year.  Ted Talks was extremely well received by the students of Barsby and the community of Nanaimo. Barsby hosted a different professional guest speaker every Wednesday for 2.5 months. The list of speakers were generated by a survey of grade 10-12 students around their post secondary career interests.  Grade 12 Teacher Appreciation Softball Game and BBQ was on Wednesday. At this event all Grade 12's serve the teachers at a BBQ during lunch time then attempt to beat the teachers at a game of softball.  "Mary Poppins", Barsby's first (in a long time) musical, directed by Russell Berg and Tom Rokeby, was very successful. The Barsby actors played to a full houses over the four night presentation. Trustee Solomon: JBCS  "Mary Poppins" was a great success and very well attended. The club requested each class bring in various appropriate needed items and made gift bags for seniors, single parents and anyone in need.  The LIS Environmental Club won the Energy Cup and will continue to be involved in energy and environmental saving activities in the upcoming years.  The LIS Leadership Ambassadors Club - performed Random Acts of Kindness (ROAK) throughout the year, even walking through the streets of Ladysmith leaving $5 gift certificates to those who came upon them.  LIS teachers incorporated Aboriginal language and culture into their curriculum. LPS  Pairing with VIU for field trips: Several LPS teachers took advantage of connections with VIU to initiate small learning opportunities and big field trips. Specific focus was placed on skilled trades, culinary arts and marine biology.  Kindergarten Beach Focus: The LPS Kindergarten beach unit ended with a field trip to Elliot's beach. Highlights from the unit included use of iPads for web predictions and ideas, supporting deep learning in Science and Fine Arts.  Technology Plan - Year 2: LPS is ahead of schedule on their technology plan. Thanks to strong PAC support, additional district funding and a $5000 grant from Coast Capital Insurance they completed all year 2 goals; Elmo Document Camera in every classroom, projector in every classroom, high quality speaker system in every classroom, 5 iPads for 2015/16 school year to initiate teacher professional learning, 20 additional iPads (on order) to be used in the 2016/17 school year, high quality Otterbox iPad cases, an iPad card with builtin baskets to deploy groups of 5 iPads, apps for education.  Choir/Musical Theatre – The LPS grade 2/3 choir and musical theatre group performed recently at the parent appreciation tea and also at a local senior living facility. This extracurricular group received high praise for the quality of its performance. Draft Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held June 22, 2016     ENO    LSS   Fun Day – Held at the end of May, the annual Fun Day was … well … FUN! Community partners included Island Savings Credit Union, Ladysmith Secondary School and Boys & Girls Club. Pet Parade – The annual LPS pet parade went off without a hitch, but included lots of fur and paws! Approximately 10 cats, 50 dogs, 8 lizards, 5 rodents, 1 pony and 3 fish visited the parent-participation event. The event ended with a "Stuffy" category, which included approximately 100 students. School Goal-Literacy – Current LPS staff and a few of next year’s teachers reviewed the school-wide data for literacy in relation to the school goal: “All students leaving grade 3 from LPS will meet or exceed grade-level expectations in reading” Although there wasn’t 100% achievement this year, there was a significant improvement by all student cohorts. The meeting focused on positive growth by the students and staff. Highlights include: increased alignment of practice using Jolly Phonics, Daily 5 and Words-theirway. Additionally staff celebrated the collaboration fostered through shared literacy blocks and PLC time Grade 3 Celebration - A group of parents is currently planning a year-end grade 3 celebration. This strong committee is organizing a potluck and fun night for June 29, to recognize the grade 3s as they transition to Ladysmith Intermediate School next year. 30x30 Nature Challenge -In May, grade 1/2 students participated in David Suzuki's 30x30 Nature Challenge. The goal was to spend 30 minutes a day outside, every day in May. They made a special effort to take their learning outside every day by doing things like art, writing, math and P.E. They also explored a few local parks. Eco-Initiatives -ENO students built a stump circle and teaching gardens (with help from parent and community volunteers). PAC also helped students plant our existing gardens, which was a positive, fun experience. The ENO May newsletter contains pictures of the stump circle and gardens. “Patati Patata” (French speaking contest) - ENO students won this contest at the district level in three of the five categories for which they were eligible. The winners from ENO included Mila Norfolk, Annika Norfolk, Ali Segreto and Michaela Segreto. Spuptitul – LSS again hosted the annual Hul’q’umi’num language competition. A record 19 teams participated in this year’s event, which demonstrably shows the success gained at growing and preserving this important cultural heritage. Several school trustees participated, as did the three First Nation Chiefs. The Cultural Art Project “Nutsumaat sayaays” with master carver, John Marston, continues inside the school entry way.
