Sincerely, Page 23 of 26 9.1 ,,1.,>vI-n-mgr .,, Alex Attal Executive Vice President Business & Application Services Fujitsu America Page 24 of 26 10.1 Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION INFORMATION SHEET DATE: November 12, 2015 TO: Business Committee Meeting FROM: Steve Sproston, Manager – IS Department SUBJECT: MyEducation BC Background: In August 2015, NLPS went live with MyEducation BC (MyEd), the replacement of BCeSIS. We were in the 6th group of districts to convert and by far the largest group with approximately half the districts in the province involved. September saw severe performance issues with the new system. These problems persisted through to early October when application modifications and an upgrade to the database server were performed. We also experienced issues around data conversion – the transfer of data from BCeSIS to MyEd that was performed by Fujitsu. Due to performance and data conversion issues, the applications group had to work approximately 400 overtime hours. One temporary position that was created to help support our schools during the transition had to be extended to March 2016. Two temporary workers were needed for two weeks to clean up the conversion of teachers and AO’s data from BCeSIS to MyEd. The additional, measureable costs for staffing were approximately $40,000. More costs would have occurred at the school level with people banking time instead of working overtime for pay. MyEd is currently performing at reasonable speeds in most areas. We are still dealing with implementation issues, but are working them out. At this time, the district’s business cycle needs are being met by the system. Page 25 of 26 10.1 From the Board of Education Email: BoardOfEducation@sd68.bc.ca November 10, 2015 Honourable Mike Bernier: On behalf of the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education, we bring to your attention the district’s perspective on the difficulties it has experienced since the implementation of MyEducation BC. These challenges created thousands of dollars in overtime costs. While we understand the need for the software upgrade, the Board agrees that the Ministry of Education should compensate the district for these unexpected costs in labour. To manage the MyEducation BC software, along with other daily duties, the district employs application specialists, system/support specialists and a project secretary. The MyEducation BC implementation resulted in a total of 404.67 overtime hours at a cost of $16,398 (from July 1, 2015 to October 16, 2015). One temporary position was created to help support our schools during the transition to MyEducation BC. Due to the ongoing problems during the implementation, the position was extended to March 2016, for an additional cost of $21,396. Again, keep in mind, these are Board level costs and do not include the numerous hours added to our school staff. In an email I had recently written to Alex Attal, Executive Vice-President of Fujitsu America, I had indicated that this change has been more than an inconvenience. The transition process has been filled with frustration, lost time for many users and it has been a complete failure that has had a significant effect on our front line staff, teachers, administrators and most importantly, our students. At recent meetings with the Ministry of Education and the BCSTA, the topic of compensation was discussed and it was asked if the Ministry would be seeking compensation from Fujitsu. We recommend that the Ministry take this matter very seriously and that it begin the process to be compensated for the increased wage expenses for all BC school districts and ensure that districts are the recipients of the reparations made available.
