NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: November 4, 2020 Education Committee Tim Davie, Deputy Superintendent Transition Learning Program Update The transition program was created to support families that were struggling to make the decision between returning to their enrolled school building (on-site learning), enrolling in Island Connect Ed (distributed learning), or pursuing other options (homeschool). Teachers started in the program on September 18, 2020, with families starting on September 21, 2020. The program began with:  499 students o 441 students in grades K-7 o 58 students in grade 8 and 9  17 Teachers (which reduced to 11 on Oct. 12th and 10 on Oct. 21st) Initially, all families were contacted by phone to introduce the program, address any concerns, and provide introductory materials to get through the first few days. Ongoing communication with families occurred via phone, email, and Microsoft Teams. Transition teachers contacted families to provide learning opportunities, give feedback on submitted demonstrations of learning, answer questions around learning options and the return to school (which were also answered at the school level). Transition teachers were also in contact with home-based schools, especially in regards to students that had diverse learning needs or were not engaging with the learning opportunities provided. A district Student Support Teacher DL position was created several weeks into the transition program, and the successful applicant worked with transition teachers to look at ways of further supporting the diverse needs of some of our most vulnerable learners. Transition teachers sent out weekly learning plans focused on literacy, numeracy, and core competencies. The plans were sent out on Mondays with fulsome enough activities to engage students for the remainder of the week. As students began to return to their enrolled-schools, register in ICE, or make decisions about homeschooling, Transition teachers provided schools with a contact log outlining all contact with families, a summary of learning opportunities provided, and a transition form outlining student progress in literacy, numeracy, and core competencies. As of Friday October 30th, of the 499 initial transition students:  276 have returned to school (or indicated that they will return to school on November 6, 2020)  62 have enrolled in DL (or indicated that they will be enrolling in DL)  1 has enrolled in private DL (not ICE)  1 has selected homeschool  159 have not provided details about their plans for after November 6, 2020
