Information Sheet: Career Technical Centre (CTC) Page 2 Discussion CTC supports students in transitioning from high school into meaningful career pathways. A key goal of CTC programming is helping students discover their purpose. Students who feel a sense of purpose demonstrate greater engagement, direction, and improved well-being. CTC presents its program options annually to all Grade 9–11 students across the district, with ongoing communication to students and families to ensure programs align with individual interests, goals, and readiness. The application process is tailored by grade level: • • • Grade 9 and 10 applicants review an informational video outlining program expectations and opportunities. Grade 11 applicants and their families attend a live information session to support informed decision-making. Applicants to Trades and Certificate programs participate in a 20-minute in-person interview, with questions provided in advance to support preparation. Admission decisions focus on student readiness, maturity, and consistent attendance. Seats are intentionally reserved for Indigenous students and for women pursuing opportunities in the trades, reflecting CTC’s commitment to equitable access and participation. The Trade Sampler program is exploratory and operates on a first-come, first-served basis. Students who have been disengaged at their catchment school often thrive in this environment, developing perseverance, confidence, and a renewed sense of purpose. These skills support both graduation and next steps beyond high school. The Dual Credit Enrichment program allows students to take up to four university-level courses. A key outcome is learning how to navigate post-secondary systems. No interview is required for entry. CTC also coordinates one-day exploratory opportunities, allowing students to visit VIU, local mills, women’s first responder programs, the armed forces, the Canadian Navy, health care settings, BC Ferries, and more.
