DPAC SD68 May 13, 2017 Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) RE: Water Testing & Water Fill Stations Dear Trustees: On April 27, 2017 the District Parent Advisory Council passed the following 2 motions: 1. That DPAC advocate for the school district to install at least one water filtration system (Water Fill Stations) for all schools that do not currently have one. 2. To write a letter requesting that all schools, including those built after 1990, are included for lead content water testing. There are several schools with water fill stations already in place, and additional fill stations being added as a counter measure in schools where lead testing came back with lead concentration fails after 5 minutes. Our membership collectively believes that the installation of fill stations in each school ensures that our students will have access to clean drinking water year round and between testing time frames. In fact, testing of water fill stations that had not had a recent filter change still passed the lead concentration test, effectively assuring clean drinking water. We understand that this is a financial request in tight budget times; however, we believe the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education needs to make clean drinking water a priority for students with the potential expenditure of these funds. For those schools built after 1990, we request that they are included in the water testing that is to be conducted during the summer months of 2017. This is not a significant number of schools, but certainly would alleviate the concerns of parents with students in those schools. Furthermore, we believe that water testing should be made a priority, and that all school testing (not just the 2nd phase of testing) is completed, and identified issues mitigated prior to the 2017/2018 school year beginning. We respect that there are a number of large scale projects and annual maintenance to be done in the summer months, but believe water testing speaks to an aspect of student safety and should be prioritized as such. The request to include schools built after 1990 for water testing and water fill stations is reflective of parent conversations around the district, and at DPAC meetings where concerns have been shared regarding unknown potential contaminants entering the school through public water pipes and into student drinking water. We do request a written response from the Board of Education to share with PAC Chairs throughout the school district. Respectfully submitted, Charlene McKay DPAC SD 68 – President cc: John Blain, SD68 Superintendent SD68 PAC Chairs
