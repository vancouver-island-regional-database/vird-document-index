CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held February 5, 2014 PRESENTATIONS Lindsay Watford Learn@Home K-7 Student Presentation on the K-7 Learn@Home Program Three students, Taryn Pinder, Amelia Watford and Nigel Lum, from the district’s Learn@Home K-7 program told the Committee members about the program and what it means to them. The students described how they combine learning at home with special classes at school. There are 96 students in the program and each one has their own individual learning plan, and the flexibility of the program allows student schedules to be structured to meet their own needs. LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Deputy Superintendent's Report for February 2014 Mr. Blain gave the Education Committee an update from the Department of Learning Services. Some of the highlights include: • Work is continuing with the Assessment Review Committee that is reviewing and piloting assessment tools. • There have been two teacher Response to Intervention in-service opportunities with approximately 90 teacher representatives from Grades 4 to 9. • Another Response to Intervention event was held as part of the Leadership Series, with 100 principals and teacher representatives. • District teams have been established to guide the implementation of the Professional Learning Community model. • E23 sessions for all K-3 teachers have focused on high-impact instructional strategies for reading comprehension, vocabulary and fluency. • The Assistant Superintendents have completed the first of their two annual visits to meet with school principals to discuss each school’s goals and vision and how it ties into the district’s strategic plan. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Beeston, Vice-Principal at NDSS told Committee members about several of the successful alternative programs located at NDSS. He noted that these programs are all very successful. • ABOUT – Aboriginal Outreach Program - . The program allows learners, the opportunity to stabilize in a supportive atmosphere that allows them to have success and create self-esteem and confidence. • CTC – The CTC is a dual credit program that allows grade twelve students to receive secondary credit toward graduation as well as a post-secondary credential in one of fifteen postsecondary trades and technical training. • House of Learning Snuneymuxw First Nation – This is a program for adult learners and is a partnership between Vancouver Island University, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools, and Snuneymuxw First Nations. • Nanaimo Correctional Centre – In partnership with the Nanaimo Correctional Centre, this Adult Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held February 5, 2014 Educational Program aims to provide inmates with educational opportunities leading to an Adult Dogwood. • Secondary School Apprenticeship (SSA) – The Secondary School Apprenticeship program supports students working in a trade field to earn both secondary credits and a scholarship upon completion. • Skills and Trades – Housed at NDSS, this program is currently offering two trades and skills programs: - Cafeteria Training and Carpentry and Framing. These programs are available to both VAST and NDSS students. • VIU Introduction to Skills and Trades – A partnership with VIU, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools, and the Coastal Aboriginal Shipbuilding Alliance. • VAST – Vocational Adult Secondary Training – The program features a self-paced flexible schedule.
