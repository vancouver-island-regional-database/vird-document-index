Presentation To The Board Of Education March 19th, 2015 Generation NOT Reallocation Does closure and consolidation REALLY rectify the deficit? Presenter : Ronni Pakosz 2001-2003 The Board Must Cut $10.8 Million to Balance The 2004/05 Budget ***District experienced declining enrollment of 1,200 students • 2004 Board recommends closing : Harewood, Waterloo, Princess Anne, and South Wellington schools • 2004 Board closes: Harewood, Waterloo, Princess Anne, Princess Royal schools • Projected savings : an average of approximately $200,000/year/school, plus the rental and or sale of the properties (SD68 Report on Small School Closures, January 21st, 2004) •2014 Waterloo and Princess Royal Schools sold 2007 Report on Facilities Renewal ***Enrollment continues to decline • Projected $1.9million dollar deficit • Board recommends closing : Rutherford, Mount Benson, Woodbank Primary, and Dufferin Crescent schools • 2008 Board closes Mount Benson and Dufferin Crescent schools • Projected savings : Mount Benson $250,000 Dufferin Crescent $272,000 2013 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan Is Revealed *** Enrollment is now down 3500 students since 2003 • Projected deficit in excess of 4 million dollars • Board recommends closing : Cedar Secondary, Ecole Davis Road, North Oyster, South Wellington, Woodbank Primary, North Cedar Intermediate schools • The plan also listed many other potential consolidations and reconfigurations over the 10 year period. • June 2014 board closes : Cedar Secondary, Ecole Davis Road, South Wellington, Woodbank Primary (2015) and North Cedar Intermediate (2015) • Projected savings? I’m not sure we ever did get an actual answer on this. February 9th-March 20th, 2015 February 26th, Public Meeting at John Barsby School They asked : “ Do you think closing and consolidating schools is necessary?” I ask : “Is this idea working?” Evidence states NO, and I believe the reason is there are too many changing variables. • Ministry budget cuts • Fluctuating economy • Job actions • Declining enrollment • Students attending out of catchment February 17th, 2015 : Ministry of Education Releases the 2015/2016 Budget • Public school districts must cut $25million in administration costs • $30 million increase to the private sector SD68 • Enrollment continues to decline • Over 900 surplus high school seats • Needs to enroll 341 full time students to see any more money from the ministry Why is enrollment down? • People are not just having fewer children • 2014 – 37 Ladysmith students leave for the Cowichan district. • 2013/14 – at least 9 leave Woodbank and NCI for Stz’uminus First Nation School • In a small circle of my Cedar friends 13 children attend NCS • There are many parents at this time considering alternate means of education for their children in the next school year. – THAT IS A SIGNIFICANT PART OF THE 341 STUDENTS WE NEED FOR MORE FUNDING. (13% of the 341 having left in 2013/14) There are many not included in this number I’m sure • Our enrollment will continue to decline as long as we cannot provide quality education and stability for families. • We need to stop pushing people out of our district and the public sector Goal – Decrease Surplus High School Seats • Stop thinking of the school buildings as only school buildings • Move the district offices to an under capacity high school. This would eliminate empty seats the ministry is counting, and give our district employees more adequate work spaces. • Designated space for a seniors center • Run smaller schools as a conglomerate with satellite locations dependent on demographics • Office space • Satellite classrooms for post-secondary education • Create an expectation that grade 10-12 students participate in a fuller course program • Partner with private/charter type schools needing building space iGen Program in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan Seniors program with elementary aged children in Saskatoon could easily be adapted for secondary students. Complimenting history, music, technology, fine arts and math programs. Goal – Ideas to Generate Funds • Using empty classroom space will draw funds in from outside sources and ministries. • Seniors center – Ministry Responsible for Seniors » With creativity this could foster relationships between students and seniors too. Crib tournament between seniors and students to help teach math? • Health clinic – Ministry of Health • Satellite location for Post Secondary – VIU or? • Branch Libraries • Private or Charter schools rent/lease space • Office space • Academies that bring students from OTHER districts • Expectation for our grade 10-12 students to take a full course load…..hmmm…. Why should our grade 10-12 students take a full course load? A student only needs English 12 to graduate. Takes it and graduates in January • Our district receives funding for one course for one semester from the ministry • Funded per student by the district puts a strain on the budget • Student has completed graduation requirements, but has limited post secondary options A student takes a full course load • • • • • • • Our district receives funding for a fuller course load from the ministry Student has completed graduation requirements and has more options for post secondary, even if not in the immediate future. Student is more prepared for the workload in post secondary education Student is able to explore other interests Students receive more learning opportunities More successful education Higher standard of education Funding Protection What happens when our umbrella is gone? What is our plan? Will we still be able to fund our 10-12’s on a per student basis? How will we fund our schools if enrollments continue to decline?
