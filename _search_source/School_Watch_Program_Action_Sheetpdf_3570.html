THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: September 21, 2011 TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Brian Hackwood, Maintenance Supervisor SUBJECT: School Watch Program - Proposal Background The following motion was passed by the Board on June 23, 2010: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to work in cooperation with the RCMP and community partners to identify three SD68 sites for potential School Watch Programs, and to develop a draft proposal (including costs and timelines) for the Board’s consideration by November 2010. Staff have completed a preliminary investigation and found no formal program that could be readily adopted to the District’s circumstances. Given there was no information on a formal program, the District has worked to develop its own program. In order to identify interested schools, staff sent out letters along with a copy of the June 23rd action sheet to gauge interest. Responses to be included in the development of a School Watch Program were received from John Barsby Secondary, Pleasant Valley Elementary, NDSS, McGirr Elementary, Cinnabar Valley Elementary and Woodbank Elementary. A meeting was held on May 24, 2011 to introduce current District vandalism initiatives and to plan for the development of the School Watch Program. Representatives from the Board, Facilities department, John Barsby Secondary, Pleasant Valley Elementary, Woodbank Elementary, RCMP, and Crime stoppers attended. It was decided to implement a working group that would develop an actual plan. The working group met on June 21, 2011 to develop a plan proposal. The group consisted of representatives from the Board, Facilities department, John Barsby Secondary, McGirr Elementary and the RCMP (Block Watch). Discussion The working group determined that the School Watch Program could be modelled after the RCMP Block Watch Program. The Block Watch Program is based on neighbours helping neighbours. In the case of an individual school, it would be neighbours looking out for the school. Neighbours to the school would be approached to join the program which would consist of monitoring the school for any suspicious activity. Non-emergencies would be directed to the District’s security company, emergencies would be directed to 911. Training for the neighbours and school representatives can be facilitated though the RCMP Block Watch Officer. s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\4 - september 28, 2011\school watch program action sheet.docx It is essential that the schools communicate information to the neighbourhood of any school based activities that are out of the norm (e-mail preferred). Meetings with the neighbourhood and school representatives need to occur at least three (3) times a school year. Anticipated supplies cost for the program are estimated to be negligible and would be picked up by the School in the first instance and by the Facilities Department if additional support is required. Implementation To determine the viability and degree of success of the program, the working group recommends a School Watch pilot program. Three schools have expressed an interest in piloting such a program: John Barsby Community School, McGirr Elementary and Pleasant Valley Elementary. Should the Board be in agreement with a pilot program approach, the program will be set up as follows: 1. Determine the School’s program coordinator - it is expected that this would be the Principal or VicePrincipal 2. Arrange for a neighbourhood meeting with the school to explain the program and to sign up interested residents that surround the school. Attendees would include:  School neighbours contacted by letter drop  The Principal/Vice-Principal coordinator.  SD68 Facilities staff representative  RCMP Block Watch Officer  Neighbourhood Block Watch Captain (if exists) 3. Following the meeting, develop a contact list to notify neighbours of school based activities taking place outside of normal operating hours. 4. Setup at least 3 meetings during the school year with the neighbourhood to go over the program. Conclusions Staff believes that a successful School Watch Program can result in lower vandalism costs.
