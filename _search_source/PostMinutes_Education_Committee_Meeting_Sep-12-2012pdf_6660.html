CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Ms. Sarah Lee, Parent from Hammond Bay Elementary RE: International Baccalaureate (IB) Primary Years Programme (PYP) Parents from Hammond Bay Elementary made a presentation about the International Baccalaureate (IB) Primary Years Program. They asked the district to consider establishing an IB Primary Years Program for students aged 3–12. They said the program focuses on the development of the whole child as an inquirer, both in the classroom and in the world outside. It provides an educational framework based upon what is currently known about how young students learn and draws on best practice in primary/elementary schools worldwide. The parents spoke about the research they have done on the program, and the enthusiastic support they have heard from other parents in the community for such a program. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY EDUCATIONAL UPDATE Report from John Blain, Deputy Superintendent J. Blain - Report Mr. Blain updated the Committee about the work that is underway to establish the district’s new Department of Learning Services and how they will begin to implement the tier intervention model in the district. He introduced the four assistant superintendents and outlined their responsibilities for specific school zones and the major portfolios. The district is also in the process of establishing Learning Services Support teams which brings professional staff together across disciplines to Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held September 12, 2012 4 directly support classrooms, and Mr. Blain and Superintendent Dave Hutchinson outlined the process and goals for these teams. Mr. Blain said that one of the goals of the district this semester is to meet the requirements of Bill 22, to keep all classes in the district at 30 or fewer students, elementary Grades 4 to 7 and secondary Grades 8-12. The only exceptions would be the ones outlined in Bill 22, such as band and drama.
