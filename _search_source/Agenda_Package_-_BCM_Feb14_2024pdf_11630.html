Background The Ministry of Education and Child Care requires districts to prepare an Annual Budget in advance of the school year, which is based on estimated enrolments, revenues, and expenses. An Amended Annual Budget is then prepared each February based on actual enrolments captured September 29th on the 1701 Student Data Collection, and revised revenues and expenses based on more current operations. The following financial report presents the districts Amended Annual Budget, which includes all changes in revenue and expense since the Preliminary Annual Budget, as well as the second quarter financial results to December 31st, with a prior year comparator. Also included is an update on the district’s investment portfolio, and a current summary of the Capital and Local Capital Funds for the Board’s information. Discussion Since the 2023-24 Preliminary Annual Budget was approved in May the district’s Operating Fund has increased by $7,477,094 in revenue and $9,786,973 in associated expenses. The majority of additional revenue relates to Labour Settlement Funding for negotiated general wage increases, and funding for the districts increased student enrolment over preliminary estimates (84.168 Funded FTE). Special Purpose revenue and expense has increased by $4,072,764 which is mostly due to the additional staffing necessary to meet class size and composition requirements in compliance with LOU17, funded by the Ministry of Education and Child Care under the Classroom Enhancement Fund. In addition to a variety of other Special Purpose Funds received by the district for 2023-24, all Special Purpose Fund balances relating to a prior year are carried forward and are reflected in the Amended Annual Budget. The districts second quarter financial results on December 31st are included within the Operating Fund and Special Purpose Fund summaries, as well, there is a prior year comparison. Note that on December 31st, the District was 50% through fiscal 2023-24 but with school-based operations being only 10 months in duration, some expense categories may only be as much as 40% spent, and some may be less due to the complexities of school start up, and the timing of expense recognitions. In addition, Operating Revenues are received pursuant to a funding schedule set by the Ministry of Education and Child Care, based on the timing of educational delivery to cover staffing and operational expenses in alignment with the school year, not equally over the fiscal year. Year one of the projection is used to calculate our “estimated” Operating Grant allocation for the upcoming school year. The funding generated from this estimate via the Ministry of Education and Child Care’s Funding Allocation System (FAS) is announced in mid-March and provides the largest source of revenue in support of our Preliminary Annual Budget. Year two and three estimates are used to facilitate provincial, and district based budgeting and financial planning. The preliminary enrolment estimates and associated Operating Grant funding amounts are then adjusted in December, after the September 1701 Student Data Collection has been submitted and actual enrolment figures are verified by Ministry. At that time, funding levels may be increased or decreased depending on the accuracy of our enrolment projections, and due to any changes in per student funding rates or categories. Any changes to the preliminary funding amounts are incorporated and presented in the districts Amended Annual Budget each February, along with any necessary expense changes to balance the budget. Subsequent to the Amened Annual Budget, the February and May Data Collections take place and any variances of enrolment and funding from preliminary projections are accounted for. Discussion In order to comply with section 106.3 (2) of the School Act, enrolment must be estimated to include the number of students who may be enrolled in educational programs provided by the board, at three distinct intervals, and for specific student categories, for the coming school year: due each year on or before February 15th. Specifically, the intervals and categories are as follows: • • • September Enrolment Count – includes School-Age, Adults, Alternate and Continuing Education, Online Learning, Special Needs, Indigenous Education and English Language Learning students. February Enrolment Count – includes Continuing Education, Online Learning, Special Needs enrolment growth and Newcomer Refugee Students. May Enrolment Count - includes Continuing Education and Online Learning students. As the district is required to project what enrolment will be well in advance of the associated school year, the district is essentially receiving an advance of funding that supports all preliminary budgeted district expenses. Once those future estimated enrolments are confirmed the preliminary funding is adjusted up or down, so it is important to estimate in a somewhat conservative manner. As well, the district utilizes a software solution called Baragar Systems which uses our 1701 Student Data Collection data in conjunction with demographic trends, school migration trends, birth rates, student participation, and a number of other minor factors to produce our districts annual enrolment projection. PROCEDURE: Active Transportation for Students The Board of Education believes that the school has an important role to play in promoting the health and fitness of students, their families and the wider community by implementing active transportation initiatives. Active transportation is any form of human-powered transportation (walking, jogging, cycling, inline skating, etc.). Active transportation provides students the opportunity to be physically active on a regular basis and help them to achieve the recommended 60 minutes of daily, moderate to vigorous physical activity. Regular physical activity can reduce the growing rate of childhood obesity, type II diabetes, and chronic health conditions in later life. Physical activity prior to the school day helps prepare students for learning by increasing their ability to concentrate on academic pursuits, decreasing anxiety and stress, and increasing socialization. It has similar benefits at the end of the school day. Active transportation also promotes several societal benefits including social, environmental, transportation and economic.
