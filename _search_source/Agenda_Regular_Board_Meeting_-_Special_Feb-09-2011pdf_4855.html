NEW BUSINESS 3.1 Draft Resolution for BCSTA AGM The Board adopted a motion at the January 26th Board Meeting approving the development of a resolution for submission to the BCSTA AGM. The deadline for submission is Friday, February 11, 2011. WHEREAS The present system for teacher bargaining is recognized as dysfunctional for all parties involved and the present system has increased dramatically the cost of processing grievances and arbitrations to Boards of Education that could exceed the cost of funding bargaining locally for some issues, and parents, students and the wider community require a stable and predictable system for teacher bargaining and a system that upholds and models the basic democratic principles of free collective bargaining: THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED: That the BCSTA requests that the Government of British Columbia to work with BCPSEA and the BCTF to develop changes in legislation that would establish a hybrid model for teacher bargaining in British Columbia. 3.2 Proposed Local School Calendar for 2011-2012 3-18 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves a consultation process to consider the adoption of a Local School Calendar for all District schools in accordance with the Board of Education's School Calendar Bylaw No. 5. 4. ADJOURNMENT Page 2 of 18 Agenda Item Number 3.2 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: February 9, 2011 TO: The Board of Education FROM: Phil Turin, Secretary-Treasurer SUBJECT: Proposed Local School Calendar 2011-2012 BACKGROUND: The Board needs to consider whether or not a Local School Calendar will be considered in the budget planning process for the 2011-12 budget. In order to be in compliance with the School Act, the Regulations and the Board’s Bylaw No. 5, a period of at least one month of consultation is required. A Board must adopt the school calendar at a meeting that is open to the public and at least a month before the meeting, give notice of the meeting, together with a copy of the proposed school calendar to the parents of students in the schools affected and representatives of employees assigned to the affected schools. If the Board approves the consultation process tonight, the Board will hold a Special Board Meeting on Monday, March 28th to give final consideration of a local school calendar for 2011/12. This will give more than a month of consultation assuming that the parents of the students and representatives of employees are notified by Friday, February 11, 2011 (approximately 46 days). The anticipated savings, if the local school calendar is approved, is estimated at this time to be similar to the estimate provided in the 2010/11 budget planning process at $500,000.
