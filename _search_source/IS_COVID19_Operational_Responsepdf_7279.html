NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: May 13, 2020 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer COVID-19 Operational Response Background This Information Sheet is intended to provide an overview of some of the responses to the COVID-19 crisis in operations. This information is not intended to address LCCs. Rentals The District has waived the rental fees for the months of April and May to a number of organizations who made requests due to the temporary closure of their operations. We are currently exploring whether the District can access any funds to replace these revenues. Facilities and Grounds Most facilities department office staff and managers have been working from home. Staff from human resources and facilities have been working together to reassign employees who have capacity. For instance, a number of our bus drivers are currently assisting on grounds and with maintenance crews. Some educational assistants who have utilized seasonal work in the past have also been brought over. We continue to monitor employees who are unable to perform their regular work to see if there are alternative opportunities to provide meaningful work. Facilities staff are also social distancing. Some staff attend the office to print cheques, do bank deposits and pick up mail. Finance staff have started work on the implementation of an online payment system. The system will immensely improve the efficiency of school offices, particularly at the secondary level. Payroll staff are in the process of completing the roll-out of electronic timesheets for support staff which should be finalized this month. This means the total elimination of paper timesheets. Technology The measures were largely covered in the Education Committee meeting. We note, however, that we are currently examining the potential financial impacts of supporting continued student access to internet as well as the continued student device loans and how that will have an operational and financial impact.
