JULY 2024 LADYSMITH LINKS STAGE 1 WATER RESTRICTION Regular Stage 1 Watering Restrictions are in effect from May 1 to October 31 annually for the Town of Ladysmith, Diamond Improvement District and Stz’uminus First Nation. We encourage you to check our site frequently for updates throughout the season. These regular annual restrictions are intended to help control water usage as we start watering lawns and gardens with the warmer, drier weather. The restrictions are in place to ensure that the Town can maintain adequate fireflows in the event of an emergency. Don’t forget that for a limited time, the Town of Ladysmith is offering one 50% rebate up to a maximum of $75 per household for installing a minimum 189 litre (50 gallon) rain barrel. The rain barrel must be manufactured from a BPA-free Polyethylene material. The rebate is available on a first come, first served basis until funds are exhausted. Learn more at https://www.ladysmith.ca/our-services/water/water-rebate-rebates. Being water-wise throughout the year can also save you money. With a little effort, we can reduce the amount of water we use at home by as much as 40%! YOUTH BLOOM IN LADYSMITH Did you know Ladysmith has a weekly program from June to December that not only empowers teenagers, but provides them with valuable life skills and knowledge? YOUth Bloom is a comprehensive initiative designed to promote health and wellness among youth aged 13-19 in Ladysmith and Stz’uminus through food literacy and cooking, mentorship and skills training. A key component of YOUth Bloom is food literacy and cooking skills, which educates youth about the interconnectedness of food and emphasizes nutritious and culturally significant foods, all while promoting a holistic understanding of diet and health. The initiative also includes farm-to-table gardening, providing handson experience in horticulture and urban farming. Additionally, YOUth Bloom offers Skills Training through certification workshops such as Food Safe and First Aid. The program also provides youth-led mentorship and leadership training, which provides volunteer opportunities that encourage community engagement, connection, and potential future employment. Funded by Island Health, YOUth Bloom is a partnership program between the Town of Ladysmith, Stz’uminus First Nation, and Ladysmith Secondary School. ENHANCING THE LADYSMITH SKATEPARK The Ladysmith Skatepark Collective will transform the skatepark into a more vibrant and inviting community space over the next several weeks. Working with the Town, the Collective will implement the following key elements of the project: • Painting the Skatepark: By adding colourful and dynamic artwork, the skatepark will become a more visually appealing area, encouraging gathering and outdoor activity. • Showcasing Artistic Talent: The project aims to highlight the creativity of local artists, providing a platform for both skatepark users and community members to appreciate and enjoy the artwork. • Engaging Skatepark Users: Involving those who use the skatepark in the enhancement process ensures that the space reflects the community’s spirit and meets the needs of its users. FIRESMART: CONTROLLING VEGETATION Did you know that uncontrolled vegetation, such as tall grass, is a fire hazard, particularly when the weather is hot and dry? That’s why it is important to maintain landscaping and ensure that vegetation on all property, including vacant land, is controlled. Help reduce the risk of fires this summer by controlling the vegetation on your property. Communities whose residents take steps to reduce their vulnerability have a greater chance of surviving a wildfire without the intervention of the fire department. When everyone is practical and proactive, it’s possible to reduce the risk of wildfire before it threatens your home and community. Some of these preventative measures cost very little and reduce fire dangers by a great deal. WILDLIFE AWARE Nature is right on our doorstep and this means always being mindful of how human activity impacts wildlife. At this time of year, wildlife can be spotted more frequently. Don’t leave garbage or compost outdoors. Use caution when on the trails. If you see a bear or cougar, please report it immediately. Reports of wildlife in an urban setting or reports of wildlife in conflict should always be reported to the BC Conservation Officer Service Conservation reporting line 1-877-952-7277 (RAPP) or #7277 on the TELUS Mobility Network. TOWN PET POLICY To ensure safety and cleanliness, dogs are not permitted on playgrounds or playing fields in Ladysmith, including the new town pitch. This policy is in place to provide a secure and sanitary environment for children and athletes, preserving the quality and usability of our recreational areas. For dog owners, Ladysmith offers several alternatives where pets can enjoy outdoor activities. Transfer Beach Park features designated off-leash fenced areas, while Holland Creek Trail is an excellent spot for unleashed walks. Please keep your dog under control. We appreciate your cooperation in following this policy as we work together to keep our community spaces enjoyable and pristine for all residents. Thank you for helping us maintain the beauty and functionality of Ladysmith’s recreational facilities.
