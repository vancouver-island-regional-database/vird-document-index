TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Stephanie Higginson Zone Name: Nanaimo District Secondary Name of School(s) reporting on: Mountain View Elementary, Quarterway Elementary, NDSS Activities happening at school location: Mountain View Elementary Mountain View has been busy with all sorts of out of the traditional classroom learning opportunities. 6 classes are participating in the “Get Kids Cooking” program with Nanaimo Food Share. Students have the opportunity to work in kitchens and gardens to learn about the connection between the two. Getting kids involved in cooking is a great experiential learning opportunity that connects curriculum and meaningful life skills together. The school is working on creating four drums with the Aboriginal EA Tina Moore. I am looking forward to learning more about this tomorrow when I visit the school. The skills and trade trailer visited Mountain View recently and once again the students were thrilled for the hands on learning opportunities that the trades trailer provides for the students. I heard from Ms. Bergman that the students young and old were thrilled with their three days of learning from the skills and trade trailer. Quarterway Elementary Last week saw the students of Quarterway participate in the annual talent show. There was singing, dancing, magic tricks, musical instruments and so much more. It is a good reminder that learning happens in many ways and the talents students demonstrated at this event is a nod to the strong learning environment we work to create in our schools. Having the confidence to stand up in front of your peers and feel the school is a safe place to do so is an important part of creating confident learners. Hats off the entire school community for their efforts in creating this environment and for the students for sharing their amazing talents. NDSS: As usual, lots going on at NDSS. The senior girls’ basketball team won the island championships. This is the first time in 30 years for NDSS. The school production of Beauty and the Beast was last week at the Port Theatre. Unfortunately it feel on the same nights as the BCSTA Board Chair meeting and Ministry of Education Partner Liaison meeting. I was unable to attend but have been told that it was a HUGE success. Working toward the board goal of Reconciliation, the school based professional development day had a reconciliation theme and was described as very powerful. ND also had their first even Hul’q’umi’num class and the students have produced a traditional weaving that they have donated to the Ab. Ed. Department. Finally, the Learning Leaders Literacy Inquiry is currently focusing on how to utilize the NLPS reading assessment to enhance cross curricular literacy practice. Trustee Stephanie Higginson – Zone Report
