LADYSMITH PROPERTY TAXES PROPERTY TAXES ARE DUE July 2, 2025 Property owners are encouraged to pay their property taxes and claim their Home Owner Grant by the July 2, 2025 due date to avoid a 2% penalty. Please note that an additional penalty of 8% will be applied to unpaid property taxes and/or unclaimed Home Owner Grants at 4:00 pm on September 9, 2025. PAYING YOUR PROPERTY TAXES We encourage you to make your tax payments online. Visit: www.ladysmith.ca/our-services/taxespayments for details. Payments are due, and Home Owner Grants must be claimed by 4:00 pm on Tuesday, July 2, 2025. If you can’t pay online, here are other options: • In person at City Hall (cash, cheque or debit card) • In the after-hours drop box at City Hall 2025 2025 BUDGET HIGHLIGHTS Ladysmith Council is continuing to prioritize projects that renew aging infrastructure, upgrade public facilities and improve the high level of service delivery that property owners have come to expect. The Town’s and Policing taxes will rise 3.1 per cent for an average residential property, based on the assessed value of your property as determined by BC Assessment. The Town does not have control over tax rates set by other jurisdictions such as Vancouver Island Regional Library, School District 68, Cowichan Valley Regional District (CVRD), or the Cowichan Valley Regional Hospital. The Town is also one of nine regionally significant facilities in the CVRD regional recreation funding model. Taxpayers contribute to the CVRD for the nine facilities and receive user fees for the region. The CVRD compiles the budgets for these facilities and determines the requisition amount for each based on usage. • At your bank, with your Property Tax Notice The capital projects to be completed this year align with Council’s Strategic Priorities for Core Infrastructure, Official Community Plan Implementation, Waterfront Area Plan, Economy and Leadership. To claim your Home Owner Grant, visit: https://www2. gov.bc.ca/gov/content/taxes/property-taxes/ annual-property-tax/home-owner-grant/apply or phone 1.888.355.2700. RESIDENTIAL TAX DEFERMENT Tax deferment is a low interest loan program that helps qualified B.C. homeowners pay the annual property taxes on their principal residence. To find out if you are eligible to defer your property taxes, please visit the Province of BC Property Tax Deferment Program https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/ content/taxes/property-taxes/annual-property-tax/ defer-taxes or call 1.888.355.2700. WHAT SERVICES DO YOUR PROPERTY TAXES PAY FOR? Each dollar that you pay in property taxes funds a variety of programs and services. These include: • Maintaining 223 acres of park land and 26 kilometres of trails • Caring for 40.8 kilometres of Ladysmith sidewalks • Placing approximately 250-300 tons of salt and 200 tons of sand on roads each winter • Cleaning and repairing 88.7 kilometres of sanitary sewer lines • Maintaining a system that treats 1,491,884,000L of drinking water and 1,210,687,000L of wastewater • Maintaining 68.1 kilometres of roads and 58 municipally owned buildings and structures The chart below shows how one dollar of your property taxes is spent. In addition to the amounts we’ve listed, property owners also pay water and sewer parcel taxes to help fund upgrades to our water supply system, including the Waste Water Treatment Plant, the Bio-solids Composting Facility, and our Water Filtration Plant. THE IMPACT OF A MUNICIPAL TAX DOLLAR POLICING 16¢ PUBLIC WORKS 15¢ ADMINISTRATION 14¢ PARKS 8¢ DID YOU KNOW? The Town of Ladysmith is only responsible for determining a portion of your overall property taxes. Altogether, 53 per cent of your bill is set by other agencies like NanaimoLadysmith Public Schools, Vancouver Island Regional Library, and Cowichan Valley Regional District. Sewer and water parcel taxes make up 16 per cent of taxes we collect and help to ensure the Town’s infrastructure is properly maintained.
