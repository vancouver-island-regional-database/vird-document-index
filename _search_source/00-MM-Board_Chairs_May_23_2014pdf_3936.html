Date: May 23, 2014 To: Board Chairs C: Trustee Representatives Superintendents Secretary Treasurers From: Michael Marchbank, Public Administrator Re: Partial Lockout By E-mail: Two Pages I am writing to you in order to ensure that the members of BCPSEA have a clear understanding of the legal basis upon which BCPSEA has declared the partial lockout which will take effect on May 26, 2014. Pursuant to section 4 of the Public Education Labour Relations Act (the “Act”), BCPSEA is the accredited and exclusive bargaining agent for all boards of education in the province. The most recent collective agreement between BCPSEA and the BCTF expired on June 30, 2013. No agreement has been concluded, and the BC Teachers’ Federation (BCTF) has been engaged in legal strike activity on an ongoing basis since Wednesday, April 23, 2014. The authority of an employer to initiate a lockout is found in section 59(2) of the Labour Relations Code, which provides as follows: Strikes and lockouts prohibited before bargaining and vote 59 (2) … an employer must not declare or cause a lockout, until … (b) in the case of an employer, either (i) section 61 has been complied with, or (ii) a lawful strike has occurred and has not been discontinued for a period longer than 72 hours. The definition of “employer” in the Labour Relations Code includes an employers’ organization, in this case, BCPSEA. While section 61 would require a vote of BCPSEA members, in this case a lawful strike has occurred and the BCTF has refused to discontinue strike action. Accordingly, no vote of the membership is required and BCPSEA has the legal authority to declare a lockout. Page 2 This is also reinforced by the provisions of BCPSEA’s Constitution and Bylaws. Schedule 2, Part II, paragraph 2.2 of the Bylaws provide in part as follows: 2.2 Members agree they will abide by all decisions of the Association regarding relations with any union representing their employees with respect to any matter for which the Association is the accredited bargaining agent. Paragraph 2.1 enables BCPSEA to seek injunctive relief if members fail to comply with their obligations under Paragraph 2.2: 2.1 Each member agrees with each other member and with the Association that the member will not do or omit to do any act or thing that would be or constitute a breach of any of the provisions of the Constitution, Bylaws or schedules and that, without limiting the remedies available to the Association or a member, the provisions of this schedule are enforceable by injunction at the initiative of the Association or any member. At our recent regional meetings, we heard two themes emphasized by trustees:  We should continue to work hard at the bargaining table and make moves as appropriate to conclude a negotiated collective agreement. As you know, we made significant moves at the bargaining table on May 15, revising our proposal on term of the agreement to six years from the previous 10 years, and offering a $1,200 signing bonus per full time equivalent teacher if an agreement is achieved by the end of June. The BCTF made no similar move at the bargaining table, which would normally be expected.  We should apply reciprocal pressure, as appropriate, to the BCTF as a result of its strike action in order to facilitate progress at the bargaining table. Given the lack of movement at the table by the BCTF and the union’s announcement that it would begin rotating strikes throughout the province commencing May 26, we reluctantly advised that we would proceed to implement reciprocal and proportionate pressure as outlined in my May 21 letter to BCTF President Jim Iker. As also noted in my letter, there remains every opportunity for the union to avoid both the pay reduction and the loss of days by its actions at the bargaining table. Please do not hesitate to contact me if you have any questions or concerns. Yours truly, Michael Marchbank Public Administrator c: Mike Roberts, Interim CEO
