NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 6, 2024 Education Committee Kerri Steel, Learning Services Administrative Procedure 249 - Animals in Schools Settings Overview Recently, the District has been approached by St. John’s Ambulance, Nanaimo as they have been working with schools to expand access to service dogs in school. Currently, the AP is drafted in such a way to limit service animals to student specific circumstances. The changes are intended to allow more general use of service dogs to support the social and emotional health of our students while at the same time assuring the safety of our students. The District is hopeful that by providing additional access, alongside our partners at St. John’s Ambulance, Nanaimo, that we will see benefits to our students. It should be noted that only appropriately certified service dogs will be allowed. The expansion is not intended to encourage individual staff to bring their pets to school, even where they are well trained. Out of interest, more information about the program can be found here https://sja.ca/en/communityservices/therapy-dog-program Background This memo will outline changes to AP 249 - Animals in Schools that provide for expanded access to service dogs in our school to support positive educational and mental health outcomes.
