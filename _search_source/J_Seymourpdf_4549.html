Good evening Trustees and staff, Thank you for allowing my voice to be heard tonight. I listened to the people of my unique neighborhood community speak last night. I was moved by the stories that make our Rutherford community distinct. I also had the opportunity to listen to your questions and statements after each speaker. It is apparent that not one person in this room is happy about the state of our current public school system and the choice that lies ahead. It saddens me that the only two years of public education that my young family has known in this school district has been with strife and worry. Yes Rutherford Elementary has issues. It has an aging facility but that aging facility encompasses passionate children and parents along with great staff members. Through this process we have been asked “what makes your school unique”. Very simply…it is the people of our close neighborhood community. We have all heard that a house does not make a home. I dare you to find a truer statement when it comes to our school. If new meant better than why is Hammond Bay so sought after. It surely isn’t the building or the 6 portables that sit on its property. It is the faculty, students and their community. How many staff members and trustees live in a brand new home? I don’t. I live in a house that needs work. What is and was more important to my family are what is surrounding us. Yes it’s true that our school needs work. We are worth the work. We are worth the long term investment. Not only that but staff have actually taken it one step further and given you a plan that suggests a 5.5 million dollar estimated cost for an expansion at a school within our Zone is the best step forward. It is not and we do not want it. Mr. Solomon spoke last night; he asked a speaker “do you consider Dover Bay part of your community”. They answered yes. Let’s clarify this. A child’s most formative years are the first 6 years of their life and their first years in education. So yes, Dover Bay is part of our community and most parents feel very comfortable with the statement. We are also very cognitive of the fact that before children can be left to their own devices and asked to make safe choices on the way to a Secondary school they have been groomed by a caring and reasonably sized Elementary school. Along the way in such an environment that students helped them, teachers encouraged them and their very own “neighborhood community members watched out for them. This all happens because they are SD 68 September 17, 2015 Page 2 very well known to one another and go to school where they live. We don’t live around Frank Ney. I had the chance to speak with Mr. Kimler regarding the business case and closure while at Frank Ney during a past public meeting. During this conversation we shared similar views on community. When it comes to the dollars and sense of a closure it is very tough to quantify. This is especially very hard given the time restraint and a plan that is within its infancy. It must be clear that this SD is prepared to spend a great deal of estimated money on something that the “maybe forced into inhabitants” DO NOT WANT. The conversation about a rebuild has not even been tabled. There is not even the simplest list of Pros and Cons or a list of probable costs associated with a new school on the Rutherford site. We don’t want a new school but if staff and trustees believe that this facility is past its prime than why not build here where the public “your clients” want the school kept. Mrs. Higginson it is unfortunate that given the nature of being timed barred and being under the gun in a consultation process has facilitated a large degree of animosity between Trustees, staff and public. Wouldn’t it be grand if we were able to look at a facilities plan without this pressure? Would decisions be more prudent, I think so. You kept on asking for ideas from presenters or options. Take our school out of the consultation process; engage our unique neighborhood community and the possibilities are endless. If closing schools in this district were the answer to our problems than why are we re-opening Cedar shortly after its closing. Re-opening one and closing another school has not proven advantageous but yet here we are. If the facilities plan at the time of closing Cedar was more in depth and had longer vision would we be spending 1 million dollars to convert it? It seems that short term decision making has plagued this district and I say enough is enough. Mr. Rae we spoke about getting community members to rally behind us. A major Nanaimo developer who spoke last night told everyone how much he is developing and will continue to develop. A realtor in our Open and Thriving group has taken you out so you can see the development that is occurring all around us. What more do you want or need? The need for our school can’t be clearer. Mr. Blain, we all know that international students add substantially more cash to our district. Why are we not monopolizing on this by extending our catchment and allowing for international SD 68 September 17, 2015 Page 3 growth within the North Zone schools and at the same time relieving stress off of an over capacity McGirr? Mrs. Bob last night you asked a speaker “How can we improve the communication and process”. Allow Rutherford to keep its doors open, let us form a committee as suggested by one of our speakers last night and within ministry guidelines and let’s work on it together. Inclusion is a topic that you would be able to lend a great deal of insight into based on your background. I would say that being involved earlier in the process, having a long term solid understanding and representation from all zones equally could accomplish just this very topic. On October the 8th during open mic at Rutherford I asked a question regarding construction costs. This one in particular still remains unanswered. I asked: Q: Who has estimated the construction costs of $5.5Million? Based upon precedent, how accurate have they been with their estimating “Construction Costs”. The response, found on SD 68 website, under the FAQ section, dated October 13, 2015 was. A: The district contracted James Bush and Associates, professional quantity surveyors, to determine the construction costs for the Frank J. Ney addition. Background information, expertise, past clients and contact information can be found on their website: http://jba.bc.ca/. I would like to give Director of Planning and Operations the opportunity to answer the questions here tonight fully. Mr. Sabo…… 1. How accurate have the estimates from James Bush and Associates been in the past on projects within our school district in particular? Before the trustees vote on Rutherford’s fate, can you please provide documents to support your answers from tonight? Rebuild VS Expansion: Of the 7 Engineering firms I found in appendix A from the fiscal year ending June 2014 in the “financial statement”, totaling nearly 1.8 million, I don’t see one mention of JBA. It would seem that SD 68 has not used them during the 2013-2014 fiscal years. This also tells me that you did not ask them to supply a cost on rebuilding Rutherford. SD 68 September 17, 2015 Page 4 Mr.Sabo: Did SD 68 request a cost analysis from JBA, quantity surveyor, on rebuilding Rutherford Elementary? It is of course evident that SD 68 staff and superintendent have their eyes fixated upon an expansion and closure and that is why there has not been a work up or discussion supporting a Rutherford rebuild. Here is another thought provoking question and answer found on the FAQ page: Q: Has the size of parking lot at Frank Ney been addressed? Will it be increased to accommodate the extra number of cars that will be arriving? A: As per Board policy, in the event a school is closed, there will be numerous school community meetings to discuss items such as a learning plan, traffic studies and playground equipment. This can be discussed during the school community meetings and at future Board meetings. Pardon me…..its board policy to discuss ramifications, after a school closure, such as increasing the size of parking lots?! How can a proper estimate have been achieved through James Bush and Associates? Exactly how does a quantity surveyor provide an estimate on part of a project that has yet to be submitted to them? When we talk about a parking lot expansion we have to talk about the following costs: 1. Do we have the footprint to accommodate the parking expansion? 2. What is the impact on the ground where it will go? Will you require to blast rock for a parking lot expansion? 3. Are there sufficient electrical systems in place to accommodate street or car park lighting? Or would you have to cut pavement, core holes and add additional conduits to facilitate this expansion. If this were the case you would be looking at additional costs of cutting the existing blacktop to access the electrical room or vault. 4. What are the costs of the fixtures? 5. What will the compaction of soil cost? 6. What will the blacktop cost? 7. Planning, procurement, tendering, approval, labor….have not been accounted for in the submittal to JBA or to this board of trustees. SD 68 September 17, 2015 Page 5 You know it’s interesting…You can stand at the building site of a future Rutherford Elementary and see the possibilities. Two beautiful fields, black top for hockey, fantastic playground, old growth cedars. The site is open and safe. A person can see exactly what Dr. Player was talking about; it surely does not take a geophysicist or a well-seasoned professional tradesperson. You stand at the Frank J Ney current school site and you struggle to see the benefits…besides the fact the facility is newer! It has no field on its property, no blacktop for hockey; it has a nice playground but has no trees. Actually it does have trees that run up and down Walley Creek on The City of Nanaimo’s property. They create a cool Salmon bearing stream that can’t be touched due to a covenant and set back. They also create a division between the school property and rented field which creates a safety hazard especially when talking about doubling a school size. What truly makes the site at Rutherford unique is that it has no land space issues and the ability to accommodate almost any type of foot print of a repurposed or new school. Dr. Player spoke on a rebuild at Rutherford in 2017, and I quote “Rutherford is an outstanding school site but the school has been created through a series of additions. There is room on this site to rebuild while the current building remains in operation”. Where is this cost estimate? Where is this discussion? Keep large expenditures off the dockets at Rutherford for the next few years, get proper estimates, increase International population and get the public excited about public education by building a new Rutherford with the monies not being spent at Frank Ney! Sincerely, Jess Seymour, Rutherford Elementary, PAC Representative
