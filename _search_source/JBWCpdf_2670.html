John Barsby Wellness Centre: Empowered, Healthy, and Resilient Youth Presentation to SD 68 Board of Education March 15, 2017 Dr Sandy Barlow, Tony Schactel, Dr Wilma Arruda Why a School Based Health Services at John Barsby? • Many students do not have a Family Doctor • Barriers to access care 2 Approach to School Based Wellness Centre • Vision: – Empowered, Healthy, and Resilient Youth • Mission Statement: – Provide low-barrier, seamlessly integrated health and wellness services which are responsive to, and co-designed by, students at John Barsby Community School. 3 4 Timeline GP4ME Evaluation 7 Subgroups formed 2nd year NDSS opens Belmont Opens Working group formed spring Doors Open School Board approved space September 2016 September Today October 2015 2014 2016 2017 June January Presentation to School Board Tour and presentation to IH Board Summer Renovations BCPSQC Quality Award Staying Healthy WG members grew to 31 representing 15 organizations Successes “The Wellness Centre is more than just a medical clinic. It’s a place where strong, trusting relationships are built” - Student 6 Services offered  Public Health Nurse (5 days/week)  Social Worker (1 am/week)  Child youth addictions councillor (space as needed (1-3 times/week)  Family Physicians (7 staff on rotation) Mondays and Wednesday (3-4 hour shifts)  Family Medicine Resident (all Physician shifts)  Admin support ( 2 am/week) Staffing the Centre • Centre staffed by; family physicians, public health nurses, child youth mental health worker, social worker/counsellor, and an administrative assistant. • Refer to community support providers to help provide full circle care for the students. 8 JBWC • Holistic • Interdisciplinary • Integrated • Accessible • Allows for early intervention • Confidential • Don’t need to miss class, get a ride or tell parents Successes • 36% of student population has accessed JBWC 10 Confirm and strengthen the continuous doctor-patient relationship, including better support for the needs of vulnerable patients. 50% report JBWC helping to improve emotional health 67% report JBWC helping to improve physical health 71% Have learned how to better manage health issues/problems 47% Miss less school since coming to JBWC 63% Have changed some behaviours as a result of coming to the JBWC 71% Have learned some new health habits through my visits to the JBWC 59% Improved school work since coming to the JBWC Data from Student Visit Survey Lessons Learned • Collaborative partnership between 15+ organizations • The presence of a welcoming place for students to discuss health and wellness can have a significant positive impact 12 Thank you to the BCPSQC 13 Enable patients who want a family doctor to find one. “It’s nice that I get to come here and my mom or dad don’t have to drive me anywhere so it doesn’t feel like I’m wasting their time or they don’t have to take time off work.” “I tell my mom and sister that I come here and they like that I come here because all the other clinics are further away and my mom doesn’t have to take time off work to drive me.”
