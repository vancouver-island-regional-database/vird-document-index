SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1200 Role of the Board of Education The Board of Education is responsible for the development of goals and the establishment of policies to guide the provision of educational services to students attending District schools and programs in keeping with the requirements of government legislation and the values of the electorate. Specific Areas of Responsibility 1. Accountability to the Provincial Government The Board shall: 2. 1.1 Act in accordance with all statutory requirements of the Government of British Columbia to implement educational standards and policies. 1.2 Perform Board functions required by governing legislation and existing Board policy. Accountability to the Community The Board shall: 2.1 Make decisions that reflect community values and represent the interests of the entire district without prejudice. 2.2 Establish processes and provide opportunities for community input. 2.3 Report Performance Plan results at least annually.. 2.4 Develop procedures for and hear appeals as required by statute and/or Board policy. 2.5 Provide for two-way communication between the Board and educational partner groups. 2.6 Model a culture of respect and integrity. -1 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1200 3. Planning The Board shall: 4. 3.1 Provide overall direction for the District by establishing purpose, beliefs and values, a mission statement, and guiding principles. 3.2 Annually set District goals and key results and approve the Ministry required District Performance Plan (educational goals), aligned with the District’s Strategic Plan. 3.3 Approve the District’s long term Strategic Plan. 3.4 Annually approve the goal setting process and timelines. 3.5 Annually approve the District Performance Plan for submission to the British Columbia Ministry of Education by due date. 3.6 Monitor progress toward the achievement of student outcomes and other desired resuls as defined in the Performance Plan. 3.7 Annually evaluate the effectiveness of the District in achieving establish goals and desired results. 3.8 Approve the Superintendent’s Annual Report on student achievement for distribution to the public. 3.9 Annual approve school improvement plans. Policy The Board shall: 4.1 Identify the purpose to be achieved and the criteria for a new policy. 4.2 Make the final decision as to the approval of all policy statements. 4.3 Evaluate policy impact to determine if policy has created the desired change. -2 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1200 5. 4.4 Provide direction regarding the mandate for employee negotiations. 4.5 Determine policies which outline how the Board is to function. 4.6 Delegate authority to the Superintendent and define commensurate responsibilities. Board/Superintendent Relations The Board shall: 6. 5.1 Select the Superintendent. 5.2 Provide the Superintendent with clear corporate direction. 5.3 Delegate in writing, administrative authority and identify responsibility subject to the provisions and restrictions in provincial legislation and regulations. 5.4 Evaluate the Superintendent and annually review compensation. 5.5 Respect and make effective the role of the Superintendent. Public Education Advocacy The Board shall: 6.1 Advocate for public education in order to advance the educational welfare of and learning opportunities for students. 6.2 Develop annually a plan for advocacy including focus, key messages and mechanisms. 6.3 Meet with municipal governments and other educational/public service governing authorities as appropriate. 6.4 Participate in sessions and make decisions regarding British Columbia School Trustee Association and British Columbia Public Sector Employees’ Association issues. 6.5 Advance District positions and priorities through BCSTA and other relevant provincial organization and associations. -3 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1200 7. Board Development The Board shall: 8. 7.1 Annually evaluate the Board’s effectiveness. 7.2 Annually develop a Board development plan aligned with District priorities. 7.3 Participate in Board/Trustee development sessions so the quality of leadership and service in the District is enhanced. Fiscal The Board shall: 8.1 Approve the principles, decision-making criteria, and assumptions for the creation of the annual budget. 8.2 Approve the annual budget which ensures resources available are allocated to achieve desired results as outlined in the District Performance Plan and the Aboriginal Education Enhancement Agreement, as well a established Board goals. 8.3 Approve annually its five-year capital plan for submission to British Columbia Ministry of Education by the date due. 8.4 Approve by bylaw disposition of land and buildings. 8.5 Approve the naming of educational facilities and land. 8.6 At least once per trustee term, review the District’s facilities planning document to ensure the directions contain therein best meets the District’s long term facility needs. 8.7 Review facilities surplus to general school needs in the District. 8.8 Approve by bylaw disposition of land and buildings. 8.9 Annually appoint or reappoint the auditor and approve the terms of engagement. -4 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1200 8.10 Review annually and approve the Audit Committee report and management letter. 8.11 Make decisions regarding ratification of memoranda of agreement with bargaining units. 8.12 Approve disbursements from the Local Capital Reserve Funds. 8.13 Establish policy for tender selection.
