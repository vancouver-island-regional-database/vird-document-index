Mr. Jamie Brennan, Chair Board of Education Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools April 15th, 2013 Dear Mr. Brennan I would like to take this opportunity to respond to your letter of April 11th, 2013. While I appreciate you’re acknowledgment of our apology for the offending letter having appeared in our newspaper, I would point out that our editor Mark McDonald was also present at the subsequent discussion held by Ralph Nilson at VIU, and we intend to be supportive of the process going forward. However, I take great exception to your further comments about the Nanaimo Daily News. By suggesting that “(I) need to take responsibility for encouraging a kind of attack journalism that does nothing to bring the varied parts of the community together”, it would appear that you may need to do further research. The Nanaimo Daily News has been supporting the City of Nanaimo and the various non profits located here for over 138 years through the donation of millions of dollars in space, cash and editorial coverage. Last spring, we arranged for Mr. Sean Aitken, author of 52 Jobs in 52 Weeks-Finding Your Passion, to appear at Dover Bay High School to speak to students and staff at no cost to the Board. I was present along with Trustee Robinson and others, and it was an excellent event. Recently, we offered to donate $5000 in space to the Dry Grad Program of Nanaimo, of which we have been long time supporters, along with making suggestions on other potential corporate sponsors. Finally, last weekend, The Nanaimo Daily News donated over $3000 in cash at the Rotary Club of Nanaimo Oceanside James Bond Fundraiser, of which the proceeds ($40,000) went to support at risk youth in School District 68. These are but a few examples of what our newspaper does every day to support our community as a great place to live and to raise a family. Rather than providing “a forum for empty rants”, our newspaper has been responsible for supporting many positive developments in Nanaimo, including the recent cancellation of a disastrous proposed hotel complex, the closing of a drug distribution outlet in Harewood, and an ongoing series on people who have chosen to move to Nanaimo and why they love the place they now call home. As a daily newspaper, we will continue to reflect the reality of Nanaimo which is 95% excellent, while not ignoring the 5% that like any city this size is not always good, in that proportion. At the same time, we will embrace our role as a key player in our democratic system by continuing to ask questions of those in power on behalf of our readers. Thank you for allowing me the chance to address your comments. Regards Hugh Nicholson Publisher The Nanaimo Daily News/Oceanside Star Division Manager VING Cc D. Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO P. Turin, Secretary Treasurer J. Blain, deputy Superintendent BCSTA: Member Boards Members of the Board, SD68
