April 25, 2016, Dear Trustees; I am writing to you as a parent of a grade nine student at John Barsby Secondary School. We live in the Cinnabar Valley area and have been affected by both the closure and the re-opening of Cedar Secondary School. When Cedar closed two years ago, Cinnabar students were given the choice of attending John Barsby or Ladysmith Secondary. My daughter and the majority of Cinnabar’s students chose John Barsby. In these past two years our children have enjoyed a fantastic educational experience with the choice of many academic courses and extra-curricular options such as sports teams, clubs, drama productions, band, etc. My daughter and the vast majority of her classmates feel a part of the school community and do not wish to leave. The re-opening of Cedar Secondary has put Cinnabar students and their families in a dif?cult position as that is now our catchment school once again. While our children are welcome to remain at John Barsby, we have been told that transportation to and from the school will be discontinued. We live in a middle class area and most families have two working parents. How are we to get our children to school and home again each day? A few points for consideration: - - i Distance/Catchment areas: The distance fromCinnabar Valley Elementary to John Barsby is 5.8 km while the distance to Cedar Secondary is 6.7 km. Other than being needed to boost Cedars’ student population, it doesn’t seem to make sense to have Cedar as our catchment secondary school. We sit between the Extension and Chase River areas which are both bussed to John Barsby. If a bus goes right past our neighbourhood, does it not make sense to ?ll it? Projected enrollment (especially in the senior grades) in the next few years at Cedar Secondary will be insuf?cient to allow funding to give our children access to the academic options available to them at John Barsby. Lack of enrollment will also seriously affect school teams, etc. As parents who want our children to have opportunities to learn and experience all that our school system can offer, we will not send our child somewhere that limits her options in her ?nal years of high school. You have seen others present on our behalf about the issue of bussing from the Cinnabar Valley area to John Barsby. This is one last attempt at letting you know the effect that this issue has on our students. While we hope that the re-opening of Cedar Secondary is successful for future generations, it is unfair to expect our children to be uprooted again when the ability to give them all that they deserve may be seriously limited for the foreseeable future. We request that our students be “grandfathered”through the remainder of their secondary school years with bus service to and from John Barsby Community School. Thank you for your time and consideration, Tanya Dean
