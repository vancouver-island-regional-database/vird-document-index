That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopt the Forest Park Elementary School Waterworks Right-of-Ways Bylaw 2024, being a bylaw that provides the City of Nanaimo with two Right-of Ways associated with access to water meters at the Forest Park Elementary School Site, pursuant to Section 65(5) of the School Act, R.S.B.C. 1996, c. 412. Background: The City of Nanaimo has requested access to a portion of the Forest Park Elementary School Daycare, located at 2050 Latimer Rd, for the purpose of checking and maintaining a new water meter (the “Works”). A new water service was required as part of the construction and could not be accommodated on the roadway. The meter is located in a concrete vault to the Southwest of the Daycare Complex. This is not atypical and a number of SRW’s have been granted in the past. A legal survey was conducted to identify a specific portion of the property to grant the right of way and will be registered against the property title. On April 26, 2023, the Board passed Bylaw, cited as “Forest Park Elementary School Waterworks Rightof-Ways Bylaw, 2023”, which granted a specific SRW for access to the water meter within Lot 4. Upon NLPS attempting to register the SRW, City of Nanaimo indicated the additional requirement of a blanket SRW to access the meters which led to this new Bylaw. Discussion: For the purposes of providing a new water service, the following Waterworks Right-of-Ways are required by the City of Nanaimo: Page 33 of 50 Action Sheet: Forest Park Elementary School Waterworks Right of Ways Page 2 i) A blanket statutory right of way protecting access to the water meters, water meter chamber, meter reading devices, electrical connections and other works for measuring the volume of water supplied to the building on the property. This blanket SRW will encompass all of Lot 4 (as shown in Appendix B). ii) A specific statutory right of way protecting the water meters, water meter chamber, meter reading devices, electrical connections and other works for measuring the volume of water supplied to the building on the property. This specific SRW will encompass a specific area within Lot 4 (as shown in Appendix B). Once the Bylaw has been passed by the Board, the Secretary-Treasurer can sign the SRW documents and the previous Bylaw will be nullified.
