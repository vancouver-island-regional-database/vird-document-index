NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCACTION PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: May 27, 2026 Board of Education Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer and Taunia Sutton, Associate Secretary-Treasurer 2025-26 Third quarter financial results Background This information sheet provides the district’s third quarter operating results, as compared with the 2025-26 Amended Annual Budget presented in February. The report also provides the amount of operating revenues and expense anticipated for the period of April 1st to June 30th along with the estimated year-end ﬁnancial position and Accumulated Operating Surplus balance based on that net ﬁnal result. Discussion Included within the report are the third quarter ﬁnancial results for the districts Operating Fund, Special Purpose Funds, Capital Fund, and Local Capital Fund. Any areas of concern, variances from budget, or trends of note are provided. The district’s Operating Fund is presented ﬁrst and includes amended budget ﬁgures, actuals at March 31st, the percent the district has collected in revenue or spent to date, as well as provides the prior ﬁscal year’s results for comparison purposes. For relevance with regards to the % collected or spent, note the following information: • • • • • • District revenues are recorded when received over the ﬁscal year, at Q3 we expect to have collected approximately 70%. As schools operate over 10 months the expense at Q3 for the categories of Teacher, EA and Substitute salaries should be approximately 70% spent. Principals, Vice Principals and Other Professionals are 12-month staff so expense levels should be approximately 75% spent. The Support Staff category is a blend of 10- and 12-month staff which puts their expected expense between 70 and 75%. Beneﬁt costs are expected to be approximately 65% spent at Q3. Costs are lower in the ﬁrst three quarters and higher in the ﬁnal as we account for 10-month employee beneﬁt coverage for the periods of July through September in June of each ﬁscal year. Supply and Services accounts are applicable to both 10- and 12-month operations so this category is expected to be upwards of 70% spent by Q3, however, depending on budgeted surplus appropriations, planned spending, and market/vendor complexities this category may be higher or lower. Overall, with district revenues at 70.6% and expenditures at 68.5% there are no signiﬁcant areas of concern. The forecast accounts for all revenue and expense variables that are able to be reasonably estimated at this time, versus the 2025-26 Amended Budget. There is a signiﬁcant anomaly of note. Pursuant to the 2025-2029 BCTF Collective Agreement taking effect March 11, 2026, many of the negotiated terms were implemented which included the incremented salary grid, as well as some revisions with regard to grid placement for Teachers Teaching on Call. As a result of the increased salaries and associated beneﬁt costs taking effect mid-March, the additional cost for the last quarter of the ﬁscal year has been accounted for within the ﬁnancial forecast to year-end. However, the associated revenue from the Ministry of Education and Child Care to cover the additional expenses has not been accounted for as there has been no indication of the amount of funding expected, nor when it will be received by the district. Information Sheet: 2025-26 Third quarter ﬁnancial results Page 3 This delay has two impacts. First, district expenses in the Teacher Salaries, Substitute Salaries, and Employee Beneﬁts category of expenses will exceed budgeted amounts, or negate what would have been underspends (surplus). Second, the actual year-end ﬁnancial position will vary signiﬁcantly from what is currently estimated and reﬂected within the report. With that, preliminary forecast results show that the district looks to end the year with $1.6 million of Operating Fund surplus (revenues over expenses), prior to the planned transfer from Operating to Local Capital and recording the expense of Tangible Capital Assets purchased with operating funds. The ﬁnal result is a deﬁcit of ($2.3M) which will reduce our Accumulated Operating Surplus balance from $12.4 million at the end of ﬁscal 2025, to $10.1 million at the end of ﬁscal 2026. As a reminder, the 2025-26 Amended Budget included the appropriation of $6.4 million surplus dollars to fund educational and operational obligations and initiatives this ﬁscal year, as well as to transfer $2.3 million over to Local Capital to support the annual Technology Plan, and other planned capital expenses not funded by the Ministry of Education and Child Care. Following the remainder of operations to June 30th and actual year-end ﬁnancial results, the conﬁrmed balance of Accumulated Operating Surplus will be presented to the Board with our audited Financial Statements in late August 2026, along with all necessary restrictions in alignment with our Surplus Policy, and the remaining unrestricted balance for future operations and risk mitigation. The ﬁrst is within the Ministry of Education and Child Care revenue source where two factors combined almost net to zero. This includes a $1 million increase in the February 1701 Student Data Collection which accounts for increased student designations over original estimates, and additional courses through both the Career Technical Center and Island Connect Ed. The second relates to the number of First Nation students living on reserve this year versus the 2024-25 school year, which generates provincial funding under our Operating Grant. However, these dollars are then immediately recovered and sent to the Federal Government who ﬂows it to the applicable First Nation. Following this funding recognition then transfer from Provincial to Federal to First Nation, the district bills the First Nations based on a prescribed per student rate, pursuant to our Local Education Agreements (LEA). The most substantial contributing factor to the increase in LEA revenue for the 2025-26 school year pertains to the reclassiﬁcation of Qwam Qwum Stuwixwulh Community School from a First Nation to Provincial school, as agreed to by the partner groups and government, resulting in the QQS students now being captured under the LEA. The impact to our revenue is $1.2 million less under Ministry of Education and Child Care, (which is why the Feb 1701 dollars are not obvious) and $1.2 million more revenue under the category of “Other”. Budget vs. Actual Variances of Note – Expenses: As previously noted, general wage increases for teachers pursuant to the 2025-2029 Collective Agreement were implemented effective March 11th , 2026, which has increased our annual estimated expense over budget. As well, any variance from budget that may have been applicable due to the timing of positions being posted and ﬁlled has been offset by this additional expense. Beneﬁt and Substitute costs have been equally impacted, both expected to exceed budget by yearend due to the additional unbudgeted costs. With regards to Principal, Vice Principal, and Other Professional Salaries, the variances from budget are due to not accounting for the pending general wage increase expenses as the executive compensation process is underway and may or may not be recorded by June 30th, as well, actual expense amounts are uncertain at Q3. Additional information: It is important to note that the information presented within the third quarter ﬁnancial report will vary signiﬁcantly to actual year-end ﬁnancial results due predominantly to the pending Labour Settlement Grant from the Ministry of Education and Child Care to cover the costs associated with the 2025-2029 BCTF Collective Agreement terms and conditions. As well, retroactive pay for Teachers and TTOC applicable to the period of July 1, 2025, to March 15, 2026, is pending, which will result in signiﬁcant increases within the salary, replacement, and beneﬁt categories of expense. However, the third quarter report does show that ongoing and expected expenses align with the districts ﬁnancial plan and baring any shortfall of Labour Settlement funding from the Ministry, we will record a moderate operating surplus at the end of the ﬁscal year, or at the least, maintain the accumulated balance of surplus on record.
