INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: Subject property The subject property is a 2.23-hectare, undeveloped parcel that will be separated from a larger “hooked” parcel in the Holland Creek Neighbourhood. The property is currently designated Neighborhood Residential under the OCP, zoned Low-Density Residential (R3-A) under the Zoning Bylaw and falls within the Multifamily Development Permit Area (DPA 4) and Riparian Development Permit Area (DPA 6). The site is surrounded by greenspace/parkland with suburban development beyond to the north, west and east. A map of the subject property is provided in Attachment A. The OCP and zoning designations for the property were changed in May of 2024 allowing a “density” transfer, whereby permited low-density units could be consolidated into a smaller development footprint. These changes resulted in infrastructure cost savings for the developer and, in exchange, the Town received a large parkland dedication. As a condition of the OCP and zoning changes, the developer agreed to certain covenant conditions allowing infrastructure servicing the project to be phased in alignment with development phases. The developer has since applied for a development permit for the first phase of the development on the “Eastern Parcel”. PROPOSAL: The developer is proposing an 84-unit multi-family development consisting of 21 fourplexes. The development requires a multi-family development permit and a riparian development permit, the latter of which will be issued separately by staff. The proposed development permit includes 3 variances: 1. a variance to the required bike parking, to reduce the number of required longterm bicycle parking spaces from 84 to 42, and the number of oversized bicycle parking spaces from 8 to 0. 2. a variance to maximum retaining wall heights allowing a 16m section of retaining wall supporting a small parking area along the northeastern side of the property to exceed the 3-meter maximum by 0.95 meters. 3. A variance reducing the setback from the future Colonia Drive dedication from 6 meters to 4 meters. Additionally, although the proposal currently meets the density requirements of the zoning bylaw, once the portion of Colonia Drive that passes through the site is dedicated1, the size of the property will be reduced. Following road dedication, the proposed multifamily development will meet the Floor Space Ratio (FSR) requirements of the Zoning Bylaw but not the Units Per Hectare (UPH) requirement. The rules under the Local Government Act for such circumstances are unclear and this situation will likely create a hardship for the owner. Subsequently, staff are recommending a minor zoning amendment to address this. The proposed bylaw will exempt the parcel from the UPH requirement (but not the FSR requirement) and correct a labelling error so the applicable section of the Zoning Bylaw can be properly interpreted. Staff are supportive of the proposed variances to the bicycle parking requirements, retaining wall height and front yard setbacks. In the case of the variances to the bicycle parking requirements, staff note that the proposal to provide bike lockers to tenants on request meets the intent of the Zoning Bylaw, while preventing a “cluttered” form and character resulting from too many accessory buildings. Development Permit guideline 11(h) specifically allows vehicle or bicycle parking to be reduced where strict compliance with zoning requirements would undermine the character of the neighbourhood. In this case, the unique housing configuration would necessitate a proliferation of separate buildings or bike lockers to meet the zoning requirements. This would result in a loss of greenspace and amenity space. Therefore, staff are supportive of the on-demand locker option. In the case of the variance to the retaining wall height requirement, staff note that a minor, isolated height variance is needed to maintain suitable grades for parking lot access. This is consistent with DP guidelines related to site grading and blending the development with existing topography and is therefore supported by staff. Development permit guideline 2(i) allows variances to setback requirements where strict compliance with setback regulations would undermine neighbourhood character. Allowing the buildings to be located closer to Colonia Drive improves the street presence of the building, creates space to locate parking behind the buildings and moves the buildings away from natural areas and adjacent properties. This helps preserve the character of the neighbourhood and supports alignment with other development permit guidelines. Proposed Zoning Amendments When the site was rezoned in 2024, density limits were calculated without accounting for the loss of land due to future road dedication. The proposed zoning changes are consistent with the intent of the original zoning amendments and do not change the effective density of the development— i.e. the UPH when dedicated road is included. This is consistent with the intended density for the Holland Creek neighbourhood in the OCP. Staff recommend approving the zoning amendment as proposed. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1.
