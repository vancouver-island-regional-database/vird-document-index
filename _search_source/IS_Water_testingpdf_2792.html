Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION INFORMATION SHEET DATE: Nov. 23, 2016 TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Brian Hackwood, Manager of Maintenance SUBJECT: Ministry of Education Policy for Testing Lead Content in Drinking Water of School Facilities ___________________________________________________________________________ Background: On February 24, 2016, the Ministry of Education directed School Districts in British Columbia to begin a program of testing for lead content in all facilities built prior to 1990. Districts were to work with their local Health Authorities to implement a testing program. In August 2016, Island Health provided guidelines for initial testing. Further correspondence was released on September 27, 2016 by the Ministry providing a developed Policy for Lead Content Testing in Drinking Water of School Facilities with the following requirements:  School Districts to consult with Local Health Authority to develop a water quality lead testing program for school facilities that would outline the following 1. Risk Assessment 2. Water Testing 3. Communications Plan 4. Mitigation Strategies  Applies to all School District Facilities built prior to January 1, 1990  The School District must complete lead content testing on all school facilities once every 3 years (1/3 each year)  Results to be presented to the Ministry on March 31 of each year. First report due March 31, 2017 On September 23, 2016, a plan for the implementation of the Ministry’s policy was approved at the staff level with the following criteria:  Environmental Engineering firm to be retained through tender process to test for lead content and interpret the results  Consultant to develop testing strategy based on Ministry policy and Island Health recommendations  Consultant to develop remediation options On October 12, 2016 Tender #2483 was released with a closing date of November 2, 2016. The Tender was awarded to Tetra Tech Engineering on November 16, 2016 Next Steps: As per the terms of the contract, the Consultant will begin testing by December 16, 2016 under the following conditions:  2 High Schools, 8 Elementary Schools and 3 support facilities to be tested based on age and any other risk factors as identified by the Consultant  Completion of testing by March 10, 2017  Testing to be submitted to Ministry by March 31, 2017  School District to work with Consultant and Island Health to establish mitigating procedures, if required.
