March 13, 2017 Dear School District Trustees and Staff, It has been one year since the Trustees voted to begin a Grade 8 French Immersion pilot program at Ladysmith Secondary School for this current school year. First of all, I would like to say a heartfelt thank you again for introducing a French Immersion program at LSS. The program has been in effect for approximately six months, and based on feedback from parents, students and teachers, it is a great success. We have also had an additional student join the class. We are very fortunate to have wonderful, enthusiastic French Immersion teachers and a bilingual librarian who have all approached the new program with passion and dedication. Liliane DeOliviera, part-time FI teacher and Vice-Principal, is an incredible asset and has really helped the students to develop their French language in exciting, engaging ways. The highlight for the class was a recent trip to Quebec City, along with a group of French Immersion students from NDSS. The trip was organized though Immersion Tours in Quebec and conducted entirely in French. The tour guide stated that this was one of the best groups that she has ever worked with, and definitely one who has spoken the most French. In addition to the French Immersion program, our students seem to be thoroughly enjoying their experience at LSS. The majority of the French Immersion Grade 8 students have been very keen on participating in extracurricular activities at the school and have joined such programs as Improv, Jazz Band, the school musical production and various sports. My two daughters in Grade 8 French Immersion have been at the school in various activities until 4 or 5PM at least three days per week since the school year began. If they were attending NDSS for French Immersion, participating in extracurricular activities might not be possible as arranging transportation back to Ladysmith would be quite difficult. Parents and students were glad to hear from the school that the French Immersion program at LSS will definitely be continuing in the upcoming 2017/18 school year, with a split Grade 8/9 class. We know that the majority of the current Grade 7’s at Ecole North Oyster will be coming to LSS for Grade 8 French Immersion. But we have heard from a couple of parents that, although they are grateful for this opportunity, they are concerned that there are currently no plans to continue French Immersion beyond Grade 9 at LSS. The hope of many families when enrolling our children in French Immersion is for them to complete French Immersion up until Grade 12 within our community and without needing to change schools. Although it may be early to make any decisions about continuing the French Immersion program at LSS into Grades 10 – 12, we would like to remind the Board the program becomes easier to implement in the latter grades, as less French Immersion classes are required. For example, in Grades 8 and 9, four of eight classes are required to be French Immersion. The requirement is reduced to three classes in Grade 10, two classes in Grade 11, and only one class in Grade 12. We hope that the Board considers continuing the program, so that our students can have the opportunity to complete their French Immersion studies and graduate with the ”Double Dogwood” diploma. Once again, thank you for introducing this pilot program at Ladysmith Secondary. It has been an exciting year for our children in the Grade 8 French Immersion program. I, as well as many other parents, would be happy to meet with you to discuss how the French Immersion Program at LSS is developing, and to answer any questions you may have regarding its continuation beyond Grade 9. We hope that our children may one day become the first graduating French Immersion class at Ladysmith Secondary School. Sincerely, Carrie Segreto (250)245-7234 csegreto@shaw.ca
