BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: February 26, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Recruitment, Selection and Evaluation of Personnel Board Policy Background: At the Wednesday September 25, 2013 Closed Board Meeting, Trustees had a full discussion with both the Superintendent/CEO and Secretary-Treasurer regarding the former Management Personnel Committee (MPC) and the current governance model. It was agreed that the Superintendent/CEO would develop a new governance policy for the Board’s consideration, so that the concerns of Trustees pertaining to the communication of the hiring of school-based administrators is addressed. Strategic Policy Boards of Education generally have a policy that speaks to the hiring of senior and schoolbased administrators. The attached draft policy on the “Recruitment, Selection and Evaluation of Personnel” fills a current gap in Board policy, while addressing the concern noted above. At the November 27, 2013 Regular Board Meeting, the decision to send the Draft Policy out for Notice of Motion was postponed, pending further feedback from Trustees. This feedback has since been received, and has been integrated into the attached revised draft documents. Recommendation: That the Board of Education approves the draft policy “Recruitment, Selection and Evaluation of Personnel” to be sent out for Notice of Motion. Attach. 1 Policy ____ RECRUITMENT, SELECTION, and EVALUATION OF PERSONNEL (REVISED DRAFT: February 26, 2014) The Board believes that the recruitment and selection of District personnel is a shared responsibility between the Board and the Superintendent/CEO. The Board further believes strong leadership and administration at the District and school levels are essential to the effective and efficient operation of the school system. Specifically 1. The Board, in the case of the Superintendent/CEO, or the Superintendent/CEO or designate, will assume the sole responsibility for initiating the advertising process and will make reasonable effort to ensure that all current District employees are made aware of staff vacancies. 2. The Board has the sole authority to recruit and select an individual for the position of Superintendent/CEO, Secretary Treasurer, and Deputy Superintendent, and will use the services of BCPSEA's "Make a Future" if appropriate. 3. The following process will be followed for Assistant Superintendent positions: 3.1 The Superintendent/CEO will establish a screening committee that will include the Chair and Vice Chair from the Board of Education and representatives from the Administrative Council Executive. 3.2 The screening committee will recommend candidates to be interviewed by the selection committee. The selection committee will be determined by the Superintendent/CEO and include Chair and Vice Chair from the Board of Education and representatives from the Administrative Council Executive. 3.3 The selection committee will attempt to achieve consensus. In the event this is not possible, the successful candidate must be supported by a majority unrecorded vote of the selection committee. The Superintendent/CEO must be one of the votes in the majority. 3.4 These positions shall have a role description and the person occupying each of the positions shall have a written contract of employment. 3.5 The compensation package, including placement on the salary grid currently in effect, will be determined by the Superintendent/CEO in accordance with the administrative procedure set by the Board of Education, and must be approved by the Board of Education. 4. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to recruit and select staff for all other District office positions. 2 5. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to recruit and select staff for all school-based positions, including Principals and Vice-Principals. 6. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to make all decisions regarding the term and/or continuing appointments, and evaluation, of administrators. 7. In the event of an unexpected or short term vacancy, the Superintendent/CEO may appoint an “Acting Principal” or “Acting Vice-Principal” without undertaking a formal selection process. 8. The Board of Education will be provided with a rationale for school-based administrator appointments and transfers, prior to a formal system announcement. 9. All offers of employment shall be conditional on the successful applicant providing a criminal records check through the Criminal Records Review Program (Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General). Additionally, the Superintendent/CEO or designate may require documentation certifying that the candidate is medically fit for the position. 10. The Chair and the Vice Chair of the Board of Education will meet with the Superintendent/CEO bimonthly and will act as a liaison to the Board on sensitive personnel issues.
