BC Early Years Centre Guide Key Service Features: It is well documented that the early years, a period of time from before birth to age six, is a critical foundation for all future learning, behaviour, health and well-being. Building from a legacy of learning and practice from the early years sector, the following “Key Service Features” characterize the priorities of a BC Early Years Centre. BC Early Years Centres offer a menu of early years programs and services through a single point of access, delivered in a manner that is integrated and efficient for families with young children. Establishing and maintaining a BC Early Years Centre requires shared responsibility between government, public systems and communities. Involvement of various partners at all levels such as health authorities, school districts, MCFD regional offices, community agencies, and local governments will support successful outcomes for young children and their families. BC Early Years Centres Key Service Features:       Offer parenting support information and/or programs to support children’s social and emotional well-being. Provide access to early intervention information, referrals, and/or services. Provide health promotion and prevention information and/or services. Provide information and/or linkages that support young children transitioning to Kindergarten. Provide information on child care programs available in the local community. Other Examples of BC Early Years Centre Key Service Features in Action The following examples provide a range of actions that could be under taken by BC Early Years Centres in realizing Key Service Features. 1. Offer parenting support information and/or programs and/or programs to support children’s social and emotional well-being. Examples:  Coordinating/co-locating parent networking groups, play groups (e.g. “Bellies to Babies”, “Babytalk”, etc.)  Offering information for parents (e.g. literature, workshops).  Co-locating with StrongStart BC or Aboriginal Head Start; adding or adjusting hours of these services to better meet community needs.  Co-locating with Family Resource Programs.  Offering support for parents (e.g. Perinatal depression) 2. Provide access to early intervention information, referrals, and/or services. Examples:  Establishing a common referral protocol for families with young children requiring the support of early intervention services. Creating a journey map for families that visualizes/explains how a family accesses supports. Offering co-located early intervention screening for children in foster care (e.g. Ages and Stages Questionnaire, dental). Supporting referral and follow-up for children identified through screening as requiring treatment. Co-locating/coordinating early intervention services and/or staffing such as Infant Development Programs, Aboriginal Infant Development Programs, Supported Child Development Programs, Aboriginal Supported Child Development Programs, Early Intervention Therapies. 3. Provide health promotion and prevention information and/or services. Examples:  Providing current, evidence-based information on healthy early child development across the early year’s continuum (prenatal to kindergarten transition).  Coordinating/co-locating/supporting early screening, breastfeeding supports, infant and child mental health services etc.  Linking to the Health Authority prenatal registry/programs and perinatal services.  Providing families with access to relevant health promotion information on programs and services offered by Public Health.  Providing evidenced-based information on physical literacy and/or nutrition to parents and/or child care providers.  Helping to ensure programs offered follow physical and healthy eating guidelines. 4. Provide information and/or linkages that support young children transitioning to Kindergarten. Examples:  Linking and/or providing families with programming that supports a child’s transition to school.  Providing families with access to information on local Kindergarten registration processes and before/after school child care resources.  Providing support and education for service providers and local schools on Kindergarten transition and child development. 5. Provide information on child care programs available in the local community. Examples:  Providing current information on how to access child care within your community.  Co-locating with the local Child Care Resource and Referral (CCRR).  Co-locating with a licensed child care facility. 6. Other  Providing services or programming tailored to the needs of your community that do not fall under any other category.
