BC Tripartite Education Agreement Supporting First Nations Student Success 2018-2023 Presentation to the Board of Education April, 2019 Background • Tripartite Education Framework Agreement (20112016) • Increase capacity in First Nations school system in BC • Funding equal with public school based on FNBR • Access to bulk purchasing opportunities • Access to Ministry expertise in areas such as data, technology, funding • Protocol Agreement between FNESC and Ministry of Education • Access to provincial exams for students attending First Nations schools • Path to Dogwood for students attending First Nations schools • First Nations specific data Background (continued) • 2016 FNESC/FNSA, BC, Canada begin negotiating what becomes the BCTEA • TEFA expired 2016 but was extended until 2017 to allow time for negotiations for BCTEA • Was extended another year with increased federal funding for technology upgrades, culture and language programs in First Nations schools. • BCTEA negotiations completed in June 2018 • Implementation planned in stages over 2018-19 school year BC Tripartite Education Agreement • Tripartite arrangement to permanently replace Master Tuition Agreement (no longer bilateral between Canada and BC) • Requirement for Local Education Agreement (LEA) where requested by First Nation • Process to issue Adult Dogwood in First Nation schools • New approach to ensure transportation to BC Public Schools • 6th Professional Development Day in BC Public Schools (by Ministerial Order) • Second First Nation Student Count for public schools (February) • Significant new funding to First Nations schools (Language and Culture, Technology, Transportation, Capital) BCTEA (continued) • New accountabilities for BC and Canada, including: • Report on First Nation Student outcomes in BC Public Schools, including community-specific data (BC) • Report on progress on implementing the UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples as it pertains to education (Canada) • New commitment to strategic data collection and sharing for the purpose of implementing changes to support First Nation Students • All funding to flow to First Nations unless the First Nation opts for Canada to pay BC directly on its behalf (still working on details) • Formal process regarding potential increases to funding formula Nominal Roll • Under the new BCTEA, Nominal Roll enrolment will be verified jointly by First Nations and school district Secretary-Treasurers before October 15th of each year. A pilot project will be used this year for joint verification, with a formal process to be implemented for September 2019. • First Nation students enrolling in more than eight courses (including dual credit students) can claim in excess of 1.0 FTE. For example, one additional course (4 credits) will equal 0.125 FTE. • Indigenous Services Canada (ISC) has sent out instructions for completing the 2018/19 Nominal Roll–BC Region. • A BC-specific approach to compliance reviews for the nominal roll (student count) will eliminate the attendance requirement that previously resulted in many students being removed from nominal roll. Transportation • In BCTEA, the Parties agree to establish a First Nation Student Transportation Fund for the transportation of First Nation students to BC public schools beginning in the 2019/20 School Year • The base amount will be the Student Location Factor in the OGM in the 2018/19 school year plus the Student Transportation Fund amount. • Commencing in the 2019/20 school year: • Canada will pay the First Nation Student Transportation Fund to BC • BC will allocate the Fund to school districts by way of a special grant • Ministry provided Boards with transportation template in February 2019, return to Ministry by April 5, 2019. • Boards to complete the Joint Transportation Plan working with First Nations Transportation (cont) • The funds may only be used for the transportation needs of First Nation Students attending BC Public Schools in accordance with a Joint First Nation Student Transportation Plan approved by one or more First Nations and the board of education • The Minister will require School Boards to report on the spending, verified using audited financial statements • A Tripartite First Nation Student Transportation Committee will assess Joint Transportation Plans (FNESC, BC, Canada) • The Parties will review the effectiveness of the strategy after 2 years, or as otherwise agreed 6th Professional Development Day • Starting in the 2019/20 school year, by Ministerial Order, one of the six teacher professional development days will be focused on enhancing First Nations student learning outcomes. • FNESC recommends that First Nations be involved in the planning of this professional development day. First Nation Student Rate • First Nation Student Rate (formerly First Nations Billing Rate) represents the education costs for a First Nation student attending a BC public school in a school district. • The rate is calculated annually by BC. According to BCTEA, BC must consult with FNESC and Canada each year regarding the rate and any changes to it, as per section 8.0 of the Funding Protocol (Schedule J). • Students can be funded in excess of 1.0 FTE if that student is enrolled in more than 8 courses. Local Education Agreements • The parties have committed to developing guiding principles to apply to the negotiation, interpretation and implementation of LEAs in BC, and to inform legislative and policy review and changes to support LEAs. • By November 2018, BC and FNESC intend to develop a Provincial LEA that will: • serve as a template/default available to First Nations and School Boards • apply, at the request of a First Nation, where there is no existing LEA between a First Nation and a School Board • BC will invest in building the capacity of Boards to negotiate and implement LEA’s. First Nations Educational Outcomes Improvement Teams • Ministry and FNESC will prioritize districts for examination • Ministry will establish “Improvement Teams” to engage with districts and develop a plan to improve outcomes for First Nations students • Teams will report to Ministry ADM who will share outcome of the Team’s intervention with First Nation(s), Board, and Aboriginal Education Council. • ADM will report to DM and DM to Minister on the outcome and any recommendations Questions
