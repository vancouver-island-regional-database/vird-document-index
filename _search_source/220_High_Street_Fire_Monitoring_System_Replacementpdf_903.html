STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Reviewed By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Richard Frost, Manager Facility Operations Chris Barfoot, Director Parks, Recreation & Culture February 7, 2023 220 High Street Fire Monitoring System Replacement RECOMMENDATION: That Council direct staff to include $55,000 in the 2023-2027 Financial Plan for the Fire Monitoring System Replacement at 220 High Street with the funds to come from the Protective Services Reserve. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The current fire alarm system in the original building is no longer functioning properly and requires replacement. The existing wireless system has been sending false alarm signals due to weak signals. A new system will be hard wired and consistent with the system being installed in the recent childcare spaces expansion. Integrating both systems together will be integral to having a safe building with a working fire alarm system. Quotes for the replacement of the fire monitoring system are around $52,000, which includes a small contingency and complete installation of the new system, as well as oversight from MKM contracting, the onsite construction management for the new childcare space project. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: N/A INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: Over the past year, the fire monitoring system at 220 High Street has been showing signs of failure issues including sending multiple false alarms. Components necessary to maintain this system are becoming obsolete and challenging to source. Shortly after the construction started for the new addition, it was identified that the current alarm system in the original building would not be compatible with the new system being installed. The electrical engineer made attempts to utilize the existing system, however this system is no longer available. Three estimates were obtained, with the proposal from Osprey Electrical Ltd. being the least expensive. Osprey Electrical Ltd. is the current electrical contractor working on the new system for the 220 High Street childcare spaces project. Osprey provided the design for the new system and a design for the existing section of the building. It is very important that the two systems work together and operate as one system. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Not replace the fire monitoring system and continue with status quo. This option is not recommended as there is significant risk with the potential for the current fire monitoring system to fail completely. 2. To fund this project differently, i.e. by reallocating funds from other projects in the 2023 Capital Plan. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: As there are no funds currently budgeted to complete this project, staff recommend that this project be funded from the Protective Services Reserve. LEGAL IMPLICATIONS: A final occupancy permit for the new addition could be denied without a fully functional fire alarm system. CITIZEN/PUBLIC RELATIONS IMPLICATIONS: Not completing the replacement could result in impacts to the operations of the BGC childcare facility.
