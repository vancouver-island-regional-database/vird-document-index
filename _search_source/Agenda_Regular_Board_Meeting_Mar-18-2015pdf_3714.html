Departure Bay Elementary School becomes a District Program, Departure Bay ‘Eco-Academy’ Kindergarten to grade 7 2. The removal of a tuition paying nature primary program Program Development and Implementation Departure Bay Elementary School has been running as a pilot eco-school since September 2014. The school has established a three phase plan with input from parents, staff and community members/organizations. This plan identifies the ecological themes of the school which are the umbrella to which the BC Ministry’s curriculum is delivered. The plan also establishes the overall eco-related student learning goals and actions to be achieved through the delivery of this program. The program will be school wide. The development of a nature primary program will be based on resources available and will continue to be a future goal. Staffing and Human Resources There is a strong commitment from staff to promote and participate in the Departure Bay Eco-School Academy. They are engaged in planning and delivering outdoor Page 51 of 55 13.1.4 learning activities on a regular basis. The DBS staff has been piloting the Ministry’s draft curriculum under the Eco-theme umbrella this school year. The school’s plan accounts for providing the staff with support and training needed to carry out the program goals. Moreover, staff has been receiving Ecology related training for the last year and a half. In addition, the school has developed a number of community partners and has established long-term ecology related student and staff development projects. The school is a designated WildBC school (Year 1 of a 3-year term). Therefore, the Departure Bay Eco-School Academy has the ability to be highly sustainable. Sources of Funding The school has secured many grants related to ecological initiatives (over $19 000 this year) and will continue to source income for programming through grants and fundraising events. Overall Benefits to the District This program has the potential to attract students to our school district from other local private programs who also offer ecology related programs. With the anticipated small number of students coming from out of catchment area, there will be a minimum impact on other schools’ enrollment throughout the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public School District. The learning and information that has been gathered by Departure Bay School in this pilot year and in the future will be beneficial to the district as a whole. There is a district need for environmental information/resources and expertise to be coordinated. The school will provide leadership and resources in environmental education to the rest of the district. The programs developed are being shared with other schools. The school has developed partnerships with schools in other districts that have similar programs. Facilities Impact Departure Bay is working collaboratively with district facilities and community partners to establish an overall outdoor facilities plan. Moreover, Departure Bay Elementary School is located near a large number of local parks and green spaces. The school is surrounded by forests, lake/ponds, wetlands and beaches. All of these are necessary features for outdoor classroom programming. Presently Learn@Home is situated within Departure Bay’s building. Currently, there is sufficient classroom facilities to accommodate additional registrants from within and out of the school district. Page 52 of 55 13.1.4 Implementation Timeline Should the Board recommend the Eco-School Academy at Departure Bay, information will be provided to the public immediately. Information with specific procedures, inclusive of registration timelines will be advertised through various media sources. Summary If the board decides to approve the program, the Departure Bay Eco-School Academy will strive to provide an engaging, relevant program that accomplishes a connection with our place, ultimately promoting lifelong environmental stewardship in our community and beyond. The Academy is designed to develop our members into responsible, ethical individuals who will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to take action and advocate for the environment. Recommendation That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo Ladysmith) approves the proposed K-7 Eco- Academy at Departure Bay Elementary School; in addition, that the board approve Departure Bay Elementary School’s name be officially changed to Departure Bay Eco-School.
