Welcome to Uplands School Students and Staff • 334 students in 16 k-7 classrooms. • Full time Principal, a .2 Vice Principal, a part-time counsellor (Mondays, Tuesdays and Thursdays), and a part-time CYCW (Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Fridays). How we roll • Strong values and high expectations in academics, art, athletics and behaviours. • Two staff members are ‘productions specialists’ and organize and present a Concert and a Talent Show each year. Inclusive attitudes Be yourself… everyone else is taken • Many in class self-regulation techniques and furniture • A belief to try to move students forward from ‘where they are’ • All are welcome here Parents • Supportive, involved and respectful parents. Educational partnership with them. • Very supportive and active PAC Community Involvement • A daily breakfast program which includes making 5–7 bagged lunches for students each day. • Organized by our North Zone Coordinator and is run by LIFE church volunteers. How to create goals? District Vision: To educate all students to become confident, curious and caring citizens by creating an inspiring, inclusive and healthy environment to work, learn and play • Look at the District Vision, scan data and look for trends (NLPS, classroom data, other classroom or school based assessments) • What do we notice? What has been consuming our resources? Social Responsibility – Goal #1 • To improve student social responsibility by improving student confidence, connectedness and belonging. Goal #1: How we started • Direct teaching of the lessons from the 6 Cedars Books • establishment of all students in Six Cedar Animal families • co-creation with student voice of a behavior matrix related to the Spirit Animals • Cedar Tickets and monthly themed assemblies Goal #1: Track and communicate behaviours • Created triplicate “Think Sheets” to communicate with home. • Used by staff outside and during class room events, to co-regulate with students so that there is restorative, thoughtful piece replacing “punishment”. • All staff not just teachers are involved in discussions with “Think Sheets”. Literacy and Numeracy: Goal #2 • To foster joyful, engaged, curious and thoughtful learners and move them forward from where they are now. Work we’re doing during PLC • Incorporating Daily 5 as a Tier 1 strategy to improve literacy skills. (Gr 5/6, 7 SST and French Prep Provider). • Improving literacy skills by implementing story telling, story baskets and story cards through an Indigenous Lens in an integrated, thematic way. (Gr 1 and Gr 2/3) • Using storytelling to improve student responses when reading/analyzing fiction (Gr 4/5 and 5/6). • Improving numeracy skills by incorporating more experiential learning methods to help make math sense in context, be more approachable and decrease fear. (Gr 3, Gr 3/4 Gr 6/7). How is it going with our learning? • Some bumps – we communicate at PLC and staff meetings about what’s working, what is not • Mostly there is a sense of stronger community at our school  • Still lots to learn!
