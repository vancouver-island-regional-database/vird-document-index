Trustee Brzovic Report – Dover Bay Zone The holiday season is a busy time at all our schools. At Dover, students are getting ready for the final academic push of of this semester, as exams will be upon them soon after they return in January. The drama department also put on the musical Mary Poppins from December 11-15th. I wasn't able to find out if they had a concert this year. I attended both the concerts at McGirr and Frank J Ney last Thursday; they were both extremely well done & unique. McGirr's featured dancing, soloists, and a unique range of songs, with references to the various ways in which different cultures celebrate this season. Both schools had students serving as MC's; at Frank Ney, the theme was 'letters FROM Santa.' These were read by the student leaders & each song reflected what the letter was about. It was very creative. At the end, the whole school joined together on the stage for a rousing version of Feliz Navidad which had everyone clapping & singing along. Today Randerson Ridge had a school wide read. Their concert was the same day as McGirr & Frank J Ney's, so I wasn't able to attend. Tomorrow there will be a band performance & assembly to honour some students who have won awards recently. While I also missed the concert at Seaview (it was the day of our last education committee meeting), I was able to attend their craft fair on December 13th. It was run by the PAC and the gym was a very busy place. Lots of neat activities that kids of all ages could enjoy. It was especially nice to see, again, that older students (from both the Grade 7 class & older, Dover Leadership students) serving as helpers at the various stations. Seaview, like many schools, is starting their mornings with a sing a long 3 days this week (Monday, Wednesday & Friday); I plan to join in on Friday. Last night's concert at Pleasant Valley was postponed due to the snow we were hit with yesterday. It was rescheduled to this afternoon & tonight at 6pm. I was disappointed to have to miss it as a result of the date change. Rutherford is hosting their concert tomorrow, with both an afternoon & and evening performance. Rutherford also has a group that meets on Fridays to provide support for parents who are new to Canada. I believe they'll be having a holiday gathering on Friday, though I'm not 100% certain at the moment. I'll confirm tomorrow & if it goes ahead I'll attend. Another neat thing that happened at Rutherford (and I believe a few other schools in my zone, but I confess I can't remember now) is that the students got to take part in the Santa bus. Committee work: I attended the latest Safe Schools meeting, which was last week. Our social justice advocates will be working on an action plan around the SOGI administrative procedures, which they will be reporting on at our next meeting. It's clear that while our team is doing good work, our social justice workers need more time in order to fully achieve our aims. Out in Schools will be returning to Nanaimo after the holidays. The School Consolidation Working Group pertaining to the proposed closure of Rutherford met on November 27th. I need to take a moment to reiterate my request that at a discussion about the concerns that have come forward from this group, and the community at large, be discussed at the earliest possible business committeRee to ensure that the Board fully understands this issues & has a chance to debate them publicly. The biggest areas of concern are as follows: catchment areas, budget, safety, playground needs, and the implications for school overcrowding in the north end due both class size/composition rules & the rapid growth that's happening in the area. Community members are also calling for a formal motion from the Board to prevent sale of the property. The group meets again on January 29th and the hope is that this board discussion could occur prior to this meeting.
