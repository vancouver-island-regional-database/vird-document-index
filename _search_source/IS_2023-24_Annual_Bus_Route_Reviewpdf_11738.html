NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 14, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer, Pete Sabo, Executive Director Planning & Operations Tracy Mowat, Transportation Manager 2023/2024 Annual Bus Route Review Background: In January of 2017, Administrative Procedure 512 (AP512) was adopted which resulted in updating a long-standing Transportation Policy. To include consideration for items such as safety and utilization, the document calls for an annual route review process. All routes are reviewed at the district level and reported to the Business Committee, with recommendations, if any. Further, the AP was amended in October of 2021 to include consultation improvement along with other minor operating adjustments. Typically, changes following appropriate consultation are within the authority of staff. However, the Board has issued direction when proposed changes have, in the opinion of the Board, significant impact on the community. A new policy in under discussion to clarify board vs staff decisions. Over the years several exceptions to AP512 have been established which continue to be noted in the annual review. Routes Below 50% Utilization The annual route review calls for the review of routes with less than 50% utilization to see if there are any staff recommendations arising. When breaking down routes into trips (some have 3 per am and pm) 30 of the 64 trips operate below 50% utilization (highlighted in red above). There are only two routes where all trips are running underutilized. Gabriola and QQS. Reasons for allowing underutilization on trips can include: o A trip connected to another trip that is above 50% o A First Nations supported route. o A Learning Alternatives route. o A Board motion to supply a route. o A route that cannot combine with others because of geography/time restraints. o Routes split into two trips due to bus over capacity. Staff have reviewed all the routes and have no recommendations arising. Information Sheet: 2023/2024 Annual Bus Route Review Page 6 2023-24 Updates Staffing Concerns 1) Bus Driver The district employs 20, 10-month staff to provide school bus service and charter trips. Bus Driver positions have long been hard to fill but since COVID major shortages are being seen across the province and throughout North America. Many districts have had to cancel routes on a regular basis. To date NLPS has managed to keep service uninterrupted (save for one route cancellation on January 15, 2024 – due to shortage of a bus), but a contingency plan that includes a cancellation strategy has been developed and shared with Learning Services. Continued efforts with HR to recruit enough casual bus drivers to cover absences is ongoing. 2) Bus Driver / Heavy Duty Mechanic The district employs four full time staff to provide school bus and facilities vehicles/equipment maintenance and repairs at our on-site repair shop. Their primary functions are to keep all vehicles running so that service to students and school facilities are not affected. They also support the school bus runs by filling in as bus drivers when needed. Each mechanic is required to have a red seal certification, trained as a heavy-duty mechanic or commercial transport mechanic, and further qualified to perform commercial vehicle inspections. The bus shop facility is a designated preventative maintenance facility with CVSE (Commercial Vehicle Safety and Enforcement, a branch of the BC Ministry of Transportation and Infrastructure) and a designated inspection facility for commercial vehicle inspections. Benefits to having the preventative maintenance (PM) program is the ability to perform in-house commercial vehicle inspections and having them valid for a full 12 months rather than 6 months if they are done at an outside shop. This saves expense to the district and down time for the buses. CVSE provides periodic audits on the facility and our processes, to ensure compliance with the regulations. Passing the audits is the main criteria for maintaining the PM program. Staffing levels in the bus shop have suffered over the past two years with two vacant positions. Working closely with HR, the ongoing efforts to recruit for these positions has been difficult. One new mechanic started February 5th and the second is still vacant. This is causing concern with the preventative maintenance program, increased vendor costs for outsourcing, and elevated levels of stress for staff. Steps have been taken to maintain service by hiring a temporary Bus Driver/Mechanic Helper for the vacant position and by outsourcing the bulk of repairs on buses and maintenance vehicles. There has been a significant increase in vendor costs, and the buses take longer to get fixed. This is causing concern that there might not be enough buses to run all routes. BCPSEA/PSEC Report Staff is currently working on a report to PSEC to request an additional market adjustment for the Bus Driver/Heavy Duty Mechanic position based on the difficulty to recruit new employees. Most of the applicants that have put in resumes have declined to move forward indicating that the hourly rate is not sufficient. The larger 84 passenger buses have not yet been available with sufficient heating options in an EV version. This downsizing of capacity is working where the buses are running less than full loads, however the department has just about reached the limit in term of routes that can accommodate the reduced size bus. Future EV bus purchases may require consideration of new route structures that meet the numbers. It could mean adding routes, or in some cases offer less room for courtesy riders. Staff have experienced extensive growing pains with the charging systems on the EV buses and have had to work closely with charger suppliers and bus manufactures to troubleshoot issues and to keep the buses running. Compatibility between chargers and buses coupled with software updates that don’t talk to each other properly are ongoing to this date. The drivers that are currently using the EV buses are incredibly positive about them regarding drivability. Reduced noise, no diesel fumes, seatbelts, and not having to visit the fuel pumps are sighted as positives. A challenge to the driver is the considerable juggling of buses to drive charters because of the insufficient range and capacity as well as the numerous issues with buses not charging properly. New Bus Routing software The new routing software has been installed and training is underway. To date all student data has been entered and several route trips have been created. Full implementation is expected by early Spring. It is predicted to assist in route reviews and route re-design for overflow issues and upcoming school openings/boundary changes. It has already proved to be effective in re-routing stops between route 12 and route 15 in the pm to eliminate a 30-minute student wait in Ladysmith. Student Management With an increase in student behaviour issues on the bus, discussions were initiated with the department of Learning Services for advice and guidance on general student management best practices for drivers, and to gain added support at the school level. Professional development time will continue to be allocated to provide drivers with additional tools to proactively address potential behavior issues and align with school practices. Routes Over Capacity Route 17 – John Barsby – Courtesy Riders This bus run from the Extension area to John Barsby sees an overload of students on some days in the AM and most days in the PM. As a temporary fix we have been using one of our Gabriola/NDSS buses to provide a single stop service to pickup/drop off excess students to the Roberta Rd stop after completing the Gabriola/NDSS run. Many of these John Barsby students are courtesy riders that should be attending Cedar Secondary but choose to attend John Barsby; some for the international program. By removing all courtesy riders from this run there would be sufficient space for the eligible riders, subject to further increase in students in the Extension area. Route 9 Cedar Secondary – Eligible Riders This bus run from the Extension area to Cedar Secondary sees an overload of students daily this year. As a temporary fix we have been using our Gabriola/NDSS bus to provide a single stop service to pick up excess students from the Country Hills stop to Cedar Secondary in the morning and Route 8 to provide the return stop in the afternoon. Route 12 Ladysmith Secondary – Eligible Riders This bus run from the Cassidy area to Ladysmith Secondary sees an overload of students daily this year. As a temporary fix we have been using the temporary Bus Driver/Mechanic Helper to provide a single stop to pick up the excess students and take them to Ladysmith Secondary. Information Sheet: 2023/2024 Annual Bus Route Review Page 9 Gabriola Route Change Requests Concerns have been received by parents of students attending Gabriola Elementary requesting that closer bus stops be provided for two additional neighbourhoods on Gabriola Island. 1) Ferne/Tait: within the walk distance to the school 2) Easthorn/Harrison: outside the walk distance to the school The obstacles cited by the families in a letter to transportation: 1. One car per family, working parents, who struggle coordinating A) drop-offs for the bus or B) drop-offs at school as they do not qualify for the bus. 2. No car, walking distances (3.2km) are too far for young children. 3. Only one option for public transport, Gertie, which becomes an obstacle for working parents who have young children who need accompaniment. 4. Families with more than 2km commute to the nearest pick-up spot, morning pickup times are too early and/or above-mentioned obstacles. Most of the island is not well lit, has winding roads, no sidewalks and only a few crosswalks. Total route time to Gabriola Elementary is 58 minutes. School bell time is 8:40am. The pm route is the same as the AM. Information Sheet: 2023/2024 Annual Bus Route Review Page 10 To include stops in the requested areas would require that the bus route be extended by 7-10 minutes for the Ferne/Tait area and an additional 7-10 minutes for the Easthorn/Harrison area. This would increase the route length to well over one hour and would put students at the school past bell time. Backing up the start time is not possible due to the secondary student trip that runs prior to the elementary run and is timed with the Gabriola Ferry. Putting on a second bus would be a significant expense and would be very underutilized, as the current bus is already. Currently no recommendations are being made for adjustments to this route.
