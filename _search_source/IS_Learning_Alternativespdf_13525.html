Discussion Indigenous Land & Learning Learning Alternatives has a Coordinator who works with the Indigenous learning team to support First Nations students. Indigenous ways of learning are integrated throughout this cohort-based program that puts cultural knowledge at the center of learning. Students participate in a variety of culturally based activities both at school and on the land. The team includes a teacher coordinator, Indigenous support teacher, EA and a language teacher. (Cohort size is usually 25-30). Introduction to Trades (ITT) Intro To Trades is a hands-on program for students in grades 10–12 that blends classroom learning with practical skills. Students work with a Red Seal classroom teacher and various other trades people, engage in project-based activities, and complete meaningful builds—like constructing “Learning Studios” used by other schools in the district. (Cohort size is usually 16-18) ABOUT (Alternative Based Opportunities United by Teamwork) ABOUT is a flexible, student-centered pathway to graduation with a focus on unique learning opportunities. It incorporates Indigenous cultural education and supports individual pacing and strengths. Information Sheet: Learning Alternatives Update Page 2 It’s rooted in teamwork and offers adaptability for learners who flourish with non-traditional schooling structures. (Two cohorts with approximately 25-30 per group). ABOUT is in the gym/cafeteria area which allows for easy access to self-regulation space and activities. Senior Learning Alternatives Senior Learning Alternatives serves students (traditionally grades 10–12, expanded to include grades 8– 12) and emphasizes academic flexibility, diversity, elective choice, and real-world experience. Students can engage in volunteer and employment readiness opportunities while completing academic requirements in a supportive environment. (3 cohorts with 25-30 students per group). Take a Hike Take a Hike is an alternative high-school program combining outdoor experiential learning, structured academics, and on-site clinical counselling. It engages youth through nature, adventure, and well-being practices to support graduation and mental-health outcomes. Take a Hike is run in partnership with the Take a Hike Foundation and integrates community involvement and nature-based activities with academic course work. (Two cohort teachers with 12-14 per group and a clinical counsellor that is provided by TAH Foundation). CROW (Mental Wellness & Supports) CROW (Creating Resiliency and Opportunities for Wellness) specifically focus on social-emotional and mental health support, indicating that wellness learning and resources are stitched into students’ learning experiences (teacher with 12-14 students per cohort with a Child Youth Family Support worker supporting the class). Adult Learning — Brannen Lake Correctional Center Learning Alternatives provides a teacher to support adult learners, four days a week, at Brannen Lake Correctional Center (this program has been in place since 2005).
