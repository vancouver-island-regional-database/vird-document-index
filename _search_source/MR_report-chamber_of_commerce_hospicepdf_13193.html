TRUSTEE REPORT – NANAIMO CHAMBER OF COMMERCE September 2025 Trustee Mark Robinson School Year: 2025/2026 A fantastic presentation by the Nanaimo Community Hospice Society was shared by their Program Manager Bonnie Ross and Sydney Hunt, who heads up fundraising for the society. With over 200 dedicated volunteers and staff, the society serves from Nanoose Bay to Cedar. Our school district shares a memorandum of understanding with the Nanaimo Community Hospice Society which serves to enhance our support system with specialized counselling services. Hospice’s counselling services have had a tremendous supporting role for our elementary aged children within our school district, offering resources for all our students experiencing grief and loss. Beyond standard counselling, Hospice’s child and youth counsellors use music, art, play and movement therapies. Nanaimo Community Hospice Society’s special asset is a fully equipped mobile outreach vehicle set up just like a children’s counselling office. This mobile support station lowers support barriers by helping connect children experiencing loss and grief to those students otherwise unable to connect with Hospice Services. Almost 95% of their revenue is derived from donations and revenues from the Hospice Thrift Store at 1825 Bowen Road; only 5% come from grants. Without the level of financial support that the community offers Nanaimo Community Hospice, this service would not be available to our students.
