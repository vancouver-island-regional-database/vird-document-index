BOARD OF EDUCATION School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) REGULAR BOARD MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: September 29, 2010 TO: Board of Education FROM: Donna Reimer, Director of Communications/FOIPoP Coordinator and Michael J. Munro, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: School District 68 Communications Plan for the 2010-2011 School Year Background: Board Procedure 1440P states that … The Superintendent of Schools shall be responsible for maintaining a communications program for School District No. 68 and will bring forward on an annual basis a report to the Board on the program. This report is provided to the Board in accordance with the Procedure. The objectives of Board Policy 1440 – Communications, are as follows: for citizens, trustees, staff and students to have full, objective, and timely information concerning School District No. 68; to provide information about Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District programs, concerns and policies; to broaden public knowledge, raise the level of public understanding and awareness, foster confidence in the schools and the District and support for public education; and, to be sensitive to the needs and concerns of the community and to encourage the multi-directional exchange of ideas between and among the Board, administration, staff, students, parents, nonparents and the community at large. In addition, Board Procedure 1440 specifically states that the district’s internal and external communications programs should: Promote public interest in, participation in and support for the operation of the school district and an understanding of and support for public education. Provide an effective flow of information about the policies, procedures, programs, and communication challenges of the school district to both the community and district staff. Develop a climate that attracts and holds good employees and provides opportunities for staff to strive for excellence in the educational community. 1 Key Objectives In February 2010, the current Board of Education determined that one of the strategies for the goal “Sustain and enhance the Public Education system in School District 68” is to communicate on a widespread, ongoing basis about the benefits of a strong public education system and the successes of Nanaimo-Ladysmith schools. Additionally, one of the strategies for the goal “Enhance the district’s Organizational Health” is to celebrate achievement and successes of students and staff throughout the district. These strategies selected by the Board of Education have been considered in developing the Communications Plan for the 2010-2011 School Year. In keeping with the Board’s stated goals, the following communications plan for 2010-11 has the following key objectives: To increase the public’s appreciation of the value of public education in NanaimoLadysmith. To increase the level of pride in the accomplishments of students and staff throughout the school district.
