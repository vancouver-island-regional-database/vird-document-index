Budget Planning Background The Board of Education is responsible for preparing an annual budget in the form and containing the content specified by the Ministry of Education. This financial plan aligns with and reflects the District’s strategic goals, objectives and priorities in providing the best possible education to students as well as ensuring the most efficient, accountable and transparent use of Public funds. The School Act requires the Board to develop, and adopt by bylaw, an annual operating budget, in the form and containing the content specified by the Ministry of Education, where estimated expenditures must not exceed estimated revenues. The Board of Education has a duty to govern the District in a fiscally responsible manner, while supporting its goals and objectives. The Board and Staff are responsible for monitoring the district’s financial position, at regular intervals through the year, in order to ensure it meets the legislated and government policy-based financial governance responsibilities. To reflect these requirements, increase transparency and financial literacy, Finance Staff have created two documents:   Overview of School District Funding and Budget Preparation Financial Discussion and Analysis Paper – The Audited Financial Statements Explained The next document required under the Financial Health and Governance requirements, that Staff are creating, is a Policy and corresponding Administrative Procedures on Budget Monitoring and Reporting. The 2019 2020 budget planning cycle will help inform this policy through the creation of Guiding Principles and Budget Criteria. Staff believe that the Business Committee should provide input on the creation of this policy. Page 34 of 78 8.2 The Budget planning cycle is outlined in Overview of School District Funding and Budget Preparation. This plan will guide the district’s vision, mission, goals and operational plans. Further, Staff are recommending no significant changes for the 2019 2020 budget. Values – Accountability, Collaboration, Community, Courage, Diversity, Equity, Ethical standards, Inclusion, Respect and Transparency  While the Strategic and Long Range Facilities plan provide long term guidance, the annual budget should more accurately reflect the focus of the current year’s needs and initiatives. In this regard Staff recommend the District have guiding principles specific to the budget process along with the setting and reprioritizing of annual budget criteria. Staff suggest that the guiding principles for the ongoing annual budget process be as noted below. If there are not disagreements with these principles, Staff will proceed in embedding them into any draft Policy and Administrative Procedures documents. Public Engagement Budget Criteria for 2019 2020 Setting decision criteria is best practice when making organizational decisions. Decision criteria in a business setting are those variables or characteristics that are important to the organization making the decision. They should help evaluate the alternatives from which you are choosing. The current model of funding for specific designations leads individuals to believe that the funding is therefore targeted to the student for which the funding is received. The current model doesn’t recognize that students have varying needs and learning styles that should be personalized in alignment with the transformation in education. Many students require more support than others and a number of factors influence their needs such as learning deficits, family dynamics and loss, disabilities, mental health, poverty etc. Staff know that children in care, indigenous learners and students with special needs all require greater wrap around supports. However, district ability to direct supports to all vulnerable learners is essential for learner success. Staff would want to see details and costing on the recommendation in order to assess impact on the operating budget.
