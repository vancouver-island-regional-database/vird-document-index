3137 Barons Road Nanaimo, BC V9T 5W5 p: f: e: w: 250 756-1237 250 756-0188 ndta@shaw.ca www.ndta68.ca March 31, 2014 Dot Neary Chairperson, Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Ave Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 Dear Ms Neary: Re: Implementation of the Collective Agreement (Article 20.3.3 Staffing Formula Non enrolling staffing ratios) As you are aware the BC Supreme Court, in its decision dated January 27, 2014, returned working conditions clauses to the Collective Agreement. The purpose of this letter is to draw your attention to specific articles that existed prior to 2002 in order to assist the Board to be in compliance with the Collective Agreement and to facilitate a smooth start to the 2014-15 school year. While the government is appealing the decision of the court, the Board should prepare for the possibility that the appeal could be denied and that the working conditions provisions will then become part of the Collective Agreement. Since the Collective Agreement was renumbered, we will use the numbering as it existed in the 2001-2004 Collective Agreement. The renumbering took place at different times in different locals. Sometimes the reference to working conditions is Article 20 (old) and sometimes the reference is Article D (new). Article 20.3.3 of the Collective Agreement states: Employee staffing ratios in each category shall not decrease below the number reported: (i) Teacher Librarians 1:702 (ii) Counsellors 1:693 (iii) Learning Assistance Teachers 1:504 (iv) Special Education Resource Teachers 1:279 (v) Support for ESL Students 1:70.9 (identified ESL student) Article 20.3.3 outlines the process where the Ministry of Education, by May 15th, provides each district with an estimated funding amount, entrusting that the District by May 30th provides the Ministry in writing, with copies to the Local and BCTF, staffing plans for each school. In the event that the District is not be able to achieve the required ratios with the estimated funds, the District shall, by no later than May 30th , of that year, submit its staffing plan to the Ministry, with copies to the Local and BCTF. Within 10 days of submission, a joint committee shall meet to address whether it is possible to achieve the non-enrolling ratios specified. If deemed unsuccessful either party may, within 5 working days, refer the matter to arbitration. Currently the Board is not in compliance with these staffing ratios, it is the expectation of the NDTA that the Board will plan for and be in compliance with Article 20.3.3, for the 2014-2015 school year.
