DPAC Anti-Bullying Communication Program The District Parent Advisory Council (DPAC) made a presentation to the Board’s Education Committee putting forward a proposal to conduct a year-long anti-bullying campaign next school year that would include partnerships with the District, CUPE, the NDTA, and parents. The campaign would include anti-bullying support materials, professional development opportunities, and presentations for parents, staff, and students. The DPAC representatives asked for Board support for the project, including $15,000 in financial support. At the June 25, 2014 Regular Board Meeting, the Board approved the following motion from the Education Committee: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) commit a minimum of $15,000 from the 2014-2015 operating budget for the School District 68 Anti-Bullying Campaign and that plan is also predicated on staff development on the second Success For All day in January 2015; and further we ask that staff make this part of the plan.” In meeting with DPAC, the DPAC Anti-bullying Committee, and Trustee Welch, the Department of Learning Services recommends the following phasing in of the joint initiative with DPAC Anti-Bullying Parent Communication Program: Phase 1 October to June 2014-15: The Department of Learning Services will work directly with DPAC and the DPAC Antibullying Committee to develop, distribute and disseminate to staff and parents the Antibullying Support brochures. The Department of Learning Services will copy brochures as an ‘in kind’ donation to DPAC and this joint initiative. District Senior Staff will oversee all presentations and communiques to district staff. Phase 2 September to June 2015-16: DPAC will prepare a presentation package for the Board of Education presenting a timeline of presentations for staff and parents including a monetary request to support the initiative. Phase 3 September to June 2016-17: DPAC will prepare a presentation package to the Board of Education presenting a timeline of presentations for students, including a monetary request to support the initiative. Recommendation: The Department of Learning Services recommends that the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public School Board approve Phase 1 of the DPAC Anti-bullying Communication Program, including the ‘in kind’ work of the Department of Learning Services.
