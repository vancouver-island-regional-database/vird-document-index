Discussion Similar to many other areas of the District and wider community, our bussing service has faced difficulty in recruiting staff. Since the last update staff are pleased to report that the district has had some success with its strategies for recruiting bus drivers, however continued work is required due to attrition (retirements, etc.). Staffing issues have recently affected the mechanic services. To address the issue, Facilities and HR have worked together to increase recruitment and visibility of opportunities in the District. Recently staff secured short-term assistance for vacation coverage and a bus driver/mechanic-helper position been approved and posted. This position will assist the mechanics with some non-trades work within the shop and help relieve mechanics of their driving duties. Further, staff have looked to augment in house capacity by increasing work requested from local repair firms. This however has been less successful than hoped due to scheduling and timing issues causing busses to remain out of the fleet longer than if attended to by in-house mechanics. The lack of repair can manifest itself with CVSE inspection failures, which takes busses off the road until they can be repaired. It also jeopardizes future approval of our in-house preventative maintenance program with CVSE. If this trend continues staff are unsure if current service levels will be maintained. Service gaps could result in the same effect as having driver shortages with students waiting significant times to be picked up (as one bus could do double duty) or if current ridership is maintained potential cancellation of a route/trip. Discussion: There is not a specific prohibition against the District participating in proms. However, Policy 3.5 – Protection of Assets and Risk Management states as follows: The Superintendent shall ensure human, financial, capital and reputational assets are protected; procedures and practices are regularly monitored; and risks are identified, eliminated or mitigated and shall: … 4. Ensure the District, Board or staff are not unnecessarily exposed to claims of liability or loss. A District sanctioned and organized event like prom attracts a host of potential liabilities from issues associated with transportation and supervision to ensuring sufficient volunteers and/or paying staff to participate. We are unaware of what practices Districts around the province have with respect to prom, however, we believe that several local District’s practices are aligned with our own. With respect to advertising and fundraising we do our best to separate the event from our schools to not confuse our community. This is based on guidance from our insurer. The issue becomes that if schools are seen as condoning events by the use of facilities or email distribution lists the District increases its risk of being associated with the event.
