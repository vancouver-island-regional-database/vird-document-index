ADJOURNMENT Page 1 of 13 Presented by: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO April 22/2014 3.1 Page 2 of 13 On April 15th, the Board approved a motion directing the Supt/CEO to “bring budget cutting proposals from within Administration for consideration by the Business Committee”. District Senior staff routinely consider administrative reductions both during and outside of the budget planning process. The Supt/CEO however, has to keep the following in mind when considering administrative reductions: * Historical context * Supt/CEO mandate * The value added by district exempt staff * Admin reductions in advance of the budget process 3.1 Page 3 of 13 * Significant senior staffing reductions in 2002 reduced the system’s capacity to effectively support: * The improvement of student learning; * Effective staffing, employee supervision, and labour relations practises; * Proper budgeting and financial oversight; * Board of Education governance work and policy development. 3.1 Page 4 of 13 * By the end of the 2002 school year, the elimination of admin staff positions included the: * Deputy Superintendent * Directors of Education (3) * Secretary-Treasurer * Safety Manager * Director of Labour Relations * Manager of Information Systems * The vacant position of Secretary-Treasurer was not counted in the 2002 data * This accounts for the view that there were fewer senior staff in 2002, however this year was an anomaly. * 3.1 Page 5 of 13 Fast forward to 2011, and it was evident that the system continued to struggle with establishing an effective Board and senior admin team. In response the Board hired a Supt/CEO, who was asked to lead improvement in the following areas: 1. Governance 2. Student Achievement and 3. Labour Relations 3.1 Page 6 of 13 * Prior to the arrival of the new Supt/CEO, the Board approved departmental reviews connected to finance, human resources, and Board committees and processes. With respect to the department reviews, it is important to note: * District office department improvements were informed by district employee feedback; * Significant restructuring was recommended, including the addition of staff; * The new Supt/CEO was held accountable for implementing the recommendations stemming from the reviews. 3.1 Page 7 of 13 * In addition to the finance and human resources department reviews, the Supt/CEO undertook changes to senior staff to better support student learning. These included: * The formation of a Learning Services Department and the creation of Learning Services Support Teams; * Of note is the fact that this team of 6 staff, has 3 members less than the pre-2002 configuration for a total learning support services reduction of 34%. 3.1 Page 8 of 13 * The district's restructuring efforts are beginning to yield results. Some examples are: * The Human Resources Department has saved $650,000 in leave replacement costs and $100,000 in legal fees in the past year; * The Learning Services Department has saved approximately $1 million annually in staffing contingency and release time reductions, shifting positions out of operations into special purpose, and miscellaneous other efficiencies; * The Business and Operations Services Department developed an asset management plan that has to date realized $1.7 million from the sale of District property; * All departments participated in the Facilities Plan initiative which has resulted in a net annual savings of $500,000 million, and deferred maintenance cost reduction of $28 million. 3.1 Page 9 of 13 * The system is reculturing around a focus on the improvement of student learning. The LS Department is advancing RTI, early literacy support, district-wide locally developed assessments and inservice, and embedded PLC time. Although we believe that locally developed assessments will eventually provide the best insight into the effectiveness of system instructional initiatives, anecdotal evidence suggests that progress is being made. Example #1: March 27th DPAC Meeting Example #2: April 15th Public Budget Meeting Example #3: Teacher Feedback – E23 Professional Learning * 3.1 Page 10 of 13 The Board has requested that proposals for administrative reductions be brought forward at this time. The fact however, is that a number of admin reductions have been made leading up to the budget planning process. These reductions include: * A Learning Services Assistant Superintendent position; * Two school Admin positions resulting from school consolidation; * The Learning Services Supervisor of Instruction position. These reductions were already counted for in the base budget. Without them, the district would be looking for an additional $450,000. 3.1 Page 11 of 13 Given the fact that there have been administrative reductions in the 2013-2014 school year, no further reductions are recommended at this time. There will, however, be additional reductions at the end of the next school year due to school consolidation (Woodbank and North Cedar Intermediate). In addition, the senior team will continue to consider additional school-based and district administrative staff reductions outside of the budget process, provided the strategic interests of the Board will continue to be supported despite the reduction. 3.1 Page 12 of 13 * 4.1 Page 13 of 13
