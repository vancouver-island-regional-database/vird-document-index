Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: November 27, 2013 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Response to Board Motion R13/01/30-29 (NLSF) Background: This report was prepared in response to the following Board Motion: R13/01/30-29: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests that staff and Board representatives continue working with the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation to develop a memorandum of understanding between the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation and Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools by the end of the 2012-2013 school year. At this time, a draft memorandum of agreement (attached) has been completed and is amenable to both parties. Once approval has been given by both parties, it is our intention to have a formal public signing in the near future. Recommendation: That the Board of Education approve the proposed Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo Ladysmith)/Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation memorandum of agreement regarding purpose, principles, and terms and authorize the Chair to sign on behalf of the Board. Attach. 1 DRAFT MEMORANDUM OF AGREEMENT (DRAFT) made as of the ____ day of ______________, 2013. BETWEEN: THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) (Hereinafter called the “Board”) AND: NANAIMO-LADYSMITH SCHOOLS FOUNDATION (Hereinafter called the “NLSF”) Background: Since 1982, the Board has enjoyed a mutually supportive relationship with the Nanaimo Schools Financial Awards Society that, in 2008, became the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation (NLSF). The NLSF is a legally incorporated, non-profit entity. The NLSF, with the single purpose of supporting students served by the Board, is led by a representative board of community members and is governed by a constitution and bylaws. This Memorandum of Agreement formalizes a relationship that has existed between the Board and the NLSF since the foundation’s inception. It details the principles and commitments that each organization makes to the other, and will be reviewed annually and/or at the request of either party. Guiding Principles: 1. To work positively together to achieve a student-centered mission. In other words, both parties will share a vision where all students enrolled with the Board experience programs and support aimed at maximizing their potential. 2. To operative as collaborative partners whereby communication is open, regular, and respectful. The results of this regular consultation will be shared openly with the public in order to ensure that our communities are kept informed of joint efforts programs, and student successes. 3. To share expertise and advice with one another in order to maximize the goals of both parties and to carry out the respective obligations identified under this agreement. 4. To support a wide range of programming and to engage in public relations efforts to support student success and to strengthen the mission of both parties. 2 DRAFT Memorandum of Agreement: WHEREAS the Board provides educational programs to students enrolled with the Board and the NLSF is committed to promote the educational growth of students of the Board; AND WHEREAS the Board and the NLSF have enjoyed a mutually supportive relationship and wish to document that relationship; THE PARTIES AGREE as follows: 1. The Board, in consultation with the NLSF, will provide such staff, office space, equipment, supplies and services, as may be determined by the Board from time to time, for the operations of the NLSF. This obligation on the Board to provide any such staff, office space, equipment, supplies and services is specifically subject to annual review and approval by the Board. 2. The NLSF will provide scholarships, bursaries, and awards to students enrolled with the Board. 3. The NLSF will plan and implement fundraising programs to address the needs of students enrolled with the Board as identified in annual consultations with the Board. 4. The NLSF will receive, provide receipts for, and appropriately disburse donations in connection with providing the matters identified in paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Agreement. 5. The NLSF will provide annual audited financial statements approved by their financial officer, representing an approved accredited accounting firm, to the Board pertaining to the financial activities of the NLSF. 6. The NLSF agrees to keep confidential any personal information regarding students or any other confidential information of the Board which the NLSF may receive in carrying out this Agreement. 7. The NLSF agrees to not engage in any activities which could, in any way, harm the reputation of the Board. 8. The NLSF represents and warrants that the signatory to this Agreement has been duly authorized to execute this Agreement on behalf of the NLSF. 3 DRAFT IN WITNESS WHEREOF the parties have executed this Agreement as of the day and year first above written.
