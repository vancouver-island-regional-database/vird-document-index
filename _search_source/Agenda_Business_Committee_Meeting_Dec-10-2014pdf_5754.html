UNFINISHED BUSINESS Cedar Elementary School Update Mr. Brent Spohn, Facilities Planning Supervisor, outlined the timelines and progress on the Cedar Elementary School project to convert the Cedar Secondary building to an elementary school. The Annual Facilities Grant two-year budget for the project is approximately $2 million plus an additional $360,000 in funding from local capital for the upgraded playfield. Mr. Spohn said that work on the playfield is complete and interior work in the school is now underway. He said the entire conversion project is expected to be completed in time for the opening of the elementary school in September 2015. Music Rooms Mr. Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations, told the Business Committee about the spaces that schools use for band and music rooms. He said that schools may use multipurpose rooms, classrooms or portables for their music or band rooms. All secondary schools have band rooms that were built especially for that purpose. Page 4 of 7 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held November 12, 2014 Mr. Sabo said that the Safety Manager was looking into the safety concerns regarding hearing issues. The Safety Officer has started his research and will work with some teachers. He is also in contact with Work Safe and is working with them on any past claims or current ones. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Carbon Neutral Program Mr. Sabo reviewed the correspondence from the Ministry of Education regarding the Carbon Neutral Program. He said his department his working on a submission as outlined in the letter in the agenda. Hydro Quarterly Report Mr. Emil Bock, Acting Energy Manager, told the Committee that the quarterly report prepared for BC Hydro outlines the school district’s energysaving initiatives. Last year, as part of an ongoing program, the district completed two projects – new boilers and lighting upgrades at McGirr and Pleasant Valley. Over the past 10 years, the program has realized a total of $6.5 million in fuel and electricity savings. Since 2011, BC Hydro and Fortis have contributed $427,000 to SD68 towards Energy Initiatives. Overview of Enrolment Data Collection - Kim Hart Ms. Kim Hart, Supervisor of Student Data, told the Business Committee about the process used for collecting student data for the Ministry of Education this fall. The data collection is the basis of funding for the district and ensures the funding is being provided for students who require support. Page 5 of 7 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held November 12, 2014 The district has seen an enrolment decline this year of 295 students. Financial Forecast Mr. Graham Roberts, gave a financial forecast to the Committee. He said that the enrolment decline this year will not have a financial impact on the budget for the current school year because the district receives a funding protection grant, but the district anticipates a funding shortfall next year of about $3.1 million. Ministry of Education Service Delivery Project Phase 2 Mr. Roberts said this item was on the agenda as information. He said that the Service Delivery Project will not be optional and that there will be further information at the BCSTA Trustee Academy in early December. Draft Preliminary Budget Calendar for 2015-16 Mr. Roberts said the budget calendar was a draft document and there would likely be changes to it and the upcoming budget process.
