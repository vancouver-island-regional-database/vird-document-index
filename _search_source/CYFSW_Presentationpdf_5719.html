Imagine… …a classroom of Kindergarten children who vibrate with excitement as they await the weekly arrival of a baby and his caregiver. With evidence-based Roots of Empathy (an anti-bullying program), young students grow with the baby while learning about vulnerability, kindness, caring, safety, empathy…and the ability to put one’s self in the place of another’s shoes. …a student struggling with an undiagnosed mental health issue who has been moved continuously from homes and schools. Finally it becomes too much and she relieves the pain with self-harm. The teacher must stop the lesson to obtain help, not only for the student in need but also for the other students trying to cope with their shock. Fortunately, the CYFSW had the training and flexibility to help ALL the students involved. …*Stories shared with permission; identifying facts have been changed. Our mission is to Get Kids to School and Keep Students in Class in partnership with teachers, administrators, support staff & outside agencies We build relationships with students and their families… establishing coping strategies integrating self-regulation tools & social skills & setting short and long term goals We are the Child, Youth & Family Support Workers (CYFSWs) of SD68 This is how we contribute to SUCCESS FOR ALL: Did You Know… …CYFSWs are trained to work with children, youth & families in matters including: Grief & Loss; Suicide & Prevention; Mental Health, Sexual Education; Gender Orientation; Child Protection; Abuse & Neglect; Substance Addictions; Eating Disorders; Family & Relational Violence; Bullying & Cyber Safety; Threat Assessments…and more. …CYFSWs are often the first point of contact for critical incidents and crisis situations, largely due to the relationships and trust already built, both within schools and the community. …A large percentage of Suicide Awareness, Prevention, and Interventions in SD68 are done by CYFSWs. …There are a growing number of children and youth not attending school due to barriers of poverty and/or trauma. A large part of CYFSWs’ work involves supporting these families. Many students have returned to school following CYFSWs working alongside parents and caregivers in the home. * IN A TIME OF LIMITED RESOURCES AND WAITLISTS, A CYFSW CAN DELIVER A MYRIAD OF PROGRAMS INTO YOUR CHILD’S CLASS. In fact (without MCFD involvement), many free community supports have almost disappeared for elementary-aged children and their families. Often CYFSWs are the only resource accessible to them. Intense Support (based on Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs) Imagine… …transferred from school to school, a 17-year-old youth presents as extremely angry. The CYFSW meets with him and asks the right questions. He opens up, telling her that he had a plan and the supplies to commit suicide in his garage yesterday, but his 7-year-old sister came home unexpectedly. Upon hearing his confession, he was taken to the crisis clinic where he received the care he needed. He is 25 now and still keeps in touch with the CYFSW. …tears of joy when a parent living in poverty receives a $28,000 Disability Child Tax Credit after the CYFSW brought it to her attention and helped her through the application process. Did You Know… …Child, Youth & Family Support Workers are one of the lowest paid professionals in SD68, costing less than $35,000/yr. …Thus, if a CYFSW can help keep just four students in school, they have covered their wage for the year. …Generally, CYFSWs work with twenty or more students/families at one time, PLUS provide myriad programs to groups and in classrooms. ... Through partnership with outside agencies, CYFSWs bring a wealth of resources into schools and classrooms.
