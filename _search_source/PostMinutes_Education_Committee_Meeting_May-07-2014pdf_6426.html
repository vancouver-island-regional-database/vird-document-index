CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held May 7, 2014 PRESENTATIONS Frank J. Ney RE: Playground Buddy Program A group of students from Frank J. Ney Elementary School, along with their principal Ms. Young, told the committee members about the school’s Playground Buddies program. Ms. Young said the program was started as a way to provide leadership opportunities for older students at the school. The playground buddies play games and organize activities for primary students and keep a look out for children who need a buddy. Dr. P. Hasselback, Medical Health Officer - VIHA Dr. Paul Hasselback, Medical Health Officer for the Central Island, spoke to the Education Committee and reviewed a number of topics that are of common interest to both the health and education systems. Dr. Hasselback reviewed the Early Development Indicators data for the school district which show that in recent years the levels of vulnerability for children coming to Kindergarten has increased in the areas of physical, social and emotional development, while vulnerability in the areas of language and communication have remained stable or improved slightly. Members of the Committee spoke to Dr. Hasselback about how they could explore ways for the health system and the school system to work together to address issues of poverty and vulnerability. LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Mr. Blain provided Committee members with an update on recent Learning Services activities. These include: • Planning for implementation of the K-3 assessment initiative in the fall of 2014. • Development work is underway for the Grades 4-9 assessment. • A district team is designing professional learning experiences that principals can use to support Professional Learning Communities in the fall.
