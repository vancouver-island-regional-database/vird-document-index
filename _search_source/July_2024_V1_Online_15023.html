The event drew well over 1000 attendees eager to honor and learn about the history, culture, and contributions of Indigenous communities across the country. The festival opened with a powerful prayer by Elder Buffy David followed by a moving ceremony led by Patrick Aleck and his drum group. The ceremony included traditional blessings, drum circles, and a performance of a participation dance that set the tone for a day filled with cultural enrichment and community spirit. The rhythmic beats of drum group resonated throughout the venue, providing a captivating soundtrack to the ““I love to see more and more people coming to join this day of celebration. We get the opportunity to build friendships and build relationships which is so important as we look to move forward together.”-Mayor Stone 05 day’s celebrations. The aroma of BBQ salmon filled the air, adding to the festive atmosphere. Artisans and crafters displayed their work, offering handmade jewelry, textiles, and artwork that reflected the deep cultural heritage and artistic talent of Indigenous peoples. Local leaders and dignitaries, including Mayor Stone addressed the crowd, emphasizing the importance of reconciliation and the need to support Indigenous communities. In their speeches, they highlighted ongoing efforts to address 06 historical injustices and build a future based on mutual respect and partnership. National Indigenous Peoples Day is a time for all of us to come together, celebrate, and honor the rich cultural traditions of Indigenous peoples. It’s also a time to reflect on the work that still needs to be done to ensure equality and justice for Indigenous communities. Today’s celebration is a step towards that goal. The festival concluded with a unity round dance, symbolizing solidarity and the interconnectedness of all peoples. “This is a day of celebration for us. We are recognizing our history and our culture. Also as leaders in this community (Stz’uminus council & Town of Ladysmith council), we are going to continue working together to build a future for our community.” -Chief John Elliott Participants formed a large circle that included people of all ages and backgrounds, dancing together in a powerful display of community and harmony. As the sun set on a day filled with celebration and learning, the sentiment among attendees was clear: National Indigenous Peoples Day is not only a celebration of culture and heritage but also a call to action for continued support and recognition of Indigenous rights and contributions. National Indigenous Peoples Day will continue to be a cornerstone for fostering understanding, respect, and partnership between Indigenous and non-Indigenous communities, ensuring that the rich heritage of Indigenous peoples remains a vibrant and celebrated part of our national identity. 07 Organizers & Sponsors Our largest event to date went smoothly and a lot of planning went on behind the scenes to make this event possible. Mayor Stone, Chief Elliott & Lisa Marie Barron (NDP Member of Parliament for Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 08 COHI Program Notification Upcoming COHI Dental Calibration Exercise Dear Parents and/or Guardians, The Children’s Oral Health Initiative (COHI) team is facilitating a dental calibration exercise, where 70 students with completed COHI authorization forms may be selected to participate in a dental screening calibration exercise. During the calibration exercise, our practitioners take note of the following observations regarding the child’s oral health status: decayed or missing teeth, extent of decay, and previous dental work completed such as sealants, fillings, and crowns. As you are aware, dental screening is already a service that COHI provides; however, the difference in this calibration exercise is that your child may be seen by up to five practitioners, instead of one. No other additional services will be provided. The purpose of this activity to help our team standardize our screening practices, and ultimately help improve the quality of our services. This exercise will take place September 10th and 11th, 2024. Each child’s participation will take approximately 25 minutes, after which they will return back to class as soon as the activity is completed. If you wish for your child to opt out of this calibration exercise, please contact Anita Seymour at: Anita.Seymour@stzuminus.com or 250-245-8551 by July 31st, 2024. If you have any further questions about the calibration exercise or the COHI program, please don’t hesitate to contact me. Today marks a significant milestone in your life. Graduation is more than just a ceremony; it is the culmination of years of hard work, dedication, and perseverance. As you stand on the threshold of a new chapter, it is a moment to reflect on your journey, celebrate your achievements, and look forward to the future with hope and determination. Throughout your academic journey, you have faced challenges and overcome obstacles. You have learned valuable lessons, not only from textbooks but also from your experiences, your peers, and your mentors. Each of these lessons has shaped you into the resilient and capable individuals you are today. Take a moment to appreciate how far you have come and the growth you have achieved. As you embark on the next phase of your life, whether it is further education, entering the workforce, or pursuing personal passions, remember that this is just the beginning. The world is vast, and the possibilities are endless. Embrace the opportunities that come your way, and do not be afraid to take risks. It is through taking risks and stepping out of your comfort zone that you will discover your true potential and achieve greatness. In this journey ahead, you will encounter moments of uncertainty and doubt. It is in these moments that you must believe in yourself and your abilities. Remember the strength and resilience you have demonstrated during your time here. Trust that you have the knowledge, skills, and determination to overcome any challenge that comes your way. Surround yourself with people who inspire and support you, and always strive to be a positive force in the lives of others. As you step into the future, hold on to the values and principles that have guided you thus far. Integrity, kindness, and empathy are qualities that will always serve you well, no matter where life takes you. Strive to make a difference in the world, no matter how small it may seem. Every positive action contributes to the greater good, and your contributions can create a ripple effect that inspires others to do the same. Take pride in your accomplishments, but never stop learning. The world is constantly evolving, and there is always something new to discover. Be curious, stay open-minded, and embrace lifelong learning. Your education does not end here; it is a lifelong journey that will continue to enrich your life and the lives of those around you. As you celebrate this momentous occasion, take a moment to thank those who have supported you along the way. Your family, friends, teachers, and mentors have played an integral role in your success. Their guidance and encouragement have helped you reach this point, and their belief in you has been a source of strength. Class of 2024, you are the leaders, innovators, and change-makers of tomorrow. The future is in your hands, and it is bright with possibilities. Go forth with confidence, pursue your dreams with passion, and never lose sight of the impact you can make. Congratulations once again. We are incredibly proud of you and excited to see all that you will achieve.
