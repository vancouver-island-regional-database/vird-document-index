Proposed 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan Our current situation Student performance below the provincial average Not enough program enrichment Deteriorating facilities – 40% beyond their useful life Costly empty space Unnecessary staffing duplication Architecturally dysfunctional buildings A structural budget shortfall We Have a Vision of Success for All Meeting each student’s unique needs Continuous improvement of instruction and assessment Enhanced facilities that support improved learning opportunities for all students What Are We Trying to Achieve? Program, service, and facility enhancements that strengthen the quality and accountability of public education Ongoing monitoring of student achievement, enrolment, and facility condition trends Reinvestment of revenue generated through the plan to strengthen programs and supports for students How do facilities improve student learning? Optimal school size ensures equitable delivery of services and programs Optimal school size ensures there are sufficient staff and programs available in each school Optimal school size offers more curricular and extracurricular opportunities for students Improved facilities provide 21st Century learning environments Commitment to Consultation and Partnerships Consultation with school communities to support effective transitions for students and staff Commitment to working with community partners to reduce duplication of service and enhance student learning experiences and program choices Summary of Recommendations Consolidate ten facilities Rebuild four facilities Rethink and repurpose the District and Learning Resource Centres (DRC/LRC) Pursue the addition of enrichment programs to a number of schools Relocate and renew the approach to the alternate programs Adjust the French Immersion program Relocate the administrative functions Proposed Plan Implementation In
