Trustee Rae Report February 2018 DPAC Meeting  A motion was passed to submit a resolution to BCCPAC: Be it resolved that the District Parent Advisory Council of Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools, research and write a resolution for submission to BCCPAC to advocate for cooperation between the Ministry of Education and the Ministry of Children and Families to ensure that students in care, who are enrolled in public education, receive the necessary funding to stay in care until they obtain graduation, thereby increasing their potential to achieve their diploma.  Water Testing and Fill Stations: PACs are appreciative of the timeline that has been moved forward to complete testing, and still feel that it is a priority to see water fill stations installed in all schools where testing requirements and lead levels were not acceptable.  PACs are looking forward to hearing back from the Superintendent/Board on the elimination of custodial fees charged to PACs when they are hosting fundraising events at their schools. These events provide much needed funds to PACs, who in turn return all proceeds of their fundraisers directly back into the school and classrooms for students. A return to when PACs were not charged these fees would mean increased funds back in the classroom.  Further discussion needed around the communication process to parents on assessment.
