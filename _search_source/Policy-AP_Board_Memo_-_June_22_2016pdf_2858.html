MEMO TO: Board of Education, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools FROM: Dave Mumford, Dave Mumford Consulting DATE: June 22, 2016 RE: Board Policy and Administrative Procedure Development The work on the process of separating Board Policy from Administrative Procedures as determined by the recommendations of the joint senior management/Trustee Board Policies and Administrative Procedures Steering Committee in 2014 is nearing completion. The attached documentation is intended to provide Trustees with an overview of the process that has occurred to date. Revision, updating and conversion have been completed for a number of documents and it is appropriate to review the work to this point in time. In addition to the administrative procedures work, the parallel development of an ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ has taken place. The purpose of the manual is to serve as an easily accessible reference for resources, documents, templates and guides that will assist school administrators in the implementation of specific administrative procedures. For example, AP 106 – Critical Incident Management and Emergency Response is now accompanied by an extensive document that is linked to regional and provincial emergency service agencies to assist schools in developing plans to respond effectively to a wide variety of potential emergency situations. The ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ is located on the district web site and can be easily accessed by school administrators and site-based supervisors as they develop emergency plans specific to the school or work place. Similarly, a new process of requesting and authorizing field trips in the district has been developed and is currently undergoing implementation, testing and review. In the documentation below, administrative procedures that appear in normal type are those approved by the Board on November 26, 2014. Administrative procedures in italics represent those that have been reviewed and revised pending Board approval or new administrative procedures resulting from legislation or regulatory changes.
