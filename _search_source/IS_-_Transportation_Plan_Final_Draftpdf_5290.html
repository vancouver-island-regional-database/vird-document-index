Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATON SHEET DATE: September 14, 2016 TO: Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Carrie McVeigh, A/Secretary-Treasurer SUBJECT: New Student Transportation Fund Student Transportation Funding Application: The district’s application for the funding will identify the following use:  That for 16-17 budget year the funding will be used to supplement the cost of providing outof-policy runs ($224,000) and the Active Transportation Program ($30,000); and  That for subsequent years, the Student Transportation Fund be used to support the transportation services identified under a revised transportation policy. Background: In August the Ministry of Education announced 14.7 Million in funding to school districts under the new Student Transportation Fund. The portion of this ongoing funding allocated to NanaimoLadysmith Pubic Schools is $244,630 and to be eligible for receiving the funding, districts were advised to submit a plan to the Ministry of Education by September 30, 2016. The Student Transportation Fund can be used in a variety of ways, including:       improving service by adding new or amended routes; improving access to local transit services; providing accessible services for students with disabilities; reducing ride times; boosting student safety by relocating bus stops and improving bus supervision; and, funding existing transportation services and investing the savings in enhanced student services. Staff reviewed the announcement and considered the following points:            The current district transportation policy has been in place for decades and reflected previous Ministry of Education policy dating back to a the time when school districts were received specific transportation funding, designed to fulfill Ministry policy, and was based on a 3 year rolling average. The current policy provides for a robust transportation service which has essentially met the needs of the school district. Over the years, other non-policy transportation services have been added (by Board directions), resulting in the provision of service that is above current policy requirements. Many of these decisions were made to address issues related to safety and school closures. Ministry of Education funding for transportation was eliminated many years ago. However, the school district has continued to provide transportation services and travel costs. As part of Becoming Sustainable and during the 2015-16 an 2016-17 budget years, it was identified that transportation services provided are above those required in policy and that the current policy requires revision. The two year plan sustainable plan revealed the need for a transportation policy review and the reduction to out-of-policy runs in the 2016-17 budget. The policy review is intended to address the new governance structure in respect of policies and administrative procedure and to permit transportation services that meet the needs of the current educational environment. During the 2016-17 budget deliberations (reference the Becoming Sustainable document – pages 24 and 25), staff proposed out-of-policy transportation reductions of about $409,620. Also included in the Becoming Sustainable document (reference pages 41 and 22), was the new Active Transportation Program ($30,000). During deliberations and receipt of parent concerns, staff were directed to not to eliminate transportation services that could possibly be perceived as “safety” related. Consequently, the final budget contained about $185,000 in reductions to transportation services. This variance between the proposed reductions and final reductions ($224,000) was essentially funded by through reducing the district’s contingency. In May of 2016, staff commenced the policy review process (reference the May 11, 2016 update to the Board and the update to be given September 14 to the Business Committee – public feedback, item 9.1).
