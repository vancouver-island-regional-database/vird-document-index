Appendix B Board Advisory Committee Terms of Reference Environmental Stewardship Action Plan Advisory Committee Type Board Advisory Committee Purpose To provide recommendations to the Board with respect to the implementation, resourcing and updates to the Environmental Stewardship Action Plan (the “Plan”). Basis of Committee Board Motion Mandate • • • • To provide recommendations to the Board with respect to which Plan goals should be prioritized in a given school year To provide the Board recommendations on resource allocation to assist in preliminary budget deliberations and potential expenditures associated with the annual surplus (if applicable) to support the Plan Receive updates from staff on the yearly progress of the Plan Review the Plan and provide recommendations with respect to any changes that may be required Authority The committee provides information and makes recommendations to the Board to aid the Board’s fulfillment of Policy 104 - Environmental Stewardship Policy. The committee will seek to achieve consensus-based recommendations. Membership The Committee will be chaired by one of the Trustees appointed to the committee. The committee is comprised of the following members: • Trustees (2) one of whom shall be the chair of the ESSAC • The Chair of the Board of Education shall be an ex officio non-voting member • Superintendent 1 Board Advisory Committee Terms of Reference • • • • • • • • Secretary-Treasurer Two members of District Leadership Team – one from Learning Services and one from Facilties A representative from CUPE A representative from DPAC A representative from NDTA A principal or vice-principal Two students One Knowledge Keeper The Committee may also invite district staff, community representatives, and subject matter experts as needed. Meeting Arrangements There shall be four meetings per year as follows: 1. October – Discuss areas of the Plan that should be prioritized in the given school year 2. December – Discuss resource priorities for the upcoming preliminary budget and to recommend to the Board potential allocations associated with annual surplus (if applicable) 3. February – Staff shall report progress to the Committee 4. May – Review and discuss the yearly reporting of progress to the Board and to review the Terms of Reference. Meetings will be held during the school year and may occur online. Additional meetings may be scheduled, as needed, to ensure the Committee fulfills its mandate. The Chair will send meeting agendas to all members electronically at least one week prior to meeting date. Minutes will be recorded each meeting and sent to all committee members before the next meeting. Reporting The Committee will report to the Board in January and June (following its December and May meetings). Review The terms of reference of the committee will be reviewed annually by committee, and any recommended changes will be brought to the Board for approval. 2
