From the Department of Finance Date: November 14, 2019 To: The Board of Education – Business Committee Subject: Financial Update and 1st Quarter Report The purpose of this report is to update the Business Committee with relevant information pertaining to the financial management of the School District as well as provide 1st quarter financials. Data Collection: 1701 – Student Enrolment Background - The Ministry of Education requires that all School Districts in British Columbia provide a three year projected enrolment estimate, due each year in mid-February, in order to facilitate the budgeting and allocation of provincial funding. The District’s approach to enrolment projections is to consider all available and relevant information, to be as conservative as possible in order to avoid preliminary overfunding but to ensure that the calculated estimates provide sufficient supports to students and District operations. Once all relevant information has been compiled and analyzed a final enrolment projection is submitted to the Ministry of Education, who then provides our “estimated” Operating Grant allocation, by March 15th, for the coming school year. The District’s preliminary Annual Budget is based upon this allocation which is then adjusted in December after the September 30 th student data collection has been verified. The information collected on Form 1701 is used to calculate the Districts number of Full Time Equivalent (FTE) students. Total FTE received by Ministry is then added to the Funding Allocation System (FAS), along with the determined allocation per student category. This recalculation is then communicated to Districts in mid-December and confirms the actual Operating Grant. At that time, funding levels may be increased or decreased depending on the accuracy of our estimated enrolment projections, which is then presented in our Amended Annual Budget each February. As well, Ministry may or may not make changes to the per pupil allocation levels as seen in the table below, that will also be communicated by Ministry in December. Average Teacher Salary: The District budgets teacher salaries (FTE) based on the average of our actual costs. With the restored class size and composition language we saw an influx of teachers to the District in late 2017/18, early 2018/19 where the demographics included both young teachers with little experience as well as experienced teachers. Initially we saw the average salary level decrease so we adjusted budget accordingly so not to overstate costs and realize a substantial surplus. However, those teachers with external experience have now provided substantiating documentation to place them on the correct salary grid level, as well, the new teachers have all now incremented to the next salary level. As such, we are seeing an increase in our average teacher salary cost which has now been updated in the budget with a total cost of $1,038,730 ISE Tuition: Revenue for the International Student Program has been reduced in the Budget to account for fewer students enrolled in the program this year. The overall reduction was $125,000 due to 9 less students anticipated/registered at this time per our 1701 Student Data Collection. We will be monitoring this closely to determine if further changes are necessary which, if applicable, will be reflected in the Amended Annual Budget. Rentals and Leases: Rentals and Leases were understated in the 2019/20 Annual Budget to avoid a premature increase prior to confirming year-end figures. The Annual Amended Budget will reflect an anticipated increase in revenue in the amount of $40,000 which better aligns the budget to actuals realized over the last four year.
