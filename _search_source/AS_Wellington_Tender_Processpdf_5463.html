THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET TO: The Business Committee FROM: Chad Dalrymple, Energy Manager DATE: Feb 13, 2014 SUBJECT: Variance from SD68 Purchasing Policy for Wellington Seismic Project Tender REFERENCE: Purchasing Procedure No.4500P section 4.1 4.1 Immediately on receipt of submission, the date and time of receipt shall be recorded on the tender envelope. The initials of the employee receiving the tender shall be affixed in close proximity to the date/time stamp. Purchasing Procedure No.4500P section 5.1 5.1 Tender openings shall be witnessed by two or more school district employees, one of which must be a purchasing department employee. All persons present at the opening, (including non-school district employees) shall be recorded on the tender opening form. BACKGROUND: Unitech has been hired through an extensive RFP process to be our Construction Manager (CM) for the Wellington Seismic project. Under construction management, the CM manages the project on our behalf, which includes organizing and managing the procurement processing. It is Unitech’s recommendation that we tender a majority of the work under one tender with 38 subcontracts. DISCUSSION: SD68’s purchasing policy is written in a manner in which it doesn’t consider the Construction Manager model for procurement. To manage complex construction jobs, we are required to waiver certain sections of our purchasing policy for this project only. Section 4 – Receipt of Tenders As Unitech is our agent, acting as our CM, they want to process the tenders through their office in Vancouver and also accept electronic tender submission. Under this model, there will be no “employee receiving the tender” as well as our policy assumes only paper submissions. Section 5 – Tender Opening Our policy requires two district employees be present at the opening of tenders, with one being from the purchasing department. Under the CM frame work, Unitech is managing the procurement process on our behalf, so we do not need two employees to be present for tender openings. Recommended Variance: To waiver sections 4 and 5 of procedure N0. 4500P for the Wellington Seismic Project and have the Construction Manager: • Prepare tender documents, writes up the Request for Tenders (RFT), conducts and closes the RFT process, reviews received tenders (paper and electronic) for completeness and conformity and prepares tender summaries and recommendations to the District. Review the tender results with the Project Manager. School District responsibility: • Our Buyer will post the tender documents on BC Bid Website, and our Project Manager will attend the opening of the tenders. Process for Approval As this project has already been approved by the Board, if the lowest bid is recommended, the Secretary Treasurer will sign off approval. If a bid other than the low bidder is recommended for award, the Board will need to sign off approval. Upon approval, the CM prepares the letter of intent and CCDC 17 contracts for signatures by the trade contractors and then the District. The School District will ultimately hold the contracts with the trades, not the CM. RECOMMENDATION: That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo – Ladysmith) that this purchasing variance be adopted for the Wellington Seismic Project.
