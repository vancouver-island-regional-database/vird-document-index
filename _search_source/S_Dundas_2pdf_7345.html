Our questions and concerns are as follows:   · Learning group size of 60 students - upon what scientific evidence was the maximum number of 60 students determined as safe for elementary school children? · Impacts on family 'bubble size' - we have followed Provincial public health advice and limited our 'bubble' size. The 'COVID-19: Return to School' plan forces us into a 'mega' bubble with over 120 students and their families, some of whom will not have been as conservative in their interactions as we have (as is evident with any trip to the park, beach, stores etc.).  This is in direct contravention to public health advice to limit the size of our bubble - how can we keep our family safe with this forced bubble expansion? · Ventilation - Given that COVID-19 can be spread through the air, why is the issue of ventilation not addressed in the 'COVID-19: Return to School' plan or in the BC CDC Guidance upon which it is based? We have done our part in helping to flatten the curve since the pandemic started but are genuinely concerned for our family's safety if BC continues with the current 'COVID-19: Return to School' plan that does not appear to build on the lessons learned from the hybrid plan in June. I think we would all prefer our children return to school (with an appropriate plan in place). Please explain how this current plan will continue to keep our, and other BC families, safe. I look forward to your answers to each of our questions.
