Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: September 24, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Trustee Criminal Records Check Policy 2.15 (DRAFT) Background: This report is prepared in response to Board Motion R14/05/28-28: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs the Superintendent/CEO to develop a draft policy/procedures by June 2014 that would require candidates for trustee, once elected, to provide a criminal record check to the District. The policy was recently sent out for notice of motion for the required period of 30 days. A total of eighteen responses were submitted. The responses can be summarized as follows: • 14 respondents registered their support for the draft policy (included were two responses recommending revisions); • 2 respondents registered their lack of support for the draft policy; and • 2 respondents provided revision suggestions without registering their approval or disapproval of the draft policy. Please see below for a revised draft of the Trustee Criminal Records Check Policy, reflective of feedback received from respondents. Please note that some feedback received recommended changes that would require the approval of the Ministry of Education (i.e. asking all individuals interested in becoming a Trustee to complete a Criminal Records Check) and hence could not be considered by a Board of Education. Also of note is the recommendation that the Board of Education participate in the Criminal Records Review Program (CRRP). Given this, the draft policy has been revised to reflect the fact that the responsibility for adjudicating the criminal records check would reside with the CRRP. Board Policy 2.15: Trustee Criminal Records Check This policy applies to Trustees who, like employees, regularly come into contact with students in the course of their duties. The intent of this policy is to ensure a high duty of care with respect to ensuring student safety and well-being. The Board must ensure that anyone deemed to be a risk is not in a position where they work or volunteer with, or have unsupervised access to students under its care and authority. 1 Procedures: 1) Each Trustee, once elected, will apply for a Criminal Records Check (CRC), inclusive of a Vulnerable Sector Search (VSS), through the Ministry of Justice’s Criminal Records Review Program (CRRP). This process will be facilitated by the Secretary-Treasurer. 2) Each Trustee will then be restricted to contact with students under the supervision of a teacher or administrator until the results of the CRC/VSS have been received by the Secretary-Treasurer, and disclosed to the Board. 3) The Secretary-Treasurer will notify the Board of Education upon receipt of a letter from the Deputy Registrar, indicating a Trustee applicant is at risk and cannot volunteer with students. 4) The Board, upon receipt of a notification indicating that a Trustee applicant is at risk and cannot volunteer with students, will take the appropriate action and/or put into place restrictions/limitations to ensure that the individual is not in a position where they work or volunteer with, or have unsupervised access to, students under its care and authority. Consequences of Non-Compliance Any Trustee who fails to submit to a CRC/VSS will be restricted from unsupervised access to schools and students until a completed CRC/VSS is submitted through the Criminal Records Review Program. Recommendation: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves Policy/Procedure 2.15 - Trustee Criminal Record Checks. 2
