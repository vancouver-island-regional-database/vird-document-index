UNFINISHED BUSINESS NEW BUSINESS Cedar Capital Update Mr. Sabo outlined for the plans for the conversion of Cedar Secondary into an elementary school for the Committee. Core changes that will be made:        Create Kindergarten suite Space for StrongStart Classroom conversions (Tech/foods/foods textiles/science/business and general instruction classrooms) Corridor to rear of building (access to new playground) Playground Relocations from Woodbank and NCI New equipment as required The new elementary school will feature a number of enhanced facilities that are already in the building, including:          Large library Two gyms Woodshop Art room Music room Drama stage Servery/storage support Multipurpose room Large administration area Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 13, 2014  2014-2015 Annual Facilities Grant Large counselling area Mr. Sabo reviewed the 2014-2015 AFG plan with the Committee members. He said that the 2013-14 grant for School District 68 was $2.6 million and the district is anticipating a similar amount for the next year. School districts are required to provide the Ministry with a plan for the use of the grant before any funds are advanced. Mr. Sabo told the Business Committee that over the next few years, the Annual Facilities Grant spending plan will focus on the district’s 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan priorities. Projects related to the facilities plan include:  The conversion of Cedar Secondary to an elementary school at a cost of $2 million, with work spread over two years;  Renovations at John Barsby to accommodate incoming Cedar Secondary students  Renovations at Ladysmith Secondary to accommodate Grade 7 students and Cedar Secondary students  Renovations at NDSS to support the move of the VAST program  Relocating portables from Davis Road to Ladysmith Primary and opening any closed classrooms that may be required at Ladysmith Intermediate and North Oyster, due to the closure of Davis Road. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Bard IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Howland Folio B14/03/13-03 That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that the 2014-15 Annual Facility Grant Plan in the value of $2,613,085 be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Dalrymple, Energy Manager,reported out to the Business Committee on the Wellington Seizmic upgrade. He said that the seismic upgrading project at Wellington Secondary has work completed on Blocks B and E, with the next step to go to tender for the remaining blocks, A, D, G, F, M and civil work. A school construction committee is looking at the design of Block F. Mr. Sabo said that the District has been working on several building envelope projects. Work is complete at Coal Tyee and Bayview, and the project at Quarterway will be complete this month. Further, he said as part of the 2013-14 Carbon Neutral Capital Plan that the district replaced the boiler at McGirr Elementary. The district did not Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 13, 2014 receive any funding for this year (2014-15) but in future years is looking at a project at Hammond Bay in partnership with the Regional District of Nanaimo to use heat recovery from the RDN’s sewage treatment plant. As well, the district has purchased a new 84passeger school bus for $139,533. Delivery of the new bus is expected in mid-June. The district will dispose of its oldest spare bus after busing requirements are confirmed in September. Princess Royal Elementary School - Disposition of Property Mr. Turin told the committee that an ad had been placed in the paper stating the Board's interest in disposing of Princess Royal Elementary School.
