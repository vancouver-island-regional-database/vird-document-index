CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Pleasant Valley Grade 7's Kathy Bergman, Principal, Pleasant Valley Elementary, Grade 7's Tayla Skolski and Owen McAlpine & Teacher, Ms. Gannon RE: Grey Cup Pleasant Valley Elementary students, Tayla and Owen shared their recent experience attending the Grey Cup and BC Lions Skills for Life Summit in Vancouver. Jeff Hasenauer, Principal, Rutherford Elementary DJ Thompson, Self-Regulation Coordinator RE: International Baccalaureate (IB) Mr. Thompson and Mr. Hasenauer provided the Education Committee with information on the International Baccalaureate (IB) Primary Years Program (PYP). They attended the 102nd Grey Cup game and along with other students from across the province, they participated in the interactive demonstrations of professions like; welding, carpentry, heavy equipment operating, truck driving and accounting. They also attended workshops that included a few BC Lions players and ministry representatives. This is following Board motion R13/06/26-33, to initiate a Primary IB program at Randerson Ridge Elementary, it was shared that the IB steering committee is ready to move ahead with the next steps in their action plan, which include: 1. IB PYP pre-implementation plan. This phase would consist of a district-wide consultative process, a facility selection process, and a five-year proposed budget plan. 2. Development of the IB PYP implementation action plan. This phase will include the completion of all details leading up to the start-up of an IB PYP. It was presented that the immediate benefit of implementing the PYP will be enrolment. Statistics have shown that independent school enrolment Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held May 6, 2015 has increased steadily approximately 5% per year while public school enrolment is declining in most of the province. Along with providing another program option for parents, the IB program is a highly recognized name worldwide by both parents and universities. The Committee requests that the IB steering committee continue with their current action plan. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT District Assessment Committee Update - Mr. Justin Green / Ms. Robyn Gray Assistant Superintendent, Ms. Gray and Teacher, Mr. Green presented to the Education Committee and update on assessment in the district. A district assessment tool was developed and launched this past fall. The district assessment review committee is currently re-examining the tool and seeking feedback. The district assessment review committee has requested from the Education Committee the approval to pursue an organic, bottom-up, collaborative approach to continue with further revisions on the district's assessment tool. They will return with an update at a future Education Committee meeting. Middle Years Development Instrument (MDI) - Ms. Robyn Gray Assistant Superintendent, Robyn Gray, on behalf of the Department of Learning Services, recommended to the Education Committee that the district does not participate in the MDI at this time. After receiving the report and presentation on MDI by Vice-Principal Learning Services, Jane Carroll, the Education Committee agreed with the recommendation. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY There was no Correspondence Referred From the Regular Board UNFINISHED BUSINESS Multicultural and Race Relations (MRR) and Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity (SOGI) Committee Update Trustee Brzovic and Robinson Assistant Superintendent Bob Esliger gave a handout and overview to the Education Committee and provided them with three recommendations: 1. Develop a new policy of Inclusion – the new policy will be a concise statement, an expression of expectation of all our school communities in bringing about safe, inclusive and socially just schools and it will be the document that holds the district accountable. 2. Develop administrative procedures and envision the committees role – the MCRR recommended to develop individual administrative procedures to reflect the current situations facing the district. A significant update is required regarding racism and the provision of culturally safe environments. 3. Implement key priorities – MCRR and SOGI agreed to three priorities: i) Ensure the Safe Harbour program is adopted at every school. ii) Staff onboarding of current policy (statement of expectation orientation package). iii) Implement “Our Schools Our Community – Taking a Stand for Inclusion” and match it to BC curriculum.
