BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Regular Board Meeting ACTION SHEET DATE: September 28, 2016 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Revised Transportation Policy Background: The district is currently planning the next phase of engaging the community in its review of the current Transportation Policy, and the development of a new policy and administrative procedure that would begin September 2017. Phase One: On April 12, 2016, the district hosted a public meeting to discuss the current Policy. The intent was to gather input which was to help develop guidelines the district should follow to support students getting to and from school. On May 24, 2016, a second public forum provided an opportunity for participants to view a draft of the online survey and provide further feedback. The input provided by the participants formed the basis for this survey. The survey was available online from June 1 to August 31, 2016. There were approximately 1,200 responses during that time. Phase Two: September 28, 2016 (Regular Board): Staff to present draft Transportation Policy and begin 30-day Notice of Motion. November 9, 2016 (Business Committee): Staff presents recommended Transportation Policy for Board approval. November 23, 2016 (Regular Board): Approval of Policy December 14, 2016: (Business Committee) Staff presents draft Transportation Administrative Procedures Recommendation: That the Board of Education receive the Draft Transportation Policy and enter into a 30-day Notice of Motion, during which time the public may provide feedback. SECTION 1 – BOARD GOVERNANCE DRAFT 3.8 Transportation The Board of Education believes in providing eligible students with safe and reliable transportation in a fiscally and environmentally responsible manner. The Board further believes that it may pay all or part of the conveyance of students by school bus or through transportation assistance. Transportation to and from catchment schools is based on the following guidelines: 1. Special Needs 2. Safety and Hazards 3. Distance and Geography The Board, while recognizing that it is not required to provide bus transportation to bring students to and from school, shall make provisions for school bus transportation within the above guidelines and administrative procedures as established by the Superintendent of Schools.
