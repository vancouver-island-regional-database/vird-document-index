That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the School Site Acquisition Charge Bylaw for implementation by the City of Nanaimo, District of Lantzville and Regional District of Nanaimo. Background The purpose of this Action Sheet is for the Board to approve a School Site Acquisition Charge Bylaw, the final step for the District to institute a School Site Acquisition Charge (SSAC) with the City of Nanaimo, District of Lantzville and Regional District of Nanaimo. Recommendation 2 of the Long Range Facilities Plan recommends that the District institute a School Site Acquisition Charge (SSAC). The Local Government Act and School Act allow a district to request that a municipality or regional district institute a development charge to go towards the purchase of future school sites in areas of anticipated growth. Discussion There has been extensive public consultation regarding this SSAC, including during the consultation for the Long Range Facilities Plan. In addition, Administrative Procedure 530 – School Site Acquisition was also implemented to ensure consistent regular review of the need for such a charge. At the Regular Board Meeting of October 27, 2021 the Board passed an SSAC Resolution for the City of Nanaimo, the District of Lantzville and the Regional District of Nanaimo which allowed for a 60 day consultation period. The consultation period is now complete and all three jurisdictions are in agreement with the implementation of an SSAC. Page 3 of 6 Action Sheet: School Site Acquisition Charge Bylaw Page 2 The Bylaw outlines the school site acquisition charges for each type of residential development. When the Bylaw is passed the appropriate Local Governments will be informed. They will then begin to collect funds which will be deposited into the District’s land capital reserve trust fund. The funds can only be used for the acquisition of eligible school sites and fees related to a site acquisition. The funds are intended to fund up to 35% of the cost of a site with the Ministry typically required to fund the remaining costs.
