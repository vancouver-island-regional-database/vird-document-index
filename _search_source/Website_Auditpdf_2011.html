STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Corporate Services Department October 7, 2025 1495-20 Website Audit RECOMMENDATION: That Council direct staff to secure a contract with Kimbo Design to conduct a comprehensive website audit for a total cost of $35,000, to be funded from appropriated equity, and that the Financial Plan be amended accordingly. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The Town’s website currently lacks user-friendliness, with navigation and accessibility issues that limit effective public engagement. To ensure a strategic and informed approach to future redesign efforts, a comprehensive website audit is recommended. This audit will assess the site’s strengths and weaknesses, guiding improvements that align with community needs and modern digital standards. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: N/A INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: The Town's current website has been identified as not user-friendly, with navigation challenges, outdated design elements, and limited accessibility features that hinder residents, businesses, and visitors from efficiently accessing information and services. These usability issues impact public engagement and the overall effectiveness of online communications. Before proceeding with a full website redesign (proposed for 2026), it is recommended that the Town undertake a comprehensive website audit. This audit will evaluate the existing site’s structure, content, functionality, accessibility, and user experience. The findings will provide a clear understanding of the website’s strengths and weaknesses, ensuring that any redesign efforts are strategic, cost-effective, and aligned with community needs. Securing a website audit contract and amending the Financial Plan accordingly will position the Town to make informed decisions and deliver a modern, inclusive, and usercentered digital platform. In accordance with Purchasing Policy 05-1790-D, staff requested written quotations from three local website design companies. One of the companies had limited experience with local government websites. The second company had experience with local governments and some accessibility and data management expertise, but these areas were less prominent in their proposal compared to other submissions. The third company, Kimbo Design, requested a meeting with staff. Their presentation showcased a range of work on other local government websites including Qualicum Beach, Squamish and Ucluelet, all of which are easily accessible and user-friendly. They also offered flexible options regarding the scope and depth of the audit features to be included; staff therefore recommend securing a contract with them. Should Council approve this request, the results of the audit will inform a future Request for Proposal for a website re-design, proposed for 2026. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Decide that a comprehensive audit of the website is not necessary. 2. Postpone the website audit to the 2026 budget cycle. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: A website audit was not included in the 2025 budget. Staff recommend funding the audit through the appropriated equity funds reserve for general government corporate projects, which currently has an available balance of approximately $400,000. LEGAL IMPLICATIONS: N/A CITIZEN/PUBLIC RELATIONS IMPLICATIONS: The Public have identified usability issues with the current website and have expressed a need for more accessible information about the Town and its ongoing projects. Conducting an initial assessment of the site's strengths and weaknesses will yield valuable insights to inform a thoughtful redesign and better meet the community’s expectations. INTERDEPARTMENTAL INVOLVEMENT/IMPLICATIONS: The audit process will include consultation with all departments to ensure a comprehensive and coordinated approach to the website review, with each department’s needs carefully considered and addressed.
