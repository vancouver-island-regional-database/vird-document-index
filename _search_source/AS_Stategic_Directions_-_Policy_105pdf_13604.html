NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: May 13, 2026 Strategic Directions Committee Robyn Gray – Superintendent Policy 105 – nuċuma:t sya:ys “working together as one” Recommendation The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive the Draft Revised Policy 105 – Justice Equity Diversity Inclusion, including the renaming of the Policy to nuċuma:t sya:ys “working together as one” and circulate as a notice of motion for 30 days, utilizing the consultation process as per Board Governance, Section 210 – Policy Development. Background This action sheet is intended to outline the process by which the Board appointed Ad Hoc Committee to revise Policy 105 – Justice Equity Diversity and Inclusion determined the recommended changes to the Policy. It will also highlight the recommended changes and rationale. Discussion Process On June 25, 2025, the Board passed the following motion: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) amend the Terms of Reference to the Safe Schools Committee as presented at the Strategic Directions Committee on June 11, 2025. This work was guided by yutustana:at, Elder, whose leadership helped ground the discussions in respect, cultural awareness, and inclusivity. Subsequent meetings continued to emphasize the importance of centering student voice throughout the process. Ultimately, this inclusive and collaborative approach led to the development of the recommendation now being presented to the Board. Substantive Changes The recommended changes to the policy are signiﬁcant and include: 1. Structural Shift: The current policy is primarily structured as a policy statement with expectations and behavioural commitments (e.g., code of conduct, inclusive practices). The draft policy: • Is reorganized into a Vision – Purpose – Objectives – Deﬁnitions format; • Aligns more closely with Section 100 (Core Values) structure • Moves from “what is expected” to “who we are” and “how we work together” 2. Incorporation of hul’q’umi’num and Reconciliation The draft policy: • Introduces: hul’q’umi’num language and teachings • Embeds land acknowledgement and teachings directly into the policy vision • Reﬂects the Board’s deeper commitment to the Syeyutsus principles, reconciliation and local First Nations perspectives. Action Sheet: Policy 105 – nuċuma:t sya:ys “working together as one” Page 3 3. Expanded Emphasis on Reconciliation, Decolonization, and Anti-Racism The current policy Includes general commitments to inclusion, equity, human rights, and addressing marginalization. The draft policy: • Integrates decolonization as an operational principle; • Commitment to anti-racism as an active, ongoing process; and • Includes action-oriented languages rather than the awareness focus of the existing policy. 4. Extensive use of deﬁnitions The draft Includes a signiﬁcant expansion of deﬁnition intended to bring awareness, but more importantly, consistency to the district. It also is more explicit in its recognition of legislative protections and commitments associated with inclusion such the DRIPA and the Human Rights Code. 5. Aligns with the Board’s Strategic Goal of Employee and Student Wellness by enhancing the focus on belonging and holistic well-being. Speciﬁcally, it adds emphasis on: Belonging Curiosity and engagement Social, emotional, and lifelong learning Recognition that success is broader than academic achievement • • • • 6. Accessible language Finally, the language has been made accessible and modern. Conclusion The Committee is proud to provide the recommendation and want to recognize the participation of our partners, and particularly our students and yutustana:at who have helped create a policy that aligns with the Board’s goals, value and vision and will serve us well for years to come.
