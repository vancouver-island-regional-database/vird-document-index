Phil Turin, CPA, CGA, Secretary-Treasurer pturin@sd68.bc.ca School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, British Columbia V9R 3K6 Phone: 250 754-5521 Fax : 250 741-5248 Mr. Turin: On behalf of the Board of Directors of the Ferns Association for Young Children and Families, and our chair/president Mrs. Shannon Gillespie, I am pleased to submit to you our letter of intent to continue our partnership with the school district in supporting youth and children and parenting teens with a formal request to prepare and submit a joint application to the Ministry of Children and Family Development (MCFD). This application will be to apply for the newly released Targeted Major Capital Grants of a maximum of $500,000.00. With this grant, NLPS and The Ferns will be able to finance the renovation or building of another classroom or portable/modular building to serve as a licensed infant and toddler childcare- 12 spaces- to enhance the Young Mothers classroom currently at N.D.S.S.. Alternatively and depending on the Learning Outcomes Team’s recommendations of directions for NLPS, a second classroom will also be built along with the first, to serve as a licensed 16 space 3-5 yr early years program. As many of the teen parents do not reach graduation completion before their child(ren) reach the age of 3 years, the model of attaching a 3-5 yr classroom to the infant and toddler classroom can ensure greater success rates. As you are aware, Little Ferns Early Learning Centre and Y.P.P. (Young Parent Program) started operations at John Barsby Community School September of 2007 with a 16 space licensed 3-5 yr classroom and a 12 space infant and toddler classroom housed together in a new modular building. This was created with the same Targeted Major Capital Grant as released by MCFD in 2006. At that time, Mr. Pete Sabo (Operations and Facilities), Mrs. Elizabeth Pennell (Early Years), Mr Bob Esliger (Student Services), Mr. Mike Munro (Superintendent), Mr. David Green (Secretary –Treasurer) and myself, representing The Ferns, submitted the original grant application. I then worked with Brent Spohn as well as Elizabeth Pennell and Pete Sabo on the plans for the centre and its programs from blueprints to opening September 2007. At this time, Little Ferns is full to teen and community parents’ children and the district has reported (Tim Davie, Principal, N.D.S.S.) an additional 37 young moms needing childcare to support their learning. In the District Achievement Contract 2013-2014, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools the vision states “Success for All- Learning Together”. We believe that it is in the best interest of the district, from both a business perspective and student outcomes focus that we continue with what is already exceptionally high quality childcare programs and effective student support services that continually achieve positive results. The Ferns and specifically Little Ferns YPP has contributed significantly to the graduation rates of both aboriginal and non-aboriginal teen parents in our district. We have partnered with the various secondary school programs including junior and senior alternatives and VAST. We continue to share with the district’s goals to meet each student’s unique needs and to enhance the early year’s experiences for vulnerable infants and toddler who in turn, become students of NLPS. A YPP can and does meet the district’s goal to facilitate personalized learning (with an emphasis on flexibility and choice). We note that in the above mentioned document, the district specifically aims to ensure the “Introduction of an inter-agency, multi- disciplinary Young Parenting Program (YPP)”. This has been achieved with Little Ferns YPP at John Barsby Community School with teen parents attending general programs in any of the secondary schools and alternative programs, or the Adult Basic Education program at V.I.U. With the expansion to a second site for more licensed YPP childcare, a second model that combines the childcare program with a designated Teen parent Classroom, can be achieved. As the “Young Moms” classroom at N.D.S.S. started September 2013, with this grant we can move forward to better support the students to remain in school working towards graduation by enhancing this model. Of course the YPP specific additional programming, such as Nobody’s Perfect Parenting Group, Home Visitations, BBT (Bounce Back and Thrive), You Make the Difference and the Teen/at Risk program Right From The Start, will be offered to the young moms, in a collaboration between both the YPP childcare staff and the Young Moms teaching and support staff. This already happens at the current site, Little Ferns. Amy Collum and designates of The Ferns/Little Ferns will work with operations and facilities in regards to the licensing of the childcare center itself, the HR responsibilities of securing and supervising qualified and YPP experienced staff, and of overseeing the early years program that supports the E.D.I., for the infants, toddler and possibly preschoolers at this new site. The criteria that MCFD requires of the applicants include that those applying “Have the knowledge, skills and experience to undertake the project “. This being a YPP specific project, we can assure you based on the outcomes of our first 7 years as the district’s YPP programs, our relationship with the Vancouver Island YPP coordinators group and the B.C. Alliance for Young Parents of the B.C. Council for the Family, that we will only continue to bring to our partnership with the district, our specific skills, training and education so that this criteria is met and exceeded. “What is already done and done well, do again.” Sincerely, Amy C Collum, M.A.Ed., E.C.E., B.A., C.Y.C., play therapy
