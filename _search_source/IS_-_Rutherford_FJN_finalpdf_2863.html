Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION REGULAR BOARD MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: June 22, 2016 TO: Board of Education FROM: Graham Roberts, Secretary-Treasurer SUBJECT: 2015-16 Capital Plan - Update on Frank J. Ney Addition Request Background: The original 2015-16 5-Year Capital Plan was submitted in November 2015. This plan was revised and resubmitted to the Ministry of Education in January 2016 based on school consolidation/closure decisions made by the Board on December 2, 2015. The District’s first priority in the revised plan is for an addition at Frank J. Ney Elementary. This request was not included as a supported project in the Ministry of Education letter dated May 24, 2016 as it did not align with provincial capital priorities of seismic mitigation and safety concerns or expansion needs. The Board bylaw on the closure of Rutherford is as follows: Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) permanently closes Rutherford Elementary School, 5840 Hammond Bay Road, Nanaimo, BC, V9T 5M6; Facility No. 068077; on June 30, 2017 or later, subject to the completion of a new wing at Frank J. Ney School. In the event capital funding is not approved for the Frank J. Ney construction, or construction is delayed such that it is not expected to be completed by September 2018, the issue of the closure of Rutherford Elementary School will be revisited by the Board. As the closure of Rutherford Elementary is contingent on capital funding for Frank J. Ney, district staff have been in discussions with Ministry of Education staff to determine next steps to garner Ministry support for the addition at Frank J. Ney. Ministry staff understand the merits of the business case (Project Identification Report) on the capital avoidance savings and operational efficiencies created on the potential closure of Rutherford along with the capital renewal this project would create. Staff have been encouraged by the Ministry to include this project as part of our 2016-17 5-Year Capital Plan submission which is due September 9, 2016. Therefore, staff continue to further build the business case for this project by: o o o Updating the seismic assessment of Rutherford Elementary Further developing the costing for an addition at Frank J. Ney Elementary Further developing actual deferred maintenance costs at Rutherford Elementary As information is complied, staff will share this information with Ministry staff in an effort to secure Ministry support of the Frank J. Ney addition. Also included: Revised Five Year Capital Plan Summary (January 2016)
