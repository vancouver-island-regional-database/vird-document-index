UNFINISHED BUSINESS NEW BUSINESS Cedar Capital Update Mr. Sabo outlined for the plans for the conversion of Cedar Secondary into an elementary school for the Committee. Core changes that will be made: • • • • • • • Create Kindergarten suite Space for StrongStart Classroom conversions (Tech/foods/foods textiles/science/business and general instruction classrooms) Corridor to rear of building (access to new playground) Playground Relocations from Woodbank and NCI New equipment as required The new elementary school will feature a number of enhanced facilities that are already in the building, including: • • • • • • • • • Large library Two gyms Woodshop Art room Music room Drama stage Servery/storage support Multipurpose room Large administration area Page 4 of 17 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 13, 2014 • 2014-2015 Annual Facilities Grant Large counselling area Mr. Sabo reviewed the 2014-2015 AFG plan with the Committee members. He said that the 2013-14 grant for School District 68 was $2.6 million and the district is anticipating a similar amount for the next year. School districts are required to provide the Ministry with a plan for the use of the grant before any funds are advanced. Mr. Sabo told the Business Committee that over the next few years, the Annual Facilities Grant spending plan will focus on the district’s 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan priorities. Projects related to the facilities plan include: • The conversion of Cedar Secondary to an elementary school at a cost of $2 million, with work spread over two years; • Renovations at John Barsby to accommodate incoming Cedar Secondary students • Renovations at Ladysmith Secondary to accommodate Grade 7 students and Cedar Secondary students • Renovations at NDSS to support the move of the VAST program • Relocating portables from Davis Road to Ladysmith Primary and opening any closed classrooms that may be required at Ladysmith Intermediate and North Oyster, due to the closure of Davis Road. Page 14 of 17 7.1 From: Tyler@Cinnabar Valley [mailto:tyler@cinnabarfarms.com] Sent: Sunday, May 04, 2014 8:07 PM To: Phil Turin Cc: Pete Sabo; Brian Hackwood Subject: Rotary Bowl Lighting Proposal Hi Phil, As requested I am submitting our proposal to add lighting to the 100M straight at Rotary Bowl for completion by November 2014. We have been working on this project with school district staff for the past 18 months and received verbal approval to proceed with the project and a quote to install the lighting in December of 2012. At that point we moved forward to secure funding through the Nanaimo Rotary Club, a long time partner in Rotary Bowl. We received $10,000 dollars from the Nanaimo Rotary Club in January 2014 and have since held a Top Soil sale that netted our club over $15,000 (750+ supporters) to put towards the lighting project. There are also several businesses in town that have contributed time and money towards this lighting project. We have now secured all the funds necessary to add lighting to Rotary Bowl and there are 1000+ users of the facility that can’t wait to run under lights this fall and winter. A bit about Nanaimo Track and Field club and Rotary Bowl Users: The Nanaimo Track and Field Club has expanded the athletic base and number of athletes training over the past several years and currently have 165 athletes training ranging in age from 6-80 years. As a result we will have 10-20+ athletes competing at the National Youth and Junior level in the coming years. Year round training is now being offered not only to our youth and juniors, but also to our junior development athletes (aged 9-13) and masters. This past winter we had training sessions at the track 4 nights per week with 50+ athletes attending. Currently we are using small construction lights and head lamps to provide some light so we can train after school. In order to facilitate larger scale training in a safe and secure manner we have put together a lighting plan with the school district management team that suits our needs and adds value to the current facility. Lighting the facility would allow year round training for all of our athletes and many other groups in the community. Track lighting would establish our club and Nanaimo as a destination for winter track & field training and enable us to grow our winter programs through recruitment and expanded training options. Track lighting would also enable our club to host track events in the evening which could include National Track League meets, local elementary meets, local elementary meets and Q track series. Other user groups at Rotary Bowl include Running Room, Front Runners, and Bastion Running Group. All of these organizations run clinics for the ever growing road race community in Nanaimo. We have estimated that 500-1000 different people use the Rotary Bowl facility each week year round. I have also attached a PDF that documents the history of construction and funding of facilities at Rotary Bowl for your information and a power point presentation I would like to use at the meeting. I look forward to speaking at the SD board meeting May 14th at 6PM. Please let me know if you require any further information.
