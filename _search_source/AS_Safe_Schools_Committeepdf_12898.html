NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 11, 2025 Strategic Direc�ons Commitee Robyn Gray, Superintendent Safe Schools Committee Recommendation The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) amend the Terms of Reference to the Safe Schools Committee as presented. Background This action sheet is intended to highlight recommended changes to the Safe Schools Committee, focus its attention on a specific task and attempt to further incorporate student voice in the work of the Committee. Discussion The Safe Schools Committee has achieved many positive things for the district over its existence. Previous focus on inclusion and anti-racism are two examples. However, stakeholders and trustees have highlighted the need for focus to ensure the best outcomes for the district and to make effective use of time of members of the committee. There are four major changes being proposed: 1. 2. 3. 4. Focusing the committee on the single task of drafting the JEDI Policy (formerly Inclusion Policy); Enhancing the roll of student voice on the committee; Changing the scope of the committee to be ad hoc; and Changing the name of the committee to reflect its updated mission. JEDI Policy The Board renamed the JEDI Policy from the Inclusion Policy. Updating the Inclusion Policy has been a priority for the Board for a number of years but unfortunately the work has not meaningfully progressed. Tasking the Safe Schools Committee to focus on the Policy with the participation of the Superintendent will ensure the Policy progresses. Student Voice The Student Voice Circle has highlighted safety concerns as part of their ongoing meetings. By expanding the Committee to include students in this work their fundamental voice will be represented. New terms of reference will involve the views of students to ensure their meaningful participation. Action Sheet: Safe Schools Committee Page 2 Ad Hoc The committee would be shifted to be an ad hoc committee with one specific task. Following the completion of the JEDI Policy the committee will cease. This does not mean that safety issues cannot be addressed in future. However, the issues may be addressed with future ad hoc committees. It should be noted that the district has two additional advisory committees. The Board’s desire to ensure the committees are effective will be assisted by this recommendation. Conclusion The Safe Schools Committee has done excellent work over the past years and will now have the opportunity to continue that tradition with the change in focus, duration and name.
