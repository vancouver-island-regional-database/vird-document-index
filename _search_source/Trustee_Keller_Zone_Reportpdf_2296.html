Highlights of Visit Wellington Secondary: I attended this school for an introductory visit. Mr. Lintott provided me with a tour of the facility. I was pleased to see the use of technology within the school. I had the opportunity to meet some of the teaching and support staff as well as some of the students. School Updates Wellington  The basketball teams have just wrapped up another successful season, competing well at the Island championships.  The music program just returned from having competed at the famous Lionel Hampton Jazz Festival in Idaho.  The school now has a provincial champion in boys wrestling.  The school now has gold medal winners at the Skills Canada Competition held last week at VIU.  Course selection for students for the 2019/20 school year is now underway.  Preparations will begin shortly for the schools foray into the Northern Games, the annual tech week and the musical theatre production. Rock City  Staff are continuing to work on the goal of Differentiating instruction to target specific skills in literacy to engage and motivate our learners at the highest levels during our PLCs.  Using our data from the Fall NLPS assessment, we structured our learning support schedule to support students for Round I.  From teacher input and report card data, we are presently doing a Joyful Literacy Blitz, targeting specific literacy skills for students K-7 for Round 2.  To celebrate reading with their families, we are hosting a “Dr. Seuss Family Literacy Night”. On Tuesday, March 5th, Rock City families are invited to bring their children, aged birth to Grade 3, to the school for an evening of stories, snacks and prizes. Trustee Keller Zone Report  The student leadership team sponsored a Sweater Day in honour of recognizing climate change and how it affects wildlife and our environment. They sold hot chocolate to raise funds to virtually adopt a Polar Bear.  On February 21st, the second Family Night was held where students and families joined in the fun games, crafts and other activities arranged by third year Child and Youth Care Students from VIU. A big thank you to Susan M’Gonigle, our community coordinator, for helping to organize this evening.  For Anti-Bullying/Pink Shirt Day, on February 27th, students and adults were encouraged to wear pink shirts to both show school spirit and to help raise awareness of the importance of having the often-silent majority stand up against unhealthy and bullying behaviours. The Student Leadership Team led an assembly for the school, explaining the differences between bullying and conflict.  The Community School Coordinator received a $3500 (Go Grant) for all the students of Rock City to examine the Garry Oak Ecosystem at Neck Point Park. Nanaimo Science and Sustainability Society (NS3) will be the facilitators for the school. This is taking place next week.  The school received a grant for a 7 day Dance Residency program (DANCEPL3Y) to come to Rock City to lead instruction in all genres of Dance for all students, K-7. This culminated in a Dance Celebration which many parents attended. It was a very uplifting and inspiring experience for teachers and students. There was a teacher workshop during one lunch hour. Departure Bay  A sweater was recently held to turn down the heat as part of the District’s Energy Cup Challenge.  Hot chocolate was served at the school as part of – everyone BYOM – mugs – by donation. The school raised over $200 and have symbolically adopted a Snowy Owl and Polar Bear from the World Wildlife Federation. The school was also able to symbolically adopt J35 (Tahlequah) through the Ocean Wise program. The school is looking forward to receiving the kit that provides information on J35, the pod and how the resident killer whales are being affected by people.
