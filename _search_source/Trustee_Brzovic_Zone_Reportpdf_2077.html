Trustee Brzovic – Zone Report Zone Report – Tania Brzovic Cinnibar: The school is using the Virtues Project. The virtue for this month is DETERMINATION. In March it was COOPERATION. I attended a special event for Ms Dodd’s Kindergarten class. The students performed a short play in the multipurpose room, then went back to their classroom to read to their parents. It was terrific to see the progress they have made so far this year. Cedar Elementary: I spent some time in Ms Hibberson’s classroom, reading with students & learning about the Six Cedars. In March, they did a Kindness challenge for the two week leading up to Spring Break. This fits in with social responsibility, which has been the PLC focus. I attended the Cub Car races in March. The students built their own cars & raced them. They were impressive! The tracks that the students are using are made of wood & they are reaching a point where they should be replaced. There were staff there from the Trades trailer who told me that at this point it would be more cost effective to replace them with tracks made of a different material (possibly aluminum?) as they would then last forever. Island ConnectEd: Staff have been looking at best practice for online course structures & they have been investigating practices at NIDES, including looking at the CANVAS LMS. The main focus for the remainder of their PLCs is to enhance student engagement in online courses & look at common best practices when redesigning curriculum. Learning Alternatives: Staff have completed a course on Bringing Mental Health Into Schools. In their PLCs, they are pursuing a range of inquiry questions around creating belonging for students & families, effectively using Google Classroom, reconciliation, literacy & numeracy and capacity building. Real focus on giving students hands on experiences & connecting with community agencies. connection with guest presentations and co-facilitating mental wellness groups with outside agency partners Cedar High: I learned about the work the staff did in February at the pro D day… Trustee Brzovic – Zone Report Nine staff members building presented 30-45 minutes each on tech opportunities and how they integrate them. These included Desmos, Google Read/Write, Podcasting, Adobe Illustrator, Google Sites, Blogging, Twitter, iMovie, Ipads in education, and the Flip Class methodology. At a recent PLC, they, held an in service session where all came together as a group and had Snuneymuxw Elder Sandra Good come in and present on the local reallarea. This is a presentation that she has now been taking to classrooms at Cedar. The purpose of the in service was to have teachers prepared to lead around the topic. Other areas of learning include: project-based learning, the circle of courage, the new Physical Health and Education curriculum, arduinos, electronics, and remote control planes and integrating these into the classroom, and finally a group working on enhancing science lab opportunities for students. Trustee Barron & I both attended High Ground conference hosted by the Centre for Civic Goverance. The opening forum on Women in Leadership really demonstrated the importance of inclusive practices to make it easier for a variety of voices to be heard & participate. The presentation on child care spaces was very informative. There are many potential opportunities for our district to access funds to support child care initiatives. The Ministry would really like to see schools working in partnerships with other agencies to address this issue. And the talk continues to see early childhood issues moved into the Ministry of Education. The last forum of the first day was broadly referenced as being about inclusion, though it looked at it from a different angle than one might expect. For example, one of the speakers presented on the way the current capital funding envelope for school construction calculates space needs, & pointed out some concerns about this formula. In a more traditional vein, Trustee Jennifer Reddy talked about forums & focus groups the VSB has done to learn more about concerns related to racism in schools, and formulate strategies to address these issues. The last session I want to highlight is the panel I attended on reconciliation. It stressed that reconciliation is a process, and that it takes time. The speakers reminded us that we have to be open to where the conversations we a having with our Indigenous partners take us, and not be too focussed on timelines & goals. The focus must be on true, authentic relationship building.
