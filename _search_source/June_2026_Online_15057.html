The new boat ramp is expected to improve safe and reliable access to the Salish Sea for community members, supporting both transportation and traditional harvesting activities that have sustained Coast Salish peoples for generations. Access to the water remains deeply connected to Stz’uminus culture, identity, and way of life. Community members rely on the Salish Sea to harvest traditional foods such as fish, crab, shellfish, and other marine resources that continue to play an important role in cultural practices, food security, and the passing down of knowledge between generations. The project represents more than just new infrastructure — it reflects ongoing efforts to strengthen the Nation and invest in projects that will benefit families now and into the future. A new boat ramp at Shell Beach is also being considered. As construction continues, community members are reminded to keep the area clear to ensure the safety of workers, equipment operators, and the public. Heavy equipment and construction crews will be active in the area throughout the duration of the project. The expected completion date is June 18, 2026. Lyme disease affects people of all ages. The CDC notes that it is most common in children, older adults, and others such as firefighters and park rangers who work outside and have higher exposure to ticks. WHAT IS LYME DISEASE? 10 Outdoor Safety Tips Be Tick Aware Wear Tick Repellent: Repellents with DEET, picaridin or lemon eucalyptus oil are the most effective. Create a Tick-Safe Zone at Home: Prune trees, clear leaves and brush, keep deer and other wild animals out of your yard. Wear a hat & tuck in hair if possible. Avoid sitting on logs or leaning against trees. Wear long pants tucked into high socks or boots. Treat Pets Monthly: Animal fur can act like a “tick magnet,” exposing you when you snuggle with your pet. Wear shoes— no bare feet or sandals. If you find a tick, remove it properly. Apply repellent on skin and permethrin on clothing. Walk on trails. Avoid long grass & bushes. Symptoms To Watch For What If You Get Bitten? Neck Pain Headache Don’t squeeze, twist or squash the tick. Don’t burn it with a match or cover it with Vaseline. Fatigue Muscle Pain Use fine-point tweezers or a special tickremoving tool. Grasp the tick as close to the skin as possible. Sweats Chills Rash Joint Pain Fever Sleep Issues Check out your symptoms at www. lymedisease.org/symptomchecker for printable results you can give your doctor. 27 Wear a long sleeve shirt fitted at the wrists. Do a tick check immediately and 3 days after being outside. Pull the tick straight out with steady, even pressure. Disinfect the bite area and wash your hands. Across our communities, summer employment gives youth the chance to gain valuable work experience while learning skills that will benefit them for years to come. For many young people, a summer job is their very first introduction to the workforce. Showing up on time, communicating with coworkers, managing responsibilities, and learning how to work as part of a team are all life skills that cannot always be taught in a classroom. These experiences help youth develop independence, professionalism, and a stronger sense of selfconfidence. Summer employment also allows youth to explore different career paths and interests. Whether they are working in administration, recreation, public works, cultural programs, or community services, every opportunity helps them discover new strengths and talents. These experiences can shape future education and career choices while helping them build resumes and references for future employment. Beyond personal growth, hiring youth strengthens our communities. Young people bring fresh energy, creativity, and new ideas into the workplace. When communities invest in youth employment opportunities, they are investing in future leaders who will continue to support and contribute to the community for generations to come. Applying for summer jobs can also teach important lessons about perseverance and preparation. Learning how to create a resume, complete an application, and attend an interview are valuable skills that will help youth throughout their lives. Even if a young person does not get the first job they apply for, the experience itself helps build resilience and confidence. Youth are encouraged to take advantage of every opportunity available to them this summer. Whether it is their first job or another step in their employment journey, every experience helps build a strong foundation for the future.
