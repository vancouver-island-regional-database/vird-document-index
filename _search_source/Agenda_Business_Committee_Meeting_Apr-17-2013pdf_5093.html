FROM THE FACILITIES DEPARTMENT Annual Facilities Grant (AFG) 2013-2014 Mr. Sabo reviewed the proposed priorities for the 2013-14 Annual Facilities Grant with ther Business Committee. The grant, which is expected to be about $2.6 million for School District 68, is for school facilities projects such as roof replacements, mechanical and electrical system upgrades. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Curley Folio B13/03/13-03 Wellington Seismic Upgrade Update That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education that the 2013-2014 Annual Facility Grant Plan in the value of $2,613,085 be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Sabo updated the Business Committee on the Wellington Secondary seismic project. He said that The Capital Project Agreement with the Ministry of Education has been signed and the Board has passed a bylaw authorizing the project. He said that staff are working on issuing the Construction Manager Request for Proposals and design meetings with consultants and school representatives. The project is expected to take more than three years, with completion in 2016. Minutes - March 13, 2013 Page 4 of 8 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 13, 2013 UNFINISHED BUSINESS NDSS Pete Sabo updated the Business Committee on the discussions the district has been having with City of Nanaimo traffic staff and the RDN Transit about improvements that could be made to improve safety along Wakesiah Avenue in front of NDSS. He said that some of the changes would be made by the city and others by the school district. It is expected that the plan will be finalized soon, with work to take place during the summer. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS Budget 2013-2014 Mr. Turin reviewed the proposed budget to the Business Committee and asked staff to speak to the various proposals on the senior management recommendations. These recommendations dealt with how the district can deal with a $4.7 million shortfall in the 2013-14 school year. Minutes - April 10, 2013 Page 6 of 8 6.2 Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 10, 2013 Mr. Turin said that it is proposed that $2 million of the $4.7 million comes from $500,000 of unrestricted surplus from the 2011-12 budget; $500,000 of staffing contingencies from the 2012-13 budget; and $1 million from a “clawback” of 2012-13 supplies accounts. The budget strategies that have been proposed to balance next year’s budget are: • • • • • • • • • • • • • Relocating the Davis Road and Quarterway Elementary English stream students to neighbouring elementary schools, leaving the two schools with single-track French Immersion programs Start a transition towards the elimination of the junior alternative program Closing the District Resource Centre at the old Dufferin Crescent school building Reconfiguration of a number of positions in the Learning Services Department, including the elimination of several positions Not hiring to fill a vacant accounting position Closing the district’s print shop operations Reduction of two caretaker positions in the Facilities Department Reductions in a number of Facilities Department Supplies accounts Reduction of release time expenditures Reduction of the planned spending on the district’s technology plan Reallocation of a number of positions to be funded by special purpose grants rather than the operating budget An increase in facilities rental rates by about 5 percent Changing the way that Protection Island students receive ferry transportation Mr. Turin said that at this time, the draft budget does not include savings from an extended spring break next year because the district is currently discussing the proposal with the NDTA, as per the collective agreement.
