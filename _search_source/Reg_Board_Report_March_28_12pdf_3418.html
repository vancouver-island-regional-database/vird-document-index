Superintendent/CEO’s Report March 28, 2012 Regular Board Meeting A. Update on the Superintendent/CEO’s Priorities (2011-2012) I. Development of a Strong Senior Management and Board of Education Team: Four Board development sessions have been undertaken to date, and a fifth is scheduled for March 31. Topics have included: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. 6. Administrative Roles, Responsibilities, Authority, and Accountability; The Individual Trustee Role; The Board Code of Conduct; The Policy Governance Model and a Framework for the Development of Board Policy; and The Establishment of Board Governance Policies. The development of a Board Vision, Mission, and Goals that will form the foundation of the District’s strategic plan, which will begin development on March 31, with a full day session with the Board and Senior Management team. Following this initial planning session, it is anticipated that the Board and Senior Management team will embark on a District-wide consultation process, and feedback will be sought from our school communities around maintaining and enhancing a quality public education in Nanaimo-Ladysmith schools. It is also important to note that the Board has committed to regular Planning and Development meetings that are in addition to regular Board and Committee meetings, and this will allow the Board and Senior Management team to regularly check in with respect to strategic planning. II. Relationship Building: Internal and External Stakeholders & Supporting the Learning Agenda:     To date, I have visited 36 schools, as well as the Learning Alternatives Sites; I continue to meet with VIU leadership and attended the February 23 meeting between the Board of Education and VIU’s Board of Governors; Met with the Elementary Counselors to discuss their perspectives on effective interventions for vulnerable students; Met with Wellington Secondary School staff for classroom visits and discussions over lunch; 1       Visited teachers Mary-Lynn Epps and Terrill MacDonald for their Grades 3, 4 and 5 Learning Showcases at Randerson Ridge Elementary School (based on the “Community of Learners” model); Joined City of Nanaimo and SD 68 senior staff for a liaison meeting; Met with District Speech Pathologist Catherine Brunt and SET BC Consultant Maureen LaFLeche to discuss assistive technology for special needs students (and the emerging world of educational “apps”); Met with District Support Services Teachers to discuss a variety of topics including selfregulated learning and response to intervention; Attended a Chamber of Commerce Luncheon and had an opportunity to gain some insight into the City of Nanaimo’s strategic planning process; and Attended a teleconference with BC Hydro representatives, where SD 68’s Brian Kingsley presented a detailed overview of the District’s energy management plan – and the significant savings realized by the District since its inception. B. Teacher Job Action Update:   With the passing of Bill 22, The Education Improvement Act, teachers have returned to regular duties. District exempt staff are no longer supervising students, and elementary students will return to regular recess schedules on Monday, April 2. In addition, a letter has been issued by James Gorman, Deputy Minister, indicating that teachers are expected to produce Term 2 and Final report cards this year. A team that includes the Superintendent, Secretary-Treasurer, Board Chair and a Principal will be attending Ministry meetings in Richmond on April 2 and 3 in order to become more familiar with Bill 22 and its regulations, including the new Learning Improvement Fund. 2
