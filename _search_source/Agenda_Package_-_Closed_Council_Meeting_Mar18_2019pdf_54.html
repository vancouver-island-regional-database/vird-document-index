Page 6 of 17 INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: The PRCAC Terms of Reference state that the initial citizen representative appointments consist of two members serving a one-year term and three members serving a two-year term ending June 30 or until the representative is replaced by Council. All subsequent terms are for two years. This is to allow for staggered terms, thus ensuring some continuity on the committee with new appointments each year. As with all Town Committees, terms are limited to a maximum of six consecutive years served, with a mandatory break for at least one year. With the extension of the current members’ terms to January 2019, the appointment cycle is delayed by a year. Therefore it is proposed that three terms be extended for one year, in order to accommodate the need for staggered terms. Since 2018, one citizen representative has resigned which has left just three citizen representatives remaining along with one representative from Area H. All three citizen representatives have indicated their willingness to stay on for one more year, should Council decide to reappoint them. Staff have also requested confirmation of the Area G and Stz’uminus First Nation representatives to the Committee so that the PRCAC can be brought up to full complement. Council reviews the applications for the Committees, reappoints Committee members and, as appropriate, holds interviews with the applicants. Recruitment for two new citizen members took place on the Town’s website, social media and via Town email. In addition, an advertisement appeared in the Ladysmith Chronicle. The application period was February 28 to March 12. It is requested that Council consider reappointing/appointing or scheduling interviews with individuals interested in filling vacancies on the PRCAC for a new term of either one or two years, as outlined in this report. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to appoint applicants directly to the Committee, or can schedule interviews before making appointments. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS; N/A LEGAL IMPLICATIONS; N/A CITIZEN/PUBLIC RELATIONS IMPLICATIONS: Appointing citizens to Town committees helps to keep citizens involved in Town business, and provides additional feedback for Council.
