Trustee Zone Report 22-Feb-2017 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  2017 Vancouver Island Parent Conference – DPAC offered to pay $140 registration fee for up to 10 people attending this year’s conference, the theme of which was “Connecting the Dots: School, Home & Community”. The event will be held at the Royal Bay Secondary School in Colwood this Saturday (Feb 25th).  Act of Kindness Month – The focus at Ladysmith Intermediate school this month was on acts of kindness, which includes today’s Anti-bullying Day (Pink Shirt Day) – today! (Feb 22nd) Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  Friendship Fruit Salad – On “Free Treat Day”, students were encouraged to bring one piece of fruit to help make a friendship mixed fruit salad.  New Teacher – LPS welcomes Mrs. Gait, who joined the school staff at the end of January to help support literacy across all grades. Mrs. Gait was hired as part of the interim funding by the Province and through a District/NDTA funding agreement. Ecole North Oyster  Valentine’s Dance – Held Friday night on Feb 17th, DJ Battle pumped up the tunes in the gym while students and parents from ENO and LIS danced away!  Starlab 2.0 – The HR MacMillan Space Centre brought their Starlab 2.0 (a digital portable planetarium) to ENO on Feb 7th & 8th. Using Starlab students were able to stand on the Moon and look back at Earth, experience a Martian sunrise and see the Milky Way galaxy. More info here: http://www.spacecentre.ca/starlab  Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) – Is a certificate program offered at ENO over 8 weeks (Thurs 6-8pm Apr 13-Jun 1) and is open to parents/caregivers outside ENO catchment. An effective, evidence-based parenting program running in 25 countries. It gives parents simple & practical strategies to promote building healthy and strong relationships & confidently managing children’s behavior. More info here: http://triplevip.ca Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Improv Teams place 3rd – Congrats to both the junior and senior improv teams received 3rd place in the Canadian Improve Games Vancouver Island finals, which took place in Victoria Feb 12 & 13.  Quebec Winter Carnival – 10 Gr8 French Immersion students soaked in some culture by travelling to Quebec City for the 2017 annual Winter Carnival. For three days, students took pride in using the French language in authentic situations.  First Nations course offering – A new “Language and Land-based Learning” course is being offered at LSS. Students take photos, write, carve, weave & participate through other forms of physical education such as canoeing or with walks through the forest. Young minds also have the opportunity to learn the Coast Salish language – Hul’qumi’num. This innovative & new course was profiled in a recent article in the local newspaper http://www.ladysmithchronicle.com/ourtown/413184993.html Trustee Kimler
