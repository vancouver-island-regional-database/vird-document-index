Trustee Kimler Zone Report 23-May-2018 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Hul’q’umi’nun Language Showcase – Free registration is open for this May 29th event, which features a student language showcase & includes lunch. The Tuesday event at LSS kicks off the Vancouver Island Indigenous Language Symposium, which is the next day, Wednesday, May 30th at Port Place. More info can be found here: http://bit.ly/2J53Uq8 Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  No report École North Oyster (ENO)  Young Entrepreneurs Show – Visit and support young entrepreneurs getting their start learning about business, creating their own products and putting their imaginations to work. There are always a variety of crafts & lots of creative surprises. Students, parents & community members are invited to come, look & buy (prices generally range from $0.50 to $4.00 per item). The event will be held on June 6th from 9 AM to 1 PM in the gymnasium.  Track & Field – Grades 3-7 students were bussed to NDSS on Tuesday May 15th for their school track meet (shared with Cinnabar Valley School). Students get to pick between 4-6 events and must choose at least one running event and one field event. The Zone Track Meet finals will be on May 29th with the District Meet held on June 7th.  Vancouver Sun Adopt-A-School Grant – Thanks to the work of Secretary Christine De Vries ENO received a $10,000 grant to help pay for a food program for a 2-year period for students in need. They also received $500 for kitchen equipment & $1000 for clothing. A link to her winning Vancouver Sun grant entry story: http://bit.ly/adopt-a-school-winning-entry Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  Community Fun Fair – Food, games & prizes are in store for May 31st from 4 PM to 7 PM! Come support LIS students, visit the school and have fun!  Sexual Health Education for Educators & Parents – All personal and sexual health resources can now be accessed from the main SD68 website. Look under “programs” from the main page or access directly at https://sexual-education.sd68.bc.ca This growing online resource contains Information about: o What students learn at each grade level, o Printable lesson plans and bosy safety posters, o Developmental age guides and conversation starters, o What SOGI education is about, o Important policies, procedures and much more.
