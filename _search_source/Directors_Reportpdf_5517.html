The Ministry indicated the District would need to consider the announcement in our formulation of the 2012/13 Capital Plan. District Fleet Replacement Program February 16, 2011 – Business Committee Meeting The vehicle fleet recommendations were reported to the Business committee. Further, staff advised that they would consider the recommendations over the next year and provide an update.       Funding has been established in the District’s budget to support lease to own payments through the Municipal Finance Authority. Using best practice Fleet Management guides and other jurisdiction programs, staff have developed a 10 year replacement program. Generally light vehicles are replaced every 10 years with heavy equipment replaced every 15 years. Staff expect to complete a Vehicle Use Analysis in the 2012/13 budget year. All outsource repairs are now done in consultation the Mechanical Foreman. Further staff training has taken place and all maintenance and repairs information is being entered into the data base. Fuel usage data is expected to be available for input shortly. Mechanical staff have been informed of the District’s appreciation for their efforts. 7. Energy Management  Carbon Neutral Action Report (CNAR) Each year the District is required to complete a CNAR. The report is due March 31st and posted online near the end of June. The attached link is to the government site showing the District’s CNAR for 2010. www.livesmartbc.ca/government.carbon_neutral/nuetral_action_reports.html. The 2011 report has been submitted and will come online near the end of June. The report has two parts: Part 1: An overview which includes an Executive Summary, actions that the District has done and intend to do and a declaration of the amount of carbon offsets purchased to declare carbon neutrality for the past year. Part 2: Actions template which lists the actions the District has done in the past year and plans to do in the future to reduce GHG emissions. CNARs are legislated requirement for all public sector organizations and managed by the Climate Action Secretariat (CAS).  Retirement of Brian Kingsley s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\business\11 - june 20, 2012\directors report.docx Brian Kingsley has announced his retirement from the position of Technical Assistant (which acts as Energy Manager) as of September 30, 2012.
