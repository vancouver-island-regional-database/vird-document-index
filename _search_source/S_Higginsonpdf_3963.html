Dear Trustees: This letter comes to you as both a member of the Save Cedar Schools Coalition and more importantly as a parent of children who are supposed to attend Cedar Elementary. I apologize for yet another lengthy letter. I did request a meeting with my zone trustee to discuss this issue, however that request was flatly denied. So, here I sit, hoping that someone out there can start to see through this plan for Cedar Elementary for what it really is, a complete and utter boondoggle. Process of the School Construction Committee for Cedar Elementary: I was one of the parent reps on the School Construction Committee for the new Cedar Elementary. Despite my feelings about having the high school leave our community, I wanted to be part of the planning and shaping of this new elementary school. We had been told Cedar Elementary would be many things, all of them were exciting and positive. However, through my own research on situations of reconstructions and rebuilds I had learned that these promises don’t always work out as planned and that careful attention from parents was necessary to ensure a smooth transition during these processes. The parents on the school construction committee worked tirelessly to gather information from parents. We sent letters home with students, created a gmail account so parents could email their ideas for the construction phase of the new school and participated fully in every meeting we were invited to. From the outset the parents on the committee were very clear that the community has great concerns over the usefulness of the playfields at Cedar Secondary. It is common knowledge in this community that two of the three fields at Cedar Secondary are not usable for 8 of the 10 school months. Unfortunately, the play field that is usable for part of the school year is located very far away from the school and you have to walk through the two flooded fields to access the usable field. To make matters worse, we learned recently that the one usable field is actually NOT usable from December-April. At the first school construction meeting we were ensured that the playing fields would be fixed and updated. The concern over fields was confirmed by the maintenance department when they were asked to do an inventory of outdoor spaces for the school construction (I intended to attach a copy of this presentation from the maintenance department as Appendix A, however the email bounced back to me as being too large, please feel free to contact me for a copy of the presention). Also of common concern for both the parents and the maintenance workers was the height of the bathroom fixtures and water fountains throughout the school, the lack of sinks with hot and cold water k-3 classrooms, traffic safety on the busy roads that run along school property lines, desk and counter heights, lack of direct access to outside from ground floor classroom and lack of covered outdoor play areas and field access. The community had numerous concerns beyond just those shared with the facilities department. These include but are not limited to addressing safety concerns around the skate park. This community has been fundraising for 10 years for that park, there is concern throughout the community that without proper safety measures, there will be an accident and the park will simply end up closed for the school day and inaccessible to the many homeschooled students in the community. Also, the ability to ensure that we would be able to continue running the numerous community schools programs that currently utilize space in all three schools in the area. Of particular concern was the possible loss of the foods rooms as these are heavily used to teach nutrition and cooking to area children and parents. The school construction committee met once without parent reps present. Then three more times with parent reps present. Those three meeting happened over the course of 5 weeks and we were told that we would need to continue meeting many more times before the plan was finalized. Throughout these meetings the parent reps on the committee continually challenged the enrolment numbers that the district staff put forward for Cedar Elementary. For the parent reps on the committee it was important to fully understand the number of students who would be attending the school so we could properly negotiate to save certain classrooms, such as the foods rooms. At the November 15th meeting we were told that we were building a school for 475 students. We challenged that since we now knew that South Wellington students had chosen to attend Chase River. The agenda for the November 29th meeting showed that there would be a discussion on the projected enrolment for the school. However at the meeting we were told that the item was being removed from the agenda. At the December 10th meeting we were told that we were now designing a school for 425 students (375 FTE and 2 Kindergarten classes). The parents again strongly objected to this number knowing now that North Oyster was proposed to be kept open under both of the suggestions from the Ladysmith Working group. During the three meetings that the parent reps were present at, the committee focussed on finding a plan for the inside of the school that met the needs of all parties involved. On December 10, after three long and often tense meetings, a plan for the inside of the school that met the concerns of the parents, the staff, the principals and the district staff on the school construction committee was agreed on. (I also intended to attach a copy of these plans as Appendix B, but again, the file was too large to make it through the board servers. Please feel free to contact me for these plans). One of the most exciting aspects of this plan was the redesign of the woodshop as it provided for the most interesting mixture of classroom and collaboration space. Most notable for this plan was the remainder of at least one foods room, access to the outdoor from the ground floor classrooms, direct access to the field from the school, the inclusion of numerous “collaboration” spaces to encourage cross grade and class collaboration and a kindergarten suite that was suitable for our youngest and most vulnerable students. It was agreed that at our next meetings we would being to focus on planning for the outdoor spaces at the school. Fast forward to mid-January, no email had been sent to try to firm up a date for the next school construction meeting. An email was sent by a teacher on the committee asking when the next meeting would be. There was no response to the request for the meeting. In early February I sent an email asking about a possible meeting date. 12 days later I received an email saying that within the week there would be an invitation for the next meeting. FOUR weeks went by before we heard anything from the district staff about another meeting. On March 6th we received an email summoning us to a meeting on March 13th, stating that the staff had recently received new information and they wanted to update the school construction committee about this information before presenting to the business committee that evening. There was no agenda, none of the usual design documents for us to review, just an email asking us to attend a meeting in the middle of the day in one week. Despite numerous requests for an agenda, none was provided. All members of the school construction committee went into this meeting blind. At this meeting on March 13th we were told that the new enrolment projections for Cedar Elementary showed an opening day enrolment of 277 students. As a result of this number, during the previous three months that the school construction committee had not been meeting, the district staff had decided to scrap the plan that had been agreed on by the school construction committee and use a more “scaled back” version. This version included no collaboration spaces, the woodshop would no longer be renovated, the foods rooms would now be renovated and one room would keep a food prep station, the lockers would remain in the school, the bathroom fixtures would not be lowered, water fountains would not be lowered, the kindergarten/strong start suite was considerably scaled back to now simply be three regular classrooms, the counter heights in the administration areas would not be altered, there was no traffic calming measures, no direct access to the outdoors from the ground floor classrooms, no covered outdoor play areas (which we have at Woodbank) and the biggest insult, the fields would no longer be remediated. This last change was of particular concern to the parents and teachers, because it shouldn’t matter if there are 475 students or 277 students, every child deserves and needs access to safe and fully functioning outdoor play fields. I noted to the district staff at this meeting that the “scaled back” renovation was not scaled back in price, it still remained at the originally budgeted $2million. Also of note for me was that in the new “scaled back version there is 16 classrooms which translates to enough classroom space for approximately 400 students. This does not include counting the music, art and drama rooms as general instruction classrooms. Therefore, this scaled back version is not scaled back in size or cost at all. It is simply scaled back in function. The parent reps and some teacher reps expressed concern and or outrage over the new scaled back version as it does not reflect any of the professional or community collaboration and concerns addressed during the construction committee. Furthermore, not only is the new plan a serious downgrade from the elementary facilities that students at Woodbank have access to, the lack of outdoor play fields and traffic calming measure mean the school is not actually a safe environment for elementary aged students. Finally, the ministry area standards for elementary schools with 175-600 students is 1.0 hectares of playfield area. Currently, there is not 1.0 hectares of usable play fields available year round at Cedar Elementary. At this meeting the parents asked what was negotiable about the plan being presented to us and we were told nothing, that this was the plan and the committee was now concluded. I was assured that these concerns would be brought forward to the business committee meeting that was being held that night. I attended that meeting only to be completely insulted by the process that unfolded. Not only did the district staff not disclose that the plan they presented did not represent the plan agreed on by the school construction committee, nor did they disclose that this plan is actually unsafe for the students who will attend the school. The two trustees who sit on the school construction committee all but remained silent over the issues brought up by the parent reps at the meeting earlier in the day. Trustee Saunders did mention the field issue, there was a brief discussion on this before a half hearted effort was made to ask staff to report back in June. In the meantime the business committee approved a school WITH NOT OUTDOOR PLAY FIELDS FOR KIDS WHO LIVE IN THE COUNTRY. Finally, to add insult to injury that night, when discussing how this facility would be considered enhanced the district staff compared the proposed Cedar Elementary to either “ministry standard requirements” or other schools in the district that are of a comparable student population. They did not do a straight school to school comparison to demonstrate all the facilities that we will currently have at NCI and Woodbank and what we would be losing with this plan. I sat through another hour of meetings in order to be able to ask questions of the business committee as a way to make it known that the current plan was not at all okay with the community in which the school was to be located. When I brought up questions about the condition of the outside of the school, those questions were kicked back to the school construction committee. I am sure you can understand my frustration and confusion when during the day we asked if we could address these plans and we were told only the trustees could direct the staff to have a different plan and when I tried to get trustees to recognize the inadequacies of this plan, it got kicked back to the school construction committee. Also during this meeting my questions and concerns were met with rolled eyes, heavy sighs and overt comical gestures by trustees at the table. In fact it was the same members of district staff with whom I had had the tense meeting that day who came to me afterward to express dismay at the process and sadness for how my questions were treated during the meeting. All of you should feel ashamed and embarrassed for how you behaved toward me and my genuine concerns for the most vulnerable children of the Cedar community that night. Overall Concerns: Trustees, I can understand why you voted in favor of this plan in June. At the time it called for a 475 student school that could be financially justified by the closing of 4 area elementary schools. Cedar Elementary was going to be the “envy of the district and the entire island” (Trustee Bill Bard in an email to a community member on September 21, 2013). Superintendent Hutchinson has stated that “The repurposing of the Cedar Secondary building to an elementary school offers exciting opportunities for elementary education in the Cedar community. Consultation with parents during the planning process will result in a facility and school educational philosophy that is supported by the majority of Cedar parents.” Finally, most recently, Trustee Brennan was quoted as stating that he voted on the plan because, “the prospect of an up-to-date, safe elementary school in Cedar was attractive”. I might not have agreed with the plan, but I could understand why you voted the way you did in June. However, would you have voted on this plan if you knew that the school would only house 277 students (again, an optimistic number), if you knew that there was no outdoor playing fields and that the $2 million would result in a substandard facility that will require all the current enhanced programs running at Woodbank Primary to be halted? I have to think the answer is no. I keep hearing from district staff that this school has the potential to be a great school in 10 years. To this I say; potential in ten years is not good enough for my children who will be going to this school NOW. And furthermore, spending $2million today, to tear apart the NEWEST high school facility in the district at the expense of other much needed maintenance projects because it MIGHT be great in 10 years is not good enough. All the wonderful and amazing programs that run at Woodbank that keep my son engaged in school and loving school will have to come to a halt if he moves to Cedar Elementary. Even just playing outside will become a complete nightmare if you think about the logistics of all the students fighting for the very small usable space that is the playground and the hard surface area. The hope that at some point the staff at the new Cedar Elementary will be innovative and think of new programming for the kids, is not good enough. Finally, this school will result in more empty operating seats that the current elementary school configuration in Cedar. This means that in order to justify the renovation and pay for the maintenance of this school in the future you will have to close another nearby school in order to fill this repurposed high school. All of these factors mean that it is time to rethink Cedar Elementary. It is not good enough. You need to stop thinking about this school in relation to the location of the high school students. There are alternatives (I am not giving up on returning our high school students to their own community, but I fear that you are not looking at Cedar Elementary with clarity because you feel that you must fill that school, at all costs, both educational and economic). This does not need to be the case. I propose that you renovate Woodbank Elementary. It can hold 229 students. You could easily put an addition onto Woodbank that will make it the right size. This renovation will cost half as much money as the current renovation costs and you will have a beautiful school WITH USUABLE play fields. I have heard time and time again that you are making decisions that are for the betterment of this district. I want to remind you that Cedar is a part of this district and my children are a part of this district. This plan is not good for the district nor is it good for my children. It is time to rethink Cedar Elementary. Thanks for taking the time…once again. Stephanie
