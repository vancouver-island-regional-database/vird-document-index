MINUTES 2025-21: That the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee approve the minutes of the October 15, 2025 meeting as amended. Motion Carried NEW BUSINESS Waterfront Area Plan  The Committee reviewed Waterfront Area Plan map and discussed opportunities to enhance the waterfront area, with a focus on creating a continuous walkway and trail network. Key points included: o Initiate discussions with neighbouring entities (particularly Stz’uminus First Nation, which controls portions of the waterfront) regarding a potential walkway connection and any required legal processes (e.g., injunctions, agreements, or permissions). o Note environmental and regulatory restrictions on foreshore development; any new walkway along water would need to be located above the high-water mark. o Plan for a ridge trail network on the upland areas, incorporating lookouts, gathering spaces, a trail connection from the town down to the waterfront, wayfinding signage, emergency exit routes, use of ICF for loop trail options, improved signage directing users to the tunnel, and the incorporation of native plantings. Page 1|2 OLD BUSINESS Rutti Park Implementation Plan Committee members provided feedback on park amenities by ranking their priorities (1–4, with 1 being the highest). The exercise built on previously collected public feedback and will help inform potential development phasing. The consolidated ranking results were as follows: 1. Drainage 2. Dog Area / Benches 3. Children’s Play Area 4. Split-Rail Fencing 5. Trail Surface / Benches 6. Water Fountain 7. Educational Signage Additionally, one Committee member proposed relocating the planned dog park from Rutti Park to Harbourview Park. The rationale was that Harbourview Park is currently underutilized and moving the dog park would better separate dog activity from a future children’s play area in Rutti Park. No changes to the Rutti Park Plan were recommended. PRC UPDATES   C. Barfoot provided an update on Parks, Recreation & Culture activities and projects. A printed copy of the update was included in the agenda package for member’s reference. NEXT MEETING 7:00pm on Wednesday, December 17, 2025 at FJCC.
