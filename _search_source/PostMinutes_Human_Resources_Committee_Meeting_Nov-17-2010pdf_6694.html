NEW BUSINESS Behavioural Interviewing Dr. Fallon gave the committee an overview of the behavioural or competency-based interview process when filling positions. Dr. Fallon said that competency-based interviewing is based on research which shows that a person’s past behaviour, combined with skills and knowledge serve as indicators of how successful a person will be in a position. The interviewing technique allows candidates to demonstrate their competencies by describing past successes and challenges. Folio HR10/11/17-03 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Bonkowski The Human Resources Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that it look into the possibility of adopting the behavioural interview process and format. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Medical Accommodation Dr. Fallon gave the Committee an overview of the employer’s duty to accommodate employees with medical conditions. He said that last year the district worked with the NDTA and CUPE to develop a process to define the best way to accommodate an employee and that process has been used with success over the past several months.
