May 4, 2015 Education Committee MRRC Update The MRRC Advisory Committee met April 13 and April 27 to review current MRRC policy & procedures, ensure a common understanding of language and develop a term of reference to guide their work. The following is a brief summary of Committee recommendations, actions & timing: Recommendation #1: Develop New Policy of Inclusion As our current policies are long overdue for a rewrite, over and beyond simple ‘housekeeping’ (MRRC & SOGI) – develop a new, overarching policy of inclusion in keeping with latest research and best practice.  At the April 30 SOGI Committee meeting, members were updated on new policy recommendation and unanimously agreed to work together with MRRC to draft a new, overarching policy  A Policy Development ‘working group’ struck comprising of members from both MRRC, SOGI & NLPS with content knowledge as follows: o Assistant Superintendent Department of Learning Services (Diversity & Equity) o Community Member & Former VIMCS Diversity Lead o DPAC Chair o SOGI & MRRC Facilitator  New policy will be a concise statement, an expression of expectation of all our school communities in bringing about a safe, inclusive and respectful schools and it will be the document that holds our district work accountable.  The Working Group will meet May 14 to envision the new policy o Draft to MRRC and SOGI committees for review and edit o Draft to David Mumford to format and edit o Draft to MRRC & SOGI for final approval prior to submitting to the Board of Education  Working Group & Trustee Co-Chairs met April 20, 2015 to review and approve SOGI & MRRC recommendations Policy Development Timing:  Draft policy sent to Board of Education early June, 2015  policy approved & adopted within NLPS early September, 2015 MRRC Update to Education Committee May 4, 2015 Page 1 of 1 Recommendation #2: Develop Admin Procedures & Envision Committee’s Role MRRC Committee recommended developing individual, administrative procedures to reflect the current situations. They recognize a significant overhaul is required to ensure leading practices are followed. The overhaul may include: changing the name of MRRC to reflect current language; potentially splitting Multicultural from Race Relations, include anti-bullying & other such topics; and, re-envisioning the role of the committee to reflect a new need. Documents noted to help guide this work include: VSB, WHO, Ontario School Board, Ministry of Education “Safe Caring & Orderly Schools” and the “Diversity in BC Schools Framework” Timing: The work of developing admin procedures will be a key focus of the committee over the 2014-2015 Recommendation #3: Implement Key Priorities Alongside the development of policy/procedural strategies, MRRC & SOGI committees stressed importance of continuing ‘on the ground’ activities that have direct impacts on students. MRRC (and SOGI) unanimously agreed to three priorities as follows: 1. Ensure the Safe Harbour program is adopted at every school within NLPS  Note: Department of Learning Services approved the program and it is currently being implemented 2. Staff Onboarding of current policy (statement of expectation orientation package)  Timing: Fall 2015. Committee will develop a brochure around inclusion for all new staff 3. Implement “Our Schools Our Community - Taking a Stand for Inclusion” and match it to BC curriculum to make easier for teachers to incorporate into standard practise  Note: Roll-out plans currently under development MRRC Update to Education Committee May 4, 2015 Page 2 of 1
