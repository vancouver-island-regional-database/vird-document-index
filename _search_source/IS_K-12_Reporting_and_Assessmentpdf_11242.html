NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: November 1, 2023 Education Committee Nicole Davey, Director of Instruction New K-12 Reporting Policy and Assessment Overview: Connection to Operational Plan Goal: Continuous improvement in students with complex and unique abilities, Indigenous and overall student population on student achievement Objective: Continuous Improvement in support for students with diverse abilities and disabilities, Indigenous and overall student population on student achievement. (Assessment and reporting update) Strategy: Create opportunities for PVP and educators to fully implement the new K-12 reporting policy and assessment. Background: The BC Ministry of Education has updated the K-12 Student Reporting Policy: Communicating Student Learning Guidelines to be implemented for the 2023-24 school year. The new reporting policy was created to better reflect the redesigned curriculum that was published in 2016. Over the past 5 years, NLPS was part of a pilot project designed to explore changes to the current reporting policy. The innovative and thoughtful work done by teachers during this time created a solid foundation for the district. Last year, an elementary district assessment group and a secondary district assessment group was created comprised of teachers, coordinators, principals/ vice-principals, along with myself. We deeply explored the new policy and how it would impact our current guidelines. By the end of the year, we updated our district guidelines and began communicating out to schools and teachers what changes to expect once the new policy was fully implemented. This year we are supporting PVP and educators with not only deeply understanding the changes to the reporting policy, but with the adoption of evidence-based, robust assessment practices and pedagogy. Information Sheet: New K-12 Reporting Policy and Assessment Page 2 Key Features of the New Reporting Policy Meaningful Communication Researchers stress the importance of ongoing, comprehensive, and timely communication with parents and caregivers. Effective communication between home and school has a positive impact on student learning Focus on Proficiency Assessment that is focused on student proficiency in relation to the established learning standards leads to improved reliability of assessment results and increased student engagement. Self-Assessment Student self-assessment leads to an increase in student confidence in their abilities, greater responsibility for their own learning, greater student satisfaction.
