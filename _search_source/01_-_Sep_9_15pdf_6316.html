CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Mr. Ron Jorgensen Member, Alberni Lions Club RE: SPOT Scanner - Eye Screening Project Mr. Jorgensen presented SPOT Scanner, Eye Screening Project – “Your Vision is Our Vision”, an initiative in which the Lions Club requests the endorsement and potential future partnership with the district. The Club's goal is to work collaboratively with the district to facilitate early access to the SPOT VS 100 screeners by students in the education system beginning this fall. Mr. Jorgensen's presentation is available online on the SD 68 website and attached to the Agenda for this evening. Mr. John Blain Learning Services Student Survey for Cedar Timeline IS Superintendent Blain shared with the Education Committee, an Information Sheet regarding next steps for the reopening of Cedar Secondary School for September 2016. The Department of Learning Services will administer a survey to all Cedar students who are currently enrolled in Nanaimo Secondary Schools. These results will help staff to project September 2016 enrolment numbers at Cedar Community Secondary School. The Department of Learning Services will then create a grade configuration model and implementation plan to be presented to the Education Committee on October 7, 2015. UNFINISHED BUSINESS There was no Unfinished Business this evening. NEW BUSINESS School Start Up Trustee Kimler Trustee Kimler expressed several concerns regarding school start up. He and some other Trustees don't believe that the first week of school is using student time the most efficiently as it could be. In his opinion, the lack of instructional time in Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held September 9, 2015 the first week of school is concerning. In response to this, staff explained the reasons why; enrolment projections are still being worked out, student transfers over the summer, class configuration/sizes. Staff explained that while students aren't sitting in the classroom, there is much learning that does occur. This sort of interaction with theme and program building needs to take place before more learning can take place in the classrooms. DPAC and NDTA shared positive experiences with school start up this year.
