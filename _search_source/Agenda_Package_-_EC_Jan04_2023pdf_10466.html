CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY 4 Page 7 of 13 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: January 4, 2023 TO: Education Committee FROM: Laura Tait, Deputy Superintendent SUBJECT: Promote Safety for Opportunities within Outdoor Education and Programming Board Goal: To be a leader in Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability District Operational Plan Objectives: Increase opportunities for outdoor education and programming in the district. Strategies: Promote safety for opportunities within outdoor education and programming within the district. Overview Within the board’s goal to be a leader in Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability, the Department of Learning Services explored the topic of Outdoor Education and how students and staff can learn on and from the land in a safe and sustainable way. Strategies within the Operational Plan were developed to meet the objective of increasing opportunities for outdoor education and programming in the district. One issue that surfaced early in our inquiry into outdoor education opportunities was safety in planning for and participating in outdoor activities. Many teachers expressed interest in providing more outdoor learning opportunities but were not confident that they had the training or experience to do so safely. Teachers within the system who have some training and experience in outdoor education assisted the district in developing a plan to provide training and certification. The Outdoor Council of Canada (OCC) is the umbrella organization for the outdoor sector. We identified certification options through OCC that would allow us to build our capacity to offer safe opportunities in outdoor education across the district. Page 8 of 13 Information Sheet: Promote Safety for Opportunities within Outdoor Education and Programming Page 2 During the 2021-2022 school year, the following training was completed:  Four teachers received their instructor certification so that they could certify additional teachers in the OCC Field Leader Level One course.  One teacher was certified in Ocean and Marine Training  Seven teachers completed 40-hour Wilderness First Aid  Two teachers completed 20-hour Remote First Aid  Thirty-One teachers and one principal completed a 16-hour OCC Hiking Field Leader course During the 2022-2023 school year, additional training provided includes: Fall of 2022  Two teachers completed 20-hour Remote First Aid  Two teachers completed a 5-day Lake Water Advanced Instructor Certification through the Recreational Canoeing Association of BC) Spring of 2023  One teacher to complete 40-hour Wilderness First Aid  One teacher to complete 20-hour Remote First Aid  Forty teachers to complete a 16-hour OCC Hiking Field Leader course  Eight teachers to complete Lake Water Leaders Certificate (they will be taught by the two teachers who completed the instructor’s course in the Fall) The above training opportunities are building capacity in each of our elementary schools to ensure there is one or more teacher who has the OCC Hiking Field Leader course. This will ensure that there is at minimum one teacher in each school who has the knowledge to safely plan for outdoor learning opportunities, allowing more and more classes to participate in outdoor learning opportunities. Each secondary school, in addition to the OCC Hiking Field Leader course, is being supported to build capacity to offer Outdoor Education opportunities for students in grades 8 through 12. Our locally developed West Coast Wilderness Board Authority/Authorized course (BAA) for grades 10-12 and Outdoor Education 11 and 12 offer students a more intensive outdoor education experience. This will provide students who have a passion for the outdoors to go deeper into this curricular area during the graduation program and in preparation for post-secondary programs in the field. Next Steps In addition to providing training and certification to staff to ensure safe outdoor education learning opportunities, strategies that link this work to other objectives within the Board’s goals are being developed. Connecting outdoor education opportunities to Truth and Reconciliation, as well as exploring opportunities to develop Indigenous-focused BAA courses alongside our local First Nation partners align with the district Syeyutsus Framework. This month, the Education Committee will have presentations from two schools on Outdoor Education. Frank J. Ney Elementary School – principal Gregg Halfyard and vice principal Quinn Olson presenting. Wellington Secondary School – principal Andrea Davidson and teacher Mike Dang presenting. Page 9 of 13 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: January 4, 2023 TO: Education Committee FROM: Laura Tait, Deputy Superintendent & Shawn Johnston, Executive Director of HR SUBJECT: Report on District Operational Plan – Manager’s Learning Series Board Goal: Safe, Caring and Health learning and working environment that is inclusive of diversity of our entire learning community. District Operational Plan Objectives: Increase leadership capacity and excellence Strategies: Build leadership capacity for educational leaders: Manager’s Learning Series Overview The work of Justice, Equity, Diversity and Inclusion (JEDI) will frame the Manager’s Learning Series for the 2022/23 school year. This framework of JEDI reflects the beliefs and values of NLPS and are ever present in our Board Strategic Plan. Each session will be planned with advancing the capacity of our leaders to drive positive change in Justice, Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion in all that we do. Please note that JEDI is not a stand alone topic or a program, it is a way of being. While specific JEDI training is important and is forthcoming, it is equally important that we are considering the JEDI aspects of the work we do every day. Each time we address a student issue, talk to a parent, interview a job applicant, or do any of the thousands of other tasks done in our system on a daily basis, we engage aspects of JEDI. Our goal is to continually advance education and awareness of JEDI in order to imbed JEDI into all that we do. This is the focus of our Managers’ Learning Series. The first session from this year focused on the topic of Justice. We explored what justice means, the various types of justice our system owes to various individuals and stakeholder groups, and how we can maximize justice in our system. We discussed approaching this challenge by first acknowledging the need for our processes to offer fair, equitable access to everyone, and a commitment to work towards that. Page 10 of 13 Information Sheet: Manager’s Learning Series Page 2 As a group we debriefed an arbitration decision where the finding was that the employer had failed to provide procedural fairness to the employee. Working backwards from the consequences of this mistake provided an opportunity to learn from the mistakes of another and strengthen our JEDI lens. Training was provided on the topic of having difficult conversations. The focus of this was to identify that frequent, constructive conversations are an important tool in managing for success and addressing issues, as well as tips to on how to structure these discussions to provide safe spaces and maximize success for all. Particular emphasis was given to the skill of deep listening, which is important in giving voice to all. Finally, Chanelle Tye, the contractor working with NLPS on our JEDI initiative introduced herself to the group, provided a short presentation on JEDI in education, and an outline of our JEDI plan for this year. A survey was provided to participants to gauge the relevancy and helpfulness of each session, the structure of the sessions, and future topics that District managers feel would be helpful. We have collected this data, and will use it to plan the future sessions. Background The Manager’s Learning Series is a professional learning opportunity where leaders from across the district, in all departments come together on a variety of topics related to leadership. This year, there are three sessions distributed throughout the school year. Justice is about how we treat each other, how we distribute opportunities, and ensuring our processes are fair and equitable; Equity is each person getting what they need to learn, work and succeed; Diversity is a representation of people’s identities and experiences, not only in NLPS but the world overall; Inclusion is making this diversity meaningful. Inclusion is embracing these differences and using them to inform what we do. Last year, the focus was on Compassionate Systems Leadership (CSL). The CSL approach comprises an integrated framework for the development of capabilities and knowledge that strengthen the capacity of individuals and collectives to effectively progress systems change initiatives. CSL draws on practices that are effective in building individual insight and well-being and extends them to include the strengthening of interpersonal relationships while deepening the understanding of how the whole system contributes to outcomes. This was a four-part series with a focus on Compassionate approaches to leadership during the pandemic and beyond, including a focus on interpersonal and intrapersonal capacities while maneuvering through systems change. The Series Learning Intentions: • • • Understand deeply, what Compassionate Systems Leadership is Share tools and techniques that enable systems thinking Build trust and relationship with our leader colleagues Number of Participants: 105 Participant Groups: Superintendent, Principal/Vice-principals, Learning Services, Facilities, Operations, Transportation, Finance, Human Resources and Information Technology. Page 11 of 13 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: January 4, 2023 TO: Education Committee FROM: Don Balcombe, Assistant Superintendent SUBJECT: Core French and French Immersion Federal Grant Funding Background In British Columbia, there are four programs to learn French in the K-12 education system. Core French, Intensive French, French Immersion, and the Francophone Program. NLPS offers Core French and French Immersion, as well as supporting Francophone students from SD93 in grades 8 through 12 at Nanaimo District Secondary School. All elementary schools in the district offer Core French in grades 5-7. Core French is offered in grades 8-12 at our secondary schools. French Immersion programs are offered at four of our elementary schools and two secondary schools. École Hammond Bay, École Pauline Haarer, and École Quarterway are single-track French Immersion schools, while École North Oyster offers both English and French Immersion programs. Ladysmith Secondary School offers French Immersion in grades 8 and 9. Nanaimo District Secondary School offers French Immersion in grades 8-12. In addition to regular funding through the Ministry of Education and Child Care, French programs have access to funding grants supported by the Federal Government. NLPS qualifies for grant funding in the following categories: E-Learning Technologies, Learning Resources, Core French, French Immersion, Teacher Professional Development, and Cultural Activities. Overview The district French Advisory Committee (FAC) is responsible for applying for grants, allocating grants, and reporting annually in accordance with the Officials Languages in Education Protocol. The FAC consists of the Assistant Superintendent-Secondary and French programs, the Director of Instruction Learning and Innovation, the French Immersion Learning Coordinator, the Core French Learning Coordinator, and all principals and vice principals from schools with French Immersion programs. The committee meets once a month throughout the school year to plan, implement, and monitor programs and initiatives that are supported by the grants. Collectively, the grants amount to approximately $250,000/year of targeted support to our French Immersion and Core French Programs. The following are some examples of resources, both human and material, that has been provided during the 2021-2022 school year within each grant category.
