Actuals provided within the report include Revenues and Expenditures based on changes made to date, to accommodate increased enrollment, the allocation of deferred revenue and the addition of restricted surplus as approved by the Board of Education. The Annual Budget will be updated for known and expected changes to operations and be presented as the Annual Amended Budget, to the Board of Education, in February 2019. At November 30th, the district was 42% through the fiscal year 2018/19. The purpose of this update is to provide the Board its current financial status along with notes to explain the variances from Budget based on actuals. Expenses - Salaries When looking at the various categories of salaries, it is important to recognize that different employee groups work at different times during the year and this is reflected in the percentage of the budget spent to date, at any time during the fiscal year. In the case of Educational Assistants, who are primarily 10-month employees, the expectation is that approximately 30.0% of the annual budget would have been spent by the end of November. School District Teachers are primarily 10-month employees, so it is presumed that approximately 30.0% of the annual budget would have been spent by the end of November. However, we do have a small group of 12 month Teachers that will inflate that percentage slightly. In the case of Principals, Vice-Principals and Other Professionals, who are primarily 12-month employees, the assumption is that approximately 42% of the budget would have been spent by the end of November. The Support Staff employee group is a mixture of 10 and 12-month employees so it is anticipated that the amount of budget expended by the end of November should be between 30% and 42%. Given the mix of 12-month and 10-month employees, it is difficult to project what percentage of the substitute’s salary budget would have been spent at any point in time. Salaries – Educational Assistants The year to date expense for Education Assistants is 31.77% of budget which is greater than the 29.29% cost in November of 2017. This higher than expected expenditure represents the increased number of Special Needs students and the additional staffing allocated to support them. For the Other Professional group, the current year’s percentage decrease reflects our challenges with regards to recruitment and retention. Salaries – Support Staff With the expected percentage of between 30% and 42% of the budget having been spent by the end of November it is reasonable for the Support Staff salaries to be at 38.09% of this year’s budget, as well, it is comparable to the 37.15% of budget consumed at the end of November in 2017. Salaries – Teachers As approximately 30% of the Teachers Salary budget should be spent by the end of November, the year to date expense at 30.40% of the budget is reasonable compared to the 28.83% spent by the end of November in 2017. The 2017 percentage was lower due to the restoration of the class size and composition language and the related shortage of teachers throughout the Province, which caused us to have unfilled positions well into the 2017-18 school year. Salaries – Substitutes The cost for Substitutes are at 17.90% of budget to the end of November, which is less than the cost at the same period in 2017/18 at 21.20%. This decrease demonstrates our challenges around filling absences throughout the District. Benefits The percentage of 27.45% expended this year on benefits to the end of November is less than the 32.83% consumed by November 2017. The reduction is due to the decreased cost seen in 2017-18 and the changes made to the budgeted benefit percentage for each employee group to align accordingly. We will be analyzing benefits prior to the Annual Amended Budget to ensure no further adjustments, per current and forthcoming costs, are needed for 2018-19. Supplies and Services The year to date expenditures for supplies and services to the end of November is at 35.98% of Budget compared to 34.28% at November 2017.
