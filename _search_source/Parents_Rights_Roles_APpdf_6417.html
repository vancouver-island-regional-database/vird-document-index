Administrative Procedure ___: Parents’ Roles, Responsibilities, and Rights The District believes that strong, continuing parental involvement in all aspects of school programs and activities provides support for measurable improvement in student learning. Further, a collaborative environment is encouraged in which the parents and families of District students are invited and encouraged to be active stakeholders in the school community. 1. Parents’ Roles: Advisors, Advocates, and Decision-Making Team Members 1.1 Parents are encouraged to participate in and influence decisions, raise issues or concerns, appeal decisions, and resolve problems. 1.2 Parents are encouraged and considered to be critical members of the School Based Team, in matters affecting their children. In particular, parents’ advice, recommendations, and support are an important component of the response to intervention planning process. 1.3 Parents are encouraged to become advocates for their children on issues that affect children. They are urged to join the Parent Advisory Council, the School Planning Council and other parent organizations. 1.4 Parents are encouraged to be partners in the school-wide improvement of student achievement through participation in the School Planning Council and other learning improvement initiatives sponsored by the school and district. 2. Parents’ Rights Parents and guardians have the right to: 2.1 Be informed of all matters related to the educational programming of their child; 2.2 Request and Receive written reports of their child’s progress at each regular reporting period; 2.3 Have access to written documents, assessments and reports regarding their child’s educational progress; 2.4 Participate in meetings involving their child’s placement or review of placement in any program; and 2.5 Appeal any staff decision that impacts the educational programming of their child (see Procedure ___, Appeals Process) 3. Parents’ Rights – Specialized Services With respect to specialized services (such as special education) parents and guardians also have a right to: 3.1 Be informed of their child’s referral to the Department of Learning Services through the School Based Team; 1 3.2 Meet with Department of Learning Services support team members involved with the assessment of their child; 3.3 Withhold written consent for special education individual assessment. (if, in the informed opinion of the Deputy Superintendent of Learning Services, a student poses a sufficient threat to his/her own or other’s educational well-being, the Deputy Superintendent may require appropriate testing.); 3.4 Participate in collaborative meetings to develop their child’s Individual Education Plan (IEP); and 3.5 Have the results of any private, accredited assessments for their child considered in education program decisions in the district. 4. Parents’ Responsibilities The District recognizes that parents are also teachers, and believes this view should be supported by the school. To this end, schools will provide parents with the appropriate support and assistance needed in understanding and meeting the expectations of this role. Given this, parents are expected to: 4.1 Set guidelines and clear expectations of good behaviour and academic performance; 4.2 Ensure that their children have a quiet place and time to read, study, and complete homework; 4.3 Discuss daily work assignments, progress reports, and report cards with their children; 4.4 Ensure that their children attend school on time every day, and promptly report any absences or lateness; 4.5 Communicate with the school through written and electronic messages, telephone and/or School Based Team meetings; 4.6 Ensure that their children have the materials needed to complete class and home work; 4.7 Take an active part in school activities such as meet the teacher evenings, Parent Advisory Council and School Planning Council meetings, parent/student activities etc.; 4.8 Keep their children healthy by ensuring they get enough sleep, appropriate nutrition, and medical care; 4.9 Bring to the attention of appropriate school personnel any problem or condition that negatively affects their children, or other children in the school community; 4.10 Help their children develop a good self-image by providing care, discipline, support, interest, and concern; 4.11 Provide the school with current and accurate home, work, and emergency contact information; and 4.12 Foster in their children a positive attitude towards school and learning. 2
