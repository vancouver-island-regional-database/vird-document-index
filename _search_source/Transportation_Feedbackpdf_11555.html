As a mother of 6 and a concerned resident of Gabriola island, I would like to bring to your attention a critical oversight. The 4km limitation for District provided transpotation is failing two Gabriola areas. There is insufficient bus service to Ferne/Hess Road and Harrison Road, where a number of your students reside. We ask that you expand the bus route to accomodate these areas by either reducing the distance criteria or simply making an exception for the following reasons... 1. UNSAFE CONDITIONS These routes have NO SIDEWALKS or safe pathways off of the road and there are NO STREETLIGHTS! Young children are expected to walk in the pitch black winter months, on the side of long steep narrow & winding roads. These conditions pose a very real safety risk and are a constant worry to our children and families. 2. DISTANCE AND ELEVATION CHANGES These routes are not only too long for young children to walk, they have sustained steep hills. It is completely unreasonable to expect young students to be able to walk 4km uphill in a timely manner to get to and from school. 3. NO OTHER ACCESS TO TRANSPORTATION Not all families have access to a vehicle or other modes of transportation. On Gabriola, we do not have public bus services like cities do. The limited service that our "Gertie" provides does not access Ferne/Hess at all. And the schedule does NOT get the children off of Harrison to school on time, nor does it go past the school at the end of the day. The poverty rate is actually quite high on Gabriola, and many families cannot afford a personal vehicle or taxi ride. All children deserve to be at school on time and receive their full education. They should not have to worry about how late they will be getting to school, or how long they will have to wait to be picked up. If a parent cannot get their child to school and there is no District provided bus, the child cannot get to school. Missing school due to lack of sufficient transportation happens and is unacceptable. Parents work. We are Not able to leave work at 2pm 5 days a week to pick up children from school, resulting in children waiting at the school with no supervision OR not going to school because they have no way of getting home. The distance criteria must be reduced or an exception made, so that ALL children can safely travel to and from school. Thank you for considering these factors in your review of the draft. From: Emery Pakos Sent: October 29, 2023 11:02 AM To: Consultation Subject: Draft transportation Policy CAUTION: External Message I'd like to provide some feedback for this policy. 1. The catchment school does not address students (riders) who may have been affected by "housing crisis" and either temporarily or permanently housed just outside the boundaries due to no fault of their own but not so far as to change catchment schools . Many people are having to move (sometimes multiple times), and may need to remain attending the school for consistency. 2. Socioeconomic barriers such as the "housing crisis" where people are evicted frequently, cannot find suitable or permanent housing, or have frequent moves need to be considered when looking at policies that ensure "equitable access to school" and catchment schools. 3. "Transportation assistance-....to families who are outside of the distance criteria to their catchment" Should be amended to add factors in addition to the "outside of distance criteria" such as or other socioeconomic factors that affect ability to access transportation to school. Also (1.1.2) should address how or whom decides that transportation assistance will be available. If people have to ask their principal, they will not often do it. Have a process that isn't humiliating. 4. Ensuring equitable access to school (1.1.1) "subject to available human and financial resources, and available infrastructure.." pretty much means...they can decide for any reason not to uphold the policy of equitable access. This should be clear, and removed or address on who decides this...people can't just have an employee say "too bad" no available/funds. Children with disabilities have a right to equitable access and the "subjects" remove this right and change it to "we can try to help but...." 5. Yearly route review should ask parents feedback on this, but also include risk assessment (safety, lighting, snow removal at areas for bus stops on roads). Also, review and redo the current list of bus routes that is confusing, and missing relevant information (Ie/ Routes 11, 12, etc). Have yearly info provided by each students family on bussing, routes, etc. -Part a, make shifts to routes...etc....transportation changes should be appropriatelyy communicated to families, and not just told to students who may not communiate it to parents well. Ie/ changes in early 2023 to bus 11/12 in Ladysmith. Thanks!
