Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION SPECIAL BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: December 1, 2015 TO: Special Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Becoming Sustainable: Recommendations from staff Background and Rationale: In April 2015, the Board of Education requested that staff review the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan (EFLP), to determine whether to continue with that existing plan or develop a new plan. In June 2015, staff presented to the Board the draft Updated Facilities Plan 2015-2021 for discussion. The Board tabled that draft plan for discussion, and requested edits to the draft. In September 2015, staff presented to the Board an updated Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update for consideration and the following motion was passed: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the Becoming Sustainable – Facilities Plan Update, in principle, for distribution and public consultation, and that the public consultation process provided under Policy No. 3605 begin September 16, 2015. The Board has now completed the 60 days of consultation as required by Board Policy/Procedure 3605 and will now consider the proposals from the Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update. Full rationale with supportive data and details, and recommendations was included in staff’s report to the Board of Education at its Special Business Committee Meeting, on November 24, 2015, entitled Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update. 1 Motions to consider on staff recommendations: Learn@Home K-12 The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that Learn@Home K-7 and 8-12 amalgamate into the former Mount Benson School for September 2016. If such a motion is approved, the Board would need to pass the following items: Bylaw 2015-01 Required to close Learn@Home K-7 Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) permanently close Learn@Home K-7 on June 30, 2016. Bylaw 2015-02 Required to close Learn@Home 8-12 Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) permanently close Learn@Home 8-12 on June 30, 2016. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) opens the school formerly known as Mt. Benson School as a new distributed learning K-12 school (school name yet to be determined) effective September 2016. Central Zone Secondary (Woodlands Secondary School) The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) the closure of Woodlands Secondary School on June 30, 2016, and that the academies at Woodlands be transferred to Nanaimo District Secondary School. If such a motion is approved, the Board would need to pass the following items: Bylaw 2015-03 Required to close Woodlands Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) permanently close Woodlands Secondary on June 30, 2016. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) relocate the Hockey, Soccer, and Beach Volleyball Academies to Nanaimo District Secondary effective September 2016. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) changes Wellington Secondary catchment area to include Cilaire as a feeder school. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) changes Nanaimo District Secondary catchment area to included Forest Park and Brechin as feeder schools. 2 North Zone (Rutherford Elementary) The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) the closure of Rutherford Elementary School on June 30, 2017 and that staff work to secure a project agreement with the Ministry of Education on the construction of a new wing at Frank J. Ney Elementary School, and that failure to enter into a project agreement with the Minister of Education will require the Board to revisit the closure of Rutherford. If such a motion is approved, the Board would need to pass the following items: Bylaw 2015-04 Required to close Rutherford Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) permanently close Rutherford Elementary June 30, 2017. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) seek to secure a project agreement from the Ministry of Education on the construction of a new wing at Frank J. Ney Elementary and that failure to secure such agreement will cause the Board to revisit closure Bylaw 2015-04. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) allows for input from School Consolidation Working Group – Rutherford before finalizing catchment changes required on the closure of Rutherford Elementary. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) relocates the Strong Start located at Rutherford to Frank J. Ney as part of the closure of Rutherford Elementary. District Elementary Skills for Life The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that it relocate the District Elementary Skills for Life program to Georgia Avenue Community School for September 2016. If such a motion is approved, the Board would need to pass the following items: Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) move the District Elementary Skills for Life Program from Rutherford Elementary to Georgia Ave Elementary effective September 2016. 3 South Zone (Cedar Area Elementary Schools) The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that North Cedar Intermediate and Woodbank Primary be consolidated into Woodbank school for September 2016. If such a motion is approved, the Board would need to pass the following items: Bylaw 2015-05 Required to close North Cedar Intermediate Be it resolved that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) permanently close North Cedar Intermediate on June 30, 2016. Recommended Motion That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) reconfigure Woodbank Primary as a K-7 grade school effective September 2016, and that students from North Cedar Intermediate School to attend Woodbank school in September 2016, and that effective September 2016 the school to be known as Woodbank Elementary 4
