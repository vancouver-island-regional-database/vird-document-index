The project will include pile driving and likely rock socketing which will cause disturbance during construction. A Qualified Environmental Professional with hydrophone will be onsite during construction to monitor subsurface noise to ensure it remains below levels of concern, or mitigating measures, such as a bubble curtain, will be installed if needed. The piles are expected to be driven with vibratory hammer to the bedrock, then embedded in the bedrock using downhole hammer within the pile. The underwater impacts from this type of construction are significantly lower than the alternative of impact hammer, and, based on experience with several other similar jobs, are expected to be well below levels of biological concern. The project is not expected to release any odour or gases during construction or operation.
