The lease was up for renewal in 2016 and was not renewed by the Owner of the property for unforeseen circumstances and subsequently the lease was terminated by FLNRORD in 2018. The Owner reapplied for a License of Occupation for the building structure which was being used as a part time residence at the time and a Specific Permission for the dock structure rebuild. The dock structure rebuild was recently approved by the Ministry but the Licence of Occupation for the building structure was denied for the approximately half (90 m2) of building area that was encroaching on Crown Land and the Ministry has instructed the Owner to demolish the extent of the structure on Crown Land and make good the area. The building in question measures approximately 9.14 M in the shoreward direction by 18.29 M along the shore which includes concrete landings on either end of a 9.14 M x 12.8 M two storey building structure. The current building and dock structure have been in this location since the 1960's and it is our understanding that there was a building on skids in this location prior to the construction of the current building. The area of the building on Crown Land is deemed to be Filled Foreshore that was filled in the 1960's to allow for the construction of the current building. We do not know whether the construction of the building was authorized or not at the time of construction. Demolishing the portion of the existing building that encroaches on Crown Land and maintaining the remaining structure that sits on the upland property is not a viable option for the Owner due to the geometry of the structure that would remain on the owners property. The portion of the building that encroaches on Crown Land can only be accessed via the Owners upland property except for access along the foreshore. The Owner is hoping to be able to purchase the property that is encroaching on Crown Land at Fair Market Value using a Direct Sale purchase.We have had an appraisal for Fair Market Value completed from a Nanaimo Appraisal Company and we have had a BC Land Surveyor calculate the area of encroachment on Crown Land. Both of these documents are included in the application.
