THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: March 28, 2012 TO: The Board of Education FROM: Bob Esliger, District Principal, Student Support Services SUBJECT: Community Schools Policy and Procedure Updates BACKGROUND In 1994 the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District applied directly to the Ministry of Education for Community School Status. The district received funding for:     Georgia Avenue Elementary School – 40K Fairview Community Elementary School - 40K Nanaimo District Secondary School - 40K John Barsby Secondary School - 40K In 1996 the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District approved Community School Status for:  Cedar Secondary Community School (without funding) In 2002 the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District provided funds to Cedar Secondary Community School (40K) and employed two part-time Community School Coordinators to cover the Cedar Family of Community Schools. Designated Community Schools that did not receive funding from either MCFD or the School District are at this time were:   Harewood Elementary School (without funding) (closed 2004) Park Avenue Elementary School (without funding) In 2003-2004 the separate funding pieces for Inner City Schools, Community Schools and Food Programs were rolled into one funding envelope and termed CommunityLINK. An advisory committee was created to guide the processes of LINK funding allocation. The CommunityLINK Advisory Committee initiated the review of community schools in June of 2009. Dr. Richard Zigler was contracted to conduct the review and it was completed in November of the same year. The final report contained 10 recommendations which the CommunityLINK Advisory Committee began to address immediately (appendix). Mr. Mike Munro, former SD68 Superintendent and Mr. David Green, former SD68 Secretary Treasurer updated the Community Schools Policy and Procedure (1999) and it went to Notice of Motion on July 22, 2011 until October 31st, 2011. On March 13th, the Community Schools Coordinators were invited to a meeting to provide feedback on the most recent updates to the Community Schools Policy and Procedures. Once the information from the Notice of Motion was reviewed and collated it was used to further inform the Community Schools Policy and Procedure resulting in the update presented to you with this action sheet. RECOMMENDATION That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) refers amended Policy and Procedure 3610 - Community Schools to the Strategic Planning Process. APPENDIX Mission: Learning Communities for Community Learning Vision: Community schools are accessible, comprehensive, providers of educational, health and social capacity building programs based on partnerships that result in thriving communities. Principles: Community Schools are accessible, safe harbours of multi-generational activities that are open to the public and reflect the educational, health and social needs of the neighbouring community. Community Schools make connections through collaborative relationships fostering partnerships that provide inclusive, supportive, integrated and resourceful programs. Community Schools build capacity, develop assets and provide tools resulting in self-sustaining competent individuals and communities. IMPLEMENTATION PLAN: 1.
