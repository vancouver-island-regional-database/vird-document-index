Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Deputy Superintendent’s Report Education Committee Meeting November, 2013 1) Enrolment 2) Early Learning 23: • • • • • ‘Early Learning to Grade 3’ (E23) just had its first day of the learning series this past week. (Monday and Tuesday) Includes all Strongstart programs, Kindergarten, Grades 1, 2, and 3 teachers These teachers will be released for six half-days over the school year, and the major purpose is the district-wide plan to focus on improving the reading levels of our young learners’ The district design team includes an Assistant Superintendent, a District Principal, a District Vice-Principal, and three Coordinators – inclusive of our self-regulation IC The two major objectives of the work is to create the following: a) Create a collaborative culture of learning b) Build common understandings around evidence-based instructional practices 3) Meeting with the Ministry of Education • Discussion about Safe School model, the ERASE program, and Threat Assessment 4) District Assessment Pilot: • • • • • In response to the passing of the district-wide assessment policy, we have implemented a district wide assessment pilot, and provided schools with assessments as follows: o K-3 – piloting 3 assessments o 4-9 – piloting 2 assessments Afterschool workshops have been provided to teachers and administrators about how to administer and mark the assessments. The district delivered five afterschool workshops to an average of 40 participants each session. The district has created teams to partner with schools to administer and mark the assessments. French Immersion K-3 options are being explored, and French Immersion 4-9 has chosen the Reli (French version of the RAD) Principals have been provided with assessment-specific spreadsheets to be used to gather and analyze assessment data. 1 5) Response to Intervention • • • • • • The district is developing a communication plan through the Department of Learning Services to provide information to school principals, teachers and parents. The district has created a leadership series in which Dr. Chris Weber will attend NLPS for session/s to build capacity with Grades 4 through 9 principals and teacher leaders. The Assistant Superintendents have organized a four-part series on self-regulated learning for the LSSTs to build their capacity in the five pillars of self regulation. The Department of Learning Services is working to extend the RTI implementation plan to cover the next two years. The district is integrating the principles of RTI throughout the E23 Initiative.
