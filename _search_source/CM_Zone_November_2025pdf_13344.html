ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee: Chantelle Morvay Date: November, 2025 School Names/Zone: Nanaimo District Secondary School, Fairview Community Elementary, École Quarterway, Mountainview Elementary, École Pauline Haarer. *I want to acknowledge that this report is only a tiny snapshot of what happens in a school over a year. I hope that this gives you a little taste of what each school is like. Annual Highlights: **Nanaimo District Secondary School 1. Overview Nanaimo District Secondary School (NDSS) built way back in 1952, has demonstrated ongoing resilience, adaptability, and strong community engagement throughout 2025. Despite working within an aging and over-capacity facility, staff and students remain committed to providing a supportive, inclusive, and high-achieving learning environment. 2. Enrollment & Learning Environment • NDSS remains one of the largest secondary schools, with enrolment exceeding 1,600 students. • In response, the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District has allocated approximately $251,000 (from a provincial facilities grant) for exterior repairs (notably replacing deteriorating stucco), scheduled in 2025 and 2026. • Despite the building’s age and tight quarters, students and staff continue to learn, teach, and collaborate effectively — a testament to their collective adaptability. • The installation of new all-body, accessible bathrooms has further advanced the school’s commitment to accessibility, dignity, and inclusion. 3. School Culture & Community Engagement • It was my privilege to address the graduating class of 2025. The ceremony was a high point of the year, full of emotion, achievement, and (of course) hopes and jokes for the future. • This year’s Parent-Teacher meetings stood out for being more streamlined than in some past years. With over 1,600 students and their families, scheduling parent–teacher meetings can be a logistical jigsaw puzzle. The school’s approach reduced complexity, easing the burden on staff and parents alike. • The school’s Halloween festivities were a hit! Attendance was high, spirits were even higher, and the group that drew the most cheers was the hilariously named “Performative Men.” Their performance brought laughs, joy, and a sense of community — exactly what school should feel like. 4. An Example of Student-Led Initiatives & Inclusion • Indigenous students led a successful redesign of the Indigenous Support Room, creating a warm and culturally welcoming space that fosters community, connection, and belonging. This initiative reflects the school’s and district’s ongoing commitment to helping students feel belonging so they can grow and thrive. 5. Health & Emergency Preparedness • NDSS has installed Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs) and has naloxone kits on hand in accordance with district and the new Ministry of Education and Child Care mandate. • NLPS principals and teachers recently completed hands-on training in AED use and naloxone administration. • Although equipment must be accessible during emergencies, students are not expected to administer first aid. • AED and CPR training has been added to the Grade 10 curriculum. Page 2 • While naloxone is not part of Grade 10 instruction this year, Island Health’s school health promoter, Griffin Russell, has confirmed that all NLPS Grade 12 students will receive naloxone training in a classroom setting before year-end. 6. Staff Achievement Highlight: Graham Giske NDSS teacher–counsellor and coach Graham Giske achieved significant recognition this year on the international stage. Giske was one of approximately 260 athletes worldwide who competed in the Adaptive CrossFit Games in Las Vegas in mid-September. Following an above-the-knee amputation ten years ago, Giske sought new athletic opportunities and discovered competitive adaptive CrossFit as a space where he could challenge himself. To qualify for the Games, athletes submitted video-based workouts for judging, with successive rounds narrowing the field to 10 finalists per division. Competitors faced a wide range of events measuring cardiovascular endurance, stamina, strength, coordination, flexibility, and accuracy. Despite balancing intense training with his work as a counsellor and teacher, parenting responsibilities, and district commitments, Giske trained six days a week- even converting his garage into a home CrossFit gym. During the competition, he finished as high as sixth place in his best event, a barbell complex. Reflecting on the experience, he emphasized both the challenge and the inspiration of competing alongside elite adaptive athletes from diverse backgrounds. His participation and performance exemplify dedication, resilience, and the spirit of the adaptive athletics community. **École Pauline Haarer Elementary 1. Overview École Pauline Haarer Elementary has had a vibrant and eventful year, marked by strong community engagement, student creativity, and continued dedication to high-quality French Immersion education. While the school faced operational challenges such as the ever-present challenge of hiring French support staff, nearby construction, and building-related vandalism, students, staff, and families consistently demonstrated resilience, collaboration, and good humour. 2. School Culture & Community Engagement • Year-End Outdoor Music Concert The annual outdoor music concert is always a highlight of the year, transforming the field beside the school into a sea of lawn chairs, proud families, and enthusiastic young performers. Students showcased their growing French language abilities through songs, actions, and undeniable cuteness, reminding everyone that sometimes the best learning happens with sunshine, singing, and a slightly out-of-sync chorus of “Bonjour!” • Christmas Market The school hosted a highly successful Christmas Market featuring a blend of local artisans and innovative student entrepreneurs and food trucks! The event demonstrated both the creativity of the school community and the strength of its partnerships. 3. Staffing Updates • Mme Bohm, long-standing principal and a steady leader for the school community, retired in June. • The school welcomed Danielle Ashworth-Clarke as the new principal, bringing fresh energy and leadership to the role. • Recruiting remains as always complex, particularly regarding French Education Assistants, whose availability continues to be limited both regionally and provincially. This remains true for all French Immersion Schools in the district and across the province. 4. Facilities & Operational Challenges • Construction Adjacent to the School Ongoing construction next door created complexities throughout the year, including noise, and traffic flow disruptions. Staff worked diligently to ensure student safety and to maintain uninterrupted learning despite the disruptions. • Vandalism & School Security The school continues to experience vandalism, particularly during off-hours. In response, the district hired security personnel to support safe and reliable daily opening of the school. This Page 3 measure has provided reassurance to staff and families and has helped maintain operational stability during key times of the day. **École Quarterway Elementary 1. Overview École Quarterway Elementary continues to thrive as a hub of French learning, creativity, and community engagement. Home to both Early and Late French Immersion programs, the school fosters students who can debate the merits of pizza toppings in two languages while developing academic and social skills. 2. Late French Immersion Program Review The Late French Immersion (LFI) program recently underwent a formal review. Key outcomes and actions include: • Parental Advocacy: The parents of Quarterway are incredibly passionate and actively advocated to maintain and strengthen the LFI program, ensuring it continues to thrive. • Some District Recommendations Implemented and ongoing: o Enhanced advertisement and promotion of the LFI program to increase enrollment. o Looking at strengthened the learning environment to better support increased enrollment of priority groups. • Program Strengths: Dedicated staff, motivated learners, and a vibrant French-language culture and committed, passionate parents. • Ongoing Review: The school district is continuing assessment and refinement into 2026. 3. Community Events & Highlights • PAC Halloween Party Quarterway’s Halloween celebration reached near-legendary status with an astonishing volume of pizza sold. Funds raised continue to support school programs and events. • Winter Wonders 2025 In November 2025, Quarterway PAC hosted a combined Christmas Market & Winter Wonders event. Highlights included: Our incredibly creative, inventive, and talented students ran their own booths, each brimming with unique flair, proving they could give seasoned entrepreneurs a run for their money. Students displayed such impressive business acumen that clearly many of them are ready to run Etsy shops long before high school. There was also a pancake breakfast at the Royal Canadian Legion 256 and vibrant engagement with families, staff, and local artisans. The event was a resounding success, celebrating student entrepreneurship, creativity, and community togetherness- all while raising funds for school programs 4. Safety & Infrastructure Raised Crosswalk – Vision Zero Project The City of Nanaimo has received a Vision Zero Road Safety Grant to support adding a new raised crosswalk to Townsite Road. Approval of this funding will leverage a City-funded project to establish a new pedestrian route linking the new crosswalk to École Quarterway, a French immersion school in Central Nanaimo. These projects align with Vision Zero’s mission to eliminate serious injuries through proactive infrastructure improvement and will significantly improve pedestrian safety for both school children and the broader community. Work to begin 2026. 6. Looking Forward Quarterway is focused on enhancing program effectiveness, strengthening student learning, and supporting community engagement. Through continuous assessment of the LFI program, proactive infrastructure improvements, and active parent involvement, the school is positioned to maintain its dynamic, bilingual learning environment. The collaboration among staff, students, families, and the PAC ensures Quarterway remains a vibrant, safe, and creative place to learn, and occasionally to eat a lot of pizza. ***Mountain View Elementary School Update School Overview Page 4 • Mountain View Elementary is not only set in an idyllic country setting, it also stands as one of the district’s larger elementary schools, with over 400 students. PAC Initiatives & Fundraising • The devoted PAC has spent many years fundraising for accessible playground equipment. • In 2024, their vision began to take shape in the form of an incredibly fun accessible playground with support and funding from the Ministry of Education and Child Care and the School District. • True to form, the PAC decided not to stop there, and they certainly haven’t by continuing to add in new and exciting elements to the playfield. Community Events & Engagement • PAC Hosted vibrant, well-attended school community events including: Outdoor Games Nights, Spring and Fall Fairs that included BC Hydro Bucket Rides, Classic cake walks and the ever-popular Chicken Bingo! • These events have not only increased school community spirit and engagement, but it has also helped advance the goal of creating a fun, accessible play area and supported additional staff-led initiatives. School Facilities Improvements • The school building itself has also benefitted from attention this year, receiving exterior envelope upgrades. • In addition, the School District has allocated budget for HVAC improvements to the gasfired system, aligning with district goals to reduce energy consumption by an estimated 1525%. A change that will save money and shrink our carbon footprint, which we consider a win on all fronts. ***Fairview Elementary School Overview Fairview Elementary is a vibrant K-7 school in Nanaimo, serving approximately 415 students and grounded in the First Peoples Principles of Learning. Community Engagement & Events Annual Spaghetti Dinner & Open House • Fairview’s beloved annual Spaghetti Dinner, paired with an open house, once again drew an exceptional crowd this year. Nanaimo Lawn Bowling Club Fundraiser • The Nanaimo Lawn Bowling Club hosted a “12-Hour Lawn Bowling Marathon” in June to raise funds for Fairview Elementary’s breakfast and lunch program. • The event showcases the remarkable support the school receives from the wider community-proving that when it comes to helping students, Nanaimo residents will literally go the extra 12 hours. • Funds raised will help ensure students have reliable access to nutritious meals during the school day.
