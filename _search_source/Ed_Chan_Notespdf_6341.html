1 □ Good evening Trustees, Superintendent Blain, District Staff and DPAC President Ms. McKay □ My name is Ed Chan, I’m the President of the John Barsby Community School PAC □ Our PAC, along with the PACs of every other secondary school in Nanaimo-Ladysmith, save Wellington’s, have joined together to create the Coalition of SD68 Secondary School PACs (CSSSPACs) □ We hope that Wellington PAC will decide to join our Coalition in the near future □ Tonight I appear on behalf of the Coalition to talk about the Long Term Facilities Planning process that our Board of Education has undertaken □ Before I start talking about school renewal and replacement, I’d like to warm up by talking about families 2 □ I’d like to begin by drawing your attention to the two photos on the screen 3 □ The first set of photos show a family – this family is a typical family in many ways □ It is steeped in life energy, passion, commitment, and duty and love between generations □ To me, the first set of photos speak to the Circle of Life 4 □ Similarly, the 2nd set of photos also shows a family – a figurative family which is also steeped in life energy, passion, commitment, care □ The young adults pictured in the 2nd set of photos are also firmly placed in the Circle of Life □ Today, some of those teenagers are our city’s leaders, some have left the Island, some have gone on to great things, gotten married, started families, run for public office □ And perhaps others have endured, overcome great obstacles, faced down substantial health concerns or had loved ones who have had to deal with life threatening illness, and perhaps even death □ But one thing that the family in the first set of photos shares with the teenagers and school in the second set of photos □ Both stories take place in homes 5 □ In one case, the story of the first family takes place in literal homes □ In the case of the students of ND, Class of 77, their story takes place in a figurative home, NDSS □ In Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District, we have 35 figurative “school homes” (29 elementary and 6 secondary) □ Within these 35 figurative “school homes” is where the magic of our children’s education happens □ We, your constituents, hope your vision for our district as Trustees is strong, far-sighted, is transparent, easy to understand, coherent □ With regard to the renewal and replacement of our school buildings, our “school homes,” we want to understand what you’re trying to do, why you’re trying to do, how it makes 6 educational sense, how it makes fiscal sense, how it makes sense for our communities □ On April 22nd, you, our Board of Trustees decided to reopen Cedar Secondary School for 2016-17, a decision we are sure you considered deeply □ But, you made the decision to reopen Cedar Secondary removed from the context of a Long Term Facilities Renewal/Replacement Plan □ Some of us in the Coalition wondered why did you decide to reopen Cedar Secondary without placing it within the context of a new Long Term Facilities Renewal/Replacement Plan □ Many of us in the Coalition didn’t get it then and we don’t understand it now 7 What would the Coalition like? o Firstly, our Coalition would like to acknowledge that each Trustee on our Board acts with the BEST OF INTENTIONS and that each of you is TRYING YOUR BEST & ACTING IN GOOD FAITH under difficult circumstances (e.g. too little money, too little time, too many tasks, so much to learn as new Trustees) o Secondly, the Coalition would like to engage constructively with our Board and influence the course of the Long Term Facilities Renewal/Replacement Plan o Third and lastly, we ask our Board to hold in abeyance the decision to re-open Cedar Secondary until the first draft of the Long Term Buildings Plan is completed o Once a first draft of the Long Term Buildings Plan is completed and the Board situates Cedar School within the context of a draft Long Term Facilities Plan THEN MOVE ONTO re-opening Cedar School – in whatever form - as long as it makes educational, fiscal and community sense To finish off, I’d like to thank the Education Committee for allowing our Coalition the chance to present tonight on the Long Term Buildings planning process. 8 I am happy to take any questions.
