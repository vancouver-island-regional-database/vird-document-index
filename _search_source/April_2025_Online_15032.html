Pestles are blunt, club-shaped tools traditionally used alongside a mortar to grind materials — a process involving pounding, pressing, or rotating substances until they reach the desired consistency. These tools hold both practical and cultural significance, representing a connection to the daily lives and practices of Stz’uminus ancestors. Now safely returned to Stz’uminus, the artifacts have been added to the community’s collection, where they can be preserved and honored as part of their heritage. After more than 60 years, two significant artifacts have been returned to the Stz’uminus First Nation, thanks to the efforts of a woman from Victoria. The woman, whose father recently passed away, reached out to the community a few months ago to facilitate the return. She explained that her father, an excavator operator, discovered the items during a dig in the 1960s while working in the Stz’uminus area. He brought the artifacts home, where they remained in his family’s possession for decades. The Stz’uminus First Nation expressed gratitude for the woman’s decision to return the pieces, emphasizing the importance of restoring cultural artifacts to their rightful place. This act of goodwill helps reconnect the present with the past, ensuring that these physical ties to history remain accessible to future generations. The return of the pestles serves as a powerful reminder of the lasting significance of Indigenous artifacts — not just as objects, but as pieces of a living, enduring culture that continues to thrive today. NEW HOME CARE NURSE Sam is a Registered Nurse with the British Columbia College of Nurses and Midwives (BCCNM) and holds a Bachelor of Science degree. Most recently, he served as the Owner and Director of Nursing Operations at Ally Home Care Ltd., where he led geriatric nursing services with a focus on preventing amputations and managing diabetic neuropathy for clients. We are pleased to announce that Sumesh Pillay, known as Sam, will be joining Stz’uminus First Nation as a Home Care Nurse in a term position, effective Tuesday, March 11, 2025. Reporting to Health Manager, Sam will provide essential Home Care nursing services to support our community. About Sumesh Pillay 09 Sam is eager to join Stz’uminus First Nation and collaborate with our team to enhance community care.
