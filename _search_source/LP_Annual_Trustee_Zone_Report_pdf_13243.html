ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Leana Pellegrin Date: November 2025 Annual Highlights: Gabriola Elementary Visiting the school is always a pleasure. The warmth and sense of community spirit is noticeable immediately and the grounds, fields, playgrounds and surrounding forest are so beautiful and fosters such deep outdoor play imagination. The PHC (people for a healthy community) has such a unique relationship with the school. They are located across the street at the common grounds where students get to experience, gardening, planting, tending, harvesting and then eating what is grown. When the weather changes to the winter month, they switched to cooking classes. The PHC and Nester’s provides the Wednesday hot lunches that is for all students and staff ran by volunteers and students helping with the cleanup. What a pleasure to join for one of these lunches and help serve the food, witnessing the beauty of sharing a meal, such a simple and impactful act. There is also a breakfast club each morning. Gabriola elementary has a morning exercise gym time, Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays before school, a football team, a volleyball team, an arts club and a drumming and singing club. Beau Wagner has joined the school for multiple sessions of carving and painting cedar. I was very lucky to join in one of these sessions where Beau shared amazing stories with the students while carving and painting. Monique Gray Smith did a presentation for the students on October 17. How lucky for them to hear from such an incredible author. There was a very interesting class put on by Erin Udul in cooperation with PHC learning all about mason bees -- what a cool hands on experience. Having a deeper understanding of the magic of our friendly little nonstinging bee was a fun class to attend with students. Dec 6th is the schools craft fair for those who can attend and support. Brechin Elementary It is such a joy visiting Brechin where interconnection runs deep and sense of community is all around. There is a community closet of extra clothes and shoes that students and families can help themselves to, including toiletries and extras. There was a partnership with the RCMP where constable Baker dropped off clothing to their community closet, including winter jackets for anyone who could use one. The last culture night had over 100 people attend where Mr. Elliott cooked a moose and elk stroganoff and there was much dancing, drumming and fun to be had. I’ve had the joy of watching Ufly in action, joining a class with a passionate teacher and seeing students light up in literacy. There is a new space that opened up this week called chill dog, and it’s for students who don’t feel a sense of belonging to have a safe place to hang out and play games or do a puzzle at recess. All of the staff have purple or pink vests for supervision time outside which I thought was really special. Just small details for everyone to have their favorite color. They also do a salad buffet in the staff room for lunch, where each person brings an ingredient for a salad bar, and they get to eat it throughout the week. Brechin has many clubs including drama, dance, Lego, choir, volleyball, football, basketball and cooking with Tony classes. In the New year there will be a bilingual buddies club. Nov 29th is the craft fair. Forest Park Elementary What a fun and busy school. There is a lot going on at Forest Park, including their beautiful garden that students get to participate in. Inclusion is at the center of the school with positive messages throughout the hallways. Kindness is very important to Forest Park and the students have a commitment to kindness through many different acts big and small. There is also a kindness club where students come together and brainstorm and promote ways to be more kind. There is also a Page 2 choir club, a friendship club, a recycling club and a social justice club beginning in January. Dec 2nd is the pancake breakfast for the school. Hammond Bay Elementary Had a great visit this week at this wonderful school. There was an assembly on for the older grades on “belong, grow and thrive”, the district’s new tagline. It was so heartwarming to witness the principals vulnerable and leading by example presentation, breaking down the three powerful words. After the Patrick Allen song, we closed our eyes and thought of how many safe adults each student has and to put your hand up with how many each student felt they had, when I peaked one eye open I was overcome with joy to see many students having enough safe adults to go to that they needed 2 hands to count them all. There were many tangible and relatable examples that fostered a deep sense of belonging and community in the gym including the reminder that not thriving is still growing. The before and after school care has started a big project of three large trees in the hallway and each student can color magnolia Sitka or cherry blossom to go on the tree. Such a beautiful show bringing the two communities together and these trees are the names of each room that provide the care. Hammond Bay had a Scholastic book fair and the students led the Remembrance Day ceremony. The clubs at the school include arts Pokémon, loose parts play, club choir and knitting is a new club that will begin soon. I got to join a music class with a very gifted teacher and got to do the amazing head and shoulders, knees, and toes on, but in French with the students. Nanaimo District Secondary School This school truly has it all, and so many students and staff squished into our oldest building. They make lemonade out of lemons every single day. NDSS has so much school spirit and has the pleasure of getting to see and learn from Lolly regularly as the resident elder of the school, how lucky! The students were fortunate to be able to see a production from ballet BC on October 27 and on November 19 they have presentations from over a dozen BC colleges in universities with a mini fair afterwards from 6:30 to 8:30. Such a great way for students to learn about their options in our province. There is a bagel bar everyday, Monday to Friday, from 8 to 830 for all students looking for breakfast. NDSS is the proud home of the district’s sports academies which include beach, volleyball, hockey lacrosse and soccer. There is also a fantastic band program at the school. Reminders of counseling and mental health supports are easy to find, including the mind shift CBT app that helps manage anxiety and stress. Each newsletter from the principal has a last line that says “thank you for sharing your amazing kids with us every single day” which you can feel so deeply in that school and is at the core of the care in this building.
