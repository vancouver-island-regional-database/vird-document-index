Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools ACTION SHEET DATE: February 8, 2017 TO: The Board of Education – Business Committee FROM: Carrie McVeigh, Secretary - Treasurer Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Capital Project Development Appointment of Construction Manager – Frank J. Ney Addition Recommendation: That the Business Committee recommend to the Board of Education of School District No.68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that staff be directed to award a construction management agreement to Unitech Construction Management Inc., subject to the successful negotiation of a construction management agreement for the addition to Frank J. Ney Elementary. Background: On January 25, 2017, staff provided an information update to the Board of Education advising receipt of a letter from the Ministry of Education in which they released $1.3M of school district ministry capital reserve monies to be used for the Frank J. Ney expansion project. Part of the update included next steps towards the closure of Rutherford Elementary which includes the construction of an addition, as designed, at the Frank J. Ney site. As funding for the project envelope has been assembled, staff are now preparing to continue with the design in preparation to procure the addition to Frank J. Ney Elementary (see Appointment for Architect Action Sheet, dated February 8, 2017). Notwithstanding the necessary approval to proceed with Low Hammond Rowe Architects Inc., the addition must be completed and ready to receive students following the permanent closure of Rutherford Elementary. This requires that the Frank J. Ney expansion project be complete in time for school opening in September of 2018. Project design is under way and the next stages include completion of the project development report followed by design development (tender documents), procurement and construction. Discussion: In considering the compressed schedule, staff are targeting a June 30th, 2018 date of substantial completion which allows approximately 17 months to complete the design, tender, and construction of the expansion. This would allow the summer months to identify and address any construction deficiencies, set up the new classrooms and ready the new spaces(s) for staff and students. Currently of all key project criteria - budget, schedule, quality and completion date is foremost in staff’s mind and represents the most risk at this time. In considering risk mitigation, staff have assessed alternate methods of project delivery (design, procurement, construction and commissioning) arriving at construction management as the preferred option. Under this method, the same as used for the Wellington Seismic upgrade, a Construction Manager acts as the owner’s agent and tenders and oversees the work of subcontractors offering flexibility in construction and accommodating the occupants. Unlike Wellington, staff wish to engage the services of a Construction Manager immediately thereby offering the design team contractor-based advice when considering construction methods and materials. This affords a greater opportunity to advance the schedule (giving better assurance of completion in keeping with the goal), more opportunity to adjust the budget as the project proceeds and greater flexibility in considering value added items to the project. With the assistance of a Construction Manager, advancing the construction schedule can be made a priority while still considering budget and quality which will best address the intent of this project. The Construction Management approach will see over 90 percent of the construction work tendered. The Wellington Construction Management contract value was approximately 1.5% of the total project envelope. Considering the timelines and District Administrative Procedure 513(Purchasing), staff recommends a variance to the tendering requirements, to award a Construction Management contract to Unitech Construction Management Inc. (Unitech). Unitech was chosen for Wellington as a result of a selection process which included a request for proposal, a site visit, review and rating of the proposals, shortlisting and interviews. Staff review indicates that given the schedule, the excellent work of Unitech on Wellington and the Construction Management procurement process used for that project, that the early selection of a Construction Manager and the immediate selection of Unitech will greatly benefit the project and specifically the ability to meet the September 2018 deadline.
