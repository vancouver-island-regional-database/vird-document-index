From the Department of Finance Date: May 1, 2019 To: The Board of Education From: Taunia Sutton, Director of Finance Subject: 2019-20 Preliminary Budget Report Budget requirements Boards are required to prepare a balanced budget where board revenues plus any appropriated surpluses fully fund the following:       annual operating expenses, annual special purpose fund (SPF) expenses, annual capital fund expenses, tangible capital asset acquisitions (from Operating, SPF’s & Local Capital), planned reduction of unfunded liability for employee future benefits, and any planned reduction of prior years’ deficits. Per section 113 of the School Act (Adoption of Budget), Boards must prepare an annual budget and have it adopted by bylaw on or before June 30, 2019 and submitted to the Ministry by this date. Guiding Principles Guiding principles communicate to staff and the school community how the Board expects the organization to behave around a particular matter or process. They guide day to day decisions and behavior. Guiding Principles for the school district budget process are:           The annual budget complies with the school act, district policy and other legislative requirements. The Board must submit a balanced budget The budget process be aligned with and support the Board’s strategic direction and goals Provide an opportunity for Staff to report out on their budgets Provide for communication and consultation with partners/stakeholders New initiatives are sustainable for at least three years Ongoing costs should not be greater than ongoing revenues District contingency reserve is established and maintained in accordance with Board Policy Initiatives not within the K-12 mandate are at least cost neutral One time revenues and surpluses shall only be used to cover one-time costs Encourage efficiencies, sustainability and best practices Budget Criteria Setting decision criteria is best practice when making organizational decisions. Decision criteria in a business setting are those variables or characteristics that are important to the organization making the decision. They should help evaluate the alternatives from which you are choosing. Decision analysis often involves weighting criteria but in this context, criteria is set in priority order. When faced with a budget decision, this order should be considered in respect of where funding is directed first, and so on. The Budget Criteria prioritized for the 2019-20 Annual Budget have been summarized under an accompanying report. Each criteria has been aligned with the most relevant Board goal and paired with the most significant budget strategies that have either been maintained within, or added to, the Preliminary Budget; pending Board approval. Budget Challenges          Funding Model Review – inability to predict or forecast future MOE revenue Collective Bargaining – unknown changes to Collective Agreement provision/costs Aging facilities/technology/infrastructure – limited MOE Capital means high demand on the District’s Local Capital reserves Space constraints due to increased enrolment and restored class size and composition language Retention and recruitment – human capital turnover, unfilled positions and recruitment costs Large population of vulnerable students requiring supports Unfunded salary increases – Exempt , Principal/Vice Principal and Teacher increments must be funded within existing budgets Classroom Enhancement Fund - for the 2019-20 Preliminary Budget Ministry only provided for 90% of 2018-19 Teacher Staffing and no funding for Teacher increments. The District therefore had to supplement the Special Purpose Fund with Operating Funds in the amount of $1.7 million dollars in order to maintain required staffing and fund current costs. Pending the fall CEF submission, Staff hope to be fully funded and recover the supplement back to Operating to cover additional classrooms required pursuant to the restored teacher language for class size and composition. As well, associated Capital needs are not funded. Budget 2018-19 funded 4 Community School Coordinator positions from Restricted Surplus. The 2019-20 status quo Operating budget cannot fund these positions without an offsetting reduction for a net zero impact Key Budget Assumptions   Revenue and expense estimates are based on enrolment projections for 2019-20 which includes an increase of 88.89 student FTE The province will fund the District’s actual student enrolment on September 30th at the current per pupil rates provided in the Preliminary Operating Grant        Current programs will be continued and maintained at 2018-19 levels, including the newly added VAST Satellite School which was funded by restricted surplus in 2018-19 but has now been rolled into the Operating Fund Salary increases for Teachers and Unionized employees, including economic stability dividend increases, will be determined in bargaining and be fully funded by the Province The Classroom Enhancement Fund (CEF) will continue to fully fund the actual costs for the required number of teachers per the restored class size and composition language The overhead costs for the ongoing implementation of the CEF will continue to be funded as part of the CEF funding. Teacher increments, Exempt staff and Principal/Vice Principal salary increases continue to be unfunded by Ministry, they must be absorbed by the District Benefit costs are being reviewed but a small decrease was applied per projected costs No adjustment has been made for inflationary factors, any increases will be absorbed within the existing status quo budgets.
