STZ’UMINUS MEN’S GROUP Stz’uminus Men’s Group offers a supportive safe space for men to share in food, stories, personal reflections, peer support, and cultural knowledge.The Stz’uminus Men’s Group provides a vital and nurturing environment, creating a supportive and safe space exclusively for men. Within this community, participants are invited to connect over shared meals, engage in the rich tradition of storytelling, and delve into personal reflections. The group fosters an atmosphere of peer support, allowing men to uplift and guide one another through life’s challenges and triumphs. A significant aspect of the group’s offerings is the sharing and preservation of cultural knowledge, ensuring that the wisdom and traditions of the Stz’uminus people continue to thrive through intergenerational connection. Guest speakers, activities, and laughs included. Why Swimming is Great for People with Diabetes Swimming offers several important benefits for people living with diabetes—both Type 1 and Type 2. It’s a safe, low-impact, and highly effective form of exercise that supports physical and metabolic health. Here’s how swimming can help: 1. Blood Sugar Control -Improves insulin sensitivity: Regular aerobic exercise like swimming helps your muscles use insulin more effectively. -Lowers blood glucose levels: Swimming helps reduce blood sugar both during and after exercise, especially when done consistently. 2. Cardiovascular Health -People with diabetes are at higher risk for heart disease. Swimming strengthens the heart and improves circulation without putting too much stress on the body. 3. Weight Management -Swimming burns calories and builds lean muscle, which is key in managing body weight—a critical factor in managing or even reversing Type 2 diabetes. 4. Low Impact on Joints -Because water supports your body, swimming is gentle on joints. This is especially helpful for people with diabetes who also have arthritis, neuropathy, or obesity. 5. Reduces Stress and Improves Mood -Exercise like swimming releases endorphins and reduces stress hormones, which can help stabilize blood sugar and reduce diabetes-related anxiety or depression. 6. Improves Circulation -Poor circulation is common in diabetes, especially in the legs and feet. Swimming encourages full-body blood flow without the risks that come with high-impact activities. 7. Enhances Flexibility and Balance -Swimming movements improve overall body flexibility and coordination, which is especially helpful for older adults with diabetes. 18 MENTAL HEALTH & WELLNESS CLINICIAN Anna Lekomtseva – Mental Health & Wellness Clinician With a passion for mental health, healing, and personal growth, Anna focuses her practice on creating safe, empowering spaces—especially for women ages 16 and up. About Richard Hutchinson Richard brings over three decades of professional driving experience and a lifelong passion for supporting others through safe and reliable transportation. Born in 1964, Richard grew up surrounded by the trucking profession, inspired by his uncles who were truck drivers. In his early twenties, he began driving a church bus every Sunday across the Lower Mainland, a role he held for ten years. This experience not only shaped his skills but deepened his connection to service and community. For the past 33 years, Richard worked as a highway truck driver, traveling across North America before retiring and returning to Vancouver Island to enjoy a simpler life. Despite retiring from long-haul trucking, his enthusiasm for driving—especially helping people get where they need to go—remains strong. When he saw the opportunity to join the Stz’uminus First Nation as a bus driver, he felt it was the perfect fit for this chapter of his life.
