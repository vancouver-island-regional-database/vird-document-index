Trustee Zone Report 28-Feb-2018 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Northern Games with LIS - Leadership students hosted grade 7 students from LIS for a modified Northern Games tournament. Students had 3 hours of fun, cultural & activity. Leadership students planned, organized & executed 6 activities for more than 100 students. Events included: Bottom Scoot, Caribou Carry, Back Push, Alaskan High Kick, Stick Pull & the Leg Wrestle. They were challenging & fun!  Elder Project – LSS hosted SD68’s “Elder Project” on February 14. Land & Language-Based Learning (LLBL) students interviewed Elders to learn about their lives & hear about their teachings. Students will turn their interviews into poetry and be published in a book. On April 4th LSS and NDSS students will have a book launch & reading at the Port Theatre at 7PM.  Land and Language Based Learning Class - The main project for this 2nd semester class is to make a traditional Coast Salish Blanket. They have purchased wool and will learn how to prepare and fleece it using traditional methods. On a large loom (built by students & staff) they will weave the center piece under the guidance of Coast Salish elders and teachers. They will use smaller looms in class to weave smaller squares to be added to the larger blanket. LLBL students & staff will be have conversations on Reconciliation & hope to engage with feeder schools & the larger Ladysmith community on this important initiative. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  No report Ecole North Oyster  Read-a-Thon Fundraiser – Raised a total of $6,493 for the school library and during the two week challenge ENO read for 109,569 minutes, which amounts to 1826 hours or 76 days of reading!  Cedar Volleyball takes a beating – Bantam girls showed improvement in their skills or calling balls & team spirit, which was especially evident during their exceptional win over Cedar Elementary. The Bantam boys team showed positivity & team-building on the court, which translated into a playoff win against Cedar Elementary. Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  Acts of Kindness Month- Some of our classes have been walking around town and handing out Tim Horton’s gift cards to strangers, making cookies for other classes, reading to seniors at the retirement home.  Language & Culture – Is a part of learning at LIS every day. Recently, the LIS culture club made cedar bark heart baskets and staff participated in a “talking circle”, creating their own talking feathers to facilitate talking circles in their classes.  Teacher Pro-D - Teachers practiced STEM (Science Technology Engineering Math) activities, computational thinking, & calming techniques/classroom management during the morning and the afternoon was spent going over First Nation resources and developing cross curricular lesson plans.
