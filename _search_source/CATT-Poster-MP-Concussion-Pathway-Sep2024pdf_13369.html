Concussion Awareness, Response, and Management for the Motion Picture, Film, & Live Performance Industries If a significant impact or motion to the head or body occurs that can cause the brain to move inside the skull that leads one to suspect a concussion CALL FOR ON-SITE FIRST AID, REMOVE FROM ACTIVITY IMMEDIATELY AND ASSESS FOR RED FLAGS STOP RED FLAGS Neck pain or tenderness Seizure, ‘fits’, or convulsion Loss of vision or double vision Loss of consciousness Increased confusion or deteriorating conscious state (becoming less responsive, drowsy) Weakness or numbness/tingling in more than one arm or leg Repeated vomiting Severe or increasing headache Increasingly restless, agitated, or combative IF YES TO ANY OF THE ABOVE: Call an ambulance or seek immediate medical care IF NO TO ALL RED FLAGS: Assess for signs and symptoms of concussion FOLLOW MEDICAL ADVICE, AND: INCREASED RISK OF CONCUSSION IF: Currently recovering from a concussion Follow initial period of relative rest (i.e., activities of daily living including walking and other light physical and cognitive activities are permitted as tolerated) for a maximum of 24-48 hours post-injury. Previous history of concussion Experiencing persistent concussion symptoms CONCUSSION SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS Engage in light physical and cognitive activity that does not result in more than mild and brief* exacerbation (worsening) of concussion symptoms. Limit screen time for the first 24-48 hours (smartphones, computers, TV) Engage in light cognitive activities (e.g. reading) Engage in light physical activity (e.g. walking) Headache / Pressure in the head “Don’t feel right” Difficulty concentrating Balance problems / Dizziness Neck pain Difficulty remembering Nausea or vomiting More emotional Feeling slowed down Blurred vision More irritable Feeling like “in a fog” Light / Sound sensitivity Sadness Trouble falling asleep Fatigue or low energy Nervous or anxious Note: Sleep is important! Do not wake during the night if sleeping comfortably *Mild exacerbation (worsening) of symptoms: No more than a 2-point increase when compared with the pre-activity value on a 0-10-point symptom severity scale. "Brief" exacerbation of symptoms: Worsening of symptoms for up to 1 hour. IF YES TO ANY OF THE ABOVE: SEEK MEDICAL ATTENTION from a doctor, nurse practitioner, or licensed healthcare professional with relevant training AFTER 48 HOURS: PERFORMERS: Refrain from repeating actions that caused initial impact and/or repetitive jarring motions ALL WORKERS: Limit physical activity and advise worker to watch for signs and symptoms for 48 hours IF SYMPTOMS OBSERVED WITHIN 48 HOURS Follow Return to Work strategy Follow Return to Activity strategy Follow Return to School strategy Follow Return to Sport strategy For more information on concussions, visit cattonline.com IF NO SYMPTOMS: ! MENTAL HEALTH During the course of recovery from a concussion, seek medical attention for mental health challenges as needed, such as: IF NO SYMPTOMS OBSERVED AFTER 48 HOURS More emotional Nervousness or anxiousness Irritability Trouble falling asleep Sadness Depression RESUME NORMAL ACTIVITY This document is an information and educational guide for the live performance industryy only, tailored to account for specific requirements and constraints experienced in that industry.
