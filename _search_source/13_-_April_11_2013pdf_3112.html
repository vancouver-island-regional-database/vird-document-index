CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Board Chair - Jamie Brennan Opening Remarks Chair Brennan thanked the public for attending this evening's meeting. He said the Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan is one of the district's three strategic planning goals and that the Board has asked the senior management team to develop a draft 10-year facilities enhancement plan to meet that goal. Introduction - Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO Mr. Hutchinson said the draft plan is expected to be presented to the Board by senior management at the April 24 Board meeting. If the Board accepts the plan for consultation, that process would take place during May and June. He said the draft plan will be a multi-year plan and that its primary focus will be how facilities Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2013 can be enhanced to provide better learning opportunities for students, taking into account optimal school size, the equitable delivery of programs and services, strengthening the system’s capacity to offer a variety of learning options for students, and providing modern, upto-date facilities. Mr. Hutchinson said that Dr. Doug Player is an experienced and respected educator, and has been working as a consultant to assist the senior management team with the definition of the key elements of the plan. Enhanced Facilities for Learning - Ten-Year Plan Presenter: Dr. Doug Player Dr. Player told the audience that the plan is directed to accomplishing the following goals: • • • • • First and foremost, to enhance learning opportunities for all students; Second, to garner new (and long overdue) state of the art replacement facilities; Third, to consolidate costly positions so funding can go to the classroom; Fourth, to save wasted maintenance funds; and, Finally, to improve the long term fiscal position of the Board. The expectations of the plan are that it will also: • • • • • Improve student achievement results; Improve program offerings for students; Renew the approach and improve conditions for alternate program students; Assist in the achievement of the Board goals; and, Assist in balancing the budget long term. The plan is a 10-year plan divided into three stages: immediate actions to be undertaken in years one and two; medium term actions to be undertaken in years three through five, and long term which includes recommendations for years six through ten. The plan recommends that the Board take the following actions: • • Consolidate ten facilities; Rebuild four facilities; Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2013 • • • • • Rethink and repurpose the DRC/LRC; Institute three new enrichment programs; Relocate and renew the approach to the alternate programs; Adjust the immersion program; and Relocate the administrative functions. Dr. Player suggestions include an arts program, advanced placement, and International Baccalaureate in some of our schools. A summary of the plan by years: Years One and Two • • • • • • • • • Relocate Alternate Programs to NDSS Relocate Elementary Life Skills from Rutherford to Fairview Rethink the District Resource Centre Consolidate Cedar Secondary with John Barsby Realign the Cedar community which includes four school consolidations (North Cedar, North Oyster, South Wellington, and Woodbank) into the Cedar Secondary building Reconfigure Ladysmith Secondary to Grades 6-12 Consolidate Davis Road and Ladysmith Intermediate Add on to Hammond Bay gym Move Grade 11 and 12 students from Woodlands to NDSS Years Three to Five • • • • • Consolidate Woodlands Secondary with NDSS, sending students to NDSS in a phased-in move Rebuild NDSS Build a new Ladysmith elementary, closing Ladysmith Intermediate Rebuild Cilaire Elementary Consolidate Departure Bay Elementary with Cilaire Elementary Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 11, 2013 Years Six to Ten • • • Rebuild Rutherford Elementary Move the District Administration Centre to the Selby Street site Move all facilities functions to the Old Victoria Road site IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Curley Folio S13/04/11-02 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receives the Enhanced Facilities for Learning - Ten Year Plan prepared by Dr. Doug Player, and refers it to staff for recommendations for the development of a plan for School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith).
