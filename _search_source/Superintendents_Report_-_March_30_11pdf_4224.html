School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT Regular Board Meeting Wednesday, March 30, 2011 School Activities during the past month This month, recognition goes out to several School District 68 students who are recent award-winners:      Prior to the March Spring Break, students from SD68 secondary schools and the CTC program had outstanding results at the Skills B.C. Canada South Island Regional Competition 2011. Seven students took gold, five students took silver, and six students went home with the bronze. In all, SD68 students won 18 of the 29 awards given at the competition, in the fields of welding, automotive, carpentry, cabinetmaking, 3-D animation, culinary and fashion design. Josiah Stefani, from Dover Bay Secondary, is one of the four top provincial prize winners of an ICBC contest to design an advertisement about road safety. He won a MacBook Pro computer as the top winner on Vancouver Island for his creative ad about speeding. Josiah’s advertisement is available on the ICBC website, and will be used in future ICBC advertising campaigns. Grade 12 Dover Bay student Ryan Yewchin placed second at the B.C. School Sports wrestling championships in February, qualifying him for the wrestling nationals in Ontario in April. The Wellington Jazz Academy’s Grade 12 band and the McCartie Trio took top honours in their divisions at the 44th Annual Lionel Hampton International Jazz Festival in Moscow, Idaho. Dover Bay Dolphins won the Island AAA Boys Championship title in March, and headed to the provincials March 15 to 19 in Langley. The team placed 13th overall at the provincial tournament. Despite the fact that this was a short month for schools, a number of special activities took place in schools around the district in March:     Students at Hammond Bay Elementary participated in an Amazing Race project, where students worked together to solve clues and tasks that were both academic and fun. The project reinforced the virtues of respect, cooperation, and tolerance. Student Support Services hosted All Kids Go To School – an information evening for parents of next year’s Kindergarten students with special needs. Grades 4 and 5 student teams from Bayview, Frank J. Ney, Mountain View, Pauline Haarer, and Quarterway elementary schools participated in Reading Link Challenges. The Reading Link Challenge was a partnership between Nanaimo Harbourfront Library and the five schools. Students were asked questions about six books they were given to read in November. Thanks to a recent donation from Van Roc Interiors, Oceanside Rotary and the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation, Georgia Avenue Community School received a commercial dishwasher. The new industrial dishwasher means that students will now consistently receive hot lunches. Michael J. Munro Superintendent of Schools/CEO -1-
