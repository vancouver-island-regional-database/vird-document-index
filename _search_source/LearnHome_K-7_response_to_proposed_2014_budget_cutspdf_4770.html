Learn@Home Parents K-7 Date: April 15, 2014 Dot Neary Board Chair School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue N anaim o, British Columbia V9R 3K6 Dear Mrs. Neary: This is regarding the School District 68’s list of proposed cuts to next year’s budget. One of the proposed cuts would reduce the secretarial hours at Learn@home K-7 and reclassify our secretary as a Secretary 1 position rather than Secretary 2. We are opposed to these cuts for the following reasons: 1. In 2011, Learn@Home K-7 underwent a Quality Review by the Ministry of Education. Over the last decade, Quality Reviews have been conducted on dozens of Distributed Learning schools in BC. The inspectors noted that our parents were the most engaged parents in the province of BC. We really feel as though we are true partners in our children's education. The teachers at Learn@Home ask us for our input into our own children's education, in finding their 'spark' and helping to nourish it with meaningful activities that cover the curriculum. This is compatible with the school district’s goal for our education system – student’s learning and parents who care about their learning and the feelings of being connected to the Learn@Home K-7 program. This proposal will reduce our connection to Learn@Home and we are strongly opposed to it! 2. Keeping the position as it is will allow our Learn@Home secretary to deal with all the financial transactions for our children at the Learn@Home K-7 site at Departure Bay School. We highly value this. Sharon works directly with the Vendor and the parents to process hundreds of transactions each year. These hundreds of transactions are accompanied by hundreds of questions by both vendors and parents. Transferring the accounting services to NDSS will create a hardship for parents and vendors. This will cause delays and loss in efficiency. We need to keep these services with our Learn@Home secretary. 3. Reduced secretarial hours will mean a day and a half a week with no secretary. This will mean parents will often hear an answering machine during regular school hours. Or it will mean our teachers will have to deal with issues related to secretarial work that will take time and attention away from their students, our children. 4. On a personal note, we run a great risk of losing Sharon Binng, our secretary. It is important to maintain continuity in a distributed learning program. We cannot lose her wealth of knowledge and efficiency in supporting this program. 5. Our children do not attend full time, therefore our needs as families for communication and contact with the school are actually greater and not less than traditional neighborhood schools. Communicating regularly with the secretary as well as school staff ensures and solidifies our connection to Learn@Home K-7. 6. The few thousand dollars saved in these proposed cuts will be more than lost if even just a few students leave Learn@Home K-7 based on a feeling of disconnection and even less service than we are currently able to access. There are already some parents talking about pulling their students from this school and registering in other DL programs in other districts if these proposed cuts go through. This means the ministry grant for these students of over $5200 per student will be lost in our district. 2 7. Our school enrollment has risen this year to an all-time high of 105 students. Students from Learn@Home K-7 had the opportunity to present to the trustees in February of this year and we were featured in Focus 68 magazine in the winter edition of 2013. Both occasions celebrated the many successes of our students in meeting the goals of the Success For All initiatives. Our school is running as effectively as we can in an era of reduced budgets. Our parents are currently engaged and happy. Please allow us to continue to participate in our children's education in an effective manner by keeping our school secretarial position just as it is. Thank You, Tina Hein and Robyn Quigg Parents at L@H K-7 3
