SES EMPLOYMENT Cover photo provided by Ramona Sampson & restored by Damien Daniels 02 03 04 05 06 07 08 ANNUAL B.C. 55+ SUMMER GAMES NANAIMO – The Harbour City came alive as thousands of athletes, supporters, and spectators arrived for the Annual 55+ BC Games, the largest multi-sport event of its kind in the province. Among the participants was our very own Stz’uminus Councillor Terry Sampson, who represented the community in slo-pitch softball, finishing the weekend with a record of one win and two losses. The Games, which rotate among host communities each year, date back to 1988 when the inaugural BC Seniors Games were held in Vernon. That first event saw 650 athletes 09 competing in 15 sports. Since then, the event has grown significantly, drawing participants from across the province and creating strong legacies of sport and community spirit in each host city. This year’s Nanaimo Games mark the largest turnout since the pandemic, with 2,700 competitors taking part in more than 20 sports, including pickleball, swimming, track and field, curling, darts, golf, soccer, tennis, and slo-pitch. The event was expected to attract over 3,000 visitors in total and generate an estimated $6.5 million for the local economy. Organizers say the Games are about more than just competition. They celebrate active living, community connection, and the joy of sport at every age. Classes joined for a special movie outing, giving students the chance to learn, connect, and enjoy being together outside of the classroom. It was a meaningful day filled with curiosity, friendship, and pride in walking this journey as one community. We are proud of how our students represented Stz’uminus with respect and kindness throughout the day.
