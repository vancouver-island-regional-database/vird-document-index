THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: November 28, 2012 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dale Versfelt, Manager, Occupational Health & Safety SUBJECT: Emergency Procedure Re Colliery Dam The District’s OHS Department has met three times with the City of Nanaimo’s Engineers and Emergency Response planners. OHS staff have attended both Community Open House meetings held on November 5th and 8th. There was minimal interest expressed by the public with regard to the school evacuation at the Open House Meetings. Most of the concern was directed to saving the dams. OHS staff coordinated a meeting with the John Barsby Administration and the City Engineers and Emergency Response planners to discuss an appropriate evacuation plan. The EQ plans for John Barsby have been modified to include evacuation to 7th and Howard Avenue, the new Mustering Location. Letters have been sent home to the parents of John Barsby students and all staff have been advised of the procedures. John Barsby held a drill on November 14th using the revised format. They made it to the mustering corner (7th and Howard) in less than 10 minutes. They advised Little Ferns Day Care about the drill. New information indicates that if the water gets to John Barsby quicker than anticipated (e.g. a total collapse of the Dam versus a slow growing breach) staff and students would move to the second floor of the school and not leave the building. Georgia Avenue Community School is expected to remain outside of the flood plain but will remain open as a community resource in case of flooding. The Transportation Department has confirmed that they will participate in transportation of students and staff from the Howard and 7th Avenue Mustering Point to the Beban Reception Centre for parent pick up following the evacuation routes allowed by the City. John Barsby’s administrative staff have communicated with Little Ferns that they have included them in the John Barsby evacuation plan. The District’s OHS Department is signed up for the City’s Emergency Alert System. We have been advised that work on the Dam is slated to begin in the summer of 2013 with an expected 6 month completion date. There will be increased risk of flooding during the decommissioning which makes summer an ideal time for us as John Barsby will not be in session. The School District is at the front end for communication updates from the City with regard to status of the decommissioning project. Assemble on corner of 7th and Howard Field
