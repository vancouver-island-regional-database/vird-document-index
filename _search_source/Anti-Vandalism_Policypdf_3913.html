Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: June 25, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Anti-Vandalism Policy 2.14 Background: This report is prepared in response to Board motion R13/11/27-15: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) develops an Anti-Vandalism policy and procedure and that it be included in the Board's Policy Manual and further, that the AntiVandalism Committee report directly to the Board. This policy was sent out for Notice of Motion in April and has been edited in keeping with feedback received. The Anti-Vandalism Board Policy is now in the following final draft form: Board Policy 2.14: Anti-Vandalism The Board shall maintain multi-faceted programs to prevent and reduce acts of vandalism and illegal entry. Definitions: • • • • “Illegal entry” is the act of breaking and entering without lawful justification or excuse. “Intruder” is a person who enters without permission. “Vandalism” is damage/loss due to willful behaviour. “Loitering” is the act of remaining in a particular public place for a protracted time without an apparent purpose. This Policy is based on the following principles: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. The primary focus of District anti-vandalism initiatives should be on prevention. Restitution should be sought from those responsible for acts of vandalism and/or illegal entry. Every effort should be made to identify the persons suspected of vandalism or illegal entry. If possible, individuals found illegally on school property shall be charged. Within cost and time constraints, every effort shall be made in the design and equipping of school facilities to deter and resist vandalism, illegal entry, and loitering. 1 In support of the Anti-Vandalism policy and principles, a District Anti-Vandalism Committee will be struck, comprised of representation from the Board of Education, the District Aboriginal Leaders for Learning Committee, District Administration, the Nanaimo Schools Administrators Association (Principals and VicePrincipals), the District Parent Advisory Council, CUPE Local 606, the City of Nanaimo, John Howard Society, regional governments, Nanaimo and District Crime Stoppers and the RCMP. The committee’s terms of reference will include the following: 1. To develop/recommend anti-vandalism strategies that are both preventative and reactive. 2. To monitor the effectiveness of anti-vandalism policy, administrative procedures, and initiatives. 3. To report to the Board of Education, on a quarterly basis, relevant data connected to the impact of vandalism, and the effectiveness of specific anti-vandalism initiatives. 4. To apply for discretionary funding in support of annual anti-vandalism initiatives, as recommended by the Committee. Recommendation: That the Board of Education approve Board Policy 2.14: Anti-Vandalism, and that the Superintendent/CEO oversees the appropriate communication and dissemination to district administrative staff. 2
