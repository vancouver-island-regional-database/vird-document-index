Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: June 8, 2016 TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Mike Ross, Facilities Planning Manager SUBJECT: Cedar Elementary Multi-Use Court Replacement Background: On April 13, 2016 the Cedar Ball Hockey Challenge Association (CBHCA) made a presentation to the Business Committee to partially fund ($20K) the resurfacing of the multi-use court at Cedar Elementary (formerly referred to as NCI), with the balance of project cost to be funded by Nanaimo Ladysmith Public School (NLPS). The Board passed the following motion at its April 27, 2016 Board Meeting: Folio R16/04/27-17 The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to develop a report on the viability of the proposal from the Cedar Ball Hockey Challenge Association and report back to the May Board meeting NLPS staff met with CBHCA on two separate occasions over the past couple of months, (April 29 and May 10), to review the intent, scope, and roles responsibilities of the project and have had further discussions with the outside paving contractor (HubCity). Further, the school supports this project as it provides enhanced outdoor space for students to use. Currently, the multi-use court gets heavy use during recess and lunch breaks. The addition of primary students to the school in September, will likely lead to increased activity on the court during those periods. A new asphalt surface without cracks and the severely sloped drainage areas would reduce tripping hazards and increase safety. Also, existing game lines are faded and require painting at some point. This project would look to address that issue. The CBHCA proposal was to provide a 2” asphalt topping over the existing multi-use court surface and did not include costing for re-painting of game lines, site work or a contingency allowance. To add these costs to the proposal this increases the costs by over $10,000. Upon reviewing the proposal, it was determined that raising the court surface 2” overall would create safety hazards around the perimeter of the court area at stairs, fence openings, building interface along south elevation, and other issues such as; rain water management from adjacent buildings/canopies and there is a strong likelihood that the existing cracks and weeds (approx. 800 lineal feet) would telegraph through to the new asphalt surface. To address these issues, additional work with a cost of over $15,000 would need to be completed. Through this review, it was determined the best course of action would be to completely replace the entire multi-use court. This option would also ensure the new asphalt surface would be covered under warranty. This has caused the cost of the project to increase from the initial proposal of around $40,000 to over $60,000 per the attached costing. This additional cost is less than the resurfacing cost when you add the missing costs in the original proposal, ($10,000), and the cost of the remediation work of $15,000 with the added benefit of the warranty. If the complete replacement option is chosen, as an added benefit to NLPS, recycled asphalt pavement (RAP) would be salvaged and used on the existing gravel fire access lane and parking area adjacent to the annex building at minimal cost. Recommendation: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) replace the entire multi-use court asphalt surface at Cedar Elementary subject to the Cedar Ball Hockey Challenge Association funding $20,000 for the project. The District will fund the remaining cost of the project through the Annual Facilities Grant (AFG).
