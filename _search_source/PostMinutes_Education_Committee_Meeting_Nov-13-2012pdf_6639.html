CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY CORRESPONDENCE REFERRED FROM THE REGULAR BOARD MEETING Ms. Tina Olesen RE: "Mind-Up" Curriculum Chair Howland asked Gifted/Enrichment Teacher Jan Thorsen to join Committee members at the table. Ms. Thorsen said she is one of two teacher trainers for the MindUp program on Vancouver Island. Ms. Thorsen said that the MindUP program is one tool for self-regulation, attention and focus that a teacher can use in a classroom, and it complements other social emotional learning such as Roots of Empathy and Friends. She noted that research has demonstrated mindful attention leads to enhanced self-awareness, selfregulation, and positive emotional states that counter negative outcomes. The program was originally piloted in Vancouver-area school districts and as a result has many ties to the B.C. curriculum. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Instructional Coordinator Darlene Crane, District Behaviour Resource Teacher Rose Tull and District Principal of Aboriginal Education Laura Tait spoke to the Committee about RTI. RTI is a flexible, fluid problem-solving model and a collaborative, systematic support for all learners. It provides a framework to connect evidence-based instructional practices. The three tiers of RTI are: QUESTION PERIOD  Tier 1 – Core universal instruction and supports – general academic and social behavior instruction and support provided to all students in all settings.  Tier 2 – Targeted interventions and supports – more targeted instruction/intervention and supplemental support in addition to and aligned with the core academic and social behavior prescribed learning outcomes  Tier 3 – Intensive, individualized interventions and supports – The most intense instruction and intervention, including such things as increased time, narrowed focus and/or reduced group size, based on student needs and aligned with Tier 1 and 2 academic and social behavior prescribed learning outcomes.
