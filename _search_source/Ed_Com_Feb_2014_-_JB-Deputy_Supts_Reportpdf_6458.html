Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Deputy Superintendent’s REPORT Education Committee Meeting Wednesday, February 5, 2014 Learning Services Update 1) General: • Changes in Learning Services Positions: o Director of Instruction: Laura Tait o Supervisor of Instruction: Darlene Crane • Projected Student Enrolment: Currently in the process of being finalized; schools have created their projections for next school year. • Staffing: We are currently communicating with the Unions on the process of consolidation and its effects on staffing. Once the Board has completed its decisions on school consolidations and closures, the department will move forward with staffing plans. • Assistant Superintendents have completed school/principal visits that included discussions about goals and vision. 2) Assessment Review Committee: • Drafted spring workplan including establishing dates for determining assessment tool recommendations and implementation strategies • Reviewed initial fall pilot feedback information from principals, teachers, instructional coordinators, and student support teachers • Parent representative meeting to preview assessment tools 3) Response to Intervention: • Two teacher in-service opportunities with approximately 90 teacher representatives from Grades 4-9 • Leadership Series Event w/ over 100 principals and teacher representatives • Student Support Teacher in-service 5 key components of reading and diagnostic assessment strategies • Initiated LSST refinement pilot to enhance efficiency and effectiveness of model 1 4) Professional Learning Communities: • Established District PLC Leadership Teams inclusive of a District Design, Communication, and Community Support Structure sub-teams • Created a communication package for school administrators 5) E23 • Provided in-service for all K-3 teachers focused on high impact instructional practices for reading comprehension in November • In-service the first week of February will include high impact instructional strategies on Vocabulary and Fluency 6) Schools: Activities: • • John Barsby: o Barsby’s Welcome Poles (four poles featuring Aboriginal student designs) are now completed and were formally Blessed during a short ceremony held Thursday morning, December 19. Parents/guardians and Barsby’s neighbours were invited to witness the Blessing. o Preparations are underway for the Northern Games which are scheduled for Friday, March 14 at Barsby. The Games, this year, is a cooperative event with Cedar Secondary and John Barsby competing together. Ecole Davis Road: o Congratulations to all the Ecole Davis Road students who participated in the first two district sports football and cross-country running events. Students developed skills and proudly represented their school. A special congratulations goes out to the bantam girls football team for winning the district championship, and to the bantam boys football team for finishing third in the league championships.
