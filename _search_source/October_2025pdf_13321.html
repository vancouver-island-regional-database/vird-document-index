SENIOR STAFF REPORTS 8.1 Jacquie Poulin, Assistant Superintendent and Margaret Olsen, Assistant Superintendent Re: School Start-Up and Summer Programs Update School Presentation from Rutherford Elementary School Jacquie Poulin and Margaret Olsen provided the Committee with an updates on summer programming opportunities that were provided to our families and updates on school start-up this September. An information sheet is attached to the agenda. Mike Lundine, Principal, shared about the re-opening of Rutherford Elementary 8.2 Kerri Steel, Director of Instruction Re: Accessibility Committee Workplan 2025-2026 Kerri Steel presented the NLPS Accessibility Committee's workplan. An information sheet, together with the workplan is attached to the agenda. 8.3 Kerri Steel, Director of Instruction Re: Building Education Assistants Through Mentorship (BEAM) 2 Kerri Steel provided an update on the Building Education Assistants Through Mentorship (BEAM) program which provides learning opportunities for individuals who wish to become education assistants in NLPS. An information sheet is attached to the agenda. 8.4 Shawn Johnston, Executive Director of HR Re: Administrative Procedure - Employee Recognition Shawn Johnston introduced a new Administrative Procedure related to employee recognition. Administrative Procedure 403.3AP – Employee Recognition, which establishes a framework for the Board to recognize and celebrate the commitment and contributions employees make to the work of the district over time. An information sheet, together with the AP, is attached to the agenda. 8.5 Mark Walsh, Secretary- Treasurer Re: Administrative Procedure 603.7 - Unexpected Health Emergencies in Schools Mark Walsh presented an updated Administrative Procedure (AP 603.7) Unexpected Health Emergencies in Schools, pursuant to the mandate of the Ministry of Education and Child Care. An information sheet and the updated AP are attached to the agenda. 8.6 Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Re: Long Range Facilities Plan Advisory Committee Mark Walsh outlined the recommendations from the Long Range Facilities Plan Advisory Committee for the focus during the 2025-26 school year and also outlined recommended changes to the terms of reference for the Committee along with rationale. An action sheet is attached to the agenda. SD25/10/08-03 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Keller IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Robinson The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the Long Range Facilities Plan Advisory Committee’s work plan for the 2025-26 school year and that the revised terms of reference for the Committee be approved.
