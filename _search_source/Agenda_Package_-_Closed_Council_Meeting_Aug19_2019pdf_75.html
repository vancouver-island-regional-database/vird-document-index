We understand the Town of Ladysmith wants to be prepared for potential interest from the market for purchasing the entirety of the Waterfront Lands outright. This update is intended to take a fresh look at the potential value the Town could command for the Waterfront in its entirety through sale to a single buyer. As part of this work, we will also examine mechanisms that the Town may use to ensure that waterfront lands development will occur according to plan, and in a timely manner, rather than be left undeveloped as a holding property. Since completion of the first iteration of the Waterfront Lands valuation, we understand that the Town has secured funding for the Arts & Heritage Hub, the Machine Shop, and there is potential funding for major remediation efforts. The available funding for the Creative Hub and Machine Shop could imply that the phasing of the project will change from what was initially assumed. These new assumptions will potentially have an impact on the values and development timing. The sections below outline Urban Systems’ proposed scope of services, fees by scope item, and anticipated project timelines. Please do not hesitate to contact us if you have any questions about this quote. Please do not hesitate to call us with any questions. Sincerely, URBAN SYSTEMS LTD. Justin M. Barer, M.Pl, MCIP, RPP Lead, Land Economics 550 - 1090 Homer Street, Vancouver, BC V6B 2W9 | T: 604.235.1701 Page 46 of 61 Date: August 15, 2019 File: 1616.0016.00 Attention: Guillermo Ferrero, Chief Administrative Officer Page: 2 of 2 Proposed Work Scope 1. Review / Update Market Inputs for Pro-forma models The pro-forma model that was created to value the Waterfront Lands will be reviewed and all input assumptions will be either re-confirmed or updated as needed. This may include: assumed values of developed units, the timing of sales, borrowing rates, development costs, etc. 2. Update Financial Analysis The updated financial analysis will look at a scenario where the Waterfront Lands are sold all at once to a single buyer, however this will also take into consideration the potential value that the Town could garner from a phased sale over time. In the original analysis, it was determined that a phased sale would allow for the Town to achieve higher returns overall, as well as maintain more control over the project. The Town must have an updated understanding of the potential value under different disposition scenarios to ensure that it is in a strong negotiating position with this, or any other, proponent. 3. Sale Structure - Benchmarking With the potential that a purchaser will look to acquire the entire Waterfront Lands at once, the Town should be aware of options available to maximize likelihood of timely development of the site. It is conceivable that the buyer is considering the lands as a long-term hold (i.e. a place to ‘park’ capital) with no intention to develop in the short-term. This section of the study will provide recommendations on stipulations that could be included in a sale to ‘push’ timely development. Examples from other land sales / developments will be benchmarked to provide insights on best practices that could be applied in this case. 4. Report Preparation The results of the updated financial analysis and benchmarking exercises will be presented in a short, memorandum-style report, which will be submitted electronically to the Town for review. Following review by Town staff, Urban Systems will participate in a conference call to answer questions and identify areas requiring further clarification. Urban Systems will then make required edits and submit a final report document. Proposed Project Fees Urban Systems is prepared to undertake the above work plan for a fee budget of $13,500, plus $1,080 in disbursements, for a total of $14,580 excluding applicable taxes. Any in-person meetings or presentations to council would require additional fees, which could be charged on an hourly basis. If the Town of Ladysmith requires further support, such as reviewing purchase offers or assistance with the negotiation process, this could also be provided on an hourly basis or through another fixed-fee work plan. Timing Our team is prepared to complete the above work scope within 4-6 weeks of project start-up. We are available to commence work within 2 weeks of your receipt of this document. Honour our Past THE 2020 to 2024 STRATEGIC PLAN – How it was developed In early 2019, Council began a strategic planning process designed to set the vision and strategic path that Council will follow for the organization and the community over the next four years. Council’s first action was to identify our vision for the future of our town, determine the strategic priorities that will guide us towards achieving our vision, and confirm our guiding principles, the values that underpin everything we do. Council then held several follow-up sessions to confirm the specific details of just how we will accomplish our strategic plan and how we will keep the community informed of our progress. Below are some highlights of the discussions that helped lead us to our plan.
