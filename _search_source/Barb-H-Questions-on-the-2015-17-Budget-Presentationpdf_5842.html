QUESTIONS ON THE 2015-17 SD68 BUDGET PROCESS 1. Ref. pg. 3 “Rentals and Leases” Under “Community Use Rentals” next year’s budget shows a reduction of approx. $81,836. This was a revenue stream created by the closure of Mt. Benson with the loss of 3 tenants. Now that Learn @ Home K-7 and 8-12 will be taking up residence here, that revenue stream is now lost. Learn @ Home does not help the district reduce its over-capacity issue. - However, it still means that District Operational Costs will increase with the use of this “school” without addressing our overall capacity issues correct? 2. Reference pg. 3 “Capital Revenue” Amortization of Deferred Capital Revenue shows a proposed 2016/17 budget amount of $5,045,000. - Can you explain what that means? What is Deferred Capital Revenue and what does that amount specifically relate to? 3. Reference pg. 3 “Net Transfers (to) from Other Funds” Under Operating to Local Capital under both budget years shows both credited and debited amounts of $1,064,200. - What does this mean and what is this for? - What Operating account is this referencing i.e. the AFG Account? - My understanding is that the District cannot transfer funds from the Operating Account to the AFG Account? Is this correct? 4. Reference pg. 4 “AFG Projects” Under the 2015/16 budget, the Cedar Secondary Budget is showing as 1 million dollars. - Is this for the conversion to K-7 or is this the cost of reconversion back to a Secondary school? If it is the reconversion, what was the total amount for the conversion to K-7? - What then is the amount of $252,000 showing in 2016/17 for Cedar Secondary Conversion? - Also, what is the AFG Administration in 2016/17 of $100,000 proposed for? Is this money being allotted for consultants and if so why are we paying them when we have Staff to administer this? - Do we have more definitive enrollment numbers for the Cedar Secondary re-opening and if those projected numbers continue to be far below optimum capacity, how does the District intend to justify to the Ministry an increase in operational expenses in the absence of that? 5. Reference pg. 4 “Administrative Officers” Elementary Principals vs. Secondary Principals. Elementary Principals appear to receive an increase from 2015/16 to 2016/17 of approx. 8% whereas Secondary Principals have an increase of approx. 20%. - Can you explain such a large spread? 6. Reference pg. 8 “Other Professionals” The Superintendent’s proposed increase from $191,802 to $229,360 is approx. 19.5%. Increases to the Spvr. of Student Data and the Transportation Spvr. are only slightly above 8%. - Can you explain that huge disparity? 7. Reference pg. 12 “Becoming Sustainable” To reduce average secondary class size by 0.8 FTE would require an addition of 6 teachers at a cost of $620,000. Adding this cost and the rollover amount noted earlier creates a total deficit of $1,470,000. Staff propose to balance the budget by realizing $970,000 from reductions and $500,000 should be applied from the accumulated surplus as this is the unrealized efficiencies on the potential closure of Rutherford Elementary. - What if Rutherford does not close at the end of next year then how are you proposing to find $500,000 in efficiencies in the absence of the closure?
