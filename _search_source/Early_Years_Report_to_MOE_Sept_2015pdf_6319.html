In Nanaimo Ladysmith we are pleased to report that Policy and Contractual requirements are consistently met at all our Centres. Our Facilitators agree that the Learning Environments are mostly exemplary with improvement plans in place for the few areas currently in the 'adequate' zone. District inclusion of our StrongStart Facilitators in our literacy initiative 'E23‐‐Early Years to Grade 3' has led to focussed attention on both 'Exploration and Creativity' and 'Language and Literacies' goals from the Early Learning Framework. Over the last two years Kindergarten Teachers teamed with District Instructional Resource Teachers to provide a series of in‐service opportunities for our Facilitators. These sessions were warmly received, increased knowledge, improved practice, and strengthened the StrongStart/Kindergarten connection in schools. All our StrongStart Facilitators provide predictable schedules and thoughtfully plan group experiences for their families. Last year 'Double Up Days' were introduced with a two‐pronged effect: 1. providing a visiting Facilitator with the opportunity to observe, learn, and help out in a different Centre and; 2. allowing the host Facilitator to plan a large group event knowing she could count on an extra pair of skilled hands. All our StrongStart programs have use of the school gym and library and participate in a variety of events such as assemblies, buddy classes, picture day and theme days. The District has a committed presence at the two local Early Years leadership tables as well as active participation with the Island Early Years Network. 1 of 2 In June of last year our StrongStart team discussed parts of the 'Reflecting on Quality Tool' and generated a list of four focus topics for professional development: 1. Aboriginal Understandings; 2. Early Language Development; 3. Supporting Parenting and; 4. Infant Development. School District and Community resource people have been engaged and a yearlong in‐service calendar has been organized to address these areas. Continuing to strengthen the StrongStart/school connection has also been identified as an important goal. In early September, StrongStart Principals were called together for a kick‐off meeting. The StrongStart professional development calendar was distributed and ideas were shared about the daily and weekly ways a Principal might support a Centre. A schedule for Principals and Facilitators to meet and talk about specific areas of the 'Reflecting on Quality Tool' has been introduced. After the Principal/Facilitator discussions, the Facilitators will have an opportunity to meet and share their reflections. Another connection building strategy involves each of the Facilitators scheduling an early fall Kindergarten visit day. We're hopeful this opportunity will build their relationship with their Kindergarten teacher and afford them an opportunity to observe the routines and expectations of the Kindergarten classroom. Continuing to address attendance/program usage analytics and increasing community awareness of our StrongStart Programs are two district areas of focus for this year. We intend to increase our web presence and introduce the use of mobile app to communicate predictable and unpredictable centre closures. We would also like to adjust our district registration process to reduce barriers for families. We have already introduced two strategies aimed at increasing attendance: 1. adjusting the start times closer to school opening and; 2. aligning the opening and closing times of all the morning programs. 2 of 2
