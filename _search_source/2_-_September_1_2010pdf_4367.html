From Other Committees SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT Mr. Munro said that many departments worked hard during the summer months to make sure school buildings and grounds are in the best shape possible for the new school year. A number of the facilities projects that were completed are: Roof replacements at Woodlands, Quarterway, Rutherford, Georgia Avenue, John Barsby, Princess Royal and Pleasant Valley Washroom upgrades at Ladysmith Primary, Park Avenue, Departure Bay, and Pauline Haarer - $200,000 Flooring was replaced at a number of schools Exterior repairs and painting at several schools including John Barsby, Frank J. Ney and Seaview - $100,000 Replaced failing boiler at Cilaire Elementary with higher efficiency unit - $100,000 Building envelope repairs at North Oyster $40,000 Renovations on the Old Student Support Services Building on Selby in partnership with the private daycare to provide expanded daycare services Network and wiring upgrades at several schools, including data connections to photocopiers to provide, scanner, fax, printer functions Traffic safety Improvements at Randerson Ridge. Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held September 1, 2010 NEW BUSINESS Capital Project Bylaw - School Bus Mr. Green said the bylaw will authorize the purchase of two 42-passenger school buses at a cost of $228,000. Funding for the purchase comes from capital funding provided by the Ministry of Education. The buses will be cleaner-burning diesel buses.
