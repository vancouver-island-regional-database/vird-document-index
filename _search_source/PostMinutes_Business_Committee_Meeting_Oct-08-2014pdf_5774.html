CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS BC Summer Games Update Chair Brennan asked Mr. Sabo to join Trustees at the table. Mr. Sabo told the Committee about the contributions the district made to the B.C. Summer Games. He said, in total, the district contributed $305,600 of in-kind services, which included building rentals, equipment rental, busing and cartage, building and grounds and custodial. In addition, about $56,000 of services and supplies will be billed to the Summer Games. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Auditors - Extension of Contract Mr. Turin said that the extending the term for the MNP Auditing firm will provide an additional year of continuity at a time when the Public Sector Accounting Standards are still changing. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Saunders IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Allen Folio B14/10/08-04 LIF - Learning Improvement Fund That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith that it extend the MNP contract for one year for the 2014-2015 fiscal year. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Turin told the Committee that the Learning Improvement Fund totals $1.8 million for teachers and $453,757 for support staff this year. The allocation of the funds has been done in consultation with the NDTA and school staff committees, for teachers, and CUPE, for support staff. The fund for teaching positions will be used for instructional coordinators; the self-regulated learning coordinator; and small school staffing, as originally planned in the 2013-14 preliminary budget. In addition, there will be 4 FTE additional elementary school support teachers and 4.93 additional FTE teachers to add blocks to secondary schools, as well as a 0.5 FTE English Language Learners Teacher position. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Saunders IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Allen Folio B14/10/08-05 That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education that the Chair write to the Minister of Education and the Treasury Board to register our concerns that the Education Fund is not in fact new money, and while helpful is far from what the need is. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held October 8, 2014 Strike Savings Letter Mr. Turin outlined the information that has been received from the Ministry of Education about the way the government plans to recover savings in school district budgets during strike action. Auditor General Correspondence on Budget Monitoring Project Mr. Turin said that SD68 is one of four B.C. school districts selected for a project by the B.C. Auditor General. The project will look at how school districts develop their budgets and monitor and control expenditures. Mr. Turin said that he looks forward to working with the Auditor General’s Office on this project. The project will result in a report to the B.C. Legislature, giving an overview of school district’s budgeting processes. Enrolment Update Mr. Turin provided a very preliminary update on student enrolment for this school year. He said that very early indications are that enrolment may be somewhat lower this year than had been projected, but more will be known after October 17, which is the official date for the enrolment count.
