Alex was an awesome teacher, who took the time to go over all the tools, safety rules, and the building procedures. But even after that I was a bit worried that some of my vulnerable kiddos wouldn’t be able to build the planter boxes. I am so happy to share that I was completely wrong, as it was the kids I was worried about who excelled at building them. In fact, they thrived and asked to stay in at recess so they could build additional planter boxes for the kids who were away. As Alex can confirm, I cried happy tears that morning watching my students so engaged and excited about this amazing handson learning opportunity! I am sure that for many of my students this will be a highlight of their grade 3 year. It is such a great introduction to another way of hands-on learning and creating, tools, and carpentry, a reinforcement of their awesome capabilities, and such an amazing boost of their pride and self confidence. They are so excited about painting them, planting their bean seeds in them, and giving them to their loved ones on Mother’s Day. Huge thank you’s to Mr. Beeston, Alex, and the School District for providing the Trades Truck and all it offers to students in our district! I am crossing my fingers and toes in hopes of being able to provide this amazing and engaging learning opportunity for my students in the years to come.
