NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 7, 2024 Education Committee Laura Tait, Acting Superintendent Ministry of Education and Child Care Announcement - Cellphones Recommendation The Education Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) request that pursuant to Policy 2.5 – Board Committees Representation and Annual Work Plan, that it request feedback from the Student Voice Circle on issues associated with cell phone limitations in schools in anticipation of potential Board policy. Background On January 26, 2024, the government of British Columbia announced a number of initiatives with respect to technology including the intention of restricting access to cell phones in schools. This action sheet is intended to provide information on next steps including a recommended motion for the Board’s consideration. The news release can be found here: B.C. launches concrete actions to keep kids safe, healthy | BC Gov News Discussion The government has not provided specifics with respect to the implementation. However, they confirmed that the intention is not a “ban” and that appropriate cell phone use for educational purposes will be maintained. Further, it is anticipated that accommodations will be made where appropriate. At this time, without the legislative instrument directing the scope of the restrictions and what will be expected of Boards (e.g. Policy, Code of Conduct changes etc.), it is premature to speculate and likely too soon to start developing a policy. However, it is important to note that most schools already have limits on cell phone use in their Codes of Conduct and teachers have the ability to set certain learning expectations in their classes. One example in the District can be found at Dover Bay https://dv.schools.sd68.bc.ca/students-andparents/personal-electronics/ Given the current realities, it may be the enforcement mechanism that will need focus and discussion. Further, it is not anticipated that the restriction will apply to employees in the classroom, however the District may also want to consider this for modelling purposes as cell phone addiction is not only a student issue. Finally, with anxiety at elevated levels we know students and parents stay connected throughout the day, and that issue will need to be addressed and not just for students with specific diagnosed mental illness. In any event, the recommended motion is to discuss the issue first with students at the next Student Voice Circle which is anticipated to be March 1. The feedback and ideas will likely time well with the direction from the Ministry.
