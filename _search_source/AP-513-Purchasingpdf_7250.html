Appendix D Administrative Procedure 513 – Purchasing AP 513 – Purchasing Purpose The purpose of this administrative procedure is to ensure that the school district’s requirements for materials, goods, equipment and services are met at optimum value. Except where otherwise noted in the attached procedure or where governed by other policies, all purchases and financial commitments for materials, goods, equipment and services are to be made through a central purchasing department. The purchasing department has been delegated authority, subject to procedure 4500, to commit the school district to binding agreement such as purchase orders, equipment leases and contracts. The purchase of goods and services shall conform to the principles of competitive bidding wherever possible. Students and staff members are not authorized to commit the school district for payment of goods and services and may not sign rental agreements (except car rentals), leases, licenses or agreements with any vendor except when approved to do so. All things being equal, preference will be given to suppliers within the school district’s boundaries. Procedure 1. General The ability to obtain best overall value for every dollar expended is the objective of the following procedures. Best value is attained through effective teamwork and consultation with all those involved in the procurement process. It is a requirement that adequate time be included in the planning for the acquisition of goods and services to accommodate the competitive and consultative processes outlined in these procedures. 2. Method Of Procurement Definitions of varying methods of procurement described hereunder, and the decision to use any particular form will depend on the complexity of the requirement, the monetary value, and the urgency. AP 513 – Purchasing Page 1 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing 2.1 Tender Tenders are offers made by firms which have been invited to submit tenders, in sealed envelopes, for goods or services; these tenders are received before a closing date and time specified in the "Invitation to Tender" document. The formality of this procedure ensures that the tender of a supplier is not disclosed to competitors before the closing date specified in the "Invitation to Tender". 2.2 Request for Quotes Request for quotes can take the following forms: a) Written quotations - from three or more sources in response to a written request. Responses may be submitted by fax; b) Verbal quotations - given by two or more suppliers representatives, usually by telephone. Such quotations are to be recorded by the purchasing office and retained with the purchase order. 3. Determination Of Supplier 3.1 $2,000 or Less Goods and services not exceeding $2,000 may be purchased at the discretion of the Purchasing Department. Knowledge of the trade by the Purchasing Staff should suffice, however, when any doubt exists alternative sources should be explored to determine the best price. 3.2 $5,000 or Less Sources of supply for requirements expected to exceed $2,000 but not $5,000 may be determined by verbal quotation. 3.3 Over $5,000 But Not Exceeding $10,000 Sources of supply for requirements expected to exceed $5,000 but not $10,000 shall be determined by written quotation. 3.4 Over $10,000 But Not exceeding $25,000 Sources of supply for requirements expected to exceed $10,000 but not $25,000 shall be determined by tender. Public invitation is not required. 3.5 Over $25,000 AP 513 – Purchasing Page 2 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing Sources of supply for requirements expected to exceed $25,000 shall be determined by tender. A public invitation to tender shall be advertised in a local paper and, at the discretion of the Director of Finance, in other areas. 3.6 In the event that only one submission is received, the decision to accept the offer shall be made, in conjunction with the end user, by: a) the Director of Finance or the Finance Manager for quotes and non advertised tenders or, b) the Secretary-Treasurer for advertised tenders. 3.7 Goods or services available from a sole source may be exempt from the tendering process and/or the quotation process. The purchasing department, in conjunction with the end user, will determine if there is a sole source situation. Where a sole source purchase is made, the purchase order (PO) will indicate that the purchase is sole source and supporting documentation, if available, will be attached to the PO. 3.8 The Secretary-Treasurer, the Director of Finance, Finance Manager, Manager of Facilities and Planning or a principal may authorize the immediate purchase of any item or service without recourse to the provisions of this procedure where: a) staff, student or public safety is in question; b) purchase will prevent damage to school district facilities; c) essential services will be restored; d) or essential physical plant services will be restored. 3.9 It is not necessary to tender or go through the quotation process for the purchase of used items and legal services. 4. Receipt Of Tenders 4.1 Immediately on receipt of submission, the date and time of receipt shall be recorded on the tender envelope. The initials of the employee receiving the tender shall be affixed in close proximity to the date/time stamp. 4.2 Tenders shall be placed in a lockable compartment and retained therein until the official opening. 4.3 Under no circumstances shall tender envelopes be opened prior to the specified closing time. Where envelopes are opened inadvertently (i.e. the envelope did not indicate that is was a tender), the envelope will be AP 513 – Purchasing Page 3 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing immediately resealed, without looking at pricing information, and the person opening the envelope will indicate the error on the envelope. 5. Tender Opening 5.1 Tender openings shall be witnessed by two or more school district employees, one of which must be a purchasing department employee. All persons present at the opening, (including non-school district employees) shall be recorded on the tender opening form. 5.2 Tenders shall be: a) opened at the same time, after the time indicated as the closing time in the "Invitation to Tender". Where the public has come to witness the opening, it shall occur not later than 15 minutes after the official closing indicated in the “Invitation to Tender”; b) initialled and dated by each two or more school district employees who witnessed the opening, one of which must be a purchasing department employee; and c) recorded on the "Tender Opening Form" which shall be signed as indicated in b above. 6. Late Tenders a) Tenders received after the closing date and time specified in the "Invitation to Tender" are called "late tenders". b) Envelopes containing late tenders are to be date stamped, entered in the "Tenders Received Record" as "unopened late tenders" and returned to the senders unopened. 7. Tender Withdrawals A bidder may withdraw a tender by written notice only, provided such notice is received by the receptionist at the main switchboard at the School District Office prior to the closing time and date set in the tender documents. 8. Tender Amendments A firm may amend its tendered price provided the amendment is received in writing or by FAX prior to the time and date tenders are due. Such amendments are to consist only of an amending figure either upward or downward from the total price included in the sealed tender and are not to disclose the new total revised bid price. 9. Tender Award AP 513 – Purchasing Page 4 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing 9.1 The award of a contract to other than the lowest bidder must be based on facts that are defensible in the event of enquiry from any source at any level. 9.2 Criteria For Selection Selection of the successful offer is based on, in no particular order, but not limited to, the following criteria: a) compliance with tender documents; b) overall cost impact to the School District; c) firm prices vs. approximate prices or escalation; d) cancellation provisions, where applicable; e) the school district’s assessment of the ability of the bidder (and their staff) to supply the goods or services within the timeframe required by the school district; f) warranty and service facilities best value based on quality, service and price; g) compliance with regulatory requirements; h) experience and reputation of the bidder/supplier; and i) other criteria as deemed applicable, by the purchasing department, to the purchase being made. 9.3 Identical Offers When tenders are received which appear to be identical in price, compliance with specifications, discounts, location of supplier etc., further careful review should be made to identify some reasonable criteria on which to base final selection. The importance of selecting a tender for reasons that can be defended is reiterated. The Secretary-Treasurer shall determine the successful bidder in the event of identical offers in respect to a tender. 10. Access To Bids And Prices 10.1 Tenders Tenders invited by public advertisement shall be opened in public and this shall constitute notice to all interested parties. A summary of tenders shall be prepared and kept on file for public information, if required. Spending Authority 12.1 $50,000 or Less The Director of Finance or Finance Manager may award tenders/contracts for goods and services to a value not exceeding $50,000. 12.2 For purchases or awards of tenders/contracts funded from capital or operating budgets which have been approved by the Board, the SecretaryTreasurer may award contracts for goods and services in excess of $50,000 where the low bid is the recommended successful bidder. 12.3 The Board of School Trustees shall award contracts for goods and services in excess of $50,000 which are being recommended for award to other than the low bidder. 12.4 Delegation of Authority AP 513 – Purchasing Page 6 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing The Director of Finance or Finance Manager may delegate spending authority, not to exceed $10,000 per instance to Purchasing Department staff members. The Finance Manager may delegate spending authority not to exceed $2,000 per instance to administrators, directors or department supervisors through alternate procurement systems such as procurement cards or prepaid purchase orders. 13. Real Property And Construction Contracts 13.1 The Director of Facilities shall be responsible for the administration of real property, construction contracts, and the services of engineers, architects and other professional and consulting services related to construction contracts and the purchase and disposal of land and real property. 13.2 The selection procedures for the services of engineers, architects, and other consulting services related to construction contracts shall be the responsibility of the Director of Facilities. 3.3 The above procedures for acquisition of goods and services shall apply to real property and construction contracts except that the Manager of Facilities and Planning has authority to sign construction contracts. 13.4 The Secretary-Treasurer or Director of Finance shall administer the tendering procedures for real property, construction of works and buildings. The Manager of Facilities and Planning shall maintain the original legal copies of such contracts on behalf of the school district. 13.5 The Secretary-Treasurer shall administer the leasing of land, real property and public facilities and shall maintain the original legal copies of all such agreements on behalf of the school district. The Manager of Facilities and Planning shall manage and administer the use of all leased land, real property and public facilities. 14. Personal Purchases Purchases in the name of the school district and the use of district procurement cards and or documents is restricted to the acquisition of goods and services required for school district purposes. 15. Surplus Material 15.1 Where equipment, vehicles or materials are considered surplus to the needs to the school district and are expected to have resale value, the Director of Finance may direct these items be disposed of in any one of the following ways: a) offered for sale to public bodies; b) sold at public auction; AP 513 – Purchasing Page 7 of 8 AP 513 – Purchasing c) sold by public or invited tender; d) sold at a fixed price public sale; e) sold privately (Private sale shall be restricted to items which have previously been offered under a, b, c, or d above or where the potential benefits of such a process are greater than the costs of it.). The cost of disposing of the item must not exceed the expected resale value. Where costs of disposal are considered to be equal to or greater than the resale value, items should be disposed of as indicated in 15.2. Equipment, vehicles or materials will not be sold to school district employees unless they are the successful bidder in a public tender process. 15.2 Where equipment or materials are considered surplus to the school district’s needs and do not have a resale value, the Secretary-Treasurer or designate may authorize items to be: a) salvaged for parts; b) scrapped; or c) traded in for credit against purchases. 16. Banking Arrangements The District will enter into a banking services arrangement by way of an RFP. The selection criteria for banking services will include, but not be limited to, the following:       the level of integrative technology available for the district’s business activities the ancillary financial services offered, such as purchasing cards, lease financing, EFTs, etc. the service fees charged the interest rate offered on deposits the financial instruments and products available the ability of the bank to handle the transactional volume of the district. The banking services agreement will be reviewed by the Audit Committee every three years, with a report going to the Board recommending either entering into a new RFP process or extending the current arrangement for an additional year. A second extension may be approved by the Board upon a second report recommending same. A new RFP process will be entered into after five years regardless.
