The program, which is sponsored by B.C. Hydro, involves students promoting energy sustainability in schools, the community and the world. Students from Dover Bay, Woodlands, NDSS and Ladysmith Secondary, told trustees about the wide variety of programs they have undertaken this year. Students conduct energy audits at their schools and compare results from year to year to see if their awareness initiatives have made a difference by encouraging people to turn off computers and lights and unplug chargers when they are not in use. They are also involved in a number of activities such as gardens, composting, and local and international environmental projects. Their awareness activities include presentations to their fellow secondary students, as well as elementary students.
