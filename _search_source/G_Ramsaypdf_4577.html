PRESENTATION TO NANAIMO SCHOOL BOARD ON PROPOSED CLOSURE OF RUTHERFORD SCHOOL Introduction: My name is Graham Ramsay and I live at 5775 Broadway Rd., a five minute walk from Rutherford School. Our family moved into Rutherford community originally in 1971 when the school was a three room school. We moved away a year later but returned to the community in 1980, like many other families then and now, in no small part because of the location and excellent reputation of Rutherford School. Our two sons attended Rutherford School and my wife taught there as well. Rutherford Community: To begin with, the word “community” has many meanings, one of which is “a group of people who live in the same area such as a city, town or neighbourhood”. In Superintendent Blain’s report on Rutherford Elementary School, prepared for the public consultation process, there is scant recognition of Rutherford community and the role that Rutherford School plays and has played in this community. In fact, on Page 21 of the Report there is an assertion that relocated Rutherford students would remain in their own community by attending other schools. In my view, this is a telling and significant misrepresentation. Communities are defined from within by the people who live in them - not by the people who live outside them. Rutherford community is a distinct community within the north end of Nanaimo. This is not something that many people who do not live in the community appreciate. Rutherford community is no less a community than Harewood, Townsite or Departure Bay. If you ask people where they live out here they don’t say “the North End”, they say “Rutherford”. The Rutherford community is unique because it was actually built around the school. The school was here before most of the houses and that is why it was built in stages. In 1971, when we first lived here, the only development in the area was at the bottom of Parkway Rd. At that time this area was not in the City of Nanaimo and was comprised mainly of forest, agricultural land and isolated residences. The only development underway at the time was on what became Broadway Rd. between Parkway and Sealand Roads. And it was just roughed in. When we returned in 1980, the community had expanded significantly and within several years had become solely residential. For this to happen, a large tract of land had been removed from the Agricultural Land Reserve between Sealand and Brickyard Roads to accommodate the expanding community. The only public facility in the community was Rutherford School. And it remains so today. In 1980, through the efforts of the principal and his staff and the parents of the children who attended the school, Rutherford became one of the first Community Schools in Nanaimo. The people who lived in our community at that time were very proud of this. For some reason, unknown to me, many years later, Rutherford School lost its Community School designation and I am certain that this was done without consultation with the community. But for most of us who live in the Rutherford community, we don’t really care because to us Rutherford School is our community school whether or not it is officially recognized so by School District 68. And to remind us, the words “Rutherford Community School” are still painted in big letters in the school gym. Our children attend our community school and most of the people in our community have, at one time or another, used the school facilities. We use the gym, playing fields, ball courts and playgrounds in the “forest” a lot more than we use what few city parks are available to us here. Go to Rutherford School on a sunny non school day and you will see people of all ages using the facilities. This has always been the case. Rutherford School is the heart of our community and we want our children educated there. Process: I think we all appreciate that the decision to close a school is one of the more difficult things a School Board has to consider. It is a painful and emotional process and has the potential to impact families and children in many ways. And we certainly don’t think it should happen to our school. Having said that, I believe that the quality of outcomes is largely determined by the process used to achieve those outcomes. For that reason I wish to focus on the process that has led us to this evening. To do this I am going to refer specifically to the document “School Closures in British Columbia – Past, Present and Future” by Dick Chambers, which was prepared for the British Columbia School Trustees Association in 2007. This is a long and detailed study of the issue and I am going to talk primarily about the longest section of the report entitled “Engaging the Community”. To begin with, Mr. Chambers states that if there will be more than one school closure and it is a complex situation, a three-phased approach is useful: 2 - In phase 1 you identify the problem and build community understanding of the issues, In phase 2 you identify the options that could address the problem, In phase 3 you build a vision for the preferred future. He states that “the essence lies in having the community come to believe that the proposed future is more desirable than the deteriorating status quo”. In order to do that he says you have to separate the issue, which is the deteriorating status quo, from the action, which is the proposed solution. The key is being able to specifically identify the problem and then create alternative solutions or options to address the problem. For example, to develop this “proposed future” in what he calls “straightforward solution/lack of urgency” situations he suggests: a) That the Board create an ad hoc committee to identify the issues around continuing operation of the school in question and that the committee’s mandate be to examine safety, educational, financial and facility issues. b) That the composition of the committee include (this is not his complete list): - a Parent Advisory Committee Rep - a Staff Rep - School Administrator and District Admin Rep - a community rep - Secretary Treasurer or delegate - Superintendent or delegate - Trustee Rep His emphasis is on communication – between the School Board, the School District and the communities of the schools that are impacted. If the School Board wants to close a school it needs to present a compelling case (Mr. Chamber’s words, not mine). As an aside, I want to note here that I have chosen one example of four different situations suggested by Mr. Chambers in his report. It is likely that the situation he calls “complex solution/lack of urgency” is applicable to the whole school district but because the proposed closure of Rutherford School has little impact on the proposed closure of other schools in the district I have provided the above example. Regardless, the principles remain the same – accurate problem identification, engaging the community in the process and creating a solution that is more desirable than the status quo. 3 With relation to Rutherford School I believe that an appropriate process was not followed. Instead, we have a situation where the community thought that Rutherford School would be rebuilt as part of the School District’s Ten Year Plan. We only learned earlier this year that the School District was presenting a different plan - to close our school. There was virtually no community engagement in the process, no ad hoc committees, no clear problem definition and no alternatives presented. The School District identified the problem and created a solution without presenting alternatives or seeking meaningful community input. The few public meetings that have been held have been heavily managed by the School District and the many questions asked by the community about the proposed closure of Rutherford School were answered selectively on a web page. The only official information made available to the community was contained in the Superintendent’s report on Rutherford School – and much of that information, in my opinion, is misleading and incomplete. This can hardly be called “engaging the community.” And I ask you how you can, as a Board of School Trustees, have faith in a process like that? Thank you.
