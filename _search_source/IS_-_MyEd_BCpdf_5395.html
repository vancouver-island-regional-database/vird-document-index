Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION INFORMATION SHEET DATE: November 12, 2015 TO: Business Committee Meeting FROM: Steve Sproston, Manager – IS Department SUBJECT: MyEducation BC Background: In August 2015, NLPS went live with MyEducation BC (MyEd), the replacement of BCeSIS. We were in the 6th group of districts to convert and by far the largest group with approximately half the districts in the province involved. September saw severe performance issues with the new system. These problems persisted through to early October when application modifications and an upgrade to the database server were performed. We also experienced issues around data conversion – the transfer of data from BCeSIS to MyEd that was performed by Fujitsu. Due to performance and data conversion issues, the applications group had to work approximately 400 overtime hours. One temporary position that was created to help support our schools during the transition had to be extended to March 2016. Two temporary workers were needed for two weeks to clean up the conversion of teachers and AO’s data from BCeSIS to MyEd. The additional, measureable costs for staffing were approximately $40,000. More costs would have occurred at the school level with people banking time instead of working overtime for pay. MyEd is currently performing at reasonable speeds in most areas. We are still dealing with implementation issues, but are working them out. At this time, the district’s business cycle needs are being met by the system.
