CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Cheryl Onciul, Carrie Segreto Parents, Ecole North Oyster RE: French Immersion at Ladysmith Secondary School Ecole North Oyster parents, Ms. Cheryl Onciul and Ms. Carrie Segreto presented to the Education Committee their passion and commitment in advocating for French Immersion at Ladysmith Secondary School for September 2016. Their presentation included possible challenges and scenarios for the program at LSS, however, as parents of French Immersion students, they indicated how a French Immersion education can positively provide opportunities in future employment as well as offer enhanced programming at no additional cost to families. Staff are currently looking into interest levels of parents and students at ENO. They have also done some work on preliminary course planning. Staff will provide a report of numbers and staffing in February. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brzovic IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan Folio E16/01/06-04 That the item of French Immersion at Ladysmith Secondary School be moved to New Business. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Mr. Derek Beeston RE: CTC: Trades Trailer/Dual Credit Presentation Derek Beeston, Career Technical Centre VicePrincipal provided an overview of the district's Career Technical Centre program. CTC Programs: Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held January 6, 2016 Dual Credit Trades and Applied Skills - for grade 12 students in one of 15 areas and credits toward their diploma; backing electrical, carpentry, etc. Dual Credit - Enrichment program - grade 12 students can take p to four university courses to get a head-start in their chosen field as well as earning credits toward their diploma. Introduction to Trades (NDSS) - grade 10 - 12 students get a full semester of Carpentry, Plumbing and Electrical as well as their academics. Leadership and Mentorship is a big part of this district program. They also receive a Certification as a Fork Life Operator as well as other certifications. Other Programs include:  Elementary Skills and Trades Program  Secondary School Apprenticeship and Work Experience  The Tool Box - trades trailer that travels to our Elementary Schools to introduce them to the Applied Skills and Trades options. It is predicted that 36% of jobs will require a University Degree and 76% of jobs will require Post-Secondary training or apprenticeship. He provided highlights from the past school year, which included the launch of a new interactive website, Mr. Beeston included the goals for the program over the next two school years and a program summary.
