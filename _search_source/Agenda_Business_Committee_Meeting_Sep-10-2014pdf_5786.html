CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Little Ferns Day Care Little Ferns Day Care staff made a presentation to the Business Committee proposing that their young parent program that currently provides child care in a facility next to John Barsby be expanded to NDSS. They asked that the District submit a joint proposal with Little Ferns for a capital grant for a facility. Jolly Giant Child Care Jolly Giant Child Care staff made a presentation to the Business Committee proposing that the District work collaboratively with Jolly Giant in creating new daycare spaces on school district property. Jolly Giant currently has child care operations in three school district buildings and is hoping to create 49 new spaces, using the grant to create two facilities in District schools and then paying rent to the district. Page 4 of 11 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held June 11, 2014 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Howland IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Neary Folio B14/06/11-05 That the Business Committee recommend to the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that the proposals received from the presentations by both the Little Ferns and Jolly Giant Child Care Ltd., and any other proposals that come forward pertaining to applications for the recently announced Ministry of Children and Family Development Child Care major capital funding for the creation of new licensed child care spaces, be referred to staff for assessment and evaluation and that a report be brought back to the September Business Committee Meeting of those applications that staff recommend to proceed. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY COMMITTEE REPORTS CORRESPONDENCE REFERRED FROM REGULAR BOARD MEETING UNFINISHED BUSINESS New Cedar Elementary School Update Mr. Sabo gave an update on plans for the conversion of the Cedar Secondary building, focusing on the playfield and playgrounds. Mr. Sabo said that $2 million of Annual Facilities Grant funds over the next two years will be used to cover the costs of the conversion. Mr. Sabo also reviewed a number of items related to the Cedar School renovation that had been raised previously by Cedar parents. NEW BUSINESS North Oyster - Local Capital Mr. Turin reviewed his Action Sheet regarding North Oyster School and the local capital with the Committee.
