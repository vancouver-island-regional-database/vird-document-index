CHAIRPERSON’S REPORT SD68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) NOVEMBER 24, 2010 As we move into the last few weeks before the Winter Holiday I want to give a special thank-you to the teachers who have been working hard on report cards and parent interviews. Any time in education is a busy time but this is especially the case when report cards are due. Thank you to all Principals and Vice-Principals, Support Staff and Senior Management who work so diligently to keep us moving forward and helping to set our students up for success. Thank you to all the support staff who have been working to keep the sidewalks safe; the buildings warm; the puddles mopped up and the children bussed to and from school safely. A great deal of organization and hard work is necessary in times of extreme weather conditions and staffs do their best to keep ahead of the ice and snow. I would like to take this opportunity to welcome our new Director of Human Resources, Robert Wesley to our school district. I would also like to thank our Senior Staff for organizing an excellent inclusive interviewing process that saw representatives from our stakeholder groups take part. Thanks also to Jennifer McNeill for all her logistical support. Congratulations and welcome to Taunia Sutton in her new role as Manager of Payroll and Benefits. On November 11, 2010, trustees were in attendance at the Remembrance Day ceremonies in Nanaimo, Ladysmith, Lantzville and Gabriola where they placed wreaths at the cenotaphs. During our moments of silent remembrance one couldn’t help but think about our responsibility to uphold democratic principles so that our young students will have a future that is safe and free from war. At a recent meeting of Nanaimo Rotary Daybreak that I attended, a representative from “Loaves and Fishes” made a presentation for an upcoming food drive. It was very disturbing to hear of the increasing need for food that this organization is experiencing. I wish to pass on to you that the speaker extended a very special thank you to School District 68 for allowing the free use of Mt. Benson so that “Loaves and Fishes” could organize their food drive. They were most appreciative for our support of the community. Finally, I would like to take this opportunity to let you know that this will be my last Board meeting as your Chair. I have served you in this position for the past two years and I would like to thank trustees for your support. I would also like to extend a huge vote of thanks to Senior Staff, Jennifer McNeil, Cathy Kelt and Donna Reimer for their day- to-day support. I look forward to working with and supporting our next Board Chair.
