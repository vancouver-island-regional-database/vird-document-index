THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: June 20, 2012 TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Custodial Review – Recommendation 7.14- Purchase of Auto Scrubbers Background The following motion was passed by the Board of Education on March 28, 2012 - Folio R12/03/28-11 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) refers the Cascade Facilities Management Consultants Report on SD68 Custodial Review be referred to staff to prepare a report on the status of the consultant’s recommendations, and provide that report to the Board by September 2012. Discussion In the custodial review presented to the board, recommendation 7.14 stated: It is recommended that all schools be supplied with auto scrubbers and appropriate training provided for this equipment. In the 12/13 budget deliberations auto scrubber were considered but not included in the same services budget and not approved. Staff has determined that 24 auto scrubbers at a total cost of $144,000 are required to meet the intent of the recommendation. Staff recommends purchasing the auto scrubbers over two budget years and suggests a budget of $72,000 for the 12/13 budget year. Automatic floor scrubbers, also known as auto scrubbers, are a type of floor cleaning machine that are used to scrub a floor clean of light debris, dust, oil, grease or floor marks. These machines have either rotary or cylindrical scrubbing head and an automated system for dispensing cleaning solution and then vacuuming it up. So, in one pass over the floor, a user can dispense cleaning or stripping solution, scrub it into the floor, then vacuum it all up with an auto scrubber squeegee attachment at the back of the machine. Auto scrubbers have a separate dispensing tank and a collection tank to keep your clean water from your dirty water and can be categorized into one of two main types: walk behind or riding. Floor Scrubbers are a more hygienic alternative to traditional cleaning methods such as a mop and bucket. Environmentally safe soaps can be used in conjunction with a reduced water system to save on both the amount of chemicals released into the environment as well as the amount of gray water produced. Some floor scrubbers are even capable of cleaning without a water and chemical system at all.
