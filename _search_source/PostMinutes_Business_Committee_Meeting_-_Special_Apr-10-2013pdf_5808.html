CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS Budget 2013-2014 Mr. Turin reviewed the proposed budget to the Business Committee and asked staff to speak to the various proposals on the senior management recommendations. These recommendations dealt with how the district can deal with a $4.7 million shortfall in the 2013-14 school year. Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 10, 2013 Mr. Turin said that it is proposed that $2 million of the $4.7 million comes from $500,000 of unrestricted surplus from the 2011-12 budget; $500,000 of staffing contingencies from the 2012-13 budget; and $1 million from a “clawback” of 2012-13 supplies accounts. The budget strategies that have been proposed to balance next year’s budget are: • • • • • • • • • • • • • Relocating the Davis Road and Quarterway Elementary English stream students to neighbouring elementary schools, leaving the two schools with single-track French Immersion programs Start a transition towards the elimination of the junior alternative program Closing the District Resource Centre at the old Dufferin Crescent school building Reconfiguration of a number of positions in the Learning Services Department, including the elimination of several positions Not hiring to fill a vacant accounting position Closing the district’s print shop operations Reduction of two caretaker positions in the Facilities Department Reductions in a number of Facilities Department Supplies accounts Reduction of release time expenditures Reduction of the planned spending on the district’s technology plan Reallocation of a number of positions to be funded by special purpose grants rather than the operating budget An increase in facilities rental rates by about 5 percent Changing the way that Protection Island students receive ferry transportation Mr. Turin said that at this time, the draft budget does not include savings from an extended spring break next year because the district is currently discussing the proposal with the NDTA, as per the collective agreement.
