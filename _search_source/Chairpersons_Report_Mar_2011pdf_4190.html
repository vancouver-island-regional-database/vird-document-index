SD 68 Chairperson’s Report Sharon Welch March, 2011 One of my goals for this year is to visit all of the schools in SD 68. I’m interested in hearing success stories, meeting staff, students, and parents, and learning more about the work of each of our schools. This month, I had the opportunity to visit all of the Dover family of schools. I started at Frank J. Ney, where Principal Gary Dodd gave me a great tour and introduced me to every class. He explained the role of the Trustees to the students and invited them to ask questions. A few days later, I returned to Frank J. Ney to hear a special PAC presentation on bullying. Next, I visited Dover Bay Secondary, where Principal John Blain highlighted the Student Support Services wrap-around model that promotes diversity. I also learned more about the SWIS program for immigrant students new to Nanaimo. At Rutherford, Principal Terry Mazzei gave me a tour and highlighted the successes of the StrongStart and District Life Skills programs. I also had the opportunity to talk briefly with the PAC chair, Ms. McLennan. At McGirr, I visited with Principal Jill Wilkie and Vice-Principal Tanya Peace. They spoke about the diversity within the school and the teachings around tolerance. McGirr also has a vibrant StrongStart program. At Pleasant Valley, Principal Kathy Bergman gave me a tour and introduced me to each class. I had the opportunity to visit with the two full-day kindergarten teachers and heard some wonderful stories about the children and their progress. I was also able to talk to some of the teachers in the staff room. It was purely coincidental that I visited this school on cupcake day, but what a great bonus! At Seaview, Principal Patrick Young talked about the Seaview citations of respecting yourself, others, and the environment. He also talked about school spirit initiatives including the four house teams – Tsunami, Avalanche, Solar Storm, and Firestorm – and the strong PAC involvement at the school. My last visit was with Principal Kelly McKee at Randerson Ridge. He talked about the school’s emphasis on social responsibility and fundraising for the community. He also showed appreciation for the PAC – a small, but very committed group of parents. I thoroughly enjoyed my visits, and I’m looking forward to going to Cedar and Ladysmith schools next. Another highlight this month was the Board-NDTA social hosted at the NDTA office. Thank you for a great time together! Finally, I would like to end with a note of thankfulness for the safe return of our Woodlands and Wellington students and our Communications Director, Ms. Reimer from Japan. Let’s continue to take time to remember all of the people who have lost friends, relatives, and their possessions in this terrible tragedy.
