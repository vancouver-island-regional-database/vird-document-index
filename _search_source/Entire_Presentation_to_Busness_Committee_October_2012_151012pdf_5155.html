Capital Asset Management Service CAMS Pete Sabo Director of Planning and Operations Brent Spohn Facilities Planning Supervisor  In 2008 the Ministry of Education issued a RFP for the supply of a Capital Asset Management Service  Guided by a steering committee, the envisioned service included:  Detailed Facility Condition Assessments (FCA) of all in scope school district facilities province-wide and  The provision of CAM Data System Services Background  VFA Canada Corporation successfully responded to the RFP  In support of this initiative, school districts and the Ministry provided VFA with base data to initially populate the database  A schedule was developed for province wide FCA’s of more than 2600 facilities in 60 school districts Background  School District 68 facilities were assessed in June and August of 2011  The VFA assessment team consisted of three professionals:  An architect  A mechanical engineer  An electrical engineer  Each in scope facility and site was assessed Facility Condition Assessment (FCA)  Once the assessment data was compiled and entered into the system VFA made the data accessible to SD 68 Facilities Staff  Sample reports and summary data are available separately Facility Condition Assessment (FCA)  CAMS is a tool used by the Ministry to:  Drive & support capital funding requests  For longer term capital planning  School districts can use the system to manage district assets and support Annual Facility Grant and long term capital planning How can CAMS be used?  The VFA data identifies “Immediate Life Safety” Requirements at several SD 68 facilities, typically:  Egress and means of escape issues  Missing exit signage  Fire separation and fire-stopping issues  A 2012-13 AFG project ($75,000) was created to address these requirements  An architect and code consultant have been hired to review these concerns  Planning is underway to take remedial action wherever it is confirmed necessary How is SD 68 using CAMS?  Of particular interest is the Facility Condition Index:  FCI is an industry standard metric that objectively measures the current condition of a facility both within and among facilities  FCI = The value of remedying deferred maintenance requirements / Current replacement value  Therefore, the higher the FCI the poorer the condition of the facility Facility Condition Index (FCI) School District 68 FCI Chart  Things to consider:  Each school district contributes financially in support of this initiative  The SD 68 annual contribution is in the order of $77,000 out of our AFG budget  Each school district is expected to provide upgrade information to VFA on an annual basis to help keep the data current Conclusion  The data requires careful interpretation  Some errors or omissions are evident  There are some inconsistencies that require investigation and correction  The information is dynamic and changes as various factors change:  As facilities or systems are upgraded and the data is updated  As regional cost factors or industry service life data factors change  CAMS will improve with age – the data will become more accurate and more valuable over time Conclusion Interior Paint Program Brian Hackwood SD68 Maintenance Supervisor  The Maintenance Department is comprised of one Department Supervisor (Brian Hackwood), 3 General Foreman (Carpentry/Paint, Mechanical/Grounds, Electrical), and a Maintenance Office Assistant.  The General Foreman’s paint responsibilities include:  assigning all work  reviewing school based work orders  corresponding with schools concerning work orders.  In consultation with schools and the Painters address paint schemes.  Questions from Principals as to interior or exterior paint schedules or anything outside of regular maintenance are forwarded to the Department Supervisor.  Leadhand status is assigned to Painters responsible for large projects such as the exterior and interior paint. Leadhand responsibilities include:  Implementing the General Foreman’s instructions  Organizing the work and the other crew members.  Reporting back to the General Foreman. Background Staffing available:  1.0 Sign Painter/Painter  3.45 Maintenance Painters  This staff Maintains:  31 elementary schools  7 secondary schools  2 alternate schools  Various other building i.e. DAC, Transportation, Facilities, Rotary Bowl Background Painting Tasks:  Year Round  Sign Painting – includes parking lot and school information signage and school mascot paintings that are in most school gyms. The mascot paintings are immediately recognized by students, staff and parents.  Graffiti – the District maintains a 5 day turnaround on non-offensive graffiti, same day on offensive. If the school requests immediate removal, same day. Elementary schools are process before secondary schools. Approximately 373 graffiti requests were processed by the Paint Department in 2011/12.  Work Orders – Approximately 221 works orders were processed in 2011/12. These include vandalism, health and safety, touch-ups, moved items and heavy use.  School Funded projects – Schools often request projects outside of regular maintenance and funded elsewhere (PAC for instance). Approximately 88 requests were received. Painting Program Painting Tasks (cont.)  Summer projects  Curb paint – safety lines to designate parking areas, bus zones, handicap parking, no parking, cross walks – this takes 2 painters most of the summer to complete.  Play lines – exterior game touched up on a priority basis.  Gym Lines – 1-2 gym floors are completely refinished each year with a complete new set of lines. The old ones are completely removed during the sanding process – each gym requires 2 painters for 2 weeks – 25 year old gyms floors now look brand new. Painting Program Painting Tasks (cont.)  November-March  Generally 3 painters (leaving 2 to deal with graffiti, work orders and school funded projects) work on interior paint.  The work requires that each classroom be moved for approximately 1 week. This work almost single handedly revitalizes an old school.  All work is completed on afternoon shift Interior Painting Interior jobs completed since the 2004-2005:  Seaview – Completed 2006  Forest Park – Completed 2007  Georgia Ave. – Completed 2008  Fairview – Completed 2009  Rock City – Started 2010 - Completed 2012  Quarterway – Planned start date winter 2012 Interior Painting Timelines to complete all interior painting  31 elementary schools - 30 years  7 secondary schools - 20 years (each requiring 3-5 years to complete)  2 alternate schools - 2 years  This represents an approximate cycle of 52 years Interior Painting Conclusions • Existing staff work hard to keep up with demands. • Priority work involves graffiti, health and safety work orders, school high priority requests and safety lines. • Game lines and gym lines considered next priorities • All work is completed to the highest standard to ensure longevity. • Schools that have interior paint completed find that there is a boost in staff morale, the overall impression of the interior improves, both staff and students exhibit “pride” in their surroundings, interior vandalism typically drops, staff perception is that school is more “healthy” and parents feel that their child is attending a revitalized building. • The current staffing allows for a 52 year turnaround in interior paint.
