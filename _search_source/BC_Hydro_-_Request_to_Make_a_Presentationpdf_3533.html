October 7, 2011 Phil Turin Secretary Treasurer, School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, B.C. V9R 3K6 Dear Phil, Re: BC Hydro Smart Meter Presentation to the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District Board of Education BC Hydro would like to make a presentation to the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District Board of Education in response to their September 28, 2011 Board decision to put the installation of smart meters on hold throughout the school district. I understand that the Board received presentations from members of the public voicing their concerns about the potential health impacts of smart meters. BC Hydro would like to respond to these concerns and present information about our Smart Meter Program either at your next Board meeting on October 26, 2011 or at a special meeting prior to that date. We would like to hear, understand, address and resolve any issues the Board might have so that the installation of smart meters in the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District can resume as soon as possible. BC Hydro Smart Meter Program Directors Gary Murphy and / or Fiona Taylor will lead the presentation. I understand that Board presentations are limited to ten minutes with questions to follow. However, due to the interest this topic has created, I respectfully suggest that our presenters would be willing to extend their presentation to ensure that all questions and issues are adequately addressed. Our hope is that the Board will reconsider its position on smart meters during this meeting. I look forward to your response and thank you for your consideration. Yours truly, Wayne Cousins Senior Key Account Manager Education Sector (K-12 Schools) BC Hydro
