IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Allen IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Welch Folio R12/10/24-17 French Immersion Registration That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves “Meeting Each Student’s Unique Needs,” “The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment,” and “Enhanced Facilities for Learning” as the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools’ District goals. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Hutchinson said that he would be reporting on the management decision around French Immersion registration. He said the district will change the registration process for French Immersion for the next school year in order to address issues that arose in the last school year. Mr. Hutchinson outlined the registration process for Kindergarten students for the 2013-14 school year. He said that details about the process and registration dates will be announced soon. Success for All Mr. Blain, Deputy Superintendent, advised the Board about a number of initiatives connected to the improvement of student learning. The proposal he outlined is that the district would hold tentatively hold a full day workshop for all employees February 1 and a half day workshop May 14th. The series of workshops will be called “Success for All.” Mr. Blain said that district has already consulted with the NDTA, CUPE and DPAC on the proposal, and that the additional day and a half when schools are closed to students meets the requirements of the School Act for the required amount of instructional time in the school year. Attendance will be mandatory. NEW BUSINESS CommunityLink Program - Mr. Bob Esliger, Assistant Superintendent Mr. Esliger, Assistant Superintendent, provided the Board with a draft document that outlines the district’s logic model for its use of the $2.1 million CommunityLINK fund as well as other financial supports for vulnerable students. LIF Spending Plan Mr. Hutchinson outlined how the district is using the $1.5 million Learning Improvement Fund this year. Following the Ministry of Education’s guidelines, the district has allocated the fund for increased time for education assistants and for the hiring of nine instructional coordinators as part of Learning Services Support Teams, with a reserve fund allocation of $400,000.
