Background Information NLPS has an overall budget of $132.5 million of which $118.2 million is the operating budget. NLPS operations are dependent on funding from the Ministry of Education (MoE). The majority of MoE funding is determined by student enrolment, specifically per student funding based on full-time equivalent (FTE). This per student funding has not kept pace with costs that have been downloaded to districts, or with inflation thus requiring districts to cut services to balance budgets. An additional contributing factor for budget reductions is declining enrolment. In 1999-2000 NLPS had 17,087 students. Since then, enrolment has dropped by more than 4,000 students to a low of 12,906 students in 2014-15. The decline in enrolment creates a strain on resources, due to the cost of maintaining excess space. In addition, there are other fixed costs which are not reduced when enrolment declines. These two factors – variable funding and declining enrolment – have created a situation where cuts and school closures are necessary to balance the budget. Failing to do so creates a structural deficit, where funding does not cover the cost to deliver existing services. The budget plan which was developed last year, “Becoming Sustainable,” was initially a two-year plan to end the continuous budget cycle of making cuts each year. By becoming sustainable, NLPS can look forward to deciding how best to invest in student learning instead of determining which services to reduce. Once achieved, NLPS can begin to plan the next steps which will be focusing on improving student learning and further enhancing facilities as outlined in the facilities plan. Reserves/Contingencies During the current year, NLPS moved out of Funding Protection, a supplemental grant from the MoE to support districts by guaranteeing that they would receive 98.5% of the previous year’s MoE revenue. While operating under this grant, NLPS needed contingencies in the base budget, as additional student enrolment would not generate additional funding. These contingencies created pressure on the operations when cuts were required to fund these reserves to balance the budget. These reserves were never fully spent, causing budgeted expenditures to be less than actual. Beginning with the 2015-16 budget, reserves ($750,000 for staffing) and contingencies ($750,000 for sick leave reductions) were excluded from the base budget and instead, earmarked against the accumulated surplus. This reduced the need for reductions to the operating budget in 2015-16. NLPS Proposed Preliminary Budget 2016-17 [Page 8 of 40] Moving forward, NLPS plans to continue this practice. An appropriate reserve as recommended by Ernst and Young in their review of Vancouver School Board would be 2% to 3% of operating expenses. For NLPS this would be from $2.4 million to $3.6 million. Consolidated Operations When the Board adopts the Budget Bylaw, it does so on a consolidated base. This means operating, special purpose, and capital budgets are combined. When balancing the budget, we balance the operating fund. The special purpose funds are targeted for specific items, and by default, balance themselves. The capital funds are where assets are purchase and amortized, and typically result in a deficit, which is allowed. When presenting financial information, we typically use a modified consolidated base, as these are expenditures which the Board controls and are truly integrated into the running of NLPS. This includes the operating fund and the following special purpose funds: Learning Improvement Fund, French (OLEP), Ready Set Learn, Community Link, StrongStart and Wellness and Attendance Management. Excluded from the modified consolidation base are: School Generated Funds, as the decisions for these are at the school level; the Capital Fund including the special purpose portion of the Annual Facilities Grant; and other miscellaneous special purpose grants which vary from time to time. NLPS Proposed Preliminary Budget 2016-17 [Page 38 of 40] Appendix E ACTIVE TRANSPORTATION PLAN Healthy Lifestyles and Safe Transportation The goal of the initiative is to enhance the quality of life for students, teachers, parents and administrators in every school, through active transportation. PacificSport will work collaboratively with the district, to train teachers and possibly parents/volunteers, and also to develop school and community awareness of the Active Participation plan through a variety of media. Goal To introduce active transportation as a means to enhance quality of life for students, teachers, parents and administrators in every school in the district. Desired outcomes include:     Improve physical/mental health and setting the stage for an active lifestyle Daily prepare students by activating the brain for enhanced learning Create safer streets through traffic calming strategies and heighten driving public awareness Develop significant financial savings for both individual families and communities as a whole 1. Training 1.1 Teachers: a. Introduction to Active Transportation is an orientation session designed to introduce safe walking/cycling practices as a part of the curriculum. Teachers are provided with a manual that covers all aspects of a safe Active Transportation program. (in service training for each school, approx. 2.5 -3.5 hrs.) b. Practicum session trains teachers on how to lead rides, assess rider abilities and make corrections where necessary. (In service training for each school, approx. 2.5 – 3 hrs.) This project may call upon parents and volunteers to support teachers or take the lead in the delivery of the Active Transportation training of students, prior to or after school. The above training can be provided to parents/ volunteers as well. 1.2 Families: Family workshops provide an orientation to parents/guardians on how to acclimate children to walking and/or cycling to and from school. This two- hour workshop provides suggestions for getting started, and guides families on how to ensure that their children learn the rules of the road, optimal routes, and enhance their personal confidence that comes from the decision making opportunities that arise. NLPS Proposed Preliminary Budget 2016-17 [Page 37 of 38] 2. Manual The Active Transportation manual is broken down into six modules of lesson plans, curricula, tips and techniques for preparing children from Kindergarten through to Grade 9 on how to be safe and responsible while walking and/or cycling throughout the community. The manual provides a standardized methodology for delivery of the program to ensure consistency of the program throughout the district, and will be available to all teachers, administrators and program leaders. 3.
