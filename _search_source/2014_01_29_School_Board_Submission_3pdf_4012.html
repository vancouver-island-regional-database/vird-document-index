Nanaimo-Ladysmith School Board 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, British Columbia V9R 3K6 January 29th, 2014 Re: Ecole Davis Road Dear Chairperson Neary and Trustees, On behalf of CPF BC & Yukon, I would like to express my support for the work that Cheryl Oncuil and the Ecole Davis Road PAC have done with regards to the proposed move of the French Immersion Program from their local school. Since November, 2013 I have received communication from the above individuals expressing concern regarding the proposed move and its potential impact on the French Immersion program in the Ladysmith community. It was brought to my attention through our conversations that the school district has some concerns about attrition in FI programming. I want to provide a provincial perspective on what we observe here at the CPF Branch level. French Immersion enrollment in BC has continued to increase over the past 7 years, even while enrollment generally has been declining across the province. Although attrition in FI programs is often a cause of concern, there are a number of strategies put in place by districts with lower attrition rates than other districts. Districts that do not place enrollment caps, but rather create split classes in order to accommodate additional students increase the chance of having a full class by the time the students reach highschool. Because of the caps on Kindergarten class sizes, when districts cap and do not allow a Grade 1 entry point, those classes are already starting at a disadvantage in terms of having a full class at the highschool level because they are starting out behind the capacity of an English stream class to capture new students. In some communities, the creation of single-track schools has decreased attrition because parents and teachers are less likely to switch a child into the English program if they start to struggle, and are more likely to support them through the programming instead of changing schools. Multiple studies have shown that in most cases, children with learning disabilities and other challenges are just as successful in French Immersion as they would have been in the English program, and it can actually put them at a disadvantage to have them switch into English midway through. For this reason, many districts have adopted a proactive approach to decreasing attrition through learning support programs for FI students, and educating teachers and administrators about the current research in this area. Through consultation with our volunteers at various chapter levels across BC and Yukon, we have observed that attrition tends to occur at key points and is more likely to happen during transitional phases when students switch schools (ie between elementary and junior, and junior and senior highschool). This happens commonly between the primary to intermediate years as well, but this is believed to be due to parents/teachers concerns about the introduction of letter grades and concern over the students’ school performance. It is the position of Canadian Parents for French BC & Yukon Branch that all children should have the right to Second Language Education in French and that elimination of barriers to participation is critical for families who wish to participate in this valuable program. I hope that your district weighs heavily the possible impacts this move could have on your program, and consider how important this program is to the residents of Ladysmith. CPF BC & Yukon appreciate that all decisions at the school board level are complex and we appreciate the relationship we have with your school district, in your participation with Concours d’Art Oratoire and in working with our chapter when it was active. We do hope to get the chapter re-established so we can continue to support your French Learners through a variety of grants, activities and cultural events as we do in the rest of BC & Yukon. Thank you for taking the time to consult with the community of Ladysmith before coming to your decision. Sincerely, Kirsty Peterson Chapter Support & Outreach Officer Canadian Parents for French BC & Yukon Branch
