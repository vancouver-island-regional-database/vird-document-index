Sylvana Dagostini JUN 10TH, 10:49AM Dear School District 68, I am writing this message in hopes to bring to your attention the drastic shortage and sad consequences thereof of French Immersion spaces in your schools. We are a family who will be potentially relocating to Ladysmith over the summer and have two boys that have been in the French Immersion program for 4 and 6 years respectively, doing extremely well. Upon trying to register them into Fl in District 68 I have discovered no spaces available in any schools whatsoever. Last year there were spaces at N.Oyster and although it varies annually, it seems that there large waitlists in all schools this year with very slim chances of any changes. I have registered my children on all Fl school waitlists and have been told by each school that most of their waitlist students are from out of town families relocating to the area. I am feeling quite sick at the thought of my children having to give up all their commitment, hard work and also as a result, their bilingual dip|oma's because of the lack of french immersion spaces. I realize this is likely a funding issue and have been encouraged by others to appeal to you on behalf of all the families who are being forced to give up their child's education as a result. There is a wonderful empty school sitting in Ladysmith Ecole Davis Rd. that if funded and opened once more, could provide critically needed space for these family's children in the french immersion program for both Nanaimo and Ladysmith it would seem. Instead of Ladysmith students having to take up Nanaimo spaces they would have access to both N. Oyster & Ecole Davis Rd., leaving the spaces in Nanaimo for families living there. I appeal on behalf of all french immersion education children for more space to be created in the program so that we do not have to give up many years we have invested in french immersion education and terminate our children's graduation/diploma choices. I do not know if the Nanaimo/Ladysmith city organizations are aware of the fact that many mainland families are relocating to the area as it is in close proximity/commutable to Vancouver. I have witnessed the gradual exodus of many families being forced out of the mainland in the last few years by the prohibitive real estate prices. Both Nanaimo and Ladysmith are poised to reap the benefits of this if only they can recognize the need for necessary services to support these families. Sadly, had I known the drastic shortage of Fl spaces we would quite likely considered a move elsewhere for the sake of our children's education. Thank you very much for your time and I hope that somehow a solution can be found. — _ .2; I...
