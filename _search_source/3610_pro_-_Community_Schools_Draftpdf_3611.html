SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3610P COMMUNITY SCHOOLS Definitions - for the purposes of this policy the following definitions apply. 1. Community Education An educational endeavor which involves an entire community in the process of addressing lifelong educational issues for the betterment of individuals, families, and the community as a whole. The definition extends the role of the school from one of the more traditional roles of instructing students to one of identifying the needs, problems, and resources of the community and acting as a catalyst in the development of facilities, programs, and leadership in support of community improvement. 2. Community School A community school is designated as such by the Board of Education and is established to facilitate the advancement of community education by providing a variety of programs and services, either directly or in cooperation with community members, organizations, and agencies and municipalities. A community school’s role is extended to identifying the needs, problems, and resources of the community and acting in partnership with other community agencies and organizations in the development of facilities, educational, social, cultural, recreational programs and leadership. 3. Community School Coordinator An employee who is assigned to a community school or to a family of community schools andwho is responsible to work with the school and community to coordinate the programs and services provided by the community school. 4. Family of Community Schools A group of individual community schools that work in a cooperative manner to recognize and support the unique educational, cultural, social, and recreational needs of a particular area of the School District. 5. Community School Review Committee A district committee established to oversee and review the activities and financial affairs of a community school or a family of community schools. Community School Advisory Council A community based committee comprised of school staff, parent, community, and municipal representatives that serves in an advisory capacity to a Community School or a Family of Community Schools. PROCEDURE Formation and Structure 1. Community schools facilitate community education by providing a variety of programs and services, either directly or in cooperation with community members, organizations, and agencies and municipalities. These programs and services could include:     enhancement of the K-12 curriculum through the use of community resources; child and youth development programs; family services such as day care and parent education classes; continuing education services consistent with programming authorized by the Ministry of Education, Alternative Education programs as jointly requested by a representative group of parents, community organizations, principal, staff and students; and general interest programming and leisure services. 2. The establishment of a designated community school shall be a Board response to a formal request jointly made by a representative group of parents, community organizations, principal, staff, and students. 3. Board approval of a community school designation depends on some or all of these factors:      a community in which the residents have expressed a desire to have greater contact with, and become more involved in, total school-community life through a needs assessment process; a joint proposal, of all parties, school staff, parents, community members, and students; a statement of purpose, goals, and objectives of the proposed community school; evidence of existing programs and services in which community personnel are actively engaged; commitment from other agencies to support community education programs; -2 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3610P   draft terms of reference for the Community School Advisory Council; financial viability and availability of School District 68 resources. 4. A community school operation shall adhere to all Board policies and procedures including security, rental regulations, and supervision. 5. A community school shall be granted its status for a period of up to five years. 6. Redesignation of a community school shall, in part, depend on   a local review of interests and needs conducted in the final year of its granted status; a positive recommendation from the Community School Review Committee. Working Relationships and Operation 1. A designated community school or family of community schools will foster participation in a Community School Advisory Council. The Community School Advisory Council will be developed and conduct itself in accordance with best practices as outlined by the Association of Community Education BC (ACEBC) and school district policy and procedure. The Community School Advisory Council may be consulted and have input into:    development/revision of the school profile; community school initiatives and services and their impact; community school budget, facility usage, and safety standards. 2. At the discretion of the Board of Education, a community school coordinator may be assigned to a particular community school or family of community schools. 3. The community school coordinator will:   report to the principal of the school to which they are assigned or to a designated principal in the case where the coordinator provides services to more than one school; communicate to the Community School Advisory Council under the authority of the supervising principal. 4. A community school or family of community schools shall annually submit an overview of its program plans for the following fiscal year to the Community School Review Committee by the end of March and a report on the current year’s operations by the end of June. 5. The Community School Review Committee shall review and assess the complete operation of the community school at the end of each year of community school designation and present a report to the Education Committee in September of each year for recommendation to the Board of Education. -3 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3610P 6. In the event of a less than satisfactory evaluation, the Board of Education may assign a one-year period of probation to be followed by further evaluation. Repeated less than satisfactory evaluations may result in:   extension of probation with a subsequent evaluation; or termination of the community school designation. Budget and Finances Each community school and family of community schools must comply with the following financial and operational guidelines and will:         operate in accordance with the District’s currently existing accounting guidelines related to the accounting for school funds under generally accepted accounting principles; be subject to the same audit and/or review procedures employed by the District’s external auditors; be entitled to maintain and manage income generated from fees, grants, and fund-raising activities; be charged applicable facility rental rates as listed in District policy; Comply with the procedures in the District’s rental policy; not operate with a deficit and will submit a balanced budget for each school year to the office of the Secretary-Treasurer not later than April 1st of the preceding school year. The budget will include provision for salary for a community school coordinator, supplies, clerical support staff, and in-service for staff and community participants. be subsidized, at the Board’s discretion, from the District’s operating budget only to the extent of the District’s contribution to the wages and employee benefits of the community school coordinator; be required to include in their annual budgets the dollar amount of the community school coordinator’s wages and employee benefits not covered by the District’s contribution. If applicable, the office of the Secretary Treasurer will provide this amount, to the community school by March 1st of each year for the following school year. Union Issues 1.
