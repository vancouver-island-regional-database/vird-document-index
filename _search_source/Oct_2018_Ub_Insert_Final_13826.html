MOVIE MAGIC IN DOWNTOWN LADYSMITH Sonic the Hedgehog created movie magic in Ladysmith during the month of September. The $90 million Hollywood production starring James Marsden, Tika Sumpter and Jim Carrey rolled into Ladysmith, creating a big-budget, filmmaking extravaganza, the likes of which the town had never before experienced. 1st Avenue was transformed into Green Hills, Montana, complete with restaurants, a hat shop, market, sheriff’s station and U.S. postal service. There were even exploding cars and fireballs! During day and night scenes, excited crowds of movie-lovers gathered to watch, while many Ladysmith and area residents were lucky enough to act as extras in the film. All the while, Ladysmith remained open for business and people were encouraged to check out their favourite stores and restaurants. employees, patrons and residents for their patience and flexibility. You will notice that the film crew worked hard to return everything to its original condition, although some shops chose to keep the colours chosen especially for the movie shoot. We are all looking forward to watching the finished product late 2019! Further, the bylaw clarifies the definition of ‘boulevard’. Did you know that the area between a roadway and a property line is a “boulevard” and is Town property? Unlicensed vehicles, unattached trailers and other items cannot be permitted to be parked/stored in these areas. This rule is standard in most communities and improves neighbourhood aesthetics as well as pedestrian and vehicular traffic safety. rules before any further compliance measures are taken. THERMAL CAMERAS DONATION Ladysmith Fire Rescue has acquired two new thermal imaging cameras thanks to a generous donation from the Ladysmith Healthcare Auxiliary. The state of the art technology cost approximately $22,000 and will allow firefighters to judge a building’s hot spots, find out where fires are located, and determine if someone is trapped within a burning structure. The cameras can also be used in other lifesaving situations, such as finding someone who may have been ejected from a vehicle following a crash. The Town and Ladysmith Fire Rescue personnel are extremely grateful for the Ladysmith Healthcare Auxiliary’s continued support of the health and wellness of our community. The bylaw has also been amended to prohibit camping and sleeping overnight in all types of vehicles on Town streets and boulevards. Working together to build our future FIREWORKS HALLOWEEN SAFETY Some people love to liven up their Halloween activities with a dazzling fireworks display, however, this is a friendly reminder that a CVRD Fireworks Discharge Permit must be filled out and submitted to the Town of Ladysmith. Please note that fireworks may be discharged only between the hours of 6:00 pm and 12 midnight on October 31 (and 12 midnight and 1:00 am on January 1). Pick up a form at City Hall or email info@ladysmith.ca Autumn brings with it falling leaves, cooler temperatures and little ghosts and goblins in search of candy. Consider wearing make-up instead of masks. • Create a fun necklace with string and glow sticks or reflective tape to keep kids visible to drivers. Or accessorize with a flashlight! • Stay on the sidewalk and never jaywalk. • Trick or treat in pairs or groups. • Make sure your kids ask you to check their candy before they eat it.
