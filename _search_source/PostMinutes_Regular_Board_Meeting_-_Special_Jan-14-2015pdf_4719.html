Toye, Executive Assistant Meeting No. 11 The Chair called the meeting to order at 9:07 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. ADDITIONS TO THE AGENDA Item 4.4 - Motion from the Business Committee Meeting Jan 14, 2015 Folio S15/01/14-01 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Kimler IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Higginson That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) to suspend the completion of Cedar Elementary for the 2014/15 school year, thereby releasing project AFG funding in favour of other maintenance projects across the district. Furthermore, that the Board of Education enter into a 90 day consultation to begin a full review of the Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan (EFLP). 60 days will be used to gather input from stakeholders and the public and 30 days will be for review and report back. At the end of the 90 days the Board will make a recommendation on the use of Cedar School and the EFLP. This process will start no later than February 9, 2015 and end no later than May 13, 2015. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held January 14, 2015 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) refer the motion from this evening's Business Committee to New Business - 4.4 APPROVAL OF THE AGENDA Folio S/15/01/14-02 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Solomon IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Robinson That the agenda be approved as amended. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Cedar - 30 Day Hold That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education to enact a 30 day hold on the construction and awarding of contracts at Cedar Elementary, but permit CUPE work that must be completed whether the board decides the school is best suited as a K-7 facility or a 812 facility. This has been completed. FSA Question and Letter Folio S15/01/14-03 Correspondence (to view correspondence, please go to the online agenda at: http://bit.ly/1xSqrJW) IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Solomon IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Bob That the Board Chair of the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) write a letter to parents that presents unbiased pro/con information on the FSA (Foundation Skills Assessment) exam, including checkboxes for opting out of the exam (as per BC legislation) and direct staff to honour parents requests to opt out of the exam, without further contact.
