March 13, 2012 Ref: 152743 James Brennan, Chair Board of Education School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) Email: jbrennan@sd68.bc.ca Dear Mr. Brennan: As you may have recently read in the 2012 Budget and Fiscal Plan, government is concentrating efforts to identify increased efficiencies in the schools, universities, colleges and hospitals (SUCH) sector. The public school sector spends approximately $840 million on district administration, operations and maintenance, and transportation services. Government recognizes that districts are continually attempting to find efficiencies in order to maximize funding that can be channeled directly to the classroom. Shared arrangements between districts provide further opportunities to more efficiently deliver “outside the classroom” services. I have notified eight Boards in the lower mainland that the Ministry of Education will engage in a process over the next 10 to 12 weeks to bring their staff together to develop a set of specific shared delivery opportunities and priorities, as well as, a roadmap to move forward. Recently, the Ministry of Education led a project with four pilot districts to assess the requirements for a shared Integrated Business Administration System (IBAS) for the sector. The findings of that assessment have proven valuable and will be integrated into the shared services review. This work builds on some of the current shared service delivery successes, such as bus procurement, WorkSafeBC claims management, and provincially negotiated voice services. I look forward to working together to identify opportunities to ensure available funding for the direct delivery of education programs for students is maximized so that all of our students continue to receive the best education possible. Yours truly, George Abbott Minister Ministry of Education Office of the Minister Mailing Address: PO Box 9045 Stn Prov Govt Victoria BC V8W 9E2 Location: Parliament Buildings Victoria
