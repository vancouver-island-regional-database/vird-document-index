NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: March 6, 2024 Business Committee FROM: Mark Walsh, Secretary – Treasurer Pete Sabo, Executive Director Planning & Operations Brian Hackwood, Assistant Director of Planning & Operations 2024/25 Annual Facility Grant (AFG) SUBJECT: Background: Each year the Ministry of Education announces the amount of the AFG, to be used by NLPS for projects that meet the Ministry of Education Policy 3.115.1. The funding was increased in 2023/24 from $2,849,677 to $3,398,734 (+$549,057) having the effect of partially covering the increased costs of wages and materials. Discussion: The Ministry of Education the is expected to announce ‘status quo’ AFG funding for 2024/25 in the value of $3,398,734. The grant is typically reduced by a contribution of $47,298 toward the continuing implementation of Capital Asset Management Services, leaving an allocatable balance of $3,351,436. CAMS is the process by which FCI scores are determined and maintained. The net amount of the grant is expected to remain equal to the funding received in 2023/24. Plans for the expenditure of the AFG funding were developed considering the following: • • • • • • • • • • AFG criteria Facilities condition reports Reported facilities issues and building element planning School/Department (5-year) Project Planning submitted June 30, 2023 Alignment to support Energy Management activities Alignment with the NLPS Operational Plan Alignment with Learning Services priorities Alignment with Ministry Capital Submissions and planning Alignment with the Long Range Facilities Plan Update Alignment with the Environmental Stewardship Policy Information Sheet: 2024/25 Annual Facility Grant (AFG) Page 2 Generally, AFG projects are completed using NLPS staff (Plan A) but resourcing and/or scheduling issues may result in Public Tenders being issued (Plan B). You will note that an overall program contingency is not apparent in the summary listed below. In accordance with Ministry reporting procedures, the contingency shown on the detailed project plan is apportioned to the projects listed on the sheet. This also applies to the administration item. The incorporation of an overall contingency allows staff to be flexible with project budgets and allows room for further alignment with other programs as well as room for the addition of emergent projects as they arise. Long Range Facilities Plan Recommendation #29 states: When applicable, review contemplated legislated changes with respect to accessibility and pursue an accessibility audit that reflect current legislation. • The BC Government passed a new provincial law in June 2021, the Accessible B.C. Act. and is now in effect. • AFG planning funding has been set aside to allow staff to prepare data to support the work of the District Accessibility Committee and Accessibility alignment within projects. For example: • o Ensures an accessibility lens on all projects. o Project may not be an accessibility project but if it makes sense to include an accessibility component, it will be considered. Other Accessibility funding includes: o Additional support for Emergent accessibility/inclusion issues o Replacement of stairway lift at Departure Bay o Design Planning for access ramp at Ecole Pauline Haarer Projects in this year’s plan that contribute to GHG reduction and resource conservation include: • 2 DDC controls upgrade – HVAC efficiency project • NLPS Strategic Energy Management Plan (SEMP) – recommissioning program will reduce GHG • Roof replacements consider upgrading roofing insulation – GHG reduction • 3 Portable HVAC electrification upgrades – High efficiency and classroom environmental condition upgrade Many of the projects include some design and construction and that the budgets, in some cases are notional. This would see the project estimate include expected soft costs, design contingency and construction contingency and may not be reflective of the final ‘construction only’ cost. AFG funding has been re-directed to Minor Technology Infrastructure Upgrades. NLPS is nearing completion of a significant IT infrastructure upgrade with costs distributed between AFG and Local Capital.
