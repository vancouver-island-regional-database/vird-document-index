BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools EDUCATION COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: April 3, 2019 Education Committee Carrie McVeigh, Secretary-Treasurer 2019-2020 Budget – Engagement Process The Budget Communication and Engagement Plan is designed to ensure that parents, students, staff and the broader school community are aware of our budget process and plan. Objectives: 1. To increase public awareness and understanding of the district’s financial position and the allocation of funds to programs and services 2. To provide assurances that the district is spending its resources efficiently, effectively and in a manner that supports learner outcomes as the top priority 3. To promote discussion on how best to allocate resources for the 2019/20 school year that reflect the Boards goals, along with budget guiding principles and criteria 4. To ensure transparency with partners, staff and the school community in the financial affaires of the district 5. To ensure that budget allocations adequately support the business departments within the organization in respect of ongoing needs, cost pressures and inflation, the maintenance of infrastructure and technology and sustainable replacement programs Key Budget Messages: 1. Ensuring public awareness and understanding of the district’s financial position and the allocation of funds to programs and services instils public confidence in the School District  The district continues to prioritize the allocation of funds to educational programs in accordance with the Board’s strategic plan, goals along with budget guiding principles and criteria  The district believes in pubic engagement that includes a feedback mechanism during the annual budget process  The district provides information revenues and expenditures during the budget process  The district posts an overview of the school district funding and budget preparation on the web site, along with other financial information  Educative aspect to ensure public is aware of district budget allocations versus district financial obligations (we really only have about 12% of our budget allocation to work with) 2. Providing assurances that the district is spending its resources efficiently and effectively  The Board of Education is financially prudent and accountable in the way we manage the funding it receives  The Board reviews the budget to ensure sustainability, the best use of funds and comparisons to other like sized districts  Public education regarding annual surplus 3. To promote discussion on how best to allocate resources for the 2019/20 school year  The Board of Education provides opportunities for the community to participate in the budget discussions via social media and public meetings and an open house event for the school community 4. Ensuring transparency with partners, staff and community in the financial affaires of the district  The Board will discuss budget decisions in public meetings  The Board will share budget information widely and in various forms including web site, social media, pod public meetings, staff meetings where appropriate etc.  The Board will use understandable language and visual aids to make the content easier to understand  Board will receive public quarterly budget updates outlining possible surpluses and deficits 5. Ensuring budget allocations adequately support the business departments within the organization  Cost pressures and inflation is identified during budget process  Investments that result in savings e.g. energy initiatives are identified during budget process  Maintenance programs and local capital needs are discussed during budget process Timelines: The Budget Process in an ongoing annual cycle of reviewing, forecasting and adjusting the current budget and actual expenditures and revenues and developing the next year budget. Much of the work commences in February/March and concludes with the passing of the Board’s Budget Bylaw which is submitted to the Ministry of Education. At the commencement of the budget process, a Budget Calendar is published. Engagement Methods: An Annual Budget Communication/Engagement Plan supports the overall objectives. In recent years, community engagement processes would include:  Online survey (Survey Monkey) to staff, students and general community. Online survey questions would provide high-level feedback. Generally, open-ended responses provided the most useful qualitative feedback, however, it was difficult to assess what was most important when reading many pages of individual responses.  Open house meetings typically organized in community (ie. North/Central/South). Open house format meetings were poorly attended. When considering staff and food costs vs. feedback return, this practice was determined to be prohibitive.  Invitation-only session typically held at Beban Park Social Centre. Invitees would include Aboriginal/Metis partners, union, local government officials, businesses, Ministries, social services, PAC, staff and student representatives. Invite-only sessions has better participation rates as we asked invitees to RSVP. Feedback at these sessions proved to be most relevant and useful.  Hiring an outside consultant to manage engagement (ie. Your Voice, 2020 Vision). Hiring a consultant helped guide budget and facilities projects. However, there is a considerable cost.
