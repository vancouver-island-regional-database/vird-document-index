BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: September 24, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Learning Services RE: DPAC Anti-Bullying Proposal Background Recently, the District Parent Advisory Council (DPAC) made a presentation to the Board’s Education Committee regarding a proposal to conduct a year-long anti-bullying campaign, during this school year that would include partnerships with the district, CUPE, the NDTA, and the community. The campaign would include materials for parents and staff, along with professional development opportunities and presentations for parents and students. The DPAC representatives asked for Board support for the project, including $15,000 in financial support. At the June 25, 2014 Regular Board Meeting, the Board approved the following motion from the Education Committee: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) commit a minimum of $15,000 from the 2014-2015 operating budget for the School District 68 Anti-Bullying Campaign and that plan is also predicated on staff development on the second Success For All day in January 2015; and further we ask that staff make this part of the plan. Due to the challenges of a late school year start-up, and the need to coordinate stakeholder involvement, across a broad group of stakeholders, the Department of Learning Services recommends that it begin preliminary conversation with the unions and representatives of DPAC regarding implementation in the 2015-16 school year. Recommendation: That the Board of Education delay the implementation of this campaign, and allow for the development of an appropriate plan during the 2014-15 school year, for implementation in the 2015-16 school year.
