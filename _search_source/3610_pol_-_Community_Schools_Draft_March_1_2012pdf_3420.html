SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 3610 COMMUNITY SCHOOLS School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) supports the concept of Community Education with services being delivered through designated Community Schools; and believes that schools are an integral part of the community; and community education should reflect partnerships with community members, organizations and agencies and municipalities to meet the needs of both public education and the community. Through Community Schools, the diverse learning needs of children and youth are met by incorporating a comprehensive range of effective educational practices. Community Schools are responsive, inclusive, culturally affirming and academically challenging. The learning environment effectively builds on student strengths to address the needs of the communities they serve. As hubs for the delivery of an array of services and supports, they use collaborative approaches to achieve learning excellence and well-being for the entire community. Community schools programs and services will reflect and incorporate the following six goals of community education: 1. Enrich the school programs through involvement and utilization of available community resources. 2. Support and resource the tenets of lifelong learning to include program opportunities for children, youth and adults. 3. Facilitate cooperation and collaboration between school and community to maximize the availability, variety and quality of community programs. 4. Provide involvement and participation opportunities for adults. 5. Increase citizen involvement in the decision making and leadership aspects of their local school community. 6. To maximize the use of the school facilities for community education. Draft: Adopted: Amended: 2010.10.21 1983.08.23 1999.03.31
