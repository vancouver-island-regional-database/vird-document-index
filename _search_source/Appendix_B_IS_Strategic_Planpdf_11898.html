Appendix B NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 27, 2024 Board of Education Laura Tait, Acting Superintendent Preliminary Strategic Plan Background: As you are aware, in the summer of 2023 the Board embarked on the creation/renewal of the Board’s Strategic Plan. August 2023 – Trustee working session to determine next steps and general priorities October 26, 2023 - November 24, 2023 – Public consultation September 2023 – Trustee working session to continue with prioritizing December 13, 2023 – Draft Strategic Plan Public Consultation feedback provided to the Business Committee October 12, 2023 – Draft Strategic Plan presented to the Business Committee October 25, 2023 – Board passed a motion to put the Draft Strategic Plan Goals and Objectives out for public consultation February 21, 2024 – Trustee working session to refine the draft Strategic Plan Staff reviewed the feedback received from various partner groups within our educational community and organized the feedback into themes which included Mental Health, Enhanced Facilities, Teaching and Learning Approaches, JEDI (Justice, Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion), Morale and Workplace, Indigenous Learning, Gardens and Green Initiatives, Transportation, Wi-Fi/Technology, and Partnerships & Communications. This structured approach enabled staff to condense and categorize the diverse range of perspectives, thus facilitating a clearer understanding of the overarching concerns and opportunities voiced during the consultation process. By aligning our objectives with these identified themes, we ensure that our Strategic Plan is not only comprehensive but also attuned to the needs and aspirations of our NLPS community. Staff and Trustees then engaged in a collaborative working session to refine the draft Strategic Plan. This session involved a comparison between the current draft Board Goals and the consultation themes, inclusive of staff recommendations and rationale. The overarching recommendations from staff emphasized the alignment of the Strategic Plan with the Enhancing Student Learning Report (ESL), a mandatory annual report stipulated by the Ministry of Education and Child Care. To ensure coherence and effectiveness, staff proposed the establishment of clear metrics for the objectives, aligning with the Board Work Plan, ESL Report, and departmental Operational Plans. Additionally, staff recommended narrowing the focus, evaluating the number of goals and objectives, and refining the language to enhance clarity and streamline communication. Information Sheet: Preliminary Strategic Plan Page 2 Through this collaborative effort, we aim to develop a Strategic Plan that is not only responsive to the needs of our community but also aligns with broader educational mandates and organizational objectives. Staff have now crafted the preliminary draft of the new Strategic Plan, which is slated for presentation, in its final form, at the April Business Committee meeting.
