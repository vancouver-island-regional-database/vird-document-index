Appendix A NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS MEMORANDUM DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 12, 2025 Environment Committee Mary Zuccaro, Energy Manager and Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Reduction Strategy – Buildings Only Purpose To outline practical options for reducing greenhouse gas emissions from our school district buildings and explain what it will take to meet Provincial and Board targets. Why This Matters • CleanBC Goal: Reduce building emissions by 50% below 2010 levels by 2030. • Board Goal: Reduce emissions by 4.5% every year. • These targets are ambitious and will require significant investment and planning What We’ve Done So Far • Optimized building control systems (heating, ventilation) through a Continuous Optimization Program (COP) – already saved 9% in gas usage at 13 sites. Funded through AFG. • Detailed mechanical studies at all sites to identify upgrade opportunities. 100% funded by BC Hydro. • Life Cycle Cost Analysis to understand costs and savings for major retrofits Key Findings • Doing nothing beyond current efforts will not meet any targets. • To meet CleanBC’s 2030 goal, we need to spend about $9.5 million by 2030 (roughly $2.4M per year). • To meet the Board’s goal, we need about $27 million by 2035 and ongoing annual spending. • Full electrification (switching from gas to electric boilers) would cost $24 million and increase operating costs (about $30K per year per elementary school). • Even with electrification, meeting both goals long-term is challenging. Page 1 of 4 Scenarios Reviewed 1. Do Nothing: No targets met. 2. Spend $600K/year: Still falls short of targets. 3. CleanBC Goal: $9.5M by 2030. 4. Board Goal: $27M by 2035. 5. Full Electrification: $24M – meets CleanBC and Board goals until 2033, but not sustainable beyond that. Bottom Line Meeting these targets will require major investment and strategic planning. Doing nothing is not an option if we want to align with provincial and board commitments.
