THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: November 23, 2011 TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Hammond Bay Gym Addition References:      Scope Options 1 and 2 for a proposed New Gym at Hammond Bay School Costing of Options for a proposed New Gym at Hammond Bay School Letter received from the Minister of Education dated February 9, 2010 Letter to the Minister of Education dated September 29, 2009 Action Sheet dated June 24, 2009 – Hammond Bay Gym – Funding Options Background: In the action sheet dated June 24, 2009, staff presented options with respect to the funding of a gym addition at Hammond Bay. Subsequently, the Board passed a motion asking staff to request the use of Ministry restricted capital to fund the addition. The Minister responded indicating that she is “not prepared at this time to approve the spending of restricted capital for this purpose until the Board has approved and submitted its Long Range Facilities Plan. As you can appreciate, it will be important to ensure adequate funds remain in the reserve for other high-priority capital related maintenance projects that may be necessary in the coming year.” On July 13, 2011 the Board of Education passed the following motion: R11/07/13-20 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to prepare a plan for the construction of a new gymnasium at Hammond Bay School; and further that it apply to the Ministry for financing for this capital project and apply local capital as available and prudent. Staff began the planning by investigating the scope for an addition to Hammond Bay gym as this would need to be known prior to calculating and requesting adequate funding. Scope options are as follows: 1. Gym expansion; or 2. Gym expansion including change rooms and a multi-purpose room. s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\6 - novembedr 23, 2011\hammond bay gym action sheet.docx Funding for the above options are estimated at roughly $800,000 each. See attached Costing of Options from J. Bush and Associates. Should the Board wish to proceed with an addition to the gym, staff recommends consulting with Hammond Bay School on “Option 1” as it satisfies the concerns brought forward by parents and it is at half the cost. As the Board is aware, staff was requested and continues to work on the ‘Asset Management Plan’. Phase 1 is to identify and confirm physical and legal attributes and is expected to be complete in November. Phase 2 is to determine highest and best use of properties. Phase 3will recommend a strategic approach for consolidation and disposal. The final stage of the Asset Management Plan is to create a plan by which the District will manage its assets. In Phase 3, staff would ensure congruency between assets, the Strategic Educational Plan, and the School District Facilities Plan and prioritize site usage and surplus sites in the context of ongoing District operations. It may be important to Trustees to address the highest educational priorities with available capital funding prior to committing to funding an estimated $800,000 for a gym addition at Hammond Bay. Given this, staff would see it as advantageous to advance the Strategic Educational Plan and combine it with the Asset Management Plan and the School District Facilities Plan to ensure a “big picture” coordinated approach to funding capital priorities in the District. Referring the addition to the gym to the strategic planning process will result in a delay to any plans for construction of an addition to the gym. Given this the school will be required to continue to operate out of a less than full-sized gym.
