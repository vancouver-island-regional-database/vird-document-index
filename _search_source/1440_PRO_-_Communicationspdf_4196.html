SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 1440P COMMUNICATIONS - Page 1 A. Communications The Superintendent of Schools shall be responsible for ensuring a communications plan for School District No. 68 and will bring forward, in September each year, a report to the Board on the communications plan. B. Communications Advisory Committee The Communications Advisory Committee shall monitor internal and external communication practices within the school district and make recommendations as needed to the Superintendent and the Board. Membership for this committee is one Trustee (who will serve as chair of the committee), the Superintendent of Schools, the Director of Communications, and one representative each from DPAC, NSAA, NDTA, DASG CUPE, Aboriginal Community and invite community representatives as needed. The mandate of the committee will be to provide advice on the district's internal and external communications programs in order to: 1. Promote public interest in, participation in and support for the operation of the school district and an understanding of and support for public education. 2. Provide an effective flow of information about the policies, procedures and, programs, and communication challenges of the school district to both the community and district staff. 3. Develop a climate that attracts and holds good employees and provides opportunities for staff to strive for excellence in the educational community.
