Meet the “Kids” The Kids on the Block is a troupe of educational puppets that dress and act like real children. Each nearly life-sized puppet character has a distinct personality and life story. Like real children, some have mental, physical, or emotional disabilities. Others have been affected by divorce, abuse, death, or other significant experiences. Mid Island Abilities & Independent Living Society supported by Rotary Club of Nanaimo Daybreak Abilities Above All The Kids on the Block puppets were developed in 1977 in response to a U.S. Public Law encouraging inclusion for people with disabilities in public schools. Today the puppets educate children all over the world about disability awareness and other important social concerns. Thanks to the generous sponsorship of Rotary Club of Nanaimo Daybreak, Mid Island Abilities & Independent Living Society is able to offer FREE Kids on the Block performances. Volunteers use puppetry to present educational programs for children grades 2-5. There are Kids on the Block programs all across Canada and in more than 30 countries. The style of puppetry used is based on Japanese Bunraku (boonra-koo). The large and colourful puppets are ‘shadowed‘ by the puppeteers who are dressed in black. Each Kids on the Block program is thoroughly researched and field tested before it becomes available to school districts, community service organizations and special interest groups. All performances adhere strictly to scripts developed by The Kids on the Block Inc. Headquarters in collaboration with nationally recognized organizations and numerous experts. Follow up material and evaluations are supplied for use by teachers or instructors. Each performance is 30-40 minutes and is best performed for groups of 20-100 students. Each performance includes question & answer periods where the audience interacts directly with the puppet characters. During this time, myths and misconceptions are replaced with facts and sensitivity.
