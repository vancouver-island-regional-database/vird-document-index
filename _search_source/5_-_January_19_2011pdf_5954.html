CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS B.C. Hydro - 2nd Quarter Update -P. Sabo & B. Kingsley Mr. Brian Kingsley, who is the district’s designated energy manager, gave the committee an update on the district’s energy management programs. Mr. Kingsley said that in 2009-10 the district’s total utility costs were about $2 million. Of that, 86 percent was for electricity and natural gas. He outlined the programs the district currently has in place as part of its energy management plan. These include ongoing upgrades to lighting and heating and ventilation systems, and the installation of the first solar power array in the district. The district receives grants from both B.C. Hydro and Terasen Gas to support energysaving initiatives. A comparison of energy costs from 2009 to 2010 show that the district was able to save about $111,000 in the first three quarters. COMMITTEE REPORTS From the Facilities Planning Committee Rotary Bowl - Verbal Update Mr. Sabo, Director of Facilities, announced that the district, in partnership with the City of Nanaimo, Vancouver Island University and the Nanaimo Track and Field Club, is undertaking a study of possible redevelopment options for the Rotary Bowl. Mr. Sabo said that the city, university and school district are each providing one-third of the $45,000 cost of the study, which will be carried out by Yates Thorn and Associates Consulting. The goal of the study is to outline what a multiuse facility at the Rotary Bowl could look like and how it could be operated. NEW BUSINESS 2010-11 Recalc and Amended Budget for 2010-11 Mr. Turin gave the committee an update on the school district’s current budget and presented an amended budget for the current school year. Mr. Turin said that the Ministry of Education announced in December that holdback funding totaling $47 million would be distributed across all school districts in the province. School District 68’s share was about $1.2 million, but reductions in other Ministry of Education funding meant that the district received about $925,000 in additional revenue. Mr. Turin said that the additional revenue was a positive development since in November the district was looking at a projected $700,000 shortfall. Once other increased costs were taken into consideration, the district was able to balance its amended 2010-11 budget. He cautioned, however, that the district cannot rely on receiving the holdback funding in future years. Mr. Turin noted that our district had not done well in past years with pay equity funding. The committee asked Mr. Turin to bring back further information to a future meeting. Opposed by Trustee Bonkowski CARRIED Draft Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held January 19, 2011 4 Enrolment Mr. Turin gave the committee an update on enrolment. He said that Ministry funded student enrolment as of September 30, 2010 was 13,604 full-time equivalents (FTE). This is a loss of 322 FTE students in the past two years. Preliminary estimates are that enrolment will decline by about a further 220 FTE next year. Budget Forecast Scenario Mr. Turin gave the committee several three-year budget forecast scenarios looking ahead to 201112 and 2012-13. The projections predict that the district may be looking at a shortfall of $3.6 million to $4.8 million by 2012-13. FOR INFORMATION Budget Calendar Received as information.
