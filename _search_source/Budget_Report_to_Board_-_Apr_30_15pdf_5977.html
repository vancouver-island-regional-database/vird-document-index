Final Becoming Financially Sustainable Budget Planning Calendar 5 – May Special Board Meeting to Adopt 2015-16 Budget 6 – May Submit 2015-16 Budget to MoE Week of 6 - May Meet with NDTA Meet with CUPE Becoming Financially Sustainable Background Nanaimo District Public schools have been in a deficit budget position 12 of the past 13 years. The District has continually struggled to deal with its budget shortfalls. During the past 4 years the district has been in funding protection. The Ministry of Education has been supplementing our funding during the significant enrolment decline. The district has used this money to soften reductions to staff and programs. The annual fund has been reduced by 1.5% of our total budget each progressing year forward since it was granted which is coming to an end next year. Becoming Financially Sustainable Background 2012-13 $1.5 million budgeted from surplus and received $4 million budgeted from funding protection Totalling $5.5 million subsidizing the operational budget 2013-14 $2 million budgeted from surplus used $2.5 million from funding protection Totalling $4.5 million subsidizing the operational budget 2014-15 $2.29 million from surplus $1.96 million from funding protection totalling $4.25 million subsidizing the operation budget Budget Allocations – Operating Fund Two Year Predicted Deficit Year 2015-16 2016-17 Total Deficit 4.5 Million 1.2 Million 5.7 Million Becoming Financially Sustainable Background The Staff believe that NLPS needs to become sustainable and live within its operating budget. By doing so it will allow the district to focus its capital and grant money into efficiency saving programs such as facilities renewal, educational programming, student support programs, and the vulnerable population of students in Nanaimo. Becoming Financially Sustainable Facilities Plan Summary Over the past eight years there has been three facilities plans, one external report and information gathered by “Your Voice” that created recommendations for facilities renewal for Nanaimo Public Schools: SD68 Renewal District Facilities Plan The Dr. Player Report 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan 2006 2010 2013 updated 2014 Staff gathered information from the above documents as well as current data and trends. Becoming Financially Sustainable Facilities Plan Summary Although each report was created by different staff at different times the data still illustrates many of the same themes that we are experiencing today: 1) Student supports is being affected by shortfalls of budget and facilities. 2) Capacity utilization is negatively impacting both the annual budget and ability to upkeep our current facilities. 3) Declining enrolment is negatively affecting capacities in current schools and creating inefficiencies in staffing. This allows the district to proceed strategically aligning district efficiencies and savings with the facilities plan. The goal being to have a financially sustainable operating budget for September 2016. This “amendment” to the “10 year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan” would be divided into three stages: Immediate actions to be undertaken in years one and two; medium term actions to be undertaken in years three to five and long term which includes recommendations for years six through ten. Becoming Financially Sustainable Budget/Facilities Two Year Plan There are three major components of the plan to build a sustainable budget for 2015-16 and 2016-17: 1) Firstly the 2015 budget must be sustainable on its own. The need to use surplus dollars from previous years will not allow us to prepare the system to maintain a sustainable budget. This means that significant cuts must be made to ensure that the district does not need to “dip into the cupboard” to support existing programs and staffing that have been protected in the past. Becoming Financially Sustainable Budget/Facilities Two Year Plan There are three major components of the plan to build a sustainable budget for 2015-16 and 2016-17: 2) Secondly the unrestricted surplus is significantly higher than in previous years due to job action. Staff recommends that this money is directed to strategic projects that enables us to support the district in maintaining long term budget sustainability and the boards three primary goals. Becoming Financially Sustainable Budget/Facilities Two Year Plan There are three major components of the plan to build a sustainable budget for 2015-16 and 2016-17: 3) By connecting both the unrestricted surplus and the school closures to create more efficiencies and savings in the system the district will have created a sustainable budget by the 2016-17 school year. Once that is achieved NLPS can then begin to analyze and plan for next steps in focusing on the improvement of student learning and the facilities plan. *Without any one of the three parts the plan will not be achievable.
