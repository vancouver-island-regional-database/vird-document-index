In this letter, the SD68 Board stressed the importance of maintaining the staffing level of five School Liaison Officers. in response, the detachment is confirming its intention to provide the same level of service again for the start of this year. There have been some changes within the unit, and the purpose of this correspondence is to inform you and your organization of these changes. The Nanaimo detachment’sYouth Officer Unit was formed out of the School Liaison Officer unit (SLO). This unit is comprised of 6 Youth Officers 5 Constables and 1 Corporal. Members of the Youth Officer Unit are police officers with a focus on schools. They will divide their time to fulfill duties both inside and outside of the schools, and attend where required to deal with criminal activity or behaviours that interfere with the operation of the school. — As in past ‘years, each of the Constables will be assigned one of the five SD68 high schools in the RCMP area, and the corresponding elementary schools within the catchment. There may be some minor alterations for our purposes, and the list of assignments is attached at the end of this letter. We are currently in the process of filling a vacant spot on the unit, and the remaining Constable will likely be named byseptember. The mandate of the Youth Officer Unit has been altered slightly from the focus of the previous School Liaison Officers. Our Youth Officers are committed to pursuing investigations into criminal and drug matters, assisting with the School Act's no trespass orders, and determining if a Restorative Justice model is applicable in these and other cases. We will still assist with Violence~ThreatRiskAssessments, deliver presentations as requested (including DAREand PARTY),and attend school functions and activities. Some additional topics we are seeking to deliver to students through the Youth Officers and other units include: healthy relationships, sexting, cyber safety, traffic safety and domestic violence (for the older youth). Due to a minor change in the unit's focus and responsibilities, a portion of time will be utilized in other community and youth based initiatives in and around the schools and community, including traffic enforcement in schools zones and working with youth at risk. The Youth Officer Unit has also formed a stronger bond with Cpl. Dave CUSSONof the Strategic Prevention Services. Cpl.
