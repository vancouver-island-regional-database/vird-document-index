CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Page 5 of 12 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held June 24, 2015 TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Higginson - BCSTA Update - On Friday the BCSTA and the MoE held a joint Executive meeting. This is the first in what is intended to be quarterly meetings between the two groups. Present were BCSTA Board of Directors and CEO, DM Byng and six (6) of the ministry's ADM's. The discussion consisted of looking at both organizations annual plan for the upcoming school year and discussion ways to support each other in the planning. The MoE signed an MOU with FNESC on Friday that is similar to the one that is signed with the BCSTA. Delta has rescinded its motion to leave the organization. This means the only non-member board is VSB. The rest of the weekend focused on BCSTA direction for the rest of the year. Issues that were expressed by our Board at the last Business Committee meeting were brought up and discussed. Trustee Kimler - Ladysmith Secondary Commencement was held on Friday, June 16. A student at Ladysmith Secondary School has received an honour by being named a 2015 "Schulich Leader". This graduate will receive $60,000 to be used for studies at Waterloo University where they will be pursuing a science degree in biomedical sciences and/or biochemistry. Ladysmith Intermediate - they put on a year-end concert on June 18. PAC did a bake sale and 50/50 draw (the winner of the draw donated 50% to PAC, so it was a big win for the school!) Ladysmith Primary - they received a $2,000 grant for literacy from Island Savings Credit Union via their "GIFT" program (Growing Island Families Together). This grant is the work of the LIS PAC and Literacy Committee working together to develop a longer-term literacy strategy. They also held their traditional year-end "pet parade" last Friday. Ecole North Oyster sends thanks to Nanaimo Foodshare, for their generous sponsorship of our emergency food program. Each Monday and Thursday, Foodshare has delivered a bin of fresh fruit, yogurt, sandwiches and baking goods in order to ensure that food is always available. The Secretary-Treasurer approved $15,000 to go to the ENO PAC to help defray the cost of new Page 6 of 12 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held June 24, 2015 playground equipment. Trustee Solomon - Georgia Ave - had their Aboriginal Day with a lot of help from John Barsby, who brought over the grade 8 class to help. The Special Olympics (Mini-O) took place at the Rotary Bowl and was a wonderful event. There are so many great mentors from the high school that help the kids. Cedar grads that are at Barsby and LSS put their hand prints on the wall. John Barsby's grad is this evening at the Port Theatre. A student won a $150,000 scholarship to attend military school. Trustee Brzovic - NDSS - Exams are taking place, their grad is tomorrow. Pauline Haarer had their last assembly today; grade 7's got flowers and an award. There were a lot of field trips this month and students were very busy with these and with all their other events. Trustee Brzovic attended the district track meet as well as the district retirement dinner and the NDTA and CUPE retirement dinners. She was also in attendance at VIU for the graduation of the vocational programs. Trustee Routley - Attended the Dover Bay Grad ceremony yesterday. Trustee Bob - Wellington Secondary School their grad is tomorrow. Carmella Luvisotto has been credited for influencing success in her students - the students attended Music Fest Nations and won four gold and one silver. Parents organized Prom for the first time and had to rally support from within the community. The renovations are underway with innovative design and contemporary learning spaces. Awards were handed out for outstanding achievement. Uplands Park - the PAC raised money and a primary playground was installed as well as a new computer lab. There was a cupcake walk and Funday and two retirees. Coal Tyee - There was an aboriginal day, parent appreciation tea, PAC led popcorn and cookie sale, burger and beverage sale. Rock City - their green garden project is going well. Departure Bay - received the Toyota Evergreen grant worth $21,000.
