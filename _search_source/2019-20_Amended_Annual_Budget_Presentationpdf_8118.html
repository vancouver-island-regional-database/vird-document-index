2019-20 Amended Annual Budget School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 www.sd68.bc.ca Date: From: Subject: February 12, 2020 From the Department of Finance 2019-20 Amended Annual Budget The purpose of this report is to provide and summarize the Amended Annual Budget Bylaw document as well as highlight the changes that have been made compared to the preliminary 2019-20 Annual Budget. Budget requirements per Ministry of Education: Boards of Education are required to prepare a balanced budget where board revenues plus any appropriated surpluses fully fund the following: • • • • • • annual operating expenses, annual special purpose fund (SPF) expenses, annual capital fund expenses, tangible capital asset acquisitions (from Operating, SPF’s & Local Capital), planned reduction of unfunded liability for employee future benefits, and any planned reduction of prior year’s deficits The Ministry of Education requires all Schools Districts to submit their budgets in the same format, presenting them in three distinct funding categories: Operating Fund, Special Purpose Funds and Capital Funds. Per section 113 of the School Act (Adoption of Budget), Boards must prepare an amended annual budget and have it adopted by bylaw on or before February 28, 2020 and submitted to the Ministry by this date. Per Ministerial Order 033/09 “Accounting Practices Order”, a capital fund deficit is permitted for the amount that amortization of tangible capital assets expense exceeds amortization of deferred capital revenue. Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools resides within the Traditional territories of the Snuneymuxw, Snaw-Naw-As and Stz’uminus First Nations. Syeyutsus – “Walking together; Walking in two worlds” Operating Budget Summary (Schedule 2): Figures provided below include the 2019-20 Annual Budget, as approved by the Board of Education. As well, all known or anticipated increases or decreases in revenue and expenditures have been accounted for and are reflected in the Amended Annual Budget. Total Increase in Operating Revenue is $3,140,859 which is predominantly due to increased enrolment of 176.733FTE over the preliminary Annual Budget. Revenue changes: The Ministry of Education Provincial Operating Grant changes are summarized below which reflects an overall increase of $3,248,997 FTE Staffing changes: Staffing increased by 33.716FTE from the Annual to the Amended Budget, of that, 29.635FTE was funded within the Operating Grant per increased Student Enrolment. Expense changes: Overall the District saw an increase in expenses totaling $2,913,433, which is summarized below. Changes include the supports put in place for increased student enrolment, salary adjustments required per the ratification of the CUPE Collective Agreement, increase to the average teacher salary, current incumbent salary changes, allocation of restricted surplus, and removal of the CEF staffing and remedy supplement as the full amount of the Special Purpose Fund has now been provided by Ministry. . Special Purpose Budget Summary (Schedule 3): Special Purpose Funds are targeted funds so new revenues are directed to the expense for which the funds are intended such as staffing, supplies and services. Any applicable surplus at year-end becomes revenue (deferred) for use in the subsequent year, per Ministry approval. Actuals at January 31st show a Fund deficit, however, this is only due to the timing of revenue received compared to ongoing expenses. Revenue increases include: • • • • • • • Classroom Enhancement Funding $1,918,657 Mental Health Grant $26,500 Changing Results for Young Children/Early Learning Framework Grants $15,082 ASSAI $90,500 Learning Improvement Fund $649 Deferred Revenue from 2018/19 $342,046 Rona Hero’s Grant $6,106 Total Increase in Special Purpose Revenue - $2,399,540 Budget Bylaw Summary (Statement 2): The 2019-20 Amended Annual Budget Bylaw has increased over the Annual Budget by $5,711,099 which includes the Operating Expense of $2,913,433, the Special Purpose Expense of $2,399,540 and the Capital Fund Amortization Expense of $398,126. The Budgeted deficit is in the Capital Fund only, and is permitted for the amount that amortization of tangible capital assets expense exceeds amortization of deferred capital revenue.
