SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 7002 Role of the Secretary Treasurer The Secretary Treasurer/Chief Financial Officer (CFO) reports to the Superintendent/Chief Executive Officer (CEO) and is responsible for all of the business functions of the School District. The Secretary Treasurer is the Corporate Officer of the Board and shall perform those duties as set out in the School Act and adhere to the Regulations as set out in the School Act. Specific Areas of Responsibility 1. Board of Education The Secretary Treasurer shall: 1.1 Maintain the corporate records of the Board of Education in a protected and accessible manner. 1.2 Provide for the safeguarding and security of the District’s corporate assets. 1.3 Approve on behalf of the Board of Education the handling of corporate legal matters. 1.4 Participate in the preparation of Board of Education committee Meeting agendas and minutes. . 2. 1.5 Authorize with the Chair of the Board of Education the official Minutes and Bylaws of the Board of Education. 1.6 Distribute all correspondence to the Board of Education. Fiscal Responsibility The Secretary Treasurer shall: 2.1 Prepare the annual and amended operating budgets and capital budgets, in cooperation with the Superintendent of Schools and the Board of Education. 2.2 Maintain appropriate budget and expenditure controls as required by the Ministry of Education and the Board of Education in accordance with current accounting standards. 2.3 Serve as the District’s management representative on the Teacher/ Trustee Liaison Committee’s Sub-Committee on budget. -1 - SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 7002 3. Leadership The Secretary Treasurer shall: 3.1 Provide leadership and supervise the overall management functions carried out by the Secretary Treasurer’s office and the District’s Finance Department. 3.2 Provide leadership to the District’s Facilities Operations and Transportation Departments by supervising the Director of Planning and Operations. 3.3 Provide leadership in the long range financial planning of the District. 3.4 Provide leadership in the preparation of the Five-Year Capital Plan and the District’s long term facilities plan. 3.5 Provide leadership as a member of the Senior Management Committee and other committees as required. 4. Employee Relations 4.1 Provide support or guidance to the Board of Education and act as a Resource person as requested in the negotiations with the CEO, the District’s senior staff, and support personnel. 4.2 Provide support in the area of union negotiations by being a member of the District’s bargaining team. 5. External Agencies The Secretary Treasurer shall: 5.1 Act as the District’s agent with architects, lawyers, bankers, auditors and other agency representatives. 5.2 Monitor the District’s insurance policies and insure that they are current and represent the best value for money and risk mitigation strategies possible. 5.3 Coordinate and maintain an effective liaison with provincial and local government and other external agencies. -2 -
