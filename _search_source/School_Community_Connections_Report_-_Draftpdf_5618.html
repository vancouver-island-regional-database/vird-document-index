The program is jointly managed by the Union of BC Municipalities and the BC School Trustees Association and is intended to promote sustainable and innovative collaboration between boards of education and local governments. In early 2010, the Supporting Neighbourhood Learning Centres program was launched through SCC. Boards of Education and local governments were invited to apply under Round 2 of the 2010 program. Grants of up to $25,000 per school district were available. Neighbourhood Learning Centres (NLCs) are welcoming places where people of all ages can access learning and community services. Each NLC is unique, reflecting the needs and priorities of the students and community it serves. NLCs support families by providing a wide range of programs and services for students, parents, families and the community both during and outside of school hours. All boards of education were encouraged to move towards this collaborative approach to the expanded use of school space, including elements of neighbourhood learning centres in all schools. The 2010 SCC program, Supporting Neighbourhood Learning Centres, was intended to continue the original goals of the 2005 and 2008 programs while actively encouraging boards of education and local governments to work cooperatively and with community partners to create neighbourhood learning centres and expand the use of schools to provide community services. This project was identified by the School Administration in conjunction with school staff and the Mayor of Ladysmith. SD Staff, School Staff, and the Mayor of Ladysmith met on April 22, 2010 and discussed the vision for the project. Subsequently, the District contacted the Township of Ladysmith to confirm its support for the grant aplication based on the following Vision: Ladysmith is an expanding community with a present population in excess of 8,200. As a bedroom community south of Nanaimo, Ladysmith continues to attract new residents whose growing cultural demands exceed those of the facilities available. Presently the Ladysmith Secondary School multi-purpose room provides the sole meeting space for a large host of local societies and government. Although acknowledged as the hub of community performances and meetings the venue is limited in terms of the appropriateness of seating, sound and technical capacity. Recognizing that there needs to be a greater utilization of school facilities within the community, Ladysmith Secondary School along with various community organizations have come together to discuss the merits of working together to increase the use of this local school facility through a community partnership. The Ladysmith Secondary School / Multi-Use Community Auditorium has been proposed as a venue through which civic, private, public and school based activities could be hosted. The creation, by means of a renovation of the existing space, would include the installation of retractable theatre seating. This along with a refurbished performance space, lobby and concession area would establish a site which would serve to uphold the community and school’s tradition of high-quality performances, education programs and community partnerships. It was agreed that to move the project forward, a meeting should be held between the city manager, the high school principal, and the consultant to brainstorm ideas which seemed to have relevance to both parties. The consultant would then follow up with community groups and others as necessary, prior to drafting a report. It was also agreed that there should be a consideration of how the community centre can be used more by the school in a collaborative fashion -- the example of the weight room was presented. There is no necessity within this project for direct public involvement. This is not a requirement of this planning phase of the ministry's funding program.
