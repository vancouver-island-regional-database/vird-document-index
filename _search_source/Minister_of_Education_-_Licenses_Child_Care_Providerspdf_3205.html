February 22, 2013 Ref: 165825 Dear Board Chairs: On February 20, 2013, government introduced Bill 8, the Miscellaneous Statutes Amendment Act, 2013, that includes proposed amendments to the School Act concerning use of board property for child care purposes. I have enclosed a copy of the proposed School Act amendment for your reference; the entire Bill may be viewed online at http://leg.bc.ca/39th5th/1st_read/gov08-1.htm. If passed, this legislation will amend the School Act to require all boards of education to establish a policy promoting the use of their property by licensed child care providers between the hours of 7 am and 6 pm on weekdays, provided the property is not required for K–12 educational programs, early learning programs, or extracurricular school activities. The proposed legislation further provides that boards may pass along the costs of making their property available to licensed child care providers, but may not make a profit. This “cost-recovery” requirement would not apply to agreements that are currently in place, but would apply to any renewals or extensions of those agreements. I am aware that many boards already support the provision of child care on school property in response to their communities’ specific needs. This provision will not significantly interfere with boards’ authority to determine how to use their property. Rather, the purpose of this legislation is to ensure that all boards promote the use of their property for child care purposes as much as reasonably possible – without disrupting educational activities – to maximize the availability of affordable child care in schools. This proposed legislative change builds on the excellent work already being done by boards across the province, and forms part of BC’s broader Early Years Strategy, an eight-year government commitment to support early childhood development and help families with child care. Thank you for your ongoing hard work on behalf of students and families.
