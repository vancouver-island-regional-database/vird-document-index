May 10, 2017 VIA EMAIL Ref: 193940 To: All Board Chairs, Secretary-Treasurers and Superintendents RE: Employment Practices Liability Program Review Dear Colleagues, I am writing to inform you that a review of the Employment Practices Liability Program (the Program) has been initiated. As you are aware, establishing an insurance risk pool for labour litigation was an innovative approach to managing legal services; for this reason, a review of the Program has always been planned to ensure that the model operates effectively and efficiently. Given that the Program has been operational for almost two years, this is a timely opportunity to conduct this review. An Advisory Committee composed of Senior Executive of the impacted ministries (i.e., Finance and Education) have commissioned this review. This will be an independent review that will be completed by September 30, 2017 by a group of legal, labour relations and financial experts from within government and the K-12 sector to allow for a thorough and broad analysis of the model. Consultations with key partners, stakeholders and school district staff will also be conducted, including the Employment Practices Liability Program Technical Committee. A report with recommendations will be completed for consideration by the Advisory Committee and Senior Executive in the Ministries of Education and Finance (Deputy Ministers Dave Byng and Athana Mentzelopoulos), as well as the Public Administrator of BCPSEA (Michael Marchbank) and Co-chair of the Service Delivery Project Steering Committee (Greg Frank, Secretary Treasurer, SD36).
