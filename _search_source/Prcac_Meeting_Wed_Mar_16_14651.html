Motion Carried NEW BUSINESS Glenn Popenko Presentation Glen Popenko, Ladysmith Minor Softball Association Umpire Coordinator, made a presentation to the Committee outlining how the Town could help local sports associations attract and keep officials like referees, umpires and other positions. His suggestions included using the Town website and Activity Guide to promote volunteerism, providing a list of all associations in town with contact numbers, and allowing access to the washroom in Aggie Hall during sporting events. The Committee suggested that Mr. Popenko, contact the Chamber of Commerce for volunteer organization’s information. Motion carried. Page 1|1 PRC Department Updates ADMINISTRATION SUMMARY February Drop-In patrons were 606 and Membership scan were 3,052 to the facility. The 2022 Spring Activity Guide is now available with registration started Monday, March 7. Printed copies are available at FJCC and locations throughout Ladysmith. PROGRAM AREA SUMMARY HEALTH & WELLNESS Registration and participation numbers continue to increase for program areas of health and wellness. Fitness centre staff continue to receive a lot of positive feedback from the community and participants about the variety of program offerings and the ability to no longer need to register to attend the Fitness Centre. AQUATICS Demand and requests for aquatic and swim programs continue to remain high, in addition to pool rental bookings. RECREATION - PRESCHOOL/CHILDREN/YOUTH The Youth Zone Pre-Teen Program continued in February at over 100% capacity. We have added additional spots to accommodate more preteen participation. Youth Zone continued in February on Fridays from 6 – 8 p.m. with approximately 14-20 youth in attendance every Friday. The Rec Room is open at lunch on Tuesdays – participation numbers continue to increase. GENERAL RECREATION & SPORT Many General Recreation - Adult programs in January and February were canceled due to low registration, presumably in part due to Omicron and changes to PHO. These programs included Spanish and French language classes, meditation and a few arts and craft classes. Many Sport – Adult programs continue to see an increase in participation. These programs include outdoor soccer at Forrest Field, indoor soccer at FJCC, and pickleball at FJCC. Staff are currently investigating program options for drop-in indoor volleyball and badminton at FJCC. SPECIAL EVENTS On Monday, February 21, we hosted two free Family Day Swims and Gym activities at the FJCC. Session #1 was from 12:00-1:30pm and session #2 was from 2:00-3:30pm. Special family-oriented activities and games were facilitated in the pools and gymnasium, with a focus on family, inclusivity, physical activity and physical literacy. Healthy to-go snacks were provided at the end of the event. The Town received a $1,000 grant from BCRPA to offset the costs to host this event. Page 1|3 PROGRAM AREA PROJECTS NEW HORIZONS FOR SENIORS Staff continue to plan and implement the four programs under the umbrella of the New Horizons for Seniors grant project. The four programs include: Cooking with Tradition, It’s not Right, Dig It! Gardening Program and Gentle Minds and Motion. All project activities will be wrapped up by the end of March.
