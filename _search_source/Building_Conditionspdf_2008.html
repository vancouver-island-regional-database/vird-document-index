STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Protective Services Department October 7, 2025 4020-01 Building Conditions RECOMMENDATION: That Council receive the report dated October 7, 2025 from the Manager of Protective Services outlining the results of bylaw enforcement investigations and actions related to:  Folio 445-0083.000 -12 Gatacre St  Folio 445-0207.500 - 422 1st Ave  Folio 445-0234.000 - 640 1st Ave EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Bylaw Enforcement has investigated three properties for potential hazards and unsightly premises as directed by Council. This report contains the results of those investigations and recommendations for next steps. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: Resolution Resolution Details CS 2025- That Council direct staff to investigate potential hazards in buildings brought 073 forward to the Committee by Councillor Paterson and return to Council with recommendations for next steps. CW 2025- That the Committee recommend that Council direct staff to investigate 019 potential hazards in buildings brought forward to the Committee by Councillor Paterson and return to Council with recommendations for next steps. INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: Downtown Ladysmith is known for its heritage and unique downtown core. Unsightly or poorly maintained properties with accumulated debris, overgrown vegetation, graffiti, or visible structural deterioration have generated concern among residents, business owners, community stakeholders, and Council. As noted above, three properties have been brought to staff’s attention due to particular concern expressed by Council. A summary of each property’s current status is provided below. 1. Folio 445-0083.000, 12 Gatacre St – Bylaw Enforcement has conducted site visits and met with the owner, who has acknowledged several deficiencies as identified under "Ladysmith Property Maintenance Bylaw 2018, No. 1970". The owner is also aware of structural concerns affecting the exterior of the property. They have committed to a course of voluntary compliance, to be closely monitored by Bylaw Enforcement in accordance with “Town of Ladysmith Bylaw Compliance Policy (09 4020 B)”. The owner will also be expected to work with Building Services to acquire any permits necessary to address the exterior structural concerns. 2. Folio 445-0207.500, 422 1st Ave – Bylaw Enforcement has conducted site visits and corresponded with the owner. A concern with the electrical service on the alley (south) side of the building was remediated immediately. Bylaw Enforcement identified some overgrowth of weeds, etc., at the rear of the building and has communicated to the owner that it will require cleanup. If voluntary compliance is not achieved, staff recommend delaying enforcement actions until construction activity associated with a nearby building has been completed. Observations from the exterior have not indicated obvious structural concerns with the building. A complete and accurate assessment would require the engagement of a structural engineer. 3. Folio 445-0234.000, 640 1st Ave – Bylaw Enforcement conducted a site visit and noted a pile of garden refuse that would be an infraction of Bylaw No. 1970. Before any enforcement action could be taken, the refuse was removed. This property is now in compliance with Bylaw No.1970 and therefore this file has been closed. No further action is required. A Fire Safety Inspection was last conducted in March of 2024.There were no major deficiencies noted at that time. As with the previous file, observations from the exterior have not indicated obvious structural concerns with the building. A complete and accurate assessment would require the engagement of a structural engineer. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Direct staff to an alternative course of action, as determined by Council.
