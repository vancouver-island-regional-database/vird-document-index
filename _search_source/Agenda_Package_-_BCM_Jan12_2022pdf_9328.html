CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY 4 Page 7 of 25 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 12, 2022 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Pete Sabo, Executive Director Planning & Operations Kelsey Bakewell, Manager of Facilities Planning Major Capital Projects Update Background Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) currently has 3 major projects that have been approved for construction by the Ministry of Education – Ecole Hammond Bay Expansion, Cilaire Elementary Seismic Upgrade and Pleasant Valley Elementary Seismic Upgrade. All three projects are well underway in construction, with completion expected in the summer of 2022. In addition to the projects approved for construction, the Dover Bay Secondary expansion design is complete and NLPS is awaiting approval from Ministry of Education to proceed to tender. Discussion All major capital projects are proceeding within budget, with contingencies still available to carry the project through to the end of construction. Planning has flagged the schedules for the construction delivery as a “risk” for each of these projects. The current construction market is facing extreme supply chain issues, beginning with COVID and increasing in effect from the latest flooding disaster on the mainland. The Hammond Bay expansion project schedule has been addressed and adjusted, while the two seismic projects are currently holding to the original schedules. The project schedules are being discussed with contractors and consultants and carefully monitored by NLPS staff. Any changes to the official completion dates have and will continue to be communicated to senior leadership for departmental planning purposes. Page 8 of 25 Information Sheet: Major Capital Projects Update Page 2 It's also important to mention the significant work that is being done to address synergistic projects. These are projects funded elsewhere and done in concert with these major capital projects. Planning has investigated old building systems, important inclusivity items and general school operation items and in working with stakeholders have approved projects to assist providing a more comprehensive upgrade or addition. Although Hammond Bay Elementary expansion is currently scheduled for completion on July 4, 2022, current schedule will allow for the school to move into the space in the summer of 2022. Currently, the steel frame of the building and the timber have been installed, and about half of the parking lot construction is complete. The next phase of the project will see the exterior shell of the building constructed, so interior works can begin. Significant systems upgrades include the fire alarm and PA system. Facilities has been working closely with the school administrators to ensure a minimally impacted school operation and has incorporated some items into the project such as: • • • • A temporary traffic circle to accommodate the pick-up/drop-off congestion, until the construction of the permanent traffic circle is complete (spring break) Installation of a doorbell camera for the temporary entrance, connecting to the office Construction of a new path around the play field to allow a safer and easier access to the school Agreement with/by the City to utilize the tennis court as additional hard surface play area for the duration of the project. Synergistically, Hammond Bay will have both the interior and exterior painted, the lead in water concern addressed, IT infrastructure upgraded, and consideration for a new VP office. Cilaire Seismic Upgrade is expected to be delivered on schedule and is within the approved budget. This past summer, with COVID pressures and restrictions in place, Woodlands Secondary renovations were completed on time and budget. Through a combination of departmental efforts and additional coordination, Cilaire was moved and set up in the new swing space in time for school to start in September. Overall, the transition was smooth, with a few “lessons learned” that will be applied in future transitions. The seismic upgrade is currently scheduled for completion by the end of June, allowing for the transition of Cilaire back to the upgraded school. Synergistically, Planning has lined up some exciting synergistic projects: • • • Cilaire will receive a full HVAC upgrade, including ventilation with MERV13 filtration and boiler upgrade. This project was initially to be funded from School Enhancement Program (SEP), and higher than expected tender pricing was funded via a combination of efforts by NLPS and Ministry staff o Carbon footprint will be reduced by 26 tCO2e annually The old ‘lift’ is being completely removed and replaced with a fully functional commercial grade elevator Each floor of the school will have an accessible and gender inclusive washroom Page 9 of 25 Information Sheet: Major Capital Projects Update • • • Page 3 A request for Cilaire as the #1 Playground Enhancement Project request for 2022/23 was submitted in the 5-year Capital Plan – this would potentially see the upper-level playground replaced A request for a Building Envelope upgrade to Cilaire was submitted in the 5-year Capital Plan – this would potentially see the upgrades of the exterior in phases Planning has started on a parking lot expansion design Additionally, Cilaire will have both the interior and exterior painted. The PA, fire alarm system, and IT infrastructure will be upgraded. Pleasant Valley seismic upgrade is expected to be delivered on schedule and is within the approved budget. Similar to Woodlands, Rutherford renovations were completed on time and budget this past summer in enough time to coordinate efforts into transitioning Pleasant Valley into the swing space. This transition went smoothly, with positive comments about how well the temporary space is working. The seismic upgrade is currently scheduled for completion by the end of June, allowing for the transition of Pleasant Valley Back to the upgraded school. Synergistically, Planning has included some big synergistic projects for Pleasant Valley: • • • • • The Pleasant Valley gymnasium will receive a HVAC upgrade. The project was initially to be funded from Carbon Neutral Capital Program (CNCP), and higher than expected tender pricing was funded through previous years’ SEP and Local Capital o Carbon footprint will be reduced by 16 tCO2e annually Three new washroom facilities are being constructed, to enhance accessibility and inclusivity The Garavanta lift has been removed from the stairwell and a commercial grade elevator is being installed in a new enclosure on the building – keeping in mind potential future expansion possibilities Planning has started on a parking lot expansion design The Franklyn Gym floor was intended to be used to replace the gym floor in Pleasant Valley, but further investigation concluded that the floor would not be suitable. However, the team saw other potential, and the flooring will be re-purposed into flooring upgrades within the building Additionally, Pleasant Valley will have both the interior and exterior painted, the fire alarm system will be replaced, IT infrastructure will be upgraded, and the gym will see a new floor. The Planning Department has had an incredibly busy year and has dealt with some extreme staffing shortages. 2022 looks to be another very busy year, but with some additional staff on-board. This summer, these 3 projects and presumably the Dover Bay expansion will be completed, leading to an incredibly busy schedule of ensuring all these spaces are ready for the 2022/23 school year. Rental Income – Community Use of Schools History*: • 2017-18: $323,750.58 • 2018-19: $316,079.16 • 2019-20: $258,058.15 (lower due to Covid impact for part of a year) • 2020-21: $155,854 (full year of Covid impact) • 2021-22: $92,475 (as of Dec. 15 /21) *Surplus space rentals, licenses and leases are not included (these proceeding largely unaffected – see attached summary). It is of note that the value of childcare (consisting mainly of before and after school care) contracts for the 2021/22 school year are currently estimated at $133,500 annually. Childcare providers operating out of SD68 facilities were given the option to reopen after Spring Break (March 30, 2020). Schools remained closed but childcare providers were given accommodation to help families who are unable to work from home and rely on childcare. A gradual resumption of approved indoor rentals began July 2020 for school partner groups such as Tillicum Lelum Aboriginal Friendship Centre, Nanaimo Science (NS3), and Community School Programs. CSP programs running during Spring and Summer break included babysitting courses, reading camps, Art in the Park, Kids Get Cooking, and various sports camps. Whenever possible, these programs were moved outside to reduce potential Covid transmission. Gabriola Recreation Society was given approval to restart operations at GES and were permitted to use their leased office space and the soccer fields during the summer. Page 11 of 25 Information Sheet: Rentals Update Page 2 As of July 2020, all approved rental groups & Community School Programs were to provide a Covid Exposure Safety Plan which aligned to PHO and NLPS plans, prior to contract approval. This ensured that rental groups had a Covid plan in place that aligned with that of the District and minimized possible disruption to school operations. All District policies are to be followed by all who are in a District facility. The instructions were to share the plans with the parents of those attending the program. All community rentals (indoor and outdoor) remained under review for the duration of Summer 2020. In September 2020, outdoor rentals were re-allowed permitting approval, however no access to buildings was available. In Summer 2021, staff began tentatively booking in groups on Rollover List from 2020 who were put on hold due to Covid. Mid-September start date (as usual) with intent to adjust date if indoor rentals were not yet approved by the date on contract. Those on the Rollover List book weekly re-occurring sessions on a yearly basis at the same school(s), at the same time(s), on the same day(s) of the week. To be clear, due to uncertain application of PHO protocols, focusing on basic school operations and lack of clarity on a process for approving rentals, none of the tentative bookings proceeded. Operations: • High touch points are disinfected daily, down from twice daily in 2020-2021. • SD68 is now using Chlorox Anywhere for most disinfection, a more benign chemical (0.014% hypochlorous acid) which allows for more access into school areas during times when spray is applied. • Clorox Anywhere application take is applied by school-based custodian or the temporary work assignments for spraying, typically no earlier than 6pm. Adjustments to spraying for rentals would be on a case-by-case basis and would need to be accommodated operationally to allow the rental to occur. Some might be easy to accommodate, while other rental requests might have significant challenges. In December 2021, a Covid Exposure Safety Plan is no longer required, and NLPS have adjusted to a Communicable Disease Plan that follows a very similar format. Discussion: The accommodation of miscellaneous indoor rentals continues to be complicated due to the needs to support school operations, and the seemingly everchanging PHO guidelines affecting various rentals and types of events. With multiple definitions of “inside event” it means different rules in place for each type of event (Indoor Sitting Events vs Indoor Standing Events, events with 50+ people vs events with -50 people, youth activities, adult sports, worship services). Page 12 of 25 Information Sheet: Rentals Update Page 3 Consideration for gradual return to Community rentals and how it impacts on the work of the Operations department is also considered – key items include the timing of event, additional cleaning required (or not) and more recently, consideration of availability of staff. As indoor community rentals do return, it is expected to proceed in the following order (subject to PHO and the desire of the rental groups): o Youth-based programs on rollover list & Community School Programs o Adult-based programs on rollover list o New requests on waitlist - processed on a first come first served basis, based on request date Process to return rentals to schools is under development: o Staff have been working with Operations and OH&S staff on a Covid Safety form, which is now being piloted. This form will be sent out to the tentatively booked rental groups for completion. Community School Coordinators will also be provided with the various Covid forms so they can begin working with their partners to reintroduce Community School Programs back into schools. o As a pilot, on December 17, 2021 staff provided Gabriola Recreation Society with a Covid Communicable Disease Plan to complete and will work on finalizing the contract once returned.  GRS was chosen as a pilot as limited recreation opportunities may be available on the island and within the Gabriola community. o Recent Covid restrictions implemented on December 20th limit indoor adult exercise and fitness activities, including participating in low or high intensity group exercise or fitness classes or adult dance classes. These restrictions are in effect until January 18th, 2022.  This does not apply to programs specifically for children and youth. That being said, Rentals have had hesitation from a few program organizers and principals regarding the Omicron variant and their preference would be to hold off on proceeding with indoor rentals. Conclusion: Staff continue to endeavor to have miscellaneous after hours space rentals return while ensuring the integrity of the schools operations, meeting the changing PHO protocols and other operational constraints. It has been a slow process, one which staff believe they are accommodating as quickly and safely as fluctuating circumstances allow. Background At the November Board of Education meeting, the Board approved the terms of reference (the “TOR”) for the Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability Advisory Committee (“ESSAC”). ESSAC has held a number of meetings pursuant to the TOR. This Action Sheet is to outline an initial recommendation from the ESSAC committee for the Board’s consideration. Discussion One of the Strategic Plan Objectives of the Board was to “Establish a Board Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability Committee”. With the passage of the TOR this objective was achieved. ESSAC has discussed the matter and have determined that an appropriate next step for the Board’s consideration is to create an Environmental Stewardship Action Plan. The Environmental Stewardship Action Plan will: 1) Cultivate personal ownership and a school district culture of environmental stewardship. 2) Guide school district practices and operations to minimize negative impacts on the natural environment. 3) Support teachers to develop awareness, knowledge, and understanding of environmental issues and initiatives.
