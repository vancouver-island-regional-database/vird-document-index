CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Derek DeGear, President, NDTA Mr. Derek DeGear said the NDTA opposes the extra week of spring break because of the lost opportunities for students. He said the week creates difficulties for many parents and the proposal affects teachers-on-call who lose wages and benefits. Ms. Kirsten Verhoeven, Nanaimo Association of Music Educators Ms. Kirsten Verhoeven said that the proposed two weeks spring break affects music teachers because many music teachers provide preparation time for other teachers. The result, she said, is that a music teacher at the top of the scale can lose as much as $1,300 in salary over the course of the school year. In addition, she said, students can lose up to 110 minutes of music over the course of the school year. Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 4, 2011 2 Mr. Rob Zver, President, CUPE Local 606 Mr. Zver said that the extended spring break takes money away from support workers who are the lowest paid employees in the district. He said parents will have additional costs as a result of the extended spring break and urged the Board to reject the proposal. CORRESPONDENCE Staff said they received 14 letters from parents and members of the public with comments on the spring break proposal. Correspondence received regarding the proposed local school calendar for 2011-12. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS Local School Calendar Consultation Process Staff reported that the online survey was conducted from February 11 to March 29. In all, there were 1,696 responses to the survey. Overall, 59.5 percent of the respondents were in favour of the extended spring break, with 36.7 percent opposed and 3.8 percent neutral.
