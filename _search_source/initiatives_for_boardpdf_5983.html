Information Systems Initiatives 2015/16 and beyond My Education BC (MyED) Student Information System This initiative transitions us from BCeSIS, a system that was used for school and district operations, to one that adds learning initiatives in support of the strategic goals of the district. For the first time, this system brings consistent technology into the classroom and adds features such as a gradebook, student/teacher portal, curriculum management, teacher collaboration and student portfolio. Secondary Labs, Elementary Labs, Secondary Teacher Laptops We equipped the Elementary Teachers with laptops in the 2014/15 school year in support of MyED. Our focus now shifts to labs in both secondary and elementary, and secondary teacher laptops. In the past, when there was little to no funding for technology, PACs would raise money to buy off lease computers from Computers for Schools (CFS) for these needs. CFS allows school districts to buy donated computers at low cost. The trouble with this program is that it allows districts to replace computers long past their optimum lives with ones that have just ended theirs. Administrative Machines The computers used by the school principals are five years old this year. As with the educational technology, they also need to be put on an evergreen program. Servers The IS department maintains a data center to support the needs of the district for critical services such as email, files service, web service and business systems. Along with those, servers are used at the schools to provide local services. These critical machines need to be under warranty. Equity, Evergreening the technology We need to be in a place where baseline technology is on an evergreen program to keep them at an age no greater than five years, and where equity is a cornerstone of the technology plan. PAC funding should never be counted on to provide baseline educational technology in any schools.
