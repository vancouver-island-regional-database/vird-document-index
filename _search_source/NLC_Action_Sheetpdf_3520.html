THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: November 23, 2011 TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Neighbourhood Learning Centres School District Facilities Plan Initiative #7 and School Community Connection Grant – Round 2 Reference:    SCCP “2010 Final Report Form – Round 2” Meeting Notes on Future Neighbourhood Learning Centres dated June 21, 2011 Neighbourhood Learning Centres Program Overview dated August 2011 Background In the School District Facilities Plan passed October 2011, there were a number of “Capital Asset Utilization Initiatives” including Initiative #7 – Development of Neighbourhood Learning Centres: Neighbourhood Learning Centres provide opportunities for people to access education and community services under one roof. Neighbourhood Learning Centres can offer a broad range of community-based services such as early learning, health services, counselling services, senior programs. The School District will establish a multi-agency community services committee to identify programs that could be offered at Neighbourhood Learning Centres at schools across the District. The School District will endeavour to develop Neighbourhood Learning Centres in partnership with First Nations, Vancouver Island University, the City of Nanaimo, Town of Ladysmith, District of Lantzville, Regional District of Nanaimo, Cowichan Valley Regional District, Vancouver Island Health Authority, provincial ministries, federal departments, not-for-profits, and other service providers. Decisions to establish Neighbourhood Learning Centres will be based on:  school considerations  community considerations  business considerations  facility considerations Subsequently a School Community Connections grant for Neighbourhood Learning Centres came available through UBCM, the District applied for a School Community Connections grant. s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\6 - novembedr 23, 2011\nlc action sheet.docx On November 24, 2010 the Board of Education passed the following motion: R10/11/24-12 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) supports the community connections application, with the City of Nanaimo as partnering agency, for funding to study the process of creating Neighbourhood Learning Centres in surplus space in SD68 schools, and further, that the Board of Education support the district in providing overall grant management. A School Community Connections grant in the value of $15,000 was approved on December 16, 2010. The intent of the grant was to “help the District to better clarify issues and develop a process for creating Neighbourhood Learning Centres in surplus space in SD68 schools.” Staff reviewed all Neighbourhood Learning Centres projects that received 1st round grants, and identified potential consultants. Staff received three replies from consultants with one declining. Staff reviewed and conducted interviews for the two remaining consultants. It was determined Alkins Project Services Inc. would be the best fit for the District due to their experience. A workshop was held with District representatives to: a) b) c) Review the purpose; Review existing situations; and To begin to develop a road map (see notes attached June 21, 2011). After the meeting, it became clear that, in order to implement Neighbourhood Learning Centres, it would be important to understand and further develop a Strategic Educational Plan and the School District Facilities Plan as these would directly affect the availability and location of surplus space to the community. The intent of the grant was to assist staff in developing a “road map” to accomplish Initiative #7. It would be difficult to proceed any further on the “road” to establishing Neighbourhood Learning Centres without a clear understanding of the specific educational goals being supported. It would also be important to understand the priorities for staff to follow to meet the intent of the Strategic Educational Plan, the Asset Management Plan and the School District Facility Plan. The consultant was subsequently asked to give a program overview of Neighbourhood Learning Centres from a provincial prospective as background for Senior Staff and Trustees (See attached dated August 2011). This Action Sheet was discussed and supported at the November 8th Facilities Planning Committee meeting. Therefore, staff proposes the following recommendation. Recommendation That the Board of Education of School District 68 refers the matter of the Neighbourhood Learning Centres and work completed to date by Alkins Project Services to the development of the District’s Strategic Educational Plan and that staff should include any recommendations with respect to Neighbourhood Learning Centres in the context of the District’s overall educational goals And: That the Board of Education of School District 68 requests staff continue to receive and evaluate community initiatives with respect to surplus space and bring forward recommendations that are within the scope of the current planning and at a term that would be acceptable to staff. s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\6 - novembedr 23, 2011\nlc action sheet.docx
