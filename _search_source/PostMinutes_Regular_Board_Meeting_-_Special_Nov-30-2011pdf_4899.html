CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS French Immersion Review Chair Welch introduced Mr. Doug Hibbins and Mr. Stuart Ladyman, consultants from Ladyman Consulting, Alkins Project Services and Trillium Infrastructure Solutions to the meeting. The review of the French Immersion program was the result of the Facilities Plan approved by the Board in the fall of 2010. The plan set out a number of initiatives that the Board should undertake to make the best use of facilities in the district. One of these initiatives was a review of delivery arrangements for the French Immersion program. The Board engaged a multi-disciplinary team of consultants to undertake the French Immersion Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held November 30, 2011 2 program review. During the spring of this year, the team gathered data and consulted with students, parents and staff members. The specific objectives of the review were:  to identify ways of continuing to provide high quality French Immersion programs that meet the needs of students and parents within current funding realities  to identify strategies to address overcrowding at French Immersion elementary schools  to inform ongoing refinement of the District Facilities Plan. The review report makes a number of recommendations related to a wide variety of factors affecting the French Immersion program, including: 1. Maintaining enrolment levels at or above the provincial average 2. Introducing Late French Immersion in 2016-17 or later 3. Maintaining at least one dual-track elementary school 4. Monitoring learning outcomes 5. Helping parents who are considering French Immersion to understand the commitment needed for student success 6. Monitoring attrition through exit interviews 7. Developing strategies to support the recruitment and retention of French Immersion teachers 8. Centralizing of French Immersion registration 9. Discontinuing the present practice of offering preferential treatment for registration of siblings 10. Ensuring out-of-district students are not registered unless there is surplus capacity 11. Considering facilities options within the district’s overall plans for student achievement and include appropriate public consultation 12. Offering some form of transportation to secondary French Immersion students.
