NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 13, 2021 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Financial Update Background On December 20, 2020, the Ministry of Education announced the recalculated operating grants for the 2020-21 school year based on the September 30th student enrollment information, as well as confirmed our Classroom Enhancement Fund submission. While the Annual Amended Budget will be presented to the Business Committee for discussion in February, we thought it would be important for the Board to understand our general financial picture as we currently know it as well as what remains unknown or unconfirmed to date. As we had previously reported at the October Business Committee meeting, the District was facing a significant funding loss due mainly to the movement of students from regular classrooms to Distributed Learning; this loss has now been confirmed. There are however some significant funding announcements that have yet to be confirmed, the most significant being the second installment of the Federal Safe Return to School grant ($2.6 million). The Ministry has indicated they have not been able to release those funds because they have not received approval to do so at this time, but we do anticipate confirmation in the coming weeks. Although Phase 2 funding is still to be confirmed, we were recently notified that Ministry held back 10% of the Phase 1 funding which they have now distributed to districts, our portion of that funding was approximately $250,000. The Ministry has also indicated that due to enrollment shortfalls and students shifting to DL province wide, there is a significant surplus of operating grant dollars, referred to as holdback, which is approximately $37 million. A portion of this holdback will be required to fund any increases to Distributed Learning enrollments that are identified in the February and May 1701 Student Data Collections, however, any remaining surplus balances will likely be distributed to Districts; the timing and amount are yet to be confirmed. We are also expecting an additional 73 international students to arrive this month, in time to quarantine for 14 days prior to beginning in class instruction for Quarter 3. This would be an increase of approximately $475,000 in funding if these students arrive. Ultimately, where there continue to be a number of unknows the District is likely to be in a position to invest in supports to address learning loss in the current school year.
