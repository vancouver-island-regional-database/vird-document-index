IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Saunders IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Allen Folio B14/10/08-04 LIF - Learning Improvement Fund That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith that it extend the MNP contract for one year for the 2014-2015 fiscal year. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Turin told the Committee that the Learning Improvement Fund totals $1.8 million for teachers and $453,757 for support staff this year. The allocation of the funds has been done in consultation with the NDTA and school staff committees, for teachers, and CUPE, for support staff. The fund for teaching positions will be used for instructional coordinators; the self-regulated learning coordinator; and small school staffing, as originally planned in the 2013-14 preliminary budget. In addition, there will be 4 FTE additional elementary school support teachers and 4.93 additional FTE teachers to add blocks to secondary schools, as well as a 0.5 FTE English Language Learners Teacher position. Auditor General Correspondence on Budget Monitoring Project Mr. Turin said that SD68 is one of four B.C. school districts selected for a project by the B.C. Auditor General. The project will look at how school districts develop their budgets and monitor and control expenditures. Mr. Turin said that he looks forward to working with the Auditor General’s Office on this project. The project will result in a report to the B.C. Legislature, giving an overview of school district’s budgeting processes. Enrolment Update Mr. Turin provided a very preliminary update on student enrolment for this school year. He said that very early indications are that enrolment may be somewhat lower this year than had been projected, but more will be known after October 17, which is the official date for the enrolment count. With the fiscal challenges facing all of us, it is imperative that all entities participate in the available shared service initiatives. In 2012, the Ministry of Education engaged Deloitte, one of Canada’s leading professional service firms, to research and identify potential shared service opportunities, and to deliver an assessment of likely costs, benefits and implications. Their report formed the basis of a Service Delivery Project, which further evaluated 14 of the 25 opportunities, and provided recommended plans of action in the areas of Procurement, Legal Services, and Attendance Support and Wellness. Over this past summer, Deloitte has been instructed to revisit their 2012 report and to refresh opportunity benefits and costs. I am pleased to share with you that this work has been completed and is available on the Ministry’s website at: http://www.bced.gov.bc.ca/deloitte_report_july_2014.pdf This letter signals the beginning of Phase 2 of the Service Delivery Project. It is focused on evaluating and supporting implementation of another group of service delivery opportunities in our sector, along with ongoing support for those launched in Phase 1. This project will be led by a new Shared Services Branch of our Ministry, created to engage and support the sector in this work. This will ensure the project receives the perspective of trustees and representatives from the BC School Trustees Association (BCSTA), who will be a vital part of the Education Partners Advisory Committee, which provides advice and input from our partners throughout the project.
