CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Ms. Kelly Poystila spoke to the Board of Education about the City of Nanaimo’s plan to locate two low barrier housing facilities near the school. The sites are within 100-200 metres of the school. Parents said that they are concerned about the possibility the housing projects will result in increased vandalism and thefts; more active drug related transactions and drug paraphernalia; and outbreak of communicable disease such as tuberculosis. They also said they were concerned about an increase of pedestrian traffic across the school field and safe walking routes for students. The parents asked for a 500 metre buffer zone around all schools in regards to the location of facilities such as low-barrier housing.
