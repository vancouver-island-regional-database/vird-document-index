TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Bill Robinson Zone Name: John Barsby Community  As I’m sure you have heard, we had our Reconciliation Celebration at LSS last Wednesday. It was a huge success. Now Ted Cadwallader will take the lead on implementation supported by the Reconciliation Committee and especially our five Indigenous Knowledge Keepers who, as you know, are a key part of that committee.  I attended the NDTA ProD at Dover and the ProD at Gabriola on the 26th. The Gabriola teachers opened the ProD to the community, and more than 70 community members attended. I will have a verbal report on both ProDs at February’s board meeting.  Best Me I Can Be – I was hoping to have more teachers committed to this program, but we only have 15 plus Miranda Chitze and Marilyn Maychak, our Indigenous coordinators are working with Ted Cadwallader and me on an Indigenous version.  We also have Chris Paugh and Shaun Weighill, two long-time Grade 6 and 3/4 teachers at Seaview and Linsey Grander and Lisa Blachuras Grade 1-2 teachers at Rock City doing a video addition to the program. Joan Brown one or our indigenous Knowledge Keepers told me that in their culture when you are taught something you have a responsibility to use that teaching for good. Linsey, Lisa, Chris, and Shaun are going to work with their students to help them see how they can use their strengths identified on their certificates to make a positive difference. They will then shoot a video of the students holding their certificates and talking about what they will do. These videos will be posted on our SD68 YouTube channel. Pretty exciting stuff!!  I’m looking forward to meeting with Jane Reynolds, our new Principal at Barsby sometime this week as Carrie McVeigh and I have committed to touring my schools together.  Sue Schlitz, an amazing Chase River teacher, has been coordinating Jump Rope for Heart for twelve years or more. This year she raised more than $6000 from that small community. That puts her total to $92000. $100000 is in now in her sights!!!!! On November 4th she’ll be taking her kids to Kiwanis Lodge to entertain the seniors there.  Bayview recently announced some extremely exciting news. During the first semester, Adam Manson has been teaching a regular Hul’qumi’num class to students in Grades 4 to 7 with exciting, you might even say amazing results. Arrangements have now been made for Adam to continue teaching this program during the second term and to add an additional elective Hul’qumi’num Class that will run each Wednesdays from 1:20pm-2:37 pm. This class will be for students who have shown an exceptional passion and commitment to learning more about the language and culture. I can’t wait to write my June report to the board. I know we will have some extraordinary results to report.
