Cedar - Yellowpoint - North Oyster - Cassidy - South Wellington Community Plans & Events, & Canada 150 Our Heritage Lands, Our Communities, ...& a Project for 2017 Spring 2016 To: Our Community Friends, Neighbours and Organizations... We're looking for partners who would like to help with Canada 150 initiatives that serve the communities of Cedar, Yellowpoint, North Oyster, Cassidy and South Wellington. You already help our communities, and give a lot of support and services to all of us who live here - and we thank you for this. We'd like to go a step further though, and partner with you for 2017. Just over a year ago we began outreach, holding public meetings as well as dropping off a letter to local business and industry in the region. We wanted to introduce ourselves, and provide an outline of our proposal for a Heritage Lands project for Canada's 150th anniversary of Confederation. We have been talking with individuals and local organizations ever since, and agreed to three initiatives. For 2017 we'd like to remember our past, and those who worked hard over many years to give us a beautiful countryside and coast to raise children and families. We'd like to leave a modest but lasting benefit by way of rest stops, benches and places to chat. And we're looking forward to a 'Heritage Sports Day' and a chance to celebrate the way the sports were played back in the days, with an old-tyme family picnic day to celebrate. We are then reaching out to individuals, local organizations and community groups, inviting partnership in this 'Heritage Lands' project. As the spring gets underway we'll be getting the plans and budget in shape, looking at in-kind contributions and funding options. Over the summer we expect to start purchasing supplies, and finalizing designs and dates ...and begin to build signs, benches and gardens. The new year will find us putting final touches in place, confirming heritage sports events and community celebrations. As residents and members of community organizations we want you to know that we value your input, and encourage your involvement as we move ahead. This is a project for the community, and we'd like to share the work, credit and ownership with one and all. We are therefore asking for your response, your suggestions, and the chance to meet with you or your organization. We'd like to discuss the three initiatives, your suggestions, and options that might be available to partner on 2017 Heritage projects. As you can please feel free to be in touch. We're available by phone or e-mail ...and we've also attached our Heritage Lands newsletter which we hope you'll find of interest. best wishes, Laurie Gourlay President, VICCS Vancouver Island & Coast Conservation Society P.O.
