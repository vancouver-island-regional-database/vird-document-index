EXECUTIVE SUMMARY – Report to the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Board of Education re: Analysis of District Level Staffing Purpose: The purpose of the report is to provide a factual overview of allocations and spending for district based administration (i.e. exempt staff only) between SD68 (Nanaimo – Ladysmith) and eight other school districts. The Comparator School Districts: All of the selected comparator school districts were chosen in consultation with the senior staff and board chair of SD68. They are: SD 23 (Central Okanagan) SD37 (Delta) SD44 (North Vancouver) SD73 (Kamloops – Thompson) SD42 (Maple Ridge – Pitt Meadows) SD68 (Nanaimo – Ladysmith) SD33 (Chilliwack) SD57 (Prince George) SD63 (Saanich) 21,763 15,710 15,262 14,101 13,852 13,177 12,978 12,806 7,317 FTE students Six of the comparator school districts were chosen because of their similar student population (FTE count) to the Nanaimo – Ladysmith School District, Central Okanagan was included as a significantly larger comparator, while Saanich was included as a significantly smaller comparator. As school district budgets are primarily connected to provincial funding based on student registrations, district populations also provide a general indication of similar yearly budgets. The report makes no value judgements about the chosen spending allocations made by each school district; nor their chosen organizational structure. Local circumstances and decision making including program offerings, the division of duties between exempt and unionized staff, board policy decisions, and historical perspectives may make differing organizational structures or spending levels of equal value to each district. This report does not identify or represent one model / school district as being superior to any other. A strong and purposeful senior leadership team can be one of school district’s strongest assets both in terms of improving student outcomes and establishing high quality management functions. Apparent differences in organizational structure between school districts may have more to do with terminology than actual differences in organizational structure or budget allocations. District leadership duties undertaken as part of an assigned position reporting directly to the superintendent may be titled as any one of the following: Associate Superintendent, Assistant Superintendent, or Director. It cannot be assumed that a position titled Associate Superintendent has significantly more responsibility or a higher level of pay than a Director’s position in another school district. Likewise, the Assistant Superintendent position in one district may have a considerably larger scope of responsibilities and duties than in another. As a general rule, however, the greater the number of exempt staff positions in a school district, the more likely there is specialization of duties within mid and lower level positions. Understanding and Interpreting the Findings: In general terms, the amount of money spent by a school district on senior administration is reflected in the following indicators: - Staff compensation is the single largest component of spending on district administration, although other small allocations such as trustee stipends do form part of the calculation. - The larger the student population of a school district, the more it spends in terms of total dollars on district administration. - The larger the student population of a school district, the less it spends in terms of a percentage of its total budget on district administration. - The more rural a school district (most often reflected in a greater number of schools), the more it spends on district administration both in terms of total dollars and percent of overall budget (even though school based administrators are not included in this calculation). It is also important to note that school district spending on administration covers a number of items in addition to just compensation paid to senior district exempt staff. For example, you will note significant differences in the reported overall spending on district administration by the Nanaimo – Ladysmith school district ($4,965,326) versus the amount spent on Other Professional Salaries ($3,427,759). The difference between the two figures accounts for spending on board governance, support salaries and other expenses connected to the overall cost of the administering the school district. The Summary of Comparator Data: The following three charts summarize the findings of the comparator data review process. Chart number 1 shows Reported 2015/2016 Summary of Senior District Positions by FTE by comparator school district for the 2015/2016 school year. This is the reported number of senior district positions in each school district by FTE. Chart number 2 shows Actual Operating Expenses by Object – Other Professionals Salaries by comparator school district as reported in the 2014/2015 school year. At the time of the writing of the original report, figures for the 2015/2016 school year were not yet available. This chart details the reported amount of money spent by each school district on professional staff salaries. Chart number 3 shows each school district’s Actual Operating Expenses by Function as reported for the 2014/2015 school year. At the time of the writing of the original report, figures for the 2015/2016 school year were not yet available. This chart shows the amount and percentage of each school district’s operating budget spent on administration. Although the full report provides a much more fulsome review of the data, basic conclusion statements regarding the comparator data and the Nanaimo-Ladysmith school district are included under each of the charts in this executive summary. It is recommended that the entire report be reviewed in detail in order to full understand and interpret the comparator data provided. Please contact the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District Office of the Superintendent for further information or clarifications.
