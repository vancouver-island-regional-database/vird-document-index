CAREER & TECHNICAL CENTRE EDUCATION BOARD PRESENTATION JANUARY 6TH, 2021 PANDEMIC EFFECT ON STAFF • Working from home was not a big departure with CTC as we were already set up for online applications and remote communication. • Adjusted to doing TEAMS meeting in place of in person • Will continue post pandemic • More efficient, convenient, less cost. (instant connection with students and counsellors) • Do miss the one-off conversations that come up during in person visits PANDEMIC EFFECT BY PROGRAM • Trades Sampler • Normally run two a year. One Mixed, One Girls Only • Pre-Pandemic Plan was to add an Aboriginal Sampler • Due to the COVID Safety plan and space allocation all were cancelled for 2020-2021 • Students were rescheduled into classes in September • Train In Trades • Shifted to up to 50% online • Front End loaded text-based portion (Up to 2 ½ months) PANDEMIC EFFECT BY PROGRAM • Switched to distance learning • Some students struggled to adapt • Recruitment is going well for 2021-22 • Work In Trades • Reduction in opportunity for students in some areas like culinary and hairstylist. • CTC changes roles and responsibilities • Numbers should be the same or increase by the years end • Switch to virtual inspections, teams/zoom calls with employers and e transferring paperwork PANDEMIC EFFECT BY PROGRAM • Enrichment • VIU shifted to 100% online • Increases accessibility for students • Decreased contact • Overall increase in enrolment • Changing entry requirements so more students can take advantage • Elementary Trades • Just starting to deliver • Put in safety guidelines and changed delivery method • Same robust content delivery • Will visit less schools this year CTC RESPONSE • Searching out new ways to support students • Building a new suite of recruitment tools • Working with partners comparing evidence for best practice moving forward • Preparing students for permanent change in practice WHAT IS NEXT? • Building better communication tools to connect with; students, parents and NLSP staff • Creating career cluster groupings to give students clearer options for life after high school • Organizing certification camps to add to students' skills sets to increase their value to employers • Redesigning our elementary trades projects to include both Aboriginal and Non-Aboriginal design to build something new. • Shifting from a delivery to support model for Elementary trades • Tool Carts • Pro D for Teachers QUESTIONS?
