BUDGET: Expected revenue for 2018 – 2019 is approximately $330,000. Of those funds the greatest share, $200,000, is budgeted to address the individual professional development needs of the district’s approximately 1000 teachers and teachers teaching on call. These funds are reimbursed to teachers in accordance with Policy and the Principles of Professional Development. A Statement of Disbursements dated January 31, 2019 is included with this report. The middle column reflects revenue and expenditures to January 31. Individual Teachers’ Professional Development Funds: $200,000 2018-19 is the first year of the three-year cycle for individual teachers’ funds. The NDTA believes that this model, in effect for nine years now, has made the distribution of professional development funds more equitable and accessible to more teachers. It also allows teachers to plan ahead and attend more expensive opportunities that would not be possible with only a single year’s allocation. This year, teachers are able to access Year 1 and 2 of the funding cycle. This amounts to $700 this year: $400 in the first year and $300 for the second year (pro-rated to FTE). Teachers Teaching on Call (TTOCs) have access to $300 in individual funds annually but are not able to bank their funds for future use. As such, the majority of individual professional development funds went toward supporting members to attend Provincial Specialist Association conferences, however funds were also used to attend a variety of other conferences, and to purchase professional resources. For a variety of reasons, we recognize that it is not always possible for our members to travel out of district for Provincial Specialist conferences. Local Facilitators’ Account: $15,000 When local teachers facilitate workshops and presentations in the district they are awarded a sum in their local facilitator’s account to cover the cost of a TTOC or access their own professional development opportunities so that they can make up the missed opportunity for their own professional development by providing professional development. District Day $60 000 This year’s District Day theme is Trauma Informed Practice. More than 30 facilitators are offering over 40 sessions on a wide variety of topics: Sharing circles, mindfulness, meditation for kids and teens, selfcare, PTSD in the classroom, Adverse Childhood Experiences, FASD, Sexual Health, trauma and refugees, coding, connecting with students through conferencing, and social justice. We have three keynote speakers, Denise Manderson who is a program director for the Hull School in Calgary will be speaking on the implementation of trauma informed care in a school setting. Angela Low is from the Dalai Lama Centre and will be speaking on Heart-Mind Well-Being. Our final keynote, Syd Bossel who was voted “BC’s Funniest New Female Comic” is an FASD Keyworker and foster parent support worker who has worked with the Dalai Lama Centre and the Neufeld Institute. She will be ending our day with a comedy performance. Supplemental Release: $45,000 Subject to available funds, the Professional Development Policy allows all teachers access to one TTOC released day in order to attend professional development activities on days they have regularly scheduled duties.
