OVERVIEW of Professional Development Activities from February 01, 2013 to June 30, 2013 District Pro-D Day February 25, 2013 – Connect 2 Learn Our District Day extended the frame and format of the past year’s Connect to Learn and followed through with the areas of interest identified by teachers in the Harvest of Ideas that support Pro-D planning for the past two years. Please refer to the attached Harvest of Ideas document for reference. The day included four Canadian keynote presenters, Dean Shareski, Adele Diamond, Lorna Williams, Tom Schimmer, who each hosted separate break-out learning sessions following the keynote addresses. The afternoon offered teachers a wide selection of workshop sessions and opportunities to pursue collaborative learning with their professional learning groups. Our overall theme of connecting to learn continued to extend our pursuit of building community (welcoming, bridging gaps, collaborating) and expanding our toolkits with strategies that work (inquiry, instructional strategies, mentoring, working together) through research based professional learning. Our keynote presenters and afternoon workshop sessions focused on the following sub-themes: Technology: baseline tools, ethics/safety, effective use of technology in classrooms Engagement : encouraging and connecting learners and community Meeting Diverse Needs: collaboration to support our learners, strategies to support all Assessment: assessment for learning, student ownership, peer and self-assessment Participant responses to District Day learning opportunities were very positive. As a result of considering feedback of the day, we have determined to reduce the number of keynotes to two in the upcoming year and offer a selection of full-day workshops in addition to ½ day workshops to enable additional in depth learning. We will also work on remedying logistical elements such as increasing the heat, turning off the bells and ensuring that the wireless internet connection is fully functioning. Topic Learning in Depth (Kieran Egan) The Daily 5 & The Café Book Secondary Math Calm Alert and Learning Learning Group Highlights This study brought the staff, students and community together to celebrate learning for learning sake through personalized inquiry. As the second year of whole staff exploration of Kieran Egan’s imaginative learning theory, we deepened our understanding through Pro-D workshops and reading materials. The LiD program enabled staff to extend our math focused inquiry centered on our school goal into a cooperative examination of ways to support interdisciplinary individualised learning. We will be continuing to build upon this. Our group met several times to discuss the book and to share ideas about how to implement the ideas and strategies presented in the book. Several of our staff members visited Cobble Hill school in Cowichan to observe 3 classrooms that are currently using the Daily Five strategies. Our activities included: 1. A math book review and exchange: we brought in and discussed our favourite math books, and shared them amongst ourselves. 2. We designed a word problem based on Dan Myer’s movie problem idea, with three acts and the students doing most of the research. 3. We facilitated a discussion at the District Pro-d day around senior math classes. 4. We made math clocks, by replacing the face of an ordinary wall clock with math-themed faces. 5. We shared resources for the new Math 12 classes. 6. We celebrated the retirement of founding member Brian McRory. Staff broke their discussion and classroom implementation of strategies into separate meetings to discuss: The Biological, Emotional and Cognitive Domains, The Social and Prosocial Domain, Self-Regulation and Special Education and SelfRegulation and Children’s Mental. Staff agreed that most Shanker’s suggestions and ideas were quite focused towards the elementary school classroom, but discussed a number of modifications that could be done to make them successful for high school students that have troubles self-regulating. The Daily 5 Many of our group members found the strategies to be helpful in our teaching practice and we plan to continue using them next year. One of our goals over the past few years is to have a consistent approach to reading instruction throughout the school for every grade level, as we believe that the students will become stronger readers when they are familiar with a common language, learned routines, and clear expectations based on best practice. The Daily 5 – Each month we read a chapter and shared our experiences implementing it in our Fostering Literacy classrooms. We discussed our successes and also challenges with this program in Independence a French Immersion setting. Enhancing RTI Inquiry Question re Vulnerable Students Some found it hard to connect with the book because daily needs and concerns tend to do with behavior rather curriculum content. Several questioned the need for more time suggesting that if assessment and program monitoring are to be key components of RTI there will have to be increased time allocated for this to be effectively carried out. In addition, the book study reaffirmed what teachers are doing in their classrooms as well as reminding and informing practices. We engaged in quality professional dialogue that lead each of us to ‘ah ha’ moments that will change our practice forever! Together, we outlined the steps of Inquiry: creating the question, setting learning outcomes, designing assessment for learning strategies, listing skills and tools and processes for successful learning, sharing criteria, sharing graphic organizers, and sharing resources and created both a digital and printed version of a ‘manual’ with outlines, explanations and examples that we can continue to develop and share. Conversations That Move Learning Forward This 5-part series, facilitated by Nancy Hinds of Hindsightco Educational Consulting, built upon the past year’s 3-part series and the existing professional learning culture of Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools which supports inclusive, self-directed learning communities. The overarching purpose of this series continued to be enhancing participants’ ability to host and engage in powerful learning conversations in schools that encourage inquiry and action related to teaching practice and student learning. The series focused on the following three goals: 1) to continue building our understanding of professional conversations 2) to increase our capacity to facilitate effective collaborative groups 3) to learn, practice and apply specific protocols (i.e. structured processes) for facilitating conversations that move learning forward June 30, 2013 Page 5 of 7 The learning series consisted of five afternoon working sessions, three of which took place during school and two of which took place in the afternoon. Response to the series was very positive. In particular, participants emphasised that they would be able to apply what they had learned with their students as well as with their colleagues. One of the most valuable aspects of participation was that we engaged in learning about how to facilitate conversations while engaging in powerful learning focused facilitated conversations about educational topics of specific significance to our professional responsibilities. At the conclusion of the series, it was agreed that some participants will be willing to put their names forward to facilitate learning conversations at schools across the district and at future professional learning opportunities put on by the NDTA. A facilitator list will be compiled in the new school year, shared with Pro-D Reps and posted on our website. Program for Quality Teaching (PQT) Teacher Inquiry We were fortunate to be supported by both the school district and the BCTF to engage in a teacher inquiry on the topic of mentorship in our district. The overarching purpose of this inquiry was to support the growth of teachers' professional learning through exploring structures that will engage, motivate and strengthen professional learning partnerships to nurture teachers who are new to the profession, teachers who are new to their roles and teachers who are implementing new significant changes to their teaching practices. Ultimately, this initiative sought to answer: How can we work together to build capacity in all our teachers by expanding our understanding of best practices for mentorship through professional learning partnerships? Through this inquiry, new and new to teachers (mentees) were supported by experienced teachers (mentees) to explore a personal inquiry question. These questions were pedagogically focused on a particular element of their teaching role. The mentees were supported to develop their area of focus for mentoring and their role as mentees through use of the book, The Mentees Guide: Making mentoring work for you by Lois J. Zachary and Lory A. Fischler. In addition, the mentees were guided though the BCTF Teacher Inquiry process by BCTF facilitator, Mary Filleul through five half day working sessions. The mentor teachers were supported to develop their understanding of the mentoring process through three meetings for sharing reflections and studying the book, The Mentors Guide: Facilitating effective learning relationships by Lois Fischer. This inquiry identified the following areas of need for further support for mentoring in our district: a) Mentorship training for mentors b) Knowledge of and access to the mentoring program for mentees c) Focus on School Support Teacher needs d) Collaborative learning partnerships to grow innovation and inquiry The Professional Development Committee has committed to further exploration of ways to support effective mentoring in the upcoming year including: a) Providing a resource library at the NDTA for mentors and mentees to access b) Developing a part of the Pro-D website with mentor and mentoring information for mentees c) Focused and facilitated professional development opportunities in after school settings d) Exploring alternative ways to strategically support mentoring relationships in addition to the existing program for new teachers through working cooperatively with VIU to integrate student teachers into a mentoring cohorts model and offering Professional Learning Partnership release funding June 30, 2013 Page 6 of 7 Joint NDTA – Board Pro-D Advisory Committee The Joint Pro-D Advisory Committee met to discuss the direction of Professional Development planning and to put forward a proposal for the new Pro-D Calendar. The following changes were made to the Calendar to support our teachers: • School-based days were shifted to earlier in the year to increase application of learned practices • May 5th was selected to enable collaboration with SD69 (Qualicum) • Elementary and secondary schools no longer have the final day on separate days • The number of days in session in both terms are balanced for secondary teachers The following is a summary of the interests discussed by Joint Pro-D Committee members. The District proposed consideration of the following instructional practices to support the Response to Intervention (RTI) model to enhance instruction and assessment practices: • Balanced Literacy Framework at the primary, intermediate and secondary level • Real-world learning experiences e.g., inquiry-based learning, project or problem-based learning, work/apprenticeship/trades experiences • Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and differentiated instruction • Aboriginal understanding • Assessment for, or, and as learning The Pro-D Committee reviewed these considerations and observed the alignment of these interests with the Harvest of Ideas solicited from teachers in 2011 (please see attached document). Planning for future Professional Development opportunities will take all of these interests into account.
