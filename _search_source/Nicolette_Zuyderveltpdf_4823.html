April 7, 2011 To whom it may concern, I am writing this letter on behalf of Liz Longmore, the child and youth care worker at Mountain View Elementary School, but also on behalf of all the Child and Youth care workers in the school district. It has been brought to my attention that as of September 2011 all child and youth care worker positions in elementary schools in SD 68 will be cut. This is a tragedy and most definitely not in the best interest of our students, especially those who need their support the most. I have had the honour of working with many child and youth care workers through out my career and have seen the difference they make in our students lives. The one on one, family, and colleague support and the group workshops they provide for students are impactful, cannot be done without, and cannot be replaced. Throughout this school year I have worked very closely with Liz as I had several students in my class with behaviour IEP’s. I saw first hand the profound difference she made in their lives. She was there for these students for support, guidance, and a safe and kind person for them to talk to. She also taught them very valuable coping, social and friendship skills. There is one student in particular who was unable to cope in a classroom setting for longer than an hour at the beginning of the year. Thankfully Liz was there for her to go to, to talk to and to learn very valuable coping skills. Liz created strategies for her to use in the classroom setting, on the playground and outside of school. Liz was also very instrumental with working with her family, getting them in touch with mental health professionals outside of the school, and getting her the additional help she so badly needed. Liz attended many meetings with myself, our principal, and later the outside mental health professionals that worked with the student. The significant difference Liz made in this students life is hard to put into words, as the difference has been so profound. She is now able to attend class for days at a time without breaking down, she is learning to talk about her problems and resolve issues in a positive and non-violent way, academically she is thriving and she is starting to create positive friendships with other students. I truly believe this is largely due to Liz, her incredible professional skills, her kind and supportive personality, and her dedication to the students, families, staff and her profession. Liz was also there for students and families who were experiencing loss due to divorces and parent separations and had a tremendous impact on their lives and all they had to cope with. Liz has also ran a Girl Talk group for our grade 5 girls ~ this was a wonderful group for the girls as they are beginning to experience so many changes in their lives. Liz was also an incredibly resource for me, any time I had questions regarding some very difficult behaviours I was working so hard on remedying she was there with a shoulder for support and very beneficial advice. Words cannot begin to express the impact Liz has had on the students’ lives and the positive ripple effect this creates throughout the whole school. These are only a few examples of all Liz does for students, families and fellow colleagues. I can honestly tell you that our school would not be the same without her there, and cannot ever begin to fathom what my students’ lives would be like if it were not for her support, guidance, and professional expertise. Child and youth care workers are irreplaceable professional resources for children’s emotional and personal well-being. Over the years and the all of the cutbacks their availability in the community is virtually non-existent and schools are the only setting in which children are able to seek out and benefit from their professional abilities. In a time when students have already been so impacted by all the cutbacks, removing child and youth care workers from the school would have a very negative and devastating impact on many students and the ripple effect of that would transcend throughout the school system and the community. I urge the board not to participate in the extinction of these incredibly valuable professionals who make such a tremendous difference in the lives of children, families, schools, and communities. Sincerely, Ms. Nicolette Zuydervelt Teacher
