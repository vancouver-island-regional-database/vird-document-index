Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: September 16, 2015 FROM: Dale Burgos, Director of Communications SUBJECT: Live streaming – Board meetings Background: On November 26, 2014, the Board of Education requested that staff in the Communications Department develop a plan for streaming communication for school Board meetings. Information: The current Boardroom configuration can only record audio. These recordings are kept for a limited time and can be requested by the public. Streaming Board meetings will provide 24-7 access to past Board meetings which will prove beneficial for those in the community that cannot attend the meetings. We have two options: 1. Continue to record the audio and post it online (YouTube) post meeting. 2. Live stream the Board meetings as it happens. Audio-only solution: There will be no cost to recording the audio and posting it online. The disadvantage would be that it is not live. Live streaming solution: Anyone who cannot attend the Board meetings can watch it live. Should the Board decide to use this option, I am prepared to create a means whereby those watching online can provide questions for question period (via social media). Live streaming will also require hardware and software upgrades:      Two High Definition (HD) cameras Audio solution Dedicated computer Encoding software/hardware A reliable network with sustainable bandwidth Live streaming requirements: Video quality and minimum threshold settings A given video resolution is dictated by the bit rate allowed by your outbound internet connection. To stream a video of 480p to YouTube (or any service), the district should have a recommended upload speed of 1.5Mbps (megabits per second). The district runs two gigabits per second in the Board office. 720p and above is High Definition (1080p is full HD) and will look great on every device, but it will require a computer with larger resources to encode the video. That said, if the content is mainly a group of people meeting around a table, the visuals may be less important than the sound quality. Live streaming encoder settings Always encode on a dedicated machine with a fast dual core or greater CPU. Having a hardwired internet connection with no less than 1.5Mbps of consistent upstream bandwidth will ensure optimal streaming performance. Wireless connectivity would most certainly affect latency and video quality. Dedicated computer specifications for streaming live broadcasts HD video streams require systems with faster computing and memory resources. Regardless of whether HD is used or not, the system running the streams should use dedicated video cards for optimal performance. The computer should not be used for anything but the stream and encoding. This will ensure optimal quality and performance of the stream throughout the broadcast.
