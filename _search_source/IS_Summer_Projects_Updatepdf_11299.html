NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: September 13, 2023 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Pete Sabo, Executive Director of Planning and Operation Facilities Summer Work Background: Facilities utilizes the summer break to extend maintenance on systems which otherwise would disrupt the operation of the schools. Further, projects (major, minor, local capital, AFG and school funded) are advanced, as above, taking advantage of the summer breaks. This year this work, as in the past, continued. Further to physical work being performed on sites, departments use the quieter time (relatively speaking) to take vacations, refresh and further catch up on departmental and district level projects and planning. Discussion: Some projects are mentioned under various departments, these items signify individual departmental ownership or leadership in parts of the same project. For example, staff continue to grow the recently formed Capital Implementation (CI) Department, and this group of staff work to construct projects which may have typically been tendered to local construction companies. This has assisted the district with sourcing work at a time when contractor shortage and higher prices have been evident. Having an expanded in-house service allows the district to advance projects quicker while utilizing project staff (CUPE trade and planning) to provide the bulk of the project. While NLPS has always had project staff (additional to the operating budget), the formation of this department was made possible by the 10 Childcare project approvals (comprising of 18 modular units). Capital implementation construct the modular units mainly with in house trades at both the Woodlands site and directly on the receiving school site. This allows better construction phasing and continues the work on the inclusion of students in the construction. Further, the department has used these daycare projects as a springboard to fully form the department and take on additional projects. This work will also allow maintenance to focus on maintenance activities vs both maintenance and Capital. The team model continues, with four schools moving to a single custodian to reduce travel times and distance. Operations worked closely with the carpentry team and City of Nanaimo to install electronic locks on the washrooms at the Rotary Bowl. This will allow students using the portable classrooms card access to the facilities during school hours. Construction at Ecole North Oyster and Mountain View Elementary prevented full clean completion. The new wing at Ecole Hammond Bay was ready for the start of school. Janitorial support for Capital and Operational priorities Half of the district’s gym floors were refinished after careful consideration and site visits from our gym floor vendors. This is a reduction from the full district in the past. The department has adopted a schedule of refinishing every other year, aligning our district with others on the island, halving cost, and opening the possibility of gym rentals during the summer months for community groups. The janitorial supplies distribution contract was tendered and awarded. Five distributors submitted bids with the incumbent, the awarded contact was to the existing vendor with reduced prices and a plan to improve service. The Operations team changed all district filters. The filter change process has markedly improved since appointing a team with a consistent leader to the process, reducing unnecessary spending and improving tracking to ensure 100% compliance. Additionally, the 2023/24 filter order was put out to tender. The next year will see a savings of approximately $6,000 while maintaining MERV 13 filters.
