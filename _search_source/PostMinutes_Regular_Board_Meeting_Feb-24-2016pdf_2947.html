CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Charlene McKay President, DPAC RE: DPAC Update Ms. Charlene McKay presented to the Board some of the opportunities that DPAC offers schools with the funding they receive. They are able to sponsor events at no charge, such as; a presentation on safer schools from an RCMP member from Victoria, a Kerri Isham workshop, You Are Your Child's Advocate (building positive relationships with school administrators) and a Coffee and Learning series on the PAC role in schools. Also, admission was offered to 35 parents to attend an anti-bullying presentation Barbara Coloroso, which was extremely well attended. There were 15 parents who attended the Vancouver Island Parent Conference in Victoria and 20 parents who attended by BCCPAC Conference. Ms. McKay believes that having parents attend the AGM is important at this level as opposed to just the district level. DPAC thanked the Board for allowing their voice to be heard and supporting the various initiatives DPAC has ongoing in the community. Trustee Kimler: LIS - Grade 7 Leadership Ambassadors, responsible for organizing events, put on a Grade 6/7 Valentine’s Day dance, Red & Pink day and “3-days of Friendship” during February. In March, they are planning a Go-Green week, where the school will do its best to be eco-friendly for an entire week. The Ladysmith Fraternal Order of Eagles donated $700 to the LIS PAC. The money goes to help fund activities throughout the year. LPS - held a New Curriculum information session for parents and community members which provided an interactive way to share key elements, guiding principles, timelines & examples of where learning is heading. A principal transfer announcement was made as Mr. Kevin Brand will be moving to Bayview Elementary school and Mr. Bob Brooks, currently Vice Principal at NDSS has been appointed as LPS principal. The transfers will occur in August 2016. LPS was featured in the Winter edition of ConnectED, with an article focusing on the first year of their four-year PAC-funded technology enhancement plan. ENO hosed a Family Valentine's Day Dance on Friday, Feb 12th. Approximately 200 people attended and some even won prizes! ENO is one of many SD68 schools participating in the Maple Sugar Festival, as a French cultural activity/fieldtrip. The public festival is this weekend Feb 26-28 at Beban Auditorium. LSS - On February 5th & 6th 24 members of our Junior and Senior Improv teams competed in the Canadian Improv Games in Courtney. Our Junior team placed 2nd and our Senior team placed 6th. Our cultural art project, Nutsumaat sayaays is well underway. This month master carver, John Marston, our LSS staff and LSS students prepared the big house facade that will be used as the backdrop for carving projects. During the lashing of the logs, LSS hosted grade 9 students from the Stzuminus Community School. Ms. Dolen and Mr. Taylor are creating a video of the art project, once it is complete we will be sharing it with the community. In the meantime, we will be including photos in our Newsletter.
