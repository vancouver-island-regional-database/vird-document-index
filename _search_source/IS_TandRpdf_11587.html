NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 6, 2023 Education Committee Director of Instruction, Indigenous Learning – Ricki Bartlett Truth and Reconciliation Board Goal: Truth and Reconciliation District Operational Plan Objectives: - Implement Truth and Reconciliation Commission Calls to Action #57 - Support innovative Indigenous educational and community partnerships - Implementation of the Syeyutsus Reconciliation Framework in the school community Strategies: - Foster opportunities for school communities to learn about the local land, language and culture - Creating structures strategies to decolonize the system - Bridging relationships with rights holders and Indigenous partners with our school teams Background The Department of Learning Services is proud of the collective ownership NLPS continues to build around Truth and Reconciliation. As a district, we have come so far in deepening our knowledge and understanding of the truth of Canada’s history. Being part of a system that has hurt Indigenous people for the past 150 years, we are committed to creating systems, structures and environments of equity – where Indigenous students and families feel a true sense of belonging in the school setting, and strong connections to the people within them. The objectives and strategies outlined above are what keep us connected to and focused on accomplishing the goals of the Board, and the objectives within the Superintendent’s Operational Plan. Information Sheet: Truth and Reconciliation Page 2 Overview Nacu mat Tatulut Supporting all the District’s operational plan objectives, Nacu mat Tatulut is a joint professional learning committee with various stakeholders committed to the learning of students within NLPS. Together with our Syeyutsus Knowledge Keepers, Nacu mat Tatulut is working to further deepen understandings of the Syeyutsus principles with the intention to create our own version of the local Coast Salish Principals of Learning document. This guiding document will act as a tool for EFP teachers and all teachers in classrooms across the District, to bring local Indigenous culture and values into teaching and learning. Learning with Syeyutsus Speaker Series is an Indigenous learning series featuring Canadian authors and thought leaders. In response to the board’s goal of Truth & Reconciliation and Canada’s TRC Call to Action #57, we have curated a speaker series over the past few years that has covered a range of topics including true histories, pedagogies, self-determination, land resurgence, and stories to spark ideas for further exploration and deeper learning on your own, with your family and/or your work colleagues. This year we are kicking off the final season with returning guest, Umeek, Dr. Richard Atleo. We will be filming the session at Wildwood Ecoforest on the traditional territory of the Stz’uminus First Nation. Indigenous students from across the District have been taught Nuuchahnulth and Coast Salish songs and will be welcoming Umeek and has family with song and traditional protocols that they have been taught leading up to this day, supported by Knowledge Keepers, Tsumquatun and George Seymour. Tsumquatun and students have been extending traditional, inperson invites to guests for the day. After singing and protocols, students will be witnesses to the filming of Umeek’s teachings from Wildwood. Following this will be a feast of traditional foods (clams, fish, etc) prepared by the students and Auntie Shirley and her family, of Snaw-Naw-As First Nation. Tune in live on January 10 at 12:00pm! https://trc57speakerseries.ca/ Information Sheet: Truth and Reconciliation Page 3 Transformative Reconciliation Curriculum Implementation Day is a Professional Development Day focused on Truth and Reconciliation. The day is hosted by the Department of Learning Service. Last year we had Dr. Dustin Louie, Director of the Indigenous Teachers Education Program at UBC, come to speak about his research on Transformative Reconciliation. We were excited to hear the feedback that Dr. Louie’s research and presentation on decolonized approaches to teaching and leading was so well received by the system. Since then we have been working with Dr. Louie on bringing his work into the District on a more intimate and intentional level. Currently he is working with our Secondary Principals and VicePrincipals, as well as the Learning Leader Teams in each school. This work will span over the year and include schools creating Transformative Reconciliation Action Plans to help lead the work of decolonizing school environments and classroom practice. Syeyutsus Dinner Gatherings Over the past few years we have been hosting Syeyutsus Dinner Gatherings for various school and district staff to support the board’s goal of Truth and Reconciliation. The dinners have hosted Principals and Vice-Principals, Senior and District Leaders, and English First People’s teachers. This year in the spirit of the work that Dr. Louie is doing with secondary district learning leader teams, the Syeyutsus Family will host this group of teachers so that they have an opportunity to learn the Syeyutsus teachings from our Knowledge Keepers. The conversations that take place in this sacred circle further promote the implementation of the Syeyutsus Reconciliation Framework into the school communities.
