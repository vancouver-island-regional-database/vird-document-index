Department of Facilities Update - Testing for Lead Content in Drinking Water November 13, 2019 Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Background Timeline as follows: o February 24, 2016 Ministry of Education directed all School Districts in British Columbia to begin a program of testing for lead content in all facilities built prior to 1990 to be completed over 3 years o Testing and remediation of 1/3 of the schools (facilities built prior to 1990) completed April 2017 o Testing and Remediation of 2/3 of the schools (facilities built prior to 1990) completed March 31, 2018 o Following Board directive to complete all Lead in Water remediation works 1 year early, testing and remediation of final 1/3 of schools (facilities built prior to 1990) completed September 1, 2018 o Maintenance phase to confirm remediation works begins Fall 2018 Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Current Program March 2019, Health Canada revises the Guidelines for Canadian Drinking Water Quality Lead levels from 0.010 to 0.005 mg/l MOE together with Island Health require re-testing of remediated facilities to ensure compliance to new standards and testing of all remaining facilities built after 1990 An emergent project was created for testing with a budget of $55,000 Testing has been taking place since June with 485 samples collected 13 facilities require further testing (remain to be tested) – expected completion date March 31, 2020 (Facilities with Autoflushers currently installed) Testing for Lead in Drinking Water Next steps All remediation process’s remain in place including automatic flush valves and signage to run drinking water sources for 2 minutes Both Environmental and Mechanical Engineer’s remediation recommendations based on the samples collected are expected by April, 2020. o Remediation works to be prioritized based on sample results o All samples and works to be forwarded to MOE and Island Health
