NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: April 10, 2024 Business Committee Laura Tait, Acting Superintendent Draft 2024-2028 Strategic Plan Recommendation The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) make the attached draft 2024-2028 Strategic Plan available to the public utilizing the consultation process as per Board Governance, Section 1, 2.17 and to report back at the Business Committee on June 12, 2024. Background On October 25, 2023, the Board began the public consultation process for the renewal or revision of the Board’s Strategic Plan. Results of the consultation were presented on December 13, 2023. On March 27, 2024, a report was presented to the Board of Education regarding the next steps for the Board's 2024-2028 Strategic Plan (the “Plan”). This Action Sheet outlines the preliminary draft of the Plan as well as a motion for consideration. Discussion The Strategic Plan is the District’s roadmap to achieve its overarching goals and objectives. It outlines a clear path for addressing the unique challenges and needs of our students and communities. Its importance lies in several key aspects: First, it communicates the Board’s vision of education to our educational community and the public. Second, it provides a structure to support alignment of resources, making the most of limited funding and assets. Third, it establishes accountability by setting specific goals and objectives with accompanying metrics in order to track and report progress. Ultimately, it enhances transparency, engages the community, and provides a stable, long-term vision, underpinning its vital role in shaping the future of our District. The Board of Education’s Plan is attached and linked above. This Plan has been created through collaborative efforts with trustees and district staff, incorporating the feedback from our community. It should be noted that there is a thematic continuity in the goals from the previous Plan. The revised Plan places a refreshed emphasis on accountability through the inclusion of clear and quantifiable metrics that are aligned with the Ministry of Education and Child Care reporting requirements. Another matter to note, is the formal removal of the Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability goal which is not intended to reflect a lack of priority from previous the current plan, but a move toward integrating these principles into other goal areas In addition, the Board has successfully implemented an Environmental Stewardship Action Plan that along with the District’s Syeyutsus Reconciliation Framework and JEDI Way of Being are guiding principles. The public will have until May 27, 2024, to provide feedback. Appendix A: Draft 2024-2028 Strategic Plan Appendix B: March 27, 2024 Information Sheet
