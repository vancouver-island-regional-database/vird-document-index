Nanaimo Science and Sustainability Society 2018 SD68 Programs Tinkering Around –Motorized Insects: Grade 2 Concept: Students create a simple circuit to animate an 'insect' using a battery, motor and weight. Curriculum connections: Applied Design, Skills and Technologies • Make a product using known procedures or modelling others • Use trial and error to make changes, solve problems, or incorporate new ideas • Use personal preferences to evaluate the success of design solutions • Use tools and materials in a safe manner • Develop skills through play and collaborative work Fall 2018: The program was provided to all schools in the district including Gabriola with approximately 800 students participating. Feedback from students and teachers continues to be very positive. Tinkering Around (Grade 2) : Pictures Citizen Science – Intertidal Monitoring: Grade 5 Concept: Students use standard ecological sampling techniques to collect data on the abundance of invasive and native clams in Departure Bay. Data is shared with scientists at VIU. Curriculum Connections: Science • make observations in familiar and unfamiliar contexts • make predictions about the findings of their inquiry • experience and interpret the local environment • observe, measure and record data using appropriate tools • construct and use a variety of methods (graphs) to represent patterns or relationships in data • identify patterns and connections in data • Identify some of the social, ethical, and environmental implications of the findings from their own and others’ investigations Citizen Science – Intertidal Monitoring: Grade 5 (Cont’d) Spring 2018: 21 schools participated with over 520 students. We also had 18 volunteers, including scientists from DFO and local environmental consulting firms, and students from VIU. Wendy Simms shared some of her findings from student interviews demonstrating that this program provides students with insights into what it means to be a scientist. Extension activity made available through the NS3 website Citizen Science (grade 5): Pictures Trash to Treasure: Grade 6 Concept: Students participate in the clean-up of a local park, categorize garbage items and determine weights, and draw graphs describing what garbage was collected. As a class, students create a piece of art from the trash for public display; this year art work was displayed for one week at the Earth Day Celebration. Curriculum Connections: Science  Observe, measure, and record data using appropriate tools, including digital technologies  Experience and interpret the local environment  Construct and use a variety of methods, including tables, graphs, and digital technologies, as appropriate, to represent patterns or relationships in data  Identify social, ethical, and environmental implications of the findings from their own and others’ investigations  Contribute to care for self, others, and community through personal or collaborative approaches  Communicate ideas, explanations, and processes in a variety of ways  Express and reflect on personal, shared, or others’ experiences of place Trash to Treasure: Grade 6 Curriculum Connections: Math • Visualize to explore mathematical concepts • Engage in problem-solving experiences that are connected to place, and perspectives relevant to the local community • Connect mathematical concepts to each other and to other areas and personal interests Curriculum Connections: Art • Intentionally select, apply, combine, and arrange artistic elements in art making • Create artistic works collaboratively using ideas inspired by imagination, inquiry, experimentation • Explore relationships between identity, place, culture, society, and belonging through the arts • Take creative risks to express feelings, ideas, and experiences Trash to Treasure: Grade 6 Spring 2018: 12 schools participated in program. With about 480 students participating in the cleanup and about 350 participating in the Earth day Art display. We are working on ways to reduce barriers to teacher participation. Sign-ups are underway for 2019 and we have 50% more students participating this spring than last year. In 2019, we will be exploring the use of digital media and stop motion animation for the art display, to enable the students capture their experience digitally. Stream Health (grade 7): Pictures Healthy Stream Program: Grade 7 (Pilot) Concept: Students learn about riparian zones, identify aquatic insects and learn about the types of habitats they live in, and use standard ecological techniques to determine water quality by measuring pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity and temperature. Students met for just over an hour in Bowen Park. Curriculum Connections: Science  All organisms need space, food, water and access to resources in order to survive  Recent impacts of humans (humans are capable of changing Earth’s landscape, climate and systems)  Observe, measure, and record data (qualitative and quantitative), using equipment, including digital technologies, with accuracy and precision  Experience and interpret the local environment  Use scientific understandings to identify relationships and draw conclusions  Consider social, ethical, and environmental implications of the findings from their own and others’ investigations Healthy Stream Program: Grade 7 (Pilot) Curriculum Connections: Math  Computational fluency and flexibility with numbers extend to operations with integers and decimals.  Use tools or technology to explore and create patterns and relationships, and test conjectures  Develop, demonstrate, and apply mathematical understanding through play, inquiry, and problem solving  Engage in problem-solving experiences that are connected to place, story, cultural practices, and perspectives relevant to local First Peoples communities, the local community, and other cultures  Connect mathematical concepts to each other and to other areas and personal interests Fall 2018: Ran extended pilot program with 10 classes. Registration of participating schools was on a first come basis. Received positive feedback from teachers and students.
