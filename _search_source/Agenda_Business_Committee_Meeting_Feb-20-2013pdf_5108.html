STATEMENT OF BELIEFS & PRINCIPLES – 2012-2013 Amended and Adopted Feb 22 2012 School District No. 68 is committed to student achievement and the success of each and every learner. To emphasize this commitment, the following beliefs and values and budget principles will be employed by the District in the development of its annual operating budget. Budget Beliefs & Values 1. We believe that schools exist for learners. 2. We believe in providing a balanced curriculum to the whole learner. 3. We believe that learning is a process that individuals can and should pursue throughout their lifetimes. 4. We believe that safe and healthy environments contribute to student learning. 5. We value the pursuit of excellence in teaching and learning. 6. We believe that education is a shared responsibility among school, home and community. 7. We believe educational leadership is important in sustaining educational programs. 8. We value the dedication of our educators and support staff and the accomplishments of our learners. Budget Principles 1. The allocation of human and material resources should be directed first toward improving student learning. 2. The allocation of resources should respond to the diversity of student needs and the vulnerability of our learners 3. Resources should be provided to all learners at equitable and sustainable levels. 4. Program choices and adjustments should be made in the best interests of students and should be guided by credible research, successful professional past practice and thoughtful implementation of new or emerging practices. 5. Student learning is best served when adequate staffing exists at all levels of the organization and the necessary infrastructure supports are in place. Statement of Beliefs and Principles Page 38 of 43 10.3 ... Budget Considerations 1. Overriding Budget Consideration The budget must be developed in accordance with all legal and legislative requirements. 2. How to provide support for School Education Plans and the District Achievement Contract. 3. How to continue providing support for vulnerable students. 4. Recognize that, given the funding constraints facing the District, the future of certain facilities needs to be considered as a strategy for fiscal management. 5. How to support learning alternatives and distance education programs to ensure access to quality education to every student in the District. 6. How to maintain a level of District and school level management and leadership support required for an organization of this size and complexity. 7. How to provide a professional growth environment that supports succession planning for leadership at all levels of the organization. 8. How to maintain adequate service levels for non-instructional areas such as building maintenance, custodial services and technology support. 9. Examine all programs and/or services for the purpose of finding budget savings while continuing to provide adequate support for positive student learning. 10. Monitor the efficiency of the District’s current practice and approach to the allocation of learning resources and assets. 11. How to provide support for initiatives for working with community agencies to address the needs of learners in the early years and to promote safe, caring, orderly and healthy schools. 12. How to develop effective strategies for improving class size and class composition ratios in the district. The Payroll Department will email the list of payroll contributors and their number of entries to the ED. All of the employee names/numbers will be entered into a locked drum that will be used for the draw on a monthly basis. The drum will be housed at the Treasurer’s office at MNP or other secure location offsite from the District. Since there are different pay-periods in the SD, the proposal is to conduct the draw on a monthly basis on a consistent date i.e. the 1st Monday of every month. Since SD employees have 2 pay-periods in a month, the proposal is to do 2 draws on the draw date. There will always be a NLSF Director present at each draw. Once the draw is conducted, it will be the responsibility of the ED to inform the recipient and send an email to all employees via Master Email informing who the draw winners are. The NLSF Accountant will write the 2 cheques for the recipients and arrange with the recipients to complete payment.
