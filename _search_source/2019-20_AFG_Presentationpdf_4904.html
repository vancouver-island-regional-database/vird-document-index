Department of Facilities Planning & Capital 2019/20 AFG March 6, 2019 Annual Facilities Grant (AFG) – Intro • The Annual Facility Grant (AFG) is a grant paid to Boards of Education to fund projects required to maintain and prevent premature deterioration of district facility assets. • NLPS must annually provide the Ministry with an AFG project and spending plan prior to allocation of AFG funding: • A list of AFG projects and expenditures expected to be undertaken during the fiscal year (April 1st to March 31st) • At the end of each school year, boards of education must report on actual AFG expenditures as part of their audited financial statements. Annual Facilities Grant (AFG) – Intro • There are 12 categories of eligible annual facility grant expenditures: 1. Roof Replacements (replacements and major repairs) 2. Mechanical System Upgrades (improvements, replacements, upgrades) 3. Electrical System Upgrades (improvements or replacements, upgrades) 4. Facility Upgrades (improvements to protect the fabric of the plant, including exterior painting, window and door replacement, building envelope repair and replacement, etc.) 5. Loss Prevention Projects (improvements, replacements or provision of fire protection system) 6. Functional Improvements (improvements of school facilities related to the provision of educational programming) Annual Facilities Grant (AFG) – Intro • There are 12 categories of eligible annual facility grant expenditures, cont’d: 7. Technology Infrastructure Upgrades (improvements to telecommunications networks) 8. Site Upgrades (site improvements including positive site drainage; repairs to sidewalks, parking lots, site access/egress, upgrading or replacement of playground equipment; perimeter safety fencing) 9. Disabled Access (improvements related to access for persons with physical disabilities) 10. Asbestos Abatement 11. Health and Safety Upgrades (improvements related to indoor air quality, traffic safety, and non-structural upgrades) 12. Site Servicing (drainage or water services, underground irrigation systems, etc.) 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Budget $2.65M • Continuous intake and prioritization of school project requests • Increased alignment with other programs and projects (Local Capital & CEF) • Increased flexibility to address emergent items • Increased preplanning for projects requiring design time 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Roof Replacements • • • Roof sections of John Barsby, Seaview, Park Avenue, Departure Bay Gutter replacement at Dover Bay Facility Upgrades • Exterior painting program: • Fairview • 80% Ladysmith Secondary • Flooring replacement program (targeted rooms/areas): • Cedar Secondary, DAC, Forest Park, Hammond Bay, Ladysmith Secondary, Mountainview and Pauline Haarer 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Facility Upgrades, cont’d. • • • • NDSS Room 126 (Ceramics room HVAC upgrade) Island ConnectED space alterations (102, 103, 106, 107) Cedar Secondary (Storage room and corridor conversion to Weight room) Coal Tyee (Millwork renewal in Kindergarten rooms 124/125) 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Electrical System Upgrades • • Strategic Energy Management Plan (SEMP) • Lighting upgrades at 11 schools and 2 district facilities Loss Prevention Projects • Fire alarm system upgrades at Island ConnectED and Gabriola 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Technology Infrastructure Upgrades • • • Annual IS upgrade program Public announcement system upgrades at Bayview, Forest Park and Randerson Ridge Health and Safety Upgrades • • Upgrade to refrigerated fountain/fill stations at Cinnabar, Coal Tyee, Frank Ney, McGirr and Randerson Ridge Brechin Enclosed Play Area Fencing 2018/19 AFG – A Year in Review • Completion activities and final projects: • SEMP and IS upgrades • Misc.
