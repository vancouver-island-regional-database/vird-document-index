UNFINISHED BUSINESS 2012-2013 Enrollment Update Mr. Turin gave an update on the district’s enrolment for the 2012-13 school year. Mr. Turin said that the numbers this evening are still unofficial but very close to the finalized numbers for the district. Mr. Turin said that while the district was expecting a drop in enrolments this year, the decline was even greater than expected. The numbers show that enrolment dropped below projections in “bricks and mortar” schools by 2.2 percent, or 281 full-time-equivalent (FTE) students. Mr. Turin said that the drop in enrolment will not affect this year’s funding, but because the district is in funding protection, we will experience serious budget implications in future years. Energy Management Priorities for 2012-2013 Mr. Dalrymple, Energy Manager, outlined his priorities and goals related to the B.C. Hydro and FortisBC programs in the district which provide incentives for staffing and energy-saving initiatives. Carbon Neutral Capital Program - Update Mr. Dalrymple outlined the scope of the district’s carbon neutral capital plan. He said while the district is required to pay into the Carbon Offset fund, it receives grants for energy-efficient projects. He said In 2011, the district’s carbon emissions were estimated at 4,391 tonnes of carbon dioxide. 82 percent was for heating buildings, 13 percent for fleet vehicles and 5 percent for the use of paper.
