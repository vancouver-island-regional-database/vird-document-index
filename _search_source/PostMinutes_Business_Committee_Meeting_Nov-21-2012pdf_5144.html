FROM THE FACILITIES DEPT Transportation Department Current Services Overview Dave Prevost Mr. Sabo, Director of Operations and Planning, introduced Mr. Dave Prevost, Transportation Supervisor. Mr. Prevost told the Committee about the transportation services that the District offers. He said there are 28 employees in the Transportation Department, plus nine casual employees. These include drivers, mechanics, the dispatch coordinator and employees providing the district’s courier and cartage services. The district transports 1,400 students daily, over 16 routes and five routes for students with special needs. The district’s buses travel about 650,000 km. per year. Mr. Prevost reviewed the district transportation policies which address ride time limitations, district walk limits and transportation assistance. He also explained that there are a number of students who live within the walk limits and are “courtesy riders.” Carbon Neutral Plan Update Mr. Sabo told the Committee that the district is planning to use its carbon neutral grant to work with the City of Nanaimo to provide three electric car charging stations on school district property and to purchase an electric car for the district fleet. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held November 21, 2012 3 NEW BUSINESS Financial Forecast as of October 31st Mr. Turin provided the Committee with an update on the district’s current financial position as at October 31. He reviewed the changes to the district finances that have taken place since the 2012-2013 Budget was approved last spring. He said that the district’s operating budget is on target and that there are several budgeted expenditures that have not been made due to the district's enrolment being lower than forecasted. He said that although there have been increases in some expenditure categories; the net result is a small forecasted surplus for the year of $387,433 at this time. This forecasted surplus does not include the unrestricted surplus funds of $480,117 from the 2011-12 year.
