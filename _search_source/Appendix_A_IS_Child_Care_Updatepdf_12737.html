Appendix A NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 6, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Child Care Update Recommendation: The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) continue providing the Before and After School Care Programs at Pleasant Valley and Hammond Bay Elementary and support, in principle, the expansion to an additional 1-3 sites subject to the assurance that the expansion will be on a without prejudice basis with respect to labour relations issues and that additional external funding is made available. Background: Attached to this action sheet are two documents previously presented to the Board outlining the benefits and challenges with respect to providing in-house childcare. This action sheet will outline the District’s experience to date and provides a recommended next step. Discussion: Generally, the positives and negatives of the programming are outlined in the previous actions sheets and reflect the experience of the District in providing programming. On balance, despite concerns in a number of areas the program is worthwhile and should be continued subject to the caveats in the motion. Benefits The benefits of Before and After School Care on site and provided by District staff are clear for families and many staff. A list of benefits includes: 1. They provide the convenience for families of being on site; 2. Workers both skilled and familiar to the students provide care; 3. The District gets to ensure that the program has positive social and educational benefits by determining the program provision; and 4. Workers get additional hours. Drawbacks 1. It is an administrative burden to process and account for without any additional staffing; 2. There is insufficient funding to support a manager solely to support the programs; 3. A school with a limited EA contingent (e.g. Hammond Bay) is at risk of being understaffed or have insufficient numbers of interested employees; 4. Parents sometimes contact the school about the childcare which may increase the workload of K-12 based clerical staff and administration. This does also occur when the childcare is operated by a third party on site; Information Sheet: Child Care Update Page 2 5. There is insufficient funding to support students with diverse needs who require additional staffing. Next steps At this time, given the continued funding of the District Principal, Early Learning and Care some of the issues associated with managing the programs for an additional year are not at issue. Moreover, given the extension of the Seamless Day program at Pleasant Valley it is worthwhile to maintain the pilot programs in place. Further, if the Ministry of Education and Childcare does determine funding is available to support programs, the District would consider the expansion of the pilot to additional sites. With funding and additional students, it would become apparent if the programs could become sustainable from fees if external funding ceases. However, at this time we are not aware of a funding stream for this. Further, any expansion would be contingent on an agreement with our partners in CUPE of the pilot nature of the programs and it would also be important that an expectation is not built that before and after care is District work. One final note, because the District Principal, Early Learning and Care is funded by the Ministry this year there was a small amount of money from fees available to support additional resources to assist with inclusion at the one of the sites. However, this funding is not likely to continue. Appendix A: September 14, 2022, Action Sheet Appendix B: February 8, 2023, Information Sheet
