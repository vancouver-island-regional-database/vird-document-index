CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Brennan - attended a Foundation Donor Tea - where there were performances by a number of school groups. He attended an open house that was hosted by the City of Nanaimo, regarding the plan for Newcastle Island. There are potential options for SD68 to work with the City of Nanaimo to bring an Education piece to Newcastle Island. He also attended an antipoverty initiative. Trustee Bob - Gave a short synopsis on the Wellington Jazz Academy and updated the Board about a student that was invited to attend the Jazz Festival. At Coal Tyee, they have been working diligently on their garden. Departure Bay has a project that is going on to extract DNA from bugs and Rock City has a green garden project they have started. Page 6 of 9 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held April 22, 2015 Trustee Robinson - has attended a couple of PAC meetings. Cliaire has organized their second annual bike festival, which is very well received. The first Chamber/YPN project will be held at Barsby tomorrow. There will be 24 professionals there to support our students. He also attended the second annual Dover Grade 8 Aboriginal Understandings event. He has written a few more Celebrating 68 stories and the first "Recognition of Outstanding Achievement" letter was delivered to the Wellington Jazz Academy. Trustee Routley - has nothing to report from his school zone at this time. Trustee Brzovic - has been spending a lot of time at NDSS. She went to the Rockin' Tale of Snow White at the Port Theatre today which was put on by Pauline Haarer. It was an excellent performance by the school. She has also attended the swim to survive, dive water safety and jump rope for heart events and fundraisers. Trustee Solomon - also attended the Rockin' Tale of Snow White at the Port Theatre and was astounded at how well it was done. Trustee Kimler - at Ladysmith Primary, there is a stream of dreams mural society. It's an ecoeducational session called "All drains lead to fish". There will also be a showing of the Lego movie, which is free, but a 50/50 draw to fundraise. He attended the Swim to Survive program and a Welcome to Kindergarten session. At Ecole North Oyster, a naturalist came to present. For Earth Day, all the classroom lights were turned off for a period of time. He attended the Charlie and the Chocolate Factory performance. At Ladysmith Intermediate, there was Jump Rope for Heart fundraiser and $4000 was raised. The Nanaimo Bar Association is having a Nanaimo Law day and they will be doing demonstrations for children. At Ladysmith Secondary, parent-teacher interviews are happening, there is an Addams Family musical and they are continuing doing dry grad fundraising.
