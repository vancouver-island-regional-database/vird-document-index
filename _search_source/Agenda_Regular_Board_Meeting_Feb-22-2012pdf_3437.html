To emphasize this commitment, the following beliefs and values and budget principles will be employed by the District in the development of its annual operating budget. Budget Beliefs & Values 1. We believe that schools exist for learners. 2. We believe in providing a balanced curriculum to the whole learner. 3. We believe that learning is a process that individuals can and should pursue throughout their lifetimes. 4. We believe that safe and healthy environments contribute to student learning. 5. We value the pursuit of excellence in teaching and learning. 6. We believe that education is a shared responsibility among school, home and community. 7. We believe educational leadership is important in sustaining educational programs. 8. We value the dedication of our educators and support staff and the accomplishments of our learners. Budget Principles 1. The allocation of human and material resources should be directed first toward improving student learning. 2. The allocation of resources should respond to the diversity of student needs and the vulnerability of our learners 3. Resources should be provided to all learners at equitable and sustainable levels. 4. Program choices and adjustments should be made in the best interests of students and should be guided by credible research, successful professional past practice and thoughtful implementation of new or emerging practices. 5. Student learning is best served when adequate staffing exists at all levels of the organization and the necessary infrastructure supports are in place. RATIONALE: Recently media has brought to our attention that there is a gap in legislation in regards to the requirement of criminal record checks for elected trustees. Employees within school districts are required to have criminal record checks, as they have access to children within our school community. As elected trustees in our school districts we have open access to the schools and the children within these communities. We are invited to take part in the events, and visit classrooms and schools. This helps us to stay connected and informed to what is truly happening in our schools, our classrooms and with students. To support student safety and equity in regards to the requirement of Criminal Record checks within our education system, government needs to amend the school act and any other legislative portions that might be required to ensure that we are supporting a safe environment for all students under our supervision.
