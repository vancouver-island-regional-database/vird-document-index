In the fall of 2012, the Board concluded a public process that identified enhanced learning facilities as a general objective to be pursued by the Board. The Board then went behind closed doors to produce the 10 Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan. Trustees then buttoned their lips and went out to “consult” with the public. Unless something dramatic happens tonight, the final Plan will be almost a mirror image of the Plan on Day 1. Attach. 1 Page 84 of 95 13.2 Policy ____ RECRUITMENT, SELECTION, and EVALUATION OF PERSONNEL (REVISED DRAFT: February 26, 2014) The Board believes that the recruitment and selection of District personnel is a shared responsibility between the Board and the Superintendent/CEO. The Board further believes strong leadership and administration at the District and school levels are essential to the effective and efficient operation of the school system. Specifically 1. The Board, in the case of the Superintendent/CEO, or the Superintendent/CEO or designate, will assume the sole responsibility for initiating the advertising process and will make reasonable effort to ensure that all current District employees are made aware of staff vacancies. 2. The Board has the sole authority to recruit and select an individual for the position of Superintendent/CEO, Secretary Treasurer, and Deputy Superintendent, and will use the services of BCPSEA's "Make a Future" if appropriate. 3. The following process will be followed for Assistant Superintendent positions: 3.1 The Superintendent/CEO will establish a screening committee that will include the Chair and Vice Chair from the Board of Education and representatives from the Administrative Council Executive. 3.2 The screening committee will recommend candidates to be interviewed by the selection committee. The selection committee will be determined by the Superintendent/CEO and include Chair and Vice Chair from the Board of Education and representatives from the Administrative Council Executive. 3.3 The selection committee will attempt to achieve consensus. In the event this is not possible, the successful candidate must be supported by a majority unrecorded vote of the selection committee. The Superintendent/CEO must be one of the votes in the majority. 3.4 These positions shall have a role description and the person occupying each of the positions shall have a written contract of employment. 3.5 The compensation package, including placement on the salary grid currently in effect, will be determined by the Superintendent/CEO in accordance with the administrative procedure set by the Board of Education, and must be approved by the Board of Education. 4. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to recruit and select staff for all other District office positions. 2 Page 85 of 95 13.2 5. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to recruit and select staff for all school-based positions, including Principals and Vice-Principals. 6. The Superintendent/CEO is delegated full authority to make all decisions regarding the term and/or continuing appointments, and evaluation, of administrators. 7. In the event of an unexpected or short term vacancy, the Superintendent/CEO may appoint an “Acting Principal” or “Acting Vice-Principal” without undertaking a formal selection process. 8. The Board of Education will be provided with a rationale for school-based administrator appointments and transfers, prior to a formal system announcement. 9. All offers of employment shall be conditional on the successful applicant providing a criminal records check through the Criminal Records Review Program (Minister of Public Safety and Solicitor General). Additionally, the Superintendent/CEO or designate may require documentation certifying that the candidate is medically fit for the position. 10. The Chair and the Vice Chair of the Board of Education will meet with the Superintendent/CEO bimonthly and will act as a liaison to the Board on sensitive personnel issues.
