Exploring IB in NLPS The Tale of Two Schools West Bay Elementary Queen Mary Community School -Declining enrollment dipped below 250 students -After successful IB implementation in nearby schools, sufficient demand and desire to pursue a second PYP school. -Dual track French Immersions suggested by district -Not supported by parents and instead IB was proposed -District could not fund the programme, so ‘optional’ $400 fee was created -2014: Now more than 400 students enrolled with in-catchment waitlists in three grades -After completing a ranking selection criteria, Queen Mary was chosen out of 9 possible sites -Student population is approx. 20% First Nations, 28% English Language Learners and over 40% of students come from single-parent families. There is also an extensive before/ after-school programme and breakfast and lunch club. -Free program for all students -Projected to add 3 divisions in 2015 and connect with Carson Graham for IB continuum What is the International Baccalaureate? • The International Baccalaureate is a non-profit organization that oversees more than 1.5 million students worldwide in 3696 schools in 147 countries. • Its main office is located in Cardiff Wales, and regional offices are located in Singapore, Maryland, Buenos Aires, and Geneva The I.B. Programmes There are 4 programmes within the IB • • • • Primary Years Programme (Ages 3-12) Middle Years Programme (Ages 11-16) Diploma Programme (Ages 16-19) Career Certificate (Ages 16-19) IB Mission Statement The International Baccalaureate Organization aims to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world… continued IB mission con’t… To this end the IBO works with schools, governments, and international organizations to develop challenging programmes of international education and rigorous assessment. Continued… IB Mission con’t… These programmes encourage students to become active, compassionate and lifelong learners who understand that other people, with their differences can also be right. IB learner profile • The IB learner profile is the IB mission statement translated into a set of learning outcomes for the 21st century • As IB learners we strive to be: • • • • • • • • • • inquirers knowledgeable thinkers communicators principled open-minded caring risk-takers balanced reflective I.B. in the Canadian Context • There are currently 329 schools across Canada offering the IB Programmes (66 PYP, 157 MYP, 151 DP). • 40 are in British Columbia • On the Island, only 6 schools offer the IB (2 PYP, 2 MYP, 6 DP) There are currently no public PYP schools on Vancouver Island What is the PYP? • The PYP is based on 5 Essential Elements. • These are: • • • • • Knowledge Key Concepts Approaches to Learning Attitudes Action Qualities of the PYP • • • • • • • Global perspectives Globally relevant content Inquiry-based learning Concept-based learning Focus on the development of responsible citizenship Collaborative approach to planning of instruction Development of “International-Mindedness” through the IB Learner Profile • A global architecture for professional development which includes online, regional and global workshop models How will it benefit NLPS? • A horizontally and vertically integrated programme of inquiry • Inquiry-based teaching pedagogy, differentiated instruction, and rigorous assessment connect with NLPS Tier 1 Universal Strategies • The 5 essential elements correlate nicely with the new BC Curriculum’s focus on big ideas and core competencies • A commitment to service and action • A standard of practice around PLC’s and Collaboration Where can it be located? • ANY of our current schools could host the PYP • Consideration might be given to: • Community • Geography • Transportation routes • Site space for enrollment Cost for sample school (Students) Consideration Phase (Year 1) - IB Application fee = $4500 USD - Introductory training for Principal and PYP Coordinator = $4000 Candidate Phase (Year 2- Year 3) - IB Fee each year during candidacy = $9500 USD - 1.00 FTE (PYP Coordinator/ Language Teacher) = $103,000 per year - Introductory in-school workshop for 10 staff = $15,000 Authorized (Year 4+) - IB Fee per year = $8000 USD - Continued 1.0 FTE (PYP Coordinator/ Language teacher =$103,000 - On-going professional development = $4000 per year Costs Year 0-Year 3= Approximately $375,000 Year 4 and thereafter= Approximately $125,000 Additional Considerations: - Facilities adjustments and revisions - Ongoing staff training - Program resources Questions?
