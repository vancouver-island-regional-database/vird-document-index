Trustee Robinson – Dover Zone February 2017 • The Frank J. Ney PAC fundraiser on Valentine’s Day, which featured lots of dancing by both students and their parents. The most popular event was the “Cake Walk”. It raised a lot of money as well. • The John Barsby "Human Library" Event, featured 28 business and community leaders who donated 3 hours of their time to talk to students about their careers. We had more “Human Books” than ever and a wider selection of careers. Wait till next year!!! This was our third year, and we’ve grown bigger and better each year. • On February 16 I visited Randerson Ridge Elementary at lunch time only to find grade three and four students gathered in the library or their classrooms practicing for a reading competition that will take place at the school on February 22. Winners will go on to district playoffs. This program was initiated by the public library system. • For the third year in a row, The Vancouver Island Symphony gathered all of our grade four students for a show featuring Rick Scott with the orchestra. The show was written and orchestrated by 25-year-old NLPS graduate, Mr. N. Rhodes, written when he was 23 years old. When they are in grade five, students can apply to sing in the Grade 5 Symphony Choir. This is an extraordinary program.
