Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held January 18, 2017 Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING BRIEFING NOTE DATE: January 18th, 2017 TO: Board of Education, Business Committee FROM: Tim Davie, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources (Designate) Autumn Storey, Director of Human Resources SUBJECT: Supreme Court of Canada decision – Phase I and II update Background: On November 10, 2016, the Supreme Court of Canada (SCC) heard the BC Teachers’ Federation (BCTF) appeal of a 2015 BC Court of Appeal decision that held that the BC government did not violate the Charter of Rights and Freedoms (Charter) when it passed the Education Improvement Act (Bill 22, which removed provisions regarding class size and composition from the Provincial Collective Agreement (PCA) between BCPSEA (BC Public School Employers’ Association) and the BCTF. The SCC was expected to address whether governments can legislate changes to existing collective agreement terms and, if so, what process must be followed before such legislation can be enacted. The case considered whether consultations between the BC government and BCTF prior to Bill 22 being enacted provided the union with a meaningful opportunity to advance its members’ workplace goals. The case was not about what is or is not an appropriate policy approach to class size and composition – both sides have asked the Court not to address the merits of education policy. Background Reference: In 2002, the BC government passed the Public Education Flexibility and Choice Act (Bill 28), which removed language regarding class size and composition from the Provincial Collective Agreement (PCA) between BCPSEA and the BCTF and prohibited future collective bargaining about these issues. Bill 28 was challenged by the BCTF, which asserted that it violated the union members’ right to freedom of association under the Charter. This challenge was successful and Bill 28 was struck down by the BC Supreme Court in 2011. The government was given 12 months to respond to the Court’s decision, after which Bill 28 would become inoperative and the contested language regarding class size and composition would be restored. Over the 12 months following the Court’s decision, the BCTF and the government met to discuss the implications of the decision but were unable to reach a resolution regarding the issues it raised. Prior to the 12 month period following the Court’s decision, the government passed the Education Improvement Act, Bill 22, which clarified that the contested class size and composition provisions would not be automatically restored in the PCA. Bill 22 also permitted negotiation regarding class size and composition in future rounds of collective bargaining. As with Bill 28, Bill 22 was also promptly challenged by the BCTF as a violation of its members’ right to freedom of association under the Charter. The BCTF challenge to Bill 22 was initially successful at the BC Supreme Court, which held that the legislation violated BCTF members’ Charter right to freedom of association. This conclusion, however, was overturned by the BC Court of Appeal (BCCA), which found that Bill 22 did not breach the Charter. The main difference between the decisions of the two levels of court related to the relevance of the constitution process that took place between the government and the BCTF prior to Bill 22 being enacted. The BC Supreme Court held that this practice was irrelevant, or in the alternative, insufficient, as it did not provide the BCTF with a meaningful opportunity to advance its members’ workplace goals before Bill 22 was passed. The Court of Appeal disagreed, holding that the consultation that took place was indeed relevant and that it was meaningful and sufficiently respectful of the BCTF members’ rights under the Charter. The majority of the BCCA held that the BC government did not violate the Charter of Rights and Freedoms (Charter) when it passed the Education Improvement Act (Bill 22), which removed provisions regarding class size and composition from the Provincial Collective Agreement (PCA) between BCPSEA and the BCTF. The Honourable Mr. Justice Donald issued a dissenting decision (refer to page 81 - 119 of the BCCA decision). The Supreme Court of Canada Decision RE: BCTF Appeal: On November 10, 2016, the Supreme Court of Canada (SCC) issued a verbal decision from the bench allowing the BCTF appeal. The SCC then issued a written reason of its decision, as follows: 2 The appeal from the judgement of the Court of Appeal for British Columbia (Vancouver) CA41560, 2015 BCCA 184, dated April 30th, 2015, was heard November 10, 2016, and the Court on that day delivered the following judgement orally: THE CHIEF JUSTICE - The majority of the Court would allow the appeal, substantially for the reasons of Justice Donald. Justices Coté and Brown would dissent and dismiss the appeal, substantially for the reasons of the majority in the Court of Appeal. The reasons for this appeal being upheld lie fully in the dissenting report from Justice Donald in the April 30, 2015 ruling of the Court of Appeal for British Columbia, Docket CA41560, which says in part: [399] In my opinion, allowing the Working Conditions to remain deleted would force teachers to continue to suffer from unconstitutional government action and legislation. Therefore, I would order… that the Minister of Education direct the public administrator for the BCPSEA … to reinstate the Working Conditions into the collective agreement immediately. Any future deletion or alteration of these terms must occur as a result of the collective bargaining process or after a constitutionally compliant process of good faith consultation. On November 10, 2016, the Supreme Court of Canada issued its decision in the longstanding litigation between the BCTF and the BC government regarding the deletion of certain PCPSEABCTF Provincial Collective Agreement provisions by the Education Improvement Act, Bill 22. The Court’s decision restored the deleted provisions, which triggered the negotiation process under Letter of Understanding No. 17 (LOU No.17) to the 2013-2019 BCPSEA-BCTF Provincial Collective Agreement. For reference, LOU No. 17 states: “If final judgement affects the content of the collective agreement by fully or partially restoring the 2002 language, the parties will reopen the collective agreement on this issue and the parties will bargain from the restored language. The Education Fund provisions (see Appendix A) will continue in effect until there is agreement regarding implementation and/or changes to the restored language.” Shortly after the Supreme Court of Canada decision was issued, representatives of BCPSEA, PSEC (Public Sector Employers’ Council), and the Ministry of Education began meeting with the BCTF to discuss the implementation of and/or changes to the restored language, as required by LOU No. 17. It is important to note that as part of the negotiations leading to the 2013-2019 Provincial Collective Agreement between the BC Teachers’ Federation and the BC Public School Employers’ Association, the two parties negotiated a re-opener in anticipation of the decision of the SCC. Next Steps: 3 Given that the language ordered restored by the SCC dates back to the collective agreements in place in 2002 (and the language itself was negotiated many years prior to that), the parties need to engage in discussion to review the provisions in the 2016-17 school context. The parties are currently conducting the analysis to determine areas that require further exploration. While the dialogue leading to the final resolution takes place, steps are underway to take action in the short-term that is directly aligned with long-term objectives to provide resources for students and teachers. Phase I: The provincial parties, to ensure they are better informed, are requested that the local parties meet to review process and ancillary language that was originally negotiated locally and that was deleted in 2002. The purpose is to review your district specific language and to answer the questions on a document as provided by the parties at the provincial table. The purpose is not to amend or discuss implementation of the language, but rather to identify issues related to the current context that require further consideration. Specifically, the local parties were tasked with reviewing restored language related to:    School-level processes (e.g., school-based team/other school committees) District-level processes (e.g., district committees referenced) Terminology and nomenclature of bargaining unit positions and references to students (This does not include any special needs designations as those will be dealt with provincially) Phase I meetings of the joint committee (Employer and NDTA representatives) took place on Friday, January 6, 2017 and Monday, January 9, 2017, with information being provided to the provincial table for the January 10th deadline. Phase II: On January 5, 2017, representatives of the BC Public School Employers’ Association (BCPSEA), the BC Teachers’ Federation (BCTF), the Public Sector Employers’ Council (PSEC) Secretariat, and the Ministry of Education (MoE) reached agreement on a Priority Measures Memorandum of Agreement as a first step in responding to the decision of the Supreme Court of Canada. The parties agreed to interim priority measures that will be implemented in the BC public education system while a final solution to the issues raised by the Supreme Court of Canada decision continues to be discussed. The key components of the Priority Measures Memorandum of Agreement are as follows:  The Ministry of Education will provide $50 million in net new funding for British Columbia’s public education system for the balance of the 2016/17 school year. The parties have agreed that this funding represents the monetary equivalent of approximately 1000 - 1100 teacher full time equivalence (FTE), depending on experience, qualifications and district-specific compensation variation; 4  The $50 million in net new funding will be used to add additional enrolling and nonenrolling teacher FTE during the 2016/2017 school year;  Where it is not feasible to use the net new funding to add additional enrolling or nonenrolling teacher FTE during the 2016/2017 school year it may be used to fund other school district-level capacity building initiatives to support teachers;  Recommendations regarding the allocation of the net new funding at the school district level will be jointly developed through a district committee established by the Superintendent and the local union president. The district committee will endeavour to reach agreement in January regarding the allocation of additional teacher FTE;  The net new funding that is not spent implementing the priority measures set out above will be applied towards the final agreement regarding implementation and/or changes to the restored collective agreement provisions;  The Priority Measures Memorandum of Agreement is without prejudice to what may be included in a final agreement regarding implementation of and/or changes to the restored collective agreement provisions;  The Priority Measures Memorandum of Agreement will terminate on June 30, 2017 unless the parties mutually agree to extend or renew it. On January 11, 2017, the BC Ministry of Education announced release of funds to schools districts, under the provisions of the Memorandum of Agreement (MoA), for use in the remainder of the school year. Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools is to receive an allocation of $1,193,298. This would equate to approximately 23 FTE added to our system for the remainder of the 2016-17 school year. School districts are encouraged to begin hiring as soon as possible. This funding may form a part of any final negotiated settlement between the parties. Funding will be distributed in the same manner as the Learning Improvement Fund, using September 2016 enrolment data as the basis for the calculation. Boards of Education are responsible for ensuring that funding is spent in a manner that aligns with the MoA, including meeting local consultation requirements between staff and the teacher union local. Phase II meetings of the joint committee (Employer and NDTA representatives) took place on Monday, January 9, and Wednesday, January 11, 2017. School Principals received direction to consult with their staff committees, through emergent staff committee meetings, so they may submit proposals to address priorities for their school with respect to allocation of temporary FTE resulting from interim funding. The joint committee met to review submissions on Monday, January 16, 2017, with the intent of having schools submit postings by Thursday, January 19, 2017 so additional FTE can be added to the system for start of second semester. 5 Phase III: Issues arising from the SCC decision rest with the provincial parties.
