KelseyToye FW: Recommendations for Changes to FacilitiesPlan for SD68—Questions and Concerns for the Board of SD68 Subject: From: CSSP [mailto:_csspsd6E§@gmail.com] Sent: Friday, August 21, 2015 2:11 PM To: Steve Rae; Stephanie Higginson; Scott Kimler;Jamie Brennan; Tania Brzovic;Jeff Solomon; Natasha Bob; Noah Routley; BillBard Cc: Kelsey Toye; John Blain; Graham Roberts; educgmmister ov.bc.ca Subject: Recommendations for Changes to Facilities Plan for SD68—Questions and Concerns for the Board of SD68 August 21, 2015 Coalition ofSD68 Secondary School Parents and Community Members (formerly Coalition ofSD68 Secondary School PACs] Dear Board of Trustees SD68, Re: Recommendations for Changes to Facilities Plan for SD68—Questionsand Concerns for the Board of SD68 We recognize that the recommendations put forward by staff in June, regarding the facilities plan, do not constitute a plan. Therefore, a consultation process has not yet started, and will not commence until the Board has an opportunity to vote on the recommendations in September. However, we would like the Board and staff to consider the following questions and concerns while reviewing these recommendations. 1. On numerous occasions, staff and trustees told people that they were "reviewing” the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan, as would be done with any long—term plan, making revisions as needed. However, the recommendations presented in ]une, along with the motion to reopen a secondary school seem to constitute a completely new plan, as opposed to simply revisions. Why was the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan completely disregarded? 2. What happens if students do not wish to return to CSS?Is there a contingency plan? Will they be given a choice? Will busing continue to be provided for students from Cedar who wish to continue attending LSS or John Barsby? This may be important, since students have been uprooted once and may not wish to be uprooted again. The Board should at least consider grandfathering busing for the next five years to allow those students who have started at Ladysmith and JohnBarsby to continue at those schools through to graduation. 4. Would the Board consider limiting CSS to grades 8-10, so that higher enrollment could be maintained at Ladysmith and IohnBarsby, giving more opportunities for all students in the senior grades. IohnBarsby is expected to see capacity utilization drop from 77% in 2014 to just over 50% in 2021, which cannot be considered consistent with the District's vision of "success for all." 5. If catchment boundaries are changed, will there be any consultation with families, eg. French Immersion, Gabriola, and other geographical changes? For students who are impacted by changes to catchment boundaries, it is similar to facing the closure of your school, in that they must leave behind friends and teachers. Parents may have moved to that area to attend the school, only to be told their children can no longer attend. Will they be given a choice? 6. What are the assumptions around optimal school size for secondary schools? What is the optimal number of students needed in Grades 11 and 12 for a school to be able to offer core courses every semester, without the 1 need for students to take Learn at Home? The 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan recommended secondary schools between 600 and 1200 students. What are the operating and capital costs compared to previous plans? eg. New wing for Frank Ney and costs ofkeeping Woodbank/NCI open, rather than consolidating both Woodbank and NCI at the CSS building What are the savings in operating costs as a result of closing schools earlier than they were scheduled to close in the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan What are the capital, operating, and opportunity costs for the following four scenarios? [Please note that we recognize CSS is reopening. However, we need to look at the opportunity costs associated with this decision, since it must be added to the cost of the other options. We would also like to draw a direct comparison to show how the proposed changes make financial sense]. The scenarios are as follows: 1. a) Cedar Secondary remains closed. Woodbank Primary and North Cedar Intermediate consolidate at CSS building. Woodlands closes lune, 2017. 2. b) CSSremains closed. Woodbank Primary and NCI consolidate at one ofthe two schools. CSS property is sold. (This is important, because there is a cost associated with maintaining an additional school property, as well as an opportunity cost associated with not realizing the sale of either CSSand one, or both, of the other schools in Cedar.) 3. c) Implement the recommendations outlined in the Junereport to the Board, including the opportunity cost associated with keeping two schools open in Cedar as opposed to keeping only one open. Woodlands SS closes June2016. 4. d] What is the cost to keep Woodlands open until the site at NDSSis developed? 10. What is the rationale for closing Woodlands a year earlier than initially planned, and without any firm commitment for moving forward with the NDSS site development to consolidate Woodlands and NDSS?One trustee has said that closing Woodlands saves 700 seats. However, reopening Cedar was not factored in, which adds back 400+ seats. So effectively, we are only saving 300 seats. 11. It appears that further consolidating is being done at the elementary level, with the proposed closure of Rutherford, which was not part of the last plan. How does it make educational sense to back off on consolidation at the secondary level, while ramping up consolidation at the elementary level? How will this plan ensure that secondary students get the programs they need? We understand smaller schools might be more effective at the elementary level, but not at the secondary level. 12. Learn at home should not be used as a replacement for offering core courses in the classroom. 1. a) Why does the District seem to be relying more on Learn @ Home to deliver core courses to secondary students? Parents have expressed concern that, in some cases, L@H is the only option for students. L@H has traditionally been considered a "?exible" alternative for students. However, it seems to be increasingly used as a replacement for classroom instruction when there are not sufficient numbers of students to fill a class. In some cases, it is the only option for students. L@H is NOT a viable option for many students. 2. b) Of all the L@H 8-12 courses that students have enrolled in, what percentage are successfully completed? Also, what percentage of students have withdrawn from L@H courses? 3. C] Any future surveys should include a question for parents of secondary students regarding the acceptability of L@H as a replacement for classroom instruction for core courses. 13. We ask that the Board consider conducting another survey before moving forward with any further plans. The “Your Voice" survey data is not useable, so to base decisions around reopening and closing schools on this data is not viable. It does not re?ect unbiased feedback from parents and community members across the District. A survey needs to be developed by staff, or a professional outside agency, with direction from all trustees. The survey needs to be administered in a manner that will maximize responses and provide valid, as well as meaningful results. 14. Would the Board please make updated bylaws and policies available on the website for parents and community members to reference? This would be beneficial for everyone. 15. Board meetings are less accessible if held at 4pm. Would the Board please consider moving times of the meetings so that they are no earlier than 5pm? Also, we ask that the Board provide at least 48 hours notice when changing meeting times or location. Please consider a more effective method of notifying the public when making these changes. 16. We would like the Board to consider approaching the Minister of Education to appoint a special advisor to help guide the District through the facilities planning process. Parents and community members are more likely to support decisions that are overseen by a special advisor who is not attached to particular outcomes. These decisions being made now will impact our District for years to come. 17. lfthe Board does not wish to request a Special Advisor, please consider putting a hold on closures for the next year to allow for further discussion and a new survey. The recommendations for closures in lune, 2016 and 2017 are far more aggressive than those contained in the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan, and do not give an opportunity to carry out a proper analysis of the impacts. Sincerely, Lori Warwick, on behalfofthe 1 l Coalition of Secondary School Parents and Community Members
