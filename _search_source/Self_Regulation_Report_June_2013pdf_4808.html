School  Self-­‐Regulation  Report     Nanaimo-­‐Ladysmith  School  District     Brenda  Whittam-­‐Neary     June  24,  2013                               Rationale  for  School  Wide  Self-­‐  Regulation     Globalization  and  technological  advancements  require  that  students  be  more  autonomous,   flexible,  and  critical  in  their  thinking.  Future  citizens  of  British  Columbia  will  be  expected  to   be  self-­‐motivated  and  to  adjust  to  new  challenges  quickly  and  efficiently  in  order  to  remain   viable  and  productive  in  the  21st  century.  To  reflect  these  societal  changes,  educational   reform  has  focused  on  the  learning  environment  by  developing  more  personalized,   problem-­‐based,  collaborative,  and  student-­‐centered  classrooms  to  better  represent  the   complex  learning  environments  that  students  will  face  in  their  post-­‐school  transitions   (Hopper  &  Seaman,  2011).  Part  of  this  ongoing  reform  involves  teaching  students   appropriate  learning  strategies  and  self-­‐regulatory  skills  to  help  them  adapt  to  these   situations  and  become  life-­‐long  metacognitive  learners  (Zimmerman  &  Schunk,  2003).  For   today’s  educator,  knowledge  of  self-­‐regulation,  its  development,  and  strategies  for   optimization  in  all  learners,  is  pivotal  for  personalized  21st  century  learners.     1.0 Deliverables     1.3            The  Consultant  shall  design  a  self-­‐regulation  demonstration  classroom.   1.4            The  Consultant  shall
