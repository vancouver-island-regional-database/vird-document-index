NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: January 4, 2023 TO: Education Committee FROM: Don Balcombe, Assistant Superintendent SUBJECT: Core French and French Immersion Federal Grant Funding Background In British Columbia, there are four programs to learn French in the K-12 education system. Core French, Intensive French, French Immersion, and the Francophone Program. NLPS offers Core French and French Immersion, as well as supporting Francophone students from SD93 in grades 8 through 12 at Nanaimo District Secondary School. All elementary schools in the district offer Core French in grades 5-7. Core French is offered in grades 8-12 at our secondary schools. French Immersion programs are offered at four of our elementary schools and two secondary schools. École Hammond Bay, École Pauline Haarer, and École Quarterway are single-track French Immersion schools, while École North Oyster offers both English and French Immersion programs. Ladysmith Secondary School offers French Immersion in grades 8 and 9. Nanaimo District Secondary School offers French Immersion in grades 8-12. In addition to regular funding through the Ministry of Education and Child Care, French programs have access to funding grants supported by the Federal Government. NLPS qualifies for grant funding in the following categories: E-Learning Technologies, Learning Resources, Core French, French Immersion, Teacher Professional Development, and Cultural Activities. Overview The district French Advisory Committee (FAC) is responsible for applying for grants, allocating grants, and reporting annually in accordance with the Officials Languages in Education Protocol. The FAC consists of the Assistant Superintendent-Secondary and French programs, the Director of Instruction Learning and Innovation, the French Immersion Learning Coordinator, the Core French Learning Coordinator, and all principals and vice principals from schools with French Immersion programs. The committee meets once a month throughout the school year to plan, implement, and monitor programs and initiatives that are supported by the grants. Collectively, the grants amount to approximately $250,000/year of targeted support to our French Immersion and Core French Programs.
