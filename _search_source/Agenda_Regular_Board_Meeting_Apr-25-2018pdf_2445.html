Page 28 of 112 12.1 Georgia Accessible Playground Project April 11, 2018 I met with Georgia PAC members Barbara and Corinna today at Georgia. These are my conclusions from the meeting:  Georgia PAC has three members. Therefore, they are happy to leave this accessible playground fundraising and building project in the hands of staff. They simply do not have the time or energy required to take on the kind of major fundraising project required to build an accessible playground. That said, they will provide advice and suggestions as requested.  The PACs main interest is in bringing the “lower playground” up to standard. Their plan sees them beginning to fundraise for this project next year. They are hoping, and expecting, that some of the “upper playground” equipment will be moved to the “lower playground” as staff develops the accessible playground project.  I would hope that the district will make every effort possible to help them bring the “lower playground” up to standard as the accessible playground project is brought to fruition. It certainly needs upgrading in large measure for safety reasons. My suggestions    That Pete and his staff add a “cherry on the top” addition to their accessible playground plans to be left for the combined Georgia/McGirr PACs to do the required fundraising. They will also work to fund those improvements the district can’t provide for the “lower playground.” The ideas is that this fundraising will take place over the next year or two or more if required. That the Georgia and McGirr PACs continue to meet as required to take advantage of the McGirr PAC’s fundraising contacts and knowledge, knowledge they are enthusiastic about sharing. I have agreed to write all required proposals with support from Twyla Spoke (McGirr PAC), Basia Henoch and Lisa Baron (Community School Coordinators). It would also be very helpful if we do hire a full-time fundraising proposal writer to have that support. Understanding Operating Surplus "Accumulated Operating Surplus" means the accumulated excess of Operating revenues over Operating expenses from prior years. Accumulated Surplus is not reflected in the Budget Bylaw document Accumulated Surplus is recorded in the AFS - Actual “Annual Operating Surplus” is the amount by which a fiscal year’s Operating revenue exceeds that same fiscal year’s Operating expenses. 14.1 Page 98 of 112 Understanding Operating Surplus “Contingency Reserve(s) or Reserve(s)” are funds taken from an Accumulated Operating Surplus that are either restricted or unrestricted. They are established for unexpected increases in expenses and/or decreases in revenues. They are used to mitigate the risk of costs being greater than estimated. “Unrestricted Operating Surplus” means the accumulated Operating surplus in the School District’s Operating Fund that has not been designated for specific uses. 14.1 Page 99 of 112 “Restricted Operating Surplus” means the accumulated Operating surplus in the School District’s Operating Fund that has been designated for specific uses.
