Purchase of NDSS Lands & Joint Development of Artificial Turf Field 2017-FEB-22 PARTNERSHIP CONTEXT • City of Nanaimo (CoN)/Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) Collaboration Agreement (Nov. 2012) • Collaboration Agreement Commitments: – Open communication – A collaborative approach to decision making where there is mutual interest – Short and long-term planning that best meets community and education needs BACKGROUND • NDSS Task Force – redevelopment of property • Uncertain future for Rotary Bowl & Serauxmen Stadium • Challenge of identifying a new site in the community for track and baseball stadium • Demand for an additional artificial turf field in the community NANAIMO DISTRICT SECONDARY SCHOOL SERAUXMAN STADIUM AND BASEBALL IN NANAIMO SERAUXMAN STADIUM AND BASEBALL IN NANAIMO Serauxmen Stadium History • July 31, 1976 - Serauxmen Stadium officially opened in a ceremony that included retired New York Yankees player Mickey Mantle and retired Boston Red Sox player Jim Piersall. • Has been operated and funded by NMBA through registration fees, government grants and fundraising. • Is considered one of the Province’s Premier Baseball venues and it is not a widely known fact that NMBA bears the cost of operation of this facility for the benefit of user groups. • NMBA spends between 30-40K annually to operate and maintain the stadium before any significant capital spending. ROTARY BOWL AND TRACK & FIELD IN NANAIMO ROTARY BOWL AND TRACK & FIELD IN NANAIMO • Nanaimo Track and Field Club has been in existence over 50 years and is currently the 5th largest club in BC with over 200 members – ranging in age from 6–80 years, with majority 9-17 years. • In the past 3 years NTFC, with the help of community partners, has contributed over $200,000 worth of improvements to Rotary Bowl and plans to continue to improve the site. • Rotary Bowl is used for many community events and by many community members. LARRY MCNABB SPORTS ZONE • In October 2007, the branding of the Nanaimo Ice Centre, the Nanaimo Aquatic Centre, Serauxmen Sports Fields, Serauxmen Stadium, VIU, NDSS and the Rotary Bowl as the 'Nanaimo Sports Zone‘ with a promotional tagline of “Learn, Train, Play” was approved. Signage was installed in 2009. • In 2011, Council approved a motion to rename the Sports Zone to the “Larry McNabb Sports Zone”, and it was dedicated as such on 2011-MAY28. OPPORTUNITY • CoN to acquire Rotary Bowl & Serauxmen Stadium • CoN to acquire ‘triangle’ for future development • CoN to partner with NLPS to build an artificial turf field on the remaining NDSS lands • Long term redevelopment of Rotary Bowl and Serauxmen Stadium by CoN Offer in Place CoN to Acquire Rotary Bowl, Serauxmen Stadium and ‘Triangle’ for $4,100,000 NLPS and CoN to construct an Artificial Turf field at NDSS at a cost of $2,400,000 ($1.2M each) ARTIFICIAL TURF FIELD PROPOSAL Joint Synthetic Turf Field Capital Cost: $2,400,000 Potential Construction Start Date: June 2017 Possible Opening Date: September 2017 NEXT STEPS NLPS is conducting a public consultation process that will conclude on Friday, March 10, 2017.
