From Other Committees SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT Mr. Munro provided the Board with an update about a number of special activities that took place at schools over the past month.  April 28, is the day that is designated each year as a Day of Mourning to remember Canadian workers whose lives have been lost or injured in the workplace.  Aboriginal students from John Barsby and Cedar secondary schools participated in the Northern Games Challenge April 1 at John Barsby.  Various elementary schools throughout the district are currently learning to jive in preparation for the 5th Annual Project Dance 2011 which will be held June 10.  Congratulations to Cedar and John Barsby secondary school’s Aboriginal film teams. Both teams had films nominated for awards at the Cowichan International Aboriginal Film Festival April 15. Page 8 of 10 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held April 27, 2011 UNFINISHED BUSINESS 8  Students in the Global Watch Club at Wellington Secondary held their yearly Global Watch Fair on April 20. Students in the Global Watch Club consider how they can be a positive influence in the world – in environmental issues, or international aid and assistance.  The Dover Bay Eco Club was one of 10 teams in the Grades 8-12 category to win the third annual BC Green Games, Science World’s province-wide contest that rewards students’ eco-actions. The school will receive $1,000 for restoring their local ecosystem.  School District 68’s Learning Alternatives Programs are presenting Windows of Opportunity WO2.3 What On Earth is 21st Century Learning on May 9 at the Port Theatre.
