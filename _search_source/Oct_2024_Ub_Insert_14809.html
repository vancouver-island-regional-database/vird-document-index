For details on these events and others, visit the Ladysmith Chamber of Commerce website: https:// www. ladysmithcofc.com/calendar SMALL-SCALE, MULTI-UNIT HOUSING (SSMUH) Following provincial regulation, Ladysmith is expanding housing options through the introduction of Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing (SSMUH), allowing homeowners to diversify their properties in various zones. In most “single-family” areas, you can now build secondary suites, coach houses, and duplexes. Townhouses and multi-unit dwellings are also permitted in R-2 and C-1 zones. However, navigating the regulations is crucial to ensure compliance and avoid potential issues. Building without the required permits can result in severe consequences, including hefty fines, unsafe living conditions, costly demolitions, and challenges with financing or selling your property. For properties located in designated extension areas, the number of units you can build is limited to what was allowed under previous zoning rules, with these restrictions in place until necessary infrastructure upgrades are completed or by December 31, 2030, whichever comes first. Extension areas are temporary, but it’s essential to check your property’s status and plan accordingly. Homeowners planning to build SSMUH should contact Development Services early to determine the specific regulations and permits needed for their project. For more detailed information on SSMUH, including zoning and eligibility, visit: https://www.ladysmith.ca/businessdevelopment/ssmuh. V9G 1A2 GET CONNECTED FIREWORKS PERMIT PROCESS HALLOWEEN SAFETY Ladysmith residents interested in discharging fireworks on Halloween night must use the new application and permitting process that is being administered by the Town. Halloween is meant to be fun; it is also important to prioritize safety so that Halloween is a positive experience for everyone. Here are some essential tips to keep the spookiness safe: The Family Fireworks Permit Application fee is $10. Separate permit classes, fees and insurance requirements apply for display fireworks and pyrotechnics. • Costume Visibility: Pick costumes that are bright or reflective. If your child insists on a dark costume, add reflective tape or carry a flashlight or glow stick. The Family Fireworks Permit replaces the permit process previously overseen by the Cowichan Valley Regional District. Permit approvals are subject to the current Coastal Fire Ban and any corresponding restrictions. • Adult Supervision: Young children should always trickor-treat with a responsible adult. You can obtain application forms at Ladysmith City Hall, located at 410 Esplanade, during business hours (Monday to Friday, 8:30 a.m. – 4:00 p.m.). Forms are also available by emailing info@ladysmith.ca or by visiting this link https://www.ladysmith.ca/docs/default-source/ bylaws-2021/fireworks-permit-application_2022_ fillable.pdf?sfvrsn=77a7f4db_2 Once completed, the forms can be submitted and paid for in person at City Hall. Please note that all fireworks permit applications are due at least three business days before the event. • Stick to the Sidewalk: Walk on sidewalks whenever possible and use crosswalks to cross the street. If there are no sidewalks, walk facing traffic and stay as far to the side as possible. • Plan Your Route: Map out a safe route in advance, avoiding streets with heavy traffic. Make sure children know to only visit well-lit homes and to never enter a stranger’s house or car. • Check Candy: Before indulging in treats, inspect all candy for signs of tampering, such as opened wrappers or unfamiliar brands. Discard anything suspicious. • Stay Alert: Be extra vigilant and aware on streets trick-or-treaters may suddenly appear. DECOMMISSIONING OF MACKIE DAM POSTPONED The Town of Ladysmith has decided to delay the decommission of the abandoned Mackie Dam until summer 2025 in order to protect salmon spawning on Holland Creek. The decision to postpone the removal of the Mackie Dam was made after the Town received new information from the Department of Fisheries and Oceans (DFO) and the Ministry of Water, Land, and Resource Stewardship (WLRS), which indicated that the timing of the removal posed a significant threat to salmon in Holland Creek. While the risk of dam failure remains, it is not imminent, therefore protection of fish spawning is the higher priority. The Town initially planned to begin removal work on September 4, but now the 1.5-kilometre section of the North Loop of the Holland Creek/Heart Lake Trail that was slated to be closed during the removal process will remain open to the public until work begins in 2025. The decision to remove the Mackie Dam stems from the 2023 recommendation from BC Dam Safety that determined decommissioning was the best option from a safety perspective. This portion of the work is expected to cost an estimated $600,000. There will be additional costs associated with the delay, however, those costs are unknown at this time. The project is being funded with a $2.75 million grant that the Town received from the provincial government to remediate three additional weirs along Holland Creek, including the Mackie Dam. BE BEAR AWARE Did you know that garbage and rotting fruit can attract bears to your neighbourhood? Please keep your garbage and compost contained indoors until your scheduled pick-up day and harvest any ripe fruit from trees. Report a bear sighting by calling: 1-877-952-7277. A reminder that there is a 44lb limit on compost bins and garbage cans. A green bin full of fruit can easily put you over that limit.
