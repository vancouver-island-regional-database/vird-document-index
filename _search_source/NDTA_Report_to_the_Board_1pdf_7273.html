BUDGET: Expected revenue for 2019-2020 is approximately $343,000. Of those funds the greatest share, $150,000, is budgeted to address the individual professional development needs of the district’s approximately 1000 teachers and teachers teaching on call. These funds are reimbursed to teachers accordance to Policy and the Principles of Professional Development. A Statement of Disbursements dated January 31, 2020 is included with this report. The middle column reflects revenue and expenditures to January 31. Individual Teachers’ Professional Development Funds: $150,000 2019-2020 is the second year of the three-year cycle for individual teachers’ funds. The NDTA believes that this model, in effect for ten years now, has made the distribution of professional development funds more equitable and accessible to more teachers. It also allows teachers to plan ahead and attend more expensive opportunities that would not be possible with only a single year’s allocation. This year, teachers are able to access funds from all three years of the funding cycle. This amounts to $1000: $400 in the first year and $300 each for the second and third years (pro-rated to FTE). Teachers Teaching on Call (TTOCs) have access to $300 in individual funds annually but are not able to bank their funds for future use. The majority of individual professional development funds went toward supporting members to attend conferences outside of Nanaimo, such as Provincial Specialist Association (PSA) conferences, however funds were also used to attend a variety of other conferences, for PSA membership fees, and to purchase professional resources. For a variety of reasons, we recognize that it is not always possible for our members to travel out of district for Provincial Specialist conferences. This year’s PSA day was held on October 25, 2019. Unlike last year, this time one of the PSAs, the British Columbia Primary Teachers’ Association held its conference in Nanaimo at the convention centre. Over 100 teachers from our district attend this event. To accommodate teachers staying in Nanaimo who did not attend a PSA event, we provided Wellington Secondary School on Provincial Specialist Association Day as a place for teachers to work on selfdirected professional development. This was a very productive option for teachers where some chose to work on self-directed activities, but a majority met to work together and collaborate on Professional Development topics that were relevant to their group’s interests. Capacity Building Grants: $0 Formerly the international conferences grant, this budget line is now used to support teachers in attending a variety of quality professional development opportunities across Canada and abroad. This allows them the ability to make up the missed opportunity for their own professional development by providing professional development. District Day $45,000 This year’s District Day theme was Innovation: Re-imagining Where We Are, the Ecology of Place. More than 35 facilitators offered over 45 sessions on a wide variety of topics: earth science, animals, climate change, anxiety, imagination, eco-education, movement, ocean stewardship and Indigenous science, google suite, Coast Salish songs, coding, outdoor education, gardening, nutrition, brain stress, creative problem-solving, loose parts, Nanaimo museum programs, zero waste, word play, mindfulness, weaving in Coast Salish knowledge, drumming, Coast Salish drum making, building a google site, primary nature programs, creating breakouts, reading roadmaps and Joyful Literacy. We had the opportunity to offer workshops in both French and English this year. To allow for longer choice workshop sessions, we had just one keynote speaker this year. Our keynote speaker, Dr. Gillian Judson, is the executive director of the Centre for Imagination in Research, Culture, and Education as well as an instructor at SFU and the author of multiple books. She spoke about the role of imagination in learning and ecological teaching and learning. Supplemental Release: $45,000 Subject to available funds, the Professional Development Policy allows teachers access to one TTOC release day in order to attend professional development activities on a day where they are regularly scheduled to teach. This is frequently used in conjunction with individual funds to attend workshops and conferences in the lower mainland. Professional Learning Groups (PLGs): $45,000 This funding is to assist colleagues with their needs to collaborate regarding pedagogy, philosophy, or grade and subject professional development initiatives. It is broadly accessed by teachers with similar interests and needs from various worksites. Some teachers participate in multiple groups. Each group is expected to create an action plan for approval and submit a short summary report of their work in June. This summary is then shared on our website so that other teachers can view what topics and activities their colleagues have been working on.
