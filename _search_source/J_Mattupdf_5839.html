Thank you Kelsey, this is very helpful. i would like to take the opportunity to express my concerns via email as you suggested below. My son goes to Mountain View Elementary and we rely on bus service to get him to and from school each day, as do many families in our area. This bus is route is always full (particularly after school) and it is my hope that the School Board will consider other options for transportation service in the area and district. There are many reasons I want to see bus service remain in our area, but the most important reason is for safety of the children. We live in a very rural neighbourhood where the roads are not highly populated and dark in the morning, the threat of bears and cougars exist, and the distance for young children to walk is not realistic. it really is a safety issues, especially in the winter when students are walking in cold weather. Some parents do not have a vehicle to drive their children, or they work and cannot drive them, and moved to the Mountain View district because bus service was available. Some children are too young to ride city transit. I fully understand the education system in BC is underfunded and money should be allocated to providing teachers, educational assistants, and higher learning opportunities to children in our district. However, providing a safe option for children to get to school is also important, and necessary. if the district decides to cut funding to buses in our district, i hope that parents will have an option to pay for bus service. There are many children on this route who live outside the walk limit boundaries, and there are many children who are considered courtesy riders. I am fully aware that my child is considered a "courtesy” rider so I would support a user pay for courtesy riders in the district to offset the budget shortfalls. The option of courtesy riders paying for bus service would allow for bus transportation to continue, alleviate financial pressures, and provide jobs for school district bus drivers. Families who request an accommodated payment plan or who are in extreme hardship can hopefully apply to the transportation department in order to be considered for support. It would not be unreasonable to suggest that a confidential fo||ow—up of the application may be required for those who apply for financial assistance. Could we not use the current ”Z Pass” card to track payment for children who take the bus as courtesy riders? I understand that other districts in the province have faced similar budget issues and moved towards a use pay option for busses and I hope that our district would consider this option rather than eliminating bus service all together. Please consider these options when looking at the budget and making decisions about cutting bus service in our district. Thank you, Janice Mattu Sent from my iPhone
