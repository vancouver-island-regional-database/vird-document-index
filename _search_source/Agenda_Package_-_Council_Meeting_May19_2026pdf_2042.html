EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: This report summarizes the status of three current capital projects, outlines associated budget implications, and recommends reallocating surplus funds to complete outstanding work and advance related capital priorities. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: N/A INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: The City Hall Heat Recovery Ventilation (HRV) installation, the Gateway Signage Lighting Improvements, and the Lot 108 Phase 2 capital projects have each encountered unforeseen technical, safety and cost challenges that have delayed their completion. This report summarizes project outcomes, status, and associated budget impacts to inform decision-making and next steps. City Hall HRV - The scope of work for this project includes the design and installation of a Heat Recovery Ventilation (HRV) system at City Hall to improve indoor air quality throughout the building as identified through Occupational Health and Safety assessments. The HRV system is intended to increase the circulation of fresh air using reheated outside air, reduce indoor contaminants, and improve the building’s energy efficiency. Page 17 of 21 The project has experienced delays due to several unforeseen and interrelated factors. The original HRV design routed ductwork through the building’s basement; however, confined space safety requirements significantly increased projected costs. This necessitated multiple design revisions to identify a solution that could be delivered within the approved budget. Further delays occurred following a hazardous materials incident involving the contractor, which resulted in a WorkSafeBC investigation. The project remains unfinished due to insufficient funding to complete the remaining work and the need to keep the building operational during normal business hours, resulting in work mostly taking place in the evening and on weekends to safely navigate the necessary safety protocols (Original Project Budget: $40,000. Additional funding required to complete: $15,000) Gateway Signage - The scope of work for this project involves the design and installation of solar-powered lighting to enhance the visibility and visual prominence of the “Welcome to Ladysmith” sign at the south entrance to the community. The lighting improvements are intended to create a stronger first impression for visitors while aligning with the Town’s sustainability objectives using renewable energy. During implementation, the project experienced delays due to unforeseen cost impacts associated with the procurement and installation of specialized infrastructure. An engineered pole is required to safely support the solar array panels, along with a concrete foundation designed to meet structural and safety requirements. The installation of this foundation will necessitate additional excavation, trenching, and electrical work beyond what was originally anticipated, resulting in increased costs and extended timelines. (Original Project Budget: $12,000. Additional funding required to complete: $7,000) Lot 108 – Phase 2- Environmental mitigation measures incorporated into the Lot 108 – Phase 2 project include bank stabilization works in accordance with the contract order, grading of approximately 261 metres of drainage ditching to improve dewatering and surface water management throughout the park, and placement of reclaimed asphalt pavement (RAP) to enhance surface stability in the new parking lot and to reduce erosion. Twenty-nine trees will be planted to support the development of a healthier, more natural park environment, contributing to improved canopy cover, ecological function, and longterm environmental resilience. These elements meet the criteria of the Climate Action Reserve as they directly support climate adaptation objectives by improving storm water management, managing extreme precipitation impacts, and improving the overall environmental resilience of the site. Additionally, two sections of the perimeter trail have been identified as being near steep slopes and would benefit with two sections of railing. Municipal Insurance Association Risk Management Grants are available to municipalities for projects that mitigate risk, including recommendations from loss control reports, security upgrades, or specialized training. Staff are recommending utilizing this grant to add two railings along the identified areas. (Current project budget:$857,091.Additional funding required to complete: $33,000). FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: All of the requested budget amendments are using reserve funds provided by the Province that have limitations on their use. The City Hall HRV is using COVID funds, which will leave the reserve at $0. The Climate Action funds can be used to fund up to 80% of a project that supports climate adaptation or mitigation. There is also grant funding available from the Municipal Insurance Association’s Risk Management Grant to fund the installation of two railings around the Forrest Field perimeter walkway.
