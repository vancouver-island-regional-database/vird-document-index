NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 6, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer and Jacquie Poulin, Assistant Superintendent Feeding Futures Fund Background On October 11, 2023, staff provided the Business Committee an overview of the steps taken with respect to the planning for the Feeding Futures Fund. This Information Sheet is intended to provide an update on the status of the program. Discussion Secondary Programs Funds have been allocated to secondary schools to continue existing programs. In addition Cedar Secondary, received a staffing and supplies allocation to provide food. With respect to Wellington, the school is currently in the final steps of instituting a cafeteria program with service provided by a support staff member. The school is currently examining methods of incorporating students into the program. The program should be operational following spring break. Elementary Programs As noted previously, at the elementary level, the District has partnered with the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation to provide prepared breakfast and lunch items to schools. This partnership is building on a relationship already established through the Food4Schools program and expands on an infrastructure already providing some services. The District continues to work with the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation to create and expand upon a space for food preparation at Woodlands. Funding for additional equipment and minor renovations was approved through the Food Infrastructure Program and renovation work has begun. Currently, the program has refrigeration storage, proper food prep tables, and some shelving. The renovations will include a commercial dishwasher, proper sized sinks, and additional storage. Since starting the pilot with just three schools in October, prepared breakfasts and lunches are now being delivered to twenty-five of our elementary schools. For two schools, Gabriola and Qwam Qwum, other arrangements have been made to support their programs through Feeding Futures. Information Sheet: Feeding Futures Fund Page 2 Approximately 1615 breakfasts and 2000 lunches per week are now being delivered to elementary schools across our district. These numbers are higher than initially anticipated. Since the beginning of October, 37 056 breakfasts and lunches have been provided through Feeding Futures to elementary age students. By partnering with Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation, the Feeding Futures fund is complemented with Food4Schools resources and donations. Feedback from schools continues to be positive. Having nutritious, prepared breakfast and lunch options arrive ready to hand out has been welcomed. Schools are reporting that staff on site are able to be providing more direct support to students as they are spending less time with food preparation. Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation is also quite responsive to feedback in regards to food options which is reducing waste and ensuring students are receiving items they like.
