MINUTES OF A MEETING OF THE MUNICIPAL SERVICES COMMITTEE MONDAY, MAY 8, 2017 CALL TO ORDER 6:30 P.M. COUNCIL CHAMBERS, CITY HALL COUNCIL MEMBERS PRESENT: Councillor Rob Hutchins (chair) Mayor Aaron Stone Councillor Cal Fradin Councillor Joe Friesenhan Councillor Duck Paterson STAFF PRESENT: Guillermo Ferrero Clayton Postings CALL TO ORDER AGENDA APPROVAL MS 2017-026 MINUTES MS 2017-027 Felicity Adams Joanna Winter Councillor Steve Arnett Councillor Carol Henderson Erin Anderson Sue Bouma Councillor Hutchins called this Meeting of Council to order at 6:30 p.m. Moved and seconded: That the agenda for this May 8, 2017 meeting of the Municipal Services Committee be approved. Motion carried. Moved and seconded: That the minutes of the Municipal Services Committee meeting held January 23, 2017 be approved. Motion carried. MS 2017-028 Moved and seconded: That the minutes of the Municipal Services Committee meeting held January 30, 2017 be approved. Motion carried. MS 2017-029 Moved and seconded: That the minutes of the Municipal Services Committee meeting held March 13, 2017 be approved. Motion carried. DELEGATION Sheila Malcolmson, M.P. Derelict and Abandoned Vessels Private Member’s Bill – overview and request for letter of support Ms. Malcolmson played a video of the removal of the Viki Lynne II from the Ladysmith Harbour, and made a presentation regarding her Derelict and Abandoned Vessels Private Member’s Bill. Council thanked Ms. Malcolmson for her presentation, involvement and cooperation with the issue of abandoned and derelict vessels. Moved and seconded: MS 2017-030 That the proposed resolution supporting private member’s legislation on derelict and abandoned vessels be added to the next Council meeting agenda. Motion carried. REPORTS Role and Meeting Format of Municipal Services Committee (discussion led by Mayor Stone and Councillor Hutchins) Councillor Hutchins led a discussion on the role of the Municipal Services Committee meetings, citing the effectiveness of these meetings to deal with strategic and emerging items, as well as those that require more in-depth discussions. He recommended that the meetings also take a thematic approach, focusing on one department at a time, when possible. Watershed Stewardship Initiatives – Discussion Mayor Stone led a discussion regarding re-engaging in watershed stewardship to protect the community watershed. MS 2017-031 Moved and seconded: That the Mayor, Councillor Hutchins and other interested Council members meet with the Ladysmith Watershed Round Table and other stakeholders to review options on governance structure regarding watershed stewardship, and report back to Council by September 30, 2017. Motion carried. Updated Parks Usage Bylaw MS 2017-032 Moved and seconded: That the Committee recommend that Council direct staff to prepare a new Parks Usage Bylaw which reflects the comments provided with respect to definitions of graffiti and liquor. Motion carried. Municipal Services Committee May 8, 2017 Page 2 Social Procurement Policy MS 2017-033 Moved and seconded: That the Committee: 1. Refer consideration of a proposed social procurement policy to an upcoming meeting of the Municipal Services Committee; and 2. Request staff to provide feedback from Cumberland and Qualicum Beach with respect to the effect of their social procurement policies on decision-making and the weighting allocated to social benefits. Motion carried. Proposed Poultry Bylaw MS 2017-034 Moved and seconded: That the Committee refer consideration of a proposed poultry bylaw to the next Municipal Services Committee meeting in order for staff to gather more information about bylaw complaints and related issues from neighbouring poultry-friendly communities, including Saanich, Duncan, North Cowichan, Parksville and Nanaimo. Motion carried. ADJOURNMENT MS 2017-035 Moved and seconded: That this meeting of the Municipal Services Committee adjourn at 8:13 p.m. Motion carried. CERTIFIED CORRECT: Chair (Councillor R.
