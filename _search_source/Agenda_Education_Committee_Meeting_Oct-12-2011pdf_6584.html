CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Minutes - October 14, 2011 Page 4 of 9 Agenda Item Number 6.1 2 Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held September 14, 2011 EDUCATIONAL UPDATE Report from Ms. Frisson and Ms. Southwick All Day Kindergarten Ms. Southwick told the Committee that this year, ten schools started the full-day Kindergarten program, joining the 19 other schools that started the program last year. The district held a forum in May for the schools that had the program last year, in order to collect tips and advice for the schools that are starting the program this year. Over the summer, the district moved portables to create space in buildings for full-day Kindergarten at four schools, and conversion work was done at a number of other schools. Ms. Southwick also gave the Committee preliminary information on enrolment. She said that the preliminary enrolment numbers show an increase over projections of about 130 elementary students, while secondary enrolment is very close to projections. Because the district is receiving funding protection this year from the Ministry of Education, the higher-than-projected enrolment does not result in any additional funding. Threat Assessment Update CORRESPONDENCE REFERRED FROM THE REGULAR BOARD MEETING UNFINISHED BUSINESS Ms. Frisson told the Committee that five years ago, the district started training staff in threat assessment procedures. Now, all administrators and a number of counsellors have received level one training and many have received level two training. This summer, four district staff members received the necessary training so that they can now provide level one and two training sessions themselves. The value of a threat assessment program is it is a tool that addresses student behavior issues on a variety of levels. There was no correspondence referred from the August Board Meeting. There was no Unfinished Business on the Agenda this evening.
