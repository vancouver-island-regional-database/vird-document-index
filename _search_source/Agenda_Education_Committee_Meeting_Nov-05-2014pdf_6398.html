Page 7 of 14 7.2 Eye See … Eye Learn® What is Eye See…Eye Learn® (ESEL)? Eye See … Eye Learn® is a program provided by the British Columbia Association of Optometrists (BCAO) that encourages families of Kindergarten children to have their child’s eyes examined. Eye exams are covered under the province’s Medical Services Plan (MSP) for children 18 years of age and under. If a family does not have MSP coverage, BCAO covers the exam service for them at no charge, and if a Kindergarten child requires eyeglasses after an eye exam, they are fitted with a pair at no charge. What is the purpose of Eye See … Eye Learn®? The purpose of Eye See … Eye Learn® is to help remove barriers to children’s academic and developmental success by providing accurate diagnosis of eye and vision problems at an early age, and if prescribed, prescription eyeglasses at no charge. The BCAO and its members believe that good vision and eye health is essential to success for young children. Our responsibility is to deliver those services in an effective manner to as many young children as possible. Eye See … Eye Learn® has been in Alberta since 2003 and in Ontario since 2009. In Alberta, ESEL is now in every elementary school in the province. Is Eye See … Eye Learn® in other school districts? Eye See … Eye Learn® is now in 6 school districts in BC: Coquitlam, Burnaby, New Westminster, Central Okanagan, Greater Victoria and Cowichan Valley. Currently, 1 in every 5 Kindergarten children in BC are covered by Eye See … Eye Learn®. In January of 2015, Abbotsford and Maple Ridge/Pitt Meadows will be added to the program. At that time, 1 in every 4 Kindergarten children in BC will be covered under the program. How does the program work? In participating school districts, families of Kindergarten children receive a parent letter introducing them to the program. Parents select a participating BCAO doctor convenient to them and call directly to make an appointment. The window of availability for families is throughout the entire school year. The campaign would include anti-bullying support materials, professional development opportunities, and presentations for parents, staff, and students. The DPAC representatives asked for Board support for the project, including $15,000 in financial support. At the June 25, 2014 Regular Board Meeting, the Board approved the following motion from the Education Committee: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) commit a minimum of $15,000 from the 2014-2015 operating budget for the School District 68 Anti-Bullying Campaign and that plan is also predicated on staff development on the second Success For All day in January 2015; and further we ask that staff make this part of the plan.” In meeting with DPAC, the DPAC Anti-bullying Committee, and Trustee Welch, the Department of Learning Services recommends the following phasing in of the joint initiative with DPAC Anti-Bullying Parent Communication Program: Phase 1 October to June 2014-15: The Department of Learning Services will work directly with DPAC and the DPAC Antibullying Committee to develop, distribute and disseminate to staff and parents the Antibullying Support brochures. The Department of Learning Services will copy brochures as an ‘in kind’ donation to DPAC and this joint initiative. District Senior Staff will oversee all presentations and communiques to district staff. Phase 2 September to June 2015-16: DPAC will prepare a presentation package for the Board of Education presenting a timeline of presentations for staff and parents including a monetary request to support the initiative. Phase 3 September to June 2016-17: DPAC will prepare a presentation package to the Board of Education presenting a timeline of presentations for students, including a monetary request to support the initiative. Recommendation: The Department of Learning Services recommends that the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public School Board approve Phase 1 of the DPAC Anti-bullying Communication Program, including the ‘in kind’ work of the Department of Learning Services.
