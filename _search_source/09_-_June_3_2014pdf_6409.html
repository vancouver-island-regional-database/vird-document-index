CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Jeanette Pongratz-Doyle RE: DPAC Anti-Bullying Campaign Ms. Pongratz-Doyle, a representative of theDistrict Parents Advisory Council made a presentation to the Board’s Education Committee putting forward a proposal to conduct a year-long anti-bullying campaign next year that would include partnerships with the district, CUPE, the NDTA, and the community. The campaign would include materials for parents and staff, along with professional development opportunities and presentations for parents and students. The DPAC representatives asked for Board support for the project, including $15,000 in financial support. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Welch Folio E14/06/04-03 That the Education Committee refers the matter of SD68 DPAC Anti-Bullying Campaign, including the two motions for consideration, to New Business on tonight's agenda. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Eco School - Hammond Bay Ms. Lisa Frey Ms. Lisa Frey, principal of Departure Bay Elementary, spoke to the Education Committee about the plans for her school to become an ecoschool. She said that this has been a year of exploring the proposal with the school community, with plans for a pilot next year leading to applying for academy school status for the 2015-16 school year. Although the idea was only proposed this year, with the help of community partners the school has already carried out a number of Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held June 3, 2014 environmentally-focused projects, including electronic recycling, Earth Day celebration, Bike to School Week, Science Saturday and a waste art exhibit. Ms. Frey said that the proposal ties in well with the B.C. Education Plan, promoting student engagement and student-centred learning and personalized learning. She said that eco-school graduates will have a clear sense of their place in the environment. E23 District Wide Assessment Ms. Laura Tait Ms. Tait, District Principal for Aboriginal Education, gave a presentation about the E23 initiative this year that brought together all teachers in grade level groups, Kindergarten to Grade 3, for five mornings of collaboration focusing on reading. The goal of the initiative, which is expected to continue next year, is to develop a culture of collaboration, building a community and developing common understandings. It is believed that this is the first time that all teachers at each grade level K-3 have had the opportunity to collaborate with their colleagues at the same grade level. As part of the E23 process, StrongStart facilitators also had an opportunity to meet together in sessions led by Kindergarten teachers. French Immersion Update - Mr. Peter Hewitt Mr. Peter Hewitt, principal of North Oyster Elementary gave the Education Committee an update on French programs in the district. Mr. Hewitt said that Late French Immersion will be offered at the Grade 6 level next year at Ecole Quarterway, with 25 students currently registered.
