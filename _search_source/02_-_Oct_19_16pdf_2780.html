CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Trustee Brzovic: JBCS - attended PAC meeting - found it was a good reminder about ministry funding; what used to be funded by the government and was PAC's are now expected to cover as there no longer is ministry funding. There is a lot of fundraising happening. There was an NDSS/JBCS football classic game fundraiser. Schools are preparing for Halloween. There was a successful Terry Fox run. Trustee Solomon: Has nothing to add at this time. Trustee Kimler: LIS  VIU International Students – All three schools in Ladysmith hosted 32 VIU Masters in Education Leadership students and participated in an information exchange about differences in education, language & culture. Students from India, China, Mauritius, Saudi Arabia, Kenya and Mexico helped to broaden horizons for the Canadian students.  Featured Student – An LIS student was featured for an article in the Ladysmith Chronicle that focused on the importance of kids washing their hands to help reduce spread of communicable diseases such as colds and flu. Draft Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held October 19, 2016  Leadership Ambassadors – Work hard at generating community and school spirit. The focus this year will be to improve pride students have in their school and reaching out to younger students by being role models and showing them they care. LPS  New Website – LPS rolled out a new website design in early October. ENO  Power Up Workshop – PAC sponsored Kerri Isham, a certified sexual health educator, who hosted a K-7 workshop on September 27 from 6-8PM “Body Smart – From Child to Teen”. This is part of a Power-Up interactive workshop for parents. LSS  Aboriginal Students Visit – LSS hosted 35 Maori students & their chaperones from New Zealand on Sept 28th. There was a whole school assembly and cultural exchange with a welcoming ceremony, students played a bone game & shared languages. The event was a heartwarming First Nations culture exchange.  Cultural Art Project “Nutsumaat sayaays” with master carver, John Marston is nearly complete, with 3 of the 4 pillars installed. (The fourth requires specialty brackets and should be up soon). Many thanks to Pete Sabo & his team for working to get the pillars installed in time for the New Zealanders.  Internet Safety – Darren Laur, RCMP Staff Sargeant w/over 29 years of policing experience will host two sessions on Internet Safety & Digital Literacy this Thursday. Trustee Bob: Had an opportunity to attend Wellington when MLA Stilwell came by and toured the school. All Elementary schools are working hard at fundraising; they're using some interesting social medial online fundraising tools. Uplands is expanding their park. They're also working on a Guest to Know in exploring Draft Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held October 19, 2016 leadership and interests. Trustee Bob will be a part of that initiative. Chamber Young Professionals of Nanaimo are working on a human library again with Trustee Robinson.
