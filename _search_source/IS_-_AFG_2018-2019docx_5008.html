BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools INFORMATION SHEET CONFIDENTIAL TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Mike Ross, Facilities Planning Manager DATE: March 7, 2018 SUBJECT: 2018/19 Annual Facility Grant (AFG) REFERENCE:  2018-19 AFG Plan (attached) BACKGROUND: Each year the Ministry of Education announces the amount of the AFG, to be used by the Districts for projects that meet Ministry of Education Policy 1.1.100. DISCUSSION: The Ministry of Education is expected to announce ‘status quo’ AFG funding for 18/19 in the value of $2,707,680. The grant is typically reduced by a contribution of $47,298 toward the continuing implementation of Capital Asset Management Services and further, $94,595 for the Next Generation Network (NGN). The net amount of the grant is expected to remain equal to the funding received in 2017/18. Plans for the expenditure of the AFG funding were developed considering the following:  AFG criteria  Priority support for the Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update.  Alignment with Ministry Capital Submissions and planning  Alignment with Learning Services priorities  Facilities condition reports  Reported facilities issues  Request from Schools You will note that an overall program contingency is not apparent on the summary listed below. In accordance with Ministry reporting procedures, the contingency shown on the detailed project plan is apportioned to the projects listed on the sheet. This also applies to the administration item. The incorporation of an overall contingency allows staff to be flexible with project budgets and allows room for further alignment with other programs as well as room for the addition of emergent projects as they arise. Also note that many of the projects include some design and construction and that the budgets, in some cases are Notional, this would see the project estimate include expected soft costs, design contingency and construction contingency and may not be reflective of the final ‘construction only’ cost.
