An Overview December 2023 Our HISTORY • Established in October 2022 • Registered as a BC Society in February 2023 • 4 Board Directors • 20+ Paid Members • 450+ social media contacts/followers Our MANDATE ADVOCATING FOR THE RIGHTS OF CITIZENS TO PEACE, SAFETY AND SECURITY IN THEIR HOMES, PUBLIC SPACES, AND BUSINESSES IN NANAIMO. Why NAPSA Exists • Extreme rise in Violent Crime Severity Index since 2020. • Escalation in open-air drug use, fires, arson, vandalism, theft, and random violence. • Citizens are fed up feeling like prisoners in their own neighbourhoods. • Citizens are being traumatized as they witness drug crimes, violence amongst street-entrenched, overdoses, and overdose deaths. Figure 1. Crime Severity Statistics for Nanaimo compared to other jurisdictions, 2017 to 2021. WHAT WE WANT 1. For Nanaimo to be a safe city for young people, elderly, families, working people with diverse cultural and socioeconomic backgrounds. 2. For all three levels of Government – Municipal, Provincial, and Federal Government to treat the overdose crisis like the Public Health & Safety EMERGENCY that it is. 3. For Governments to recognize the intrinsically linked and overlapping social crises that are producing the current Public Health & Safety Emergency, and act in a coordinated, cooperative fashion across party-lines: • Addictions and Mental Health • Housing and Affordability • Justice System and Law Enforcement 4. For Nanaimo’s MLAs and MP to recognize the need for public accountability and transparency in the non-profit industry and work towards de-escalating the crises rather than capitalizing on, and inducing, growing societal needs. 5. For Nanaimo’s MLAs to display visible, action-oriented leadership in getting what Nanaimo needs to improve public safety in all of Nanaimo and area.
