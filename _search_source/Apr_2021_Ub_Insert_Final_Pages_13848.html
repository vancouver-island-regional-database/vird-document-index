The public washroom is being built in the alleyway between 521 and 531 1st Avenue making it within short walking distance from many shops and restaurants. The exterior design of the washroom will feature hardy plank siding and landscaping to complement the neighouring downtown businesses and amenities. Inside, all of the features will be touchless, helping to limit the spread of bacteria and decreasing unnecessary water usage. The downtown washroom aligns with Council’s strategic priority for implementing and supporting projects that enhance the downtown. It is also included in our FiveYear Financial Plan. Each year between January and April, Public Works flushes both water supply mains and distribution water mains. Potable water is flushed at a high velocity through the water mains to ensure all piping is refreshed and minor sediment within the pipes is removed. This is one of the methods relied upon by the Town to help keep our drinking water safe and clean. During the flushing period, water users may experience some discolouration of the water supply. Running a cold tap for a short period will help restore water quality back to normal. If you or someone you know depends on medical equipment that uses water, please call Public Works at 250-245-6445 for flushing dates. Yearly water main flushing is also a reminder to clean the screens on your faucets as well as the pressure reducing valve (where water enters the house). This should be done on an annual basis.
