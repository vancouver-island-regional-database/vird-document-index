Discussion Focus for 2025-26 The Committee set the following actions contained in the Plan as priorities for action in 2025-26: 1. Reduce consumption of resources. Specifically, focusing on the development of an Administrative Procedure on land use guidelines in the district; By approving this recommendation, the Board is directing staff to create an AP on Land use Guidelines. This AP would be in alignment with Policy 104 – Environmental Stewardship. 2. Support learning opportunities that educate about and strengthen food security. Similarly, this recommendation will focus staff time in supporting both educational and potential infrastructure support from existing, new and/or reallocated funding sources. Action Sheet: Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability Advisory Committee Page 2 3. Focusing on GHG Emissions reduction. This recommendation will ensure that funding is allocated to address the issue of GHG reduction, ensure staff time focuses on applying for additional funding sources and if additional funding is available (e.g. surplus) that the Board is provided with options to support the strategy. 2025-26 Workplan The proposed workplan is attached as Appendix “A”. The workplan, directly tied to the proposed revised terms of reference, is intended to ensure that the Committee will meet in advance of the Board being asked to make major financial decisions as well as in advance of the Board approving the work plan. The timing is intended to align with important dates for the Board (e.g. workplan, budget and surplus). Terms of Reference The proposed terms of reference is attached as Appendix “B”. Like the changes previously approved by the Board to the Safe School Committee, the recommended terms of reference are intended to focus the work of the Committee, alleviate staff workload and ensure the effective use of volunteer committee member time. It should be noted that the Student Voice Circle may also address issues associated with Environmental Sustainability. However, those priorities will be determined by student voice.
