NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 6, 2024 Education Committee Don Balcombe, Assistant Superintendent Outdoor Education Board Goal To be a leader in Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability. Objective Increase the opportunity for outdoor education and programming in the district. Overview Within the board’s goal to be a leader in Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability, the Department of Learning Services explored the topic of Outdoor Education and how students and staff can learn on and from the land in a safe and sustainable way. Strategies within the Operational Plan were developed to meet the objective of increasing opportunities for outdoor education and programming in the district. During the 2021-2022, and 2022-2023 school years, the district supported teachers to complete training in Outdoor Education. This included seventy-two teachers certified with the Outdoor Council of Canada (OCC) Hiking Field Leader Level One. In addition, several teachers have completed courses to better prepare them to lead Outdoor Education programs, including Wilderness First Aid, Ocean and Marine Training, Remote First Aid, and Lake Water Advanced Instructor Certificate. We continue to invest in the staff who are leading our outdoor experiences for our learners. Release time for teachers to collaborate and provide mentorship helps build capacity throughout our system. Schools continue to expand learning opportunities for students, both at the elementary and secondary levels. When surveyed, elementary schools indicated that most divisions are spending more time outside learning on the land. Schools report that there has been a growth in outdoor learning opportunities this year compared to previous school years. Secondary schools are also continuing to expand outdoor education opportunities for students. Schools with established Outdoor Education programs continue to see high interest among students. Cedar Secondary Community School runs two classes each year, Ladysmith Secondary typically runs four classes of Outdoor Education programming each year, and NDSS offers five or six classes each year. John Barbsy Community School received support in 2022/2023 to offer two classes of Outdoor Education and was able to fill two complete classes for 2023/2024. Wellington Secondary has grown from three classes in 2022/2023 to four classes in 2023/2024. Dover Bay received support for two of their six Outdoor Education courses in 2022/2023 and grew to ten classes for the 2023/2024 year.
