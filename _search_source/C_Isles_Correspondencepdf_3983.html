_______________________________________ From: Kim Iles [kiles@island.net] Sent: February-28-14 10:02 AM To: Dot Neary Subject: Harewood School Art After three months, we do have an answer to the question can the community pay for the supplies, and have local students learn about community history while making art to beautify a public eyesore. Time has passed, the project it dead, however I do want to comment on the process involved in reaching the decison. Two comments: 1. Thanks for your help. It shouldn't have been necessary. (See next comment.) It is sad that our students and community will miss out on a great opportunity due to lack of school district support...minimal support at that. Life happens, though. We have not decided if we will even hold a 'party' - the Teacher's Historical group has had to put their things in storage, including things from Harewood School. Pity that. 2. Of more concern to me is the process the school board used to reach this decision. One would think someone might be interested in actually talking to our group about our plans. If the problem was insurance re people inside the school, perhaps we could have paid for a day of insurance for someone to put the art into the windows. Perhaps plans of the school could have provided much of the information the teacher needed to prepare the art. Who knows what might have happened given a real conversation. I hope the school district treats other potential partners, particularly those wishing to do good things for you better than they treated the Harewood Neighbourhood Association. I know people are busy busy busy. However, when we don't work together, as schools, citizens and community groups, far less happens for our children. That is sad for everyone.
