NEW BUSINESS 2013-2014 Amended Budget Mr. Turin reviewed the school district’s amended budget for the 2013-14 school year and said that school districts are required to prepare an amended budget once the funding for September 30th enrolment is finalized. Mr. Turin said that overall, there is no new money for our district this year. Enrolment in the district as of September 30 was 13,174, which is 89 students fewer than had been projected in the preliminary budget. Mr. Turin said that because it is projected that the district will be facing a shortfall in next year’s budget of $5.6 million, this year’s amended budget has earmarked $2.5 million to be carried forward to address that shortfall. This includes $1.5 million surplus carried forward from last year, plus an additional $1 million to be saved through a “clawback” in this year’s spending. The “clawback” is made up of $400,000 in staffing contingencies and $600,000 in services and supplies. Mr. Turin said that this leaves about $3 million in adjustments that will have to be made to balance next year’s budget and that we are facing a very difficult situation and will have to look at major restructuring. To emphasize this commitment, the following beliefs and values and budget principles will be employed by the District in the development of its annual operating budget. Budget Beliefs & Values 1. We believe that schools exists for learners. 2. We believe in providing a balanced curriculum to the whole learner. 3. We believe that learning is a process that individuals can and should pursue throughout their lifetimes. 4. We believe that safe and healthy environments contribute to student learning. 5. We value the pursuit of excellence in teaching and learning. 6. We believe that education is a shared responsibility among government, school, home and community. 7. We believe educational leadership is important in sustaining educational programs. 8. We value the dedication of our educators and support staff and the accomplishments of our learners. Budget Principles 1. The allocation of human and material resources should be directed first toward improving student learning. 2. The allocation of resources should respond to the diversity of student needs and the vulnerability of our learners 3. Resources should be provided to all learners at equitable and sustainable levels. 4. Program choices and adjustments should be made in the best interests of students and should be guided by credible research, successful professional past practice and thoughtful implementation of new or emerging practices. 5. Student learning is best served when adequate staffing exists at all levels of the organization and the necessary infrastructure supports are in place. Page 12 of 40 11.5 Budget Considerations 1. Overriding Budget Consideration The budget must be developed in accordance with all legal and legislative requirements. 2. How to provide support for the Strategic Plan, School Education Plans and the District Achievement Contract. 3. How to continue providing support for vulnerable students. 4. Recognize that, given the funding constraints facing the District, the future of certain facilities needs to be considered as a strategy for fiscal management. 5. How to support learning alternatives and distance education programs to ensure access to quality education to every student in the District. 6. How to maintain a level of District and school level management and leadership support required for an organization of this size and complexity. 7. How to provide a professional growth environment that supports succession planning for leadership at all levels of the organization. 8. How to maintain adequate service levels for non-instructional areas such as building maintenance, custodial services and technology support. 9. Examine all programs and/or services for the purpose of finding budget savings while continuing to provide adequate support for positive student learning. 10. Monitor the efficiency of the District’s current practice and approach to the allocation of learning resources and assets. 11. How to provide support for initiatives for working with community agencies to address the needs of learners in the early years and to promote safe, caring, orderly and healthy schools. 12. How to develop effective strategies for improving class size and class composition ratios in the district. Page 36 of 40 11.8 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET TO: The Business Committee FROM: Chad Dalrymple, Energy Manager DATE: Feb 13, 2014 SUBJECT: Variance from SD68 Purchasing Policy for Wellington Seismic Project Tender REFERENCE: Purchasing Procedure No.4500P section 4.1 4.1 Immediately on receipt of submission, the date and time of receipt shall be recorded on the tender envelope. The initials of the employee receiving the tender shall be affixed in close proximity to the date/time stamp. Purchasing Procedure No.4500P section 5.1 5.1 Tender openings shall be witnessed by two or more school district employees, one of which must be a purchasing department employee. All persons present at the opening, (including non-school district employees) shall be recorded on the tender opening form. BACKGROUND: Unitech has been hired through an extensive RFP process to be our Construction Manager (CM) for the Wellington Seismic project. Under construction management, the CM manages the project on our behalf, which includes organizing and managing the procurement processing. It is Unitech’s recommendation that we tender a majority of the work under one tender with 38 subcontracts. DISCUSSION: SD68’s purchasing policy is written in a manner in which it doesn’t consider the Construction Manager model for procurement. To manage complex construction jobs, we are required to waiver certain sections of our purchasing policy for this project only. Section 4 – Receipt of Tenders As Unitech is our agent, acting as our CM, they want to process the tenders through their office in Vancouver and also accept electronic tender submission. Under this model, there will be no “employee receiving the tender” as well as our policy assumes only paper submissions. Page 37 of 40 11.8 Section 5 – Tender Opening Our policy requires two district employees be present at the opening of tenders, with one being from the purchasing department. Under the CM frame work, Unitech is managing the procurement process on our behalf, so we do not need two employees to be present for tender openings. Recommended Variance: To waiver sections 4 and 5 of procedure N0. 4500P for the Wellington Seismic Project and have the Construction Manager: • Prepare tender documents, writes up the Request for Tenders (RFT), conducts and closes the RFT process, reviews received tenders (paper and electronic) for completeness and conformity and prepares tender summaries and recommendations to the District. Review the tender results with the Project Manager. School District responsibility: • Our Buyer will post the tender documents on BC Bid Website, and our Project Manager will attend the opening of the tenders. Process for Approval As this project has already been approved by the Board, if the lowest bid is recommended, the Secretary Treasurer will sign off approval. If a bid other than the low bidder is recommended for award, the Board will need to sign off approval. Upon approval, the CM prepares the letter of intent and CCDC 17 contracts for signatures by the trade contractors and then the District. The School District will ultimately hold the contracts with the trades, not the CM. RECOMMENDATION: That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo – Ladysmith) that this purchasing variance be adopted for the Wellington Seismic Project.
