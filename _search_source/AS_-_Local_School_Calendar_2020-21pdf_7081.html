NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION REGULAR OPEN MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 11, 2020 Board of Education Superintendent/Acting Secretary-Treasurer, Scott Saywell Local School Calendar 2020-2021 Recommendation That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the attached draft 2020/21 Local School Calendar. Background On the February 13, 2020, the Business Committee passed the following motion: “That the Business Committee recommend that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the attached draft 2020/21 Local School Calendar, in principle, while the district continues to receive feedback, and that finalized document be brought forward to the regular March Board meeting. This will maintain the Ministry timeline for calendars to be submitted by March 31, 2020.” The draft calendar was shared via email and district news to staff, School Messenger email directly to families and social media. It was also shared with the Leaders for Learning group. Feedback was requested through email until March 10, 2020. The district received 46 emails from the community. Feedback primarily focused on the following topics (in order of most frequency): - Majority agree with two-week spring break (more family time – rest). Heard from teachers/families. General agreement with calendar. Incorporate Easter holidays with spring break. Agreement that February professional learning days align with family day weekend. Some disagree citing childcare costs. Suggestion to move professional learning days. Shows minimum instruction hours, but what is actual? Coincide non-instructional days with holidays or weekends so parents don’t need to find childcare. Keep two-week spring break but split the weeks (one in November). - Increase number of instructional hours. Saved funds from two-week spring break could be used for EA professional learning – to be on par with teachers. Align spring break with Cowichan (one week earlier). Suggestion to work three months on and 1 month off (like Australia). Break earlier before Christmas. Comments about early dismissal days and the hours shortened as a result (ie lunch).
