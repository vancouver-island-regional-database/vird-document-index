ATTACHMENT D TOWN OF LADYSMITH POLICIES AND PROCEDURE MANUAL TOPIC: BEER GARDEN & SPECIAL OCCASION LICENCE APPLICATIONS APPROVED BY: DATE: February 17, 2014 RESOLUTION #: 2014-087 (Amended) Res. 99-595 Risk Management Policy adopted (includes insurance requirements) POLICY: Permitted areas for Special Occasion Licences are as follows: Aggie Field, Forrest Field, Aggie Hall and Transfer Beach Park. The Director of Parks, Recreation and Culture has the authority to approve Special Occasion Licences. 1. Each application must include: a) b) c) d) e) f) g) h) i) j) k) 2. an accompanying letter detailing the event(s) for which the licence is being required; the location of the licence area; the hours of operation; confirmation that tables, chairs, benches, picnic tables, etc., have been or can be obtained, if necessary; number and locations of portable toilets, if applicable; details of control of entrants and exits; confirmation that staff will be provided to ensure vehicles are parking in a safe and legal manner, if necessary; name of event coordinator; number and location of extra garbage containers, if necessary; refundable damage deposit of $100.00 for clubs/businesses and $100.00 for family events; assurance that food is available to patrons. Conditions of Licence: a) b) c) d) e) f) g) h) the area created for the licence to be enclosed and patrolled to the satisfaction of the RCMP, which must include suitable barriers; the hours of operation to be between 11:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. (Aggie Field) and 11:00 a.m.. - 10:00 p.m. (Transfer Beach) a minimum of two hours closure of the licensed area prior to any other liquor permits being issued in the area (i.e. the area could be defined as the Community Park); concessions to be allowed to remain open for beyond the closing of a beer garden; licences not to exceed four days in duration for any one event; once an application has been approved, no change(s) will be allowed unless Council approval has been obtained; prior to holding the event, submit to the municipality evidence of public liability insurance coverage of at least Two Million ($2,000,000.00) Dollars with the municipality as an additional named insured and complying with all insurance requirements for the special event. That applicants promote “a game plan for a safe drive home” for their event. Applicants should obtain from our Parks and Recreation Department an information package which has been compiled by ICBC under their Counter Attack program. 09 – 4320 - A BEER GARDEN & SPECIAL OCCASION LICENCE APPLICATIONS TOPIC: PAGE 2 OF 2 DATE: February 17, 2014 CANCELLATION OF LICENCE: The licence can be immediately revoked by order of the RCMP, if in the opinion of the RCMP that the license is being abused, conditions of the licence are not being met, or it is in the public interest to revoke the licence. 09 – 4320 - A
