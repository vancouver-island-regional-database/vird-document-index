CHAIRPERSON’S REPORT WEDNESDAY, OCTOBER 27TH, 2010 At the end of the second month of our new school year we find ourselves in exciting times. From individual school initiatives to broader-based provincial initiatives we have cause to celebrate, to investigate and to ponder. I want to congratulate the Ladysmith Secondary STAAR students and their teachersponsor Guy Tohana for their outstanding work in raising awareness and supporting diversity in their school and community. To this end the group received the Diversity Championship award at the Diversity Gala last month awarded by the Central Vancouver Island Multicultural Society. The STAAR students have done some outstanding work and well deserve the recognition. Pleasant Valley Elementary School held a Potluck Dinner for their school community on October 14th and they met with resounding success. The gym was full of parents, children, staff and wonderful dishes of food. Staff acknowledged the work of individuals who had contributed to the beginning successes of the Clan Initiative. On October 20th, 2010 we had the opportunity to host a celebration for the re-opening of Rotary Bowl after the completion of the re-surfacing project. What a track!! It is a super asset for our students and community. There were approximately 30 people in attendance and myself and Mr. Al Johnston of the Track and Field Club cut the ribbon. Donations for the project came from: Nanaimo & District Track and Field Club - $60.000 Sikh Temple -$1,500 Bastion Running Club - $1,500 Nanaimo Community Foundation - $15,000 Coastal Community Credit Union - $5,000 School Community Connections Program – a partnership of the Union of B.C. Municipalities, the B.C. School Trustees Association and the Province of B.C. up to 25% of the project and the City of Nanaimo which participated in the grant application for that program School District #68(Nanaimo-Ladysmith) - up to $100,000.00 There were many happy faces at the celebration-an excellent example of jointpartnership. Page 2 Finally, this past Friday, October 22, 2010, I attended a Ministry of Education Conference at the Pan Pacific Hotel in Vancouver. The purpose was for the Ministry to talk about their vision of 21st century learning and, in particular, personalized learning. There was also opportunity for table discussion and questions and answers. The Ministry representatives’ (James Gorman and Rod Allen) presentation included a comparison of what we do now and what we might do with an acknowledgment that there was already a great deal of teacher inquiry and personalized learning occurring in our districts. A shift would include several significant reforms with a new emphasis on parent engagement. This movement could also include an emphasis on new forms of schools. The key word was flexibility! Students need to be taught how to learn rather than be taught content. While writing, numeracy and literacy are very important other skills must be addressed such as –critical thinking, problem solving, creativity and innovation, collaboration, teamwork, leadership, cross-cultural understanding, communications, computing, IT literacy and career and learning self-reliance. Three questions that the delegates were asked to discuss were: 1. What are the most exciting aspects of the Ministry vision for personalized learning in the 21st century? How will the students benefit? 2. What will be needed to successfully translate the vision into action in your district? 3. What do the Ministry, BCSTA and other partner groups need to do now to further shape the vision? There were many responses. There is an appreciation that conversation is taking place before final decisions are made in the spirit of co-governance. One trustee was both terrified and electrified - and I heard the acknowledgment by the Ministry officials that in order to have true personalized learning there might have to be less centralized, prescriptive curriculum and more authentic assessment. Much to investigate and ponder! The BCSTA will take the next step and ask all districts to identify areas of personalized learning that are taking place within the district. These will be collated and shared and provide a foundation to help shape the vision. This will be our Secretary-Treasurer’s last Board meeting with us. As I mentioned in my September report, Mr. Green has served us well and we wish him all the best in his new position in Langley. To everyone – have a safe and happy Hallowe’en!
