Non-Replacement – Budget Strategy # 10 Budget Strategy #10 Implementation  Replacement of secretaries  For schools with only one secretary on staff, the person will be replaced every time.  For schools with more than one secretary on staff, there will be no replacement for the first day of absence. However, school-based principals have the flexibility to decide if there is a need to replace a clerical person because of special circumstances. Budget Strategy #10 Implementation  Replacement of EAs (including Aboriginal EAs)  EA 3 won’t be replaced on the first day unless the back-up person is also absent.  EA 2 who were identified by principals as essential because of safety issues or educational needs of children will need to be replaced. Otherwise, you need to direct your EAs not to request a replacement for their first day of absence. However, school-based principals have the flexibility to decide if there is a need to replace an EA because of special circumstances. Budget Strategy #10 Implementation  Replacement of elementary and secondary nonenrolling teachers (including Aboriginal support teachers)  Full-time librarians not providing preparation time, full-time SSS teachers, full- time Learning-Assistants Teachers, and Counselors won’t be replaced on their first day of absence. In the case that a non-enrolling teacher is part-time with no enrolling teaching assignment (including providing prep. time), she/he won’t be replaced for the first day. However, school-based principals have the flexibility to decide if there is a need to replace an EA because of special circumstances. Budget Strategy #10 Some Numbers ….  EAs  Non-replacement of EAs – Target = $ 28,000.00  Actual saving over three months = $ 9,000.00  Estimated saving over ten months = $30,000.00 Budget Strategy #10 Some Numbers ….  Non-enrolling teachers  Non-replacement of non-enrolling teachers – Target = $ 74,000.00  Actual saving over three months = $ 12,000.00  Estimated saving over ten months = $40,000.00 Budget Strategy #10 Some Numbers ….  Clerical staff  Estimated saving over ten (10) months = $6,000.00 Budget Strategy #10 Perceived impacts as stated by employees ….  Safety impacts  The impact of being shorted an EA in the Skills for Life Program is potentially dangerous … it could be easy to miss some crucial items/situations/problems …  Having an EA away for day without a replacement jeopardizes the learning and safety of some of the most vulnerable students in my class …. Should not the safety and learning of our children be the first and foremost concern? Budget Strategy #10 Perceived impacts as stated by employees ….  Emotional stress and professional status  As a group, this action has created a feeling of being undervalued by our employer.  We are by nature kind, caring, and giving group of people, not being replaced causes us great deal of guilt and stress. Many come to work sick, rather than having students missing out … Budget Strategy #10 Perceived impacts as stated by employees ….  Educational impacts  Our most vulnerable students struggle at the best of time and this measure does not help their struggle …  Structured small groups are often run or supported by EAs … Learning groups need to be lumped together or cancelled when the leader is away … Budget Strategy #10 Perceived impacts as stated by employees ….  Impacts on clerical staff in secondary school sites:  The impact of having no replacement on the first day is significantly huge to each office staff as we have to double-up our responsibilities and deadlines as we have to do the job of someone else.
