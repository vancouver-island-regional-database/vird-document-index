Advocates for the Rights of Children (ARC) “Your child may not be able to attend our school for a full day”, “We will not be able to provide the support your child needs”, “Please take your child home at lunch and recess because we cannot adequately ensure their safety”. These are all statements from administrators that brought us together in June 2011. We are the Advocates for the Rights of Children (ARC). We were forced into action due to District 68’s budget cuts to Educational Assistants. These cuts are directly impacting the level of support to children with Autism in our district. Many of our children are left with minimal or no support in the classroom or on the playground. This lack of support is affecting the safety and the quality of education our children have a right to receive. There are a number of concerns that our children are not receiving the necessary, vital support they need and would like the board to be open to hearing our stories and discuss the BC Ministry of Education’s Special Education Services—A Manual of Policies, Procedures and Guidelines (February 2011). The top three concerns of ARC are: 1) Lack of E.A support 2) Lack of Education provided to Teachers and E.A’s 3) Funding provided to the district for children and youth with ASD be targeted and spent on programming and services ensuring documentation is kept on record 4) Lack of adherence to the BC Ministry of Education’s Special Education Services—A Manual of Policies, Procedures and Guidelines (February 2011)
