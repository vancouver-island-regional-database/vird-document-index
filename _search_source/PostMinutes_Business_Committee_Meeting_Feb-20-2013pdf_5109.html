CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY FROM THE FACILITIES DEPARTMENT Transportation Review - Status Report Mr. Turin spoke about the Transportation Review Status Report. He said this is a draft working plan for implementing the recommendations of the recently-completed Transportation Review. The Transportation Review was prepared by an outside consultant and contains a number of key findings and recommendations. The report recommended that there be a comprehensive review of transportation policies and that the district acquire transportation management software that would improve the ability of the district to identify opportunities to increase efficiencies. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Chad Dalrymple, the district’s Energy Manager, updated the Business Committee on the district’s energy management program. He said that the district spends $2 million a year on energy for its 44 active buildings. Currently, 50 percent is spent on natural gas, 43 percent on electricity; and the balance on propane, fuel oil and carbon offsets. In the past ten years, the Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held February 20, 2013 district has saved $5 million in costs through a variety of energy efficiency programs. Mr. Dalrymple outlined the projects underway and the plans for the future. Opposed by Trustees Allen and Welch CARRIED Mr. Turin spoke to the Committee about the Financial Forecast. The financial forecast for the current budget year projects that the district is on target to end the year with about $500,000 in surplus. This, combined with a surplus from last year of about $500,000, and a clawback of $1 million this year from supplies accounts across the district, would Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held February 20, 2013 give the district a cushion of $2 million, with which to begin to address the projected shortfall next year of $5 million. He said that currently, consultations are underway with stakeholder groups regarding the 2013-2014 budget and that the 2013-14 funding announcement is expected around March 15th.
