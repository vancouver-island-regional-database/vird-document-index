Direct staff to implement the “Commercial Interest Information Form” attached to this report, to streamline the review process for all mobile vending park requests. Motion Carried CS 2026-106 That Council direct staff to proceed with a phasing plan and cost estimates for the 6th Avenue Improvements and include Phase 1 construction in the 2027 budget for Council’s consideration. Motion Carried OPPOSED: Councillor Stevens. 6. REPORTS 6.1 PRC Facility Project Update CS 2026-107 That Council direct staff to amend the 2026-2030 Financial Plan to: 1. Allocate from the COVID reserves $15,000 to the City Hall Heat Recovery Ventilation (HRV) project to enable completion of the remaining work; 2. Allocate $7,000 from the Climate Action Reserve to the Gateway Signage Lighting Improvements project to address additional infrastructure and installation costs; and 3. Allocate $33,000 for the Lot 108 – Phase 2 project, with $25,000 from the Climate Action Reserve and $8,000 from the Municipal Insurance Association Risk Management Grant to address climate mitigation features and add walkway railings. Motion Carried OPPOSED: Councillor Stevens. 7. BYLAWS 7.1 Bylaw Status Sheet Town of Ladysmith Regular Council Meeting Minutes: May 19, 2026 3 8. Motion Carried OPPOSED: Councillor Paterson. 9. NEW BUSINESS 9.1 Notice of Motion CS 2026-109 That Council authorize the rising and reporting of all instances in which a member of Council has been sanctioned since 1995, including: the date of the sanction, the substance of the sanction, the actions taken by Council, and any dissenting votes, to be included in the Rise and Report - and that the Council Procedure Policy be amended to require that all future instances of sanctioning of a Member of Council be risen and reported. Motion Defeated OPPOSED: Councillors Gourlay, McKay, Paterson and Virtanen. 10. QUESTION PERIOD Councillor Stevens vacated the meeting at 7:30 p.m. A member of the public asked whether the $7,000 allocated to the Gateway Signage Lighting Improvements project might be more suitably allocated to installing an accessibility push-button for the downtown public washrooms, in light of the municipality’s recognition of National AccessAbility Week. Another member of the public addressed Council regarding a matter not listed on the agenda. The Mayor advised that the matter did not fall within the scope of Question Period. 11.
