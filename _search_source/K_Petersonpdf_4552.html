Future Vision of Learn@Home K-12 Learning Alternatives PAC with Parent Input Facility Learn@Home K-7 has reached and exceeded capacity in our space at Departure Bay School Office and Library Our Classroom Our Playground Computer Area Teachers Office Facility Many families are not participating in lessons because it is too crowded and chaotic on classroom days and children with sensory issues are not able to attend. Our own facility would allow for guest speakers, workshops, professional development, plays etc. which we are unable to utilize in Departure Bay. We lose classroom space in the Spring when Departure Bay has special events such as a Seashore Program creating extra demand on already crowded spaces for both schools. We feel amalgamating the K-7 students with the 8-12 students will allow for easier transition between grade 7 to grade 8 for the students and allow some Gr. 8 & 9 more support through easier access to elementary curriculum and resources. It would also allow flexible programming such as cross enrollment for grade 8 and 9 so dancers can do a combination of learn@home, high school classes in their catchment and dance half the day at the studio. Having our own facility would give us a sense of community, continuity and belonging. We believe we are the face and future of education; having our own facility would legitimize this belief and allow our school to be promoted and accepted. Funding Our enrolment has grown steadily over the past 13 years. Learn@Home K-7 would continue as a Distributed Learning school (amalgamated with Learn@Home K-12). In addition, we want to explore opening a fully funded blended school: brick and mortar/Distributed Learning (BM/DL blend) in our new location. This new elementary school would be open 50+% to meet Ministry Guidelines for full funding ($7138/student, up from $6030/student); we are presently a Blended DL school with many students attending classes regularly now but less than 50% (to meet DL guidelines). Many parents are in favor of this increase in attendance to 50%+ to qualify for full funding with the Brick and Mortar/DL (BM/DL Blended) School. We want Learn@Home to run the new BM/DL blended school so we can attract & work with specially trained DL teachers to help develop programs to meet our students’ needs. We can offer high interest, relevant programs face to face as well as a Distributed Learning component. This offers the most flexibility for our varied learning needs. Community We recognize the need for community partnerships and peer mentorship with local groups and businesses. We would like to explore creative collaborations with local business to provide a richer educational experience for our students; we already work closely with a number of community groups but would like to expand this. Learn@Home provides an option for high performance athletes and for local students who travel or live on neighboring islands. We provide an option for students with health issues or special needs who cannot function in a regular classroom. Together we are a community of learners. The Face & Future of Education • We already meet or exceed the Ministries Guidelines for 21st Century Learning- bcedplan.ca. We offer the flexibility, choice & high standards outlined in this Ministry initiative. The Face & Future of Education • We are well on the way in implementing the new Ministry curriculum. We can develop customized, locally-focused learning modules (e.g. Lang. Arts or Social Studies infused/integrated with Aboriginal World Views & Perspectives in the Classroom curriculum) that will be a resource both within our district and beyond our district. In Conclusion We do not see ourselves as a marginalized, insignificant portion of the education system (which is the impression visitors to our facilities presently get). We need respect and funding for this cutting edge school; it needs to be at the forefront of SD68, not hidden in the back of other facilities. This support will help increase enrolment by attracting new students and re-attracting DL students who have left our district for other BC districts (allowable under DL guidelines). We want a facility for September, 2016 that can support both our present Learn@Home school as well as the possibility of a 50%+ fully funded Brick & Mortar/Distributed Learning blended school run by Learn@Home that has a flexible program, the potential for dynamic community partnerships, and the ability to meet a variety of educational, developmental, social and cultural needs. Don’t miss this opportunity to be a leader in showcasing what is possible in implementing 21st Century Education in BC. We can be a model for other school districts looking to adopt and move forward with flexible, progressive, individualized learning that meets the needs of our students.
