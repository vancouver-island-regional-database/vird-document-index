BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: January 29, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Learning Services RE: Local School Calendar BACKGROUND: At the March 27, 2013, Board Meeting the following motion was adopted: Folio R13/03/27-14 That the Board of Education of School District 68 establish an Ad Hoc Committee, reporting through the Business Committee, to develop a three-year Local School Calendar that will commence September 2014. The Department of Learning Services subsequently struck a Committee to discuss the following topics relative to a District Three-Year Calendar Plan: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. Balanced School Calendar Coordinated Professional Development time Spring Break “Success for All” Collaboration/In-service Time 1 The Ad Hoc Committee’s recommendations as presented to the Board’s Business Committee on January 23, 2013, are as follows: Items Recommendations 1. Balanced School Calendar “It is recommended that this item requires a specific working group to investigate the research and models that could be implemented, resulting in a recommendation to the Board.” 2. Coordinated Pro D Calendar “It is recommended that the Pro D calendar committee meet later in the spring to confirm dates.” This remains an annual process supported by the NDTA. 3. Spring Break “It is recommended with the 3-year calendar plan that spring break remain as status quo, 1 week.” 4. Success for All “It is recommended that we continue to support two days of “Success For All.” This form of in-service and leadership is recognized as forward thinking, and invaluable to the system. 5. Collaboration/ In-service Time The committee recommends to the Board to direct staff to report back to the Board a proposed 3-year plan for collaboration time at the January Board meeting. This proposal would include 2 options, one for a collaboration model for the next 3 years starting in September 2014, the other for the next 3 years including the second semester of this year to begin to work on the Board policy on the “Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment” (Policy 1.9). • The proposals will include feedback mechanisms to inform improvement issues and adjustments. • This presentation will include the NDTA, NSAA and District presenters. From these recommendations came the direction from the Board (March 27, 2013) to senior staff to develop a Three-Year Local Calendar, which is attached as a Draft for your review. Recommendation/Motion: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopts the Three-year Local School Calendar, as presented by the staff.
