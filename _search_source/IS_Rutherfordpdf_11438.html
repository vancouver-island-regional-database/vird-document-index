NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 13, 2023 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Rutherford Elementary Background In October 2023, the Board of Education supported the opening of Rutherford Elementary School in September 2025, with the following motion: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) direct that Rutherford Elementary School be reopened as of September 2025 and that staff are directed to create a School Transition Working Group to address issues associated with reopening the facility and provide the Board with recommendations with respect to appropriate catchments for Rutherford and the surrounding schools and potential programming, if applicable, to ensure the long-term viability of the school. This information sheet is intended to outline the process and timeline for the planning of the school to reopen. Discussion Following this meeting District staff will send out invites to the Rutherford Working Group. The purpose of the Rutherford Working Group is to deliver a report that will provide the Board with recommendations on the following issues: 1. The Catchment Boundaries of Rutherford and impacted schools including Randerson, McGirr, Frank J. Ney, Departure Bay and possibly Seaview and Uplands (likely minor if any) and Pleasant Valley (unlikely); 2. The manner in which the school will open (e.g. a staggered multi-year process or an immediate start) and whether enrollment will be voluntary or mandatory; 3. Discussions to ensure long-term viability including potential programming; 4. Discussions regarding the status of the facility including condition but also potential aesthetic improvements; 5. Whether a new name should be considered; and 6. Potential staffing implications (note that where collective agreement implications are present the committee and the Board may have limited say). Catchment Boundaries Currently, Licker Geospatial has been engaged to provide updated development and enrollment forecasting for the North End. The external group will provide support to finance's enrollment projections on future expected enrollments. They will also be able to provide scenarios as requested by the Working Group (within certain parameters): Information Sheet: Rutherford Elementary Page 2 1. Parts of the catchment area of Departure Bay Elementary must move to Frank J. Ney; 2. Growth at Randerson must be limited; 3. Parts of the McGirr catchment must be incorporated in the new Rutherford catchment to ensure a sufficient school population. Nevertheless, there will be some options available to the Board. The Configuration of the School at Opening The most optimal situation for the open is for new boundaries to be set and students within those boundaries to be moved. However, the community may have concerns that the Board must consider. The Working Group will discuss the issue, potentially survey parents and then determine what recommendation to make to the Board. Without limiting the outcomes some possible configurations are as follows: 1. 2. 3. 4. K-3 with mandatory attendance and then working to k-7 over 4 years K-5 with mandatory attendance and then working to k-7 over 2 years K-7 with the same requirements K-7 allowing voluntary enrollment (likely creating combined grade classrooms) etc. Long Term Viability While the boundary discussion is intended to provide long term viability of the school the Working Group may wish educators to lead a discussion on a focus for the school. While there are a host of complexities with such a discussion, the Board is intending to make Rutherford a viable long-term school and staff are committed to doing our best to deliver that goal. Facilities Matters There are not a lot of specific facilities issues to address given the recent upgrade to house Pleasant Valley. Rutherford is an older and not particularly attractive school. It should be noted that the grounds are excellent and the facility is very functional. Therefore, the Working Group will spend some time understanding what options are available to enhance the school prior to opening. School Name The Working Group will discuss whether the Board should consider a new name. The Working Group would not be tasked with making a recommendation on what a name would be but rather whether the Board should consider engaging its policies to pursue this topic. Potential Staffing Impacts Individual discussion with our unions will be prioritized if the collective agreement is engaged. However, the Working Group will engage in general discussions regarding school staffing and resource allocations and will consider the shifts that may be required to facilitate the catchment changes from an operational and educational perspective, aligned with regular district-based resource allocations falling within budget. For instance, if Rutherford opens with 250 students and shrinks surrounding schools, how will we address the issue? Notably, if the process is intended to help Departure Bay and Randerson then they will lose the most students (and therefore the most staff). However, Frank J Ney will be impacted significantly having the most student turnover (e.g. Departure Bay students shift to Ney, Ney students shift to Rutherford).
