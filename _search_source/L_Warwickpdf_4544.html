NDSS PAC November 4, 2015 15-11-04 NDSS PAC 1 Background • French Immersion has been located at NDSS since 1988, when it was moved from John Barsby. – NDSS was considered a more central location, with easier access to bus routes. • A new library was built to accommodate the large book collection that comes with the program. • Parents have been actively working to reduce the attrition rate at the secondary level, which is generally high in French Immersion programs. • Reviews of the program’s location were undertaken in 2003 and 2008. “Once a French Immersion program has been established, districts should develop policy that will serve to maintain and strengthen the program.” – BC Ministry of Education Website 15-11-04 NDSS PAC 2 Then and Now In 2003… “The District's 191 Senior Immersion students attend NDSS. The continuation of French Immersion through to Grade 12 is important but problematic with small enrolments. The District can probably only ever expect to have one centrally located secondary Immersion Program…A coherent, consolidated program of French Immersion at the elementary school level leading to secondary school is needed…However, changes in the location of these programs are traditionally controversial. Considerable discussion and consultation should be undertaken before any reconfiguration takes place. - Source: District-Wide Facility Planning Study School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), 2003 04 02.” In 2015… We have 348 French Immersion and 40 Francophone students enrolled at NDSS. 15-11-04 NDSS PAC 3 Growth in Secondary French Immersion SD68 350 311 282 300 250 230 256 326 328 2013 2014 348 266 200 150 100 50 0 2008 15-11-04 2009 2010 2011 2012 NDSS PAC 2015 4 Why Keep French Immersion at NDSS? “Having established a program, school districts should promote the program and recruit students. Should the enrollment become insufficient, school districts should consult parents to find solutions. If there is no resolution to the problem, the district should give at least one year's notice to parents of any changes contemplated to permit full discussion and to allow parents time to consider alternatives for their children.”-- BC Ministry of Education Website 15-11-04 NDSS PAC 5 Central Location • Largest number of FI students come from central and North Nanaimo • Plans to add Grade 8 and 9 FI at LSS?
