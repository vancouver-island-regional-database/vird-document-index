DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET February 14, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer and Taunia Sutton, Associate Secretary-Treasurer 2023-24 – Amended Annual Budget and Second Quarter Financial Report (Q2) Recommendation The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) give the School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 2023/2024 Amended Annual Budget Bylaw all three readings at one meeting on February 28, 2024. The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) adopt the Amended Annual Budget of the Board for the fiscal year 2023/2024 at the Board of Education meeting on February 28, 2024. Background The Ministry of Education and Child Care requires districts to prepare an Annual Budget in advance of the school year, which is based on estimated enrolments, revenues, and expenses. An Amended Annual Budget is then prepared each February based on actual enrolments captured September 29th on the 1701 Student Data Collection, and revised revenues and expenses based on more current operations. The following financial report presents the districts Amended Annual Budget, which includes all changes in revenue and expense since the Preliminary Annual Budget, as well as the second quarter financial results to December 31st, with a prior year comparator. Also included is an update on the district’s investment portfolio, and a current summary of the Capital and Local Capital Funds for the Board’s information. Discussion Since the 2023-24 Preliminary Annual Budget was approved in May the district’s Operating Fund has increased by $7,477,094 in revenue and $9,786,973 in associated expenses. The majority of additional revenue relates to Labour Settlement Funding for negotiated general wage increases, and funding for the districts increased student enrolment over preliminary estimates (84.168 Funded FTE). Special Purpose revenue and expense has increased by $4,072,764 which is mostly due to the additional staffing necessary to meet class size and composition requirements in compliance with LOU17, funded by the Ministry of Education and Child Care under the Classroom Enhancement Fund. In addition to a variety of other Special Purpose Funds received by the district for 2023-24, all Special Purpose Fund balances relating to a prior year are carried forward and are reflected in the Amended Annual Budget. The districts second quarter financial results on December 31st are included within the Operating Fund and Special Purpose Fund summaries, as well, there is a prior year comparison. Note that on December 31st, the District was 50% through fiscal 2023-24 but with school-based operations being only 10 months in duration, some expense categories may only be as much as 40% spent, and some may be less due to the complexities of school start up, and the timing of expense recognitions. In addition, Operating Revenues are received pursuant to a funding schedule set by the Ministry of Education and Child Care, based on the timing of educational delivery to cover staffing and operational expenses in alignment with the school year, not equally over the fiscal year. Investment Portfolio Update: As noted in the Operating Fund summary with regards to Investment Income, interest rates are expected to soften over the coming year. As well, in December the two-year Royal Bank of Canada GIC with quarterly payouts matured with the final payout deposited into our operating account. Both scenarios substantiated the decision to maintain the current budgeted estimate even though it appears actual revenues will be in excess of budget at year-end. With the rate softening however, it’s estimated the rate of return on our investments will decrease over the balance of the fiscal year, resulting in total investment revenue being equivalent to the current budgeted target.
