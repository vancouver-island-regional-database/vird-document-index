CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY UNFINISHED BUSINESS Local School Calendar Consultation Mr. & Mrs. Michael & Liz Munro IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Saunders Folio S12/04/10-02 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receives the correspondence from Mr. & Mrs. Michael Munro regarding the local school calendar consultation. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Presentations Ron Farino Ron Farino, president of the Canadian Union of Public Employees (CUPE), Local 606, said that as funding pressures on school districts increase, 2 Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 10, 2012 greater numbers of districts are looking at changing the timetable with such strategies as an extended spring break. He said that the funding shortfalls are created by the provincial government, but that the extended break strategy has direct impacts on CUPE members and communities. He asked the Board to meet with CUPE to have discussions and try to reach some common ground. Karen Lawrence Karen Lawrence, an Education Assistant at Rutherford Elementary, asked the Board to reconsider the proposal to extend Spring Break next year. She said that the nine days of Spring Break would mean a loss of income for tenmonth employees, and the extended school day will result in additional unpaid time for Education Assistants. Chair Brennan said the Board will hold a special meeting on Wednesday, April 18, 2012, at which time trustees will make a decision regarding proposed changes to the Local School Calendar.
