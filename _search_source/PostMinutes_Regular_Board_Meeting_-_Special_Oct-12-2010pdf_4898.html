CARRIED UNANIOUSLY PRESENTATIONS - DISTRICT FACILITIES PLAN Alice Barlow - Ecole Davis Road School PAC Alice Barlow-Kedves, on behalf of the Ecole Davis Road School Parent Advisory Council said that parents at the school are frustrated with the long wait for renovations or the replacement of the school building. She outlined a long history of Davis Road parents advocating for upgrades to the school and said that Davis Road should be the top priority in the facilities plan. Janis Chung Janis Chung, a Pauline Haarer French Immersion parent, said that she is pleased to see that there will be a review of the French Immersion program in the district. She said that Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held October 12, 2010 2 the planned review demonstrates that the district values the French Immersion program and that parents would like to participate in the review. She said that the French Immersion community wants the secondary program to be housed at NDSS. Bill Johnston Bill Johnston, on behalf of a parent group, the Dover Sports Council, questioned the way that greater use of school facilities by the public would be implemented. He asked if there would be clear guidelines for setting priorities and resolving conflicts of interest. He said that the guidelines should put students’ interests first. Patricia Huggins Patricia Huggins said that she supports the Board’s commitment to keep current facilities open and to use surplus space for community uses. She suggested some revisions to the plan, including green initiatives and some measurement of the social costs of closing schools. She also suggested that the Board establish a working group, drawn from the community, to look at ways to best use surplus space. Barb Humpherville Barb Humpherville, on behalf of the NDSS Parent Advisory Council, thanked the Board for moving away from the previous facilities plan, and drawing up the new plan with its guiding principles.
