With the help of local organizations and businesses we’ll look at designs and a common theme ...and work with residents, volunteers and supporters to put each sign in a special place. Alongside our welcome signs, and at community centres and the 18 or so locations we expect to place benches, let’s add a heritage garden ...and a hitching post for your horse or bike! We’d like everyone to have the chance to stroll through their neighbourhoods, find a nice place where they can sit down to rest and relax, and maybe have a bit of a chat with friends and neighbours. We want to have a day of fun and celebration in 2017. And what better way than to plan a day when we can play ‘Heritage Sports’ - the way the games and sports used to be played! For instance, 125 years ago in 1892, the Nanaimo Thistles kicked off a soccer match with the Snuneymuxw Wanderers. Canadian Sports Hall of Fame soccer champion Harry Manson led the Wanderers. So, we’re thinking 2017 would be great for a re-match! Heritage Treats? A Heritage Day celebration deserves treats and heritage recipes, maybe an apple pie bake-off? With BBQ’s fired up, and lots of ice cream and lemonade all around, we’re looking at a day to enjoy the company of friends and family, and the 150th birthday of our country! But maybe this time school kids play, and teams come from all over the area? Maybe play baseball and lacrosse too? Or hold plowing matches with oldtyme tractors (& old farmers!)
