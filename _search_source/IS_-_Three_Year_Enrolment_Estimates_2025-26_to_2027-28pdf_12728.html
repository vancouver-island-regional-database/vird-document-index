Discussion The Ministry of Education and Child Care provides Boards of Education with an annual operating grant based on the number of students that will attend their schools. Accordingly, Ministry requires estimated enrolments in advance of the school year in order to provide a preliminary operating grant. Following that, in September of each year districts are required to submit their student data via the 1701 Student Data Collection process which confirms the actual number of students in attendance; Operating Grants are updated based on this information in December of each year. There are two additional data collections throughout the school year, the intervals and data collected within each is summarized below: September Enrolment Count – includes School-Age, Adults, Alternate and Continuing Education, Online Learning, Inclusive Education Level 1-3, Indigenous Education and English Language Learning students. February Enrolment Count – includes Continuing Education, Online Learning, Inclusive Education Level 1-3 enrolment growth and Newcomer Refugee Students. May Enrolment Count - includes Continuing Education and Online Learning students Once enrolment estimates are submitted, Ministry calculates preliminary operating grants using a Funding Allocation System (FAS) which applies per student rates, supplements for unique and diverse student abilities, as well as other supplement funding categories applicable to each district. In midMarch the Ministry of Education and Child Care announce the next school year’s Operating Grant amount, which provided the majority of funding to support that operational year and associated Annual Budget. (Please see Appendix A – SD68 Enrolment Estimates 2025-26 to 2027-25) Information Sheet: Three Year Enrolment Estimates (2025-25 to 2027-28) Page 2 2025-26 Enrolment Estimate: Although we have experienced some softening at elementary level over the last couple years, numbers remain relatively flat for the coming school year so there is little change anticipated for K-7. Total estimated change from September 2024 is 23 FTE students. At the Secondary level we have a large Grade 7 cohort entering Grade 8 and a small Grade 12 cohort exiting, resulting in an overall increase in Grade 8-12. In combination with anticipated moderate growth, the total estimated change is 200.991 FTE students over September 2024. The consolidated change for Elementary, Secondary, and some minor shifts within district programs for February and May enrolment estimates, is a total of 236.805FTE. This includes: 213.33 FTE increase in basic student enrolment for September 2025 19.750 FTE for February 2026 3.7250 FTE for May 2026. In addition to basic student enrolment, estimates for unique student diversities are also required and have been calculated using the current student cohorts, moving them through the grade levels and then adding an incoming K-cohort based on historic trends; a final adjustment is made in collaboration with district leads in each of the associated categories to arrive at the amount that will be submitted to the Ministry of Education and Child Care. Important to note that for the 2025-26 school year we are anticipating very little change to these student categories. (Please see Appendix B – SD68 Summary of Projected Enrolment 2025-26 to 2027-25) 2025-26 Operating Grant Impact: Based on the estimated enrolment changes noted, the district’s current 2024-25 Operating Grant will increase by a total of $1.56 million for the 2025-26 school year. This is calculated based on the per student funding rates remaining the same, however, it’s anticipated that the rates will be increased moderately to account for the Cost-of-Living Wage adjustment that is currently being funded by Special Grant, not yet rolled into the per student rates. The $1.56 million also assumes the “Other Funding Categories” will remain static, however as much as shifts here are anticipated they are unable to be determine with any confidence so for the purposes of building the preliminary Annual Budget remain as is. (Please see Appendix C – Estimated 2025-26 Operating Grant per Enrolment Projection) Summary: The district’s estimated enrolment for 2025-26 is somewhat conservative, predominately based on the assumption that current cohorts will continue, along with a small amount of growth. This provides us the opportunity to maintain current levels of support for the coming year, and to absorb inflationary cost pressures without any negative impact to the system, which is an extremely positive position for the district to be in.
