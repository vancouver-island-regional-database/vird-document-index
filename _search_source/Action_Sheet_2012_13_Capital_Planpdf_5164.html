THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT #68 STAFF REPORT U TO: Business Committee DATE: Sept 19, 2012 FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Proposed 2012/2013 Five-Year Capital Plan U REFERENCE: U Proposed 2012/2013 Five-Year Capital Plan (attached) U BACKGROUND : U The Five-Year Capital Plan Each year, Boards of Education are required to submit a Five-Year Capital Plan detailing the high priority capital projects needed for their school districts. Once all submissions have been received, the Ministry analyzes individual capital project requests, and assigns a priority ranking on a provincial basis. Based on analysis of the highest priority requests, the Ministry establishes a long-term capital plan that will inform the decisions of the Minister and Government’s corporate capital plan. The main elements of the Phase 1 Five-Year Capital Plan are the provision of new space, rejuvenation or replacement of schools, Seismic Upgrades, Mechanical/Energy Systems Upgrades, Building Envelope Remediation and school bus replacements/acquisitions. Capital project requests will be considered for support in the third year (2014/15) of the Ministry’s 2012/13 Capital Plan. Only capital requests for Building Envelope Remediation, Mechanical Upgrades, Seismic Upgrades, new busses and bus replacements will be considered for support in the first two years of the 2012/13 Capital Plan. School District Facilities Plan As described in the Education Facilities Planning and Procurement Process Review, a district-wide comprehensive School District Facilities Plan (SDFP) should form the basis for investment decisions. Such plans should take into consideration education program requirements and trends, operating capacities and current condition of existing facilities, current land use, anticipated changes in land use, absorption rates, yield rates, community demographics, local community and economic development strategies and other considerations. The SDFP would provide a comprehensive rationale for specific capital projects that may be proposed as part of the District’s Five-Year Capital Plan. In addition, the SDFP provides a district-wide framework for other key local decisions such as school consolidations, locations for district programs and maintenance priorities. The SDFP should outline concrete plans for a ten-year planning horizon and general consideration for the longer term. The scope and emphasis of each SDFP will vary depending on the specific circumstances and priorities of each school district. The 2012/13 Capital Plan instructions Action Sheet - Proposed 2012/2013 Five-Year Capital Plan September 19, 2012 Page 2 advise that while formal submission in not required, the SDFP will provide the critical context for discussions with the Ministry regarding high priority project requests. U DISCUSSION: Development of a Facilities Plans: The District previously developed and adopted a School District Facilities Plan as required by the Ministry of Education. District is currently developing a Strategic Education Plan (SEP) which is expected to be complete in the fall of 2012. Staff expects that the final SEP will inform the SDFP which will in turn assist staff recommending capital plan priorities. Until such time as the Strategic Education Plan is completed, the district is submitting a 2012-13 Five-Year Capital Plan that reflects similar priorities to the ones that were submitted in the 2011-12 Five-Year Capital Plan. Should capital priorities change the District may wish to submit an amended plan to the Ministry.
