CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Ms. Lisa Wengel & Mr. Ken Connolly Pastor, Compass Church & Owner, Pheasant Hill Homes Ltd. RE: The Demolition of Harewood Elementary Ms. Lisa Wengel, Pastor and Owner of Compass Church, along with Mr. Ken Connolly, Owner of Pheasant Hill Homes Ltd., presented a plea to the Business Committee to halt the demolition of Harewood School. Ms. Wengel and Mr. Connolly proposed a deal in which the school district would turn the building over to the church and it would assume responsibility for renovations. The contract for demolition has already been awarded and should the Board consider this request, in addition to the cost to the district for the demolition, there would be additional cost penalties for services and time rendered thus far. Also, Trustee Brennan stated that we don't own the land and it must be used for Educational purposes. Ms. Wegel was hoping to re-purpose the building to house other church ministries as well as a potential to share the building with an organization such as Loaves and Fishes. This item was moved to Unfinished Business. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Higginson IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Kimler Folio BC16/02/10-03 The Business Committee recommends that this item be moved to Unfinished Business, item 10.1. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held February 10, 2016 UNFINISHED BUSINESS Harewood School Demolition Trustees feel that the District is not in a position at this time to be giving buildings away that we don't own. Especially as there would be additional costs to the District due to having to cancel the demolition contract, which is already under way. Trustees feel that there has been ample time to bring forward these types of requests and unfortunately it is too late and the demolition will proceed. Transportation Review Update Mr. Roberts Mr. Roberts went through the Busing per School hand out with the Business Committee. The next piece of the Transportation Review will be to look at the territories. Nanaimo's territories are all very different and some pose more of a safety concern than others. International Baccalaureate AS Mr. Blain Superintendent John Blain presented to the Business Committee a report on the next steps for the possibility of an International Baccalaureate (IB) Primary Years Program (PYP) in the district. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Robinson Folio BC16/02/10-04 The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that the implementation of a new International Baccalaureate Education program not be advanced at this time. If the Board were to move forward with an International Baccalaureate program the recommended earliest start for staff would be September 2017. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held February 10, 2016 Opposed by Trustee Routley CARRIED NEW BUSINESS During the Updated Facilities Plan consultation, there was a discussion of catchment areas for schools and facilities that have low capacity utilization. AS - Catchment Consultation Mr. Blain IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Higginson Folio BC16/02/10-06 The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) to implement the catchment area adjustment, effective September 2016. Students and families that are currently in the affected areas will have the opportunity to choose either school for the 2016-17 school year. Beginning in the 2017-18 school year, the Student Registration and School Choice Policy and Procedure 3022 and 3022P will be in effect.
