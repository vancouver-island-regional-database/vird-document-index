Budget Planning for 2012-13 School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) Planning for the Future  Board is embarking on a strategic planning process  Board’s strategic plan will define the school district’s vision, mission and goals  The focus of the strategic plan will be improving student learning  The strategic planning process will include extensive consultation with parents, students, employees and the public Planning for the Future  Initial work on the strategic plan to be completed by June, with follow-up next fall  Schools and district departments will align their own planning with the strategic plan  In the future, the Board’s budget will be aligned with the strategic plan  Because the 2012-13 budget will be approved by May, it will not include any specific strategic planning initiatives but should give sufficient flexibility for some initiatives to start in 2012-13 Sources of Revenue for SD68 Majority of revenue comes from the B.C. Ministry of Education  The district is funded based on enrolment  In 2011-12, the district received $117.8 million in provincial revenue  The district expects $116.4 million in provincial revenue in 2012-13.  Approximately $6.1 million in additional revenue comes from rentals, investment income, international student fees and special grants  5% 95% Ministry of Education Grants - Ministry of Education funding announcement: - $4 million funding protection grant for SD68  Grant is $1.4 million less than the current year  Funding protection grant will be reduced each year in the future  Districts are protected to 98.5 percent of previous year’s funding  In 2013-14 grant will be an additional $1.7 million less  In 2014-15, grant will be an additional $1.7 million less About Funding Protection  Without grant, SD68 would have been facing an additional $4.0 million in budget challenges  If more students than projected arrive in September 2012, the district will NOT receive additional funding until there has been an increase of 592 students  If additional special needs students are identified, SD68 will NOT receive additional funding  For this reason, $435,000 in additional teacher staffing contingencies have been included in the budget (4.75 FTE) Supplement for B.C. Education Plan  Special supplement for 2012-13 of $20 per student  SD68 will receive $270,804  Funding is to be used to assist in implementing the B.C. Education Plan  This funding is not confirmed for future years Learning Improvement Fund  Intended to allow districts to help teachers meet complex needs in classrooms with such things as additional teachers, education assistants or other paraprofessionals  $60 million in 2012-13 for entire province  Original funding for SD68 for 2012-13 was $744,000 – strike savings has now increased fund to $1.5 million for next year  Province-wide funding will be $60 million in 2013-14 and $75 million in 2014-15  The plan for this special purpose fund must be developed by the end of the school year Plan to Balance the Budget  Difference between revenue and expenditure projected to be $1.6 million  Senior management is proposing that the Board use $1.6 million in surplus carried forward from current school year to balance the 2012-13 budget  Projected surplus from the current year is the result of funding protection money the district received this year Funding Strategic Plan Initiatives  Proposed budget does not include any new initiatives – for example, implementation of the district’s technology plan and strategic planning initiatives  Funds for initiatives could come from the Local School Calendar proposal for next year ($366,000) Looking Ahead to Future Years  District will continue to experience enrolment decline for several more years  Funding protection grant will continue to decline  Use of surplus this year to balance the budget compounds the budget challenges for next year  Forecast shortfall for 2013-14 is $2.1 million and an additional $3.3 million for 2014-15  Current fiscal situation is not sustainable  District has to begin planning now for structural changes in coming years Process for This Year  Tuesday, April 17 – Public meeting for the Board to receive input on the 2012-13 budget Wednesday, April 18 - Special Board Meeting to consider a decision on the proposed Local School Calendar.
