Dear Nanaimo School Board, Today, the lot that the present school building is on is the last of these lots meant for community use. James Beck died in 1903 and, over the years since then, these school and church lots were gradually lost to the community. This is the last one left and when it’s gone, we can’t get it back again. It’s important for the community of South Wellington to have its own recreational space such as ball fields, playgrounds and parks. This property provides just that and if this space is lost to us, we have no other land to use for these purposes. These areas are what bring a community together and they provide healthy places for our children to play. I would respectfully ask that the land itself be kept for the use of the community of South Wellington as was intended by James Beck 120 years ago. Thank you for your consideration. Helen Tilley (South Wellington resident and local historian) *Map of the Beck Estate was obtained from the Land Titles Office in Victoria A Bit of History of our South Wellington Schools by Helen Tilley James Beck, a pioneer in this area, pre-empted land in that part of Cranberry District, now known as South Wellington, in the 1860s. Before that he worked for the Hudson’s Bay Company as a machinist and carpenter. James Beck sold the coal rights under this land in February, 1884. In 1900, James Beck mapped out the town of South Wellington on his property, setting aside three lots for churches and three lots for schools. These residential and school lots can be seen highlighted on the attached map* of the Beck Estate, dated from the early 1900s. It was the custom in those days for land owners of large parcels of land to set aside parts of their property for the use of the community. There were four schools built on these three “School” properties over the years. The property marked “School” along the “Cassidy Road” on the map, which would now be Schoolhouse Road, was where the first school was built; locally known as the “Red School” it was built in 1898. The second school built in South Wellington was on the lot marked “School” on the upper left of the map and was at the top of the hill on Minetown Road. This school was locally known as the “Green School” and was built in 1912. The third and fourth schools were located on the property marked “School” at the bottom of the map along Morden Road and that is where the present school, built in 1969, is located. On the lower part of this same property, where the present tennis court is located, the “Superior School” was built in 1918.
