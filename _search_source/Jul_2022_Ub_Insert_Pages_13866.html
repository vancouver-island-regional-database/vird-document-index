TRANSFER BEACH PUBLIC WASHROOMS, PICNIC SHELTERS NOW COMPLETE The Town of Ladysmith has now completed a significant portion of the Transfer Beach Park improvements and the public washroom facilities were recently reopened in time for summer as more people visit our popular waterfront destination. Additionally, both the Kinsmen Shelter and Sportsmen Shelter are now available for day use and rental bookings after significant upgrades were undertaken to improve the overall aesthetic and layout. These enhancements and others are the result of a $701,552 Provincial grant received by the Town through the Tourism Dependent Community Fund stream. Ongoing projects at Ladysmith’s waterfront park as a result of this grant funding include construction of a shelter over the Salish Wind canoe and building a walkway down to the beach, increasing the overall accessibility of the green space for those with mobility challenges. The public washroom renovations add nearly 260 sq. ft. to the existing building and reconfigure the floor plan to achieve better functionality and overall use of space. Interested in booking a picnic shelter at the beach for your next event? Contact the Town by calling 250.245.6424. ATVS ON TOWN TRAILS All-terrain vehicles (ATVs) are fun to drive for many and allow riders to access off-road trails they might not easily reach on foot. Please be aware that only authorized ATVs and other motorized vehicles are allowed on all Town of Ladysmith trails. During the warmer summer months, please take extreme care with any activity in forested areas that could pose a risk of wildfire. GENERAL LOCAL ELECTIONS COMING UP IN THE FALL General Local (municipal) Elections in B.C. are held every four years on the third Saturday in October. The upcoming local election is right around the corner on October 15, 2022 from 8:00 a.m. - 8:00 p.m. at Frank Jameson Community Centre. Ladysmith residents will vote for a mayor and six councillors for the Town of Ladysmith, as well as up to nine trustees for the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District. Advance voting will take place on October 5 and 12, 2022 from 8:00 a.m - 8:00 p.m. at City Hall. Voting is an important part of the democratic process; this is your opportunity to decide who will represent you to make decisions on the future of Ladysmith. Similarly, serving your community as an elected official can be a rewarding experience. If this is something you are considering, there is a lot of information available to help you make the decision. LADYSMITH YOUTH BRING NEW LIFE TO FJCC MURAL MEET OUR SUSTAINABILITY AMBASSADOR The Town of Ladysmith is pleased to collaborate with Ladysmith Secondary School students to complete a new vibrant art mural design at the Frank Jameson Community Centre. The first phase of this public art and youth engagement project is now complete. Grade 9-12 Visual Arts students displayed their talents along the front wall of the FJCC. Drawing on inspiration from the theme of ‘Connection’, eight individual concept sketches created by youth were amalgamated into a collaborative design. The mural reflects the natural beauty of our local landscape, culture and people, as well as activities that locals enjoy – keeping us “connected” to where we live. The Town’s objective through the mural project is for youth to discover a positive way to express themselves The Town is excited to have launched our second year of the summer Sustainability Ambassador Program with the continued goal of engaging the community in water conservation and efficient recycling. Sustainability Ambassador Ava Smith is entering her second year at the University of Victoria where she is studying Geography. Ava was raised in Ladysmith and is excited to give back to her hometown. In June, over 120 plants were distributed as part of the 2nd Annual Native Plant Giveaway organized through this program. Watch for more outreach initatives in the coming weeks, including recycling audits and pop-up engagements. FortisBC Low Level Flying this Summer - Talon Helicopters will be performing low level flying with LaSen Inc. for FortisBC between July 11, 2022 and August 31, 2022 during daylight hours, as per authorization issued by Transport Canada. The work is FortisBC’s annual inspection of its high-pressure natural gas lines. The aim is to make sure the system continues to operate safely.
