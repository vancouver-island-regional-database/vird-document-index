NANAIMO DISTRICT SECONDARY SCHOOL HIGH SCHOOL FIELD LACROSSE ACADEMY: A PROGRAM OF CHOICE TO: School District Education Committee FROM: Mr. Joel Smith, Teacher, Nanaimo District Secondary School Mr. Tim Davie, Principal, Nanaimo District Secondary School and District Learning Alternative Programs DATE: February 4, 2015 SUBJECT: Implementation of a Field Lacrosse Academy at Nanaimo District Secondary School BACKGROUND The idea for a Field Lacrosse Academy in Nanaimo has existed for many years. Throughout the various organizations in Nanaimo there has always been a desire to see the lacrosse community expand into the high school system. For Nanaimo Lacrosse, the time is now. Both minor associations, the Nanaimo District Lacrosse Association (NDLA) and the Nanaimo Junior Varsity Raiders Field Lacrosse (NJVFL) continue to experience yearly growth in terms of registration. In 2014, NDLA had 438 players registered. This made NDLA the fourth largest association in British Columbia. The Nanaimo Raiders had over 130 players registered. The proposal to bring a field lacrosse academy to Nanaimo has unified and brought together support from all lacrosse organizations in Nanaimo. The consultation process has found partnerships with the NDLA, NJVFL, Senior â€˜Bâ€™ Nanaimo Timbermen, Senior â€˜Aâ€™ and Junior â€˜Aâ€™ Nanaimo Timbermen, and the Nanaimo Masters Lacrosse Club. Through this un-paralleled support the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy will have access to guest coaches, mentorship opportunities, youth community involvement and much more. It speaks to the desire of the lacrosse community to come together and support this project. Initial feedback for the proposed field lacrosse academy has been nothing but positive. The academy will focus on the development of the overall student athlete. Within the academy model, academic success, character and leadership development are paramount to the success of the athletes. It is our goal to have our student athletes experience success through their academic and athletic endeavors while enrolled in the Nanaimo District Secondary School Field Lacrosse Academy. In addition to the general goals of the program, the Field Lacrosse Academy has the opportunity to showcase potential student athletes to Colleges and Universities throughout Canada and the United States. Over the past ten years, on Vancouver Island alone, there have been over 40 student athletes who have gone on to Division One and Two NCAA Universities directly from Field Lacrosse Academies. The potential for scholarships and post secondary education can only be further developed through the development of the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy. A turf field on the NDSS site would be of great value to the Field Lacrosse Academy. The addition of a Field Lacrosse Academy within this region speaks to the overall long-term development of the area. At this time there are 20 students registered ranging from grades 9-11. The class has students enrolled from both streams within the NDSS site. The Fundamentals of Lacrosse class has acted as bridge between the sites and has given more programming options to students. Through the NDLA and NJVFL membership, we have sent out a letter of interest that offers a synopsis of what we are planning at NDSS and gives the Nanaimo lacrosse community a chance to offer insight. At this time we are still gathering data, but feedback again has been positive and a number of parents have indicated interest in putting their son or daughter in the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy. The Field Lacrosse Academy at Spectrum Secondary School currently has 24 students registered in their program for the 2014-15 year. Last year they had 20 enrolled. It would be feasible to offer this program in the first year for up to a total of 30 students at the grade 9-12 level (based on 1 block per semester). At the low end of the spectrum it would be possible to offer this program to a minimum of 20-25 students at the grade 9-12 level (based on 1 block per semester). Based upon expressions of interest, and precedence set by other local high school academies, and the experience at the Spectrum Field Lacrosse Academy, it is considered likely that up to 30 students would, in fact, register for such a program in the first year in which it was offered. We strongly believe this number would only continue to grow as the academy is established. At this time, the program has been designed to include grade 9-12. We have verbal commitments from the Nanaimo Masters Lacrosse Club, Nanaimo Senior B Club, and Nanaimo Senior A and Junior A organization to support the program. DIRECT STAFFING IMPACTS Within the NDSS site, based on one block per semester, the addition of the Field Lacrosse Academy will not significantly affect staffing. When the program demand calls for two blocks per semester, staffing will increase. As the program numbers increase, the demand for other courses will rise resulting in an overall increase of FTE status at the school. At this time we have initiated a consultation process with community Minor Lacrosse, through the NDLA and NJVFL, to gather data on the long-term sustainability of the academy. Through a variety of social media outlets and direct membership email correspondence, we have asked the lacrosse public to express their interest in the program. We have put a soft deadline for this consultation as February 25th. As we assess the data a clearer picture will develop as to possible impacts on future staffing issues at NDSS and in turn other local high schools. Our plan has been submitted to the NDSS Staff Committee for review. FACILITIES IMPACT With the focus of the academy on Field Lacrosse, the facility impact at NDSS would be minimal. One of the biggest attractions to have this academy established at NDSS is the accessibility of a variety of facilities. For the majority of the training, students will be outside on the school field or the neighbouring Rotary Bowl field. Close proximity of the Nanaimo Ice Centre means a dry floor surface is accessible if needed. Facility use will have little to no impact on regular Physical Education classes at NDSS. In working with the Physical Education department at NDSS, the upcoming Fundamentals of Lacrosse class has been placed in the regular facilities rotation for semester 2. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE Should the Board of Education recommend the approval of the Field Lacrosse Academy at NDSS, information would be provided to the students immediately. An information session will be held for all parents and prospective participants of the Academy. Information regarding registration guidelines, enrollment dates and Academy specific details will be advertised through various media outlets. We will also continue to work with the NDLA and NJVFL to contact all registered and eligible players via membership email. As the 2015-2016 school year is fast approaching we will work quickly to incorporate the academy into the NDSS timetable in order to alleviate any scheduling conflicts that could occur. We will be ready to begin the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy in September 2015. EFFECTS UPON OTHER SCHOOLS While we do anticipate movement from out of catchment schools we do not feel the impact on other schools throughout the district will be substantial. We will be attracting students to NDSS who have the desire and dedication to become better lacrosse players and better citizens. All students who participate in the NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy will be eligible to participate in other school-based sports. Generally, the students taking part in this program will be highly motivated and skilled individuals who thrive for sport. In turn, the respective schools these students leave to attend NDSS will see a drop in prospective members of their athletic departments. Given the nature of the Academy program and the likelihood that many of the students will continue to play community Box and Field Lacrosse, it is difficult to know how many will compete for NDSS in other sports. SUMMARY If the board decides to approve the program, the Field Lacrosse Academy will work diligently to promote academic excellence and build the relationship between character development and leadership skills. The program is designed to develop our student athletes into better lacrosse players and ultimately better citizens. We will work diligently to enhance the student experience at NDSS and further develop the unique culture within the NDSS site. We are committed to lacrosse in Nanaimo and through partnerships with our community lacrosse organizations we have a great opportunity to further establish Nanaimo as one of the leaders in lacrosse on Vancouver Island.
