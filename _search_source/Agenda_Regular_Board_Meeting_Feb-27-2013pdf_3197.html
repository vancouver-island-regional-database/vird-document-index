Welch General Decisions Made by the Board  Personnel Matter General Matters Discussed by the Board November 27, 2012, November 21, 2013 Page 22 of 59 Agenda Item Number 11.1 February 22, 2013 Ref: 165825 Dear Board Chairs: On February 20, 2013, government introduced Bill 8, the Miscellaneous Statutes Amendment Act, 2013, that includes proposed amendments to the School Act concerning use of board property for child care purposes. I have enclosed a copy of the proposed School Act amendment for your reference; the entire Bill may be viewed online at http://leg.bc.ca/39th5th/1st_read/gov08-1.htm. If passed, this legislation will amend the School Act to require all boards of education to establish a policy promoting the use of their property by licensed child care providers between the hours of 7 am and 6 pm on weekdays, provided the property is not required for K–12 educational programs, early learning programs, or extracurricular school activities. The proposed legislation further provides that boards may pass along the costs of making their property available to licensed child care providers, but may not make a profit. This “cost-recovery” requirement would not apply to agreements that are currently in place, but would apply to any renewals or extensions of those agreements. I am aware that many boards already support the provision of child care on school property in response to their communities’ specific needs. This provision will not significantly interfere with boards’ authority to determine how to use their property. Rather, the purpose of this legislation is to ensure that all boards promote the use of their property for child care purposes as much as reasonably possible – without disrupting educational activities – to maximize the availability of affordable child care in schools. This proposed legislative change builds on the excellent work already being done by boards across the province, and forms part of BC’s broader Early Years Strategy, an eight-year government commitment to support early childhood development and help families with child care. Thank you for your ongoing hard work on behalf of students and families. To emphasize this commitment, the following beliefs and values and budget principles will be employed by the District in the development of its annual operating budget. Budget Beliefs & Values 1. We believe that schools exist for learners. 2. We believe in providing a balanced curriculum to the whole learner. 3. We believe that learning is a process that individuals can and should pursue throughout their lifetimes. 4. We believe that safe and healthy environments contribute to student learning. 5. We value the pursuit of excellence in teaching and learning. 6. We believe that education is a shared responsibility among government, school, home and community. 7. We believe educational leadership is important in sustaining educational programs. 8. We value the dedication of our educators and support staff and the accomplishments of our learners. Budget Principles 1. The allocation of human and material resources should be directed first toward improving student learning. 2. The allocation of resources should respond to the diversity of student needs and the vulnerability of our learners 3. Resources should be provided to all learners at equitable and sustainable levels. 4. Program choices and adjustments should be made in the best interests of students and should be guided by credible research, successful professional past practice and thoughtful implementation of new or emerging practices. 5. Student learning is best served when adequate staffing exists at all levels of the organization and the necessary infrastructure supports are in place.
