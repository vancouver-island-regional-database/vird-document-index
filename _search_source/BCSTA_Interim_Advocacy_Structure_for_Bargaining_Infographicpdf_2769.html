BCSTA Interim Advocacy Structure for Bargaining This model is designed to ensure that the interests of Boards of Education are fully represented in the provincial government’s newly established interim collective bargaining structure, the tenets of which are: (1) Bargaining will now be the direct responsibility of government, conducted through an appointed negotiator who is accountable and reports directly to government; (2) The interests of Boards of Education will be represented by a single organization, which will be BCSTA; (3) BCSTA’s primary advocacy role will not be constrained; (4) The expertise of BCPSEA staff will continue to support the bargaining process. Boards of Education Bargaining Representative Council Bargaining Advisory Committee BCSTA Board of Directors Primarily responsible for bargaining. Appoints lead negotiator. Bargaining Agent Acts as key link between Boards and the Bargaining Advisory Committee. Existing conflict of interest rules apply. Interim composition: one representative per Board (current BCPSEA Rep Council members). Synthesizes input from Bargaining Representative Council and advises BCSTA Board of Directors. Existing conflict of interest rules apply. Interim composition: nine trustees from 2013 BCPSEA Board of Directors, two BCSTA Board of Directors, one BCSSA rep, one BCASBO rep, BCPSEA staff member, BCSTA staff member. Establishes BCSTA advocacy positions based on input from the Bargaining Advisory Committee, member resolutions, policies and bylaws. Advocates the interests and perspectives of Boards to the Bargaining Agent. Advises BCSTA Board of Directors regarding bargaining developments and outcomes for recommendations back to Boards.
