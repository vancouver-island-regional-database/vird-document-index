CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Mr. Munro presented his annual Report on Student Achievement. The Superintendent noted areas of strength for the district as well as challenging areas. Our areas of strength are  The five-year trend for the percent of English 10 students who receive a C- or better has improved from 95 percent in 2005-06 to 97 percent in 2009-10. For Aboriginal students, the percent has improved from 85 percent to 96 percent.  This past year, improvements were seen in the number of Aboriginal students eligible to graduate and the percentage of first-time Aboriginal Grade 12 graduates. Our challenging areas are  The six-year Dogwood completion rate is a continuing concern with the district’s completion rates continuing to lag behind the average in the province.  Too many of the district’s Grade 4 students are not meeting expectations in reading On the Grade 4 FSA in reading, 26 percent of the students taking the exam in 2009-10 were not meeting expectations. Mr. Munro said that the district’s Early Development Indicators continue to demonstrate that about 30 percent of SD68 students entering Kindergarten are vulnerable in at least one area of their development. Mr. Munro said the district, in partnership with the Ministry of Children and Family Development, have taken steps to ensure that children in care are known to school staff and provided with the appropriate support.
