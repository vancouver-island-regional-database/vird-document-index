please go to the online agenda at Page 33 of 40 3.3 Correspondence (to view correpondence please go to the online agenda at Page 34 of 40 3.3 Dear Board Members, We are writing in response to the latest budget proposal in which the English stream students at Ecole Davis Road Elementary will be relocated to LPS and LIS for the start of the next school year. As parents of a current and future English stream student(s) at Ecole Davis Road we would like to voice our extreme displeasure with the proposal and ask the board members to vote down this incredibly short sited and destructive idea. After attending the information session at Ecole Davis Road on Monday, April 15th it became very clear that this proposal was tabled with the intent of mortally wounding Ecole Davis Road so that the end game of achieving a sole elementary and secondary campus in the center of Ladysmith could be achieved. If the overall intent is to create a “big box store” approach to the education of our children then we have the right to know so we can make the appropriate choices moving forward. As recent arrivals to this school district we don’t pretend to know everything pertaining to budgetary constraints but we do know one thing – Ecole Davis Road is a great school! Since arriving at Ecole Davis Road and enrolling our oldest son in grade two we have experienced what every parent hopes for when they send their little ones off to school. An environment filled with learning, caring, sharing and fun, a school that encourages creativity and individuality in a safe, comfortable and inviting atmosphere. Ecole Davis Road is that school. Being parents and making choices for our children is not always easy but when our son comes home every day with a smile on his face and is eager to learn and participate at school we know we made the right choice. We now ask you to make the right choice. A bigger school does not make a better school. Listen to the people at the heart of the matter, the staff, teachers, parents and most importantly the children. Your decisions affect our lives in more ways than you can possibly imagine. We believe in our children and we believe in Ecole Davis Road. Make the right choice. V9G 1A2 Municipal Hall (250) 245-6400  Fax (250) 245-6411  info@ladysmith.ca  www.ladysmith.ca April 17, 2013 Mr. Jamie Brennan Chair, Board of Education School District 68 Via e-mail c/o Mr. Phil Turin, Secretary-Treasurer Dear Mr. Brennan and Trustees: I am writing with the support of Council of the Town of Ladysmith regarding the proposed changes to Ecole Davis Road School. Having also had the opportunity to speak about this matter with SecretaryTreasurer Phil Turin, I understand and appreciate the challenges involved with your budget deliberations and the necessity to make difficult choices when revenues are declining. However, I wish to express both my concern and extreme disappointment over the process undertaken by the School District in regards to the removal of the English stream from Ecole Davis Road School. Although this was announced at a School Board meeting on April 10, it is my understanding that the full proposal was literally dropped on the community on Monday, April 15th, with an expectation that individuals were required to register with School District staff within less than 48 hours in order to have a "one time only" opportunity to address the Board of School Trustees regarding this matter on Thursday, April 18th. The community/school/children's reaction? Anger, outrage, disbelief, anxiety, fear, and tears. Obviously, this has not been an appropriate model for public engagement. This proposal is no small decision. Although the school will continue to operate, this proposal would in effect close a neighbourhood school for some one hundred and ten (2013/2014) children who do not enrol in the French immersion program. This is a neighbourhood school that has served this portion of our community for over five decades. I ask, is this the appropriate public consultation process for the closure of a neighbourhood school? On behalf of the Council of the Town of Ladysmith, I respectfully request that the Board of School Trustees table consideration of this proposal and facilitate further community discussion on this matter.
