Goals • Empower branches and local boards to take these materials. • Support from BCSTA through resources and strategy direction. • Emphasize that this campaign is non-partisan. Why Non-Partisan? The BC Election Act requires registration and reporting for third parties that pay for “election advertising” and imposes spending limits on them. Under the Act, "election advertising" means the transmission to the public by any means, during the campaign period, of an advertising message that promotes or opposes, directly or indirectly, a registered political party or the election of a candidate, including an advertising message that takes a position on an issue with which a registered political party or candidate is associated…” (Election Act, s. 228). There are some exceptions set out in the Election Act (e.g. the publication of a column in a newspaper article; a group sending a document directly to its members) A Few Audiences to Consider • • • • • • • • • Political parties and independents Boards of Education (trustees) Local Governments Voters (72% have no children in K - 12) Parents and Grand Parents Youth Business Community Service Clubs Other sectors that rely on a well educated work force.
