CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY • Linaya Roberts, Grade 7 student, spoke on behalf of child youth and family support workers and recounted how an individual in that position provided support to her during a difficult time. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 15, 2014 • Maddy McConnell, Grade 7 student, talked about how her child youth and family worker has helped her resolve her difficulties and build her confidence. • Abbey Sitler – Grade 7 student, talked about the ways that child, youth and family support workers have a positive influence on students. • Rob Zver, CUPE Local 606 president, said that the budget should share the reductions equitably among employee groups and noted that there are no reductions proposed for administration positions. He asked trustees and senior staff to work with the union to see if they can find other ways to balance the budget. • Mike Ball, NDTA president, said that budget shortfalls are all too familiar for school districts, because of underfunding by the provincial government. He asked why there were no proposed cuts to administration positions. He spoke on behalf of teacher-librarians and elementary counsellors and discussed the importance of reducing class sizes. • Elementary counsellors outlined the role of counsellors in elementary schools and the higher number of students at risk in the district, compared to the province. They discussed the ways that elementary counsellors support the RTI model and self-regulation initiatives and urged the Board to not make any reductions to the number of elementary counsellors. • Teacher-librarians talked about the important role that secondary teacher-librarians play in professional learning communities and in assisting teachers and students in identifying resources. They noted that the new library software program being purchased by the district is in no way a replacement for teacher-librarians. • Andrea Berkey, an English teacher, spoke on behalf of secondary teacher-librarians. She said when she asked teachers at her school about the ways they work with the teacher-librarian to support students she discovered a wide variety of important initiatives. She said that teacherlibrarians are secret agents of change and will Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 15, 2014 support 21st century learning in the district. • Child, youth and family support workers provided a presentation that illustrated the work that they do supporting groups of students and individual students. They said their mission is to get students to school and keep students in class, in partnership with teachers, administrators, support staff and outside agencies. • Rick Rathy spoke about the proposed elimination of the Career Technical Centre (CTC) coordinator position for next year. He said that the provincial government is focusing on increasing the number of students involved in careers training and he outlined the ways that the CTC coordinator communicates with students and parents and makes them aware of the opportunities in the trades. • Sandra Walsh, a child, youth and family support worker, said that these employees support the most marginalized students in the system and help facilitate belonging and self-worth for students who need support. She said that there is not enough support for these students currently, and the proposed reductions would have a serious impact on them. • Four John Barsby teachers spoke on behalf of teacher-librarians. They said that teacherlibrarians inspire students to read and to learn and that the teacher-librarian is the hub of the school. They described the way that the teacherlibrarian helps build literacy for all students and supports the introduction of technology in a school. • Bob McKerns, a maintenance employee whose position is affected by the proposed reductions, described the way that the work carried out by his position saves money for the district and supports safety. • Carla Sampson, program manager for Ladysmith RCMP victim services, spoke about the partnership that her program has with school child, youth and family support workers in supporting youth in times of crisis. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 15, 2014 • Christian Ostaffy, shipping and receiving clerk, said that his position is cost-effective for the district and that it is not practical to think that all shipments could be delivered directly to schools. • Kelly Dunaway, speaking on behalf of CUPE, Local 606, outlined the importance of support staff positions. He said that the CUPE positions being proposed for cuts would directly affect students. He urged trustees to ask for other options that would include administrative reductions. • Del Plensky, a maintenance employee whose position is proposed for reduction, said the district does not currently have an adequate number of people maintaining school grounds. • Susan Reno, a child, youth and family support worker at the secondary level described the work she does to help vulnerable students stay in school. • Rob Lumsden, electrician general foreman, spoke about the proposed reduction of one electronics technician position. He said that the position repairs a wide variety of equipment across the district and that it saves the district money. He said that electricians are not able to work on this specialized equipment.
