DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET January 10, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer District Programs Background On September 27, 2023, the Board of Education passed the following motion: That the Board of Education of School District no. 68 (Nanaimo- Ladysmith), direct the Superintendent to direct staff to present at January’s 2024, Education Committee Meeting, a review of all the alternative learning programming within the school district as it pertains to the budget and the boards current strategic plan of Inclusion and student success. This Information Sheet and the attached District Program Report is in response to that motion. Discussion The report provides an overview of the following district programs: • • • • • Learning Alternatives Career Technical Center District Life Skills Island Connect Ed. Inclusion Outreach Team (Tier 3 Program) For each of the programs noted, the following information is included: • • • • • • Overview of the program and educational services provided or available to students Funded student enrolment Program resource allocations Program expenses including staffing, supplies and services Number of staffing hours and positions for CUPE, NDTA and Administrators Any variance between funding and expenses It is important for the Board to note that district funding goes directly into a centralized pool and is then allocated out to schools, programs, departments, contractual obligations, district operations and district initiatives per internal formulas, estimated needs and in priority order as resources allow. For further clarity, some schools and programs are known to be more costly than others and certain types of educational delivery is less expensive than others, the variance allows the District to provide both. It is important not to look at each program’s financial information in isolation of each other. Appendix A: District Program Report
