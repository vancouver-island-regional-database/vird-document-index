Steve Rae, Chair Carrie McVeigh, Secretary-Treasurer Page 6 of 10 Charlene McKay 4907 Denford Place Nanaimo BC V9V1R3 Steve Rae, Board of Education Chair School District #68 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo BC V9R 3K6 March 13, 2018 Dear Chair Rae, I write to you today with a request for consideration during the upcoming budget discussions that will be had by the Board of Education for the following fiscal year 2018/2019. I request that the Board of Education consider the following: 1. To continue the Route 1 bus transportation route to Dover Bay Secondary, with consideration of reviewing the time of student pickup and drop off. This would support the following: 2. This bus could then be utilized to transport students from Rutherford Elementary to Frank J Ney Elementary on its return trip to Transportation Department. The reverse could be done for the afternoon. I have provided the following information/opinions for your consideration: 1. Keeping Route 1 in place would eliminate the need to pay transportation assistance to the current 12 users of this route. I fully understand that this route is an expense greater than the transportation assistance. However, the ability to support students transitioning to a new school post school closure is extremely valuable to the current Rutherford parent community as well as the current Frank J Ney parent community. It is clear that transporting students from Rutherford to Frank J Ney will ease the burden on Rutherford parents, but for Frank J Ney parents seeing a bus bring students to school instead of the possible 60+ cars in an already congested traffic loop would be better for reducing potential traffic congestion at our school. 2. Historically, prior Boards as well as the current Board of Education have provided this type of assistance to support the transition between school communities. While often in the past this has been done on the single criteria of distance, I would request a thorough review of the potential revised route with special consideration given to those families travelling up to 4.5 km to their new catchment school be given extra thought. The conditions of Hammond Bay Road may be “safe” for a teen or young adult to safely navigate (i.e., as mentioned in the Information Sheet, January 17, Subject Transportation Admin Policy 512, Pete Sabo/Dave Prevost), but for elementary students ages 5-12, the same is not true from a parent perspective. 3. Should Route 1 be kept and potentially revised to include busing students to/from Rutherford to Frank J Ney, the budget aspect of this decision would not be considered a new or extra expense as it already exists within the current fiscal year’s budget. 4. The Frank J Ney parent community has been advocating for well over a year to work on infrastructure at Williamson Road and Hammond Bay Road, as this is a significant area of safety concern for parents at our school. Page 7 of 10 a. Parents at our school are currently involved in an active safe travel planning committee to look at increasing Active Travel to and from school for our students. The general limits of this work, however, will be to a maximum 1500 m/1.5 km. b. Should we be successful in increasing the active transportation at our school and eliminating some of the traffic created by driving our students to/from school, this still leaves the other 3 km between that “safe area” and the remainder of the roads from our school to our Rutherford family’s homes up to another 3.7 km away. In essence, we will reduce our traffic congestion by families close to the school, only to see it increase as our new families will likely be driving their students to school (based on survey results from the travel planning committee). c. If the parents in the Frank J Ney community will not allow our children to cross Hammond Bay Road due to the safety concerns of speed of cars and the lack of adherence by drivers to follow the laws allows for safe passage across a traffic corridor on a clearly marked crosswalk, how can we possibly expect our incoming Rutherford families to spend 4.5 km on the same road walking with their children? d. If the advocacy of Frank J Ney parents is successful in attaining recommendations for traffic calming in and around our school, the potential wait for infrastructure to be put in place is at best 2-3 years away. As a reference, McGirr Elementary had a Safe Walk planning committee in 2007, and only now, in 2018, are they seeing the near completion of installation of the last traffic calming/safety infrastructure upgrade based upon those recommendations. 5. Lastly, as a related consideration for the families of Rutherford Elementary who will soon be joining the Frank J Ney community is the need for daycare. Having students bussed to/from Rutherford would provide further support for many families who have a student that could get off the bus and walk home for the day from the bus stop. Many parents are considering daycare for their children who would have previously just walked home after school only because they do not want their students travelling so far on Hammond Bay Road on foot/bicycle. By being a little creative with Route 1, you have an opportunity to support a large group of students in their transition to a new school with very little impact to the overall school district budget. I believe I am offering you a creative way to find a solution to assist families in their transition, reduce future traffic congestion at Frank J Ney Elementary (this is already a very significant issue), and ensure the safety of students getting to and from school without spending a large amount of money on something additional. Perhaps, this is something that can be approved on a temporary basis to be reviewed again after the Safe Travel Planning Committees from local schools are completed and their potential infrastructure recommendations are available for review by the Board of Education. In the time I have spent working with you and the Board, I have heard time and again for people to bring solutions to the Board to consider, be creative, give us something we can look at as option. I have tried here to provide you with a potential creative solution for the Board of Education to support families in my neighborhood and my growing school community. I am a Rutherford Elementary School parent and am concerned about the safety of our kids during the 40 minute walk from our area to Frank J. Ney Elementary when Rutherford closes at the end of this school year. I was given your name by Trustee Stephanie Higginson to request a bus route hazard evaluation for our children in the hopes that there is a possibility that there could be school bus transportation provided, as the walk to Frank Ney down Hammond Bay seems a dangerous route for young kids. The street is busy, narrow and without crosswalks across the merging streets where, during busy hours, cars are inching out trying to find space in the traffic on Hammond Bay to turn left and right impeding pedestrians trying to cross, especially on Parkway Drive. There are no traf?c lights the full length of the walk, which means uncontrolled traffic at every intersection. The biggest concern is how narrow the streets are, without a shoulder, and how busy the traffic is. During part of the year, the students will be walking to school in the dark, as they will have to leave 40 minutes ahead of bell time to reach Frank Ney. Considering how often we encounter rainy, dark weather in our city, most of the year will have our children walking in difficult driving conditions on a narrow, busy street. Before the vote to close Rutherford, we have had Trustees do the walk or bike the distance from Rutherford to Frank Ney on a sunny summer Sunday and they believed that the walk was "far, but pleasant” and so the vote was passed. I see from the checklist that the bus hazard evaluation includes such information as lighting, traffic lights, intersections, paved shoulder etcetera for criteria and am grateful that this will be an objective opinion using standard evaluation methods and not personal preference. I live on Parkway Drive and see the line up of traffic daily during busy times trying to turn right and left onto Hammond Bay and clogging the intersection. It is my hope that this evaluation will be done during the times when the kids would be leaving for school, which would be likely around 7:45 or so, so that there is an accurate depiction of whether it is safe for young children to be walking this route or whether a school bus may be necessary. In addition, if the evaluation is done during the months when it is not dark during that time, if consideration could be given to also checking the route when it is dark to assess for lighting and visibility would be greatly appreciated as this would be the most worrisome aspect of the walk. Although there are other routes to Frank Ney other than Hammond Bay, the hills in the neighbourhood make walking to Frank Ney using the less busy streets an impossible task for young children. Once again, thank you for your consideration and I look forward to hearing from you. Sincerely, Anita Hatch
