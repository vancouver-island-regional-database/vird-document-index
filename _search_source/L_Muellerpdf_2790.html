Lorena Mueller 1128 Carey Place Ladysmith, B.C. V9G 1P9 November 7, 2016 School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, B.C. V9R 3K6 Attention: Jamie Brennan, Natasha Bob, Tanya Brzovic, Scott Kimler, Stephanie Higginson, Steve Rae, Bill Robinson, Noah Routley, Jeff Solomon Dear Trustees, In September 2015 I sent you a letter expressing my concerns regarding my daughter’s class beginning the school year with a substitute teacher, as the teacher that had been in the position had posted to another position. I explained that this wasn’t the first time one of my children started the school year without a permanent teacher, and substitutes were in these positions for several weeks before a permanent teacher was hired. Shortly after I sent this letter, I learned that a class at another school in town had a substitute teacher for three weeks, after the teacher went on her scheduled maternity leave in October. Neither class should have had a substitute, as the departure of the teacher from both positions was predictable. In my letter I had requested that hiring practices be investigated with actions taken to prevent this from recurring. I received a reply indicating the board was also concerned, and staff had been asked to investigate. I did not receive any information on the results of the investigation. A few weeks ago I learned that the English kindergarten / grade 1 class at Ecole North Oyster had started the school year with a substitute teacher, as the teacher that had been in the position the previous year had accepted another job posting. What should have been an exciting start in learning school routines was delayed. I am sure the students were disappointed and confused, as my daughter was when this happened to her kindergarten class. When I heard that this had occurred, I too was disappointed, as it indicated to me that measures which would prevent this from recurring had not been implemented. I also wondered how many other classes in School District 68 started the school year with a substitute teacher. Therefore, I am again requesting that the hiring process be investigated, with actions to prevent this from recurring identified, and an action plan to address them developed and implemented. I would like to be informedof the findings of the investigation, and what actions will be taken to prevent this from recurring. Thank-you for looking into this. I look forward to your response. Sincerely, Lorena Mueller
