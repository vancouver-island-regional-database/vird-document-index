May 8, 2014 Ref: 174376 To: All Board Chairs All School Districts Dear Colleagues: I am writing to you as a follow-up to the BC Public School Employers’ Association’s (BCPSEA) April 30th letter to school trustee representatives in response to the BC Teachers’ Federation (BCTF) ‘Stage 1’ strike action. In its letter, BCPSEA indicated that, in response to the BCTF’s stage 1 job action and due to the fact that there has been a lack of progress at the bargaining table to date, it is BCPSEA’s intention to take the following reciprocal action: 1. Transfer of the Cost of Benefit Premiums to the BCTF BCPSEA intends to use the provisions of section 62 of the Labour Relations Code to require the transfer of benefit premiums to the BCTF. Specifically, in advance of the due date of the premiums payable for June benefit coverage, BCPSEA will require the BCTF to pay the employers’ cost of all health and welfare benefits, with the following exception: the employers will continue to pay the premium costs for the life insurance, accidental death and dismemberment plans, and employee family assistance plans. BCPSEA indicated that it will return to the union an amount equal to this payment if the parties conclude a tentative agreement, and the agreement is ratified by the union by the end of June 2014, and the union does not escalate its job action into a full work stoppage, or otherwise cause a serious and immediate disruption of educational services. 2. Cancellation of the June Administrative Day Failing the conclusion of a tentative agreement before the June 27th administrative day, the administrative day is cancelled and teachers will not be paid for that day. If an agreement is achieved before the administrative day, it will go ahead as a paid day. The purpose of this letter is to inform you that it is the Ministry of Education’s intent to recoup savings realized by boards of education resulting from the transfer of benefit premiums to the BCTF, the cancellation of the June 27th administrative day, and any escalation of job action on the part of the BCTF (if applicable). Consistent with the approach taken during previous strike situations, the Ministry intends to recoup from boards of education, 80% of savings realized – the remaining 20% will be left with boards of education in recognition of the fact that additional costs can be incurred at the district level as a result of job action. … /2 Ministry of Education Office of the Minister Mailing Address: PO Box 9045 Stn Prov Govt Victoria BC V8W 9E2 Location: Parliament Buildings Victoria -2- I would appreciate it if you would please share this letter with your local school trustees. Ministry of Education staff will follow up with Superintendents and Secretary-Treasurers shortly with further information and instructions.
