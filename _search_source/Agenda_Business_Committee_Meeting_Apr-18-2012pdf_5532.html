UNFINISHED BUSINESS Custodial Review Report Cascade Facilities Management Consultants presented the results of a review they conducted of the school district’s custodial services. The district hired the consultants to prepare the review to gain an independent perspective of the service levels, staffing and organization of custodial services. As part of the review, the consultants compared SD68 to six other benchmark districts; received input from departments, schools and CUPE; and made on-site inspections of six schools. They analyzed current custodial staffing and workload, evaluated procedures and reviewed supervision of custodial staff. The report makes a number of recommendations related to department management, organization, staffing and practices. Financial Update as of February 29th for the 2011-2012 Budget Year Mr. Turin reported that the school district’s finances for the year are on target and a surplus of $1.7 million is expected at June 30. Mr. Turin reported on several highlights from the Ministry of Education 2012-13 Funding Grant announced this week: • The district will not receive funding for the new category of vulnerable students; however, will continue to receive $2.1 million in Community Link funding. • The allocation of funds for transportation is decreasing by $180,193; however, the district will be protected in decreasing amounts for the next two years. • There is a new funding category called Supplement for the Education Plan in the amount of $270,804 for this year. • Funding protection in the amount of $4 million has been received and is $1.7 million less than funds generated by the previous formula. Mr. Turin recommended several changes to the current budget schedule. Details will be announced once approved by the Board. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS 2012-2013 Budget Mr. Turin presented a draft budget for 2012-13 school year at tonight's meeting. He said that thanks to a projected surplus this year, the district will not be required to make any reductions to next year’s budget. Mr. Hutchinson outlined how the district’s strategic plan will influence future budgets. He said that future budgets will center on the district’s strategic plan goals focusing on improving student learning. April 11, 2012 Page 7 of 8 Agenda Item Number 6.2 2 Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 11, 2012 Mr. Turin said that the projected difference between revenue and expenditure next year is $1.6 million and that senior management is proposing that the district use $1.6 million in surplus from this year to balance next year’s budget. He warned that the current district budget levels are not sustainable over the long term. Next year, the school district expects to receive $4 million of funding protection in its Ministry of Education grant. Funding protection has been provided to districts to cushion the funding loss that comes with declining enrolment, but the amount of the grant is gradually being reduced. Mr. Turin said that this year’s surplus is due to the funding protection grant the district has received this year and that the draft budget does not include any new initiatives – for example, implementation of the district’s technology plan and strategic planning initiatives but that funds for initiatives could come from the Local School Calendar proposal for next year ($366,000). Mr. Turin noted that starting with the 2012-13 budget all school districts are required to follow the Public Accounting Standards and that both the Special Purpose and Capital Funds must now be included in the overall budget.
