BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: April 12, 2017 TO: Business Committee FROM: Secretary-Treasurer, Carrie McVeigh SUBJECT: Trustee Remuneration Recommendation: That the Education Committee recommends the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive the report on Trustee Remuneration dated April 12, 2017, and refer the matter to public consultation to amend the current policy on Trustee Remuneration such that Policy 2.6, Section 7.1.1 be amended to read: “7.1.1 Trustee remuneration is to be reviewed by the Board in September in each year; such review is to be based on remuneration paid to trustees of comparable British Columbia School Districts.” Background: By Board motions R16/03/30-13 and SB16/08/31-14 (motions included at the end, for reference), staff was requested to: a. prepare a comparison of other similar districts’ trustee remuneration, including remuneration for the Board chair, vice chair and committee chairs; and, b. draft an administrative procedure for annual review of trustee remuneration based on comparative boards. Comparison to Other Districts Attached to this Action Sheet is a table showing the remuneration data for four other school districts. All of the districts have student populations that are comparable to the number of students enrolled in NLPS. In addition to the remuneration given to Chairs, Vice-Chairs and Trustees, the table shows the other support provided to elected officials as well the method each district uses to review or increase the remuneration paid. None of the districts listed have a different rate of pay for committee chairs. It is interesting to note that two districts (three including NLPS) base increases on the Consumer Price Index. One district bases increases on a weighted average of increases given to their employee groups in the previous year. One district reviews trustee remuneration and, if an increase is approved, the amount of the increase is prescribed in policy. That district has not increased trustee remuneration since the December 2014 inaugural meeting. Discussion It is awkward for a board to increase its own remuneration. Critics will accuse a board who grants themselves an increase in pay of being self-serving and diverting funds from the classroom. Other critics will maintain that candidates run for office out of a sense of community service and should not be paid at all. Supporters will maintain that there needs to be fair compensation for those individuals who are willing to serve in public office. Some locally elected boards resolve this conflict by setting the rate of pay for the next board in advance of the election process. Other boards look to retired trustees or elected officials in other organizations, municipalities for example, for recommendations on trustee remuneration. Still other organizations base the remuneration for their own elected officials on what their peer organizations pay their elected officials. One thing that creates a problem is to not address the issue of remuneration for years on end. It is very difficult for a board to then justify a “catch-up” increase in pay. Review of Existing Policy Policy 2.6 Governance and Management contains the existing NLPS policy dealing with trustee remuneration. Section 7 of the policy states: 7.1 Under Section 71 of the School Act, a Board may authorize the payment of remuneration to be paid to Trustees. The Income Tax Act allows part of this remuneration to be declared as a tax-free expense allowance. 7.1.1 Trustees remuneration is indexed to the cost of living each September. There are no Procedures accompanying this policy. New Policy To amend this policy so that the remuneration of trustees is reviewed annually based on comparable districts, the following wording change would meet the intent of the Board’s motion: Replace existing 7.1.1 with “Trustee remuneration is to be reviewed by the Board in September in each year; such review is to be based on remuneration paid to trustees of comparable British Columbia School Districts.” An Administrative Procedure would not be required to implement this policy. R16/03/30-13 The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to investigate and report back on the following: (a) The necessary steps & ministry response to the possibility of reducing the current SD68 Board from nine members to seven; (b) What are the estimated savings from reducing the number of trustees from nine to seven?; (c) SD68 trustee stipends (how do they compare; stipends of board chair, vice chair & committee chairs versus trustees at large; should stipends increase or be tied to COLA (cost of living allowances)?; (d) options for a ward system (versus the current "at large" system). Report should be delivered for consideration on or before October 26, 2016. SB16/08/31-14 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve adjustments to trustee remuneration, effective September 1, 2015 and September 1, 2016; and That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) direct staff to provide the Board, at the October Regular meeting, with trustee remuneration comparisons; and That staff develop and present a draft administrative procedure, at the October Regular meeting, for the annual review of trustee remuneration based on comparative Boards.
