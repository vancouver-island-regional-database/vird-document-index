May 21, 2014 Ref: 174675 Dear Teresa Rezansoff and Board Chairs: As we continue to work through challenging times on behalf of our young learners throughout the province, I want to thank you for the opportunity we had to engage at the BCSTA AGM in April. Your questions, expressions of concern—and of support—are all appreciated. Our work together makes a difference every day and I value my relationship with local boards of education in our co-governance work. Like you, I am deeply committed to a strong education system and want to ensure that every young person in British Columbia can succeed. During our time at the AGM and at meetings with boards throughout the province, I heard from many trustees about the issues and complexities of your work at the local level. One often-referenced topic was government’s recently announced cash management strategy, developed in response to one of the Auditor General’s recommendations. It asks government to work with school districts and other government organizations to better manage cash flows. As you know, a central part of this strategy is for government to work with boards on a case by case basis to contribute available local funds to the overall cost of upcoming capital projects in their districts. We have heard your concerns clearly about the process and we will work with the Ministry of Finance to explore actions that respect the overall goal of managing public debt, but also balance the issues identified by boards. There is no doubt that we continue to work through fiscally challenging times and need to redouble our collective efforts to reduce overhead through administrative savings and shared systems. At the end of the day our decisions—both provincially and locally—need to focus on learning outcomes for all students. I want to expand on the successes of the Shared Service Delivery Project over the past few years so we can achieve greater efficiencies. We know there are more opportunities and that is why I have appointed Mike McKay as a strategic advisor to work with the Ministry team and districts to consider new approaches to administrative savings and shared services within a framework of co-governance. …2 Ministry of Education Office of the Minister Mailing Address: PO Box 9045 Stn Prov Govt Victoria BC V8W 9E2 Location: Parliament Buildings Victoria -2- High on the list of current system challenges is the bargaining mandate. Please be assured that we remain committed to a successful conclusion to bargaining, preferably before the end of the current school year. We want stability for students, parents and teachers. However, any agreement must align with the government’s bargaining mandate and be affordable for BC taxpayers. I am confident that, despite our current challenges, we share a goal of helping all young British Columbians thrive and succeed in an ever-changing world. I thank you for your efforts to support student learning in BC’s education system and I look forward to continuing to work with you in the spirit of collaboration, cooperation and co-governance. Sincerely, Peter Fassbender Minister of Education
