SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3570P PROTECTION OF STUDENTS Standards of Conduct for Employees Working with Students And Complaints of Improper Conduct I Standards of Conduct for Employees Working with Students a. Respect the position of trust and influence which exists between employees and students in the school environment. b. Act in the best interests of students. c. Speak and act toward students with respect and dignity and deal with them judiciously, mindful of their individual rights and sensibilities. d. Establish and maintain the boundaries of an appropriate and professional relationship. e. Do not engage in any sexually related behaviour with a student with or without consent. f. To provide students with an emotionally, intellectually, and physically safe environment. g. Take action to address student misbehaviour which is similar to that of a kind, firm, and judicious parent. h. Maintain confidential information they receive about students unless there is a lawful or proper reason or requirement to do otherwise. Respect the confidentiality of student information and respect the privacy of students and families unless disclosure is required by law. i. Complaint Procedure a. Form of Complaint Where a student or parent wishes to make a complaint about the conduct of an employee, the Principal or other School District administrator shall receive the complaint. The student or parent shall be encouraged to make the complaint in writing. All complaints shall be investigated and a written record of the complaint shall be made by the Principal. The Principal or other School District administrator shall consult with the Superintendent or his/her designate regarding the manner of investigation of the complaint. b. Responsibility for Investigation The Superintendent or designate shall determine whether the complaint shall be investigated by the Principal, School District administration, or an external investigator. c. Consideration of Suspension pending Investigation The Superintendent shall determine whether the employee should be suspended for the protection of students, under Section 15(5) of the School Act, pending the outcome of the investigation. Should a teacher be suspended under Section 15(5) of the Act, the Superintendent shall report the suspension to the B.C. College of Teachers. -2- SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3570P d. Criminal Conduct Alleged Where the allegations in the complaint appear to involve criminal conduct, the matter shall be reported to the Police and the investigation of the complaint shall be coordinated with the Police. The School Board may suspend an employee under Section 15(4) of the School Act where the employee is charged with a criminal offence that, in the opinion of the Board, renders the employee unsuitable to perform his/her duties. e. Notification to Employee The employee who is the subject of the complaint shall be notified as soon as practicable and in accordance with the relevant collective agreement or contract, unless such notification would prejudice the investigation. In the case of allegations of criminal conduct, notification to the employee will be coordinated with the Police. f. Notification of Parent/Guardian Where the parent did not initiate the complaint, the parents/guardians of the student who is the subject of the complaint will be notified of the allegations of misconduct involving their child by the Superintendent or designate. g. Investigation The nature of the investigation will depend on the nature and seriousness of the complaint. However, in all cases, the investigator shall seek a fair, thorough, and objective investigation which is sufficiently thorough to ensure a proper assessment of the complaint. The investigation shall be conducted without delay and within a reasonable period of time. The Superintendent or designate shall review the conclusions from the investigation which has been conducted and determine whether any disciplinary action should be taken against the employee and, where appropriate, make a recommendation for disciplinary action to the School Board. The Superintendent or designate may decide that further investigation is necessary before any decision or recommendation can be made. Relevant collective agreement provisions shall apply to the investigation process and the employee shall have the right to union representation. -3- SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3570P The School District administrator conducting the investigation shall maintain a written record as to the outcome of the investigation of the complaint, whether or not the complaint is substantiated. In the case of an investigation by an external investigator, the Superintendent or designate will ensure that a copy of the report of the investigator is maintained in the School District. These records, and any other records which result from the investigation, will be stored in a confidential file in the office of the Director of Human Resources. h. School Board Hearing In the event that a School Board hearing is required under the collective agreement, the Superintendent or designate shall report on the matter to the School Board with other School District administrators present as appropriate. The employee and his/her representative will have an opportunity to respond to the allegations. The School Board may ask questions of the employee or members of administration regarding the complaint and other relevant matters. i. Counselling The Superintendent or designate shall give consideration to whether counselling should be offered to students, employees, or other persons as a result of the matters under investigation. j. Report to the BC. College of Teachers Should the Superintendent or the School Board take disciplinary action against an employee who is a member of the B.C. College of Teachers, it shall be reported to the Registrar of the College in accordance with Section 16 of the School Act by the Superintendent or designate. k. Privacy Considerations The requirements of the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act apply to the protection of privacy and the disclosure of information concerning the investigation of the complaint. l. Publication of Outcome of Investigation -4- SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 3570P The parent of the student who is the subject of the complaint will be notified of the outcome of the investigation to the extent permitted by the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act.
