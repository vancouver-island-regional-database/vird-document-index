From the Department of Finance Date: May 29, 2019 To: The Board of Education From: Taunia Sutton, Director of Finance Subject: 2019-20 Annual Budget Bylaw Presentation The purpose of this update is to provide and summarize the Annual Budget Bylaw document as well as highlight the changes that have been made compared to the 2018-19 Amended Annual Budget. Background and Information: On May 1, 2019 the Board of Education was presented with high level information on how the preliminary 2019-20 Annual Budget was created which included guiding principles, budget criteria, key challenges and assumptions, changes in the Operating Grant and necessary adjustments to revenues and expenditures. Also included was a document that highlighted particular budget items or strategies that were then tied to budget criteria and Board goals in order to communicate how the current budget supports them. Further, on May 8, 2019 the Board was provided with a preliminary 2019-20 Annual Budget report for review and discussion. Budget requirements: Boards are required to prepare a balanced budget where board revenues plus any appropriated surpluses fully fund the following:       annual operating expenses, annual special purpose fund (SPF) expenses, annual capital fund expenses, tangible capital asset acquisitions (from Operating, SPF’s & Local Capital), planned reduction of unfunded liability for employee future benefits, and any planned reduction of prior years’ deficits. Per section 113 of the School Act (Adoption of Budget), Boards must prepare an annual budget and have it adopted by bylaw on or before June 30, 2019 and submitted to the Ministry by this date. Per Ministerial Order 033/09 “Accounting Practices Order”, a capital fund deficit is permitted for the amount that amortization of tangible capital assets expense exceeds amortization of deferred capital revenue. 1 Schedule 4 – Capital Revenue and Expense This schedule discloses the budgeted revenues and expenses for the Capital Fund. Revenue amounts include capital funding that the district will recognize, or account for, to support capital operations over the course of the fiscal year, as well as interest earned on Local Capital balances. Budgeted expenses include the amount we must expense for the amortization of all our tangible capital assets which includes items such as buildings, equipment and technology. The Schedule also includes the amount of funds transferred from the Operating Fund to Local Capital for the purchase of tangible capital assets such as technology and may also include a portion set aside to support capital projects not funded by the Ministry of Education but necessary to ensure our facilities are maintained and upgraded as necessary. The Capital Fund balance, as presented on Schedule 4, budgets a deficit of ($1,466,129) due to the difference between the revenue we anticipate will be recognized, the expense of capital asset amortization and the transfer to Local Capital. This deficit is the only budgeted deficit identified on Statement 2 of the Budget Bylaw.
