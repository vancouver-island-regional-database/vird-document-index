ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee: Tim Harris Date: Oct 23, 2025 School Names/Zone: Ladysmith Primary Highlights: Last school year, I had the opportunity to visit the school on two occasions. During one of those visits, Principal Neil kindly provided a tour of Ladysmith Primary, which offered a great chance to see the learning environment firsthand. It was wonderful to meet staff and students and get a sense of the strong sense of community within the school. Below are several initiatives and highlights taking place at Ladysmith Primary: - ROARS! Assembly: celebrating students who are showing what it means to be Responsible, Open, Adaptable, Respectful, and Safe. - Great BC ShakeOut, a province-wide earthquake drill that helps us practice staying safe during an emergency. - Formative Assessment Conferences (1 hour early dismissal all week) - Conference Week: OCTOBER 20 – 23 - Volunteering- If you're interested to support/volunteer your child's classroom, you must fill out a Criminal Record Check (CRC) which is good for 5 years. Ladysmith Intermediate School: Last school year, I was fortunate to visit the school several times and appreciated the opportunity to see the staff and students in action. While I haven’t yet had the chance to visit in person this year, I’ve been in regular contact with Dionte through email. He has been sharing monthly newsletters, which have been a great way to stay informed about the many activities and accomplishments happening at the school. Below are a few recent highlights from the school: - Sept 29th Monday- Tour de Rock & Orange Shirt Day Parade - Tiger Pride Awards;- Its for hard work, positive attitude, and commitment to showing school spirit make our community stronger. - Honoring Truth and Reconciliation: Students Participate in Blanket Ceremony - Students took part in the Terry Fox run - Cops for Cancer – Tour de Rock at LIS! - Parent/Teacher conferences are scheduled for October 20th-23rd. Ladysmith Secondary School: Last school year, I had the opportunity to visit the school several times and appreciated the chance to connect with staff and students. While I haven’t yet made an in-person visit this year, I’ve been in regular communication with Shelly through email. She has been sharing monthly newsletters, which provide a helpful overview of the school’s current activities, achievements, and upcoming events. - Terry Fox Relay event - September 30th is the National Day for Truth and Reconciliation- wear orange shirts - AP exams in May of 2026. - Great Shakeout. This will be our first Earthquake Drill of the year. - Immunization consent packages were sent home. - LSS Grad 2025- It was great to attend.
