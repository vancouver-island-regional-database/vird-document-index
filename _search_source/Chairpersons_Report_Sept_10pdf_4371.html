CHAIRPERSON’S REPORT OPEN BOARD MEETING WEDNESDAY, SEPTEMBER 29, 2010 I would like to welcome back all SD#68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) staff for our 2010-2011 school year. I hope that you all had a great summer and were able to enjoy the sun, the rest and for some of you time to participate in Professional Development opportunities that were available in the summer. Thank you to all staff for your hard work to make school start-up go as smoothly as possible. This time of year is always a challenge addressing various issues such as Bill 33, scheduling, BCeSIS and student transfers just to name a few. Notwithstanding all the start-up challenges, there are many remarkable start-up initiatives that have been organized this year and over the past years. These initiatives help to create for students an environment of learning, caring and social responsibility and a sense of community within their schools. On behalf of all the Trustees, I would like to recognize that our Secretary-Treasurer David Green will be leaving us in the next month or so to take the Secretary-Treasurer’s position in Langley, BC. David has worked in this district for 8 years. David has done an outstanding job with the District’s finances. In all his years here we have had continuing Ministry cutbacks and he has worked hard to find the least invasive ways to address the harsh results of these cutbacks. We will miss you and wish you all the best in your future endeavors. As well, Gerald Fallon, Director of Instruction for Human Resources will be leaving the district in December to take up his new position as Assistant Professor in the Educational Studies Department in the field of Educational Leadership and Policy at UBC. This is an outstanding opportunity for Dr. Fallon. SD#68 has been well served by Gerald. He has observed open and constructive communication and worked hard to move the district forward in such areas at the Mid Contract Modification agreement. We will miss you. The Long-Term Facilities Plan has been completed and tabled for final input. I want to thank the members of the Facilities Planning Committee –Trustees and Staff, who worked long and hard to create guidelines and ultimately a facilities plan based on those guidelines in order to achieve a “made in Nanaimo” model for the communities in our district. Page 2 I also want to applaud the direction that the Ministry of Education has taken in their Neighbourhoods of Learning initiative and their recognition that our buildings and lands are valuable assets. SD#68 has, for many, many years, been involved in work to create community schools and look for ways for our City government, RDN and communities to be involved in joint-use. We are excited to work with the Ministry as we move forward on the Facilities Plan. Congratulations to the Ladysmith Secondary STAAR group who, this past week, won a Diversity Challenge Award in a competition held by the Central Vancouver Island Multicultural Society. The students have done outstanding work in raising awareness of and supporting diversity both in their school and community. Thank you to Mr. Guy Tohana, their teacher-sponsor who has worked tirelessly on their behalf. I would like to remember Mr. Tony Henig who served for a number of years as a Trustee in School District #68 and who died recently. Tony cared deeply for the children. He was a quiet, respectful gentleman who always put children first. Once again, to all the staff, welcome back and best wishes for a successful school year!
