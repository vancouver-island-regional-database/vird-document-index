Rutherford School is a Community Maureen Shakespeare Good Evening School District Staff, School Board Members, fellow Rutherford School Presenters and Parents and all other attendees: I am a parent with one child currently attending Rutherford School and along with my husband we have had 6 other children attending the school since 1984. We have had a long, positive history with the school. We are not prepared to accept the School District Report “Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update” without making our voices heard. As long as our family has been in attendance or involved with Rutherford school we have found it to be the central point of the Community, the Rutherford Community. Initially the school and community covered the areas which are now included in the McGirr, Randerson Ridge and Frank J. Ney school areas which have now become communities of their own. The reason, the developmental growth of the North End of Nanaimo. This growth has not stopped, nor does it appear to be close to stopping. At the present time it is occurring with developments in the Linley Valley, McRobb and Nelson areas. There is still green space in the larger area which include the above mentioned schools. Whether we want to admit it or not these developments are diverse mixtures of single people, families and retirees. Each development means growth, children, etc. The numbers will not be going down; they will go up. Before long a plan will have to be developed for opening or building another school. Why??? We are short sighted; the systems we use do not always reveal the real picture as the system is only as good as the developer, also often we see what we want to see. In the past, each time Rutherford came either close to or overcapacity the School District had to review its planning and a decision was made to develop a new school and change the catchment areas for the new school. It should be noted by all present that although the catchment areas were changed, Rutherford’s school catchment was not changed. As a result the number of students using the school would also become smaller. It was a heart wrenching process watching the school get smaller as well as the Rutherford Community. In some cases, long term “school” attendance with friends was disrupted and many tears fell. In each of the 1 following school years numerous people, including school staff, worked hard to keep the children and families involved and go through their grieving process. In the early 1990’s the School District & Board made the decision to designate some, not all school, as Community Schools. Rutherford was designated a Community School and we were proud of that. The title “Rutherford Community School “was taken to heart by the school but especially by the parents. We were and still are a Community. We have maintained that presence with pride – Not only is our School Open and Thriving but so is the Community. We have so many of our former students coming back to visit, support and be involved at the school after they have moved onwards. Many take great pride in coming back and talking to the teachers and students. This pride is very evident at our yearly Holiday concert during which former students are called on stage to participate with the current students in a song. It is a moment that students, parents, grandparents do not forget. It is a coming together of the community. The land for Rutherford School is now owned by the School District, however, it was donated to the district in order for a school to be built there. The Rutherford Family saw the area begin to grow and develop and wanted to ensure there was an elementary school close by. As the school was built the houses and community began to develop around it and then further developments expanded further outwards. In honour of the donation and the foresight of the family the School District named the school Rutherford. What an awesome legacy for the community. Now we are looking at closing it down for the stated reason it is getting outdated, older, needs work and the enrollment numbers are not large enough. How do you expect to keep the enrollments up when the catchment size has never changed since McGirr and the other schools opened? Over the years as the community has grown so has the school. It has established two wonderful, large playing fields that are used extensively, a forested play area for children, a fully enclosed kindergarten playground, a further two section playground for the older children. These playgrounds have been replaced and repaired by the School’s Parent Advisory Committee along with donations from the larger community. The field area is the place to be when it snows; many in the community go to the site to share in the fun of playing in the snow. People watch other people’s children if the parents cannot be there. 2 It is well known that neighbourhood public schools play an important role in our communities and the schools become the face of the community where they are situated. They are the gathering places for so many events and children love to point out when driving or walking by, “this is MY school”. They feel a sense of belonging and safety and this carries on over the years. For many this is where they made their first connections, often through the Strong Start Program. A further area of concern to the Rutherford Community is the proposed loss of the Skills for Life Program to another school which has to be remodelled to receive the program. Rutherford School was remodeled for this program many years ago and the parents and students have a strong presence in our community. This program is a very important and valued part of the school. All children and teachers in the school have benefited in so many ways from interacting with the children. They have learned valuable skills and understanding that they can use in all stages of their lives. Often times the children become Ambassadors for the children in the school, community as well as when some of them move on to the programs in the High Schools. The school and community have made sure to include all children and have them learn and accept each other for who they are. In the consultation meetings I attended this was a quality and opportunity that was going to be lost to students in the North End. Many of you here tonight were and are witness to the impact of this process of closing schools and the effect of this on the community, family and friends. Cedar Secondary School is a primary example of the impact of closure and then reversing the decision after the fact – confusion, frustration and anger. If this impacts the adults and older students think about how much this process is impacting younger children; the children who are attending Rutherford and those that are going to in another year or two. This school has history, 50 years’ worth, as well as attendance of generations of families, friendships and marriages, mine included. It has been mentioned time and again that our children will attain educational benefits if Rutherford closes. The only benefit that has been mentioned is that the children would be in a “newer” school which means better facilities. Many, if not most of us, are not concerned about how good the facilities look, we are concerned with a having a safe school, supportive, caring teachers and staff, 3 involved parents in the school and happy children that are connected to their school community. Rutherford was on the list to be updated but has now been taken off before a final decision is made. This is not acceptable. Information has been supplied that some of the proposed schools for Rutherford students to attend are full to capacity and the use of portables will be implemented. Not a good choice at all and creates numerous health issues for younger children. I went through this process with three of mine; it was a nightmare. It is well known that neighbourhood public schools play an important role in our communities and the schools become the face of the community where they are situated. They are the gathering places for so many events and children love to point out when driving or walking by, “this is MY school”. They feel a sense of belonging and safety and this carries on over the years. For many this is where they made their first connections, often through the Strong Start Program. In closing I would like to encourage each of you to think about the information that has been presented tonight. Is it a wise choice to be seeking to close a vibrant, open and thriving community school? Is it a wise choice when the schools you are proposing to place the children into are nearly or at capacity? It is a wise choice to have to increase the size of the other schools by using portables or seeking monies to add on to one of the schools? Is it a wise choice to make young children walk a long distance to school, although within this School Districts’ policy? Their safety and security is not only the parents’ responsibility but the school districts as well. Is it a wise choice to have them walk along a busy Hammond Bay Road for a long distance? Is it a wise choice to walk along the back roads to a school, where there has been significant sightings of cougars and bears? What about the increased potential for pedophile activities? They too reside in our communities! Is it a wise choice to have younger children attend larger schools? What is the benefit? Look around you to see the problems it is causing in other districts. Bigger is NOT better! Is it a wise choice to create a less safe environment for our younger children to grow and develop in? It is a wise choice to create increased spaces for children to be bullied? Our School District stands for “Success for All” “Learning Together”. Let us model this and keep Rutherford School and Community Open and Thriving. Thank you! 4
