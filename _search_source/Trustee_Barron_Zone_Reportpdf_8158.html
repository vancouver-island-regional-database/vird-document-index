TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron Name of School(s) Visited:  Gabriola Island Elementary  École North Oyster Elementary School (ENO)  Barsby Secondary School Highlights of Visit – School Name  Gabriola Elementary: I had the privilege of participating as a guest judge in a spaghetti bridge building contest. The student’s built bridges using minimal materials including spaghetti, glue and colour. The bridges were then tested and awarded in areas including holding the most weight, most colourful and best design. The students were visibly excited about the activity which involved the student’s expressing themselves creatively, working collaboratively in groups, and integrating multidisciplinary subjects including science and math.  École North Oyster Elementary School (ENO): I had a great visit at ENO. I had a tour of the new playground which is near completion thanks to the great work through the PAC, Playspace Committee, BCAA, volunteers and community support. Looking at the equipment, it is evident that the playground equipment involved much planning and organizing. The students are thrilled by their new, well deserved playground.  Barsby Secondary School: Visiting Barsby the same day of our board meeting. Events/ Meetings  Safe Schools Committee: Updates through the Safe Schools Committee include ongoing work around the Administrative Procedure (AP) 347, Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity. Areas currently being reviewed and recommended for update in AP347 include the sections that speak to support for students and/or staff that are Transitioning at school/work, Gender and Sexual Alliances (GSA) in schools and Class Placements. The Committee would also like to invite all board members to the Pride Parade on June 6th 2019. More details around the parade will be sent out closer to the day. Finally, the Committee would also like to share the following short video with the board: “Why I’m not Racist is only Half the Story”.  VISTA: Attended the 2 day Vancouver Island School Trustees Association (VISTA) Annual General Meeting which took place in Courtney, BC. At this meeting, we had the opportunity to see and learn more about unique educational programming happening in SD71 including creative blended learning programs as well as a tour of the U’mista Cultural Society’s Residential School exhibit where we were provided with the opportunity to learn more about the impact of residential schools on Indigenous students, their families and communities. Trustee Brzovic is the current Second Vice President of VISTA and will be running for the position of First Vice President in the upcoming election taking place at the VISTA meeting this coming week. Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron – Trustee Report  BCSTA Regional Workshop: In this two day workshop, we had an opportunity for us as a board to “dig a little deeper” around best practices for board policies, strategic planning, financial oversight and communications. Trustees and staff from other districts were also present, offering an opportunity for districts to share information and learning.  Strategic Planning working sessions: The board is nearing the end of the work around the development of our Strategic Plan. In our most recent meeting, we discussed the board’s goals and objectives within the Strategic Plan.  Youth Mural Project: Attended the unveiling of a mural commemorating Makayla Chang on March 17th 2019. The mural in Makayla’s honour can be found at Harewood Centennial Park. Makayla was last seen alive in March 2017. The mural is a beautiful piece of art to commemorate and honour Makayla’s short life while also giving voice to other marginalized youth. Makayla was 16 when she was murdered and the case currently remains unsolved.  Civic Governance Forum: Attended the Columbia Institute’s 2 day annual Civic Governance forum. The forum was full of learning, networking and information sharing. We had an opportunity to participate in a meeting for women in leadership which included discussions including providing space for diverse voices and barriers experienced by women to enter and remain in politics. The Minister of Municipal Affairs and Housing Selina Robinson presented around some of the broader challenges families are experiencing in accessing affordable housing. I attended sessions which including topics such as the high child poverty rate in BC and how this high rate impacts our public schools, inclusive classrooms and also ways in which food security and learning go hand-in-hand. The Minister of Social Development and Poverty Reduction Shane Simpson, the Minister of State of Child Care Katrina Chen and the Minister of Agriculture Lana Popham also presented.  Nanaimo Business Awards: Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools was nominated and voted as a finalist in the category of most inclusive workplace at the Nanaimo Business Awards which I attended on April 11th 2019. Congratulations to Coco’s Café, the welldeserved winner.  Met with Nanaimo’s MLA Sheila Malcolmson: Shiela Malcolmson reached out to discuss student priorities in the region. Ms. Malcolmson is looking forward to supporting and working with Nanaimo Ladysmith School board in her recently elected position as MLA for Nanaimo.  Budget Development Sessions: Attended the invite only budget session during the day and the evening budget open house on April 16th 2019. I had an opportunity to hear from students, community members, local elected officials and staff around what they feel are most important for us as a board to consider in our upcoming budget. Possible areas of support from the Board for future planning/budgeting  École North Oyster Elementary School (ENO) holds a Social Justice League. This group is peer lead with teachers who volunteer their lunch hours to allow the group to meet at least once a week. This group allows a safe space for students, grades 5-7, to meet, discuss, and participate in activities around gender identity and sexual orientation. This group is in its second year and has been consistently well attended by students. Our Gender and Sexuality Alliances at both the secondary and elementary level directly aligns with our Board’s Inclusion Policy 2.10 and should be considered as a board in future district budgeting and planning. Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron – Trustee Report
