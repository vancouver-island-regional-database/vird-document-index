February 15, 2017 Ref: 192742 John Blain Superintendent of Schools School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) Email: jblain@sd68.bc.ca Dear Mr. Blain: On behalf of the Aboriginal Education team at the Ministry of Education, I would like to invite you and your school district to participate in the field trials of the Equity Scan Project. In response to the Report of the Office of the Auditor General and its recommendation for the Ministry to work collaboratively with school districts on a variety of strategies, the Aboriginal Education team has embarked on the construction of an equity-scanning tool. This tool will assist in identifying the strengths and barriers affecting Aboriginal student achievement. Currently, the collaborative phase and consultations across the ‘field’ for this work have been undertaken, and a framework has been drafted to guide the consultations and vision for a systemic scanning toolkit for six participating school districts. The Equity Scan framework will provide a focus at the school district level and is founded on student achievement using four pillars: Policy and Governance, Learning Profile, Learning Environment and the Pedagogical Core. Participating districts will work with the Ministry through Aboriginal Education Field Liaisons, Kaleb Child and Dr. Scott Benwell, during the 2016/17 and 2017/18 school years to inform the development and co-construction of the scanning tool. It is expected, participating districts will have two lead representatives to join Mr. Child and Dr. Benwell for a collaborative session of pilot districts to be held in Richmond on Thursday, March 9, 2017. The training will be followed by in-district visits during the remainder of the 2016/17 school year and during the first half of the 2017/18 school year.
