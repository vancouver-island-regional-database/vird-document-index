Page 6 of 37 7.1 3137 Barons Road Nanaimo, BC V9T 5W5 p: f: e: w: 250 756-1237 250 756-0188 ndta@shaw.ca www.ndta68.ca March 7, 2018 Pursuant to Article F.20 Professional Development of the Collective Agreement, the Association has prepared the following report to the Board of School Trustees for the period July 01, 2017 to January 31, 2018. BUDGET: Expected revenue for 2017 – 2018 is approximately $315,000. Of those funds the greatest share, $280,000, is budgeted to address the individual professional development needs of the district’s approximately 1000 teachers and teachers teaching on call. These funds are reimbursed to teachers accordance to Policy and the Principles of Professional Development. A Statement of Disbursements dated January 31, 2018 is included with this report. The middle column reflects revenue and expenditures to January 31. Individual Teachers’ Professional Development Funds: $280,000 2017-18 is the final year of the three-year cycle for individual teacher’s funds. The NDTA believes that this model, in effect now for nine years now, has made the distribution of professional development funds more equitable and accessible to more teachers. It also allows teachers to plan ahead and attend more expensive opportunities that would not be possible with only a single year’s allocation. This year, teachers are able to access all unused funds from the previous years in the cycle. This amounts to $1000 over the three years: $300 in the first two and $400 this year (pro-rated to FTE). TTOCs and Part Time Teachers have access to $200 in individual funds that are not on a three-year cycle. To encourage attendance at PSA Superconference, this year’s individual amount was increased from $300 to $400 for full time teachers and increased from $100 to $200 for TTOCs. As such, the majority of individual professional development funds went toward supporting members to attend this conference, however funds were also used to attend a variety of other conferences, and to purchased professional resources. October 20, 2017 Professional Specialist Association Day (PSA) – Superconference! Within the BCTF, there are 33 Provincial Specialist Associations that provide channels for members to share common interests and support one another within those contexts. In previous years these PSAs would hold conferences across the province on the third Friday of October. This year, 25 of the PSAs coordinated to put on the PSA Superconference over two days at the Vancouver Convention Centre hosting well over 200 sessions for more than 10 000 delegates. The teachers from Nanaimo who attended as delegates and presenters reported a very positive experience! For a variety of reasons, we recognize that it is not always possible for our members to travel out of district for these fabulous events. To accommodate those teachers staying in Nanaimo, we provided Wellington Secondary as a place to meet and collaborate on Professional Development that was relevant to local topics of interest. Page 7 of 37 7.1 March 7, 2018 Page |2 Capacity Building Grants: $25,000 Formerly an opportunity to send local teachers to international conferences, this budget line is now used to support teachers in attending a variety of quality professional development opportunities across Canada and abroad. To date, seven applications have been approved and approximately $11,000 allocated. 2017-18 Capacity Building Grants: Engineering for Science Classroom Institute (San Francisco) Summit 20 Conference (Vancouver) Brief Focussed Counselling Skills Workshop (Edmonton) BC Schools Focussed Mental Health Conference (Langley) Dalai Lama Centre for Peace+Education Heart-Mind Conference (Burnaby) Global Education (Ecuador) It is notable to observe that the focus of all of these opportunities is on social, emotional and experiential learning. None are on traditional skills and processes. Local Facilitators’ Account: $20,000 When local teachers facilitate workshops and presentations in district they are granted a sum in their local facilitator’s account to cover the cost of a TTOC so that they can make up the missed opportunity for their own professional development. District Day: $45,000 This year’s District Day theme was Innovative Education: A Focus on Transforming Practices and Minds. More than 30 facilitators offered over 50 sessions on a wide variety of topics: Nutrition, technology, assessment, and First People’s Understandings. Brian Hamm’s Keynote challenged the audience to live curiously and go beyond, while Sylvia Duckworth’s keynote debunked the talent myth. A full list of the facilitators and their sessions is attached below. Supplemental Release: $45,000 Subject to available funds, the Professional Development Policy allows all teachers access to one TTOC released day in order to attend professional development activities on days they have regularly scheduled duties. This is frequently used in conjunction with individual funds to attend workshops and conferences in the lower mainland and Whistler. Professional Learning Groups (PLGs): $34,000 This funding is to assist colleagues with their needs to collaborate regarding pedagogy, philosophy, or grade and subject professional development initiatives. It is broadly accessed by teachers with similar interests and needs from various worksites, and some teachers participate in multiple groups. Each partnership is expected to create an action plan for approval, and submit a short summary report of their work in June. To date there have been 19 PLGs approved that include 156 participants.
