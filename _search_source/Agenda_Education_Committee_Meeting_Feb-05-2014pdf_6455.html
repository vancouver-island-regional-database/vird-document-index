LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT John Blain, Deputy Superintendent, introduced, Laura Tait, District Principal of Aboriginal Education. Ms. Tait spoke about the presentations that would be happening this evening and said they highlighted many of the initiatives underway in our schools supporting Aboriginal Education. Ms. Tait noted that the Aboriginal graduation rate has increased by six percent last year – from 49.9% in 2011-12 to 56.2% in 2012-13. While the improvement is to be celebrated, there is still much work to be done. • Pleasant Valley Elementary Principal Kathy Bergman described the work that has been done at that school over the past four years to integrate Aboriginal learning into the school culture. Elder Jerry Brown was on hand with students displaying their skill with traditional drumming. • The Bayview Shawl Dancers proudly displayed their ceremonial shawls, which the students created for themselves and demonstrated traditional dances. The group has a 10-year Page 4 of 7 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held December 4, 2013 history at the school. • Principal David Birnie described the program at Coal Tyee and showed video of Aboriginal Education Assistant Linda Walls and Teacher Aimee Blow working with Kindergarten students on traditional singing. • Students from John Barsby demonstrated their traditional drumming and singing. The group, led by Aboriginal Education Support Teacher Mandy Jones is much in demand for school and community events. • Students Kianna Watts and Malcolm Beaton described the way that Aboriginal understanding are woven into Teacher Lynn Brown’s Grade 11 English class, and the powerful impact that has had on them personally. Mr. Blain thanked everyone for participating in tonight's meeting.
