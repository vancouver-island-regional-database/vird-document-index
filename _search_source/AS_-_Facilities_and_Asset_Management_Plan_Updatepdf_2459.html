One of the plan's objectives is to ensure there is adequate population in each school to offer as wide a course selection as possible. Dr. Player's recommended plan is a 10-year plan divided into three stages: immediate actions to be undertaken in years one and two; medium term actions to be undertaken in years three through five, and long term which includes recommendations for years six through ten. Dr. Player's plan recommends that the Board take the following actions: 0 Consolidate ten facilities; Rebuild four facilities; Rethink and repurpose the DRC/LRC; Institute three new enrichment programs; Relocate and renew the approach to the alternate programs; Adjust the immersion program; and Relocate the administrative functions. Dr. Player also stressed that it is important that the Board focus on its core business, which means disposing of a number of properties so that the money can be added to the district's capital account, allowing for upgrading and maintaining buildings that are not directly addressed in his recommendations. in summary, Dr. Player said that his recommended plan would: 0 Give the students the facilities they deserve and the program choice they need; 0 Raise the achievement expectations throughout the district while giving students more challenging learning opportunities; 0 Give teachers better working conditions with fewer split classes, decreased preparations and more resources; 0 Address the facilities at the low end of the facility condition index ensuring better use of maintenance funds and better situations for custodians; 0 As a byproduct, improves the operating budget and provides additional funding for capital costs. In short, he said, the recommended plan provides for renewal throughout the district in a manner that would enhance learning for all students.
