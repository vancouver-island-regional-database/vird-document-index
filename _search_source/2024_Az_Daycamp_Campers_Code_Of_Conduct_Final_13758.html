CAMPER'S CODE OF CONDUCT: The Campers Code of Conduct is to ensure Campers understand the procedures at Ladysmith Parks Recreation & Culture Adventure Zone Daycamp to keep themselves, other campers, and leaders safe to ensure a positive experience at camp! Please read this list with your child to ensure they understand each point. Once you have done so, please have both you and your child sign and return. In order to keep a positive and safe environment at Adventure Zone Daycamp we ask that you understand the following: • For your safety, follow the instructions of the Leaders. • Listen carefully when the Leaders are talking - to be respectful, one person talks at a time. When playing games, listen carefully to the directions. • Ask questions and speak to your Leader if you need something or do not understand the instructions. • Respect others’ personal belongings by only touching your belongings, and not others, including backpacks, lunch kits, water bottles, first aid kits, leader’s fanny packs, daycamp trolly, leader’s radios, the speaker, etc. • Leave personal toys and electronics at home. We want campers to experience camp and engage directly with each other. Adventure Zone Daycamp is also not responsible for lost personal toys and electronics. • Keep personal things neat and in a designated space. • Practice proper hand hygiene – to help keep campers and leaders healthy, and to help prevent the spread of infectious diseases to others. • Practice safe cough/sneeze etiquette by doing both in our elbows or tissue and washing our hands directly afterwards. • In addition to the Recreation Facilities Code of Conduct, there is a zero tolerance for bullying at camp. This may include but is not limited to name-calling, signaling out, hitting, shoving, and aggressive behavior. Be kind to one another by being inclusive and respecting each other. • Keep to the ground - there are no climbing trees, shelters, goalposts, tables, chairs, etc. • Stay within the boundaries - the boundaries are established by the leaders (in general we stay where we can see and hear the Leaders). • Use a buddy system when going to the washroom. Find a buddy, tell a leader where you are going, and tell the same Leader when you are back. Both campers are required to wash their hands after using the bathroom. • Only eat your lunch packed by your parent(s)/guardian(s) - no sharing food (siblings included), and no food trucks. • Behaviour is encouraged by the Go For Green method. o Green = GREAT o Yellow = YIKES (3 yikes means the leaders will call parents for pick-up). o Red = STOP (may be sent home). o So we Go for Green! • HAVE FUN!
