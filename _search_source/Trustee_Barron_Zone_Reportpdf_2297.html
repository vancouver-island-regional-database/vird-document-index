TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron Name of School(s) Visited:  Dover Bay Secondary  Frank Ney Elementary Highlights of Visit – School Name  Dover Bay Secondary: Visited the Rainbow Alliance at Dover Bay Secondary. The group meets twice a week in a welcoming, safe space where LGBTQ2+ students and allies have opportunities to build relationships and connections and may participate in items including projects that promote positive change. The highlight was sitting with the students during their “check in”. One student shared that attending the group was the highlight of her week. It was inspiring to see the support provided through this group and the positive impact on the school community through an emphasis on health, wellness and feelings of engagement and connectivity amongst students.  Frank Ney: visiting Frank Ney same day as Regular Board meeting on February 27th 2019. Events/ Meetings  CUPE Meet and Greet: Attended Open House and had the opportunity to connect with CUPE Executive.  Syeyutsus Celebration: Ladysmith Secondary hosted a lunch, in celebration of the Syeyutsus Framework approved by the Board of Education. We shared a delicious meal with 3 nations, Indigenous community partners, students, staff and stakeholders. We were also honoured with beautiful drumming, singing, dancing and stories.  Strategic Planning Working Session: Our board continues to do the important work around strategic planning.  Strategic Planning Open House: Had the opportunity to hear from the community thoughts important for us to consider through the Strategic Planning process. I had the opportunity to hear feedback and discuss important areas for moving forward with a strategic plan that guides our vision for the future of our district.  City Council and School Board Meeting: City councillors, School Board trustees and Staff shared a meal, information and brainstormed ways in which we can collaborate moving forward. Through this ongoing networking and information sharing, more opportunities to better serve our community may be presented.  Joint Pro D Committee Meeting: Attended meeting to discuss the upcoming school year’s calendar. Draft to be presented to the board on March 27th after a 30 day consultation period. Input can be emailed to the board to consider. Possible areas of support from the Board for future planning/budgeting  Dover Bay Rainbow Alliance: Gay/Straight Alliances in our secondary schools directly supports our current district’s mission surrounding supporting our student’s to become “healthy, confident, life-long learners and socially conscious citizens”. Ensuring our LGBTQ2+ students feel safe and welcomed in all our district’s school communities benefits all students. As we move forward, I would like to invite all trustees to visit, or continue visiting, our district’s Gay/Straight Alliances to ensure the board can best support the work being done through our district to ensure safe, inclusive schools.
