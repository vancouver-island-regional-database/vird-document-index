January  31,  2017     School  District  68  Board  of  Trustees     Re:  Rutherford  school  closure         In  a  recent  release  from  the  district  super  intendant  it  was  stated  that   Rutherford  Elementary  would  be  closing  at  the  end  of  the  2017/2018  school   year.    I  attended  a  Rutherford  PAC  meeting  in  December  where  the  super   intendant  was  invited  to  speak  at.    During  the  open  forum  style  meeting  many   questions  and  matter  of  opinions  were  brought  up  and  conversed  upon.    One  of   those  was  if  any  consideration  has  gone  into  the  new  seismic  code.    I  am  not   certain,  however,  I  believe  you  cannot  add  an  addition  to  a  structure  of  this   percentage  and  not  upgrade  the  rest  of  the  building  to  current  codes.    Since  the   code  has  recently  changed  one  would  assume  the  whole  building  would  need  to   be  upgraded.     The  response  given  by  the  super  intendant  was  that  this  funding  is  just  a   stepping-­‐stone  in  this  project.    Once  the  funding  was  secured,  plans  would  be   drawn  up  and  it  would  be  put  to  tender,  and  that  at  any  point  the  project  could   be  stopped.    Given  there  was  no  dollar  amount  given  in  the  bylaw,  I  am  curious  of   the  quick  statement  that  the  school  will  close  with  one  quarter  of  the  proposed   funding  missing  instead  of  stating  the  process  will  be  moving  forward.    It  seems   there  is  no  amount  of  money  that  will  stop  this  project  from  happening.    Is  this   because  SD  68  outright  owns  the  Rutherford  lot  and  would  receive  100%  of  the   land  value  if  sold?     Another  point  that  was  brought  up  during  the  PAC  meeting  that  did  not  sit   well  with  me  is  the  super  intendant  stated  the  expansion  would  leave  57  open   seats  as  of  enrolment  numbers  today.    He  stated  empathy  for  the  198  students   that  would  have  to  move.    The  problem  was  after  he  left  the  meeting  the   principal  gave  his  report  and  stated  220  students  currently  attended  Rutherford.     I  requested  the  numbers  from  the  school  district  and  then  phoned  the  schools   individually  to  compare  the  enrolment  numbers  and  these  are  the  results.     Enrolment  from   School   District   Enrolment  from  schools   Difference   Randerson  Ridge   328   329   1   Mcgirr   401   418   17   Rutherford   198   220   22   Frank  J.  Ney   232   244   12       If  the  super  intendant  is  basing  the  57  open  seats  off  of  his  numbers  he   will  be  incorrect.    That  number  should  be  23  open  seats  based  on  enrolment   today.    One  of  my  concerns  is  the  4.5  acres  across  the  street  from  Frank  J.  Ney  for   sale.    I  would  wager  that  would  be  split  into  7,000  square  foot  lots  in  no  time,   close  to  25  of  them  after  adding  a  road.    Not  to  mention  the  proposed  sale  of  a   portion  of  the  green  thumb  nursery.    I  honestly  feel  this  a  Band-­‐Aid  solution  and   in  5-­‐10  years  Rutherford  will  be  needed,  if  the  school  is  still  there.    Rutherford  is   an  old  school  that  needs  work  that  is  not  in  the  budget.    However  with  25%  of   the  purposed  budget  missing  to  add  on  to  Frank  J  Ney  it  was  published  publicly   that  Rutherford  would  close.    It  is  my  understanding  this  has  not  been  put  to   tender  yet,  no  timeline  has  been  put  forth  and  the  assumption  that  a  project  of   this  size  can  be  done  in  a  year  and  a  half  without  disrupting  classroom   operations  may  be  challenging.    The  super  intendant  stated  that  he  just  follows   the  advise  of  the  trustees,  so  I  am  asking  who  decided  to  make  the  closure  of   Rutherford  final?    I  was  given  the  functional  capacity  of  the  four  schools  when  I   received  the  enrolment  numbers  from  the  district.    Other  than  Rutherford,  the   only  school  that  is  below  its  functional  capacity  is  Randerson  Ridge.    One  would   assume  this  is  good  since  Linley  Valley  is  nowhere  near  completion  of  building   their  proposed  subdivision.    McGirr  is  at  115%  of  its  functional  capacity  and  I   was  told  a  child  has  a  better  chance  of  winning  the  French  emersion  lottery  at   Hammond  Bay  than  enrolling  as  an  out  of  catchment  for  Kindergarten  next  year.     So  I  can  only  assume  after  Frank  Ney  is  finished  there  will  be  23  available   elementary  seats  for  all  of  North  Nanaimo,  as  of  numbers  today.     In  closing,  thank  you  for  your  time  and  consideration  on  this  subject.    I   understand  it  is  a  tough  issue,  however  I  hope  that  when  you  chose  to  run  for   trustee,  you  understood  that  when  you  raise  your  hand  to  spend  millions  of   dollars  it  should  not  be  to  balance  a  one  or  two  year  budget.    I  hope  it  would  be   for  the  better  of  child  education  and  long-­‐term  stability.    If  recent  events  have   shown  us  anything  it  is  the  provincial  government  doesn’t  seem  to  have  an  issue   supporting  growing  communities,  i.e.  Surrey,  Langford,  North  Nanaimo.    I  feel  we   should  look  at  projected  developments  and  population  growth  done  by   independent  experts  that  specialize  in  their  fields.    The  super  intendant   repeatedly  stated  that  he  is  directed  by  the  board  of  trustees  and  implements  its   directives.    Direct  him.     Thank  you  for  your  time.     Yours  sincerely,     Eric  Ostergaard   5971  Cody  Place,   Nanaimo,  B.C.  V9V  1J7   home  –  250-­‐729-­‐6101   cell  –  250-­‐514-­‐5668   eostergaard31@gmail.com
