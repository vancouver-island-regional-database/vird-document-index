NLPS Late French Immersion (LFI) Program Review Introduction This review, completed in the spring of 2025, is an exploration of the Late French Immersion (LFI) Program in NLPS. The purpose of the review is to examine retention, equity, student outcomes and long-term sustainability of the program. All program reviews focus on alignment of the program with the NLPS commitment to inclusive practice and programming, and the focus on student needs, and district priorities. This review, conducted in spring 2025 by an internal team, employed an appreciative inquiry approach as a strengthsbased exploration. Its purpose is to inform future decisions on how to provide high-quality, equitable learning opportunities across all programs, with a particular focus on LFI. Context Late French Immersion has been offered in NLPS since 2014, beginning with a Grade 6 cohort of 23 students at Quarterway Elementary School. The introduction of LFI was based on recommendations from a 2011–2012 external review of the entire French Immersion program. Retention rates remain low across key transition points, and participation in the program is not equitably reflective of the district’s broader student population. Questions about long-term sustainability, alignment with district priorities, and equitable access must be addressed to ensure all learners have meaningful opportunities to engage with second-language learning pathways. Upon analysis and discussion of all trends, including the recent increased enrolment in Gr. 6 for the 2025-26 year, and in consultation with the Ministry of Education and Child Care French Immersion Policy, the review team makes the following recommendations: • • • • • • • • • • Continue to review long-term viability given consistently low Grade 12 retention (30%) Evaluate whether the original rationale for establishing Late FI remains relevant in the current district context Evaluate the high cost of maintaining a small, low-enrolment program Review demographic trends, noting limited representation from Priority Populations and consider how their enrolment in Late FI could be enhanced Align future program structures with equity, diversity, and inclusion commitments and Ministry equity goals Increase French language use in classrooms and strengthen oral/written supports Complete common formative assessments for all grades in literacy/numeracy Strengthen supports at key transition years where attrition is highest Strengthen and coordinate promotional efforts across schools, PACs, and district channels, ensuring clear messaging and rationale for Late FI, supported by tools such as infographics and handouts Analyze staffing, space, and financial resources currently dedicated to Late FI September 2025
