Electronic meetings are permitted under Ministerial Order No. M192. The Town of Ladysmith is using Zoom Webinars to conduct our meetings. Please read on to find out how you can “virtually” attend meetings and ask a question live during Regular Council Meetings. How to register A registration link will be included on each agenda cover page which, once you clicked on will direct you to a very short registration form. After you fill out the form, you will immediately receive a confirmation email with your own unique link to join the meeting. You’ll also receive two reminders of the meeting: on the day before the meeting, as well as one hour before the meeting starts. By “virtually” attending, it will feel as if you are sitting in the Council Chamber watching the meeting. There is an option to ask questions live during Question Period as well – your video will remain off, but you will be able to ask questions. You’ll receive all the information you need in your confirmation and reminder emails. Please note that only Council meetings include Question Period (CoW meetings do not). You can join the meeting using either your computer/ tablet or your telephone. We recommend downloading the free Zoom App from the Google Play or Apple App Store for your smartphone or tablet. It’s still a good idea to be prepared to deal with snow on our streets. In the event of a snowfall, the first priority for the plows is to clear access to public buildings such as the Community Health Centre, RCMP detachment, Ambulance Station and Fire/Rescue Station. We then clear and maintain all main roads on a 24 hour basis as the snow continues to fall. Our primary concern is keeping the main roadways, First Avenue, Fourth Avenue, Dogwood Drive, Davis Road and Chemainus Road open to traffic. Weather permitting, we then clear snow from the main hills throughout the Town, focusing on widening the roads we have already cleared to make room for traffic. Local neighbourhood roads will be cleared after the main thoroughfares (listed above) are completed. Through roads will be cleared first, and then cul-de-sacs. Did you know it can take about 48 hours to clear all 60 kilometres of roads in Ladysmith? Remember that it is your responsibility to shovel the sidewalks bordering your property by 10:00 am after a snowfall. This helps to ensure the safety of your neighbours and other pedestrians. Our community continues to grow and welcome new residents. Developer Lamont Land LP is starting construction of a bridge crossing at Colonia Drive in order to access a land parcel it owns within the Holland Creek Local Area Plan (HCLAP). The HCLAP, which forms part of the Town’s Official Community Plan, outlines planning, design principles, land use, transportation and servicing policies for this 360 acre area, more than a third of which is preserved as trail and parkland. The traffic assessment for this new neighbourhood concluded that the area should be served by three accesses – Dogwood Drive, Colonia Drive and Thetis Drive. The bridge crossing at Colonia Drive will eventually form part of a future collector road through this predominantly residential neighbourhood near the However, we do understand and can appreciate that community members will have additional questions regarding this project. The developer’s proposed crossing design includes a two-lane road with a multiuse pathway on one side for pedestrians and cyclists. Two pedestrian tunnels will pass underneath the roadway and connect with the existing trail system. The Town will keep you informed of the progress of the bridge crossing, as well as notify you of any temporary closures of the Holland Creek Trail during construction, through the town’s webpage. For the latest information about this project, including a list of Frequently Asked Questions, visit: www. ladysmith.ca/hollandcreekcrossing. BUSINESS LICENCE RENEWALS DUE BY FEBRUARY 28 The Town of Ladysmith offers inter-municipal business licences with Duncan, North Cowichan and Lake Cowichan. Your Ladysmith business licence is valid when you offer mobile business services within these three other communities. The cost of an annual business licence is $100. Renew by February 28, 2021 to avoid a $25 late payment fee. If you are a new business in Ladysmith and apply after July 1st, your cost will be prorated to $50 for the balance of the calendar year. Ladysmith business owners who carry out work in additional Central Vancouver Island municipalities can purchase an Inter-Community Business Licence for an additional $170.00. Following are the participating municipalities: Campbell River, Parksville, Qualicum Beach, Courtenay, Port Alberni, Cumberland, Duncan, Lake Cowichan and Nanaimo. WATER MAIN FLUSHING PROGRAM Regular water main flushing is an important part of the Town’s comprehensive water management program. Each year, between January and April, Public Works flushes both water supply mains and distribution water mains. Potable water is flushed at a high velocity through the water mains to ensure all piping is refreshed and minor sediment within the pipes is removed. This is one of the methods relied upon by the Town to help keep our drinking water safe and clean. During the flushing period, water users may experience some discolouration of the water supply. Running a cold tap for a short period will help restore water quality back to normal. If you or someone you know depends on medical equipment that uses water, please call Public Works at 250-245-6445 for flushing dates. Yearly water main flushing is also a reminder to clean the screens on your faucets as well as the pressure reducing valve (where water enters the house). This should be done on an annual basis.
