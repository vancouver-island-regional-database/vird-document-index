Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Addition Timeline of events: 1995-1999 • The district received planning support for an addition to Hammond Bay. • Before the addition project was approved for construction, the ministry (in response to a ‘request’ from the community) funded an emergent project for $1.49M to seismically upgrade the school and to provide building improvements with the remainder. This was called Phase 1 • Phase 1, completed in 1999 consisted of new space, alterations, reorganizations and shelled in areas in conjunction with the seismic upgrades. • 2 ‘shelled’ in rooms were subsequently completed out of AFG. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Arial View: Phase 2 (Main floor) was to include: • The demolition of the old washrooms • A new library/computer room in the location of the old washroom • The finishing of the roughed in washrooms to replace the those demolished Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Main Floor Plan Phase 2 (Lower floor) was to include: • Connection between the gym and main building • A gym expansion • Change room facilities • A purpose build MP room w/kitchen Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Lower Floor Plan 1999-2010…….. • The phase 2 scope, gym expansion in particular, remains a district priority and continues to be included in the 5 year capital planning process and ministry submissions. • March 25, 2009 – board passed a motion directing S/T to prepare a report on options for financing full gym at Hammond Bay. (Folio R09/03/25-29) • June 24, 2009 – report presented and staff recommend use of restricted capital, other options considered: – 5 yr. capital plan process – Local capital funds – Borrowing authority – Disposal of property – Redirect other ministry capital Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion 1998-2010…….. • September 29, 2009 – District sent request to Minster of Education requesting use of Ministry Restricted Capital to Fund Hammond Bay Gym Expansion. • February 9, 2010 – Response from Minister of Education stating it is not prepared to approve spending of restricted capital for this purpose until the Board has approved and submitted it’s Long Range Facility Plan. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion 2011-2012……….. • July 13, 2011 – board passed a motion requesting staff to prepare a plan for the construction of a new gymnasium at Hammond Bay and further to that it apply to Ministry for financing for this capital project and apply local capital as available and prudent. (Folio R11/07/13-20) • November 23, 2011 – staff developed and present 2 options (w/ costing) based on 1995 phase 2 plans; – Option 1 – Gym expansion ($800,000.00) – Option 2 – Gym expansion including change rooms and a multipurpose room ($1,600,000.00) Staff recommend that the Board refer the matter of the Hammond Bay Gym Addition to the Strategic Planning process which incorporates the Asset Management Plan and the District’s Strategic Educational Plan process. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion 2013-2015………. • March 19, 2014 – District staff proceed to re-visit gym addition costing for the gym addition (options 1 and 2) as per scope/plans provided Nov. 23, 2011 to present to joint committee comprised of City of Nanaimo and District 68 staff. • June 10, 2015 – Updated Facilities Plan (2015-2021) Years 1 and 2 (2015/2016): calls for staff to work with the City of Nanaimo to begin planning for the Ecole Hammond Bay gym. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion 2016………. • September 30, 2016 - Ecole Hammond Bay projects included in 5 year capital plan submission, in district priority: – EXP #2 - Hammond Bay Gym Addition – EXP #3 - Hammond Bay (80K/200 TO 80K/300) – SEP #4 – Hammond Bay HVAC Upgrade (Synergistic project w/ New Heat Exchange) – CNCP #1 – New Heat Exchange Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Next steps……. • As a next step, staff will revisit the gym addition design (to bring to current ‘PDR stage’ requirements) in order to ensure: • Design meets current building and local regulation • Geotechnical is reviewed • Update Hazmat reports • Structural/Seismic review • Update costing • Prior to beginning the gym PDR, staff will re-visit Phase 2 (master planning/feasibility) to ensure that assumptions made 20 years ago are still valid and that all of the current planning requirements are considered. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Next steps……. • The key purpose of the master plan will be revisit of the building configuration and the outcome will inform the district with respect to; – Future capacity requirements – Functionality – Class room requirements, Multi-purpose room and gym locations – Site elements such as safety, parking, play space, etc. Ecole Hammond Bay Gymnasium Expansion Communication……. • Re-engaging the Architect (KMBR) and moving ahead w/ some planning and design work could create a perception that project is approved to completion, some communication may be required.
