May 26, 2020 Dear Trustees’, I am writing this letter in support of obtaining the South Wellington School property, 1536 Morden Rd. These types of opportunities come but once in a lifetime. The Community of South Wellington has shown great interest in obtaining this property since the closure of the School in 2013. The School provided a gathering place for all members of the Community as well as the students and their families. All events held, Christmas Concerts, Fun Fairs, Fundraisers, Breakfast Program, Coal Miner Days, these events were for all of the Community. When the School was closed, all of the connections to various events were lost. In the 1900’s, James Beck donated two properties in South Wellington for churches and three properties for schools. Upon the death of the last surviving Trustee of the Beck Estate, the South Wellington School property was donated to School District 68 for a transfer of One Dollar. The District also acquired the other two school properties the same way. Unfortunately, the other two properties have been sold. The District has applied to the Government for funding to demolish the building since it has no further use for a School in this area. Since the building is not being used as an educational facility, it does not require seismic upgrade for recreational use. This is a prefect opportunity for the School to be used as a Community Centre/Hub. South Wellington and Cassidy could greatly benefit from these sorely needed amenities. Cedar received support from the RDN and the District in saving and using the old three-room school, which is used by the Community of Cedar. The North Oyster School was moved across the road onto an acre of donated land to become a Community Centre. The Cowichan Valley Hub is a one hundred year old school that has a coffee shop and is used by the Community and is being restored to its original state. The Saltair School, which was closed and sold, sat empty for ten years, then was purchased by the CVRD, is now being used as a Community Centre. This would be a great asset to have for South Wellington and surrounding areas, we pay taxes, but have never seen any effort by the RDN, in the past, of having any interest in our Community… There is an assessment being conducted by the RDN on the structural health of the South Wellington School building, even if this comes back being unfavourable, the least that should happen is that the property remains in the Community for the use of the Community.. This Community is in dire need of a Community space/hub. There are no other parks in the area that are useable, other than Thelma Griffith park, which the Community worked closely with the RDN to revamp the park which was overgrown with blackberry vines and various other bushes. It is now used frequently, but is geared towards very young children. It is of great importance that South Wellington and surrounding areas have access to their own recreational/cultural space i.e. the fields for soccer and softball as well as the playground and hockey/tennis court. Please take the time to seriously consider the ramifications of losing this Community space, instead, help build a legacy that would benefit the Community of South Wellington for decades to come. Thank you, for your consideration in this matter. Susan Toth
