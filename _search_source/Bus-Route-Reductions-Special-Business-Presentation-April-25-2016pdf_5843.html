FACILTIES TRANSPORATION Busing/Bus & Vehicle Maintenance/Courier + Cartage Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools BUDGET PREPARATION FOR 2016-2017 Impact Statement: Transportation – Reduction of courtesy seats Description: This strategy utilizes the current Board policy and Administrative Procedure to create efficiencies in transporting district students. The district is currently updating the existing policy and procedure since it is significantly outdated (1986). Staff examined a variety of guidelines when looking at the existing routes and potential efficiencies which include: Courtesy (ineligible) riders, travel assistance, distance and geographic location, history of route, number of rides and number of duplicated routes, including ones that the regional district was maintaining. For more detailed information, see Appendix C – Bus Route Reductions and Impacts. District Goal: Meet each student’s unique needs Impact: Following Board Policy to create consistency. Maintaining transportation for our special needs students and those living in unique geographic locations. Educating all students with healthy lifestyles and safe transporting of oneself in an Active Transportation Plan (Appendix E – Draft Active Transportation Plan) _________________________________________________________________________________________ District Goal: The continuous improvement of instruction and assessment Impact: The development of an Active Transportation Plan for all students and families. Efficiencies and funds will be redirected into student learning and classroom. _________________________________________________________________________________ District Goal: Enhanced facilities for learning Impact: Funding saved can be utilized for enhancing facilities. As the district continues to becoming sustainable, it is necessary to create operational efficiencies that can be redirected into learning and the classroom. BUDGET PREPARATION FOR 2016-2017 Impact Statement: Transportation Courtesy Service from CCS catchment to LSS and JBS (yearly cost) Description: Supplying bus transportation for students to attend their previous high school of choice due to the closure and reopening of CCS Does not meet the current board policy. District Goal: Meet each student’s unique needs Impact: Has the potential of creating inequities for other students that are out of catchment that have or will be affected by board decisions. Students presently from Cedar at John Barsby and Ladysmith secondary school could maintain student attendance at their present school through bus transportation. This may slow the enrolment into the new Cedar Secondary of their catchment students. _________________________________________________________________________________________ District Goal: The continuous improvement of instruction and assessment Impact: Supplying bussing to a school of choice for a student would allow students remain at their school of Choice. _______________________________________________________________________________________________ District Goal: Enhanced facilities for learning Impact: This strategy works against the district focus on becoming sustainable and the additional cost would have to be found in existing funding in the system resulting in other cuts and lay-offs.
