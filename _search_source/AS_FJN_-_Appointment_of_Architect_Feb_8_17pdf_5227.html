Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools ACTION SHEET DATE: February 8, 2017 TO: The Board of Education – Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Mike Ross, Facilities Planning Manager SUBJECT: Capital Projects Development Appointment of Architect – Frank J. Ney Addition Recommendation: That the Business Committee recommend to the Board of Education of School District No.68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that staff be directed to award the contract to Low Hammond Rowe for the design and construction supervision for the addition to Frank J. Ney Elementary subject to the successful negotiation of a client/architect agreement. Background: To support the proposed Rutherford closure and increased enrollment at Frank J. Ney as a result of adjustments to catchments in the district’s North Zone, staff were directed to engage the professional services necessary to develop a Ministry standard Project Investigation Report (PIR) for an addition to Frank J. Ney that allows for an increase in capacity from 40K/200 to 40K/400. Low Hammond Rowe Architects Inc. (LHRA), formerly Chow & Fleischauer who completed design and construction of the original Frank J. Ney in 1991, were retained in a prime consulting role to lead a team of sub-consultants in developing the PIR which included a thorough site/building analysis along with estimated project cost and was published July 28, 2015 and updated in September of 2016. On January 24, 2017, staff received a letter from the Ministry of Education’s Capital Delivery Branch confirming their approval for the school district to access $1,326,852 in restricted capital funding for the purposes of the Frank J. Ney Expansion Project. On January 25, 2017, staff provided an information update to the Board of Education advising of the Ministry’s letter and further, that staff would proceed with the closure process of Rutherford Elementary and building of an addition at Frank J. Ney Elementary. Discussion: In respect of the tight timelines to construct the addition at Frank J. Ney by September 2018, and in consideration of District Administrative Procedure 513 (Purchasing) and AP501 (Selection of Architect), staff recommends a variance to the tendering requirements for the selection of an Architect by engaging the services of LHRA to provide f u l l d e s i g n s e r vi c e s i n a pr i m e c o n s u l t i ng r o l e f or t he a d d i t i o n at Frank J. Ney to increase capacity from 40K/200 to 40K/400. Jackson Low, a partner in LHRA, would assume the role of Prime consultant and lead a team of qualified sub-consultants in preparing the report. LHRA have substantial elementary school design experience including District 68 schools, the existing Frank J. Ney, addition and alterations to Ladysmith Intermediate, alterations to Cedar Elementary (formerly NCI) as well as seismic mitigation/upgrade projects of similar value and complexity for School Districts 61 and 63. Staff recommends that LHRA be engaged immediately to permit the appropriate level of district input/involvement and to ensure that planning and design are completed in a timely manner so that construction can proceed such that the project be complete for s tudent transition in September 2018.
