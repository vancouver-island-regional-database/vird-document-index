In addition to living in Departure Bay Eco- School’s catchment, I would choose the eco-school option over Montessori, French Immersion or private schooling. My choice is based primarily on my knowledge of educational literature about the benefits of outdoor learning and the importance of instilling an ethics of care of the environment in our next generation of learnersl. I am a faculty member in the Faculty of Education at Vancouver Island University (VIU)where my areas of expertise are science education, principles of teaching and learning, and research methods, thus I am well positioned to draw on educational research to make an informed choice for my child. I know that the staffand trustees of School District 68 are also familiar with the demonstrated value and benefits an eco-school program can provide for its learners as the district has supported the eco-school program from its inception and in the revised facilities plan, provisions have been made to ensure that the program will continue by suggesting that the program could be accommodated by Cilaire Elementary School. However, I have some serious concerns about the ways in which such a transfer would negatively impact the current eco-school program. Elaborate plans have been made and initiated to completely redesign the school surroundings to support outdoor and environmental learningz. The easy access to coastal habitats at Departure Bay beach, salmon habitat in Woodstream Park and several types of forest habitats in Linley Valley and other surrounding parks are key to the kinds of educational activities and programs that have already been developed and will be developed over the next few years. These place-based learning initiatives cannot be transferred to another site and would need to be rethought and re-designed with a new site in mind. I worry that more resources would be required from the district such as busing and supervision in order to offer the same diversity of activities at another site such as Cilaire. Secondly, an eco-school program is dependent on support from and connections with community partners and Departure Bay Eco—Schoolhas established partnerships with a wide array of stakeholders such as Department of Fisheries and Oceans, Nanaimo and Area Land Trust, and Nanaimo Science and Sustainability Society which are just a few of over 20 community partnerships and contributors named on Departure Bay Eco-School's websitel. I fear that if the school district does not demonstrate that they value the program that Departure Bay Eco-School's leadership and teaching team have developed so far, that community members too will withdraw or reduce their ~ generous support.
