Losing Connections We Lost so Much More Than a Building Losing Connections • Introduction • Importance of Community in child’s education • Activities between Students in Cedar Schools • Positive Impacts • Negative Effects • Conclusion Introduction The loss of Cedar Community Secondary has been devastating to the Community of Cedar. • Parent-teacher interaction more difficult • Meetings, parent teacher events hard to attend • Extra-curricular activities- hard for bus students to participate • Financial impact of having to pay for transportion. Education is a Shared Responsibility Community School Family Education is a cooperative effort. Communities provide schools with a context and environment that can complement and reinforce the values, culture, and learning the schools provide for their students. Loss of Community • Loss of connection between adults in the community. • Loss of connections between students at different schools. • Many wonderful, educationally beneficial interactions between the students of Cedar Secondary, North Cedar Intermediate, and Woodbank Primary. Woodbank Primary • Literature Project between Grade 8’s and Grade Ones • 4 week project encompassing language arts, aboriginal, and fine arts learning outcomes. • Little Buddies comprehension was excellent and they felt they learned more because of their Big Buddies • Big Buddies reported feeling they had made a positive impact and wanted to continue such activities. High School Buddies • Buddy Relationships between Grade 8 English Students and Grade 3 students • Pen pal letters • Language based activities planned by Big Buddies • Fun activities planned by Big Buddies • Planned collaboration with the Textiles teacher to sew book bags for the Primary students Cedar Leaders and Grade 8 Students • Cedar Leaders and their teachers brought Grade 8 students into Woodbank Primary for craft activities at Christmas and Easter. Primary students loved the activities and interacting with the Grade 8’s. The Grade 8’s soon assumed a leadership role with the students. Volunteers for Special Events High School students often assisted with special events, like Gingerbread House Making or Pumpkin Carving Annual events like the Corn Roast , Christmas Craft night, or Fun Fairs were possible because Community School Coordinator could easily muster volunteers. Woodworking Students built crosses for our Remembrance Day Ceremony. Drama and Music The Cedar Secondary Band has performed for us on several occasions. The Drama students have performed skits for the primary students on topics such as safety and bullying. North Cedar Intermediate • Volunteer referees for volleyball and basketball games. • Cedar Secondary Band performed for the students • Drama students performed anti-bullying skits for the Students. • Cedar Leaders and Grade 8 students did craft activities with the students. • Phys. Ed students assisted with Track and Field Day • Beyond the Hurt trainees worked with groups around bullying Mentoring partnerships There were mentorship opportunities set up for the children at NCI through the Community Schools Coordinator. – Photography Class - NCI students were taught basic photography principles by students in Photography. – Computer Class - Students in computer classes could work one-on-one with NCI students to learn basic keyboarding and computer skills. – Art Class- Art students worked during class time to help young artists create “Masterpieces.” Positive Impacts For Younger Students They admire and look up to their big buddies. The one on one helps form a bond and gives struggling students support. Increased confidence, especially from extra reading practice. Littles get to see the teenagers in a different light. Comments “I think it’s the rapport that the high school students have. It helps the younger students relax with their learning.” “I really liked the high school kids and I can hardly wait to get to high school. “I look forward to when I see my buddy and I never want to miss that day.” Positive Impacts • Benefits for High School Mentors • Improved self-esteem, social responsibility, problem solving strategies and communication skills. Completion of volunteer hours for scholarships. • Involvement in the community with recognition to themselves and others that teens can make a difference. • A heightened sense of belonging in their school community. • Relationship with the Littles can be very empowering. Impact of Transition to High School • Having a sense of being part of a community and having exposure to the high school and its environment can help intermediate students make better transitions to the high school. • Helping teenagers, like the grade 8’s, feel connected to their community and admired by the little buddies, in turn helped their self esteem and feelings of responsibility. • This also eases their transition into the high school community. Students’ experiences in their first year of high school often determine their success throughout high school and beyond. Impact on Community • Mentoring is widely recognized as contributing to strong and healthy communities. • Experiential learning activities such cross-age groupings, community service activities, Big Buddy programs and mentoring in computers and the arts improves student engagement and enhance student learning. • This is good for the well-being for the entire community. ? Wasn’t this whole plan about enhancing student learning? Conclusion • The facilities plan was supposed to enhance student learning and opportunities but in fact has been extremely detrimental to student engagement and learning in the community of Cedar. • The amazing learning opportunities that can no longer happen impact student success on many different levels.
