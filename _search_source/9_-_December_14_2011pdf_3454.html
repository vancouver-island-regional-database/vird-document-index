A number of students from several secondary schools attended a rally before the Board meeting and stayed for the meeting. The students said that they need the exam week time to study and that teachers need time to prepare for the second semester. They also said there was a lack of consultation and they believe the changes are not fair to students and staff. Mr. Hutchinson said that as a result of the discontinuation of 12 optional provincial exams this year, the district looked at productive ways to use the instructional time that was formerly used for exams. He said that while the district is making changes to the exam week schedule this year, the exact details are still being finalized and he suggested students talk to their school principals to provide input into the schedule for the week.
