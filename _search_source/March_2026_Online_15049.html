Grade 9 boy who was bullied for wearing a pink shirt. When is it? Today, we honour that spirit by reaffirming our commitment to creating safe spaces for our children, youth, Elders, and families. Pink Shirt Day takes place on the last Wednesday of February each year (in 2026, it was February 25). Many schools and communities continue activities into March. How It Started Pink Shirt Day began in 2007 in Nova Scotia after two high school students, David Shepherd and Travis Price, organized classmates to wear pink in support of a Their simple act of solidarity grew into a nationwide movement promoting antibullying awareness. What It Represents Pink Shirt Day promotes: • • • • • Kindness and empathy Inclusion and belonging Standing up for others Safe schools and workplaces Addressing cyberbullying It encourages people to speak out against bullying rather than remain silent. As a First Nations community, we understand the deep and lasting impacts that bullying, discrimination, and exclusion can have on individuals and on Nations. These harms echo across generations. Standing against bullying is not only about addressing harmful behaviour — it is about strengthening our values of respect, humility, courage, and care for one another. We encourage our members to: Choose kindness in words and actions Support those who are being bullied 10 PINK SHIRT DAY Speak up when witnessing harmful behaviour Lead by example in our homes, schools, workplaces, and community spaces Let us continue building a community where every person feels seen, valued, and safe — where our teachings guide us to lift one another up rather than tear one another down. Together, we stand for respect. Together, we stand for kindness.
