EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: A Zoning Bylaw Amendment application has been received for 450 Thetis Drive for the purpose of adding Tourist Accommodation as a site-specific principal use. The Community Planning Advisory Committee (CPAC) is being asked to provide feedback regarding the application in accordance with the CPAC Terms of Reference. INFORMATION/BACKGROUND: Subject Property The subject property is zoned Single Dwelling Residential (R-1) and falls within Development Permit Area 6 (DPA6) - Riparian Development Permit Area and Development Permit Area 7 (DPA7) – Hazard Lands Development Permit Area. The subject property is 5,404 square metres (1.3 acres) in size. The subject property contains a three-storey single-family dwelling constructed in 2009. The house is approximately 502 m² (5,400 sq.ft.) in gross floor area and has 7 bedrooms and 6.5 bathrooms. One additional bedroom and kitchenette is proposed in an accessory shed on the property. Figure 1: Subject property map The subject property is located near the Battie Drive and Thetis Drive intersection, approximately 200 metres west of the intersection of Thetis Drive and Hall Road. The property is predominantly surrounded by single-family residential on three sides and Town of Ladysmith boundary on the south side/CVRD Area G zoned Forestry/Natural Resource 1 Zone (F-1). A Development Variance Permit (3090-24-01) was received on December 21, 2023 to vary the height of a shed that was constructed higher than the permitted height due to the site topography. At the time of reviewing this application, staff noted Zoning Bylaw infractions for a Bed & Breakfast Business Licence. A letter was sent to the applicant from Planning staff, dated November 28, 2024, outlining actions required to bring the property into compliance. In response, a Zoning Bylaw amendment application (3360-25-01) was received on December 22, 2024, to rezone the property to allow for Tourist Accommodation as a principal use for the property. Existing Short-Term Rental Regulations The nightly rental of dwelling units is currently limited to the C-2, C-4, CD-7 zones and certain areas of the A-RR zone. Some residential zones (RU-1, R-1, R-2, R-2-B) permit the nightly rental of up to four guest rooms within a single unit dwelling as a bed and breakfast. Rooms in bed and breakfasts are not allowed to contain kitchens or kitchenettes and are not permitted on properties where there is already a secondary suite, coach house, two-unit dwelling, or a multi-unit dwelling. Rental of an entire dwelling unit for short-term rental accommodation of paying guests is allowed in zones permitting Tourist Accommodation use. A business license is required to operate a Tourist Accommodation or Bed and Breakfast business. The Short-Term Rental Accommodations Act In late 2023, the Province of British Columbia enacted the Short-Term Rental Accommodations Act (STRAA) to strengthen regulations and enforcement for STRs and increase protections for long-term rental housing. The STRAA applies to STRs listed on vacation rental platforms and other websites, such as AirBnB, VRBO, Facebook Marketplace, Kijiji, and Craigslist. As part of the legislation, the Province requires that each STR host display a valid business licence number on their listing wherever a business licence is required by the local government. STR Platforms are required to share listing data with the Province every month, which in turn is shared with participating municipalities through the Short-Term Rental Data Portal (“STR Portal’). The STRAA also required all STRs to register with the Province by May 1st, 2025. STR platforms are required to remove advertisements and prevent new bookings of STRs without valid provincial registration numbers on June 2nd, 2025, and platforms must cancel all existing reservations for unregistered STRs after June 23rd, 2025. Operating a STR without a valid registration number may also result in fines and/or tickets for the operator. Ladysmith STR Program Council adopted Bylaw No. 2205 on April 15th, 2025, which amended the OCP to establish Temporary Use Permit guidelines to allow a limited number of TUPs for STRs where ShortTerm Rentals are not permitted under the Zoning Bylaw, or to approve rezoning applications where the applicant can demonstrate the benefits of the tourism offering provided by the STR outweighs the loss of housing. PROPOSAL: A site-specific amendment to the R-1 Zone is proposed that will allow Tourist Accommodation as principal use on the subject property. The current zoning on the property allows for single unit and two unit dwelling as principal uses and bed & breakfast as an accessory use. 2 The STR as an entire house at 450 Thetis Drive was booked for 81 nights in last twelve months. Highlights of the proposal include: • 7 spacious guest rooms with 6.5 bathrooms and a full kitchen. • 4 living areas • A media room with 85-inch TV • Indoor Jacuzzi bathtub • Outdoor dining with a barbecue • Ocean views and fruit trees As it is a site-specific amendment, the proposed Zoning Bylaw amendment will only be applicable to 450 Thetis Drive and not to other properties in the R-1 Zone. The Neighbourhood Residential designation is intended to provide residential areas with diverse housing types, tenures and densities. Some key OCP objectives include the following: • Create 10-minute neighbourhoods. • Make active transportation the easiest and most enjoyable way to get around. • Increase housing diversity and affordability. • Support for Tourism and Economic Development. Housing affordability and creating active and connected neighbourhoods are priorities within the OCP. These priorities should be considered when determining whether allowances for STRs will enhance the Town’s vision without adversely impacting other key objectives such as increasing the attainability of affordable, diverse housing. Zoning Bylaw The subject property is zoned Single Dwelling Residential – R-1 in “Town of Ladysmith Zoning Bylaw 2014, No. 1860” which permits bed and breakfast as an accessory use. The proposed STR currently does not meet the bed and breakfast zoning regulations because the rental area is for the entire dwelling unit, is over the maximum allowable 4 guest rooms, and has full kitchen facilities, therefore a Zoning Bylaw Amendment is required. Tourist Accommodation use permits more than 4 short-term rental rooms with kitchen facilities on the property. The proposal meets all other regulations in the Zoning Bylaw.
