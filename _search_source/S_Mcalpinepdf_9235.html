From: To: Subject: Date: Karen Matthews Karen Matthews FW: Meeting on mandatory vaccination October 27, 2021 12:02:07 PM From: sarah mcalpine < > Sent: October 27, 2021 10:51 AM To: Charlene Mckay ; Greg Keller ; Jessica Stanley ; Chantel O'neill ; Elaine Wilkinson ; Stephanie Higginson ; Bill Robinson ; Tania Brzovic Subject: Meeting on mandatory vaccination CAUTION: External Message To whom it may concern , I am writing this letter today as it has been brought to my attention that school districts 68 will be having a meeting to discuss vaccine mandates for the teachers in this school district . Thought I am not a teacher or staffed anywhere within school district 68 or at any school district for that matter, my child does attend a school with in this district. I am writing today because Canadians need to be able to have a voice , and the voice needs to be heard with regards to our personal choice on medical treatments . In Canada the choice on whether or not to accept or not accept medical treatment has always been an individual right, the Canadian national report on immunization (1996) stated immunizations are not mandatory in Canada and cannot be made mandatory because of the Canadian constitution. Section 2 of our charter guarantee these rights and freedoms through the freedom of conscience (subsection a) and the freedom of thought, belief, opinion ,and expression (subsection b). Without an individual having the freedom to make their own medical choices we no longer live in a democratic society.   The charter of rights and freedoms protects Canadians fundamental rights and freedoms, essentially keeping Canada a free and democratic society . Our Canadian nation is founded on principles that acknowledge the dignity and worth of the human person and the position of the family in a society of free men and free institutions ,it also continues to say that men and institutions remain free only when freedom is founded upon respect for moral and spiritual values and the rule of law . Over the last 18 -24 months I have never witnessed such a division with in my country and my community such as the us vs them mentality we are seeing today . we have had the opportunity to hear about and learn from the atrocities that our fellow Canadian first nations faced at residential schools and how under the direction of our federal government RCMP officer were given lawful orders to remove indigenous children from their homes and family's and transport them to residential schools . These actions that were directed by our federal government are actions our communities are still trying to recover from to this day. Please don't allow this mandate to become the next great lesson.   I strongly believe that should the government mandate be scrutinized under a court of law it would not hold up to the weight of injustice it is imposing upon our Canadian people . As a mother , as a member of this community and as a member of school district 68 I send this letter on behalf of all teachers to plea on their behalf that school district 68 does not make the vaccine mandatory for staff, and allows its employees to continue to teach our children without being under the thumb of threat and Coercion. These teachers in our district and all district across our country are helping to shape our great nations children our future ! and should be allowed to do so under the pretense of freedom not fear .
