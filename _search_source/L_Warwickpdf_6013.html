Facilities Plan: Supporting Improved Learning Opportunities for Students NDSS PAC March 19, 2015 Presentation to the Board of Trustees Role of Schools in the Community Community Centres or Centres of Learning Community Centres Public information Group activities Social support Community resources Development programs Schools Support student achievement Prepare students through education Support diversity Work with community partners Schools can perform an important role in the community. A community component is becoming increasingly important in securing capital funding. However, first and foremost, schools are centres of learning. They become communities in themselves. NDSS serves families all over the district, and the site provides community services to Nanaimo and surrounding areas. It is a community based on common interests. NDSS PAC 2 Why Continue With the Facilities Plan? • Support improved learning opportunities  Ensure enrichment, skills training, and support programs are available to all students in the district • Reduce operating expenses and direct funds to the classroom  Closing and consolidating schools is necessary to reduce operating expenses & ensure funds are directed where they are most needed • Reduce uncertainty around school closures • Better facilities to support student learning  40% of our schools are past their useful lifespan  Inefficient and expensive to maintain  “Trustees Consider Moving School Board Meetings” “…it’s a very professional building. It offers things that our current building doesn’t have. If we’re going to continue in that building, we’re going to have to spend money.” – News Bulletin, March 3, 2015  Better facilities! This is what we want for our kids! NDSS PAC 3 Impact of the Facilities Plan • Questions we need to ask about the facilities plan:  Are we achieving the goals and objectives?  What has been the impact on student programs, operating costs, capacity utilization?  Do the assumptions still apply, or has information changed?  What is working? What isn’t working?  How do we close and consolidate schools with minimal disruption to students and families?  How do we decide which schools to close if no one wants it to be their school?  What, if anything, needs to change? • Ministry supported the District’s 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan, and directed the District to develop a preliminary plan for the NDSS site. NDSS PAC 4 NDSS Site Development Plan The proposed NDSS site development is a major component of the facilities plan. A new high school to consolidate Woodlands and NDSS in central Nanaimo has been on the table for a number of years. The Ministry supports the preliminary plan and has directed the District to keep working with its partners. Key components of the NDSS portion of the plan: • ✔Consolidate Learning Alternatives (VAST, Five Acres, Ravens Lelum) at the present site  Benefits – Cafeteria program, access to more courses for all students, increased efficiencies through consolidation of administrative functions, increased capacity utilization. • Consolidate NDSS/Woodlands into a new school on the NDSS site, and create a multi-use site to serve students and the broader community.  Improve capacity utilization and reduce operating costs  Joint use with VIU, City of Nanaimo, and community groups (involving stakeholders is key for gaining support from the Ministry) NDSS PAC 5 Impact of Not Continuing With the Plan • If the District does not take decisive action and move forward, uncertainty over schools closures will continue to upset families and tear communities apart. • The District will continue to lose credibility with the Ministry, as has happened in the past, if we change our minds again. This will prevent us from getting in the queue for capital funding. • Consolidating schools and optimizing services are necessary to reduce the operating deficit, unless we cut programs. Maintaining too many schools with low enrolment is no longer feasible. • Seeking more funding will not solve all of the problems. Resources will always be limited. We need to find ways to effectively and efficiently use these limited resources and direct funds toward educating and supporting our students. NDSS PAC 6 Securing Capital From the Ministry • A number of capital projects are included in the 10-year plan, with a view toward further consolidation and upgrading facilities  Provides framework for school consolidation decisions  Forms basis for capital investment decisions • • Prioritize projects for five-year capital plan, submitted annually Ministry assigns priority ranking • Incorporate multi-use facilities and community component through strategic partnerships (e.g. VIU, City of Nanaimo, community groups) NDSS PAC 7 Securing Capital From the Ministry (cont.) • A district-wide plan is necessary, and we must be able to demonstrate that we will continue to deliver on the actions to close and consolidate schools  Capacity utilization needs to improve  Need to reduce operating expenditures, increase efficiencies • District needs to provide a portion of the funding for capital projects  Funds from sales of school property, partners, etc.  2.5M earmarked for new/rebuilt high school NDSS PAC 8 Where Do We Go From Here? • Do we need a working group to share knowledge and ideas going forward  Brainstorm ways to minimize impact on communities • Continue to meet with VIU, City of Nanaimo, and community groups to determine feasibility of moving ahead with the new high school • Take necessary steps to get into the Ministry’s queue for capital funds  The Ministry has capital. New schools are being built in other districts. Education is changing. It is moving toward a model of collaboration, incorporating more open spaces and emerging technology to support learning. Let’s not get left behind! NDSS PAC 9
