CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Solomon: Bayview has a family movie night on December 3. Their Christmas concert is December 17. Georgia Avenue - Celebration of life for Dominik Billy who passed away in the house fire one year ago. Woodbank has a craft night on December 10 and their Christmas concert on December 15. Cinnabar - attended PAC meeting and gave an update on the facilities plan and there was some discussion about busing. Trustee Brzovic: Dover Bay - Second semester prep, timetable changes and grade 11 students submitting their applications should they wish to participate in CTC in grade 12. There was an impactful assembly from MADD on Nov 24. Report cards and parent teacher interviews coming up. Frank Ney - Their PAC is active with a Purdy's chocolate fundraiser, hot lunch days, book fairs and bake sales. There was a Remembrance Day assembly and they took part in Metis Days on Nov 18/19. McGirr Elementary - Ms. Wilkie has now retired and Mr. Halfyard is settling in nicely. He will be starting a floor hockey league that will run over recess for grade 6/7 students. There are a lot of successful sports teams at the school, such as; cross country, volleyball and tripleball. Randerson Ridge - They had their annual Christmas Craft fair (held at Dover Bay). Trustee Robinson: Brechin - There was a ski and swap. Last year they raised $1500. There were 18 rotary volunteers that were there helping out along with some teachers and Ms. Crane. Trustee Kimler: LSS - Met with the PAC President to discuss the possibility of French Immersion grades 8 & 9 for 2016. Ms. Margaret Olsen is the interim Principal. Over $2,000 was raised during this year's book fair. Special thanks to Pamela Puska, the school librarian and all the students that helped make the fair a success. LPS -The first year of the school technology plan is nearly complete. The school worked with PAC to install ELMO projectors and a laptop in all of the classrooms for teachers to enhance their instruction. They also have 5 iPads for trial, to further inform implementation during year two of the plan. PAC’s total contribution this year was $11,000 Ecole North Oyster - Their successful book fair sold $6,000-worth of books, resulting in over half of gross sales back in the form of new books for the library. PAC’s McNab’s corn-maze concession raised $3100 & a school dance raised $900 and had over 150 in attendance. Many thanks to local businesses for helping the PAC on these events.
