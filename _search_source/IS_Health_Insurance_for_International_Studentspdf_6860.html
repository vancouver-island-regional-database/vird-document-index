NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: October 9, 2019 Business Committee Secretary-Treasurer, Carrie McVeigh Health Insurance Coverage and Fee for International Students Board of Education Strategic Plan – Board Goal Safe, caring and healthy learning and working environment that is inclusive of the diversity of our entire learning community. Information In early August of 2019, the Ministry of Health announced a new health fee for international students. Effective September 1, 2019, international students are assessed a health fee of $37.50 per month. The fee will increase to $75 per month on January 1, 2020, when the Medical Services Plan (MSP) premiums for BC residents is eliminated. The new fee applies to all international students, including K-12 students, who have an immigration status as “Student” and a Study Permit with Health Insurance BC (HIBC). The government stated that school districts are not expected to collect or process the fee for eligible students enrolled in public schools; rather, the fee would be issued by Revenue Canada to the address on file with the MSP system. However, NLPS has collected health insurance fees and for various reasons, including legal considerations and practical operational issues, the department will continue to do so. The introduction of the International Student Health Fee ensures that international students continue to have access to health services as the government eliminates MSP premiums for BC residents. The provincial announcement aligns the Board’s goal of safe, caring and healthy learning and working environment that is inclusive of the diversity of our entire learning community. It is essential that our international students enjoy the educational programming in Nanaimo with the same health benefits afforded other students. Background The Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools International Student Education was established in 1998. Since 2004, the District has been purchasing a private comprehensive health insurance package that includes all MSP services plus a host of additional health benefits, for its international students. During this period, the District has not enrolled students in MSP, which was supported by a legal opinion issued through BCSTA in 2012. As a prudent District, Staff had, just prior to the announcement, asked BCSTA for an updated legal opinion which confirmed the 2012 opinion – that international students do not need to be enrolled in MSP. Understandably, the announcement produced a flurry of activity in the school community around two MSP issues 1) enrollment and 2) fees. Some of the questions being raised by various associations including BCASBO, BCSTA and IPSEA, were specifically around mandatory MSP enrollment for eligible international students. NLPS staff sought an independent legal opinion which in summary, advised that international students should be enrolled in MSP and should also have private insurance for coverage during times and for claims that MSP coverage would not apply. Around the same time, BCSTA sought another legal opinion which supported the requirement to enroll all minor international students in MSP. It should be noted that public school districts across the province have varying practices in respect of health insurance for international students. Some districts exclusively use MSP, others use exclusive private health insurance and some enroll international students in both MSP and private health insurance. Further, the announcement was unexpected by many school districts and, no consultation had taken place with members of the International Public Schools community. Thousands of students had already been enrolled in BC schools based on existing fee structures and legal agreements. The International Public Schools Education Association of British Columbia (IPSEA), wrote a letter on August 9, to the Ministers of Health, Finance, Education, and Advanced Education. The letter outlined the associations concerns about the immediate implementation of new MSP Fees and requested a delay in implementation until September 2020. That request was denied. In September, the Ministry of Education made the following statements for clarity on this matter:    Under the Medicare Protections Act and Regulations, anyone living in BC for six months or longer must enroll in the Medical Services Plan. This includes international students who meet the eligibility requirements. There has been no change to his requirement, which was in place prior to the introduction of the new health fee. A new health fee for international students has been announced by the Ministry of Health. Beginning September 1, 2019, international students with a valid study permit of six or more months on file with Health Insurance BC, and who have active MSP coverage will be assessed a health fee of $37.50 per month. This fee will increase to $75 per month on January 1, 2020, when MSP premiums are eliminated. Provisions in the Canada Health Act and the Medicare Protection Act ensure MSP coverage and access to care continues while enrolled and eligible, which is not connected to the payment and status of the health fee. In conclusion, the District serves as the custodian for most of its international students and as a prudent parent, the district will now register all eligible international students in MSP and in private insurance as noted below. For example, minor students on a study permit of 6 months or more are required to enroll in MSP after the three month waiting period; December 1, 2019. Multi-year students currently attending NLPS will be enrolled effective September 1, 2019 (where applicable). Previously, NLPS international students were enrolled exclusively with a private insurance carrier that provided exceptional coverage (services normally covered by MSP and additional benefits) with coverage taking effect immediately. In light of the directives from government, District Principal Hutchins has negotiated two plans at reasonable rates with this private carrier, Guard.Me. Guard.Me International Insurance provides several plan options. As noted, for the last several years the ISE Department has enrolled all students in guard.me@school. The plan provides comprehensive coverage that includes services normally covered by MSP and additional benefits. The other plan, guard.me@bcschool, provides for secondary coverage i.e. services not covered by MSP and, is therefore used in conjunction with MSP. Health Insurance Coverage Details and Budget Implications Single Semester Students – Not eligible for MSP In 2019-20 and subsequent years - only single semester students will be in enrolled in this plan.  Guard.me@School is designed for students who are not on MSP, or for short-term students who need full coverage and are not eligible for MSP. The plan and BC Schools Plan also includes coverage for such high cost events as:  Medical Air Evacuation to Home Country  Family Transportation and Subsistence Allowance - For family members’ travel/accommodation to be with their child during a lengthy hospital stay or in the event of a fatality.  Repatriation or Burial of Deceased Full Year Students In 2019-20, with the requirement to join MSP after three months, our full year students will be enrolled in guard.me@BC Schools for 10 months and MSP for 7 months.  Guard.me@BCSchools is designed for long-term students who will need full coverage for the first 90 days until MSP is effective, then following, the plan acts as a top up / PLUS plan and has the MSP Disruption rider which covers the student (1) while waiting for their Study Permit to be renewed (if applied for renewal within 30 days of expiry) and their MSP is expired or (2) if the Study Permit goes into restoration. Multi-Year Students In 2020-21, students returning to us for a second or third year will be required to enroll in MSP at $75 per month for 12 months. Most of these students live with a relative or family friend and will be encouraged to purchase additional insurance. Those students whose Study Permit will expire during the school year, and the District serves as Custodian, will be required to purchase Guard.mePlus BC with MSP Disruption rider.  Guard.me PlusBC with MSP Disruption rider – this is the top up/ Plus plan as noted above and includes the MSP Disruption rider. This plan is meant to be used for students who have current/ valid MSP. The rider covers the student (1) while waiting for their Study Permit to be renewed (if applied for renewal within 30 days of expiry) and their MSP is expired or (2) if the Study Permit goes into restoration. Financial Implications for the 2019-20 School Year For the 2019-20 School Year there will be an estimated loss of revenue to the school district of $64,680, with the enrollment in MSP, based on the established fee schedule. Administration of MSP for International Students may also require some re-allocation of staff resources. The budget shortfall will be reviewed during the Amended Budget as a priority. Fees for health insurance have been adequately set to cover costs for the 2020-2021 school year.
