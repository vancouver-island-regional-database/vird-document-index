CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Mr. Joel Smith and Mr. Geoff Steel Teacher and Principal of NDSS RE: NDSS Field Lacrosse Academy - First Year Overview and Look Ahead Mr. Joel Smith, Teacher and Coach at NDSS, wanted to thank the Board for the opportunity to present again, one year later after they approved the Lacrosse Academy. In the inaugural year, there were 60 athletes (grades 8,9,10 and 11,12 groups) registered in the Lacrosse Academy. This year, they have 52 athletes registered. The Junior team has played 15 games with an overall record of 3-9-3. The Senior team has played 14 games with an overall record of 6-8. The Juniors had a great provincial run, 3-1-1, but lost in the final. The Seniors had a 2-2 record, lost in overtime to the Provincial Champs. The fee structure for participants has changed from $600 to $800. 100% of students have committed to return to the Lacrosse Academy for next year. Senior Team Captain, Graden Soucy, addressed the Education Committee and said that this has been his best year of high school. He has met so many wonderful people and their participation in the three tournaments this year have been a great experience. Woodlands Environment Club Energy Cup Challenge Woodlands Environmental Club students, Graeme Arkell and Tristan Tolley presented to the Education Committee. The students talked about ways to save energy throughout the school; shutting down computers, program the 149 computers to turn off 2 hours earlier than before (saved $.39 / computer), sweater days on Friday's, serving hot chocolate to students, lights out lunch, ceramic heater trade in, and spring break shutdown. During lunch hours members of the Environmental Club go throughout the school doing audits, to Page 4 of 53 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held April 6, 2016 ensure each classroom is complying with these energy saving ways.
