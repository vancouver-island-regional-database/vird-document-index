THE BOARD OF SCHOOL TRUSTEES OF SCHOOL DISTRICT. 68 ACTION SHEET To: The Board of Education From: Robyn Gray, Assistant Superintendent Date: April 30, 2015 Subject: Middle Years Development Instrument ______________________________________________________________________ Background After a presentation to the Education Committee on April 1, 2015 on the Middle years Development Instrument, the Board of Education directed staff to explore participation in the Middle Years Development Instrument. Exploration Process The Department of Learning Services met on three occasions and reviewed the following Human Early Learning Partnership (HELP) information:  The MDI Fact Sheet 2014  Sample School Report MDI-4 Results, 2014-15—School Data  Sample Middle Year Development Instrument—Survey of Grade 4 students 2014- 2015  Sample Middle Years Development Instrument—Survey of Grade 7 students 2014- 2015  Why Middle Years Matter Info sheet  School District and Community summaries http://earlylearning.ubc.ca/maps/mdi/nh/. Potential Benefits  Some potentially interesting social emotional data would come from participation  We could choose to capture the same cohort of children that participated in the EDI with the MDI for comparison. For example, in 2016-17 the same students that participated in the EDI in 2012-134 will be in Grade 4   Both the Grade 4 and Grade 7 surveys are available online at the HELP website. Should the program be approved and students register in this program of choice and leave their catchment area school, it is expected that the staffing impact will be very small both at the receiving school and at other secondary schools these students leave. Considerations  MDI participation would further tax both Grade 4 and Grade 7 classrooms where learning time is already lost to Ministry Satisfaction Survey participation.  MDI participation would further tax both Grade 4 and Grade 7 classrooms where learning time is already lost to FSA participation.  MDI participation requires a teacher or principal to supervise the distribution and completion of the survey. All the students must be online for one to two class periods. Depending on the number of computers available, this could mean significant additional supervision blocks.  Community stakeholders would likely be interested in the data, but the cost of participation—both time and money—falls solely on the school district.  The survey participation cost of $21,000.00 is steep and we would prefer to target any available funds to support the Department of Learning Services’ current goals of the continuous improvement of instruction and assessment, and meeting each student’s unique needs. Summary The Department of Learning Services recognizes that we have a variety of data sources: EDI, Satisfactions Surveys, FSA, The McCreary Report, and the MCFD vulnerable index. All of this data is utilized in different ways by a number of our staff and schools. Some of the MDI survey items are of interest to the Department of Learning Services. As a district, we are able to add questions to the Ministry Satisfaction Survey, therefore, we could potentially identify a few add-on questions for each grade level gleaned from the MDI. The one-time cost of adding these questions would allow us to track students and schools over time on issues related to our goals and the strategic plan. Recommendation On behalf of the Department of Learning Services, we recommend to the Board that NLPS does not participate in the MDI at this time. Participation could be revisited in the future when a more thorough comprehensive plan is developed which emphasizes a need for the MDI data.
