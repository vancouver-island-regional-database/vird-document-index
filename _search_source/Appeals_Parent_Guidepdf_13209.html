Parent Student Appeals: A S T E P -BY-S TE P G U ID E The NLPS Board of Education will hear appeals on decisions that significantly affect a student’s education, health or safety. Please note: The Board of Education does not typically hear appeals regarding classroom placement, out-of-catchment placements, resourcing or interventions (ie. EA hours or specific EA). See the full Policy 502- Parent Student Appeal Bylaw Section 1.7 for additional decisions that are not eligible for a Board appeal. If your concern is not appealable, please see 502.1 Addressing Parent Complaints (hyperlink) for more information. The appeal process is confidential, and you are protected from retaliation. The Board will only alter a decision it feels is unreasonable. To submit an appeal, you must complete the following steps. STEP 1 COMPLETE AND SUBMIT THE NOTICE OF APPEAL TO YOUR SCHOOL PRINCIPAL. • • The principal will arrange a meeting. Following the meeting, within a reasonable amount of time you will receive a summary of the meeting outcome, follow up actions to be taken and the rationale for the decision. STEP 4 If you have followed the steps above and your concern has still not been resolved, YOU MAY SUBMIT AN APPEAL TO THE BOARD OF EDUCATION. • • Fill out the APPEAL FORM. Be sure to fill our all sections and clearly explain what resolution you are looking for from the Board of Education and why you are asking for this resolution. You will receive a timeline for when the Board will review your appeal within 45 calendar days. Appendix C STEP 2 STEP 3 If your concern has not been resolved in Step 1, If your concern has not been resolved in Step 2, YOU CAN APPEAL TO THE DIRECTOR OF INSTRUCTION BY COMPLETING THE STEP 2 FORM AND SUBMITTING YOUR DOCUMENTATION. YOU CAN APPEAL TO AN ASSISTANT SUPERINTENDENT BY COMPLETING THE STEP 3 FORM AND SUBMITTING YOUR DOCUMENTATION. • • • • The Director of Instruction will arrange a meeting. Following the meeting, within a reasonable amount of time, the Director of Instruction will provide a summary of the meeting, follow up actions and rationale for the decision. The Assistant Superintendent will arrange a meeting. Following the meeting, within a reasonable amount of time, the Director of Instruction will provide a summary of the meeting, follow up actions and rationale for the decision. IF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION HEARS YOUR APPEAL: PREPARE FOR A HEARING IN FRONT OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION. • • • You will be given a date for a hearing with the Board of Education within 45 days of submitting your appeal. At the hearing, be prepared to present your point of view, listen to staff responses and answer questions from the Board. You may bring an advocate or support person with you to the hearing. DECISION • • Following the hearing, you will receive an email with the Board’s decision. If you are unsatisfied with the decision, you may be able to appeal the Board’s decision to the Superintendent of Appeals with the BC Ministry of Education and Child Care.
