CLASSROOM PLACEMENT Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools Emotional Social Academic AF T Physical Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools (SD68) is committed to the holistic develop of every learner. Each learner is seen as unique, bringing individual strengths and gifts to the context of their classroom. When we consider classroom placements, retention and acceleration of our learners, we will consider all domains of development. DR Indigenous Peoples of Canada have long since utilized a holistic approach to understanding human development through the quadrants of a medicine wheel. In adapting these teachings, NLPS uses a similar approach for understanding our learners. For the purposes of education the following terms are meant to reference the aspects below: Emotional This quadrant is meant to capture both the emotional aspects of a child. This would also connect with the child's Core Competencies Academic This quadrant captures academic achievement in areas such as literacy and numeracy. This quadrant can also capture the development of student motivation, engagement and communication skills. Social This quadrant reflect the child's social development and social skills and relationships. This would also connect with the child's Core competencies Physical This quadrant captures the students maturity in relation to physical development typical of age range. Due to enrollment and classroom composition, multi-grade classrooms are commonplace in our elementary schools. A team of administrators and teachers at the school level utilize a holistic approach to place students in classes. The team meets multiple times to discuss and review class placements and considers factors such as academic achievement, cognitive abilities, social and emotional development, self-regulation, as well as physical, medical, and other pertinent information. BC Student Reporting Program T AF DR The BC Ministry of Education have found that combined classroom placement does not affect academic achievement. Research by Joel Gajadharsingh at the University of Saskatchewan found that students in combined classes do just as well as those in single grade classes. According to John Goodlad’s research, within a single grade classroom there can be a development span of 5 years and a split can have up to 6 years. Teachers have a number of different lessons going on at the same time as they must differentiate to their students’ abilities and interests, in both combined and single grade classrooms. BCs Redesigned Curriculum The BC redesigned curriculum emphasizes student development in curricular competencies and core competencies. Students in a combined grade classroom will be working towards similar competencies with content and expectations that are specific to their grade.
