October 29, 2014 Ref: 177404 To: All Board Chairs All School Districts The Government of BC is committed to working with school districts, health authorities, postsecondary institutions and crown corporations, to reduce overhead costs and look for efficiencies and savings through collaboration and shared services. With the fiscal challenges facing all of us, it is imperative that all entities participate in the available shared service initiatives. In 2012, the Ministry of Education engaged Deloitte, one of Canada’s leading professional service firms, to research and identify potential shared service opportunities, and to deliver an assessment of likely costs, benefits and implications. Their report formed the basis of a Service Delivery Project, which further evaluated 14 of the 25 opportunities, and provided recommended plans of action in the areas of Procurement, Legal Services, and Attendance Support and Wellness. Over this past summer, Deloitte has been instructed to revisit their 2012 report and to refresh opportunity benefits and costs. I am pleased to share with you that this work has been completed and is available on the Ministry’s website at: http://www.bced.gov.bc.ca/deloitte_report_july_2014.pdf This letter signals the beginning of Phase 2 of the Service Delivery Project. It is focused on evaluating and supporting implementation of another group of service delivery opportunities in our sector, along with ongoing support for those launched in Phase 1. This project will be led by a new Shared Services Branch of our Ministry, created to engage and support the sector in this work. This will ensure the project receives the perspective of trustees and representatives from the BC School Trustees Association (BCSTA), who will be a vital part of the Education Partners Advisory Committee, which provides advice and input from our partners throughout the project.
