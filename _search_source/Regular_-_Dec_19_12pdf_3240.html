Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT Regular Board Meeting Wednesday, December 19, 2012 Superintendent/CEO’s Priorities (2012-2013): 1. Advancing the Learning Agenda:  RTI implementation continues, as does the development of the Instructional Coordinator model and the Learning Services Support teams;  The Self-Regulated learning demonstration classroom is up and running at Seaview elementary and plans are underway to establish similar classrooms at Dover Bay Secondary and Bayview Elementary. To date the classroom has hosted visits for Learning Services Support teams, Kindergarten teachers, teachers from a number of system elementary and secondary schools, and trustees.  We continue to implement the BC Ed Plan reading initiative “Changing Results for Young Readers,” and we are also initiating some development work with respect to a $30,000 Ministry initiatives grant we received this year that was provided to school districts that were pursuing innovations with respect to special education. Our system is focused on simplifying the Individual Education Plan form/process, as well as the implementation of RTI.  The Strategic Plan, Draft One, has been approved by the Board of Education. The document includes a commitment to achieve the District goals (Meeting Each Student’s Unique Needs, The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment, and Enhanced Facilities for Learning), starting with the development of the following: o Strategy#1: Board Policy 1.7: Meeting Each Student’s Unique Needs; o Strategy #2: Board Policy #1.8: The Continuous Improvement of Instruction and Assessment; and o Strategy #3: A Ten-Year Capital Plan that Delivers on the Goal of Enhanced Facilities for Learning. 2. Strengthening Communications:  The Director of Communications has developed a communications plan for the District Strategic Plan (please see the attachment in the agenda);  The District is proceeding with its “Success for All” system-wide professional learning days which will focus on a number of topics of strategic and operational interest (i.e. Response to Intervention, Developing Respectful Workplaces, and Protocols for Managing Student and Staff Safety). As well, Learning Services Deputy Superintendent John Blain has struck a steering committee to help shape the structure of the day, and the committee includes representation from the Board of Education, the DPAC, the NDTA, CUPE, and senior management. 3. Developing a Performance Management Culture:  The Board of Education recently approved new governance policies and procedures, designed to concentrate the Board’s work on strategic policy governance. Under this model, trustees set the stage for performance management by establishing Board and District goals, and a District strategic plan. It is then the responsibility of the Superintendent/CEO to oversee the development of relevant Board policy and the implementation of the strategic plan.  Within the new policies, Policy 2.13 “Monitoring Superintendent Performance,” requires an annual evaluation of the Superintendent/CEO, and this is currently underway.  As the establishment of a strategic policy governance model creates a clear dividing line between the roles and responsibilities of the Board, and the roles and responsibilities of the Superintendent/CEO, work is now underway to revise the existing Board Policies and Procedures Manual. As there are approximately 600 Board Policies, Procedures, and Bylaws in the existing governance manual, a consultant (Dave Mumford) has been hired to undertake this work. Further, a steering committee has been established to provide guidance to Mr. Mumford throughout the process. This is a joint Board/senior management committee that will provide periodic updates to the Board of Education with respect to this initiative. It is our intent to complete this work by the close of this school year.
