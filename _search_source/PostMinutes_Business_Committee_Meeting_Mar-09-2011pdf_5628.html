COMMITTEE REPORTS From the Facilities Planning Committee Consultation on School Boundary/Catchment Changes Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations told the committee that the proposals he presented this evening are mostly housekeeping changes. The changes would affect catchment areas for Coal Tyee, Pleasant Valley, Forest Park, Quarterway and Chase River. He said that if the Board approves the recommendation, the district would carry out a full consultation process in the fall of 2011 before the Board made decisions on any possible changes for September 2013. If the catchment areas were to be changed, students currently attending the affected schools would not be required to change schools. UNFINISHED BUSINESS Budget 2011/12 - Update Pay Equity - Follow-up to Board Motion Mr. Turin told the Committee that management staff have been working on developing budget proposals to bring forward to the Business Committee in early April. He said that the district expects to receive information about Ministry of Education funding levels later this week. Folio R11/02/28-21 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that it communicates its concerns over the allocation of pay equity funding and make every effort to secure additional funding for this district. Chair Murchie said he had asked to have the item - pay equity follow-up added to the Agenda so that the follow-up process could be discussed. Trustees asked Mr. Turin if he could contact the Secretary-Treasurer of the Abbotsford School District about lobbying the Ministry for additional pay equity funding. Trustees also asked Trustee Welch if she would contact the Abbotsford Board Chair as well. NEW BUSINESS Forecast 2010-11 Mr. Turin reviewed the budget for the current school year, 2010-11, and provided a forecast for year end. He cautioned that there are still several unknown factors, which could significantly affect the year-end position. He noted that the district reduced expenses this spring by about $900,000, in order to offset any possible budget shortfall in the current year and to have some surplus funds to help address next year’s anticipated shortfall.
