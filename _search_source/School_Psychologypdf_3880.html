THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS ACTION SHEET DATE: September 10, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: Bob Esliger, Assistant Superintendent SUBJECT: Regulation of School Psychologists in the College of Psychologists in BC BACKGROUND: Since 1987 school psychologists have been exempt from the requirement to be members in the College of Psychologists of BC. In 2003 BCSTA passed a resolution requesting that school psychologists continue to be exempt from the requirements outlined in the Health Professions Act. The purpose of this exemption was to allow school psychologists to continue to work in school districts without meeting the full requirements of the College. In 2005 the Ministry of Education extended the exemption with the expectation that all school psychologists meet “either the standards for registration with the College of Psychologists or the standards for membership in the BC Association of School Psychologists (BCASP) within a reasonable time”. Since that time, the College of Psychologists has been working with the BC Association of School Psychologists to develop a pathway that would allow school psychologists to become registrants in the College, and therefore become a regulated profession under the Health Professions Act. This pathway would allow school psychologists to continue to support students in school settings with their existing academic qualifications while adhering to the College’s standards of conduct. The proposed changes would not require any changes to the practice or qualifications of school psychologists now or in the future. It would allow them to be registered in the College in a special category that would restrict their practice to supporting students in a school setting. BCSTA has contacted the BC Association of School Psychologists to confirm that its members support the recommendations. RECOMMENDATION: 1. That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) write a letter of support for the regulation of school psychologists with the College of Psychologists of British Columbia (CPBC) and that the Board endorse the College’s proposed pathway for porting BCASP (BC Association of School Psychologists) over to under the proposed “special registry”. 2. That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), after discussing the proposed changes, indicate their level of support for the proposal by accessing the following online survey: http://fluidsurveys.com/s/schoolpsychologists/
