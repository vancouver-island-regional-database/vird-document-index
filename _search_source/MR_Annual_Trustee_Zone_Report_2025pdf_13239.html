ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee MARK ROBINSON Date: NOVEMBER 16th, 2025 School Names/Zone: John Barsby Family of Schools - John Barsby Community School Bayview Elementary School Chase River Elementary School Georgia Avenue Community School Park Avenue Elementary School Learning Alternatives Program Annual Highlights: John Barsby Community School John Barsby Community School continues to be a hub of energy, teamwork, and Bulldog pride in Nanaimo’s south end (as it always has). Upon being appointed to the role, the principal and I had a great walk around the school, meeting staff and students and seeing first hand the school community in action. From advanced robotics classes to joining students in Friday karaoke (which a fun highlight!), this first visit as the trustee rep. left me with fond memories. After touring the facilities, the principal and I sat down together and chatted about all the wins and challenges at John Barsby. Serving grades 8–12, the school emphasizes inclusion, student support, and safety through programs like the ERASE bullying initiative. The John Barsby Parent Advisory Council (PAC) meets regularly throughout the school year, providing parents with a strong voice in school planning and activities. The school community is also known for its creativity in fundraising — for example, the annual “Bulldogs Manure Sale,” which raises money for sports equipment and student activities, has become a fun local tradition. With ongoing community engagement, strong athletic programs, and a dedicated PAC, Barsby continues to model what it means to be a connected and caring secondary school. Of special and particular note: Barsby hosted the 15th annual district Indigenous games this year, adding traditional Coast Salish games to the events, which was a success in sportsmanship, athleticism, and enhancing local culture. My most recent visit was the 14th of this month. The principal and I walked the school and chatted with students, before observing the Bulldogs practicing for their home game that morning. After checking out some of the amazing artwork around the school, we checked in Page 2 on the construction progress for the new accessible washrooms at either end of the school. One should be completed by winter vacation and another by spring break. This facilities enhancement came directly from the students themselves though Student Voice, and it is so exciting to see students play a meaningful part in facility investment decisions. Bayview Elementary School Bayview Elementary, proudly known for its motto “A Community Paddling Together for All,” is a shining example of collaboration and inclusivity. The school serves students from kindergarten through grade 7 and fosters a welcoming environment where every child can thrive. The Bayview PAC meets monthly — with childcare and pizza provided — creating a relaxed, family-friendly way for parents to stay involved. The school also benefits from a wide range of community supports, including breakfast and snack programs, the BC Fruit & Vegetable Program, and partnerships with local organizations. Through teamwork, nutrition programs, and positive family engagement, Bayview continues to build a strong sense of belonging and community pride among students and families alike. When winter holidays approach, Bayview holds an annual "Christmas Store" where students can shop for gifts for family and friends using donated items. It's amazing to see the entire community come together to fill the library full of items each and every year-- such a highlight! Every time one visits Bayview, they are warmly welcomed by a learning aquarium, with living aquatic creatures that students interact with daily. The aquarium has many benefits from helping students regulate, to empathy building by giving some students a sense of moral obligation to care for their shared school family members. Early in the year, the principal was kind enough to give me a tour around the school, and I was impressed with the utilization of space in a growing school. Bayview is one of our older schools, about the same age as NDSS (around 70 years old) and not getting any younger. For its age, Bayview looks amazing inside with all the great art work and displays -- almost like a museum. The staff I met were all phenomenal and everywhere I looked were incredible creations by the students. Of special note is that in the staffroom there are weekly games for staff to participate in to stay connected with one another. Must make for interesting lunches! Chase River Elementary School Chase River Elementary is a growing, vibrant K–7 school nestled in Nanaimo’s south end. Known for its caring staff and active parent community, the school has made major strides in enhancing its learning environment. Recent upgrades include new flooring, washrooms, technology (iPads and Chromebooks), and an exciting new adventure playground — all helping to create engaging spaces for learning and play. Page 3 The school’s PAC meets monthly (with an accessible online option) and provides strong leadership and consistent support for school projects and events. This partnership between families and staff keeps Chase River focused on its mission of supporting every child’s success. With a forward-looking approach and a caring community, Chase River Elementary is flourishing. The district has invested capital dollars at Chase River to expand its capacity, as this is another school facing overcapacity in the face of a growing student population. I am fortunate to have known the principal for some time, as she was vice-principal the last two years at one of my previous zone schools (Randerson Ridge). The principal invited me to a very spectacular musical montage performance of popular songs throughout the decades. I was impressed by the students and staff putting together something so amazing! My latest visit just last week to Chase River allowed me to not only check in on the progress of the additional classroom space being built, but also our little sea creature friends in the living aquarium at the main entrance. The principal and I had a friendly chat and shared some moments before it was time to say our farewells. Georgia Avenue Community School Georgia Avenue Community School embodies its name — it’s truly a community school. With a wide range of programs from StrongStart to the District LifeSkills Program, the school supports learners of all abilities and backgrounds. Its emphasis on belonging and inclusion ensures that every student feels part of something special. At the beginning of my appointment to the role, Georgia Avenue's principal gave me a great tour around the school, and we sat afterwards and talked about some of the unique challenges to the school that staff are working hard to build successes out of. I have since been blessed in several other visits to the school, most recently this November with Nanaimo-Gabriola MLA and Minister of Social Development and Poverty Reduction Sheila Malcolmson, as we toured the new on-site daycare facility together. A shining star for the whole province to emulate, this was my second visit. The Georgia Avenue PAC meets regularly, offering childcare to make participation easy for families. Parent leaders, staff, and community members work hand in hand to support enrichment activities, family events, and student learning. The school’s culture of care and cooperation shines through in every aspect of school life, making Georgia Avenue a place where kindness, learning, and community spirit thrive together. Something distinctly original to the school that adds something special is that one of the first Page 4 things noticed when coming to the front doors at Georgia Avenue is instead of a bike rack with locks, there is a large and colourful bike box. My most recent visit to Georgia Avenue Community school was only a week or so ago (at time of this writing), joined by Nanaimo-Gabriola MLA and Minister of Social Development and Poverty Reduction Sheila Malcolmson. Together we toured the expansive seamless daycare facility, which has the capacity to care for pre-enrolment children of all ages until they are ready to transition to elementary school. A gem of a facility and according to the Minister, this is the out-of-the-gate template for things to come right across British Columbia. Park Avenue Elementary School Park Avenue Elementary stands out for its exceptional sense of community and family involvement. With a strong focus on hands-on learning, technology, and outdoor experiences, the school provides a well-rounded education for its K–7 students. The Park Avenue PAC plays an essential role in school life, organizing family movie nights, dances, and salad bar lunches, as well as funding technology upgrades and field trips. The school also boasts community gardens and other collaborative projects that bring students, parents, and staff together in meaningful ways. Through its teamwork, innovation, and family focus, Park Avenue Elementary continues to nurture both academic growth and community pride — making it a wonderful place to learn and grow. After bringing staff some treats (hope they like freshly baked muffins!) I spoke with some staff members as well as perhaps the newest SD68 (NLPS) staff member to the district, on her very first day. I met with the principal (volleyball under his arms) and we had a long chat getting to know one another and learning about the great many successes Park Avenue has experienced as well as some of the continuing work to improve. Probably the principal with the longest commute to work, his dedication is truly inspirational. My most recent visit to Park Avenue was just a few days ago, and this time the treats were a little more healthy (veggies and dip). The principal and I were able to meet and share some time together before I spotted a teacher doing something amazing outside: shouting out times table questions while walking around the school. P.E. and math all in one! Learning Alternatives Program In addition to my role as the trustee for the John Barsby family of schools, I also serve the Learning Alternatives Program as the trustee rep. I can’t stress enough how impactful this program is for hundreds of kids right across our school district. In fact, it is such a shining beacon of success that other districts across BC are eyeing this program to model. Most recently, SD42 (Maple Ridge & Pitt Meadows) visited our L.A. facility to learn and bring back the very best of what we do. Page 5 What is the Learning Alt. program? The Learning Alternatives (LA) program in Nanaimo, provides flexible and supportive educational options for students who have struggled in traditional school environments. Based at the former Woodlands Secondary School site, its mission is to offer “dignity, purpose, and options” to help every learner find success. The program emphasizes belonging, mastery, independence, and generosity, aligning with the First Peoples Principles of Learning. Learning Alternatives includes several specialized streams such as Senior Learning Alternatives (for Grades 8–12), Take a Hike (outdoor experiential learning and mental-health support), Intro to Trades, Ravens Lelum Young Parent Program, and FLOW (Family Learning Outreach & Wellness). These diverse offerings ensure students can pursue academic goals, vocational skills, or personal growth in a setting tailored to their needs. Recent developments include consolidating all programs at one site to improve collaboration and access to resources. The school is known for initiatives like a school farm and land-based learning projects (language, cuisine, dance, and so much more), as well as partnerships promoting digital literacy and community involvement. Overall, Learning Alternatives has become a model for inclusive, holistic education—helping Nanaimo students reconnect with learning and graduate with renewed confidence and purpose. My most recent tour around the school left me in awe with the dedicated staff who offer so much of their hearts and energy for the kids. It pleases me to say that staff have shared with me that our recent investment in school-based supports from our budgetary surplus has resulted in positive results and they expressed kudos to the board and our superintendent for out collective commitment. “A game changer” (verbatim). One of the many great sights I came across during my visit was a cozy corner upstairs where students can do their nails and learn a new skill at the same time. While stocked with polish and everything one needs to do their nails, on their wish list was another nail table to reduce the time students have to wait to get their nails done. Because of this need, I have donated a brand-new professional portable manicure table complete with workstation, pull out drawer, and interlocking wheels. Something worthy to share is the fact that instead of simply buying new portables, our school district actually builds our own learning studios. Not only that, but students at the Learning Alternatives site have a large hand in the construction. Not only do we build our own learning studios in-house, but this program creates many learning opportunities for students wishing to go into the trades upon completing high school. I would be remiss in not mentioning the creative music studio space that students are so fortunate to be able to spend time and learn in. Beyond being able to make full musical compositions, kids are able to do such things as podcast shows upstairs in a quiet space of their own. Page 6 Learning through cooking is another major aspect of the Learning Alternatives program. Beyond this (and the morning bagel station), it should be worth noting that for many of the kids that are enrolled here, this is where they receive most of their fuel for the day. To be able to support these kids on a daily basis is extraordinary. Without hyperbole nor exaggeration, It cannot be stressed enough how critical this program is to our entire district. I have a great sense that administration and staff at Learning Alternatives love their job and have immense passion in their work. This is reflected by how much support and love they radiate upon our students.
