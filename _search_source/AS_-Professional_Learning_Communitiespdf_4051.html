BOARD OF EDUCATION NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: January 29, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent, and Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) as a Professional Learning Community: Collaboration Time Rationale: This past fall, Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) began the process to fully implement our framework of Response to Intervention (RTI) in order to unite us in a collective vision for meeting our district goals: 1. The continuous improvement of instruction and assessment 2. Meeting the unique needs of each learner The RTI framework focuses on collaborative problem-solving to improve all students’ learning, and to close the achievement gaps for our struggling learners. ‘Effective schools’ research identifies that teacher collaboration, sharing of effective instructional practices, and problem solving are critical factors for improving student learning. NLPS recognizes that professional collaboration focused on student learning is critical for improving student achievement. Recently, the Ministry of Education passed Bill 36. Bill 36 calculates the instructional hours and has given some flexibility of instructional time to allow school boards to personalize student learning needs, and promote flexible scheduling options that will support the improvement of student learning. Background: This past school year, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools provided a number of opportunities for collaboration: 1) The ‘Success for All’ series of workshops provided opportunities for all staff to collectively learn together in order to improve student learning. 1 2) Many schools in Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools have created collaborative learning opportunities by revising their school timetables within the Collective Agreement, although these tend to be complicated and non-systematic across the district. 3) District collaboration has occurred throughout the Early Years to Grade 3 (E23) reading series, the District Assessment Pilot, as well as the Dr. Weber sessions with our Grades 4-12 teachers. 4) The District Leadership Series where Learning Services partnered with Human Resources to provide topics specific to leadership and learning – some of which included presentations with the Nanaimo District Teachers’ Association. Concurrently, the topic of In-Service and Collaboration Time was a discussion item at the District’s ThreeYear Calendar Professional Development Committee which consists of Trustees, NSAA President, CUPE President, NDTA President, Deputy Superintendent, and an assistant superintendent. It was determined that a sub-committee would be struck to plan, assess, and coordinate the possibility of “In-Service/ Collaboration Time” within our school system. This Professional Learning Community (PLC) sub-committee included representatives from all partner groups: NDTA President, DPAC Chairperson, NSAA representation, and Learning Services. The purpose was to build consensus related to the use of collaboration time, as well as clarify the definition of professional development versus staff development. In addition, at a local school level ‘ad hoc’ versions of collaboration time have been created and have grown out of a necessity to support student learning, however, the sustainability of these versions are in question and are very much fragile in their structure. The result of the PLC sub-committee was a proposal that was commonly understood and agreed upon by all partner groups, NDTA, CUPE, DPAC, and NSAA. Proposal: All schools would dismiss their students one hour early on Mondays when school is in session. During this time, teachers/staff would attend professional learning opportunities within a collaborative structure, focusing on district wide in-service and collaborative intervention problem solving related to student learning – both of which would be supported by the district and led by the school principals and teacher leaders. The Proposal includes: A. Three (3) Mondays this Spring 2014: March 31, April 28, and May 12 B. April/May 2014: Community Feedback Process C. Full Implementation Fall 2014-2015: Every Monday for 32 weeks Discussion: The Department of Learning Services recognizes that the shift to a Professional Learning Community model is the critical component to moving Response to Intervention to full implementation. In support of this, a system-wide collaborative team infrastructure is necessary for the sustainability of our framework. Moreover, it is ‘the essential’ to advancing district strategic goals connected to the continuous improvement of student learning. 2 The technical pieces have all been given full consideration: 1) The district will ensure that families are supported, if necessary, through afterschool program development 2) Consistent afternoon scheduling to support families with children attending different schools 3) All bussing and daily scheduling will be adjusted to accommodate this change 4) 32 weeks of one-hour in-service/collaboration time meets the instructional requirements outlined in Bill 36 5) Moving NLPS to a ‘Professional Learning Community: Collaboration Time’ will partially remove the costs associated with annual release time for system-wide professional staff in-service. Recommendation: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves the implementation of the NLPS Professional Learning Community: Collaboration time model for the spring of 2014 and the 2014-15 school year. /jlm 3
