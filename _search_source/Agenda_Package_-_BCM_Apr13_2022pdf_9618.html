Discussion The changes to the AP are mainly intended to reflect current practices with respect to enrolment in schools, fee collection etc. For instance, students are now registered through central registration. This is to ensure one centralized location to track enrolment. In addition, there are minor edits to reduce redundancy and to reference Board goals. There are also now references to “short term” program students as the program has begun to expand opportunities to increase revenues. The AP has been aligned with current timelines for the collection of fees. Perhaps most significantly, recently the ISE program, learning services, secondary principal and finance staff has met and have determined that the school incentive payment shall be limited. Schools will continue to receive support for the direct costs of international students. However, additional monies are being centralized in the ISE program. This shift will the ISE program to alleviate the administrative burden on schools for the provision of some supports. It will also provide the ISE program the flexibility to arrange additional educational supports on an as needed basis. For instance, where a school has a particular high need for additional ELL supports the program will have the flexibility to support those needs. Additionally, the program will have the funds to support growing outdoor education programs across the district, even where the ISE population may be limited. This shift will not only benefit the ISE program it will benefit all students, and particularly in schools seeking to grow their outdoor learning opportunities. Background: As the Board is aware, a capital bylaw is required under the School Act (S. 143). It is non-financial in nature and, only one bylaw is required for all capital projects approved by the Ministry. The bylaw once adopted by the Board, authorizes the Secretary Treasurer to execute project and funding agreements related to the expenditures contemplated by the underlying capital plan. The Capital Planning cycle consists of: 1. The Capital Plan preparation process starts in the spring with submissions due June 30th. During this period of time a lot of communication goes back and forth between our Facilities department and Capital Branch staff at the Ministry of Education. The Ministry of Education provided districts with a five-year Capital Plan intake spreadsheet and Capital Plan instructions in early spring. 2. The Capital Plan intake/submission process considers the following: Minor Capital: a. School Enhancement (SEP) b. Carbon Neutral Capital (CNCP) c. Bus Acquisition (BUS) d. Playground Equipment (PEP) e. Building Envelope (BEP) Page 52 of 67 Major Capital: a. Additions (EXP) b. New Schools c. Site Acquisitions d. Replacements (REP) e. Demolitions f. Seismic Mitigation (SMP) 3. Before submitting the 5 year Capital Plan for approval, a Board must approve the proposed Capital Plan for its school district by resolution. This resolution must be provided to the Ministry as part of the submission process. At this stage, a bylaw is not required. The Board approved the 2022/2023 plan in June 2021, and the Secretary-Treasurer signed the resolution. 4. Ministry staff spend the summer and fall reviewing all 60 school district submissions and they provide approval for school district plans in the spring, at around the same time as the new year in take process commences. To date, the Board has: 1. Approved the 2022/2023 5 year Capital Plan submitted to the Ministry of Education by resolution in June 2021. 2. Received the Response letter dated March 15, 2022, approving funded projects by the Ministry. The adoption of the bylaw allows the Secretary-Treasurer to sign the funding agreement which will release funding from the Ministry to the school district for the approved capital items/projects. In essence, the adoption of the bylaw approves the 5 year capital plan as approved by the Ministry of Education and permits the Secretary-Treasurer to operationalize the projects approved.
