Trustee Zone Report 25-Jan-2017 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)   Literacy Day – Today students were encouraged to dress up as their favorite author as a way to celebrate literacy. Door Decoration Winners – Mrs. Caver’s Grade 5 class took first place, while the two runner up’s were Mrs. Teneycke’s Grade 7 class & Mrs. Knight’s Grade 5/6 split class. Pictures of all the doors can be found on the new school website: http://li.schools.sd68.bc.ca Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)    Schools on Stage – Ladysmith Primary was drawn as one of the schools to participate in this Port Theatre program. The Schools on Stage program is a partnership between the school district & the Port Theatre. It’s been running for the past 15 years and it allows two to three elementary schools in the district to attend a free day at the theatre every year. More information on this year’s venue will be coming. Knitting Club – This new noon-hour club was started for grade 2 & 3 students so they could learn basic knitting stitches & work on a simple project. Once students learn the basics of knitting, it is hoped they will help teach younger students. Thanks to Mrs. Robb for volunteering to start this club. New Teacher – As part of the MoE/SD68/NDTA interim funding agreement, LPS has been approved to hire a part-time, temporary teacher to will work with existing staff to support literacy from grades K-3. Ecole North Oyster    Christmas Store – The 3rd annual Christmas store, where both English & French classes participated in selling, wrapping & directing traffic, was a success. $1,125.45 was raised, which will all go toward various grade 6/7 activities. Thanks to the students and all those that donated fabulous items for the sale & who came to purchase items for friends and family. Literacy Activities – “Reading is a Family Affair” & reading activities started on Jan 18th and will run until Feb 3rd. They include – “Read-a-Thon” where students read each day for two weeks to obtain certificates & a chance at 4 grand prizes donated by Ladysmith & District Credit Union https://www.ldcu.ca; 2nd Annual Book Swap the week of Feb 3rd; Guess the Book Character; D.E.A.R. “Drop Everything And Read” school event & culminates in a Pajama Day & celebration assembly on Friday, February 3rd. Lieutenant Governor visit – This Friday (Jan 27) the Lieutenant Governor of BC, the Honourable Judith Guichon, will be stopping by for a visit at ENO. Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  The first day of second semester is January 30th. Second semester timetables have been handed out to students last week, but as a result of the interim funding from the MoE, counsellors are rescheduling and balancing classes. This funding will provide more choices for students and reduce teacher/student ratios in several classes. Counsellors will call students who wish to make changes to their timetable in grade-descending-order, starting with grade 12s. Trustee Kimler
