BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools ACTION SHEET DATE: June 8, 2016 TO: Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent SUBJECT: New School Name for Amalgamation of Learn@home K-7 and Learn@home 8-12 BACKGROUND 1. Learn@Home started in 2003. Over the years, the school has evolved in ways that see students enrolled in the school learning in diverse ways: at home, face-to-face, cross-enrolled in our local schools, and, in some cases, out of country. As such, the school name, Learn@Home, has become somewhat of a misnomer – it only describes one small piece of our learning profile. 2. With the consolidation of Learn@Home K-7 and 8-12 under one school roof, an important decision was made to make the most of this exciting time by rebranding the school through a change in name. The goal being to select a name that is more reflective of the pedagogy and diversity of the school – a school that strives for connectedness to students face-to-face and online, and is connected to people in Nanaimo, people on Vancouver Island and to students elsewhere in the province. 3. A 4 stage process was approved in consultation with the school’s PAC, it involved: i. Stage 1 - A survey going out to the various stakeholders (students, parents, staff, administrators) asking for suggestions for a new school name that diversity of learning and learning opportunities at the school. ii. Stage 2 - Once the names were collected, the suggestions from Stage 1 were grouped into themed suggestions and a new survey was created asking the various stakeholders to vote on their top choices of the collated names.  Note – There was a gleaning process which removed names that referred to existing or pre-existing programs (Pathfinders, Aurora, etc.), referred to games or pop culture (HALO), or were inaccurate in nature (Education without Borders).  An opportunity was also given to provide new suggestions to allow for new ideas that may result from individuals the names brought forward from Stage 1. iii. Stage 3 - An 8 member selection committee made up of representatives from each stakeholder group (K-7 Coordinator, 8-12 Coordinator, NDSS Vice Principal, next year’s Principal, Staff Committee Chair, PAC Chair, Student Council, and CUPE) reviewed the votes and new suggestions from Stage 2. The committee looked for themes in the voting and considered all suggestions.    iv. Three finalist names were identified by the committee:  Island ConnectEd  Arbutus ConnectEd, and  Coastal ConnectEd Note – Due to term “ConnectEd” being chosen, the vote was delayed as the committee contacted Mr. Dale Burgos, Director of Communications, to discuss the use of the term “Connected” which is also the name of a NLPS publication. Mr. Burgos approved the use of the name. The selection committee voted on the final three names with the following results,  Island ConnectEd receiving 5 votes  Arbutus ConnectEd receiving 2 votes, and  Coastal ConnectEd receiving 1 vote Stage 4 – Requires the vetting of the selected school names by the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public School’s Board of Education. 4. Why “Island ConnectEd”? o “Island” gives a geographical reference but isn’t too limiting; we see our students as encompassing more than just Nanaimo. o “ConnectEd” refers to many important aspects of the vision of our school  We want to build a strong sense of Connectedness both with our students and teachers, and with our school partners (other high schools, dance academies etc.) in our ongoing efforts to help students be successful in their studies.  We CONNECT students with EDucation  We are connected through online studies.  ConnectEd is more indicative of our delivery than terms like online, distributed or blended. We are CONNECTED to students in a variety of ways. o The acronym of Island ConnectEd is ICE. The committee briefly discussed a shared belief that this acronym lends itself well to potential branding – the ice of Mt. Benson ... A white Spirit Bear … etc. (But … the discussion around branding will be saved for another day. It will be the next step once a school name is finalized.) o The selection committee feels “Island ConnectEd” effectively reflects our vision of our school in a unique way. RECOMMENDATION The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves the change in school name from Learn@Home to Island ConnectEd, also referred to as Island ConnectEd K-12 as per Board Policy No. 4007 and Procedure No. 4007P.
