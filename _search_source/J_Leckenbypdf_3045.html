KelseyToye Subject: FW: Barsby 2016 Cinnabar Valley kids n Bus transportation, From: James Leckenby [mailto:theieckenbys@gmai|.com] Sent: Thursday, October 01, 2015 7:50 PM To: John Blain Subject: Barsby 2016 Cinnabar Valley kids n Bus transportation. Dear Mr Blain: As someone who normally does not write or get political, it has taken me some time to compose what I would like to say to you. I have been hearing various information about Barsby next year and would like some clari?cation please. 1) Is it true that my son in grade nine who was moved to Barsby in grade 8, when Cedar closed, is consideredan out of catchment student and will have to apply to attend Barsby in grade 10, but we can't apply till spring?. (a school he has now attended for 2 years by the SD68 decision to close Cedar) kind of ironic and upsetting that he isn't considered a student of a school he has attended for 2 yrs now. And if he stays at Barsby there will be no bus from the valley. 2) My oldest who just graduated last year from Barsby, after attending Cedar for 4 yrs, and then being forced to choose between Barsby and Ladysmith for his grade 12 year. After having a difficult academic year due to move, when 90% of his friends went to Ladysmith. (he ended up being Co—Valedictorianat Barsby last year) and enjoyed the school. Cameron Also was affected in grade 7- 8 (6yrs ago) at Cinnabar when the catchment was changed and half his friends went to Cedar and the rest to Barsby. 3) Now my daughter who is in grade 7 at Cinnabar will have to choose at the end of this year (again half her friends are going to Barsby because siblings do and half to Cedar, with some to Ladysmith). I just can't get my head around how so many Cinnabar family's are being affected for the 3rd time in 6 years when so many other areas of the city are not. It is a very unfair burden for these kids who have been disrupted multiple times as has the community. I can understand the frustration of all involved and we will be attending the meeting on Nov 4th to voice our Views. It is my wish that, (it is all for the kids) as we keep getting told repeatedly by SD 68 trustees and staff that they actually will have discussion and seriously consider input from parents and students, and not be a forgone conclusion. I attended many of the meetings when Cedar was closed ( and it was a foregone conclusion) despitewhat we kept being told. I see that through the efforts and communicationoutput of the Save Cedar trustee's and some valley parents, this has enabled them to get the school re—openedand bus next year for Ladysmith. However please not again at the disruption of the kids attending John Barsby, who were made to go their by SD 68 decision. We are all prepared to be vocal to save Barsby and the bus for our kids. My son has actively become involved in band (no band at Cedar) and they are planning a band trip in 2017. I appreciate you taking the time Mr Blain to read this letter and understand it is a difficult situation for SD 68. However it is hard to explain to my children why our family and others like us in the Cinnabar valley / Chase river area are being constantly affected by SD 68 Trustee decisions. 1 Please feel free to contact me if you have any questions regarding my letter for clari?cation, Regards, Jamie Leckenby, a concerned Cinnabar / John Barsby parent.
