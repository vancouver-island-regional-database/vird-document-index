Mentorship Committee Final Report 2025–2026 • • • • • • • • • The Mentorship Committee met a total of five times during the 2025–2026 school year. Early in the year, the committee focused on revising the mentorship application and ensuring it was easily accessible online. The committee approved and funded four delegates to attend the Teacher Mentorship British Columbia Mentorship Matters Conference in Vancouver in October. The purpose of sending delegates was to gain insight into mentorship programs in other districts and bring back relevant learning and ideas to inform our local work. During the year, it became apparent that funding levels in both the BCPSEA Grant and the NDTA Mentorship Fund were declining at unsustainable rates. In response, the committee reduced the number of release days available per application from four days to two days. Applicants remained eligible to reapply, with requests for additional days considered on a case-by-case basis. We had a total of 81 mentorships. As at March 2026, there was $127, 999 remaining in the BCPSEA grant (provincially bargained), and $36, 035 in the NDTA Prior Year Remedy account. At the request of the NDTA Executive Committee (EC), the Mentorship Committee developed a policy and procedures document to guide the management of future funding and applications. This work involved reviewing documents from other locals, discussing draft versions, and revising the document through multiple meetings. The final policy and procedures were approved by the EC in April. With the approval of the new policy and procedures, there is no longer a need for a standing Mentorship Committee with a designated chairperson. Instead, the policy outlines the creation of an ad hoc committee at the beginning of each school year to consult with and support the Professional Issues Officer regarding the annual mentorship budget and funding priorities. The policy also prioritizes funding for teachers within their first eight years of practice, while allowing for a limited number of applications from teachers with more than eight years of experience, depending on available funding. Committee members, past and present, gathered for a final meeting to celebrate and acknowledge the work accomplished over the past four years.
