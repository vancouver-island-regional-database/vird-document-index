CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Ian Kalina, Executive Director Boys & Girls Clubs of Central Vancouver Island RE: Creating L:icensed Child Care Spaces in SD68 Ian Kalina, Executive Director of the Boys and Girls Club of Central Vancouver Island spoke to the Committee about the possibility of the school district working with the Boys and Girls Club to build dedicated space for community before and after school care programs. He said the Boys and Girls Club is the largest licensed child care provider on Vancouver Island, and that they would like to see its relationship with the school district evolve from a renter of childcare space to the “preferred” partner in the planning and development of licensed child care at district schools. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held September 18, 2013 NEW BUSINESS Five-Year Capital Plan Mr. Turin told the Committee that the 2013-14 Capital Plan, in the amount of $119.5 million, outlines the district’s requests for capital projects for new school construction, renovations, seismic upgrades, building envelope projects and buses. This year the plan reflects the priorities set by the Board when it approved its 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Howland Folio BC/13/09/18-05 That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that the 2013-2014 FiveYear Capital Plan in the amount of $119,510,063 be approved and submitted to the Ministry of Education. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Budget/Actual Forecasts Critical Dates Mr. Turin reviewed the critical dates for budget and forecasts over the next three years. He did advise that the change in committee meeting dates will have some impact on the financial information for the November Business Committee meeting. Enrolment Update Mr. Turin said the elementary enrolment numbers are up slightly over the forecast. The district receives funding protection for enrolment changes, therefore, there will not be a financial impact from any enrolment increase. Print Shop Update Mr. Roberts, Assistant Secretary-Treasurer told the Committee that effective July 1, the Print Shop is recovering its full costs of labour and material. Our accounting/invoicing system allows us to collect data as of July 1st. He said he will provide further updates as more information is gathered. Draft Budget Calendar - 20142015 Mr. Turin said the budget calendar in the Agenda is a dynamic schedule for the upcoming budget year. These are the dates for the regular and Special Business Committee meetings for the Spring. These meetings are subject to change. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held September 18, 2013 John Barsby - Update on Preparation for Intake of Students from Cedar Secondary Mr. Hutchinson told the Committee about the transition work that will be done over the coming year as several schools undergo major changes. He said that there will be School Transition Working Groups to help plan the transition of Cedar students to John Barsby; Junior and Senior Learning Alternate students to NDSS; and the expansion of Ladysmith Secondary to a Grade 7 -12 school with French Immersion. BC Education Learning Plan Mr. Hutchinson told the Committee about the district’s plan for the expenditure of the B.C. Education Plan budget. The Ministry is providing a grant of $264,694 for SD68. The grant is expected to support literacy and skills and trades. The grant will support initiatives for self-regulated learning; early learning to Grade 3 and skills and trades.
