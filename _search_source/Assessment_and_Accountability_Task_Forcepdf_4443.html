THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: June 17, 2011 TO: The Board of Education FROM: Chris Southwick, Assistant Superintendent, on behalf of the Assessment and Accountability Task Force SUBJECT: Assessment and Accountability Task Force Recommendations BACKGROUND Two Board of Education motions have provided direction to the work of the Task Force on Assessment and Accountability. R09/09/30-22 That the Board of Education of School district 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to establish a District task force on assessment and accountability. R11/01/26-28 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests that the Assessment and Accountability Task Force make recommendations on how the district can assess achievement and progress of both student cohorts and specific programs or initiatives. In addition to these motions, the Trustees clearly indicated that the work of this Task Force must include the voices of teachers as expressed through the NDTA, as well as other partner groups as appropriate. DISCUSSION For the first year of the Task Force, three members of management participated in this work; two Assistant Superintendents and the District Principal of Learning and Instructional Support. NDTA members included the President and Vice-president of the NDTA and the Coordinator of Literacy, Learning and Instructional Support. During the second year of the Task Force, management representatives included the District Principal of Aboriginal Programs to replace the District Principal of Learning and Instructional Support; and the NDTA vicepresident elections changed those representing the NDTA on the Task Force. In late spring of the current year, the local President of the District Parent Advisory Council was included in the group to represent the voice of parents. Following extensive conversations, the Task Force has agreed upon the following statements of belief:    The purpose of the assessment must be clear, transparent and respected; Both formative and summative assessment practices are necessary to support student learning; The summative structures of the BC Performance Standards and report cards are key indicators at the school and district level and should be utilized to assess achievement and progress of students and specific programs and initiatives. In addition, the Task Force agreed upon the following recommendations to be forwarded to the Board of Education for its consideration. The Task Force recommends that:  The District focus on formative assessment as a necessary process to support learning in SD68;  The District facilitate ongoing purposeful collaboration in both schools and the district to support appropriate assessment and accountability, and,  The District provide resources to continue to communicate and collaborate in order to build a shared understanding of assessment and evaluation and to support the learning process for all stakeholders in SD68. RECOMMENDATION It is recommended: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to implement the following recommendations and to provide an update to the November 2011 Education Committee on progress to date:  The District focus on formative assessment as a necessary process to support learning in SD68;  The District facilitate ongoing purposeful collaboration in both schools and the district to support appropriate assessment and accountability, and,  The District provides resources to continue to communicate and collaborate in order to build a shared understanding of assessment and evaluation, and to support the learning process for all stakeholders in SD68.
