Bill Robinson’s Trustee Zone Report Everything in my Zone seems to be flowing smoothly. I excitement remains at a high level as four different men are continuing to turn out to two of my PACs and the Barsby staff and community are even more excited as they prepare to welcome Jane Reynolds as their new principal Our Reconciliation Committee has been very busy during the last month or so. We have made 16 personal consultations with various partners and many committee plus other meetings. I have attended all of those meetings. Best Me I Can Be – I am expecting to have at least 20 new teachers doing the program this year in both French and English. As a result of a meeting with Joan Brown, one of our Indigenous Knowledge Keepers, and an upcoming meetings with our Indigenous Coordinators, I am looking forward to finding unique ways to indigenize the program. I’ll have more information next month. Young writers! This spring Literacy Central Vancouver Island is offering a FREE writing workshop for writers aged 15 - 30. This is a multi-genre workshop with an emphasis on short story and fiction writing. Weekly meetings will include discussion of the elements of storytelling, writing activities, and workshopping of participant stories. The workshop will be led by Greg Brown, Nanaimo-based writer and faculty member at the University of Virginia's Young Writers Workshop and the Creative Writing for Children Society in Vancouver. To sign up, or to find out more information, please contact Greg at gdb.brown@gmail.com. Premier’s Awards for Excellence in Education – I have been encouraging everyone I connect with in the district to spend time thinking of employees who deserve this award and I encourage you to do the same. I have added a detailed description of the process below. Georgia Accessible Playground - We have been working on this since shortly after the McGirr Accessible Playground was officially opened. With support of $110,000 from the Ministry plus local funding, Pete Sabo recently assured me that we will see work beginning, if not finished, this school year. Nanaimo Foundation Community Grants - The Nanaimo Foundation has launched a Neighbourhood Small Grants program in the Harewood community and they are looking for residents to get involved. Neighbourhood Small Grants range from $50 to $500. These grants are awarded to individuals, rather than to charitable organizations. Projects can be anything from block parties, to potlucks, art making, storytelling, neighbourhood walks or cleanups, invasive plant removal, scavenger hunts, clothing or book swaps and much more. I have been asked to review the grant applications and to help make decisions about which ones are funded. I am also doing my best to promote this opportunity through our schools in the area.
