V9G 1A2 Municipal Hall (250) 245-6400  Fax (250) 245-6411  info@ladysmith.ca  www.ladysmith.ca April 17, 2013 Mr. Jamie Brennan Chair, Board of Education School District 68 Via e-mail c/o Mr. Phil Turin, Secretary-Treasurer Dear Mr. Brennan and Trustees: I am writing with the support of Council of the Town of Ladysmith regarding the proposed changes to Ecole Davis Road School. Having also had the opportunity to speak about this matter with SecretaryTreasurer Phil Turin, I understand and appreciate the challenges involved with your budget deliberations and the necessity to make difficult choices when revenues are declining. However, I wish to express both my concern and extreme disappointment over the process undertaken by the School District in regards to the removal of the English stream from Ecole Davis Road School. Although this was announced at a School Board meeting on April 10, it is my understanding that the full proposal was literally dropped on the community on Monday, April 15th, with an expectation that individuals were required to register with School District staff within less than 48 hours in order to have a "one time only" opportunity to address the Board of School Trustees regarding this matter on Thursday, April 18th. The community/school/children's reaction? Anger, outrage, disbelief, anxiety, fear, and tears. Obviously, this has not been an appropriate model for public engagement. This proposal is no small decision. Although the school will continue to operate, this proposal would in effect close a neighbourhood school for some one hundred and ten (2013/2014) children who do not enrol in the French immersion program. This is a neighbourhood school that has served this portion of our community for over five decades. I ask, is this the appropriate public consultation process for the closure of a neighbourhood school? On behalf of the Council of the Town of Ladysmith, I respectfully request that the Board of School Trustees table consideration of this proposal and facilitate further community discussion on this matter. Sincerely, Rob Hutchins, Mayor Town of Ladysmith
