THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations DATE: December 14, 2011 SUBJECT: Consultation on School Boundary/Catchment Changes References   Proposed changes to catchment area – information sheets  Coal Tyee to Uplands and Rock City  Chase River to Park Avenue  Pleasant Valley to Mountain view  Forest park to Mountain View  Quarterway to Mountain view Action Sheet and attachments dated March 9, 2011 Background At the March 30, 2011 Board meeting the following motion was passed: R11/03/30-13 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to proceed with the consultation process for the recommended boundary adjustments and return to the Board of Education with recommendations for final boundary adjustments after the consultation process is complete. Subsequent to the above motion, Facilities staff worked with Donna Reimer, Director of Communications, to draft information sheets for affected parents and plan an open house meeting which was held on November 28, 2011. Staff reviewed the proposed boundaries listed in the Action Sheet to verify that the catchment boundaries were accurately represented. Resources included:  The School District 68 catchment map  Davenport Mapping information  City of Nanaimo electronic map  Google Earth mapping; and  Cadastral information from the provincial government’s web site s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\9 - december 14, 2011\school boundary changes december 2011.docx The Information Sheet for each boundary change (attached) was distributed to schools and sent home with students at the affected schools on November 21, 2011. The Communications Department made this information available on the District web site and though the news media. In response to the information that was sent home, two e-mailed questions with respect to the proposed catchment changes were received by the Communications Office. Both were essentially unrelated to the proposed boundary changes. The November 28th catchment open house (6:00-8:00 pm at the District Board Room) was hosted by Pete Sabo, Brent Spohn and Donna Reimer. No members of the public attended. The following information was provided:  Poster boards with maps  Survey forms  Original catchment action sheet  District catchment map  Details of boundaries changes and key areas that require more definition  City of Nanaimo’s version of school district catchment map  News release on November 21, 2011  The proposed boundary changes that were sent home on November 21st Clarification on specific portions of certain catchment areas was provided:  Chase River to Park Avenue: south-east portion of the catchment  Chase River to Park Avenue: west portion of the catchment  Pleasant Valley to Mountain View: north portion of the catchment In the Park Avenue-Chase River area one portion is commercial development and another is an undeveloped residential area. In the Pleasant Valley-Mountain View area a portion is currently a wooded area. Staff have attempted to follow property boundaries; however, in undeveloped areas it is difficult to foresee how development will proceed. When development does proceed, staff will be able to advise regarding school attendance questions arising out of the boundary changes being approved today. Additional detailed maps were available at the open house to illustrate these boundary changes. Although boundary definition in these areas is more challenging, this is not critical as there is no student population affected. The remaining boundary changes were straight forward and follow existing boundaries and/or natural features. Discussion All the information provided at the open house on November 28th is presented at this Board Meeting. Staff are willing to discuss any details regarding the changes being recommended. Staff face ongoing challenges regarding the mapping of catchment areas and will continue working to further refine catchment areas. Although catchment mapping to date has been accurate for District purposes, it is staff’s intention to move towards more accurate and consistent mapping as cost effective technology and staffing levels allow. The recommendations are made in accordance with Policy 3022. The recommended adjustments reflect realistic attendance patterns, support existing choices by families, clean up issues from the Parkway s:\ea-ck\2011-2012\agendas\board regular\9 - december 14, 2011\school boundary changes december 2011.docx construction and affect few students. Staff will grandfather existing student affected by the changes. The new boundaries will take effect for September of 2012. The following motions are presented to the Board to enable staff to put in place the boundary changes consulted upon. Resulting actions will include:  Notify affected schools of changes to their boundaries,  Produce new catchment maps for school use  Notify Baragar Demographics and the City of Nanaimo of changes affecting their work  Provide new District maps (with revisions) to District users  Update catchment files for future use.
