From Other Committees Page 10 of 13 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held November 24, 2010 SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT 10 Mr. Munro reported on a number of events throughout the district during the past month. •District 68 Teachers Lynn Brown and Laura Tait were recognized for their presentation at an Aboriginal Education workshop in West Vancouver recently. •As part of Wellington Secondary’s Halloween for Hunger program, the school collected 1,183 food items. That amount will be matched by Shaw Cable and donated to local food banks. Wellington is continuing its campaign to address issues of poverty by sponsoring a Christmas hamper drive. •Grade 11 Chemistry students from Cedar Community Secondary are once again this year participating in a national contest for growing crystals. •Dover Bay AAAA senior girls’ volleyball team and the Cedar A senior girls volleyball team are both heading to the provincial finals after winning their Island tournaments. The NDSS AAA senior girls team finished third at the Island tournament and will need to win a wildcard to advance to the provincial finals. This is particularly exciting for Dover Bay since they are hosting the AAAA Girls Volleyball Provincial Tournament this year on December 1-4. •High school wrestling in Nanaimo recently kicked off the 2010-11 season with the Hubbub Takedown Tournament. •The Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation reports several successful initiatives over the past few months. •Thanks to Principal John Blain and staff at Dover Bay for providing, on short notice, a warm, safe shelter for McGirr Elementary students when that school was evacuated on Monday, due to a suspected gas leak. •School District 68 is pleased to announce the first issue of its new magazine for parents – ConnectED. This magazine will go home with elementary students and be available in secondary schools and in a variety of community locations and online. Thanks to a partnership with Family Matters Publications and the support of advertisers, this publication is being produced at no cost to the district. Mr. Munro also provided the Board with an Page 11 of 13 Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held November 24, 2010 11 updated report on the organization of classes in the district, as of October 30.
