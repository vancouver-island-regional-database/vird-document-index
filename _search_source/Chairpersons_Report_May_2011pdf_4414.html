SD 68 Chairperson’s Report Sharon Welch May, 2011 May turned out to be another very busy month! This month, I visited schools in the Ladysmith, Wellington and Learning Alternatives families, as well as more from the NDSS and Woodlands families. Ladysmith Intermediate Principal Cathal Walsh talked about the strong fine arts programs including music, art, and drama. He was very pleased with the school’s new band program. We also discussed his restitution model for discipline, which has resulted in a 90% decrease in out-of-school suspensions. Davis Road Principal Doug English highlighted student achievement in the Odyssey program, which focuses on creative thinking and problem solving. Five teams of Grade 3-7 students participated. One team won the regionals. Three other teams progressed to the provincial competition and placed 2nd, 3rd, and 7th. Congratulations! Ladysmith Primary Principal Parmjit Parmar talked about the great environment and supportive PAC at his school. He was very happy with his school’s close relationship with Ladysmith Secondary. The Grade 8 students were coming to help the primary students make Mother’s Day gifts, and 10-15 high school mentors (Grades 11-12) visit the primary school twice each week. Ladysmith Secondary Principal Dave Street provided a beautiful lunch, prepared by Red Seal Chef Holly Knox for Trustee Neary and I. We discussed significant environmental work done by the Green Team, and we went for a tour that highlighted the amazing transformation of the skateboard park to include a BMX bike area. Congratulations to the improv team for placing 8th out of 300 teams in Canada! Brechin Principal Diane Goodman talked about many upcoming events, such as track and field, swimming lessons twice each week for May and June, the Grade 7 wrap-up activities, and field trips. The school’s goal of increasing literacy has involved guided reading, a special intermediate reading program, and various special events. North Oyster Principal Roger Douglas talked about the strong fitness and music programs (including Grades 5-7 Band) at his school. He highlighted literacy work, First Nations activities, the swim program, and the Family Life program. Principal Douglas would also love to offer a StrongStart program at North Oyster. Cilaire Principal Mike Ireland highlighted the Accelerated Reading Program that contributes to the school’s literacy goal. Students read and earn points that reflect their comprehension of the materials. Last year, they read 9,000 books. This year, they have been challenged with reading 10,000 books. If they meet this goal, they can shave Principal Ireland’s head! Now that’s an incentive! Rock City Principal Lillian Carefoot talked about many events that the students were participating in this year: concerts, the parent tea, the year-end school visit to Rathtrevor Beach, the Earth Day neighbourhood clean-up, and the Art Club supported by two Wellington students. I also chatted with several staff members about their concerns and ideas for improvement. Coal Tyee Acting Principal Robbie Dhillon gave me a tour of the school, and then I had the opportunity to talk with several staff members in the lunch room. I enjoyed learning about many school initiatives and events and getting to know the front-line staff better. Uplands Principal Peter Skipper talked about the upcoming Fine Arts night. He explained that all Uplands students participate in Grade 5 Band, and then they choose between Grades 6 and 7 Band and Visual Art/Drama. Principal Skipper highlighted the school’s gardening club and the UPS Awards Program. Learning Alternatives District Principal Scott Saywell talked about the success of the Career Technical Centre (CTC) – he mentioned that many of the students have jobs before they are finished school (heavy duty mechanics, hair dressers, etc.). He also highlighted the 2,232 courses taken through the Learn@Home program. NDSS Acting Vice-Principal Cheryl Garrett promoted the student production called “Night of the Dance” in late May. She also highlighted the Fine Arts programs, the $4,000 raised in the “Ride to Conquer Cancer” initiative, and “Change for Change” project. Wellington Secondary Principal Steve Thompson, and Vice-Principals Carol Girard, and Tom Mason shared information about the success of their music programs, including Steve Jones’ award from the City . The art program is also flourishing. They are looking forward to concerts, a Fun Day with Grade 7 students, and their awards ceremonies. Departure Bay Principal Lisa Frey talked about the school’s guided reading program and the Costco Readers’ Program that involves Costco staff coming into the school with booklets and literacy activities for the students. I also had the opportunity to talk to staff about their concerns and ideas. Hammond Bay Principal Francine John talked about the school’s literacy goal. She mentioned that parents help with guided reading and literacy circles. Principal John also highlighted the school’s buddy system, where younger students are partnered with those who are 4 Grades higher (K & 4, 1 & 5, 2 & 6, 3 & 7). Over the next couple of weeks, I will be finishing my school visits with the remainder of the NDSS and Woodlands families and the Junior and Senior Learning Alternative schools. In addition to my school visits, I had the opportunity to attend the BCPSEA Conference in Richmond with Trustee Nelson Allen, Superintendent Michael Munro, and Secretary-Treasurer Phil Turin. A series of discussions about 21st Century Learning and bargaining updates filled the two days. I also attended the DRC’s Open House with many interesting displays of resources available for our classrooms. I ended up seeing stars with Shelley Beleznay in the Starlab. What a great event! Thank you to all of our staff for enhancing our students’ educational experiences!
