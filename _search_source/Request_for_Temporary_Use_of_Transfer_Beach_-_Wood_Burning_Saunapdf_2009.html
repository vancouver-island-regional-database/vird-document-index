STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Parks, Recreation & Culture Department October 7, 2025 3340-01 Temporary Use of Transfer Beach Park Space – Wood Burning Sauna RECOMMENDATION: That Council: 1. Deny Wildwood Saunas’ request to operate a mobile wood-burning sauna business at Transfer Beach for the on-going use of public park space; 2. Affirm that the use of public space by a commercial business that uses a wood burning appliance be restricted to special events or activities, contingent upon the business holding a valid Town of Ladysmith or Inter-Community Business License; and 3. Direct staff to amend “Town of Ladysmith Parks Usage Bylaw, 2017, No. 1933” to include provisions that permit the short-term use of mobile wood burning appliances in designated park areas for approved special events. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Wildwood Saunas has submitted a request to operate a mobile wood-burning sauna at Transfer Beach Park. This proposal introduces a unique recreational offering that aligns with growing public interest in wellness experiences. However, it also raises questions about the use of public spaces for commercial activities involving wood-burning appliances for long-term use. To support informed decision-making, staff have reviewed responses from other Vancouver Island municipalities regarding similar operations, particularly in the context of the Inter-Community Business Licence (ICBL) program. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: N/A INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: The Town of Ladysmith has received a request to have a commercial business operate a mobile wood-fired sauna unit this winter on weekends at Transfer Beach until May 2026. It is important to note that the Town of Ladysmith participates in the Inter-Community Business Licence (ICBL) program, which streamlines business licensing across multiple jurisdictions and allows eligible businesses to operate in participating communities without needing separate licenses. As a result, requests such as this one are often submitted to multiple municipalities. During a regional meeting with Finance staff, it was noted that similar requests had also been received by regional partners and other municipalities on Vancouver Island, prompting the following responses:  Esquimalt permits wood-burning saunas only on private property or as part of a special event permit when hosted on public land.  Victoria has approved similar requests but noted instances of unauthorized use of public spaces by sauna operators, despite initial assurances that operations would be confined to designated areas.  Sidney expressed no concerns with this type of use but did not state whether formal approval had been granted.  Nanaimo approved a similar operation for private locations only, with fire department oversight and compliance with commercial standards. The request from Wildwood Saunas is to provide a unique mobile wellness experience by offering traditional wood-fired sauna sessions that are booked online. The request is to operate at Transfer Beach Park until May of 2026 using the park on Saturdays and Sundays. This request presents both opportunities and challenges. On one hand, mobile sauna services can enhance the recreational experience at the waterfront and support local wellness tourism. On the other hand, the use of wood-burning appliances in public spaces raises concerns related to:  Fire safety, particularly during dry seasons or in proximity to natural vegetation.  Air quality and emissions, especially in high-use public areas.  Public access and equity, as commercial use may limit availability of space for general park users.  Enforcement and monitoring, given the potential for operators to deviate from approved locations or conditions. Feedback from other municipalities suggests a cautious approach, with most permitting operations only on private property or under special event permits. The City of Victoria’s experience with unauthorized use of public land highlights the importance of clear conditions and enforcement capacity. Transfer Beach Park is a high-profile, multi-use public space with significant community value. Any commercial activity must be carefully managed to ensure alignment with Town policies, public expectations, and environmental stewardship. Staff are recommending that the operation of the mobile sauna units be permitted only on private property or in public spaces during special events, consistent with the approach taken by several other municipalities and amend applicable bylaws accordingly for Council’s approval. This option balances support for local business with the need to protect public space, minimize risk, and ensure equitable access to Transfer Beach Park. Staff are recommending amending applicable bylaws to reflect this recommendation. Should Council choose to pursue any of the proposed alternatives for longer term use of the park space, the use or expansion of commercial activity must continue to address key considerations, including fire safety, emissions, equitable access to public space, and the overall enjoyment of the park setting. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Permit the operation under a Temporary Use Permit. 2. Permit the operation only on private property with a current ICBL or Town of Ladysmith Business License. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: N/A LEGAL IMPLICATIONS: Ladysmith currently has no restrictions or bylaws that prohibit this type of use in public open spaces, such as parks. Staff are recommending to only permit wood-burning mobile units and type of use during special events. CITIZEN/PUBLIC RELATIONS IMPLICATIONS: This use on a regular basis has the potential to impact the air quality, emissions and overall park enjoyments in the immediate vicinity, especially in high-use public areas. INTERDEPARTMENTAL INVOLVEMENT/IMPLICATIONS: If Council wishes to permit this use, Ladysmith Fire Rescue will be required to inspect the units and PR&C would be required to provide oversight and navigate any negative impacts on the park and those visiting.
