CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Michelle Cruickshank, Manager Community Development for Special Olympics BC Michelle Cruikshank from Special Olympics British Columbia made a presentation about their program. This program provides high-quality programs and competition opportunities that help people with intellectual disabilities celebrate personal achievement. The organization has several youth programs, starting with a focus on developing basic motor skills, and progressing to sport skill development and sport training. Special Olympics BC said they would like to work with the district to develop and implement unique physical activity programs for students with special needs. The Education Committee is recommending that staff be asked to consider starting Special Olympics programs in schools. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Saunders IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Brennan Folio E14/11/05-03 Dr. Lloyd Mah, Chair Eye See..Eye Learn BC Association of Optometrists That the Special Olympics presentation be moved to New Business. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Dr. Lloyd Mah, spoke to the Education Committee about the Eye See . . . Eye Learn program, which is sponsored by the B.C. Association of Optometrists. This program is designed to help remove barriers to children’s academic and developmental success by providing diagnosis of eye and vision problems at an early age. The program encourages families of Kindergarten students to visit an optometrist for an eye exam. The exam and any glasses prescribed are at no cost, either through the parents’ insurance or through the program. Currently the program is being promoted in six school districts in B.C. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Deputy-Superintendent's Report Mr. Blain provided the Education Committee with a report on class sizes in schools this fall. He said that all of the 148.5 intermediate class divisions in the district have 30 or fewer students. All 174.5 primary divisions also meet all class size requirements. At the secondary level, 792.5 classes have 30 or fewer students, with 40 classes exceeding 30 students because they have been organized on a rationale of program support, for such classes as band, theatre, or distance learning, as allowed by class size legislation.
