What to expect when the Board of Education hears your appeal under the School Act: The Board of Education hears appeals at the Board of Education Office – 395 Wakesiah Ave, Nanaimo BC, V9R 3K6. What happens at an appeal: • The Board Chair will start the hearing with a “Call to order”. This formally begins the meeting. • The Board Chair will begin introductions. Members of the Board of Education (school trustees) will be present, in addition to relevant school district staff. (For example: Superintendent, Assistant Superintendent, Director of Instruction.) • The Board Chair will then confirm with you what you are appealing, and why you are making this appeal. • You will then have 15 minutes to present your reasons for the appeal. • District staff will then have the opportunity to present information to the Board. • The Board Chair will then adjourn (pause) the meeting. • You will be asked to step out of the meeting. • District staff, not including the superintendent, will also step out. • The Board will determine if they require further clarification from yourself or staff. • You and the district staff will then be asked to return, and the Board may ask questions. • You will then be offered an opportunity to provide any closing remarks. • You and district staff, not including the superintendent, will then leave. • The Board will then discuss your appeal and make a decision. • You will be sent a letter with the Board of Education’s decision from the office of the Secretary Treasurer within a reasonable timeframe.
