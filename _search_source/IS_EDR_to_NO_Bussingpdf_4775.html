THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: February 18, 2014 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Bussing to North Oyster for Davis Road French Immersion Students On February 6, 2014, the Board of Education approved the following motion: Relocate the Davis Road K-7 French Immersion program to North Oyster Elementary, North Oyster becomes a dual track school September 2014. The District will explore transportation options, for a specific transitional period, including a user pay system. Staff recognizes that Davis Road parents are waiting to make their decisions about their children’s placements for next year until they know whether there will be bus service to North Oyster. The purpose of offering transportation would be to allow students currently in French Immersion at Davis Road to complete their program. Having reviewed the situation, staff believes that bus transportation can be provided for current Davis Road French Immersion students to North Oyster. In order to provide this service, current bus routes 10, 11 and 12 would be altered. This would enable these bus routes to provide service from the Davis Road/Ladysmith area to North Oyster. There will be some morning timing changes on the routes as a result of the added service, with some students being picked up earlier than they are currently. Also, please note that some Davis Road French Immersion students using the bus service to North Oyster will be on the bus longer than 30 minutes and this will not conform to Board Policy 4400P(2). The proposed new morning schedules are attached. The cost would be approximately $12,000 per year to increase bus driver time and mileage. This cost will rise annually with the increase of wages, fuel prices etc. Please note that if the Board proposed to add new bus routes rather than revise existing routes, the cost would rise to $280,000 bus purchase costs and a yearly operating cost of $90,000. Staff is proposing that the bus service be provided for current French Immersion students only; all other bus service would be provided as per Board Policy. Courtesy riders would be permitted if space permits (as per Board Policy 4400P) but courtesy bus stops would not be implemented. English program students who are siblings of students attending the French program would be given priority for any available courtesy space. Kulleet Bay would continue to remain an option area (the North Oyster option area for Stz’uminus First Nation allows students to attend Ladysmith Primary and Intermediate Schools or North Oyster Elementary School). 1 Staff are not recommending a user-pay option. Because of the logistics in implementing a userpay system, it is staff’s belief that if a user-pay system were to be implemented, it should only be considered as a district-wide initiative. RECOMMENDED MOTION: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (NanaimoLadysmith), utilizing existing transportation routes, provides school bus transportation services from stops at Davis Road Elementary, Ladysmith Primary and Ladysmith Intermediate to North Oyster Elementary for students currently enrolled in French Immersion at Davis Road Elementary. The service will be offered on a transition basis until June 2021, with parents receiving annual reminders that the service will be discontinued after that date.
