BFW:Letter to The Board of Education Re: Bus Servicefrom Lower Lantzvilleto Dover Bay Secondary From: Ian and Cristi Lundman [mailto:iclundman@shaw.ca] Sent: Monday, April 25, 2016 2:44 PM To: Kelsey Toye Cc: Communications ; Tania Brzovic Subject: Letter to The Board of Education Re: Bus Service from Lower Lantzvilleto Dover Bay Secondary Dear Board of Education, I have serious concerns about the removal of bus services for many of the children living in Lower Lantzvilleand attending Dover Bay Secondary. In our situation, we live a 4.2 km walk or drive from Dover Bay, so my children are considered courtesy riders. In orderto walk to Dover Bay, they must start at least an hour before they need to be at the school. This means beginning theirjourney in the dark, during the winter months. The shortest route is up DickinsonRd, out of Lantzville.Dickinson has no sidewalksthrough Lantzville and inadequate street lighting for safety of kids on the road in the dark or dusky hours. The walk up Dickinson Road is also a very tough one as the hill is very steep. This in turn, tends to lend to drivers increasingtheir speed coming down the hill. It is not a route I have every considered safe or been comfortable with my children walking or biking. In inclement weather, as we often experience in the winter months, the safety issues are compounded by decreased visibility. A Public transit does not provide adequate service through Lantzville,to get kids down Dickinson Road after school. They must wait a couple of hours in order to catch an appropriate bus. Bus passes for two students will cost $82 each month. Driving our students to and from school is not a feasible option due to our work schedules, although I believe this is the option most families will choose after their bus service is removed. It is not reasonable to expect kids to walk over four kilometers to and from school, particularly up and down a steep hillthat does not have the necessary attributes to maintain pedestrian safety. Most of us would not walk this distance to and from work on a regular basis. This means there will be more cars on the road headed to Dover, further degrading the safety of the situation for our student pedestrians. There is also an issue with major traffic congestion around Dover Bay Secondary, in the mornings, particularly on McGirr Road. greener and more sustainable, has theimpact of more vehicles on the road been In the school district's quest to become considered? Most students who lose bus service WILLbe driven to and fromschool. I also have concerns about the impact on school attendance for students who lose bus service. For students from families who may not have the means or the desire to fully support their child getting to school, absenteeism will likely rise as students will not have an accessible means of transportation. Has a paid ridership system been considered for all bus students? What about the real life feasibility of the distances considered appropriate for students to walk? In short, I donot consider the bus service from Lower Lantzvilleto Dover Bay Secondary to be a frill.It is a service essential to protect the safety of our students, to ensure attendance, and to promote environmentally sustainable school access. I will happily walk the route with any interested parties. I invite you to join me, to load up backpacks with books, and test out the feasibility of the walk up Dickinson Road, from Lower Lantzvilleto Dover Bay School, for our high school students. Sincerely, Cristi Lundman i
