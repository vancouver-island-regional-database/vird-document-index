Reference source not found.: Re: Elimination of two community school positions Thank you for the opportunity to comment on the proposal to eliminate two Community School Coordinator positions in the upcoming school district budget. As you are probably aware, the Harewood Neighbourhood Association, (formerly the Harewood Project Society), was the ‘founding’ community group that enabled the School District #68 to apply for government funding for the Harewood Family of Community Schools. This long time relationship gives us insight into the workings of the community school programs. In fact we continue to work in partnership with community schools providing funding for the staff and most materials for thesummer parks program at Centennial Park that includes recreation, literacy components and food for children 6-12. Since 1997, HNA has again and again presented before and provided information to the school board re community school programs. It was May 15th and 24rd 2019 that we last wrote to the school board, re proposed changes in the delivery of community school programs. From that letter let me quote. “It is indeed too bad the original school board policy re ongoing evaluation of community schools was not carried out, as we were led to believe it would.” There is a good reason to mention that quote. Again, it appears promises that were made to and by the school board have not been kept. 1. The School Board passed a motion on May 29th, 2019, which required community consultation re proposed changes to community schools. “ The Board will maintain Community Schools for another year, and any proposed changes to be made in consultation with various partner groups.” As you are considering cutting two of the five Community School coordinator positions, can we ask if you have consulted partner groups regarding this proposal?
