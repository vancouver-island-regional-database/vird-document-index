At June 30, 2019 this equates to a range of $660,860 $1,321,721. Staff recommend restricting $375,000 to support the start-up of the Qwam Qwum Stuwixwuhl partnership with Snuneymuxw. Staffing/Unusual Expenses Reserve The policy recommends that the District maintain a Staffing/Unusual Expenses Reserve of 0.5% 1.0% of actual operating expenses of the fiscal year. At June 30, 2019 this equates to a range of $660,860 - $1,321,721. The District has maintained a staffing/unusual expense reserve of $1,500,000 for staffing and replacement cost contingencies for the past 5 years (since 2014/15). Due to the Ministry only releasing 90% of the School District’s CEF grant funding, Staff recommend allocating the $1,500,000 normally restricted in the Staffing/Unusual Expenses Reserve to supplement the CEF grant funding under the Targeted Constraints Reserve. It is expected that the remaining 10% will be released after the September 30th enrolment count. Once fully funded these funds ($1,704,537) will be available to the District for the standard contingencies that the District maintains for staffing and replacement as well as to increase the unrestricted operating surplus. Any change in fund restriction will be brought to the Board for approval. Targeted Constraints Reserve The Targeted Constraints Reserve has no minimum or maximum as it must be based on monies that need to be brought forward into the next fiscal year that can only be expended in a specific/target area of the District’s budget. The total restricted in the Targeted Constraints Reserve is $2,806,648 for the CEF supplement mentioned above, Aboriginal Education programs, schools supplies, mental health grant, facilities projects, and the fire plan. No money has been allocated to the Emergency Events / Project Contingency Reserve or the Emergency Capital Asset Replacement Reserve. ---------------------------------------------------------The policy recommends that the District maintain an overall Restricted Operating Surplus of 1.2% - 2.5% of the actual operating expenses of that fiscal year. At June 30, 2019 this equates to a range of $1,586,065 - $3,304,302. After the recommendations noted above this equates to a total of $3,181,648 restricted, which is 2.41% of total 2018/19 operating expenditures. Unrestricted Surplus The District’s new Surplus/Deficit Policy suggests that the Board review the Unrestricted Operating Surplus balance per the audited financial statements, and consider allocations to other reserve accounts to provide funding for strategic and other initiatives. and the need to fund the District’s share of the Hammond Bay expansion, staff is recommending that $1,250,000 be transferred to Local Capital. The policy recommends that the District maintain an unrestricted operating surplus balance of between 0.5% and 2.0% of actual operating expenses of that fiscal year. At June 30, 2019 this equates to a range of $660,860 - $2,643,441. The current balance is $1,280,009 which is 0.97% of the 2018/19 Operating Fund expenses. However, given the ongoing demands on Local Capital due to the lack of funding available for Local Capital initiatives ---------------------------------------------------------This leaves the district’s Unrestricted Operating Surplus with a balance of $30,009 (0.02% of the 2018/19 Operating Fund expenses). This amount is inadequate as an unrestricted reserve/contingency should an unforeseen circumstance take place; however, the Board would potentially unrestrict the Staffing and Replacement Cost contingencies in the event of an emergency. Local Capital As advised over the last few years, the Ministry is not funding new classroom requirements being driven by inclining enrolment and the reinstatement of teacher collective agreement contract language and therefore, related capital costs need to be funded by Local Capital. These costs include space renovations, additional portables and addressing an aging infrastructure. In addition to these costs, other significant projects that will require Local Capital funds are as follows: • • • District-wide Wi-Fi Upgrade – planning and installation Continuing Technology Plan Investments District Finance/HR System Upgrade Given the toll that transfers to Local Capital have had on the District’s operating budget, the growing expenses due to the incline in student enrolment, Staff advise that the District maintain a “Status Quo” approach to budgeting and financial management. It is important that the District be in a position to continue to address ongoing Local Capital needs and to re-build a healthy Unrestricted Surplus to address unforeseen circumstances.
