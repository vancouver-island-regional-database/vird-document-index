From: To: Subject: Date: Karen Matthews Karen Matthews FW: Re-start Concerns Wednesday, September 2, 2020 9:09:49 AM From: Holly B <> Sent: August 17, 2020 5:37 PM To: Holly B <> Subject: Re-start Concerns CAUTION: External Message ﻿ ﻿ ﻿To Whom It May Concern, I’m writing today with deep concerns for my children. I do not feel my children’s health, safety, and education has been considered when planning for school re-opening. Let me first give you an idea of my situation. I am the single parent to two children. For the 2020/2021 school year, currently my daughter is entering Grade 12 in the Skills For Life Program, she has designation and meets criteria for 3 separate designation categories as well as is medically complex and on immunosuppressants, and considered high risk/vulnerable for Covid 19 She requires 1:1 EA support for health and safety. My son is entering Grade 9 and is neurotypical. I also have my own disability. We are connected to the community and receive supports through MSD, MCFD/CYSN, yet often feel overwhelmed and isolated. Since Covid 19, March 2020 many supports disappeared. Respite, school, OT,PT Therapies, sub specialist appointments at BCCH all came to a halt. For the past 5.5 months we have been instructed by our Primary Care and Complex Care Teams to Protective Self Isolate. We have kept our bubble to 5 people. Not having anyone into our home, no respite for 5.5 months, our only outings have been walks outdoors and to receive necessary infusions/treatment in the hospital. We have been told not to return to in person school, and that includes my son as he could potentially infect my daughter. Here are my concerns: We are disproportionately disadvantaged. We are the ones at greater risk of falling behind. We have accessed food hampers throughout the pandemic but if these programs all move to schools we will be left out. The amount of care 1:1 is beyond what I can provide if I am also expected to be full time teacher to my 2 very different children. How will we maintain the vital social connection to community and peers when we are self isolating? The 3 options stated by SD68, none are truly an option for my family. 1. In person, catchment. Is not an option under doctors protocol. 2. DL is not an option as none are accepting SN students and many are also full for grade 9 students as well. 3. Homeschooling, this provides me with no funding or support! I’d like to make very clear that my children not attending in person school is not my personal choice but rather carefully weighed risk/benefit by our healthcare providers. I feel my family should be offered equitable access to education, that fosters learning, supports mental health, keeps my children safe, and is not further isolating or segregating them from community and peers. We should have equitable access to education and supports! I would like to highlight a resource that BCCH Complex Care team suggested medically complex families check out. It has excellent information regarding schooling from a teacher at Canuck Place. https://blogs.vsb.bc.ca/cpch/ Also noted that other families are being offered creative solutions including a family being offered a Teacher/EA to come to the families home 20 hours per week!! Also it should be noted that SD69 made EA’s available to families and much more flexible support was given from April-June. As well as SD69 has recently passed a motion to have a 4th option of home learning from the students catchment school. I would like to see immunocompromised students and those with complex learning needs and the siblings/families put at the forefront of planning by SD68. These are the families that have many needs, high levels of anxiety and children/students that will continue to have detrimental effects, if we don’t plan, support and equip. We must all communicate, collaborate and champion together during these very challenging times. Let’s not further marginalize our most vulnerable. Let’s empower teamwork to develop something innovative that we can all be proud of! SD68 has a statement Education For All. I’d like to see that be true. I appreciate the opportunity to express my concerns, I welcome conversations to further discuss my families needs and how we can best be supported and I expect a timely response so that informed decisions can be made should a different course of action be needed.
