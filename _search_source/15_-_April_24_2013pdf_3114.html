CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan Mr. Hutchinson presented the proposed Enhanced Facilities Plan to the Board and said that the Plan is one of the district’s three strategic planning goals and the proposed plan has been Draft Minutes of the Regular Board Meeting held April 24, 2013 designed to meet that goal. Mr. Hutchinson said that it is recognized that the district needs to improve student performance, to offer students more challenging programs and to replace aging substandard school facilities. He said that one of the proposed plan’s primary objectives is to create schools of optimal size – 200 to 400 students at the elementary level and 600 to 1,200 students at the secondary level. Mr. Hutchinson said that the proposed plan includes a commitment to consultation and partnerships He said that Dr. Doug Player, an experienced and respected educator, worked as a consultant to assist the senior management team with the definition of the key elements of the plan. Dr. Player’s recommendations were presented to the Board on April 11 at a public meeting. The proposed plan reflects Dr. Player’s key recommendations, although some of the timing is different. The key recommendations of the plan include: • Consolidate ten facilities • Rebuild four facilities • Rethink and repurpose the District and Learning Resource Centres • Pursue the addition of enrichment programs to a number of schools • Relocate and renew the approach to the alternate programs • Adjust the French Immersion program • Relocate the administrative functions The Board will consult on the proposed plan during May and June.
