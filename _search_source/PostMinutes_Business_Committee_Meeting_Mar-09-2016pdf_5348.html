CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS Mr. Allen Johnston Teacher & BCHS T&F Committee Member RE: BCHS Track and Field Championships Mr. Johnston presented to the Business Committee for information that the BCHS (BC High School) Track and Field Championships will be held in Nanaimo at the Rotary Bowl this year. This is the first time the championships will be held off the mainland. They meet will be held on June 2, 3 and 4th. The entire meet will be live streamed for each particular event. There will be 2500 athletes, 300 coaches, 60 officials, 235 schools and 2000+ spectators. Trustee Robinson & Rolanda Murray Canada Learning Bond Coordinator RE: BC Training and Education Grants Trustee Robinson introduced Rolanda Murray, Canada Learning Bond Coordinator at VIU. There are currently a large group of children living in families that make less than $45,000 per year that are eligible for this money, but are unaware and don't know that the Canada Learning Bond exists. Ms. Murray's job is to go out into the community to help gain awareness regarding the Canada Learning Bond and The BC Training and Education Savings Grant. The goal would be to also partner with schools to come in and present, making students, teachers and parents aware of access to the Canada Learning Bond and the BC Training and Education Savings Grant. The Canada Learning Bond is specifically for low income families (those families that make less than $45,000 per year) and the maximum available is $2000. The BC Training and Education Savings Grant is a contribution from the BC Government of $1,200 Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 9, 2016 to eligible children through the BCTES grant. To be eligible, a child and their guardian be residents of BC, the child must have an RESP, be born in 2007 or later and be between their sixth birthday and the day before their ninth birthday. Trustee Brennan is confident that the Director of Communications will make this information available in schools. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Trustee Kimler - 2016-17 Budget Referendum Trustee Kimler would like to have a discussion about having a referendum regarding the budget. He is neither supportive, nor unsupportive, but would like to have the dialogue at the table. Trustee Rae believes the cost of a referendum with no guarantee that we would get it back is to much of a risk for him. Trustee Higginson is concerned with the restricted time frames and the cost laid out by the school act. There have been referendums in other districts that have all been unsuccessful. Trustee Robinson thinks that if we were to have a referendum, it should be done in conjunction with and election to offset the costs. Trustee Routley is opposed. He would like to see two budgets. A balanced one and a needs one. Trustee Solomon agrees with Trustee Robinson and timing it with an election year. He believes that it would have little chance of success.. Trustee Bob agrees with her fellow Trustees. Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held March 9, 2016 Trustee Brzovic agrees with her fellow Trustees.
