TRUSTEE LIAISON ANNUAL REPORT Charlene McKay, School Trustee School Name/Zone: Qwam Qwum Stuwixwulh School Year: 2022/2023 Annual Highlights: • • • • • • • • • • • • The student population grew again this year. It was first 80 students when opened in its new location, and has now grown to be 120. Practicum students were a new addition to the school this year. Work has been underway this past school year to create a plan for Indigenous Immersion: Hul’q’umin’um teachers are working with the school to create supports for all school staff to share more Hul’q’umin’um in their classrooms. This will also include support for professional development. The school held their first ever winter play. Students have embraced their school as an extension of their home and are successfully building relationships with the adults who work in their school. The school’s basketball team had a great year, which helped them prepare for some students participating in the Junior All Natives Tournament in the spring. Students at QQS participated in their first school district track meet this year and enjoyed the day at the track. Throughout the year, students continue to learn on the land, and about the protocols that are undertaken for culturally important events in the forests around the school. There are cultural supports are regularly available; for example, smudging. This is often led by community role models. The school began and expanded a number of after school programs where students could participate in sports, crafts and other activities like homework club. On National Indigenous People’s Day, a spring play was held in Hul’q’umin’um, and there were cultural performances at the school for all the students and staff as well as community members. This was followed by a successful school fun day.
