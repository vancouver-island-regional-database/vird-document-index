LADYSMITH LINKS JAN 2026 2026 DOG LICENSES SIGN UP FOR E-BILLING All dogs over the age of six months must be licensed in the Town of Ladysmith. Annual dog licences are available at City Hall (410 Esplanade) during regular office hours, the Frank Jameson Community Centre (810 6th Avenue) and For Pet’s Sake pet store (416 1st Avenue). We encourage residents to sign up for an email copy of your quarterly utility bill. The fees for dog licences are as follows: For neutered or spayed dogs, the fee is $31 if purchased by January 31, and $41 if purchased between February 1 and December 31. For dogs that are not neutered or spayed, the fee is $49 until January 31, and $59 between February 1 and December 31. Coastal Animal Control Services provides animal control services and pound facilities for the Town of Ladysmith. Coastal Animal Control is located at 2202 Herd Road, Duncan, BC, and can be reached on a 24-hour basis at 1.250.732.7720. BUSINESS LICENCE RENEWALS DUE BY FEBRUARY 28 The Town of Ladysmith inter-municipal business licences with Duncan, North Cowichan and Lake Cowichan are now available. The cost of an annual business licence is $100. Renew by February 28, 2026 to avoid a $25 late payment fee. Ladysmith business owners who carry out work in Central Vancouver Island municipalities can purchase an InterCommunity Business Licence for an additional $170.00, making it $270.00 for the pair. Following are the participating municipalities and districts: Campbell River, Parksville, Qualicum Beach, Courtenay, Port Alberni, Cumberland, Duncan, Lake Cowichan, Comox, Lantzville, Sooke, North Cowichan and Nanaimo. Please email your name and account number to finance@ladysmith.ca. Once registered, you will begin receiving your quarterly utility bill directly in your inbox. NEW GARBAGE COLLECTION VENDOR The town has a new garbage collection contractor - GFL Environmental, and 2026 collection schedules are now available. In 2026, each household can place one garbage container at the curb. The container must be no larger than 120 litres and weigh no more than 20 kilograms (44 pounds) when full. Rules for recycling and organic containers remain the same: Residents can place unlimited blue recycling bins or yellow recycling bags at the curb, that contain accepetd items. Each household may place one organic container at the curb, no larger than 45 litres (10 gallons). Compostable or biodegradable plastics bags are not permitted in your tote/bin. Paper bags or newspaper can be used as an alternative. The list of acceptable items remains unchanged. To find out what is acceptable and what route you’re on, please visit www.ladysmith.ca/our-services/garbage-recycling. In case of inclement weather, GFL plans to deploy a second truck the following day to pick up any missed collections. If severe weather causes delays beyond one day, residents will need to hold their materials until their next scheduled collection day. CIVIC RECOGNITION PROGRAM For future service issues, please call 250.245.6400 and select option 8 to connect directly with GFL. Ladysmith is home to residents whose outstanding efforts—whether personal, public, or professional— have brought recognition, achievement, or meaningful contributions to our community. Through the Town’s Civic Recognition Program, individuals and organizations can be honoured with awards that celebrate their impact. Do you know someone, a team, or an organization in Ladysmith that deserves to be recognized? To nominate them for an award, complete a nomination form and send it by email to cs@ladysmith.ca.
