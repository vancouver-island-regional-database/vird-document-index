The Seaview Elementary catchment is quite large, encompassing both sides of Highway 19 and and extending north to the Snaw—Naw-As First Nation Reserve. Our community does not have sidewalks and there are narrow or non-existent shoulders on most of the roads. Currently Seaview Elementary has approximately 85 students that regularly take the bus and of those only 8-16 would be considered eligible to ride the bus according to the proposed changes. The removal or restriction of bus service raises a number of issues for Seaview parents. The safety of children is the biggest concern. If bus service is cut to courtesy students in Upper Lantzville, then children would need to cross Highway 19 in order to attend school. Highway 19 does not have an overpass or underpass at the Superior and Ware Road intersections, and the highway experiences a high volume of traffic traveling at high speeds. The second concern of the Seaview PAC is the lack of alternative transportation options for students. Lantzville has very limited transit service,and the current schedule and route would not provide transportation to Seaview Elementary. There are also a number families living in upper Lantzville and the Snaw-Naw-As First Nation Reserve that do not have access to a vehicle. Bussing their children to the school is their only option.
