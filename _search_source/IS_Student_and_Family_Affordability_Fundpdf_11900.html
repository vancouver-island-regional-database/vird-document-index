NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: April 10, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Student and Family Affordability Fund Background: Recently the Ministry of Education and Child Care announced additional funding in the current school year for the Student and Family Affordability Fund. This information sheet will outline the District’s entitlement, as well as the plans for the use of the funds. Discussion: The District has received approximately $495,000 from the Ministry of Education and Childcare’s Student and Family Affordability Fund (“the SFAF”) https://news.gov.bc.ca/releases/2024ECC0013000293. In addition, the District has approximately $88,000 as of January 1, 2024, carried forward from the previous SFAF allocation. These funds are intended to be used solely to alleviate inflationary financial pressures on families in our schools. In the last school year the SFAF was used to subsidize food, field trips, and the purchase of supplies and even household items. The funds are not intended for the District to use for educational purposes, staffing, internal supplies or other inflationary issues faced by the District's operating fund. The initial SFAF fund was intended to be one year only. However, it is apparent that inflationary pressures continue to face families and that schools are well placed to know the families in need of support and to quickly and efficiently get the funds in the hands of the those in need. The District will attempt to support needs in our schools in the current school year and anticipate carrying forward additional funds to be fully expended by June of 2025 (the 2023 $88,000 will be fully expended by June). Staff are currently in the process of making a determination of how the funds will be divided and we will be providing some immediate support to the Nations, Learning Alternatives and the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation to ensure supports are available in the summer. Staff are also planning consultation with stakeholders prior to allocating the remaining funds, however, we anticipate a similar structure to the previous allocation. Finally, it should be noted that the funds we received are roughly equivalent to the previous year’s allocation for the SFAF minus the amount that the Ministry has provided for the Feeding Futures Fund, which is also assisting students and families facing food insecurity in all of our schools. Staff intend to report back progress in the fall on the expenditure of the funds and are very hopeful that we will see consistent expenditures with little or no surplus predicted beyond June of 2025.
