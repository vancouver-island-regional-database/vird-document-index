Purpose The Safe Schools Committee will act as an advisory committee to the Board of Education for Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools. The Safe Schools Advisory Committee (SSC) celebrates and honours the rich expression of diversity in our classrooms and throughout our school communities. The SSC will provide input and feedback focusing on equity, diversity and inclusion, including but not limited to, anti-racism, anti-ableism, cultural safety and sexual orientation and gender issues. Scope The committee provides input and feedback to the Board on any matter related to the strategic goal of Safe, Caring and Inclusive Schools, as well as the Inclusion Policy and its related procedures. The committee will also monitor the progress and best practices of the JEDI (Justice, Equity, Inclusion, and Diversity) Strategic Plan as contained in the Superintendent’s Operational Plan. Authority The committee reports to the Board of Education. The committee may strike subgroups to achieve the work of the committee’s work plan. The committee will seek to achieve consensus-based actionable recommendations, and where consensus is not achieved the committee shall vote on a primary recommendation as necessary. Page 47 of 63 Safe Schools Advisory Committee Terms of Reference Membership Members of the committee are either invited by the Board Chair or appointed by their respective organization. The Committee will be chaired by the Trustee appointed to the committee, or in their absence the alternate. The committee will be comprised of the following members: • • • • • • • • • • • • Trustee (2, Co-Chairs of Committee) Indigenous Rights Holders Syeyutsus Knowledge Keepers CUPE Representatives (2) NDTA Representatives (2) DPAC Representatives (2) NSAA Representatives (2) Learning Services Representative (1) Human Resources Representative (1) Community Members (as invited) BIPOC Members (invited to join based on interest to participate) Disability Members (invited to join based on interest to participate) The committee membership will be guided by the commitment to ensure members have a diversity of lived experience and perspectives, identities, and expertise including, but not limited to, cultural, racial, gender and/or sexual orientation, and disability. Meeting Arrangements Meetings will be held at least 4 times per school year. A sub-committee for student voice will be established to provide an opportunity for students to provide their input on the effectiveness of the JEDI operational plan strategies related to safe, caring and inclusive schools. This sub-committee will meet twice per year. Additional meetings may be scheduled, as needed, to ensure the committee meets to fulfill their mandate and annual work plan. Guest speakers may be invited to share information from the community that would be of benefit to NLPS and the Safe Schools Committee. This Policy sets out the School District’s commitment, standards and expectations regarding the appropriate practices for the collection, use and protection personal information. PRINCIPLES The School District and all Staff shall uphold the privacy, confidentiality and appropriate use of personal information in compliance with the School Act, FIPPA and the procedures, including by: • being open and transparent about the purposes for which personal information may be collected and used by the School District; • collecting and using personal information only as necessary to carry out the School District’s authorized programs and activities; • sharing personal information internally with staff only on a need-to-know basis; • sharing personal information with third parties with the knowledge and consent of affected individuals, unless otherwise authorized or required under FIPPA, the School Act or other applicable laws; • ensuring personal information is protected against unauthorized access, use, disclosure, loss or destruction; and • complying with FIPPA and all Procedures for the accuracy, protection, use, disclosure, storage, retrieval, correction and appropriate use of personal information.
