NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 14, 2023 Business Committee Greg Keller, Board Chair Strategic Plan Background This Information Sheet is responsive to the requirement of Policy 1.2 - Strategic Plan that the Chair of the Board, with the assistance of the Superintendent, provide a report on the renewal, revision or extension of the Strategic Plan 6 months in advance of its expiry. The current Strategic Plan ends on December 31, 2023. This report will provide an outline of the process for renewal in line with Policy including proposed timeline for renewal and public consultation. Discussion Policy 1.2 - Strategic Plan provides the following requirements in advance of the completion of the Strategic Plan: Creating the Strategic Plan a. The Chair of the Board with the assistance of the Superintendent shall provide a report (the “Report) to the Board at least 6 (six) months prior to the expiry of the Strategic Plan that outlines the process to renew, extend and/or revise the Strategic Plan. b. The Report and Action Sheet shall include: i. The proposed term of the Strategic Plan which shall be between 3 (three) and 5 (five) years; ii. Any requirements of the Ministry of Education to be included in the Strategic Plan; iii. A general framework for consultation with the community, including Rights Holders; iv. A copy of the most recent District Review Story that highlights the progress of the current Strategic Plan; and v. A proposed motion for the Board to initiate the process. Current Strategic Plan The current Strategic Plan has been an enormous success. Focused on 4 goals, each goal area has progressed in meaningful and measurable ways. Requirements of the Ministry of Education of Child Care Given that the Ministry of Education and Child Care has specific requirements outlined in the Framework for Enhancing Student Learning Policy and Ministerial Order M302, it is logical that the Board consider both documents when reviewing the Strategic Plan. The Framework for Enhancing Student Learning plays a crucial role in guiding Boards of Education in the development of their strategic plans. The framework provides a province-wide vision for education and serves as a foundation for improving student learning outcomes. Ministerial Order M302, the Enhancing Student Learning Reporting Order requires Boards to submit to the Minister a report completed in accordance with the Order between June 30 and September 30, or a date otherwise determined by the Minister. The Framework for Enhancing Student Learning places a strong emphasis on student success and wellbeing. Boards of Education are encouraged to develop strategic goals and objectives that prioritize improving student outcomes, engagement, and overall well-being. This may involve setting targets for graduation rates, academic achievement, social-emotional development, and other indicators of student success. While the Framework for Enhancing Student Learning provides a province-wide vision, it recognizes the importance of local context and flexibility and Boards of Education have the autonomy to tailor their strategic plans to their specific district's needs, challenges, and strengths The framework ensures a coordinated and cohesive approach to education across the province while allowing flexibility for districts to address their specific needs and aspirations. Consultation Framework The framework promotes a collaborative approach to decision-making and planning. Boards of Education are expected to engage with various stakeholders, including students, parents, educators, and community members, in the strategic planning process. This collaboration ensures that the Strategic Plan reflects the diverse needs and aspirations of the community and fosters a sense of ownership and shared responsibility for student learning outcomes. As noted at this time, the consultation process will be outlined in a report in the fall.
