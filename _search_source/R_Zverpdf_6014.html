YOUR VOICE 2015 THE 10-YEAR FACILITIES PLAN REVIEW Good evening Trustees, Senior Management and Members of the audience. My name is rob Zver and I am the President of CUPE Local 606, which represents the support staff employees in School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith). I want to thank you for giving me the opportunity to speak to you regarding the review to the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities Plan. I want to start off by stating that the plan that was adopted by the old Board of Trustees may have some merit in it. But that is where it stops. This plan, in my opinion, had more to do with closing schools, uprouting communities, displacing students and saving money as our Provincial Government continues to underfund both the operational and capital project side of Public Education. You, as Trustees, are left with little choices but to either close schools or lay-off even more employees to make up the shortfall. We need to keep standing up to this Government and demand adequate funding be provided to the Public Education system, so that we are not always tasked with closing schools and laying off CUPE employees to save much needed funding. Over the years the district has sold off, closed schools and surplus land all in the attempt of building a new high school. I think we can all agree this may never happen. Saying that brings me back to the money that is currently on hand. You as Trustees, were voted in by the people in this school district and must represent all communities equally by standing up for Public Education, keeping schools open, using that funding to revitalize our schools and make them all better if possible closing only those that have out-lived their usefulness. Twenty or more decent schools are better than one new one.
