April 29, 2014 Ref: 172252 Dot Neary, Chair Board of Education School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) Email: dneary@sd68.bc.ca Dear Ms. Neary: Thank you for your letter regarding the Foundation Skills Assessment (FSA). Measures like FSA are key accountability tools for government and school districts. These measures provide educators and policy makers with important student performance information. This student performance information enables the province and school districts to identify long-term trends, including performance by specific groups of students. A random sampling design cannot provide information about specific groups of students such as Aboriginal students. The FSA year-to-year data helps schools, districts and the province to be accountable to students, parents and British Columbians at large. However, I too share your concerns about the use of data from FSA to rank schools. Ranking schools or districts based on FSA results invites misleading comparisons that ignore the particular context of each school and the community. The Ministry of Education does not support using FSA to rank schools. Access to FSA data is governed by Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy legislation. This legislation determines what must be made available in the public interest and what can be withheld in the interest of the privacy of an individual. Aggregated school and district level results are public information, except when the size of the group is very small, and the Fraser Institute or anyone in the public can request the data and use them to articulate a particular point of view. I welcome opportunities to discuss topics such as assessment with all stakeholders. An Advisory Group on Provincial Assessment has been meeting to discuss BC’s future provincial assessment system. This Advisory Group is co-chaired by the deans of education from UBC and SFU and the members include representatives from stakeholders.
