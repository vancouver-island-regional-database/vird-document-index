27, 2013 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Response to Board Motion R13/02/27-25 Background: This report was prepared in response to the following Board Motion: R13/02/27-25: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests that the Superintendent/CEO develops a plan for Board approval for publicly reviewing/revising policy/procedures. As the system continues to implement a policy governance model, it is necessary to standardize the processes for developing and communicating new or revised Board policies and Administrative procedures. In keeping with the governance model, and the expectations of the Superintendent/CEO with respect to Board policies and Administrative procedures, please find attached draft administrative procedures that address the Board’s interest in having a consistent approach to policy and procedure review, development, communication, and implementation. It is important to note that the Board currently has a policy on Policy Development (2.7), but there are no Administrative procedures that speak to Board policy dissemination and communication. Similarly, district administration does not have a procedure that speaks specifically to Administrative procedure development, review, and dissemination. Please note that the attached procedures are adapted from similar procedures developed by Saskatoon Public Schools. Recommendation: That the Board approves the plan to establish new Administrative procedures for “Policy and Procedures Communication” and “Review of Administrative Procedures” in fulfillment of the request from the Board, as per motion R13/02/27-25. Attach. 1 Page 84 of 103 13.1 AP___ Policy and Procedures Communication The Superintendent/CEO is responsible for facilitating the development, implementation and evaluation of Board policies, as well as the development, implementation, evaluation, and approval of Administrative (operational) procedures. 1. The Superintendent/CEO will ensure that the Board Policy Handbook and the Administrative Procedures Manual are available on the district website. This will ensure that all District internal and external stakeholders can access Board Policies and Administrative Procedures at their convenience. 2. Paper copies of the aforementioned documents will be provided to Trustees, members of the Administrative Council Executive and Administrative Council, and school Principals. 3. It shall be the responsibility of the Principal to: 3.1 Disseminate to, and interpret Board policy and administrative procedures for the school staff; and 3.2 Ensure updated copies of Board policies and administrative procedures are available in the school. Reference: School Act 22 (1), 85 (2); Policy 3.7: Role and Responsibilities of the Superintendent November 2013 2 Page 85 of 103 13.1 AP_______ Review of Administrative Procedures 1. An annual review of the administrative procedures will be conducted through the office of the Superintendent/CEO. This review will focus on the following criteria: • One section of the Administration Procedures manual will be reviewed, yearly; • Each procedure is the responsibility of Administration as delegated by the Board or defined by legislation; • Each procedure is consistent with the District’s strategic direction; and • Each procedure ensures clear consistent direction for District office and school administration. 2. When substantive issues arise during the review process, the district will solicit input from trustees, District Administrative Centre personnel, NDTA and CUPE representatives, and parent advisory councils. 3. A review of a specific administrative procedure may be initiated at any time by a formal request from the Board, a parent advisory council, a school administrator, or an employee who is personally affected by a specific procedure. This review may also be initiated in response to legislative changes or school district needs. The request for review shall detail the concerns associated with the administrative procedure and, if possible, offer suggestions for revision. 4. The office of the Superintendent/CEO shall determine an appropriate process for reviewing a specific administrative procedure when requested to ensure fair and reasonable consideration is given to the request.
