CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY 4 Page 7 of 25 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 13, 2021 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Financial Update Background On December 20, 2020, the Ministry of Education announced the recalculated operating grants for the 2020-21 school year based on the September 30th student enrollment information, as well as confirmed our Classroom Enhancement Fund submission. While the Annual Amended Budget will be presented to the Business Committee for discussion in February, we thought it would be important for the Board to understand our general financial picture as we currently know it as well as what remains unknown or unconfirmed to date. As we had previously reported at the October Business Committee meeting, the District was facing a significant funding loss due mainly to the movement of students from regular classrooms to Distributed Learning; this loss has now been confirmed. There are however some significant funding announcements that have yet to be confirmed, the most significant being the second installment of the Federal Safe Return to School grant ($2.6 million). The Ministry has indicated they have not been able to release those funds because they have not received approval to do so at this time, but we do anticipate confirmation in the coming weeks. Although Phase 2 funding is still to be confirmed, we were recently notified that Ministry held back 10% of the Phase 1 funding which they have now distributed to districts, our portion of that funding was approximately $250,000. The Ministry has also indicated that due to enrollment shortfalls and students shifting to DL province wide, there is a significant surplus of operating grant dollars, referred to as holdback, which is approximately $37 million. A portion of this holdback will be required to fund any increases to Distributed Learning enrollments that are identified in the February and May 1701 Student Data Collections, however, any remaining surplus balances will likely be distributed to Districts; the timing and amount are yet to be confirmed. We are also expecting an additional 73 international students to arrive this month, in time to quarantine for 14 days prior to beginning in class instruction for Quarter 3. This would be an increase of approximately $475,000 in funding if these students arrive. Ultimately, where there continue to be a number of unknows the District is likely to be in a position to invest in supports to address learning loss in the current school year. Page 23 of 25 NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 13, 2021 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Administrative Procedure 507 - Fencing Background As part of our ongoing review of our Administrative Procedures, this memo is to inform the Board of the removal of Administrative Procedure 507 – Fencing (the “AP”). Rationale The AP is not followed by the District and we have not been able to determine how long it has been since we have enforced it. Specifically, it requires that prior to the installation of a fence at a school site that a neighbour is obligated to pay 50% of the costs of the fence. Further, it envisions wooden fences in urban areas and three pronged barbwire fencing for rural areas. Giving that fencing is ubiquitous on our properties and we have no intention of not installing fencing and/or other appropriate barriers between our sites and neighbouring properties, or the road, the AP is unnecessary. Further, if a neighbour is interested in assisting with the cost of fencing to install a product that differs from the District standard we may consider it, but would not require an AP to do so. Further, we do not have a standard fence that differs between urban and rural areas. For these reasons, AP 507 - Fencing is unnecessary and will be deleted. Our intention was to bring the draft LRFP to the January Business Meeting. This memo is to update the Board and the community on our status. Update Currently, our consultant assisting with our longer-term growth projections have provided us preliminary information, but due to a small delay in getting permissions from external parties, we have not yet been able to run various growth scenarios to finalize those numbers. We will be finalizing the longer-term growth pattern in the near term to support the LRFP and the School Site Acquisition Charge. In addition, due to COVID our short-term projections (Baragar) which arrived in December are problematic because of the significant variation in bricks and mortar enrollment this year. We understand this is a provincial issue. Currently, staff are in the process of: 1. Reviewing our Baragar projections; 2. Reviewing our distributed learning enrollments to determine which schools’ students came from, and will likely return to 3. Reviewing areas of expected growth or decline 4. Accounting for cohort retention and in-out migration, and 5. Estimating enrollment based on these factors. We also note that some of this year’s projection data has come in significantly lower than previously estimated which may impact potential space requirements or alter preliminary planning. Because of these factors (particularly our shorter-term projections), we will not be a position to finalize the LRFP assumptions for a January presentation, instead we anticipate a February date for presentation of the initial LRFP draft.
