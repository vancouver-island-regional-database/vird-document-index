NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 7, 2024 Education Committee Laura Tait, Acting Superintendent Completion Rates Overview The Ministry of Education and Child Care provides school districts with a variety of reports throughout the school year. In late December or early January, the Ministry shares school completion rates from the previous year, along with historical data from the prior five years. This “Satellite” data allows the district to track trends over time, as well as take a closer look at disaggregated data for subsets of learners. These completion rates are included, along with Foundation Skills Assessment data, Student Learning Survey results, Grade to Grade transitions, and transition to post-secondary, as part of the broader Enhancing Student Learning report that School Districts report back to the Ministry in the Fall. Background School completion rates are collected by the Ministry of Education and Child Care each year. For students who entered grade 8 in September of 2018, completion rates are reported as part of the “5year” completion rates. The Ministry also reports “6-year” completion rates, to track students who may require additional time to complete their schooling. Both completion rates provide insight into how our school district is supporting student success. The data is disaggregated to include completion rates for our Indigenous learners, both on-reserve and off-reserve, as well as data on our students with diverse abilities and for our Children and Youth in Care. Overall, the trend in completion rates over time for the district shows improvement. This is true for both 5 and 6-year completion rates, and within our disaggregated data for our Indigenous students, students with diverse abilities, and Children and Youth in Care. The gap between our district results and the provincial average has closed for all groups over the past six years. Results for Indigenous Student OnReserve has a wide range of completion rates over the years, as the cohort is small and changes in completion rates from one year to another result in large changes in the percentage of students attaining school completion. A closer look at the success of our Indigenous learners will be presented along with our How Are We Doing (HAWD) Report later in the year.
