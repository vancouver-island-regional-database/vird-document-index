CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY FROM THE BUSINESS COMMITTEE 2014-2015 Annual Budget Mr. Turin distributed documents related to the budget and reviewed them with the Board. Trustee Saunders asked that her statement regarding the 2014-2015 budget be included in the record of tonight's meeting. These comments, questions and observations are mine and mine alone and no way reflect the work done by the budget committee. I have grave concerns over this budget. Although it was done in a democratic way I feel it could have been done in a more collaborative manner. We, as Trustees, must submit a balanced budget and count on our Senior Administrative Team to not only bring forward a balanced budget but one Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held May 5, 2014 that reflect the idea that cuts should be kept as far away from the classroom as possible. I, as do others, recognize that as we receive fewer students that fewer teachers and education assistants will be needed. So, why do we have more senior managers and non-enrolling teachers than we have ever had since I was first elected in 1993 with more than 3000 fewer students? The Board has goals around ‘response to intervention’ and ‘self-regulated learning’ but we don’t have buy-in from most teachers and some of their school administrators. Would it not have been better to slow things down by cutting some of these positions that are not as successful as we had hoped then re-evaluate by asking how we could help teachers in their classrooms and at their schools? Once a buy-in has been achieved those positions could be put back into the budget or something quite different could appear through consultation with those we are trying to assist. The example of our Assessment goal being successful is because of the participation we have from every stakeholder group. We have school administrator positions that are currently vacant, why are we giving such small assignments to Principals and VP’s? Should we continue to exploit them or should we be looking at a different model that sees larger assignments at perhaps two different schools? Is succession planning any less real using the ‘Teacher-inCharge’ model? Most of the VP’s we hire were at one time TIC’s. I think Principals would get more satisfaction if they had a full time position even if it was at two different schools. We were told there were many different ways to staff but were given only two. We are now in the 21st century so why do we staff like we did in the 20th century? Every cut to the classroom had a positive rationale that said we can do this, we have a better way, the software will solve the problem but in reality we are dealing with flesh and bone people who do not take to change well. It is always the best to take it slowly, answer all the questions, listen to the concerns and then make decisions, so why the rush? Our goals are still the same and will be Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held May 5, 2014 achieved sooner or later. We don’t give up on our goals and plans but should on occasion, back up and re-evaluate. Many years ago, we had a budget committee made up of all the employee groups, management, Trustees, parents and students that worked in closed session looking at the budget in a very detailed manner. They then made recommendations to the Business committee for acceptance. It was a hard slog but it worked for a long time and I would recommend that we re-examine that format. I acknowledge that most of our budget woes stem from that basic fact that funding has not kept up with the needs of the education system. Government makes demands and downloads costs of their own making to us thus leaving us without the ability to move ahead with our education agenda. If the Government would seriously look at a 1% surcharge on profits made by large corporations in BC, this province wouldn’t be in the situation it finds itself and the most vulnerable citizens wouldn’t have to wait for the government to start making a profit before their needs are looked at. In my 18 years as a sitting Trustee, this will be the first time I will not be supporting the budget.
