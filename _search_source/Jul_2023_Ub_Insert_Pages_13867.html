ATVS ON TOWN TRAILS All-terrain vehicles (ATVs) are fun to drive for many and allow riders to access off-road trails they might not easily reach on foot. During the warmer summer months, please take extreme care with any activity in forested areas that could pose a risk of wildfire. Please be aware that only authorized ATVs and other motorized vehicles are allowed on all Town of Ladysmith trails. POVERTY REDUCTION STRATEGY SUPPORTS FRUIT RESCUE PROGRAM Do you have extra fruit on trees or veggies in your garden that you could use some help picking? Each year, an abundance of apples, cherries, plums and more are grown locally, but not always harvested. As part of the CommUNITY Together to End Poverty Hw-nuts’-ulwum Poverty Reduction Strategy, the Town of Ladysmith is supporting the Ladysmith Resource Centre Association (LRCA) in launching a Fruit Rescue project to make sure any extra food grown is harvested and shared within the community. How it will work: Area residents are invited to contact the LRCA’s Fruit Rescue Coordinator, Shannon Crowards, by emailing shannon@lrca.ca or calling 250-245-3079, if you have fruit trees or gardens that you would like help harvesting. After each harvest, one third of what is collected will go to the owner, one third will be shared with the volunteers and the last third will go to the LRCA Food Bank and/or other partner organizations who will distribute the fruit within the Ladysmith and Stz’uminus communities to those who need it. LOCAL BURNING AND WILDFIRE BANS BC Wildfire Service prohibits campfires throughout the Coastal Fire Centre, except for the Haida Gwaii Forest District. These prohibitions apply to all public and private land and help prevent human-caused wildfires and protect public safety. In addition to open fires being prohibited, the following activities and equipment are also restricted: • Fireworks; • Sky Lanterns; • Burn Barrels or Burn Cages of any size or description; • Binary Exploding Targets; • Air curtain burners; • Tiki and similar kind of torches; and, • Chimineas. WILDLIFE AWARE Nature is right on our doorstep and this means always being mindful of how human activity impacts wildlife. At this time of year, wildlife can be spotted more frequently. Don’t leave garbage or compost outdoors. Use caution when on the trails. If you see a bear or cougar, please report it immediately. Reports of wildlife in an urban setting or reports of wildlife in conflict should always be reported to the BC Conservation Officer Service Conservation reporting line 1-877-952-7277 (RAPP) or #7277 on the TELUS Mobility Network. RECYCLING AUDITS The Town sustainability program is back this summer, with our ambassadors Ava and Taylor completing recycling audits across the community. Ladysmith currently sits at a 4.2% contamination rate for its recycling, which includes only non-recyclable items. However, this statistic is likely higher as it doesn’t include items such as glass, soft plastics and flexible plastics. These items contribute to contamination and can instead be brought to either the CVRD Peerless Road Recycling Centre or the Junction Bottle Depot. We’re encouraging households to be aware of recycling best practices as we all work together towards our goal of waste reduction. If any contamination is found, it will be left in a clear bag along with a flyer containing more information on disposal options.
