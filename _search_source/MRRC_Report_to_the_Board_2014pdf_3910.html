Hul’qumi’num Language and Culture • First the committee approached the Nanaimo North Rotary Club and received $1000 to print three classroom sets of two new Hul’qumi’num children’s books (see attached copies) which will be given to our Hul’qumi’num SD68 teachers. • We have also worked with former NDTA president, Derek DeGear to come up with a list of short sentences and phrases frequently used in the classroom setting. This list has now been translated into Hul’qumi’num. We plan to make this list available in both print and audio formats. Jerry Brown will voice the translations for teachers who would like to use more Hul’qumi’num in their classrooms. • We also plan to introduce some simple Hul’qumi’num signage for schools that would like to use them. These include: • Washroom signs for both boys and girls washrooms • Office • The “Welcome” sign below. We are working to have this professionally laid out on an appropriate background WELCOME THIXWUM MI CH NUWILUM “Please come in” Spuptitul (Hul’qumi’num Language Competition) Our biggest achievement to date was our first ever SD68 Spuptitul (Hul’qumi’num Language Competition), a daylong event that was held at Ladysmith Secondary on May 13. The event was funded in large part by Laura Tait’s Elder Council ($5,000 available not all spent). MRRC provided T-shirts for all participants at a cost of $1079.57. – See attached photos. The event was catered by the Ladysmith Secondary School culinary class who did an extraordinary job. Everyone was impressed with both the excellent food and service. Close to 200 students and adults attended. The Spuptitul was opened by the chiefs from all three First Nations in our school district; Chief John Elliott (Stz’uminus First Nation), Chief John Wesley (Snuneymuxw First Nation) and Chief David Bob (Snaw’naw’as First Nation) as well as David Hutchinson (Superintended/CEO School District 68). The student participants were drummed in by 15 student drummers from Pleasant Valley School. The gym was charged with excitement The fact that all three chiefs took the time to attend our Spuptitul told us that they value our effort to introduce more Hul’qumi’num language and culture into our district and that we have their support if and when required. A total of twenty teams with six players on each team participated. They came from SD 79 (Cowichan Valley), Qwam Qwum Stuwixwulh School (Snuneymuxw First Nation), S-hXiXnu-tun Lelum School (Stz-uminus First Nation) plus six schools from SD68 (Ladysmith Secondary, first in their category, Ladysmith Primary, Cedar Intermediate, Brechin, Bayview, Davis Rd). A teacher observer also attended from SD 69 (Parksville/Qualicum). Word has spread to several other SD68 schools that are now planning on participating next year. We are also looking forward to growing the event up and down the Island over the next few years. Our plan is for each district to organize its own spuptitul for the four age groups (K to 3, 4 to 7, 8 to 10 and 11 and 12) and then organize a competition for the winning teams from each participating district. There is no doubt that this year’s event impressed the participating students, adults and other observers. There were large trophies given to the winning team in each age group and each participant received a T-shirt and a medallion. It was definitely a first class affair, especially in the eyes of the students. The best example of the enthusiasm and pride felt by participating students happened at one school. The students had learned in advance that one of their teams had won a trophy and the entire student body waited in front of the school to greet their “champions” as they got off the bus! The Spuptitul featured four different events. The Hul’qumi’num language competition was judged by six First Nations Elders. We also offered soccer, basketball and lacrosse workshops, which were run by basketball and soccer players from VIU and lacrosse players from the Timbermen lacrosse team. Our plan was to have Hul’qumi’num speakers teaching the players selected Hul’qumi’num words and phrases often used by players of each sport. The goal was that the students would leave excited about being able to continue using what they had learned at the Spuptitul when they are playing these sports at school and in their communities. This did not work as well as we had hoped. We have ideas to strengthen that next year. We see this Spuptitul as our next major step as we move further toward celebrating and growing awareness of the Hul’qumi’num language and culture in School District 68.
