École Hammond Bay Elementary School PAC Presentation to the School District 68 Board of Trustees March 19, 2015 The Good ❖ Fantastic Location ❖ Supportive Administrators ❖ Dedicated Teachers ❖ Engaged Students ❖ Dynamic Music Program ❖ Competitive and Inclusive Sports Programs ❖ Amazing Parents The Bad Current Enrollment 347 Students Projected Enrollment (2022)* 397 Students Where will they go? ● from 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan Appendix D – Planning Considerations Data History of The Long-Suffering Hammond Bay Gym ● 2006 - a contingent of more than 100 parents and students attended a board meeting to support a presentation stating the case for a full-sized gym ● 2007, 2009, and 2011- further presentations to the Board by the Hammond Bay Elementary Large Gym Committee ● 2006-2011- Large Gym Committee met with provincial representatives twice on the advice of the Board Board Actions ● December 2009 - Board sent a letter to the Ministry asking for approval to use capital reserve to add on to the Hammond Bay gym ● February 2010 - Board received letter from Ministry stating that no approval would be given without a long range facilities plan, which was subsequently undertaken ● July 2011 - Board passed a motion to prepare a plan for the new gym, apply to the Ministry for financing, and to apply local capital as available and prudent French Immersion Program Review November 2011 ● “...Hammond Bay Elementary, a single-track school, is operating at more than 150% of capacity now and is expected to be at more than 160% of capacity within 10 years.” p. 34 ● “...use local funds to expand gym and possibly add multi-purpose space at Hammond Bay” p. 36 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan (2013) “Build an addition to the Hammond Bay Elementary School gymnasium.” To be done in years 3 to 5 (2015-17), p. 8 ThoughtStream Process 2013 Hammond Bay had one of the highest participation rates in the district. Repeatedly, the issue of an inadequately-sized gym was a top-rated concern for participants. What Purposes Does a Gym Serve? ● ● ● ● ● ● ● ● ● teaching of the physical education curriculum team sports practices and games assemblies presentations non-sport extracurricular space (e.g., drama) professional development space space to allow for PAC initiatives (e.g., book fair) science fairs, young entrepreneurs fair community facility rentals Ability of Hammond Bay Gym to Meet the Demands of a School Gym X PE curriculum some of the Prescribed Learning Outcomes for PE cannot be met (e.g., basketball, track and field); teachers must be creative in delivery of the curriculum X Team Sports cannot host games or tournaments; cannot practice plays (e.g., basketball court has inverted, overlapping keys; 2 pass maximum to get across the space) Why We Can’t Host Team Games X assemblies cannot fit whole school in the space; administrative hassle for regular school assemblies; safety concern in case of emergencies (e.g., no adequate interior space to create a safe meeting place for our children) X presentations some presentations or performances cannot be hosted; multiple presentations required, increasing costs and loss of class time; parents have to take additional time off from work to see their children in multiple concerts; gym closed to PE when an event is planned Why Not Use the Multipurpose Room? A portable classroom, unattached to the school used regularly by the band program as well as before- and after-school care The Bottom Line Our enrollment exceeds the maximum capacity allowed in the gym and is only increasing Bathrooms in the gym area are grossly inadequate (1 toilet and sink per gender) No changing facilities No storage for risers and non-permanent staging Maximum seating capacity when conforming to SD 68 Occupational Safety and Wellness Department regulations for set-up is about 116 Gym must be closed for PE when being used for other purposes Lack of multipurpose space at the school What Can Be Done? Make the Hammond Bay Gym a priority. This is no longer an issue of “wouldn’t it be nice?”, it is an issue of safety and success for our students. Follow through with a long range facilities plan to increase the Ministry’s likelihood to approve funding and release capital. Honour the commitment to the Hammond Bay students and their parents. The Parent Community at Hammond Bay, both past and present, has tried numerous times to make headway on this issue. Please don’t make us come back. It is in your hands to make this a priority. Our students deserve better.
