THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: June 20, 2012 TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Brian Hackwood, Maintenance Supervisor SUBJECT: School Watch Program Update Background The following motions were passed by the Board of Education: June 23, 2010 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to work in cooperation with the RCMP and community partners to identify three SD68 sites for potential School Watch Programs, and to develop a draft proposal (including costs and timelines) for the Board’s consideration by November 2010. September 28, 2011 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to support a pilot of the School Watch Program at John Barsby Community, McGirr Elementary, and Pleasant Valley Elementary schools, and further that staff be requested to present the results of the pilot program to the Board of Education prior to the completion of the 2011/2012 school year. Staff arranged mail outs inviting the public to meet with interested community members adjacent to the pilot schools. Approximately 100 mail outs were distributed for each school. Individual schools also put out newsletters, PAC notices and posted meeting dates. Facilities staff along with the RCMP Crime Stoppers representative and the School Principal attended each meeting with the following results:    November 9, 2011 John Barsby Community – No members of the public attended. November 17, 2011 Pleasant Valley Elementary – approx. 15 people attended. November 24, 2011 McGirr Elementary – No members of the public attended. During this time there had been some significant vandalism at both Woodbank Primary and Cinnabar Valley schools and both schools subsequently requested to be part of the program. As two of the original three pilot schools were unable to generate any public interest it was agreed that Woodbank and Cinnabar could participate in the pilot. Meetings were held:   February 15, 2012 Woodbank Primary – Approximately 20 people attended February 22, 2012 Cinnabar Valley – Approximately 12 people attended At each of the meetings, the working principles of School Watch were discussed. Principals committed to an exchange of information with the group and to hold regular meetings at the school. Discussion To date the program continues to operate at Pleasant Valley, Cinnabar and Woodbank Primary. All three Principals have stated that they remain in communication with the residents and that they believe the program is having a positive effect. The program operates at the school through the Principal with minimal involvement by District staff. To date, extensive support has been offered by RCMP Crime Stoppers. Vandalism costs for the months following implementation of School Watch are as follows: Cinnabar Pleasant Valley Woodbank Primary February $0.00 February $354.21 February $0.00 March $100.99 March $0.00 March $49.59 April $92.37 April $0.00 April $0.00 May $168.24 May $0.00 May $0.00 While the School Watch Program cannot be credited for the low incidences of vandalism, it would appear to contribute together with the other programs. Conclusions Staff believe that the School Watch Program has contributed to lower incidences of vandalism. Its continued use and expansion into other schools can only help in strengthening the District’s Vandalism Program. It is staffs intention to continue to work with the RCMP to support any school interested in adopting the School Watch Program.
