BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 ACTION SHEET CONFIDENTIAL DATE: September 2, 2015 FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO SUBJECT: Becoming Sustainable – Facilities Plan Update Background: On April 22, 2015, the Board of Education directed staff to review the current 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan (EFLP) and provide a framework for a revised plan to be made available to the public no later than June 30, 2015. At the Regular Meeting of the Board on June 24, 2015, the Board received the Updated Facilities Plan 2015-2021, and directed it back to staff for further analysis to be presented at a Special Board meeting on September 2, 2015. Rationale: All plans need continuous updates as projections and recommendations need to be reviewed annually and as changing circumstances dictate. Additionally, through the budget process, student learning and facilities renewal have been linked with “Becoming Sustainable”. The updated plan addresses excess capacity, sustainability and facilities renewal. Specific amendments and changes to Becoming Sustainable Facilities Plan Update from the original 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan are as follows: 1) Two advisory committees will be established to consider: a. Next step recommendations for the redevelopment of the Nanaimo District Secondary site, b. Next step recommendations for the redevelopment of school facilities in the community of Ladysmith. 2) Advancement of the closure of Woodlands Secondary School to June 2016; 1 3) Review of locations of Learn@home 8-12 as well as the district programs located at Nanaimo District Secondary, John Barsby Community and Woodlands Secondary schools; 4) Closure of Rutherford Elementary in June 2017, with an additional wing created at Frank J. Ney Elementary; 5) Closure of Woodbank Primary or North Cedar Intermediate to June 2016 and the amalgamation at either site for September 2016; 6) A study to be performed during the 2016-17 school year for the Nanaimo Central Zone (Elementary) with recommendations for future school developments; 7) Amalgamation of Learn@Home K-7 and Learn@Home 8-12 into one location within two years. Recommendation: Staff recommends the following Motion: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the Becoming Sustainable – Facilities Plan Update, in principle, for distribution and public consultation, and that the public consultation process provided under Policy No. 3605 begin September 16, 2015. 2
