Objective Increase the opportunity for outdoor education and programming in the district. Overview Within the board’s goal to be a leader in Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability, the Department of Learning Services explored the topic of Outdoor Education and how students and staff can learn on and from the land in a safe and sustainable way. Strategies within the Operational Plan were developed to meet the objective of increasing opportunities for outdoor education and programming in the district. During the 2021-2022, and 2022-2023 school years, the district supported teachers to complete training in Outdoor Education. This included seventy-two teachers certified with the Outdoor Council of Canada (OCC) Hiking Field Leader Level One. In addition, several teachers have completed courses to better prepare them to lead Outdoor Education programs, including Wilderness First Aid, Ocean and Marine Training, Remote First Aid, and Lake Water Advanced Instructor Certificate. We continue to invest in the staff who are leading our outdoor experiences for our learners. Release time for teachers to collaborate and provide mentorship helps build capacity throughout our system. Schools continue to expand learning opportunities for students, both at the elementary and secondary levels. Page 8 of 19 When surveyed, elementary schools indicated that most divisions are spending more time outside learning on the land. Schools report that there has been a growth in outdoor learning opportunities this year compared to previous school years. Secondary schools are also continuing to expand outdoor education opportunities for students. Schools with established Outdoor Education programs continue to see high interest among students. Cedar Secondary Community School runs two classes each year, Ladysmith Secondary typically runs four classes of Outdoor Education programming each year, and NDSS offers five or six classes each year. John Barbsy Community School received support in 2022/2023 to offer two classes of Outdoor Education and was able to fill two complete classes for 2023/2024. Wellington Secondary has grown from three classes in 2022/2023 to four classes in 2023/2024. Dover Bay received support for two of their six Outdoor Education courses in 2022/2023 and grew to ten classes for the 2023/2024 year. Currently, the AP is drafted in such a way to limit service animals to student specific circumstances. The changes are intended to allow more general use of service dogs to support the social and emotional health of our students while at the same time assuring the safety of our students. The District is hopeful that by providing additional access, alongside our partners at St. John’s Ambulance, Nanaimo, that we will see benefits to our students. It should be noted that only appropriately certified service dogs will be allowed. The expansion is not intended to encourage individual staff to bring their pets to school, even where they are well trained. Out of interest, more information about the program can be found here https://sja.ca/en/communityservices/therapy-dog-program Background This memo will outline changes to AP 249 - Animals in Schools that provide for expanded access to service dogs in our school to support positive educational and mental health outcomes.
