Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, B.C. V9R 3K6 Apr 10, 2012 To the Board of Education, I am concerned that the proposed cut in educational days by extending Spring Break an additional 4 days will negatively affect my children’s education within School District 68. I am surprised that the Board of Education is bringing this proposal back to the table this year when it was previously voted down. The results of last year’s online survey were concerning to me. I read most of the comments and it appeared that the majority of the respondents wanting to extend the break just simply wanted an extra week off. Nobody suggested that the extra week off would be a positive benefit to the students education. I hope the Board takes the results of the survey with a grain of salt as I suspect they will be weighted heavily with students voting in favor of the extended break. I can’t see how the proposed extra 32 min a week will be quality instructional time. It’s just that I’m not sure the children are capable of absorbing the additional information each day. More information is being crammed into fewer instructional days. I believe that two weeks is too long to shut the educational system down for. Some children’s minds will have to be restarted and refreshed after such a long break. I can imagine a struggling math student having even more trouble getting back up to speed when classes resume. I think that the core subjects of Math and English have to come first. If you need to make cuts then you shouldn’t be doing it in a place that may affect these core subjects. Most of the children's extra curricular activities like, gymnastics, music and swimming all continue through the extended break. Normally all of these activities only run during the school year and they have the same breaks as SD68. It appears that the rest of the community isn’t on board with a 2 week spring break. A number of parents are already pulling their children out of class for other extended holidays and sick days. Add this to the amount of non instructional days in a year and I don’t see how we can afford to shorten the school year even further. I am also concerned that there are many low income families that will have to bear additional cost for childcare for the extended break. Hopefully the board thinks about all of these factors when making the decision regarding altering the school calendar. We are not in support of the proposed change. Regards, Sandy and Colin Bollinger 5443 Mildmay Rd Nanaimo BC
