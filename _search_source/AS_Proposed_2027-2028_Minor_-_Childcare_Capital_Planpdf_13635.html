NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 10, 2026 Strategic Directions Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Proposed 2027/2028 (Minor – Childcare) Capital Plan Recommendation The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith), in accordance with provisions under section 142(4) of the School Act, approve the proposed Minor Capital Child Care Plan for 2027/2028, as provided, for submission to the Ministry of Infrastructure, Education & Child Care Capital Branch Background The Ministry response letter approved 2026/2027 capital requests in March of 2026, and subsequently the Ministry Capital Plan instructions have requested that the following Minor Capital submissions for the 2027/2028 year be submitted by the following dates: May 15, 2026 – 2027/28 Childcare Capital Program (SASG) September 29, 2026 – 2027/28 Minor Capital Programs (SEP, CNCP, PEP, BUS, FIP, BEP) Minor Childcare Captial Program (school age on school grounds - SASG) items are small scale projects intended to convert an existing K -12 space to create new childcare spaces as well as the purchase and installation of ﬁxtures, furniture, and equipment required to obtain a license. After coordination with Learning Services, the following childcare projects were submitted. Due to a short submission date as mandated by the captial plan guidelines, the projects were submitted on May 15th, 2026. The Ministry understands that the motion supporting the submission will follow based on the board meetings scheduled. To this end, staﬀ timed this motion with the Major Capital Plan discussion. The remainder of the minor capital submission will be brought forward to a meeting prior to the submission deadline of September 29th. Typically, staﬀ bring forward the major and minor plans together in June. This change is due to further discussion required with Ministry staﬀ to consider alignment of our minor programs with Clean BC carbon reduction goals. This additional discussion was suggested by ministry staﬀ as a result of the advocacy letter sent by the board.
