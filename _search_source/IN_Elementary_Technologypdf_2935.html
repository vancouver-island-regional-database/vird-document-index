Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools Board of Education Education Committee Information Sheet DATE: March 2, 2016 TO: Education Committee FROM: Steve Sproston, Manager – Information Services SUBJECT: Elementary Technology Governance To meet the needs of education into the future, NLPS needs a governance structure of educational stakeholders. The following two groups are tasked to move districts forward, specifically with education technology in the elementary classroom.  The District’s Educational Technology Advisory Group (ETAG) ETAG is comprised of representatives from the following educational stakeholder groups: o o o o o o o Elementary and Secondary Teachers Student support services Elementary and Secondary Principals Instructional Technology (Director) Teacher-Librarians Department of Learning Services (Assistant Superintendent) and Information Services (Manager) The role of ETAG is to provide input into educational technology to be used in strategic capital planning.  The District’s Educational Technology Management Group (ETMG) is comprised of: o Assistant Superintendent o Director of Instructional Technology, and o Information Services Manager The role of ETMG is to make final recommendations to the Administrative Council Executive (ACE) relative to strategic educational initiatives, and considering account budgets and human resources. The following initiatives are recommendations based on the result of this process. Page 1 of 2 Technology Refresh Teacher laptops: Every elementary teacher was provided with a laptop in 2014-15. Elementary Labs: We have begun the process of refreshing all of the elementary labs with new equipment. Fifteen schools will receive their labs this school year, with the remaining schools equipped for next school year. Going Forward Google Apps for Education (GAFE) and Chromebooks Offered by Google, GAFE is an integrated communication and collaboration solution for the classroom. The core apps are Google Classroom (classroom management system), Email, Sheets (spreadsheet), Docs (word processing), Slides (presentation) and Calendar. The Chromebook is an inexpensive browsing appliance that allows students to access all of the Google suite. We are currently piloting both GAFE and Chromebooks and expect to make a decision about moving forward for the 2016 budgeting process. Equity Equity needs to be a cornerstone of any strategy in NLPS. The district needs to ensure that we provide a core suite of technology for all schools so that no school is reliant on PAC funding for educational technology. All of the technology will be centrally sourced and bought in bulk to increase efficiency.
