TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron Name of School(s) Visited:      Learning Alternatives (Junior, Senior, Raven’s Lelum & About Programs) Career Technical Centre (Introduction to Trades) Georgia Avenue (Skills for Life program) Bayview Elementary Forest Park Activities happening at school location: a. Related to school goals/school plan:  Learning Alternatives: District Principal of Learning Alternatives, Brett Hancock, took me on a tour of the Junior, Senior and Raven’s Lelum Learning Alternatives programs located at Barsby and the ABOUT program located at Woodlands. It was wonderful to have the opportunity to visit these programs and to see first hand the positive educational impact these programs have on our students. I became very aware through my visits with students and staff that the strong relationships build through these learning environments positively contribute to the student’s overall well being and learning that results.  Career Technical Centre, Introduction to Trades program I had the pleasure of visiting the Career Technical Centre’s Introduction to Carpentry program in their new location at Woodlands. Although still getting settled, the staff and students were excited about their new space and the long term potential of remaining in this space. I was able to see some of the great work being done by these students and to learn more about the career opportunities in trades that are made available to students through this program.  Georgia Avenue Life Skills program: It was a pleasure to have a visit at the Skills for Life program at Georgia Ave. The Life Skills program teacher, Daniel Dawes, took myself and Trustee McKay for a tour and visit where we met with students and dedicated staff. As always, I was pleased to see the great learning happening through this program and the community built at Georgia Avenue as they get settled since their move from Rutherford. The need for an accessible playground for the students remains.  Bayview Elementary: Trustees and staff were invited to share breakfast with many of the Bayview Elementary school staff. Bayview Principal, Kevin Brand, presented the great work happening at Bayview around reconciliation including language lessons. Adam Mason, and other district staff, spoke to us about the work occurring in the district teaching Hul’q’umin’um’ to our students. We then had the opportunity to participate in a fun, engaging classroom language lesson along with students. I was deeply moved and impressed by the learning taking place, both through the mind and heart, occurring at Bayview with the goal of reconciliation.  Forest Park Elementary: I had the pleasure of attending Forest Park’s Holiday Concert. Families filled the gymnasium to see students perform which included songs, acting and dance. The concert was well organized and entertaining. I was happy to see the kid’s proud faces as they performed and was happy to share in the laughter that filled the gym from the kid’s jokes integrated into many of the performances. Vice Chair Lisa Marie Barron – Trustee Report Related to Board goals/Strategic Plan  Learning Alternatives programs (Junior, Senior and Raven’s Lelum) located at Barsby: These programs, and the students and staff within, are experiencing space constraints and is a factor to be considered by the board as we move forward with our upcoming and ongoing Strategic Planning and Long Range Facilities planning processes. Possible areas of support from the Board for future planning/budgeting  Georgia Avenue’s Skills for Life program Continued communication with Georgia Avenue’s staff and community around the status and progress surrounding playground plans. Upcoming Event  The finishing touches are being made to a new school sign at Learning Alternatives, ABOUT program, Woodlands location. The date of unveiling will soon be announced and trustees will be invited to attend at this time. Other items to Report: Met with Provincial Candidates along, with Trustee Mckay, to discuss education in our district and how the candidates, if elected, would work in partnership with our board to ensure public education for our students is made a priority. Attended BCSTA/BCPSEA trustee two-day academy. Continued trustee learning, networking and knowledge sharing.
