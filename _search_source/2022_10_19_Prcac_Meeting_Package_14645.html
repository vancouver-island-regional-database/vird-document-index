Motion Carried Page 2|2 PRC DEPARTMENT UPDATES PROGRAM AREA SUMMARY: Health & Wellness On average, Fitness Centre participant usage for September was around 1,800, with an additional 180 users participating in drop-in fitness and sport classes and sessions. In addition, we offered 21 registered health and wellness programs, with over 200 registered participants. With the September weather being so amazing and people spending more time in local parks and trails, user numbers are lower in comparison to past Septembers. However, with school back in session, the Fitness Centre is seeing an increase in student attendance and participation. Many youth are booking orientations with Fitness Attendants in the Fitness Centre, which is a positive sign that they are keen to learn about proper form and exercise technique. Aquatics On average, there were 2,000 participants in drop-in aquatic programs. Demand for aquatic fitness programs and classes continue to be high, with high levels of participation in each class. Drop-in attendance to swims such as Early Bird, Length and Everyone Welcome swims is lower than normal, in comparison to past September statistics, but similar to Fitness Centre attendance, we feel the weather is a factor. As with most organizations, we continue to face staffing challenges in the Aquatic department. Program Supervisors continue to work diligently on recruitment strategies and aquatic leadership opportunities, with the hope to increase staffing capacity. Recreation-Preschool/Children/Youth PreTeen is back with full registration. 16 youth in attendance every Wednesday afternoon. The group has been playing a variety of sports in the gymnasium, including pickleball. Rec Room @ Lunch has reopened on Tuesdays at lunch. The Youth Zone is open on Friday nights from 6 - 8 p.m. and has regular attendance since September 16th. General Recreation & Sport Fall 22 programs are underway and we are providing high quality programs and workshops for the community - with favourite programs and also some new opportunities with gardening, ballet, music and art. FACILITY MAINTENANCE SUMMARY: Facility Projects The projects at Transfer Beach are complete with the exception of the washrooms. One key component is currently being manufactured before it can be installed. Another project that was completed at Transfer Beach was the removal of the old concession building. The old structure was deemed beyond repair and was removed in late August. Removing this building has resulted in increased views of the water from the large grassy area. A new addition to the park was the canoe shelter. Its purpose is to help protect the ‘Salish Wind’ canoe from the elements. Please see the attached pictures of before and after of the Kinsmen & Sportsman shelters, washrooms, and the removal of the concession building. Included are some pictures of the new Salish Wind canoe shelter. Community LAFF  LAFF had their official opening of the shelter this past Wednesday. With the nice weather the shelter has been a great addition to Aggie Park. The new shelter will be well utilized by LAFF and the community. Vaccination Centre  The vaccination clinic at Davis Rd. Elementary is still ongoing and open every Monday. The clinic is seeing a steady stream of public and will continue their program until the end of December.
