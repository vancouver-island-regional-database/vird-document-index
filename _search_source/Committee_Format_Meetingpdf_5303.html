Committee Format Meeting - May 24, 2016 at Board Office Attendees: Regrets: Graham Roberts, John Blain, Stephanie Higginson, Tania Brzovic Scott Kimler * Notes reflect conversation during the meeting and input from members after the fact.  Decision to review previous suggestions. Two items seen as basically housekeeping matters.  2.2 to be adapted: "A Closed Session of each Regular Meeting shall be held as early at 4:00 p.m. based on the agenda. This meeting will occur before the Regular Meeting of the Board unless there is insufficient business to warrant such a meeting or unless a change in starting time has been communicated at least 48 hours in advance. Should the business be incomplete at the 6:00 p.m., the Closed Session shall be recessed and reconvened following the Regular Meeting."  4.7.5 to be adapted: "Persons wishing to ask questions of the board upon completion of Committee and Board sessions shall do in writing on the form provided which includes their name, street address and email address. The back of the Question Period Form will display conditions outlined in 4.7.2, 4.7.3 and 4.7.4. The question will be reviewed by the Chair to ensure the question is appropriate and follows outlined procedures. The individual will be asked to verify his/her self and be allowed the opportunity to read out their own question. Each individual shall be limited to one follow-up question upon receiving a response. Questions and responses will be included in the video of the meeting and the meeting minutes and will be part of the public record"  There was considerable discussion regarding 4.1. There was general consensus that this review involving who sits at the table and how meetings are to occur would require considerable thought. Having trustees attend a retreat or a special meeting may be called for to get further feedback from the entire Board. Jeff concurred with feedback from Scott that representatives should not be at the table. Reference to Eli Mina who expressed concern over this as it allows potential for stakeholders to lobby and influence votes. Are we getting the best feedback possible from the right people? Should we have more input from teachers, students, community etc. John stated that the unions have ample opportunity to provide input during regular discussions with administration. How best to involve students? Perhaps a Student Council who we can meet with every few months. Jeff wanted stakeholders available but not at the table. Lobbying trustees during Board sessions was problematic as is tabling & bringing back motions, which further allows stakeholders the opportunity to continue to lobby & influence decisions. Scott felt strongly that there is a need to re-configure the boardroom seating and changing the table was an option. Tania was less concerned about moving in and out of motions and did not see that as a reason to change our format. We all agreed that having our backs to staff is a concern. There was some uncertainty as to how and when stakeholders could provide input.   Stephanie suggested a different format whereby there would be an education committee and a business committee with a broad range of attendees along with 4 trustees each. These meeting would occur during the day. Recommendations would then be brought to one evening meeting of the Committee of the Whole which would also allow for presentations. Idea is that the work be done in the committees then brought to the entire Board for further discussion before going to a final decision at the Board meeting. Some concerns expressed: How do we choose the attendees? Would the entire Board be okay with recommendations?  We all agree that input from stakeholder groups was important but Steph's biggest objection is that we have created a committee of the whole with stakeholders at the table having the ability to lobby the Board about motions at the time the motion is being debated. She would like staff to have a larger role in the discussion. A correct balance was called for. Steph provided input from other jurisdictions regarding how they conduct their business. While the committee structures are different for every single board, ours is very unique in that we have every trustee on every committee. She thought that we could structure things better.  It was discussed that we would seek input from our Union reps but there is no agreement that this is called for nor appropriate.  First two items to be reviewed and sent to June business committee. Board to be made aware that discussions were occurring. Previous Board motion was to have recommendations regarding format changes brought to the Board by the end of June. This may not occur.
