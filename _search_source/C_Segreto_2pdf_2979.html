January 7, 2015 Dear School District Trustees and Staff, We would like to thank you again for allowing us to present to you yesterday regarding a possible French Immersion program at Ladysmith Secondary School. We hope that we were able to show that such a program would be feasible in Ladysmith with relatively low costs but with high benefits to the School District, the students, and the community. There is perhaps some confusion regarding the current number of Grade 7 students enrolled at Ecole North Oyster (who would be potentially become the first Grade 8 Fl class at LSSif the program was to go ahead.) There have been 13 students in the class for the past 2 years, which can be confirmed by the school's principal, Jacqueline Catchpole. We would also like to address some of Trustee Brennan's concerns regarding a French Immersion program at LSS. He mentioned that former Superintendent Mike Munro stated that when the Board approved the FI program to start at Ecole Davis Road Elementary back in 2004, there would be no plans for a Fl program to be introduced at LSSin the future. But over the subsequent years, there have been many plans made by the School Boards of the time, whichhave also changed. For example, for three years beginning in 2010, Ecole Davis Road was prioritized in the District's 5-Year Capital Plan as #1 to be replaced. Instead, it was closed in 2014.
