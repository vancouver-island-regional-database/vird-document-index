May 22, 2019 1132864 Charlene McKay Board Chair Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools 395 Wakesiah Ave Nanaimo BC V9R 3K6 Dear Ms. McKay: Thank you for your letter of April 16, 2019, to the Honourable Adrian Dix, Minister of Health, to share your concerns and your support for a national pharmacare program. I am pleased to respond on his behalf. I would first like to recognize the considerable difficulties health problems can present and that the medical costs associated with health conditions are a significant concern for many British Columbians. As you know, PharmaCare is the Ministry of Health program that helps British Columbia residents to pay the eligible costs of many prescription drugs and certain medical supplies. Please note that the BC government is supportive of national pharmacare to improve the affordability, accessibility and appropriate use of prescription drugs for all Canadians. To guide the development of a national pharmacare program, BC proposed four key principles, which were adopted by all premiers at the July 2018 Council of the Federation meeting: • • • • Improved access through removing cost barriers for patients should be the focus; Development should be based on the best available evidence about potential benefits, risks, costs, and reliability of supply; Provinces and territories must retain responsibility for the design and delivery of public drug coverage; and Federal pharmacare funding must be long term, adequate, secure, and flexible and take into consideration present and future cost pressures. Ultimately, a national pharmacare program should result in consistent patient access across the country while ensuring best value for money. …2 Ministry of Health Office of the Assistant Deputy Minister Pharmaceutical Services Division PO BOX 9652 STN PROV GOVT Victoria BC V8W 9P4 Telephone: 250 952-1464 Fax: 250 952-1584 -2You are likely aware that on February 27, 2018, as part of Budget 2018, the federal government announced the creation of an Advisory Council on the Implementation of National Pharmacare (the Council). The Council has initiated a national dialogue that includes working closely with experts from all relevant fields as well as with national, provincial, territorial and Indigenous leaders, including in BC. The Council provides independent advice to the federal government on how to best implement national pharmacare in a manner that is affordable for Canadians and their families, employers and governments. The Council provided an interim report to the federal Ministers of Health and Finance in March 2019, which can be viewed at: https://www.canada.ca/en/healthcanada/corporate/about-health-canada/public-engagement/external-advisorybodies/implementation-national-pharmacare/interim-report.html. The Council will be submitting a report to the federal government in the summer of 2019. BC continues to address the accessibility and affordability of drugs for BC citizens through its universal income-based drug plan, Fair PharmaCare. On February 9, 2018, the BC government announced the elimination of PharmaCare deductibles for working families with the lowest incomes in the province. The $105 million being invested in Fair PharmaCare over three years will eliminate or reduce the deductibles for 240,000 BC families. As of January 1, 2019, families with net annual incomes between $15,000 and $30,000 are no longer required to pay a Fair PharmaCare deductible. Fair PharmaCare co-payments have been lowered for all families with net incomes of up to $45,000 and eliminated for families with a net income of up to $13,750. This is a crucial step forward in improving the health and lives of thousands of British Columbians. Thank you for your note expressing your concerns. We share with you the desire for a sustainable health care program that better supports the needs of British Columbians. The concerns you have raised are well taken. Sincerely, Mitch Moneo Assistant Deputy Minister Pharmaceutical Services Division
