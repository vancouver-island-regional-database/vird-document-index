ATTACHMENT A The following list includes the actions that were selected as priorities at the Dinner & Dialogue events that took place between December 2023 and May 2024. These have helped to shape the project focus moving forward and include a status update for where they are now. Participants additionally identified what resources and capacity will be needed for each action, what roles would be required and who should directly be involved. We are thrilled that a total of 22 volunteers signed up under one or more of the following actions: Theme: Public Transportation Action: Establish a community-owned and operated van/shuttle/ transportation system for gaps in transit services and/or special events (e.g., Ladysmith between Stz’uminus, the Bay, to Nanaimo, to Duncan, Diamond) ➢ Status: planning for this is underway now through a separate grant project, lead by Social Planning Cowichan and a Transportation Action Committee that includes an elected representative from the Town of Ladysmith. The dinner and dialogues have been very successful at increasing awareness about the interconnected challenges linked to transportation and this planning project has been helpful in identifying what the specific gaps and needs are for improvements while exploring the best options for addressing them. The final plan will be shared with those who participated in the community engagement over the months of April to August, 2024, with the Town of Ladysmith, CVRD, Stz’uminus First Nation and others as well as publicly on the CommUNITY Together to End Poverty Facebook page: https://www.facebook.com/communitytogethertoendpoverty Theme: Affordable Housing & Adequate Living Conditions Action: Consider an incentive program for promoting the constructions of Secondary Suites and/or Coach Houses. This program could be promoted for a limited time to encourage immediate uptake. Some examples could include a reduction in taxes for a limited period of time or waiving of full or partial development cost charges, building permit fees, etc. ➢ Status: Under section 483.1 of the Local Government Act, the Province of BC has mandated that the Town amend its zoning bylaw to permit “Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing (SSMUH)” in “Restricted Zones” by June 30, 2024. Changes are underway and being implemented now. Other avenues continue to be explored to assist with reducing the cost of housing that are within the control of the municipality. Theme: Adequate Income Supports and the Means to Make Ends Meet Action: Advocate for adequate income supports, including Universal Guaranteed Basic Income. ➢ Status: This is a challenging action to implement locally, however the Senate is studying it through Bill S-233. Future exploration may be possible with those interested in joining actions being taken at the Basic Income Canada Network as they advocate for a national program. ATTACHMENT A Theme: Food Security/Equity Action: Encourage community-supported school breakfast and lunch programs for all children ➢ Status: New provincial and federal funding is being allocated to the school districts with each school determining how to allocate their funds based on school needs. Our regionally assigned Dietician with Island Health is working with the schools to explore options for improving access for healthy food for students and sits on the Food Equity Sub-Committee as part of the PRTG. We will check-in in the fall to see what action may be possible to support this work. ➢ In the meantime, Ladysmith Family and Friends (LaFF) through their programming and free outdoor pantry and Ladysmith Resource Centre Association (LRCA) with their programming and the Food Bank support getting food to where it is needed most. You are welcome to reach out to either or both to access their services and/or support their work by volunteering or donating today! Theme: Access to Mental Health and Addiction Services Action: Improve awareness about the range of mental health services and supports available locally & extend mental health services from larger centers to make them available in Ladysmith on a regular basis through satellite offices (including psychiatric nurse, outreach support, harm reduction services) ➢ Status: We have had both an Island Health representative on the PRTG as well as the Executive Director of the LRCA discussing where improvements can be made here as both provide mental health services. The LRCA is exploring ways for funding and expanding their current services. More work can be done to explore other options. Themes: Food Security/Equity, Addressing Stigma, Discrimination & Racism & Mental Discomfort and Stress Action: Establish more community gardens, shared garden spaces, fruit tree harvesting/gleaning and shared food preservation and community seed, plants and fruit tree donations/exchanges/libraries. With a focus on: • Action: Building relationships between Indigenous and non-Indigenous communities with shared activities, gatherings, events. AND • Action: Organizing regularly scheduled community events and social gatherings that are free for a variety of ages. ➢ Status: Some funding has been applied for already by various partnering community organizations for continuing the community dinners, encouraging social gatherings among Indigenous and non-Indigenous communities and engaging in food equity building activities. It is the goal of the PRTG members that they will continue to work together in coordinating some of these activities and events in a collaborative manner.
