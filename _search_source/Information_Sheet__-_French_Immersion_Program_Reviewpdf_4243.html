BOARD OF EDUCATION School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) REGULAR BOARD MEETING INFORMATION SHEET TO: Board of Education RE: French Immersion Program Review FROM: Michael J. Munro, Superintendent/CEO Pete Sabo, Director of Facilities DATE: February 28, 2011 _______________________________________________________________________________________ Background: At the January 5th, 2011 Board Meeting the following motion was passed: Folio S11/01/05-24 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to provide a status report once the RFPs for the French Immersion study have been evaluated. Discussion: On January 28th at 2pm the Request for Proposals process closed at the SD68 office in accordance with tender policy and procedures. One proposal was received from Trillium Infrastructure Solution Inc. (in association with Ladyman Consulting, and Alkins Project Services) Copies of the proposal were circulated and on February 2nd. The proposal was reviewed by Mike Munro, Superintendent/CEO, Francine Frisson, Assistant Superintendent, Chris Southwick Assistant Superintendent, Phil Turin, Secretary Treasurer and Pete Sabo, Director of Facilities. Using the criteria listed in the RFP package it was determined that the proposal received met the requirements of the terms of reference and that the French Immersion Program review should be awarded immediately to Trillium Infrastructure solution Inc.(TIS) so that the study would be completed on or before the target date of May 31, 2011. At the meeting it was also agreed to award the project to TIS subject to a discussion regarding expenses and possible option to reduce fees. A staff steering committee of Mike Munro, Francine Frisson, Mike Lundine, Dave Stupich, Pete Sabo and Donna Reimer was selected to work with the consultant. Subsequently staff worked with TIS to identify possible savings, agreed that a budget of $50,000 should be carried in the 11/12 AFG for the project and on February 18th the project was awarded to TIS. On February 24th the steering committee met with the consultant to review the work plan and possible fee savings.
