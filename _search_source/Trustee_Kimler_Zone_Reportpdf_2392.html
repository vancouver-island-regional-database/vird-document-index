Trustee Zone Report 26-Sep-2018 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)    LSS LEARNING COMMONS WEBSITE – Students at Ladysmith should be thankful that they have an active and engaged Teacher-Librarian in Allison Girard. She started a SquareSpace blog in 2017 and launched a Library Learning Commons Website in 2018 (which is partly based upon an original Learning Commons WIKI by by retired Teacher-Librarian Deborah Graham. The Learning Commons Website can be accessed at www.lss-library.com and is broken into four main categories: Learning Commons Services; Research (links & citation information); Books & Reading (blog compendium) & Library Catalogue (links into the SD68 Destiny library management system and audio books). Kudos to Allison Girard! ENROLMENT – LSS has an enrolment of 610 students MYED PARENT PORTAL – All secondary schools hosted a portal information night. LSS held theirs on Sept 20th. Parents were shown how to access and log into the system and the type of information they can access: ongoing student learning, attendance & report cards (no more paper report cards). École North Oyster (ENO)  A NEW FOCU SCHOOL – As a new focus school, ENO is now getting more supports: A Child & Youth Care worker full-time (instead of 3 days a week; a half-time reading support person and the staff are getting professional learning & taking a team-based approach with the goal of a 90% literacy rate by the end of grade 3.  PLAYGROUND UPDATE – After winning $100k from the BCAA Playspace contest, PAC and staff have been working toward designing a playground. BCAA is a partner in this effort and the next design meeting will be on Oct. 15th. Thanks to Pete Sabo & Tim Davie for stepping in and lending a hand on this project. Construction is anticipated to begin in 2019.  ENROLMENT – ENO has 340 students and 4 portables. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  NO REPORT Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  OPEN HOUSE – LIS is hosting their open house on September 27th. It’s a great opportunity to meet teachers and see first-hand all the things happening in the classroom.  ENROLMENT – LIS is close to 300 students for 2018/19, organized into 11 divisions.
