NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: November 4, 2020 Education Committee Tim Davie, Deputy Superintendent Administrative Procedure 249 – Animals in School Settings Background Staff have proposed that Administrative Procedure 249 – Animals in School Settings (AP 249) be updated. The update to AP 249 will provide a framework to support school teams in facilitating open communication and best practices with respect to therapeutic animals. This updated administrative procedure will also align with other districts. Information Highlights of the significant amendments to AP 249 are as follows: • • • • • • • Current AP 249 did not make any reference to therapeutic animals Service Animals and Therapy Animals serve two different roles. We have amalgamated the both terms into “Assistance Dogs” This update allows students to utilize an Assistance Dog where there is a specific diagnosed disability, and a medical professional has identified this support as key to the learner’s success at school The draft AP 249 provides a clear description of school, district, family, and handler responsibilities It ensures that any animals present in school buildings will be thoroughly trained and certified in providing services to students AP 249 contains an Assistance Animal Agreement, outlining the key aspects of this AP, to ensure everyone understands the expectations Additionally, there are supportive template letters and signage for school teams to use, where an assistance animal will be present in the school Appendix A: Updated Administrative Procedure 249 – Animals in School Settings Appendix B: Administrative Procedure 249 – Adopted: Nov 21, 2016
