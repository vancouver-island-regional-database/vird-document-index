Kelt, Executive Assistant Meeting No. S1 CALL TO ORDER The Chair called the meeting to order at 5:10 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. APPROVAL OF THE AGENDA IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Howland Folio S12/09/12-01 That the agenda be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS 2011-2012 Audited Financial Statements Mr. Turin said the statements report that the district ended the school year with a surplus of $3.2 million. Of that, $2.7 million was identified as restricted surplus, earmarked for specific purposes in the 2012-13 budget and $480,117 is an unrestricted operating surplus. He said when the Board set the 2012-13 budget, it planned that there would be $1.6 million in surplus funds from last year to bring forward into this year’s budget. Some of the purposes for which the surplus has been restricted include school supplies and learning resources, Aboriginal education programs, technology renewal, and equipment. Mr. Turin said that the surplus is a result of controls that district management put on spending last year, along with funding protection money that the district received from the Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held September 12, 2012 2 provincial government. He pointed out that over the past 12 years, the district has only has a deficit once and that all of the other 11 years the district has been able to end the financial year with a surplus. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Welch IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Neary Folio S12/09/12-02 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approves the 2011-2012 Audited Financial Statements.
