McGIRR ELEMENTARY SCHOOL HEATING AND VENTILATION SYSTEMS REVIEW for the 2013/14 CARBON NEUTRAL CAPITAL PROGRAM Prepared For: SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 Prepared By: RPE File 12422-N721 December 2012 RPE File 12422-N721 McGirr Elementary School CNCP December 2012 Page 1 INTRODUCTION Rocky Point Engineering Ltd. has been engaged by School District 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) to review the condition and age of the above noted school and provide recommendations for implementation of strategies that will reduce fossil fuel consumption, overall building energy use and greenhouse gas emissions and improve comfort and safety of the school’s occupants. McGirr Elementary School in Nanaimo is 4,174 square metres (44,912 square feet) in overall area, originally constructed in 1990, with a major addition and renovation project completed in 1998. EXISTING HEATING EQUIPMENT The heating boilers installed are three Weil-McLean cast iron, atmospheric appliances, with a total combined input rating of 2400 MBH. Two of the boilers date original construction of the school in 1990, while the third boiler was added as part of the 1998 addition. Improved energy efficient appliances are available today, which will assist in reducing energy consumption costs and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The boiler plant is currently piped with no primary pumps on individual boilers, so when the plant is active water flows through all three boilers. In this instance the non-firing boilers act as reverse heat exchangers whenever their interior temperature is lower than the heating loop temperature, resulting in wasted energy. No. 1 – Existing heating plant consisting of 3 Weil-McLean atmospheric boilers, with no primary pumps No. 2 – Piping in the boiler room is in reasonable condition and can be incorporated into the upgraded plant. RPE File 12422-N721 McGirr Elementary School CNCP December 2012 Page 2 RECOMMENDED UPGRADE STRATEGY Heating Plant The focus of this funding application is strictly related to replacement of the old boilers with new, higher efficiency condensing boilers, reconfiguring existing piping into a primarysecondary arrangement and new digital controls for the new heating plant. Primary pumps should be provided with any new boilers. The school has four secondary zone pumps, which are aged and should also be replaced. DDC Controls As part of the proposed upgrade it is important to include a new digital controls system to allow more precise temperature control within the building and energy management strategies to be implemented. Time of day and week scheduling of boiler equipment operation will help control building energy consumption.
