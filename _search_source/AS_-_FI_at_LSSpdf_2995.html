BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: January 27, 2016 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO RE: Program Development of French Immersion Grades 8 and 9 at Ladysmith Secondary Background The Board approved in the Facilities Plan Update that staff “Discuss and review the feasibility of creating a Grades 8 and 9 French Immersion program at Ladysmith Secondary (LSS).” Since the move of Ecole Davis Road students to North Oyster Elementary School, the K-7 French Immersion program has shown continued success. Parents and staff have brought forward the idea of having a Grades 8 and 9 French Immersion program at LSS, with French Immersion students currently choosing to either transition to LSS for their regular Dogwood Certificate or go to NDSS for their dual-track Dogwood Certificate. The district would also look at the potential of attracting out-of-catchment and out-of-district students to register for LSS French Immersion. Along with direction through the 10-year Facilities Plan and the Facilities Plan Update, staff heard from the Ladysmith Parents Group and the district French Immersion Principals Group in support of French Immersion Grades 8 and 9 program at LSS. As well, the French Immersion Review of 2011 (Stewart Ladyman) both supports and recommends the overall growth and, specifically the Ladysmith growth of the French Immersion program. Proposal 1. This proposal would support the creation of a Grades 8 and 9 program for Ladysmith Secondary School French Immersion (LSS FI). Staff have identified 13 students at both Grades 6 and 7 at North Oyster FI who would be interested in LSS FI for Grades 8 and 9, indicating that in two years, there would be enough students registered to run a combined class size of approximately 26 students. 2. Staff believe that there would be an additional annual enrolment of 3-4 students from outside the district. 3. To ensure adequate staffing for the program for only one group of Grade 8 students during year one, the district would require a maximum of .250 fte support. This would be removed after the first year, when the second group of Grade 8 students registers at LSS FI. 4. The additional cost of materials and text books would be acquired from the support of the district’s French Immersion Principals Group and the Department of Learning Services. 5. This pilot program is contingent on a minimum annual registration of 13 FI students. *Baragar suggests that these numbers will increase gradually to 16 - 18 students arriving from North Oyster. Staff will also track students from outside of the district and monitor the program success. Page 1 of 2 *Based on Baragar Projections Budget .250 FTE Staffing (2016/17) $26,000 Supplies and Textbooks (2016/17 & 2017/18) 10,000 Total for full implementation of Pilot $36,000 Recommendation That the Board of Education approves that the Department of Learning Services and Ladysmith Secondary School staff continue with the implementation plan to create a Grade 8 French Immersion Program at Ladysmith Secondary School to begin September 2016. The pilot program would be offered based on a minimum of 13 students registering for Grade 8. This program would move forward to include Grades 8 and 9 for September 2017. Page 2 of 2
