Agenda Item Number 14.1 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) ACTION SHEET DATE: October 20, 2010 TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Facilities SUBJECT: Vandalism Initiative – Mosquito Device Introduction The following motion was passed at the Business Committee meeting held on June 23, 2010 by Trustee members of the committee: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to investigate implementation of ‘mosquito’ devices at two SD 68 sites during the 2010-2011 school year, and to report to the October Business Committee on the costs and implementation issues for the Board’s consideration. What is a Mosquito The Mosquito is an electronic device intended to disperse groups of people by emitting a harmless but highly irritating high frequency sound that can generally be heard only by young people. Background In accordance with the above motion, Facilities staff identified four schools which were deemed good potential candidates for the use of the “mosquito” device based on a history of vandalism, costs and impacts to school operations. Staff spoke with the principals of each school with respect to their thoughts as to whether the mosquito would be effective in reducing vandalism at their school. Discussions All four individuals responded positively that the mosquito would likely reduce loitering and therefore vandalism at their school site. All individuals indicated that they would like to have their facility considered as one of the two SD68 sites mentioned in the above motion and that, if chosen as one of the sites, agreed to provide feedback to the District on its operation. Staff were then assigned to investigate the installation of mosquitos at the four facilities. Investigations in the installation of the mosquitos included discussion with the manufacturer’s representative regarding technical and operational issues, site visits to determine suitable locations, availability of electrical supply, etc. Vandalism Initiative - Mosquito Device Page 8 of 13 ... Agenda Item Number 14.1 Based on review of the sites, it would appear the sites have at least one primary area where most of the vandalism occurs. Secondary areas on sites were also investigated considering that if a mosquito was placed in the primary area it might drive the vandalism to the secondary area. Consideration for installing mosquitos in all areas was given. The final investigation of costs indicates that the average cost to locate a single mosquito is approximately $2,000 for supply and installation. Some sites require more than one mosquito in the primary area(s). Also, costs to cover secondary areas were considered. Rock City $4,000 $4,000 $8,000 Primary Areas (covered play area at the Annex and playground) Secondary Area (two covered play areas on main building) Total Uplands $4,000 $2,000 $6,000 Primary Areas (covered play area near gym and court yard) Secondary Area (by Kindergarten area) Total If approved projects would be immediately scheduled for installation, subject to availability of product. Recommendation That staff recommends to the Business Committee the installation of the mosquito device at Rock City and Uplands, in the primary locations only, for a total of $8,000.00 and that the installation be funded from the Vandalism Initiative Budget approved in the 2010/2011 Annual Facilities Grant. Agenda Item Number 14.3 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 OFFICE OF THE SECRETARY-TREASURER INFORMATION SHEET TO: Business Committee FROM: J. David Green/Pete Sabo DATE: October 14, 2010 SUBJECT: Update on Rental Rates Previous to the 2010-2011 fiscal year the district included the GST in the base published rental rate. The amount of revenue actually recorded was therefore net of the GST. When the HST came into effect staff added the 12% to the published rental rates so that the revenue recorded was in fact the published rate. In addition all rental rates were increased by 10%. The result of this is that all contracts received at least an increase of 10% and some contracts up to 22%.
