The Board is responsible for monitoring the overall organizational health and financial performance. Reviewing and approving financial statements is one of the ways the Board fulfills its mandate for oversight. Recommendation That the Business Committee recommend that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) approve the School District Statement of Financial Information (SOFI Report), as presented, for the year ending June 30, 2019. Background The Board of Education is responsible for ensuring that management fulfils its responsibility for financial reporting and internal control and for approving the financial information included in the Statement of Financial Information. Pursuant to Sections 156 (Accounting Practices) and 157 (Financial Statements) of the School Act and Ministerial Order 033/09, Boards of Education (Boards) must submit their financial statements in the form required by the Minister. The Audited Financial Statements were due September 30th. They were approved by the Board of Education on September 25th, 2019, through the following motion: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive and approved the 2018-2019 Audited Financial Statements which includes the surplus allocation. School Districts are corporations as explained in Schedule 1 of the Financial Information Act. For consistency with the Budget Transparency and Accountability Act respecting Public Accounts reporting, a Statement of Financial Information (SOFI) must be submitted by each school district within six months following the corporation's fiscal year end. The Board’s year end is June 30 th and therefore, the SOFI report is due by December 31 st. The SOFI consists of an Approval of Statement of Financial Information (SOFI), Management Report, Audited Financial Statements, Schedule of Debt, Schedule of Guarantee and Indemnity Agreements, Schedule of Remuneration and Expenses, Statement of Severance Agreements, Schedule of Payments for Goods and Services and a Reconciliation and Explanation of Differences to Audited Financial Statements. The BC Legislative Library retains previous years' Statements of Financial Information submitted by school districts via the Ministry, and school districts may be contacted to obtain their statements. The Ministry of Education compiles the statements annually. Statements of Financial Information (SOFI) for all school districts reporting under the Ministry of Education are available on the Ministry website.
