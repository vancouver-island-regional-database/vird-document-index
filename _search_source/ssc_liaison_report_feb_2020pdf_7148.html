Jessica Stanley February 2020 Safe School Committee Liaison Report Updates - Staff informed group about provincial and island based SOGI work. At a provincial level, a SOGI framework is being considered to create standardization of SOGI inclusive efforts and to share efforts/challenges. A minimum criterion of actual SOGI inclusive efforts is being considered for rainbow crosswalks so as to ensure the signification is meaningful. As well, cultural considerations were discussed. The SOGI Island Network discussed opportunities for non-gendered sports in schools and considerations regarding overnight fieldtrips and homestays. - SWIS workers from the Multicultural Society attended. They share information (film) and discussed concerns supports new students to Canada. The benefits of cultural sensitivity awareness and training were discussed. - Anti-racism Arts Festival and 48 hour film challenge http://www.canadianculturalmosaicfoundation.com/register-for-48-hour-challenge.html Old Business - Terms of Reference draft was discussed. Work will continue. New Business - 3 year plan draft was reviewed. SOGI work has a clear plan and a solid understand of recent work. Anti-racism and cultural sensitivity work have received less attention in recent years and a review of the working being done in schools and of the challenges currently experienced was determined as necessary before further elaborating the plan. Ideas discussed included a “safe harbour” indicator like rainbow stickers to indicate racism-aware spaces, how to create more culturally safe schools, and how to better understand and support mental health issues from a cultural perspective.
