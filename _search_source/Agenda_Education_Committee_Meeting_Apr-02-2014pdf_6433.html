The Partnership is made up of individuals and representatives from a diverse set of agencies throughout Nanaimo. The original intent of the group was to plan collective action to improve the health and development of children aged 0-6. Currently, the mission is to create a community that supports the optimal development of children and their families. Over the years, the partnership has worked diligently to raise the awareness of the importance of the early years. Currently GNEYP is funded by two sources. The Ministry for Child and Family Development (MCFD) provides Children First funding that allows GNEYP to contract a part time coordinator. This ŌĆ£backboneŌĆØ funding is intended to provide a resource to lead the initiative and, with a community development approach, work across sectors to assess, identify and plan for the unique needs of young children in their community. The impetus for the 2014 consultation process was driven by significant changes brought about from retirements, staff changes and the decision to change the working relationship between Greater Nanaimo and Ladysmith. For many years Ladysmith and Nanaimo worked with a joint leadership approach. For sustainability reasons and the desire to have an opportunity to focus on the unique needs of each community, the collaboration has been redefined with separate steering committees. The first was to examine the working structure of GNEYP in order to address barriers to meaningful participation. The second was to build an Early Years Plan which reflects the current needs and priorities as seen by both parents and services of Greater Nanaimo. Tracy Smyth and Tammy Dewar of Raising the Village Consulting were hired to conduct a community consultation process to unearth a range of perspectives required to influence a new direction. The consultation process worked to re-focus and re-energize GNEYP and better position the group to harness the enthusiasm and passion of a wider range of people who are willing and able to contribute to collaborative work to support young children and their families. The results, described in detail in this report and reflected in the 2014-2016 Greater Nanaimo Early Years Plan, address the following questions: 1. What are the priorities that will help to strengthen the effectiveness of the existing Partnership (including relationships, communication and structure)? 2. What are the priorities that will help the Partnership to best support the young children and families in the Nanaimo area? The consultation included the application of relationship mapping software, personal interviews, online surveys to potential partners and parents along with dialogue sessions at the GNEYP table and with the Nanaimo Early Years Leadership Team (NEYLT).
