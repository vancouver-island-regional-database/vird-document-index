THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: April 8, 2015 TO: Business Committee FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Dave Prevost, Transportation Supervisor SUBJECT: Report on busing to Ladysmith Secondary References (attached)  Cedar – Staff Responses to Trustees’ Questions including - January 8, 2014 Information Sheet – Report on Busing to John Barsby & Ladysmith Secondary - SD68 Transportation Policy and Procedure 4400 and 4400P  Information Sheet - Cedar Secondary Student Attendance Pattern 2013/2014 Background On March 18, 2015, the following motion was passed by the Board of Education: The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to provide and updated report, the viability and cost of providing busing for the students from the Cedar/Cinnabar area to Ladysmith Secondary School for one year. For the 2014/15 school year Baragar reports the following student attendance information: In preparing this Information Sheet, staff reviewed the previously provided information sheets on busing costs from the Cedar area to Ladysmith Secondary (LSS), considered the use of spare buses as well as the application of the Board Policy on transportation. 1 s:\secretarytreasurer\executiveassistant\2014-2015\agendas\bus com\10 - apr 8 15\april 8 2015 cedar to lss bussing info sheet.doc Staff contacted Heather Hill, Regional Director, Capital Management Branch of the Ministry of Education who confirmed that the use of spare buses for the provision of service for one year would not be objected to by the Ministry of Education. The cost estimates provided are based on transportation consistent with the service provided for Cedar students to John Barsby (JBS), the designated catchment school and in accordance with Board Policy. Current stops for students attending JBS from the Cedar area were used in the formation of the service which essentially means that a student could arrive at an existing stop and catch a bus to either JBS or to LSS. The area considered for service is the “old Cedar Secondary” catchment area which includes Woodbank Elementary, North Cedar Intermediate and Cinnabar Elementary. Please note that previously, the South Wellington catchment area was added to the current Woodbank Primary and North Cedar Intermediate catchment areas. Also note that optional busing from the old South Wellington catchment area to Chase River for K-7 students has been in effect since the closure of South Wellington. Two scenarios are presented. Scenario 1 would provide space on buses equal to the number of students currently attending LSS from the old Cedar catchment. As per the information above, this would be space for 165 students. Understanding that the provision of busing may influence more students to attend LSS from JBS, staff chose to provide a second cost estimate based on the existing 165 students, plus 50% of the existing Cedar students who currently attend JBS. In accordance with the above, this would be 100 additional students for a total number of students bused for Scenario 2 equal to 265 students. Given that staff cannot know how many students may choose to attend LSS if busing is provided, the costings are thought to provide ‘bookend’ costs for the service being considered. Discussion The cost estimates below are based on using retired/spare buses. Using retired buses on a daily basis will result in an increased repair and service costs; these estimated additional costs have not been added to the operating cost estimates below but are shown as a separate contingent cost. Costs are influenced by a number of factors:  LSS is further away from the Facilities compound; LSS is a 22.5 kilometer further drive than JBS, increasing driver wages & kilometer costs.  The buses providing bus service to JBS (or other routes) would not be able to transport students to LSS due to time/bell schedule conflicts causing the need for additional buses and drivers. 2 s:\secretarytreasurer\executiveassistant\2014-2015\agendas\bus com\10 - apr 8 15\april 8 2015 cedar to lss bussing info sheet.doc Scenario 1 - Bus Service to LSS (for 165 students) This service would be based on the current number of students attending LSS - 165. Use of Retired/Spare Buses A retired bus is one that has reached the Ministry of Education requirement for replacement and has been replaced with a new unit. Typically retired buses are disposed of. The Ministry of Education allows Districts to keep a retired bus in its fleet for use as a “spare”. As mentioned above, the Ministry of Education permits the use of spare buses on a regular route on a “temporary basis” only. This will allow the District to utilize available spare buses to provide the additional service to LSS for one school year. 3 s:\secretarytreasurer\executiveassistant\2014-2015\agendas\bus com\10 - apr 8 15\april 8 2015 cedar to lss bussing info sheet.doc Spare buses continue to be a part of our Preventive Maintenance Program and therefore, scheduled to receive safety inspections, repairs and servicing. These buses are considered to be in good, safe condition. Spare buses are put into service when a bus is in the shop for repair/servicing or a bus is off its normal bus route to do an out of District charter/field trip. Spare buses are used for approximately 2,000 to 5,000 kilometers each year. New bus routes to LSS for one year will put approximately 43,000 kilometers on each of the retired/spare buses. Buses with 400,000 to 500,000 kilometers will require more servicing and repairs than they would otherwise require. All operations of the buses will be impacted i.e. brakes, tires, engine, exhaust, transmission, rear axle, suspension causing greater servicing costs for each retired bus. These costs are difficult to estimate and the District should consider having a contingency fund set aside for these repairs. It would not be unreasonable to expect additional repair costs in the range of $10,000 a year for each spare bus. If the service is requested by the Board, staff would consider organizing the buses and routes such that the spare buses put back in the fleet were put on shorter routes and/or routes closer to the maintenance yard. Using our retired/spare buses takes away from our ability to provide bus service to our schools in the form of field trips. By using up to four spare buses, the District will only be able to use up to two buses to provide field trips that require a bus for the day as we must have at least one bus on standby for breakdowns/emergencies. This would reduce the number of charters available to schools. Other Scenarios Considered A possible cost saving measure could be to implement centralized bus stops versus providing bus service from each bus stop in the JBS attendance area. This scenario has not been considered as it does not meet current Board Policy. Bell Schedules The LSS bell schedule will not require adjustment in any scenario included in this Information Sheet. Board Policy There may be a requirement for a variation to Board Policy 4400P 2b which limits secondary students from riding the bus for more than one hour per trip. Preliminary estimates of ride times to Ladysmith exceed the one hour per trip.
