Page 7 of 11 7.1 Page 8 of 11 10.1 Page 9 of 11 11.1 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT #68 STAFF REPORT U TO: Business Committee DATE: October 9, 2013 FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations Brent Spohn, Facilities Planning Supervisor SUBJECT: U Gender Neutral Washrooms REFERENCE: U Board Motion # R13/02/27-17: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) requests staff to bring a report back to the Board on the feasibility of costs in creating gender neutral bathrooms in SD68 facilities by September 30th 2013.” U BACKGROUND : U This is an issue facing many public organizations worldwide. Strategies to address this issue include the development of policy. What is the objective of a gender neutral policy?  To make an organization’s facilities more inclusive for those facing challenges with gender specific washrooms What are gender neutral washrooms? U  Gender neutral washrooms do not have gendered signage and do not require the individual using them to define into a gender  There are three main ways to provide gender neutral washrooms: o Single stall or single user lockable washrooms available to people of all genders o Multiple cubicle washrooms without gendered signage o Using and re-identifying existing handicapped washrooms as “Accessible” washrooms FEASIBILITY : U Provision of gender neutral washrooms throughout School District 68 may be feasible as part of new construction and significant renovation. Implementing gender neutral washrooms in existing facilities would typically involve:  Identifying single user washroom(s), preferably those accessible from a corridor  Re-signing these washroom(s) with appropriate signage  Changing or modifying the hardware to make the washroom accessible to all  Publishing a list locating all gender neutral washrooms in the District A two phase approach to implementation is recommended. Assuming adoption of the single user model the Phase 1 Planning stage will investigate and identify issues. Preliminary estimated costs could amount to: Page 10 of 11 11.1 Staff Report – Gender Neutral Washrooms September 18, 2013 Staff planning time Page 2 Site visit all facilities and consultation with administrators and stakeholders. Identification of appropriate single user washroom(s) if any. $6,000 Subject to resolution of issues identified during Phase 1 preliminary estimated costs for Phase 2 Implementation could amount to: Purchase and install new signage Provide and/or re-key door hardware Publish location list Contingency TOTAL Phase 2 U Costs for replacement signage and installation $4500 $1500 Re-painting doors etc. $500 $3000 $9,500 DISCUSSION: Provision of gender neutral washrooms presents a number of issues for this school district:   In general, renovating to provide additional single user washrooms is not practical for several reasons: o Space is not available for additional washroom facilities at most schools o Ideally, such washrooms would open from a corridor o If space were available current budget may not support construction  Washroom renovations are expensive and in the order of $25,000 - $50,000 or more depending on complexity and complications Some organizations re-sign handicapped accessible washrooms as also gender neutral or accessible o   U This could be problematic as most schools have limited handicapped accessible washroom facilities and those they do have must be available for the handicapped K-12 public school systems have unique supervision requirements o The need to properly supervise may limit the provision of unlocked publically available washrooms, particularly in elementary schools o Secondary schools may also have a supervision issue though this is likely less about student safety and security and more about inappropriate behavior behind locked doors o Locking these washrooms and providing keys is contrary to the concept of gender neutrality (the act of requesting a key identifies the user) This Staff Report is the work of the Facilities Department and lacks a wider District perspective. Staff suggests a working group be formed to review the information and further identify issues before returning to the Business Committee with recommendations. The working group should include a Trustee, an Assistant Superintendent, elementary and secondary school Principals, the Facilities Department and a parent representative. RECOMMENDATION : U That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) that a Gender Neutral Washroom Working Group be established to consist of stakeholder representatives necessary to review the staff report and make recommendations to the Business Committee.
