School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT Regular Board Meeting Wednesday, November 24, 2010 Local Teachers Share Knowledge with Peers Our district has received a note of thanks from the West Vancouver School District for a presentation by two of our teachers at an Aboriginal Education professional development opportunity on November 12. West Vancouver is in the process of developing stronger Aboriginal Education programs with the Squamish First Nation. SD68 Teachers, Lynn Brown and Laura Tait, shared their work on co-teaching and inquiry-based learning with West Vancouver elementary and secondary teachers. Lynne Tomlinson of West Vancouver said that their teachers were thrilled with the relevance of the presentation and the resources that were shared by Lynn and Laura. Wellington Secondary Helps the Hungry As part of Wellington Secondary’s Halloween for Hunger program, there was a haunted house for Wellington feeder school students on October 29. The entrance was a non-perishable food item of a cash donation. Students also went out to trick or treat for non-perishable food items. The school collected 1,183 food items. That amount will be matched by Shaw Cable and donated to local food banks. Wellington is continuing its campaign to address issues of poverty by sponsoring a Christmas hamper drive. The school is accepting donations of unused Canadian Tire money and gift cards. Cedar Community Participate in National Contest Grade 11 Chemistry students from Cedar Community Secondary are once again this year participating in a national contest for growing crystals. Two of the class’s best crystals were recently taken to the University of Victoria for judging. National results will be announced in the New Year. Secondary Sports Dover Bay AAAA senior girls’ volleyball team and the Cedar A senior girls volleyball team are both heading to the provincial finals after winning their Island tournaments. The NDSS AAA senior girls team finished third at the Island tournament and will need to win a wildcard to advance to the provincial finals. This is particularly exciting for Dover Bay since they are hosting the AAAA Girls Volleyball Provincial Tournament this year on December 1-4. High school wrestling in Nanaimo recently kicked off the 2010-11 season with the Hubbub Takedown Tournament. The event had approximately 100 boys and girls from the ages of 10 to 18 participating and proved to be a huge success. Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation The Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation reports several successful initiatives over the past few months. The Stock the Lockers campaign, in partnership with Staples, raised $50,000. Of the money raised, $20,000 will go to purchasing school supplies for students, with the balance going towards the Student Support Fund. -1- School supplies were sent to schools in August and October, and a third instalment will be provided in January, if required. This year, both secondary and elementary schools have received money from the Student Support Fund, thanks to the success of the Stock the Lockers program and other community donations. In November, two elementary schools received grants to purchase music equipment. $2,500 went to Park Avenue from the Nanaimo Blues Society and $2,000 to Quarterway Elementary from Island Radio. McGirr Elementary’s Temporary Evacuation Thanks to Principal John Blain and staff at Dover Bay for providing, on short notice, a warm, safe shelter for McGirr Elementary students when that school was evacuated on Monday, due to a suspected gas leak. Thanks also to everyone who helped in this unusual situation – staff at McGirr, the Fire Department, Terasen Gas and SD68 facilities staff. The situation was resolved in about an hour, and McGirr students were able to return to their school. ConnectEd School District 68 is pleased to announce the first issue its new magazine for parents – ConnectED. This magazine will go home with elementary students and be available in secondary schools and in a variety of community locations, as well as online on the district website. Thanks to our partnership with Family Matters Publications and the support of advertisers, this publication is being produced at no cost to the district. The magazine will be published four times a year and we fully expect that the number of pages will grow. Michael J. Munro Superintendent of Schools/CEO -2-
