Board Chair, Board of Trustees School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, British Columbia V9R 3K6 via email Request for Support PROMOTING AND IMPROVING THE COMMERCIAL, INDUSTRIAL, CIVIC AND SOCIAL WELFARE OF THE TOWN OF LADYSMITH AND ITS TRADE AREA Our established mandates: • • • • To enhance and advance the commercial, industrial, civic and general trade interests of the Ladysmith and its surrounding area To undertake an active leadership role in the community by initiating, coordinating, and developing programs for the benefit of the business community To encourage communication between private and public sectors, business and labour To be a representative of the business community and speak on behalf of the business community to municipal, provincial and federal governments Since its establishment in 1930, the Ladysmith Chamber of Commerce has been a valuable resource and support network to the local business community. While Chamber membership offers important benefits such as group insurance and discount programs, the role and responsibilities of the Ladysmith Chamber of Commerce involves much more. Serving over 125 members in our trading area, we listen to our members, seek understanding of your needs and promote connection and business. WE ARE ASKING FOR YOUR SUPPORT AN EXCITING EVENT LADYSMITH CHAMBER OF COMMERCE is proud to present the 2014 Spirit Awards on April 11, 2014 at the Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS). This event is the highlight event of the year in Ladysmith which focuses on Business, Youth and Leadership in our Community. Each year, we present awards to businesses or persons nominated for various categories. This year, we have partnered with LSS with an intention to involve the youth in our community because we believe they are our future and can offer a great deal of perspective, motivation and enthusiasm. This is going to be a Red Carpet event and appetizers, sweet and savory will be prepared by the Culinary students at LSS. Students from the Music and Drama departments will also be performing throughout the evening for the listening pleasure of our guests, nominees and families. We are hoping to have local Wineries in attendance offering samplings of their wine also. We do not intend to sell any liquor, but have it sponsored by local businesses. We, therefore, ask for permission from the Board of Trustees to allow Wine to be served to persons over the age of 19 in attendance at our event on April 11, 2014 at Ladysmith Secondary School. Trusting you have enough information to make an informed decision, should you have any further questions, please feel free to contact me at the information below. We look forward to a positive response.
