BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: February 10, 2016 TO: BUSINESS COMMITTEE FROM: John Blain, Superintendent/CEO RE: District International Baccalaureate Primary Years Program Implementation Plan (IB PYP) BACKGROUND The Board of Education directed staff to meet with the International Baccalaureate Steering Committee and develop an implementation plan for the district: The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) that it refer the item of IB to district staff (cost, school site potential, and staffing), and that they meet with the presenters from the meeting and provide a recommendation to be presented at the Education Committee meeting in February. The IB Steering Committee (in attendance: Superintendent/CEO John Blain, Assistant Superintendent Tim Davie, Trustee Stephanie Higginson, Parent John Lam, Parent-Teacher Sofia La Bounty) met Tuesday, January 12, 2016 and Thursday, February 4, 2016 (DJ Thompson in attendance) at the DAC to discuss the IB PYP implementation plan for Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS). The Committee reviewed: 1. previous reports presented to the Board by a variety of sources 2. general overall concerns and considerations 3. requirements to facilitate program (IB implementation phases) 4. implementation components and timeline 5. budget costs of both start-up and ongoing annual costs Previous Reports (Appendices):  IB & SD 68 - Phase 1 of the International Baccalaureate Primary Years program was completed in June 2014 with the recommendation to not proceed with the program implementation and to remove it from the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan. 2. There was no recommendation in the Becoming Sustainable: Updated Facilities Plan to proceed with an IB program in NLPS. The updated plan was approved by the Board of Education on September 2, 2015. 3. The Department of Learning Services does not support this initiative, based on analysis, and the rationale that has been highlighted in previous reports:     District vulnerable students/school focus Costs Strong consideration of the present New Curriculum for all NLPS students Inclusion concerns, and displacement of current students 4. Staff is currently involved in major facilities changes for next school year, and is stretched to meet the Board’s timelines. 5. Staff will also be presenting recommendations on individual school catchment areas for review, this spring. These changes could affect capacity utilization within the district, excess space at individual schools and, specifically, the Central South Zone of Park Avenue, Georgia Avenue, Bayview, and Fairview elementary schools. RECOMMENDATION It is recommended that the implementation of a new International Baccalaureate Education program not be advanced at this time. If the Board were to move forward with an International Baccalaureate program, the recommended earliest start for staff would be September 2017. /jlm Page 4 of 4
