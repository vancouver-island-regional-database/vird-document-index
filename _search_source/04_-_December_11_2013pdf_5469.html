CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY PRESENTATIONS BC Hydro - Wayne Cousins Mr. Wayne Cousins of BC Hydro recognized the school district and staff for the work that has been done over the past 10 years in conserving energy. He presented Mr. Chad Dalrymple, Energy Manager, with a plaque acknowledging his efforts. Mr. Cousins said that the district is a leader in the province in pursuing energy savings and congratulated the Board, senior staff and facilities staff involved in the initiatives. UNFINISHED BUSINESS There was no unfinished business on the Agenda this evening. Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held December 11, 2013 FACILITIES PLANNING DEPT. Auto Scrubbers - Operations Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations gave the Business Committee an update on the new auto-scrubbers that have recently been purchased. The scrubbers were one of the recommendations from the 2011-12 Custodial Review. The district purchased 10 new scrubbers this year, and custodians report that they make the work of floor cleaning less physically demanding and free up time for other cleaning tasks at schools. Equipment Replacement - Brian Hackwood, Maintenance Supervisor Mr. Hackwood gave the Business Committee an update on the vehicle replacement program which has been in place since 2009. The district has 52 vehicles, 13 pieces of equipment and 11 trailers. Mr. Hackwood said that the district is half-way through the 10-year replacement program and so far has replaced 25 vehicles which were between 32 to 18 years old and also has replaced six pieces of equipment and four trailers. Energy and Capital Plan Update - Chad Dalrymple, Energy Manager Mr. Dalrymple gave the Business Committee an update on building energy consumption and cost patterns, along with conservation programs implemented in schools. He also updated the committee on the building envelope project at Quarterway Elementary and the seismic upgrade project at Wellington Secondary. NEW BUSINESS Financial Forecast Graham Roberts, Assistant Secretary-Treasurer, provided the Committee with the first financial forecast of the 2013-14 school year. He said that because it is still early in the year, the forecast is very preliminary, with a small projected surplus of $31,337 in the $121 million operating budget.
