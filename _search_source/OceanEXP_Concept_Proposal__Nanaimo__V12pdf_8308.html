Proof of Concept Proposal March 2020 The purpose of this document is to introduce OceanEXP, Canada’s Centre for Ocean Exploration – and identify Provincial funding sources for the non-profit Nanaimo Deep Discovery Association to complete the Proof of Concept stage of the OceanEXP project. This stage includes schematic design, technology development, engineering and architectural plans, marketing and a capital funding campaign. The Vision – Imagine! “To create a unique opportunity for people to explore beneath the surface of the oceans empowering them to leave a legacy of ecological sustainability.” Oceans around the world are at risk! OceanEXP is all about exploring and experiencing the world’s oceans, and with that, Ocean conservation is critical for the future of our planet. OceanEXP will look at how technology and innovation, paired with responsibility and passion can help us recover and re-nourish our planets greatest and most essential resource. 1 OceanEXP-Canada’s Centre for Ocean Exploration immerses the visitors in the stories of Canada’s most inspiring subsea explorers, technologist and pioneers. It is a unique opportunity for people of all ages to get up close and personal with marine life from the world’s five oceans, to become immersed into the environment and interact with all it has to offer without even getting wet! Utilizing virtual and artificial technology, we’ll bring the underwater environment into the hands of our visitors inspiring future generations to appreciate our vulnerable ocean world! Gateway to Opportunities OceanEXP will provide an iconic attraction for the community drawing visitors from around the world and projected to add jobs and inject over 250 million dollars into the local economy over the first five yearsþ. It is consistent with the our ‘Vision’ to be a community that is livable, environmentally sustainable and full of opportunity for all generations and walks of life. 2 Description On the surface the building itself will be a simple, sophisticated and stunning design representing the purest of forms, and one present throughout the oceans – the sphere. At sea level it will exist as symbiotic and complimentary to the surrounding waterfront park and yet present as an impressive and iconic landmark recognizable around the world. Inside (and beneath) the facility we give life to a journey of interactive digital experiences1 that will transport the visitor underwater providing an experience where the participants not only feel immersed beneath the surface of the ocean, but have an opportunity to interact with it. All our experiences will be created using pioneering digital technology, incorporating augmented reality, virtual reality, multi-sensory theatre experiences, robotics, and good old fashion physical displays that you can touch, feel and use. OceanEXP will be unlike any other water experience on the planet, why? Well, we’ll have no water! We won’t have any tanks, no mammals or marine life in captivity, no touch tanks, and no fish. Utilizing this technology will enable us to evolve and adapt to a changing world, educational values, and generational interests. Our experiences will be where educational and technology collide, in spectator fashion. In addition to providing a spectacular experience with thrilling rides and interactive educational exhibits, OceanEXP will also be Canada’s Centre for Ocean Exploration and home to an impressive compendium of subsea technology back-dropped by dynamic multimedia displays. It will celebrate Canada’s legacy and contribution to ocean exploration and tell the stories how our pioneers developed some of the world’s greatest subsea technology. 1 During the projects initial conceptual stage, the facility’s physical format incorporated water displays (tanks, pools and aquariums). As the project evolved it became apparent our objectives could be better accomplished by incorporating technologies such as virtual and artificial reality. It allowed the project to better control the quality of the experience, the sustainability of the facility and positioned the capital and operational strategies in a more economically practical position. The purpose of the facility has not change, however our approach to developing it has evolved. 3 Purpose …An Essential Part of Nanaimo’s Strategic Plan OceanEXP is all about exploring the world’s oceans, and with that we are indebted to our seas. The visitor markets were selected by NDDA and comprised Metro Vancouver, other parts of BC, Seattle, Edmonton, Calgary and Toronto. Research analysis indicated potential attendance projections as a high as 85,900 per year which generate annual revenue as much as $2.5 million dollars! The potential visitor market size for the OceanEXP is approximately 1.5 million people. From the MNP Study Figure 7 (page 21) OceanEXP a very positive indicator is that 24% of the people surveyed from places like Washington indicated they would definitely visit OceanEXP and another 51% stated they would probably come. Similar trends were reported in other areas around Canada and the United States. 8 Economic Feasibility The facility will be unique and is expected to appeal to a range of visitors, including both adults and children. Many argued that the age of exploration was over. The poles were mapped, Everest was conquered, and two men had already touched the blackness of the ocean’s deepest trench. But as a schoolgirl, when I watched Armstrong and Aldrin walk on the lunar surface, the age of discovery was hardly over. For me, it had just begun. My generation launched the first technical exploration of our water planet. Instead of flying into space, I focused on an even more remote frontier—deep ocean vistas and unexplored, underwater caves. If we acknowledge that oceans serve as the lungs of the planet, then submerged caves are the circulatory system transporting our most precious resource through the veins of Mother Earth. To find life on other planets, we look for water. But we are only beginning to recognize that ocean exploration and conservation is essential for securing our survival as a species here on earth. Canada’s 220,000 km coastline and eight-percent lake coverage represent more waterfront than any other country in the world. We possess nine percent of the world’s renewable freshwater and only half a percent of the world’s population. When the world faces seemingly insurmountable challenges, people often turn to Canada; a resourceful, responsible player on the world stage. And today, Canadians are at the forefront of advanced engineering technology used for exploring the ocean. You can imagine how gratifying it is for me to serve as an Ambassador for the Canadian Center of Ocean Exploration.
