ADVISORY COMMITTEE SEXUAL ORIENTATION AND GENDER IDENTITY REPORT TO THE BOARD WEDNESDAY, MARCH 30, 2011 The Advisory Committee on Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity (SOGI) was established by Board Policy /Procedure3909/3909P passed at the September, 2010 Open Board meeting. Based on the composition defined by Procedure 3909P, invitations were sent out to stakeholders, students and community to participate as members of the advisory committee. The Advisory Committee has received overwhelming support in terms of membership and has met now on 4 occasions –February 1, 15, March 1, 28, 2011 with the next meeting scheduled for April 18th, 2011. Working within the terms of reference established by the procedure, the committee has developed the following 4 working goals:  Goal #1. to conduct needs assessments in order to identify sexual orientation and gender identity issues and priorities faced by SD68 students, staff and families.  Goal #2. to create an action plan that addresses the issues and priorities identified by the needs assessment  Goal #3. to create awareness of the new District Policy 3909 and Procedure 3909P at all work sites in the district  Goal #4. to educate and provide tools/resources to promote the goals of the policy Members of the committee have taken on various tasks to move the goals forward.    ] Goal #1 - information gathering –example –EGALE –School Climate Study; Transitioning information, Literature Review –UofA, etc. Goal #2 – discussion about surveys/focus groups for the fall based on the information received in Goal #1 Goal #3 : * Art Contest- open to all students of SD68. - current Prize monies donated by Advisory Committee, Learning Alternatives and Student Support Services. Page 2 The purpose of the contest is to raise awareness of the new Policy/Procedure 3909 and to use the art work to promote awareness - for example through branding on SOGI literature. A great deal of thanks goes to Bob Esliger, District Principal of Student Support Services for his energy and help moving this idea into a concrete action. Other Goal #3 considerations: *awareness raising through Principals to staff at staff meetings *presentations to city councils – Anti-Homophobic Day – May 17th. *invitation to RCMP Liaison officers  Goal #4 – discussions of various forms of literature – example rack cards, brochures. In a very short period of time, this committee has come together with a tremendous amount of energy and commitment. To date we have had 7 students attend our meetings. Their presence makes all the difference and their information is informative and speaks to the fact that School District 68’s development of Policy/Procedure 3909 has been very necessary and will prove to be very supportive. Respectfully, Donna Allen, Chairperson Advisory Committee –Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity
