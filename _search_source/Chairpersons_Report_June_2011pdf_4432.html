SD 68 Chairperson’s Report Sharon Welch June, 2011 June continues to be exciting with all the wrap-up activities and graduation events! I visited several more schools and attended many special events. At Woodlands, Principal Dave Stupich promoted the upcoming ‘Wizard of Oz’ performances and highlighted the “wrap in a snap” lunch program spearheaded by one of the Woodlands students. Principal Stupich also informed me of strides the school has made in increasing student achievement. Principal Mike Lundine from Pauline Haarer also talked about successes in increasing student achievement through fun activities such as Pyjama Day, games, and guided reading circles. He highlighted the schools strong athletic and music programs. Principal Darren Brick and Vice-Principal David Cooper of Mountain View Elementary were thrilled with their Project Student Success initiative and also the well-attended after-school science and technology events. The Games Club is also working very well. Principal Scott Saywell and Vice-Principal Tim Davies at the Junior and Senior Learning Alternatives schools were very pleased with the over 90 percent attendance at parent-teacher interviews this year. They talked about their wonderful staff team and told me inspiring stories of student successes. Principal Gregg Halfyard from Fairview was excited about the school’s role in the community – especially being able to provide activities on evenings and weekends. Principal Halfyard was pleased with student successes that were partly due to the use of learning groups, school-wide fitness, and a strong music program. Tomorrow, I finish my school visits with Gabriola Elementary and Principal Tricia McKay’s staff. I’m looking forward to the island get-away! In addition to my school visits, Trustee Neary and I attended the Ladysmith Secondary Banquet and Prom. What a wonderful celebration of our students’ achievements. A special ‘thank you’ goes to Principal Dave Street for inviting us to be a part of this special event. Trustees Donna Allen, Andrea Bonkowski, Ron Dale, and I attended the Honours Night at the Port Theatre. The Nanaimo-Ladysmith Schools Foundation did a wonderful job of coordinating the awards and the event. Thank you to all of the donors and staff who made this evening possible. Congratulations to the students who won awards in academics, the arts, sports, leadership, school spirit, and community participation! I had the honour of speaking at our Board Retirement Dinner last Thursday night. It was a wonderful evening where we honoured 65 retiring staff members who served SD68 students for a cumulative total of 1,593 years! May all of our retirees have a safe, happy, and fulfilling retirement! After a long day of interviewing potential new Superintendents on Friday, Trustee McNamee and I attended the NDTA Retirement Dinner. Thank you to those who organized this lovely dinner at the Coast Bastion, and congratulations again to our retirees! My fellow Trustees and I are looking forward to many upcoming graduation events. Congratulations to all of our graduating students – may this be the beginning of adventures and challenges that will lead you to new heights! Finally, I would like say a very special ‘thank you’ to our retiring Superintendent, Mike Munro. Throughout my school visits, I heard many stories of how Superintendent Munro has touched people’s lives and how sorry they are to see him leave the District. Mike – I have enjoyed our weekly meetings and deeply appreciate all the support you have given me as Board Chair and all of the Trustees in our challenging roles. We wish you all the best in your travels, and we know that you will continue to serve students, staff, and communities in powerful ways. Thank you so much for all you have done for School District 68. We will miss you very much.
