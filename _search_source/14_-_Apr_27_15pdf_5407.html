CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS School Calendar Discussion Superintendent John Blain shared with the Committee the five recommendations: Draft Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 27, 2015 1) Extend the existing three-year local school calendar to 2017-18 2) Elementary school local schedule guidelines 3) Secondary school local schedule guidelines 4) Two week spring break 5) Removal of one district implementation day and replacing it with an instructional day Motion from April 22, 2015 Regular Board Meeting: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive this information that Mr. Burgos presented regarding the calendar and refer it to the Special Business Committee on April 27, 2015 for discussion, with a decision on April 30, 2015. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Higginson IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Rae Folio SB15/04/27-02 Function 4 - Mr. Roberts That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive and accept the local school calendar for the years 2014-15, 2015-16 and 2016-17 including all five staff recommendations with a letter from the Chair and the Superintendent to be sent to our stakeholders within 30 days. Opposed by Trustee(s) Brzovic, Routley CARRIED A/Secretary-Treasurer, Mr. Roberts, addressed the district administration portion of the proposed budget. His presentation focused on changes in the following areas: • • • • • • Information Systems - Mr. Sproston. Office of the Superintendent Department of Learning Services Human Resources Payroll department Finance department Trustees/Board office The district's Manager of Information Systems (IS), Mr. Sproston, shared with the Committee the following initiatives his department is currently undertaking: Draft Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held April 27, 2015 • My Education BC (MyED) is a new student information system that will be replacing the current BCeSIS. MyED will add learning alternatives, consistent technology in the classroom, features such as Gradebook, student/teacher portal, curriculum management, teacher collaboration and student portfolio. • Focusing on secondary and elementary computer labs and laptops. • Replacing computers for school administration. • Upgrading of district servers. • Creating an evergreen program to replace computers every five years. Facilities, Operations, Maintenance, Transportation Mr. Sabo. The district's Director of Planning and Operations, Mr. Pete Sabo, provided a budget planning overview for 2015-16 and 2016-17. His presentation is attached to the agenda. The following is part of his presentation: • 15% rental rate increase proposed for 2015-16. • Significant reductions in operations and maintenance service levels. • Transportation of students is not required to be supplied by the district therefore the elimination of approximately 400 courtesy rider spaces is proposed in the 2015-16 budget. • The district is also investigating the potential for the implementation of bus fees for eligible riders in the 2016-17 school year.
