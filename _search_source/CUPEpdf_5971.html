CUPE APPROACH Budget 2015-2016 Becoming Financially Sustainable Background as reported by Senior Management. That the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools have been in a deficit budget position 12 of the past 13 years and during this time we have never seen such deep cuts to one part of the working force, while seemingly hanging onto the largest surplus in the history of the district. Becoming Financially Sustainable Background as reported by Senior Management In each of the 3 years the district made cuts to staff and the surplus grew and the budget was balanced. Question: Why were we cutting when surpluses were growing? This trend shows that the district was able to maintain services throughout the year while still building on a larger surplus each year. How can this be done if we have no money? You already have a 4.1 million dollar surplus at your disposal and CUPE feels that this current budget will have a surplus amount just as the last three years have shown. We ask you, as Trustees, to use that surplus to maintain the services that are being provided to this district rather than reducing an already slim work force even further. Budget Allocations – Operating Fund Two Year Predicted Deficit (SOFI Report June 2014) Actual Surplus 2014 - $7,837,609 Internally Restricted Unrestricted Operating Surplus - $3,668,454 $4,169,155 2015-2016 deficits of 4.5 million add surplus of $4,169,155 and the deficit would only be $330,845. Through efficiencies and current vacancies CUPE would commit with working with Senior Management to erase this amount and that would balance this years’ budget. This would allow all of us one more year to build a balanced budget that works for all employee groups and provides a safe and meaningful education to all the students.  Other efficiencies have already been found amongst the other employee groups that could be applied by the Trustees to build in a contingency or could be provided as a surplus for next years’ budget. Becoming Financially Sustainable Surplus Expectations for 2014-2015 Budget As in previous years the Board has run numerous surpluses and we believe this year will be no different and here is why: Budget documents on surplus savings shows $850,000 on reduction in replacement costs, we believe this has already been achieved this year. $850,000 staffing contingencies were also built into the budget. CUPE believes that this $1,700,00 has already been achieved in the current years’ budget and as Trustees we would hope that you would use operational dollars on operational services and not put these monies into other areas for future planning. In closing, CUPE 606 Executive and all the support staff employees urge you, the Trustee’s to instruct senior management to take last years’ surplus and direct that money to be used in maintaining services and allow all sides to take the next year to build a much better plan around how we can achieve a sustainable budget.
