Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORT Regular Board Meeting Wednesday, February 27, 2013 Superintendent/CEO’s Priorities (2012-2013): 1. Advancing the Learning Agenda:  We continue to advance RTI, SRL, and the Instructional Coordinator and Learning Services Support Teams models (these were significant areas of focus at our February 1, 2013 districtwide Success For All day);  I currently sit as a member of an interprovincial committee, where senior officials from British Columbia and Ontario school districts, as well as the Yukon and British Columbia Ministries of Education, contribute to the development of the Canadian Self-Regulation Initiative (CSRI). Joined by York University neuroscientist, Dr. Stuart Shanker, the committee is striving to organize the core principles of theory and practice connected to SRL. The ultimate end of the committee’s work will be the creation of classroom research that tests the efficacy of the model with respect to the improvement of student learning, and the reduction of teacher stress. The CSRI met in Vancouver in January, and is in the process of developing a website found at http://www.self-regulation.ca/about-us/canadian-self-regulation-initiative-csri/.  I presented, along with Board Chair Jamie Brennan, to a fourth year Education Class at VIU. Topics ranged from the Board’s strategic policy governance model to inclusive education;  I attended meetings of the Tillicum Lelum Friendship Centre’s Community Partners group, an inter-organizational initiative devoted to the development of an innovative early childhood education and community health centre in Nanaimo. Under the leadership of Tillicum Project Manager Chris Beaton, the group meets regularly to assist with the development of this innovative integrated human service model that is focused on early childhood education;  I attended Dover Bay’s symposium on 21st Century Learning (along with Trustees Bard and Neary), and met some great parents who had a strong interest in emerging trends in teaching and learning. I also attended the recent session on RTI led by District Instructional Coordinator Darlene Crane, and was impressed by her breadth of knowledge in this area. Congratulations to the Dover Bay PAC, and teaching and administrative staff who have worked together to provide these opportunities for information sharing and dialogue.  I attended the Aboriginal Education Leadership Council meeting and participated in discussions connected to better connecting the district’s focus on Aboriginal student success to the RTI model, and universal tier one strategies. It is important to remember that Aboriginal peoples’ ideas about healthy human development and learning have been proven effective with all children (regardless of ancestry), and are closely linked to the practices attached to selfregulated learning. 1 2. Strengthening Communications/Partnerships:  I met with the Snuneymuxw Chief (Doug White) and Council, along with District Principal of Aboriginal Education Laura Tait. Deputy Superintendent John Blain and I met with Charlotte Elliot with the Stz’uminus First Nation. The purpose of these visits was to have some preliminary discussions around the development of proposals connected to formal education-focused partnerships intended to help strengthen student achievement and the overall development of the local First Nations and public school systems;  I attended the Nanaimo School Administrators’ Association (NSAA) Annual Retreat, and presented on the District’s strategic plan;  John Blain, Deputy Superintendent, along with Scott Saywell, Assistant Superintendent and myself met with VIU senior staff to discuss expanding dual-credit offerings;  I continue to meet with VIU President Ralph Nilson and we are currently exploring the development of a collaboration agreement;  I met with pediatrician Dr. Wilma Arruda to discuss possible joint ventures between VIHA and NLPS, particularly in connection with providing therapeutic classrooms for students with severe emotional and behavioral challenges;  I have attended executive meetings with the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation, as well as Chamber of Commerce luncheons;  We are currently pursuing a partnership with VIHA and Tillicum Lelum to provide a Nurse Practitioner in the Bayview school area; and  The district-wide Success for All day presented an opportunity to communicate on such topics as developing a respectful workplace, and our key learning initiatives connected to RTI and SRL. 3. Attachments 3 Dave Mumford Consulting 250.751.1860 Office 250.714.4008 Mobile davemumford@shaw.ca 5754 Garnet Place Nanaimo, BC V9T 6S1 MEMO TO: Board Policies and Procedures Steering Committee Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO FROM: Dave Mumford, Dave Mumford Consulting DATE: January 10, 2013 RE: Policy Review Project Framework Policy Review Project Framework The following framework describes the recommended actions for the development of a ‘Board Policy Manual’, based upon the new Governance Policies and Procedures, and the creation of a ‘Board Procedures Manual’ and an ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’. The purpose of these documents would be to facilitate the separation of Board policy and stewardship from the administrative and procedural work undertaken by staff. ‘Board Policy Manual’ - The ‘Board Policy Manual’ contains policies that reflect the Board’s mandate with regard to sound governance and stewardship. ‘Board Procedures Manual‘ - The ‘Board Procedures Manual’ would contain procedures aligned directly with governance policies of the Board. It would serve as an important source of information to Trustees containing easily updated information relevant to governance issues. It might also include elements of a ‘Trustees Handbook’. A ‘Board Procedures Manual’ could be efficiently revised as items warrant and placed on the District’s web site with a secure link for Trustees to access information and easily reference. ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ - The creation of an ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ would serve as the primary written source of administrative direction for the Nanaimo-Ladysmith school district. The ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ would be fully aligned with the ‘Board Policy Manual’ and an extension of policy in the form of operational procedures. In many cases, administrative procedures have supporting legislation, ministerial direction or municipal by-laws affecting their application. 4 Creating an ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ offers the opportunity to collect, catalogue, formalize and regulate all procedural processes in the district. Administrative procedures are public processes that would carry the same authority as a Board Policy. All references to Board responsibility would not be directional but would recognize Board-retained authority as outlined in the ‘Board Policy Manual’. The ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’ could also be referenced to other administrative documents, such as an ‘Administrative Procedures Handbook’, that may be developed to provide more detailed guidelines on selected matters. Categorization – It would be timely to consider revising the categorization of the ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’. Reducing and simplifying the classifications into five categories might be appropriate and effectively serve the review process: 100 - General Administration 200 - Instructional Programs and Materials 300 - Students 400 - Personnel and Employee Relations 500 - Business Affairs Procedures placed in the 100 section are those of a general administrative nature or those which may have applicability to other categories in the ‘Administrative Procedures Manual’. The procedures in 200, 300, and 400 are specific to each of the titles. The Business Affairs procedures include finance, facilities, and student transportation matters. (NTD: There are currently eight categories in the existing policy manual.) A logical flow of procedures would be attempted in the categories.
