This event, historically, has been held in the high school gymnasium, organized by parents and school administrators alike, and marketed by posters hung on school walls, daily announcements, and school newsletters sent home. Since 2013, Prom in School District 68 has been left to be funded and organized solely by the parents of graduates. Parents receive no contact information of other students and parents. We are not allowed to advertise on school premises. We do not have the aid of school administrators. With the added cost of a venue, rising costs of food, labour, and goods, we have estimated this event will cost approximately $50,000 for this year. Furthermore, it is very challenging to connect with as many parents as in the past. After 2.5 months of communications, our Facebook page has only reached a little over a third of parents that could potentially be involved in Prom. This makes heavy work, on fewer hands. Other concerns are: -Inclusiveness: how do we reach these other families and make Prom known? Page 11 of 23 -How can we reach families with financial needs. Shouldn't each child be able to attend prom We are hoping for: -the ability to use the school for communication between families (newsletters, Posters on School walls, rail announcements) -if not as a venue for the Prom, perhaps other events such as a bottle drive or a car wash could be held in the parking lot? -help with identifying families with needs We greatly appreciate your time when contemplating this issue. Appendix A: Information Sheet dated December 7, 2022 Page 13 of 23 Appendix A NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 7, 2022 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Prom Ceremonies Background: This Information Sheet is intended to provide some background and context to the District’s lack of participation in prom events. Discussion: There is not a specific prohibition against the District participating in proms. However, Policy 3.5 – Protection of Assets and Risk Management states as follows: The Superintendent shall ensure human, financial, capital and reputational assets are protected; procedures and practices are regularly monitored; and risks are identified, eliminated or mitigated and shall: … 4. Ensure the District, Board or staff are not unnecessarily exposed to claims of liability or loss. A District sanctioned and organized event like prom attracts a host of potential liabilities from issues associated with transportation and supervision to ensuring sufficient volunteers and/or paying staff to participate. We are unaware of what practices Districts around the province have with respect to prom, however, we believe that several local District’s practices are aligned with our own. With respect to advertising and fundraising we do our best to separate the event from our schools to not confuse our community. This is based on guidance from our insurer. The issue becomes that if schools are seen as condoning events by the use of facilities or email distribution lists the District increases its risk of being associated with the event.
