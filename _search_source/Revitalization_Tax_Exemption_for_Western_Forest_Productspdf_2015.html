STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Financial Services Department October 7, 2025 1970-04-25-02 Revitalization Tax Exemption – Western Forest Products RECOMMENDATION: That Council amend resolution CS 2025-207 to include an additional address of 1221 Rocky Creek Road, folio 1345.020 for the Revitalization Tax Exemption Agreement – Economic with Western Forest Products. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: At the August 5, 2025 meeting, Council authorized entering into a revitalization tax exemption – economic agreement with Western Forest Products. The qualifying project was a lumber drying kiln. Though the application was for 101 Gladden Road, the actual project also spanned the adjacent Western Forest Products property located at 1221 Rocky Creek Road. This amendment is to give staff authorization to list the two folios in the agreement. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: CS 2025- That Council: 2025- 08-05 1. Approve entering into a Revitalization Tax Exemption Agreement – 207 Economic with Western Forest Products for the property located at 101 Gladden Road, folio 0904.000; and 2. Direct staff to amend the language in “Town of Ladysmith Revitalization Tax Exemption – Economic Revitalization Bylaw 2012, No. 1807” to require an application to be received at the time of building permit. Motion Carried OPPOSED: Councillor Stevens. INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: This amendment is to ensure that all the properties affected by the construction of the kiln are listed in the Revitalization Tax Exemption – Economic agreement. In this area, there are 6 parcel identifiers (PIDS) that relate to 3 folios but only 2 civic addresses all of which are owned by Western Forest Products. By amending the resolution to include this second address, the assessment value for the kiln will be reflected on applicable property(ies). Should Council approve the recommendation, this revitalization exemption will freeze the assessed value at the 2022 assessment levels and BC Assessment will be able to make the proper determination as to where the kiln valuation was applied. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Provide an exemption on only one of the properties (101 Gladden Road or 1221 Rocky Creek Road). FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: The property assessment from BC Assessment lists the property at 1221 Rocky Creek Road, folio 1345.020 as: Class 4 – Major Industry Class 6 -Business Total assessment 2022 6,798,000 355,000 7,153,000 2023 7,253,000 464,000 7,717,000 Gross Taxes (all) Gross Taxes (municipal) 666,753 599,995 650,172 576,636 2024 2025 7,581,000 9,785,000 476,000 535,000 8,057,000 10,320,000 715,122 623,592 828,861 707,138 The assessed value would be frozen at the 2022 valuation. LEGAL IMPLICATIONS: Staff are working with Western Forest Products to ensure the complete details are included in the Revitalization Tax Exemption Agreement which is set by bylaw. CITIZEN/PUBLIC RELATIONS IMPLICATIONS: N/A INTERDEPARTMENTAL INVOLVEMENT/IMPLICATIONS: Finance is the lead for this project.
