November 1, 2016 To All School Board Trustees of SD68 I like to believe I only resort to contacting the Board when presented with a serious issue. As you can see below, I wrote once in 2013 of my concern to not send South Wellington students to Chase River upon the closure of South Wellington Elementary. The result of which was that we were given a reprieve and students had the choice to go to either Chase River or Woodbank/NCI (with something like 59 students choosing Chase River and 1 choosing Cedar). With the re-opening of Cedar Highschool, this choice was removed last spring, and my daughters were forced to switch schools this year to maintain busing. It has been a far from easy transition, and both my kids now firmly dislike going to school. This is hard for me to hear as a parent and University Professor who is passionate about education. One of the major compounding issues for their disdain for their current school is with the Bus Route 8’s return schedule. In the morning a direct bus is provided and students face at most a 15 minute commute. In the afternoon, however, South Wellington students are forced onto an over-crowded bus to go on a nausea inducing ride through the cedar country-side. Students are often sitting 4 to a bench and often do not arrive at their stop until 60 minutes after they board. The result is that my daughters are chronically ill when they arrive, which is why I have chosen to write this letter. I believe that there is more than enough demand from the South Wellington region for its own direct bus route, and given the history these students have had to deal with – the closure their school, then the cancelling of their desired bus route after 3 years - a direct route is the right choice to make. As of now, every day that I can manage it, I am forced to pick up my kids from school. If I need to make further changes to my schedule to be driving my kids to their school, then my family will be looking a private school options. I also would like to close with a plea to remember the families of South Wellington. We have tried our best to make our plans based on the School District plans, only to have those completely up ended when the Board simply changes direction. I originally petitioned for the Chase River option as our students were slated then to go to John Barsby Community School. With the closure of our community school, our kids have been lost in a shuffle to always support another community’s need, and this nausea inducing long return Bus Route 8 makes us feel like a burden and after thought to SD68. Sincerely, Todd Barsby Parent of South Wellington students. April 29, 2013. To All School Board Trustees of SD68, I am writing regarding a concern I have on the implementation strategy for the proposed 10-year Enhanced Facilities Plan. Specifically I want to highlight the years of educational shuffling that will be required of the current Grade 2 students at South Wellington Elementary by proposing to send them to Woodbank Elementary. School Year 2012-2013 2013-2014 2014-2015 2015-2018 2018-2023 Current Plan: A Current Grade 2 South Wellington Student Will Attend: South Wellington Elementary Woodbank Elementary North Cedar Intermediate Cedar Elementary Community School John Barsby Community School These Grade 2 students are looking at having to attend 5 different facilities to graduate, with 4 facility changes occurring in a 4 year period. I hope you can appreciate how educationally damaging this aspect of the implementation plan will be to those students and I would like to draw your attention to a reasonable alternative plan. An alternative implementation strategy for South Wellington Elementary students would be to send these students to Chase River Elementary, a school to which they are currently administratively attached and geographically linked. School Year 2012-2013 2013-2018 2018-2023 Alternate Plan: A Current Grade 2 South Wellington Student Will Attend: South Wellington Elementary Chase River Elementary John Barsby Community School This alternate plan would dramatically reduce the burden that these students have to endure in order to build a school district with optimally sized schools for all. Additionally, using the numbers provided within the Enhanced Facilities Plan, this will help to lower the Cedar Elementary Community School student numbers (projected enrollment of 510) into the plan’s recommend 200-400 student range, while at the same time keeping Chase River within this range. Importantly, there is an adequate amount of time to plan on accommodating these additional 65-67 students from South Wellington area into the 2022 renovation plans for Chase River Elementary which has 153 currently registered students, with a projected enrollment of 276 in 2022-2023. Please, when deciding on the different aspects of the 10-year Enhanced Facility Plan amend where students currently attending South Welling Elementary school will be sent. Please send them to Chase River. The current strategy places an unreasonable burden on our young students. Sincerely, Todd Barsby Parent of a South Wellington Student.
