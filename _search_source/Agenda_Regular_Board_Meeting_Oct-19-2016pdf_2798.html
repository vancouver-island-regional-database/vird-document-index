Scheibel, Executive Assistant Meeting No. R01 The Chair called the meeting to order at 6:00 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. Chair Rae acknowledged Orange Shirt Day on Friday, September 30, 2016. Orange Shirt Day honours the survivors of the Residential School System and remembers those who did not. The reason we wear orange is based on the real life story of Phyllis, a survivor of St. Joseph Mission School in Williams Lake, BC, whose brand new orange shirt was taken from her on her first day when she was just a little girl. An estimated 150,000 First Nations, Inuit, and Metis children were made to go to Residential School away from their homes and families. Today, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools participated in Orange Shirt Day which helped raise awareness of the legacy Indian Residential Schools with the intention of moving our country towards healing and hope. Thank you to all who have taken part in this very important awareness campaign. TRANSFER OF ITEMS TO OPEN MEETING AGENDA There was no Transfer of Items to the Open Meeting Agenda this evening.
