EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Loan Authorization Bylaw 2031 is presented for Council’s consideration and first three readings. The Town has an opportunity to apply for grant funding through the Investing in Canada Infrastructure Program to significantly support major works in the Town’s water supply system. These include raising the Holland Dam in order to increase storage capacity in Holland Lake; a water supply interconnection between Holland and Stocking Lakes, and twinning of the Stocking Lake water supply main. These interconnected works will significantly increase the storage and supply capacity of the Town’s water system to address the impacts of both climate change and population growth. Page 3 of 6 DISCUSSION: Council first gave direction to staff to apply for grant funding for upgrades to the Holland Dam in 2018. Unfortunately, that application was not successful. The Town has a new opportunity to apply for funding to support critical upgrades in the Town’s water supply system. It is intended that this grant application include the entire project, which could be completed in phases, rather than the Town submitting separate grant applications for each phase of the project. The grant application deadline is February 26, 2020. Any time a local government wishes to borrow funds for a period of more than five years (longterm borrowing) it is required by the Community Charter to seek the approval of the electors of the Town. This can be done by referendum or by alternative approval process. The recommended approach for this borrowing instance is through alternative approval process. This is a more cost-effective process than a referendum. This instance of elector approval is specifically tied to the grant opportunity. If successful, the grant will reduce costs to the Town and taxpayers significantly, and the grant would cover approximately 73 per cent of the total project costs. Once approval of the Inspector of Municipalities has been received, staff will report back to Council on the alternate approval process and timelines. I approve the report and recommendation(s). Guillermo Ferrero, Chief Administrative Officer ATTACHMENT(S): Water Supply Projects Loan Authorization Bylaw 2031 Page 4 of 6 TOWN OF LADYSMITH BYLAW NO. 2031 A bylaw to authorize the borrowing for Water Supply Works _____________________________________________________________________________ WHEREAS it is deemed desirable and expedient to carry out works to increase capacity and security of the water supply system servicing the Town of Ladysmith, including increasing the storage capacity of Holland Lake, constructing a water supply interconnection between Holland Lake and Stocking Lake and twinning the Stocking Lake Water Main. AND WHEREAS the estimated cost of the works including expenses incidental thereto is the sum of $23,220,000.00 of which the sum of $6,200,000.00 is the amount of debt intended to be borrowed by this bylaw; NOW THEREFORE the Council of the Town of Ladysmith in open meeting assembled enacts as follows: 1. The Council is hereby empowered and authorized to undertake and carry out or cause to be carried out works for the water supply system generally in accordance with general plans on file in the municipal office and the Town of Ladysmith Council Strategic Plan for 2020 to 2023, and to do all things necessary in connection therewith and without limiting the generality of the foregoing: a) To borrow upon the credit of the Municipality a sum not exceeding $6,200,000.00. b) To acquire all such real property, easements, rights-of-way, licenses, rights or authorities as may be requisite or desirable for or in connection with the construction of the said improvements to the water supply system. 2. The maximum term for which debentures may be issued to secure the debt created by this bylaw is 25 years. Citation 3. This bylaw may be cited as “Town of Ladysmith Water Supply Works Loan Authorization Bylaw 2019, No. 2031”.
