3137 Barons Road Nanaimo, BC V9T 5W5 p: f: e: w: 250 756-1237 250 756-0188 ndta@shaw.ca www.ndta68.ca March 31, 2014 Dot Neary, Chairperson, Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Ave Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 Dear Ms Neary: Re: Implementation of the Collective Agreement (Article D.2: K-3 Primary Class Size) As you are aware the BC Supreme Court, in its decision dated January 27, 2014, returned working conditions clauses to the Collective Agreement. The purpose of this letter is to draw your attention to specific articles that existed prior to 2002 in order to assist the Board to be in compliance with the Collective Agreement and to facilitate a smooth start to the 2014-15 school year. While the government is appealing the decision of the court, the Board should prepare for the possibility that the appeal could be denied and that the working conditions provisions will then become part of the Collective Agreement. Since the Collective Agreement was renumbered, we will use the numbering as it existed in the 2001-2004 Collective Agreement. The renumbering took place at different times in different locals. Sometimes the reference to working conditions is Article 20 (old) and sometimes the reference is Article D (new). Article D.2.6 outlines the process that needs to be followed in the spring of each year with regard to primary class sizes. A meeting with the Local must occur before May 30, and: The district shall make the Local aware of any potential non-compliance with primary class size maximums and the reason for that non-compliance. As well, Article D.2.6 requires the District, if it concludes that it is not able to achieve the primary class size maximums, to submit its staffing plan to the Local, the BCTF, and the BCPSEA. Following this, a joint committee works to resolve the non-compliant staffing plan and if necessary either party can refer the matter to expedited arbitration. Thank you for your attention to this matter and other articles that were restored in the Court Decision of January 27, 2014. Sincerely, Mike Ball President, NDTA pc.
