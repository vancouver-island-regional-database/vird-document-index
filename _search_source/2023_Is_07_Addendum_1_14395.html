Project Description reads: “Holland Lake is impounded by two dams on the east and west sides that were built circa 1980 as indicated by Thurber Engineering Drawings.” REPLACE WITH: “Holland Lake is impounded by two dams on the east and west sides that were built circa 1980 as indicated by Willis Cunliffe Tait & Company Ltd. drawings.” QUESTIONS Questions have been paraphrased. 1. Question: What is the budget for this scope of work? Answer: The Town has not set a budget for this specific scope of work. The budget for the entire project is $15,547,387. The evaluation team will evaluate the estimates relative to the project budget. 2. Question: What is the relationship between the Town and Mosaic Forest Management Corp.? Answer: The Town and Mosaic have a positive relationship that includes road use agreements and annual watershed meetings. Both parties keep each other informed. The consultant will be able to use the Town of Ladysmith RFP No. 2023-IS-07 Addendum # 1 Page 3 of 3 forestry roads, although the Town may need to escort equipment or trucks to the lake. The Town has a full time watershed operator who can assist with travel to the lake and communication with Mosaic. 3. Question: What is the status of the Town’s water treatment plant and supply system? Answer: The Town’s WTP was commissioned in 2020 and is capable of treating turbid water due to construction; however, the lake directs water into a natural watercourse and is then retrieved further downstream, which is environmentally sensitive. The Town has a second water supply system via stocking lake, although this lake reservoir is much smaller than Holland Lake. The Town relies on Holland Lake to supply water over the summer dry period. 4. Question: What volume increase to the reservoir does the Town expect. Answer: The Town has not set a specific volume increase at this time. The Town hopes to increase capacity by the most practical amount based on constraints including budget, environmental, geotechnical, and topographical.
