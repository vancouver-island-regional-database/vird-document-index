Answer: The removal of resources is in the SD 68 Requirements section on pages 4 & 5 of the revised AP, which outlines BC Ratings from the Consumer Protection BC Guidelines. Resource removal should be based on application of this criteria, and ensuring age relevant / appropriate selection. Selection of resources involves many people, including teachers. Purchases of school-based and district-level resources rests with district staff, principals and professional staff, including teachers. Teachers are directly involved in the selection of Learning Resource process. Learning Resources purchased by the district and schools must be vetted through the criteria as set forth in the revised AP 223 – these selections adhere to the Learning Standards of Provincial Curriculum and Focused Education (formerly ERAC). Helen Tilley (South Wellington resident and local historian) *Map of the Beck Estate was obtained from the Land Titles Office in Victoria Page 34 of 94 A Bit of History of our South Wellington Schools by Helen Tilley James Beck, a pioneer in this area, pre-empted land in that part of Cranberry District, now known as South Wellington, in the 1860s. Before that he worked for the Hudson’s Bay Company as a machinist and carpenter. James Beck sold the coal rights under this land in February, 1884. In 1900, James Beck mapped out the town of South Wellington on his property, setting aside three lots for churches and three lots for schools. These residential and school lots can be seen highlighted on the attached map* of the Beck Estate, dated from the early 1900s. It was the custom in those days for land owners of large parcels of land to set aside parts of their property for the use of the community. There were four schools built on these three “School” properties over the years. The property marked “School” along the “Cassidy Road” on the map, which would now be Schoolhouse Road, was where the first school was built; locally known as the “Red School” it was built in 1898. The second school built in South Wellington was on the lot marked “School” on the upper left of the map and was at the top of the hill on Minetown Road. This school was locally known as the “Green School” and was built in 1912. The third and fourth schools were located on the property marked “School” at the bottom of the map along Morden Road and that is where the present school, built in 1969, is located. On the lower part of this same property, where the present tennis court is located, the “Superior School” was built in 1918. WALSH SCOTT SAYWELL SUPERINTENDENT / CEO SECRETARY TREASURER / CFO District Overview Informational Section Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools is the second largest school district on Vancouver Island and resides within the traditional territories of the Snuneymuxw, Snaw-Naw-As and Stz’uminus First Nations. In addition, the District is a proud community partner with the Tillicum Lelum Aboriginal Friendship Centre, Kwumut Lelum, and the Mid Island Métis Nation. The District boasts approximately 14,700 students and offers a full range of programs from Kindergarten to Grade 12 with additional programs for pre-school and adult learners. There are 27 elementary schools; 8 secondary schools (including Learning Alternatives and Career Technical Centre); one distributed learning school – Island ConnectEd (K-12); and approximately 2,200 employees. The District encompasses the city of Nanaimo, the town of Ladysmith, the District of Lantzville, Gabriola Island, North Oyster, Cedar and many other communities which represent a population of 126,047 (2016 Census). The District’s annual consolidated Budget is $166 million. Page 42 of 94 3 Message from the Superintendent I am pleased to present the proposed recommended budget for 2020-21.
