THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: March 27, 2013 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent and Phil Turin, Secretary-Treasurer SUBJECT: 2013/2014 Local School Calendar BACKGROUND: At the January 30, 2013 the Board adopted Motion R13/01/30-26 as follows: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to take forward proposed changes to the local school calendar, in compliance with Bill 36, to stakeholders and that as part of the process that staff provide detailed information on the Success for All and Professional Learning Community initiatives and that the consultation include an additional five day spring break.” As a result of this motion a consultation process was undertaken on three possible changes to the traditional calendar for the 2013-14 school year as follows:  Schedule two "Success for All" inservice days for all staff, with students not attending on these days - Tuesday, September 3, 2013 and Friday, January 31, 2014. The Learning Services In-Service and Professional Development budget will support these days and will be at no additional costs to the Board.  Start school an hour later every Wednesday to allow staff to meet focusing on strategies that will improve student learning.  Extend Spring Break in March 2014 by five days, from March 24 to 28 as a budget strategy to save approximately $400,000. RECOMMENDATIONS: Motion # 1: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopts a Local School Calendar for 2013-2014, that schedules two “Success for All” in-service days for all staff, with students not attending on these days – Tuesday, September 3, 2013 and Friday, January 31, 2014, as per the attached proposed Local School Calendar.” Motion # 2: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopts a Local School Calendar for 2013-2014, starting school an hour later every Wednesday to allow staff to meet as Professional Learning Communities, focusing on strategies that will improve student learning.” Motion # 3: “That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopts a Local School Calendar for 2013-2014, subject to meeting the requirements of the NDTA Collective Agreement pertaining to school year, that extends Spring Break in March 2014 by five days from March 24 to March 28 as per the attached proposed Local School Calendar.”
