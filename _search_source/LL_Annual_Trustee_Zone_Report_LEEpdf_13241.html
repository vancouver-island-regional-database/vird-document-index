ANNUAL TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Lee Date: Nov 2025 School Names/Zone: Dover Bay Family of Schools Annual Highlights: Frank J Ney They are proud of their social emotional and functional skills support groups. Their team (classroom teachers, admin, ISTs, EAs, support staff, child and youth care workers and counsellor) have looked closely at student wellness data and functional needs of some of their students and created groups to address these needs. Their groups are in full swing, and they feel like they addressing the needs of their most vulnerable students. McGirr Students and staff are proud to have all learnt Patrick Alecks Equality song. This song is all about how we can stand together as one and sing as one. Proud to sing this loudly at assemblies when the whole school gets together. Working hard as a school to advance reconcil ACTION. Mcgirr also has a busy sports schedule with an abundance of teams. Randerson Ridge A huge win for Randerson is the completion of the main field. For students to be able to have that space to run and play has made all the difference for the school. Happy to have an outdoor learning space where classrooms can sit and learn in nature. Seaview Together as one – nucamat. This is the theme for everything that happens inside the walls of Seaview. Rich in culture and blessed to have Adam teach culture 3 days a week. What a gift for the students and the staff. The school is proud to be working hard learning the Coast Salish anthem and can’t wait to have it mastered. This school was also the elementary school the winner of the energy cup last year. Pleasant Valley Bursting at the seams and full of positive energy. Students love the fish tank that is right inside the entrance for a place to have a break and reset while watching the magnificent creature’s swim. The school is organized by coast salish animals as a way of connecting to the theme of personal awareness. Each animal has specific traits, values and characteristics that help students connect to being good humans. Dover Bay Students and staff have been working hard on learning hul’q’umi’num with the help of tsumqwatan and the students from the hul’q’umi’num class. We raise our hands to you for encouraging the use of the original language spoken in our region. Many athletes were honoured with a Civic Merit Award from Nanaimo City Council for their excellence in athletics.
