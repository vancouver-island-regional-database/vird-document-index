DISCUSSION: This project will see the development, implementation and evaluation of a health clinic within John Barsby Community School (JBCS) which is situated in the inner city area of Nanaimo. The clinic would provide interdisciplinary health services to a marginalized population of children, youth and their families that lie within the area. The elementary feeder schools include Bayview, Fairview, Park Avenue and Georgia Avenue Community Schools. John Barsby Community School is also proximal to Tillicum Lelum Friendship Centre/Health Centre and the Snuneymuxw First Nation. John Barsby Community School also houses the Little Ferns Program. Many children, youth and their families that live in this area have difficulty accessing health services and, therefore, often do not have their health needs met. Providing health services in the school would allow for timely, appropriate, close to home health care. The project will help mitigate the lack of appropriate health service delivery and create a health care “safety net” for this vulnerable population. This project supports Island Health’s strategic priorities of 1. Improved Health of High Needs Populations and 2. A Sustainable and Integrated Network of Health Services. The project will endeavour to develop a continuum of care structure and could include services delivered by:  community health nurses,  a Nurse Practitioner (NP) if avaialble,  Family Physicians and  Family Practice Residents,  Paediatricians and  Child and Adolescent Psychiatrists. Community based services that would integrate with the medical practitioners could include:  school staff (classroom teacher, counselor, principal, vice principal, child and youth care workers, school psychologists and other support staff)  child and youth mental health clinicians (aboriginal and non aboriginal),  child wellness therapists and family support workers (Tillicum),  social workers (MCFD and KL) and  other community supports as determined by individual need. Page 3 of 5 The services provided by the clinic could include the following:  Direct health care by the most appropriate health care practitioner  Referral to a higher level of health care as needed – nurse or NP to Family Physician to Paediatrician to Child and Youth Psychiatry – all of which could have a “clinic” opportunity in the clinic.  Fully Integrated interdisciplinary assessments and management including: o General health concerns o Sexual health o Mental health concerns o Developmental and learning difficulties o Chronic illness  Education and health promotion for students, staff and community Guiding principles of the clinic model could be:  interdisciplinary assessment and management which would be  dependent on the utilization of a “connector person” who would assist the patient/family in the navigation when needed (facilitated)  integrated, comprehensive service appropriate to the health needs of this population  “one stop shop” for health and related services  seamless care delivery Which will:  build on existing programs and partnerships  provide health practitioners a coordinated system to measure and track client progress  do more to make families aware of available services  identify the services and work together to build capacity to fill any gaps  identify opportunities to collaborate and coordinate their services and make the most effective use of people, skills and resources Preliminary discussions with JBCS administration, SD 68, NRGH Family Residency Program Lead, Island Health Director of Public Health Services and the MHO, Tillicum Lelum Friendship Centre Administration, Snuneymuxw First Nation Health Director, and Aboriginal CYMH Team Leader have occurred and there is positive response and initial interest to participate.
