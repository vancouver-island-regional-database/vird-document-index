I was asked to write this letter by the NDTA Executive. Originally the NDTA Executive tasked me with encouraging you to add the School District as a signatory to the Pharmacare Consensus Principles (attached to my original letter, but also available here with an updated list of signatories: htt s://nursesunions.ca/ harmacare-consensusgrinciplesl ). However, the coalition of signatories is limited to national, provincial, and territorial organizations. School Districts cannot be signatories, and nor can local unions. After I sent the letter to Chair McKay, it was brought to my attention that Trustees belong to a provincial organization: the BC School Trustees Association, and that the BCSTA is hosting its Annual General Meeting this spring. As elected officials charged with the educational well being of the students of BC, I believe that the BCSTA should support universal pharmacare. About one-third of working Canadians do not have employer funded prescription drug coverage. Many of our students will not have access to the medications they need because their families cannot afford them. Access to health care, including medication, is a basic human right. It is time for universal pharmacare in Canada. When my letter comes up in the agenda on February 27, please consider a motion to recommend to the BCSTA AGM to join the coalition in signing the Pharmacare Consensus Principles and showing support for truly universal medicare in Canada.
