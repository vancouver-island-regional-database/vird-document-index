As such, future funding for Local Capital is dependent on either additional asset sales, of which we lack inventory of (*Ministry must also approve the District retaining associated funds as Local Capital Funds, not restricting them for future Capital projects), and/or transfers from Operating where surplus balances exist. The amounts indicated under ‘Proceeds from Disposals’ include the following property sales; 2012/13 – Diamond Primary and City of Nanaimo land Transfers 2013/14 – Waterloo, Oliver Road and City of Nanaimo Land Transfers 2014/15 – Princess Royal 2017/18 – Old Victoria Road, NDSS Serauxman Stadium/Rotary Bowl Local Capital Budget/Project Summary: In order to ensure that the District is protected financially from unforeseen events, cost overruns that may be incurred from MOE funded capital projects, maintenance due to aging facilities issues not funded by Ministry, initial cost associated with capital project plan developments (PDR) which may or may not be approved, physical space constraints and other emergent costs, a contingency reserve is in place. It’s essential that Local Capital funds, and the contingency reserve, be maintained and effectively managed over the long term to actively mitigate negative impacts to students and district operations.
