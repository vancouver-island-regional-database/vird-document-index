NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 11th, 2026 Strategic Directions Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Proposed 2027/2028 (Major) Five-Year Capital Plan Recommendation The Strategic Directions Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) in accordance with provisions under section 142(4) of the School Act, approve the proposed Major Five-Year Capital plan for 2027/2028, as provided, for submission to the Ministry of Infrastructure, Education & Child Care Capital Branch. Background Aligned with the Board’s goals and the Long Range Facilities Plan (LRFP), this document provides an overview of the major capital projects the district anticipates undertaking. Minor capital projects are addressed through a separate memo and motion. The capital plan continues to focus on the district’s two primary challenges: capacity constraints and significant seismic requirements. While annual updates reflect changes in enrollment projections and other variables, the overall direction of the plan remains largely consistent from year to year. Discussion The Five-Year Capital Plan Each year, Boards of Education are required to submit a Five-Year Capital Plan detailing the high priority capital projects needed for their school districts, in accordance with the Capital Plan instructions. Each year capital plan instructions are published which guide staff on the preparation and submission: https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/education-training/k-12/administration/capital/planning School districts' annual Five-Year Capital Plan submissions play a crucial role in identifying priority capital projects for the Ministry's upcoming fiscal year. Additionally, these submissions offer valuable insight into the district’s priorities for future years, contributing to the Ministry's long-term capital planning. The Annual Five-Year Capital Plan Submission is guided by the Long Range Facilities Plan (LRFP), through which school districts assess their capital needs over the next five years. This process includes prioritizing capital requirements, conducting preliminary assessments of project needs, options, scoping, and costing, and subsequently developing and submitting the Five-Year Capital Plan. Along with the plan, supporting documentation, including a Project Request Fact Sheet (PRFS), is also provided. The Ministry provides each school district with a written response to their annual Five-Year Capital Plan submission once the assessment of all submissions is complete, and funding for fiscal year 2027/2028 is announced by the Ministry. See attached response letter to the 2026/2027 Capital submission. Operating Capacity Previously, class sizes for Kindergarten, Grades 1-7, and Grades 8-12 were set in legislation, and were mandatorily applied to all school districts across the province. Currently, class sizes are negotiated as a working condition for teachers in their local contract with the board of education. As such, operating capacities vary between school districts. Individual school districts must determine the operating capacities of existing schools to calculate their capacity utilization. For the purpose of these submissions an elementary school capacity is calculated as the average class size over the last 3 years multiplied by the number of classrooms available, by Ministry standards, in each school. Portable Demand Based on approved addition and new school projects and enrollment projections, a surplus of 12 units is anticipated in five years. Due to the age range of existing portables, some units are not cost-effective to upgrade, as renovation costs approach those of full replacement. As a result, staff will identify units with high upgrade costs and poor overall condition for potential disposal or sale, with a focus on school sites that have received recent additions, contain multiple portables, and have no projected enrollment growth to support their continued use.
