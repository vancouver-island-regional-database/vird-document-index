EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: This report fulfills the statutory obligation to provide Council with the official results of the 2025 By-Election held March 1, 2025. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: Resolution Resolution Details CS 2024That Council appoint the following individuals as officers for the Town of 299 Ladysmith 2025 By-Election: • Donna Smith, Consultant, as Chief Election Officer; • Sue Bouma, Manager of Corporate Services, as Deputy Chief Election Officer; • Sydney Ianson, Corporate Services Administrative Assistant, as Deputy Chief Election Officer; and • Hayley Young, Corporate Services Administrative Coordinator, as Deputy Chief Election Officer. DISCUSSION: Section 158 of the Local Government Act states that the Chief Election Officer is required to submit a report to Council within 30 days of the declaration of official results. The report must include a compilation of the ballot accounts. The official results of the 2025 By-Election for the office of Mayor were determined on March 3, 2025, and the declaration (Attachment A) was shared to the Town’s website, notice board and social media on the same date. The ballot account reconciliation (Attachment B) references five ballots unaccounted for, and I can confirm that they are unreturned mail ballots. Page 14 of 19 Voter turnout for this by-election was 22.93% compared to 23.98% for the 2022 General Election. The Town uses the Provincial Voters List for its voting opportunities and for the 2025 ByElection, the total number of registered voters on the list was 7,579. There are also eight registered Non-Resident Property Electors. These are electors who do not live in Ladysmith but own property within Town boundaries and, upon their accepted application, are entitled to vote in the Town’s elections. They will remain on the list for future elections unless the property changes ownership. Table 1, below, includes a breakdown of votes for each candidate at each voting opportunity. A total of 37 mail ballots were run through the voting machine at the end of the general voting day (March 1) and are included in that day’s total. Table 1 Each election has its challenges, and trying to get the word out to voters is one example. The Town offers election information via newsletters, social media, the website and the newspaper, however perhaps something more visual, such as signage, is needed to let electors know that an election is on the horizon. Some suggestions for signage include:     large reusable signs placed in the Town’s sign stations during each election reusable “lawn signs” similar to the type that candidates use, but for placement on boulevards directing people to the voting place on voting days reusable “lawn signs” specifically to get the word out prior to the election with a QR code directing people to the Town’s website reusable magnetic signs for Town vehicles with the election logo and a QR code The suggested reusable signage would be a one-time cost that would cover many elections and all signs would include the “Ladysmith Votes” logo. Page 15 of 19 The Town’s current signage consists of three sandwich board signs which have not been replaced in several years, one of which is now unusable. I approve the report and recommendation.
