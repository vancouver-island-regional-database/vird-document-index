STZFN and the School Board agree that the unique educational needs of First Nations students are best met through recognition and respect for their culture, language and heritage. The following agreement provides the details of a joint educational venture between STZFN and the Board. Both partieswish to make it clear that underlying the provisions is a sincere determination to reach a greater level of understanding, communication and cooperation with respect to education of the STZFN children. 3.0 STATEMENT OF SHARED INTENT During the term of this agreement STZFN and the Board intend to work together to achieve the following objectives: 3.1 To work diligently to assess the educational needs of STZFN students with their families and arrange the delivery of services that will meet these needs. 3.2 To enhance and affirm a strong identity, a sense of belonging, pride in heritage, and healthy self-esteem in STZFN students. 3.3 To develop policies, programs, and procedures that increases the “and respect of all educationailistaff for cultural"and linguisticdifferences. To facilitate and increase the percentage of Stz’uminus First Nation graduates 3.4 9/15/2015 awareness Page 5 of 22 who demonstrate skills of self-sufficiency and responsibility such that they are able to secure employment or gain entrance into post-secondary institutions. 3.5 The Board and the STZFN will work collaboratively to define the appropriate steps for the STZFN involvement in the Boards hiring process and once developed will be added as an appendix to this Agreement. 3.6 To actively involve, in collaboration with STZFN, Elders of STZFN and other First Nations resource people in the school system. 3.7 To collaborate with STZFN education personnel to provide opportunities in training and professional development activities of the school district. 3.8 To endorse the Federal and Provincial policies on employment equity. 3.9 To work together to increase awareness of First Nation culture among all students and, subject to funding being available, to provide for the integration of Stz’uminus cultural values and information about the Stz’uminus First Nation in appropriate curriculum areas. 3.10 To work together, subject to the Provincial Curriculum guidelines, to provide a curriculum that reflects the linguistic and cultural differences that exist between STZFN and other students and designed to enhance the learning experience of both. 3.11 To review regularly the education needs of STZFN students and arrange for the delivery of services that will meet these needs. referring the matter to the Dispute Resolution Committee. 16.2 In the event that STZFN Education and Board personnel are unable to resolve a dispute, a letter will be written to the Superintendent of the Board and the STZFN Administrator outlining the details of the dispute and the steps that have been taken to resolve the dispute. Within 10 working days of receipt of this letter, the Superintendent and the STZFN Administrator will meet to discuss and attempt to resolvethe issue. 16.3 Failure of the Superintendent and the STZFN Administrator to resolve a dispute 9/15/2015 Page 15 of22 will result in the dispute being elevated to the Board of School Trustees and STZFN Council through written request. Within 10 working days of receipt of a written request, the Board of School Trustees and the STZFN Council will meet in an attempt to resolve the dispute. Failure of the Board of School Trustees and the STZFN Council to resolve the dispute will result in the Dispute Resolution Committee, as outlined in articles 15.4 to 15.6, dealing with the issue if either party requests in writing that the issue be elevated to the Dispute Resolution Committee. 16.4 Dispute Resolution Committee If a dispute in respect of any interpretation of this Agreement arises between STZFN and the Board which is not settled, the parties shall establish a panel consisting of three members. The panel shall be called the Dispute Resolution Committee. The purpose of the Dispute ResolutionCommittee shall be to resolve as expeditiously as possible any dispute arising under this Agreement so as not to impair progress in the implementation of the provisions of this Agreement. STZFN and the Board will each appoint one member of the Dispute Resolution Committee and the two appointees shall agree upon the appointment of the third member, who shall be the Chair of the Dispute Resolution Committee. The Dispute Resolution Committee will convene within ten business days, or such longer period of time as may be reasonably required to appoint the third member of the Dispute Resolution Committee and, to consider and resolve the dispute. 16.5 Proceedings All proceedings before the Dispute Resolution Committee shall be informal. When a dispute is under consideration by the Dispute Resolution Committee, the Dispute Resolution Committee shall determine the manner in which the parties shall proceed to carry out their respective obligations under this Agreement until the dispute is resolved. No formal rules of evidence shall apply to proceedings of the Dispute Resolution Committee, however, established legal procedure and evidentiary rules may be used as a guide in conducting the proceedings. Proceedings of the Dispute Resolution Committee shall not be open to the public. 16.6 Decision The Dispute Resolution Committeeshall render a written decision to the parties as soon as possible after the conclusion of its proceedings; the Dispute Resolution Committee shall be required to provide reasons for its decision. The Dispute Resolution Committee shall decide the extent to which each of the parties shall bear the reasonable costs of resolving any particular dispute. Any decision of the Dispute Resolution Committee may be submitted by either of the parties to binding arbitration as provided for in Article 16 if the decision is not acceptable to a party. 17.
