SRV1 City of Nanaimo Staff Report for Decision MAY 25, 2026 WOODGROVE AREA PLAN Page 2 BACKGROUND The preparation of a Woodgrove Area Plan for the Woodgrove Secondary Urban Centre is Council Priority Action 196 in the Integrated Action Plan, and a key policy direction in the “City Plan Bylaw 2022 No. 6600” (the City Plan), the City’s Official Community Plan. City Plan (Official Community Plan) identifies the Woodgrove Secondary Urban Centre as one of seven urban centres where future growth and densification is anticipated, with a goal of creating complete communities in urban centres. Area Plans are a key tool used for exploring and setting out more detailed policy direction on a range of matters including land use; transportation; servicing; parks, recreation, culture and wellness. The following Woodgrove Area Plan project updates have been presented to Council:  The Woodgrove Area Plan Phase 1 - Baseline Assessment Report was presented at the 2025-MAR-24 Council meeting.  The Woodgrove Area Plan Phase 2 – Growth Scenarios Assessment Report was presented at the 2025-JUN-16 Council meeting.  The Woodgrove Area Plan Phase 3 - Policy, Actions, Monitoring Framework and Design Guidelines Report was presented at the 2026-JAN-19 Council meeting. Woodgrove Area Plan Process The process of preparing the Woodgrove Area Plan was informed by a UBCM Complete Communities Grant. The assessment was divided into three phases: Phase 1 – Completing a Baseline Assessment, Phase 2 – Testing Growth Scenarios, and Phase 3 – Identifying Policies & Actions. Throughout all the phases, public involvement was a cornerstone of the process, ensuring that the Woodgrove Area Plan reflects the community’s vision for a complete community. The assessment and community engagement have culminated in the preparation of the Woodgrove Area Plan. DISCUSSION Phase 3 | Engagement Summary Results The Woodgrove Area Plan: Phase 3 Engagement Summary report (Attachment A) encapsulates the main findings and strategic direction emerging from the third and final phase of engagement for the Woodgrove Area Plan project. The report summarizes the outreach conducted in June and July 2025, synthesizes the perspectives of 536 participants who were asked their level of support for the recommended policies for the Woodgrove Urban Centre. Outreach & Communications: Input on Phase 3 was sought via a survey (online and hard copies) and two virtual workshops. Participants were prompted to indicate their support/opposition for 31 high-level policy directions, as well as suggested changes. Outreach efforts to inform the community of the survey included sending over 200 letters to addresses in the Woodgrove Urban Centre, distributing over 150 flyers, 10 social media posts reaching over 56,800 views, 62 bus posters, four e-notifications, two newspaper ads and one media release. These outreach tools helped engage 368 new participants who had not yet participated in the project. Much like Phase 1 and 2, a majority of participants were Nanaimo residents, 25% of which live in the Woodgrove area. Respondents were predominantly employed, homeowners, and aged 20 to 65. City of Nanaimo Staff Report for Decision MAY 25, 2026 WOODGROVE AREA PLAN Page 3 Key Take Aways: Overall trends indicated very strong support (between 70% and 90% of participants total) across all 31 of the recommended policies. Strongest support was for including policies on:  Shops and services close to home  Parks, recreation and cultural amenities  Improve transit and active transportation  Walkable, mixed use public spaces  Diverse housing options Between 12% to 20% of participants raised questions and concerns regarding the following:  Building heights (over 6 storeys) and density  Traffic impacts, congestion and parking  Transit exchange relocation (desire for more details)  Competition with downtown  Use/access of “private parks” Community feedback in addition to the findings of the Phase 3 report, were used to inform the preparation of the Woodgrove Area Plan. The introduction of an MDP will give clarity to developers early in the process of the City's expectations for larger multi-phase sites. To support this new policy direction, a Master Development Plan Policy (Attachment D) is proposed to provide clear administrative guidance. The Master Development Plan policy is recommended to be required for a proposed development site with a Future Land Use Designation of Primary or Secondary Urban Centre and a parcel size of 2.5 hectares or greater. The development site may be the entire property, a portion of a property or set of properties under zoning bylaw amendment, subdivision, or development permit application. The outline of the Master Development Plan Policy includes a purpose, scope, policies, and content requirements. The Master Development Plan Policy is drafted in a manner that allows for flexibility to reflect the site context and applicable City Plan (Official Community Plan) land use policies. City of Nanaimo Staff Report for Decision MAY 25, 2026 WOODGROVE AREA PLAN Page 5 The Master Development Plan Policy, is modelled on the City of Coquitlam policy, and will ensure a developer provides a finer level of details in terms of land use and density, the street and block layout, location of parks and open spaces, servicing and utilities, provision of public and civic amenities, public benefits and development phasing. The Master Development Plan Policy also ensures the site’s relationships to adjacent lands are taken into account, in terms of land use compatibility, site access, and transportation network and infrastructure needs, with specific regard for neighbouring remnant parcels, ensuring these sites are able to redevelop under their Land Use Designation. The Urban Centre will be characterized by vibrant, pedestrian-oriented streets; institutional, commercial, and civic employment anchors; and a central transit hub linking the area to other urban centres and regional destinations. Multi-modal streets will encourage walking, rolling, and cycling, while a connected network of parks, plazas, and public spaces will provide opportunities for recreation, cultural expression, and community gathering. Public school and wellness facilities will be integrated with residential, commercial and/or green spaces, fostering intergenerational connection and daily activity. Natural environment— such as a robust terrestrial and aquatic ecosystems, green roofs, and energy-efficient buildings will ensure a resilience and regenerative ecosystem. A complete community is defined as a neighborhood or area where residents can access daily needs, such as housing, work, groceries, natural areas, recreation, within a short walk or cycling/rolling. This urban planning concept integrates land use and transportation to promote mixed-use development, walkability, and access to diverse housing and amenities for all people, regardless of age, income, ability, or background. To realize this vision, vacant lots will need to become sites for infill; underutilized lands will need to be repurposed for higher and more intensive uses; natural areas will need to be protected and reintroduced, and broader services will need to be added to improve resident’s quality of life. Adding housing, jobs, and services in places where some of these uses already exist is a cost effective way for a city to grow and utilize existing infrastructure without expanding its footprint. WOODGROVE TRANSIT EXCHANGE SITE OPTIONS A Woodgrove Transit Exchange Site Options Report (2025) was prepared to identify and evaluate potential site locations for a new Woodgrove Transit Exchange and the implications as it relates to the Transit Oriented Area Legislation. The evaluation included input from a Steering Committee that included the Regional District of Nanaimo, the Ministry of Transportation and Transit and BC Transit. For the purpose of the Complete Communities Assessment, the transit exchange was relocated to the centre point of the intersection of Island Hwy N/Hammond Bay Rd/Aulds Rd. The exact new location will be determined at a future date. Key outputs of the report include potential sites for the exchange and estimates of the transit service capacity required for peak and daily vehicle trips, fleet types, requirements and concepts for layover and breaks. SCHEDULE B E2.3 MASTER DEVELOPMENT PLANS A Master Development Plan may be required for the following applications: f Zoning Bylaw amendments f Subdivision f Development Permit A Master Development Plan is applicable to the entire property or set of properties under application. Master Development Plans will implement the policy direction of City Plan and applicable Area Plan, by providing a finer-grain of detail in terms of land use and density, the street and block layout, location of parks and open spaces, servicing and utilities, provision of public and civic amenities, public benefits and development phasing. A Master Development Plan should also take into account the site’s relationships to adjacent lands, in terms of land use compatibility, site access, and transportation network and infrastructure needs, with specific regard for neighbouring remnant parcels, ensuring these sites are able to redevelop under their Future Land Use Designation.
