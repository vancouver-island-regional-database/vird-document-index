NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: October 24, 2018 Board of Education Carrie McVeigh, Secretary Treasurer 2017 / 2018 Surplus – Recommendations Recommendation: That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) transfer $2,000,000 from the 2017/18 unrestricted surplus balance to local capital to ensure capital needs for the district are adequately funded. Background: At the October 10, 2018, Business Committee meeting, the Business Committee passed the following motion: The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) transfer $2,000,000 from the 2017/18 unrestricted surplus balance to local capital to ensure capital needs for the district are adequately funded. Last month the Board approved the Audited Financial Statements for fiscal year ending June 30, 2018. On Note 12 of the statements, is the Board’s Accumulated Surplus. The Board’s operating surplus is $7,181,685. Restricted Surplus The restricted portion of the surplus is $4,554,883 and contains new restrictions that fall under the Targeted/Constraints Reserve, due to work that could not be completed last year. These are: • • • • Facilities Projects Learning Alternatives – Woodlands site Technology Purchase Orders Community School Coordinators Of the various restricted reserves identified in the new policy, monies were only allocated in two areas; the Staffing Reserve and the Targeted/Constraints Reserve. There were no monies allocated to the Planning, Emergency Events or Emergency Capital Asset Replacement reserve. The Staffing Reserve of $2,000,000 is slightly higher than the maximum amount provided for in the policy due to the addition of $500,000 for the Employee Payroll Tax, which we expect will be government funded and become unrestricted monies. The Targeted/Constraints Reserve has no minimum or maximum but, this year is higher than normal for reasons identified above. Given that the restricted surplus is larger than last year and, staff have recommendations for the use of the unrestricted surplus, no further recommendations to restrict funds is forthcoming. Unrestricted Surplus The District’s new Surplus/Deficit Policy suggests that the Board review the Unrestricted Operating Surplus balance per the audited financial statements and consider allocations to other reserve accounts to provide funding for strategic and other initiatives. The policy recommends that the District maintain an unrestricted operating surplus balance of between 0.5% and 2.0% of Operating Fund expenses. At June 30, 2018 that range would equate to $625,000 to $2,500,000. The current balance is $2,626,802. Therefore, the Board may wish to consider a reallocation of up to $2,000,000. Management recommends that the Board consider reallocating an amount of this surplus to Local Capital because of the increasing pressures on that account. The Ministry is not funding new classroom costs being driven by inclining enrolment and the reinstatement of teacher collective agreement contract language in 2018/19 and therefore, related capital costs need to be funded by Local Capital. These costs include space renovations, additional portables for 2018/19 and proactively preparing for portable needs in 2019/20 by either procuring new portables or refurbishing older ones. In addition to these costs, other significant projects that will require Local Capital funds are as follows: • • • • • • District-wide Wi-fi Upgrade – planning and installation Continuing Technology Plan Investments NDSS Redevelopment – planning and accumulation of capital contribution Telecommunications Replacement in Schools District Finance/HR System Upgrade Data and Analytics Project – planning for a common platform Given these pressures on Local Capital, management recommends that an additional transfer of $2,000,000 from the Operating Fund to Local Capital be included in the 2018/19 Amended Budget.
