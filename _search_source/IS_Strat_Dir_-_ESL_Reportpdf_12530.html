Discussion Enhancing Student Learning Reporting Order Authority: School Act, sections 81 and 168 (2) (t) The Enhancing Student Learning Report (ESLR) is intended to fit within each district’s continuous improvement cycle. It acts as an annual touchpoint for districts to reflect and report on the progress made toward the district strategic plan priorities. This allows districts to adapt strategies to focus on areas for growth on an ongoing basis, and to communicate progress with the public. The Framework for Enhancing Student Learning (ESL) MUST include information and evidence of the following: 1. Intellectual Development • Students will meet or exceed literacy expectation for each grade level • Students will meet or exceed numeracy expectation for each grade level • Number and percentage of students who are completing grade to grade transitions on time 2. Human and Social Development • Students will feel welcome, safe and connected to their school 3. Career Development • Students will graduate 4. Establish categories for each of the following populations and report on those categories separately: Indigenous students ON/OFF reserve, Children and Youth In Care, Students with Disabilities or Diverse Abilities 5. Board’s approach to continuous improvement of student achievement Information Sheet: Enhancing Student Learning Report Review, Completion Rates Page 2 As part of the Framework for Enhancing Student Learning’s Annual Review, the provincial review team pays particular attention to 5 focus areas: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. Approach to continuous improvement Ongoing data and evidence review (qualitative & quantitative data) Ongoing strategic engagement focused on student learning Alignment Improving equity of learning outcomes for Indigenous learners, children and youth in care, and students with disabilities or diverse abilities The review team then builds consensus on strengths and considerations for each district in these focus areas. Our NLPS report has both strengths and areas of growth, with much improvement over this past school year. Strengths: • Ongoing data and evidence review: Clear evidence that the district has embedded comprehensive data and evidence review processes; thorough analysis and interpretation of multiple sources of data. • Ongoing strategic engagement: Broad engagement for the development of the strategic plan and structures to support ongoing engagement • Improving equity of learning outcomes: Demonstration of focus on Indigenous learners Considerations: • Approach to continuous improvement: Much improvement on articulating the district’s continuous improvement cycle, however more explicit description of the application of the process is needed • Alignment: Clearer articulation of the district’s approach to overall alignment (horizontal & vertical) • Improving equity of learning outcomes: Evidence-informed, targeted strategies for cohorts of Indigenous learners, such as FN learners on/off reserve Overall, the feedback targeted areas where intentional focus is occurring, but may not have been articulated in the ESL report. This information sheet provides a summary of the feedback received on Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools’ 2023/2024 Enhancing Student Learning Report. Completion Rates The ESL Report is required to include completion rates that are also disaggregated for the three priority populations: Indigenous learners, children and youth in care, and students with disabilities or diverse abilities. The most recent completion rates (2023/2024) have been released by the Ministry of Education and Child Care. Overall, NLPS student completion rate is 90% (6 Year) and 87% (5 Year). Information Sheet: Enhancing Student Learning Report Review, Completion Rates Page 3 The six-year completion rate is the percent of students who graduated with a Certificate of Graduation or Adult Graduation Diploma, within six years from the first time they enroll in Grade 8.
