ATTACHMENT E – September 28, 2021 Recommendations The following table provides a summary of the recommendations for amendments to the regulations for coach houses from the September 28, 2021 staff report to Committee of the Whole and notes on how these recommendations are proposed to be implemented through: • “Town of Ladysmith Zoning Bylaw 2014, No 1860, Amendment Bylaw (No. 46) Bylaw 2022, No. 2100”, • “Ladysmith Officers and Delegation of Authority Bylaw (No. 3) 2022, No. 2101”, and • “Ladysmith Unparalleled”, “Official Community Plan Bylaw 2022, No. 2200”. Recommendation 1. OCP change: New development permit guideline: Nearby two-storey buildings. Encourage two storey coach houses to be constructed only where there are other two storey buildings nearby. 2. Zoning change: Remove second storey living area requirement. This separation of space does not reduce the massing of coach houses. In fact, it likely increases massing in some cases. 3. Amendment to Officers and Delegation of Authority Bylaw: Staff-issuable single-storey coach houses This will incentivize one-storey coach houses by reducing application processing times by a minimum of 2-3 weeks. 4. Zoning change: Create floor area credit for interior staircases in two storey coach houses Interior staircases are more aesthetically pleasing, more comfortable for occupants, and safer in poor weather, compared to exterior staircases. 5. Zoning and OCP change: Replace zoning requirement for 6m separation from principal dwelling with DP guideline. Section 6.5(b(v) of the Zoning Bylaw requires coach houses to be located at least 6 metres from the principal building. Bylaw No. 2100 amends the definition of “coach house dwelling” in the Zoning Bylaw to allow a coach house to be located on both storeys. Bylaw No. 2101 amends the Delegation of Authority Bylaw to allow the Director of Development Services to approve coach houses that are 5.0m or less in height. New OCP includes a DPA guideline discouraging exterior staircases. Bylaw No. 2100 allows for a floor area credit of up to 9.0 square metres for an interior staircase. Zoning change: Different side yard setbacks for one and two storey coach houses Bylaw No. 2100 increases the side and rear yard setbacks from 1.5m to 2.0m for coach houses that are over 5.0m tall. Increase the side yard setback by 0.5m for two-storey coach houses. 7. Zoning change: Allow more siting options for coach houses, particularly on corner lots In some cases (particularly for corner lots) it makes sense for coach houses to be located in side yards. 8. Zoning change: Exclude 45m2 of covered parking area from site coverage calculations for one storey coach houses. Many homes in Old Town do not have garages and staff have found multiple examples where owners wishing to have both a covered garage/parking area and coach house have no choice under the Zoning Bylaw but to build a two-storey coach house to provide both a dwelling unit and covered parking. 9. Zoning change: Change accessory building regulations to incentivize one storey coach houses. A coach house is considered an accessory building and only two accessory buildings are allowed per parcel. This encourages owners to avoid losing indoor accessory building space by relocating it into the lower floor of a two storey coach house. 10. Zoning change: Remove floor area maximum for secondary suites in certain zones The Zoning Bylaw limits secondary suites to 40% of the single-unit dwelling or 90 m2, whichever is less. New development permit guideline: Supplemental variance Staff are proposing that a development permit guideline be added specifying that the Town may require that a proposed twostorey coach house be lowered to one-storey in cases where the reduction in height is necessary to achieve the intent of the guidelines. Bylaw No. 2099 amends the OCP adding guideline as described. 12. Zoning change: Correct the minimum parcel size for coach houses on corner lots The minimum parcel size for coach houses for parcels on a lane is 660 m2, but for parcels on a corner parcel, the minimum parcel size is 668m2 which excludes some corner parcels in Old Town. Bylaw No. 2100 allows coach houses on parcels larger than 550m2 (with lane access or corner parcels and in zones where coach houses are permitted). The overall size of the principal building in all cases is still controlled by other zoning regulations including parcel coverage. Bylaw No. 2100 removes the 90m² maximum size for secondary suites in the R-2, R-2-A and R-2-LW zones which are the zones that allow coach houses. The maximum size of the suites would still be limited to 90m2 in all other zones. Note the recommended change from 660m² to 550m² as the minimum parcel size for allowing a coach house in the R-2 Zone. This is to accommodate the existing houses on High Street. The existing narrow properties on High Street are approximately 277m² and some of the existing houses are on two parcels. The lower minimum parcel size would allow these property owners to construct a coach house if they consolidate their two properties. This modification from the original recommendation was prompted by enquiries from property owners on High Street asking if it would be possible for them to construct coach houses. 3 Recommendation Notes on Implementation Note also the recommended change to a minimum of 668m² for all other parcels, the existing requirement is for 780m². The main impact of this change will open the option of a coach house to the areas of the R-2 zone without lane access (e.g. Root Street, 4th Avenue extension). 668m² is the largest minimum parcel size for residential parcels in our Zoning Bylaw. 13.
