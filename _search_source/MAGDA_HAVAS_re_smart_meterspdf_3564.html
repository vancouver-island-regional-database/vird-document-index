WHAT DR. MAGDA HAVAS SAYS ABOUT SMART METERS DR HAVAS states that FCC & Canadian standards for smart meters are insufficient to protect public health & if smart meters are placed on every home, they’ll contribute significantly to our exposure to microwave radiation which is both unsafe and unwise. Outdated FCC standards & Canada’s Safety Code 6 are based on thermal effects, i.e. the amount of radiation that would heat an adult male in peak physical condition in the US Military when exposed to radar. But adverse health consequences occur at rates well below the thermal guidelines (Inglis 1970). Therefore, several countries have opted for biologically based guidelines that also consider dangers to different body types, e.g. children, pregnant women, the elderly, and those with electrohypersensitivity (EHS). In Switzerland, about 5% of the population has EHS. If the same fraction of population in the US has EHS, that would amount to a staggering 15 million people. Those with immune deficiency, metal implants, pacemakers or deep brain stimulators for Parkinson’s disease are also at risk. Russia, Switzerland, Poland and China have developed stronger guidelines well below FCC & Canadian standards, i.e. 10 versus 1000microWatts/cm2 or 1% of FCC standards. EMR pre WW11 was negligible, but today we’re designing more equipment that relies on higher frequencies in the electromagnetic spectrum, e.g. all types of radar, radio, TV and cell phone antennas and city-wide WIFI and WiMax antennas. The pollution continues indoors with cordless phones that emit EMR even when not in use, WIFI computers and Internet, IPads, smart phones, WIFI baby monitors and video games. The smart meter is yet another source of EMR pollution. As part of this system, household appliances are being designed to communicate directly with smart meters, all in WIFI, which will ultimately increase the levels of radiation within the home. In Switzerland, further precautions must be taken to keep EMR emissions as low as possible in areas designated as “Places of Sensitive Use” where people spend more than 2 ½ days a week, e.g. living and bedrooms, classrooms and kindergartens, certain workplaces, hospitals and nursing homes. FCC & Health Canada Guidelines are inadequate as they don’t take into account 9 variables when determining the impact of microwave radiation:      Power density Intensity & relative phase of all EMR components Specific frequency ranges Waveform characteristics Exposure factors     Specific occupations Level of control over exposed populations Individual differences The presences of other environmental stressors In addition to WIFI radiation from smart meters, they also contribute to the “dirty electricity” problem by reradiating EMR along wires both inside and outside the home. Those mentioned above who may be adversely affected by WIFI EMR will likely be adversely affected by “dirty electricity.”
