THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 688 MINUTES OF SPECIAL BOARD MEETING HELD APRIL 18, 2013 Trustees J. Brennan, in the Chair D. Allen B. Bard K. Howland D. Neary B. Robinson - 6:47 pm S. Welch Absent: N. Curley T. Saunders Staff D. Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO P. Turin, Secretary-Treasurer J. Blain, Deputy Superintendent P. Trick, Associate Superintendent/HR P. Sabo, Director of Planning/Operations D. Reimer, Director of Communications C. Kelt, Executive Assistant Meeting No. S14 CALL TO ORDER The Chair called the meeting to order at 6:01 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. APPROVAL OF THE AGENDA IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Neary IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Howland Folio S13/04/18-01 That the Agenda be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Budget Presentation - P. Turin Phil Turin gave an introductory presentation about the 2013-14 budget. He said that the district is facing a $4.7 million deficit next year. Complete information about our proposed budget is available at: www.sd68.bc.ca/News/budget2013-14.asp Chair Brennan said the first presenter was Mr. Justin Green, president of the NDTA. Presentations Justin Green, NDTA president, urged the Board put the needs of school communities first and to reconsider proposals affecting a number of teachers. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 18, 2013 Kelly Dunaway, CUPE 606 General Vicepresident, expressed concern about the proposed reductions to support staff in the District Resource Centre, the district Print Shop, the Nanaimo Ladysmith Schools Foundation, the Finance Department, and custodial positions. He also spoke against the loss of wages for education assistants in an extended spring break. Maia O'Donnell and Katie Dodd, Davis Road students, told the Board about the positive atmosphere at their school and asked why the district is considering moving the English stream students out of the school. Teacher Librarians and Adrianna Furlan spoke about the proposed closure of the District Resource Centre and outlined the reasons the district still needs resources for schools to be housed and distributed centrally. Nancy Henry, parent at Davis Road, said that she believes there could be better solutions than the proposal to move the English stream students out of Davis Road to save $100,000. Alice Kedves, parent at Davis Road, suggested the Board reduce management positions to make budget savings. Karen Feduik, parent at Davis Road Elementary, questioned the district’s enrolment projections for Davis Road and said that the district would lose students if the plan goes ahead to move Davis Road English stream students. Leonard Seigler, parent at Quarterway Elementary, said that the English stream students’ education will be disrupted if the plan goes ahead to move them to Brechin and Forest Park Elementary schools. Jenny Van Horne, parent at Davis Road Elementary, said that school trustees are responsible to reflect local priorities, values and expectations. She outlined the ways that the Davis Road school parent community has supported the school with such things as equipment and books. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 18, 2013 Jeremy Kerr, a parent at Davis Road Elementary, said that the proposal to move English stream Davis Road students would be highly disruptive and suggested the district has not fully identified the costs associated with the proposal. Brandie Lewis, a parent at Davis Road Elementary, said that the proposal related to Davis Road is not consistent with the district’s strategic plan. Jane Saunders, Janelle Mould and Helen Fall, representing Davis Road school staff, said that the disruption to their school is not worth the relatively small amount of savings that would be gained. Jade Ballard, a Davis Road parent, said that Davis Road is the neighbourhood school for the English stream students in that community and she urged the Board to keep the school intact. Lance and Darla Martin, Quarterway Elementary parents, said that the proposal to move English stream students from that school to neighbouring schools discriminates against the English stream students for whom Quarterway is their neighbourhood school. Michelle Lieffertz, a Davis Road parent, said that the quality of education at Davis Road is more personalized because of the size of the school and the community that supports the school. Jeff Lewis, a Davis Road parent, presented results of a survey of parents at that school, saying that a number of parents said they would relocate their children outside of the district if the English stream students are moved out of the school. He pointed out that there has been a very short timeframe for the parents to respond to the proposal. Sharron Onciul, a grandparent and volunteer at Davis Road, said that the family concept of K-7 schools is the best method of delivering education to elementary students. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 18, 2013 Erin Dovey, a teacher and parent at Davis Road, said that moving the English stream students away from their neighbourhood school is not an environmentally responsible option. Heather Trawick, a teacher and parent at Davis Road, said that the school is a vibrant community and that the duality of French and English enriches all students at the school and she spoke against the changes proposed at both Davis Road and Quarterway. Priya Bhatti, a Davis Road parent, said that having a dual track school is supportive of students who, because of learning challenges, may need to change from the French Immersion program to the English program. Chantal Blumell, a Davis Road parent, expressed concern about the short timeline given to parents to respond to the budget proposals. Cheryl Onciul, a Davis Road parent, asked the Board to delay making any decisions about changes at Davis Road while trustees look at the larger picture of changes that might be proposed in the ten-year facilities plan. Lorena Mueller, a Davis Road parent, said that split grade classes at Davis Road are successful and suggested that budget reductions should be made in management staff. Madison Lussier and Stephanie Lebitschnig, students at Cedar Community Secondary School, spoke about the facilities plan recommendations for the Cedar area and said budget reductions should be made at the district office instead of in schools. Wade Fotherby, speaking on behalf of Davis Road, said that the proposal for Davis Road would change the makeup of the Ladysmith community and that the change would drive families to private schools. Draft Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held April 18, 2013 Correspondence: Folio S13/04/18-02 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Allen IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Neary That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receives all of the correspondence regarding budget initiatives and refers them to the Budget Committee. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY QUESTION PERIOD Chair Brennan responded to the questions from audience members.
