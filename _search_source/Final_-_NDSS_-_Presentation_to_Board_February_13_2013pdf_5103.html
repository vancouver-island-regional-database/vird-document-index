Nanaimo District Secondary School Safety Improvements March 2013 Update Pete Sabo Director of Planning and Operations  The Board requested staff look at the safety issues in the front of NDSS.  Staff met with City of Nanaimo and RDN transit representatives to review the issues.  The City and RDN had been working on the matter and had a viable option for the District to consider.  District staff have considered the suggestions and reviewed them with School Administration. Introduction Concept Plan – City of Nanaimo  Currently, students walk straight out from the school doors and across Wakesiah to catch the northbound bus.  The SD plan is to tie into the relocated crosswalk.  Relocation of the crosswalk will make its use more desirable.  A direct path to the crosswalk combined with fencing will help guide students towards the proposed crossing. Concept Plan – SD68  The final result will include:  Two new walkways, safer paths to bus stops, and site improvements lending to social spaces.  Improved pedestrian safety through separation of pedestrian and vehicular circulation.  Upgraded approach to the main entry of school.  Proper curb and parking island definition.  Enhanced crosswalk and traffic calming right at main school entrance.  Increased overall safety Benefits  School administration have reviewed the plans with schools staff and stakeholders.  The City and RDN are currently working on detailed plans for the relocation of the existing crosswalk.  The SD plan is complete, and estimates have been obtained and a budget recommended in the 13/14 AFG.  Staff to work with RDN and City to coordinate final planning, costs and funding as well as implementation issues – next meeting is March 14th.  Advance planning for work during the summer of 2013, with completion expected end of August, 2013.
