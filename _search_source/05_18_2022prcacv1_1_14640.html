Motion Carried Page 2|2 PRC Department Update ADMINISTRATION SUMMARY April Drop-In admission were 981 and Membership scans were 3,700 to the facility. PROGRAM AREA SUMMARY: HEALTH & WELLNESS All provincial health orders were lifted as of April 8, 2022. With the removal of appointments, the mask mandate and vaccination passport requirements, Fitness Centre Staff were starting to see some familiar faces return after the two year ‘break’. For the past two years, and due to physical distancing space requirements most Health and Wellness programs had been offered in the gymnasium, but we have smoothly transitioned back into the Upper Hall, for many of these programs. This will now provide more opportunity for use in the gymnasium. Registration and participation numbers continue to be steady for program areas of health and wellness. On average, Fitness Centre participant usage for April was over 2,000, with an additional 470 users participating in drop-in fitness and sport classes and sessions. AQUATICS April was another busy month in Aquatics. On average, there were 2,700 participants in drop-in aquatic programs. Red Cross swim lessons continue to be very popular with high registration and attendance. PRC Leadership Staff continue to work on strategies to increase Aquatic Staff capacity and numbers. RECREATION - PRESCHOOL/CHILDREN/YOUTH Youth Zone on Fridays continued in April. Approximately 20 in attendance. Rec Room is open @ lunch on Tuesdays with approximately 10 in attendance. Matt observing positive connections made between youth each week. Staff continue to solidify the plan and details for Adventure Zone Summer Daycamps. The planning and coordination also include hiring Summer Daycamp staff. The Town was successful with an application to Canada Summer Jobs 2022, which provides wage subsidies to create job placements for youth. Five Summer Daycamp positions will be partially funded by Canada Summer Jobs 2022. Page 1|3 PROGRAM AREA PROJECTS & SPECIAL EVENTS Youth Week Staff continued to work with community partners and confirmed activities for Youth Week, including a NEW Charcuterie Culinary Workshop with local business Fig & Olive, and golf with the Ladysmith Golf Club. Storybook Walk On Tuesday, April 5, in partnership with LRCA – Adventures in Early Literacy, we launched another series of Storybook Walks at Kinsmen Park – a community supported project promoting literacy and movement in the outdoors. A pop-up event was organized at the park with the kick-off theme being ‘pen pals’ and Dear Dragon by Josh Funk. A perfect book for all to read as they wander, imagine and interact with nature. For 2022, books will be selected bi-monthly and installed on Tuesdays. The Town and LRCA are continuing to plan and organize an ongoing series of pop-up events at Kinsmen Park for the duration of the year. Each pop-up will complement the Storybook Walk theme and focus on bringing together community to promote physical literacy and reading.
