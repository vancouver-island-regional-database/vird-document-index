NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: March 6, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Paper Reduction Strategy – Update Background: The District recently adopted a Print Strategy as ‘paper and GHG reduction strategies’ under the District’s Environment Stewardship Action Plan (ESAP) requirement to “Reduce our impact on the land and implement restorative efforts”. The Print Strategy was developed through a comprehensive review of the print and copy environment throughout the organization. Staff in Finance and IT worked with an external consultant as well as school and district staff at all schools and sites to examine options to limit paper and electricity use associated with printing and copying taking place in the District, while also addressing our aging fleet of copiers and the inflating costs of paper. Specifically, the strategy envisions three priorities to lessen paper and electricity use as follows: 1. A significant reduction of inefficient, single use “standalone” printers in the District replaced by larger multifunction copier/printers (MFP) dispersed through schools to ensure appropriate geographic placement for staff to ensure convenience; 2. The use of a print management software program that requires all printing to be released at source by individual users with individual passwords for access and that provides visibility and reporting on printer and copier usage data; and 3. Communication about the strategy's importance to promote less paper usage and address concerns about the impact of the removal of single use machines. An overview of the proposed Print Strategy was presented to the Business Committee on November 09, 2022. This information sheet provides a summary of the implementation status of the Print Strategy across the District along with observations on its initial impact. Discussion: Implementation and Status Following the adoption of the Print Strategy, a comprehensive implementation plan was developed with the goal of completing the rollout at every district school and administrative site by September 2023. Information packages outlining the present and proposed print/copy environment, details on the new devices, cost information, estimated environmental benefits, reductions of standalone printer devices, support and training plans were communicated with all school and site administrators providing an opportunity for concerns, questions, and change requests to be addressed. Following acceptance of the implementation plan, the rollout began in March 2023. As of September 2023, the new printing environment based on the Print Strategy was completed. Information Sheet: Print Strategy – Update Page 2 Summary, Impact and Preliminary Results Reduction of Standalone Printers • • Standalone printers are associated with higher overall environmental costs vs MFPs. As of September 2023, we have reduced our fleet count from 382 to 146 standalone printers, a reduction of over 60%. Printers removed were handed over to our vendor for environmentally responsible recycling and disposal. Our MFP fleet was increased slightly from (85 to 93) units to accommodate printing workloads redirected from removed standalone printers. The fleet size is now actively managed, where requests for new or replacement printers are now being reviewed and approved only where solid rationale exists (e.g., to address accessibility needs). Staff are currently developing Internal Procedures to streamline and formalize this process. Impact: o An assumed positive energy and financial impact will have resulted from the removal of 60% of our standalone printer fleet, where printing workloads have been diverted to more efficient MFP devices. Part of the Print Strategy is to actively manage this fleet size and continue to make further reductions where they are possible and make sense. o An anticipated negative impact from staff ‘missing’ their classroom or desktop printer has not materialized to a significant, or lasting degree. This may be attributed to attention made towards thoughtful and strategic device count/distribution planning where equity was factored in, stakeholders were consulted, and concerns addressed prior to implementation. Software-Managed Printing, Monitoring and Reporting A new print-management software tool, a key component of the Print Strategy, is now fully deployed in our district. The software provides important features to reduce the energy and environmental costs of printing and copying and to provide valuable and insightful printing and copying statistics. • “Find-Me Printing”: This is a new printing method where a user sends a print job from their computer to a virtual ‘queue’, and then releases their printout at any MFP device they choose to walk up to by entering their unique PIN code at the printer. This feature serves to reduce abandoned or accidental print jobs and increases the security and privacy of confidential or sensitive print content as documents are only released to the MFP in the user’s presence at the printing device. Impact: o Find-Me Printing is the most prominent change experienced by users in the District, as it replaces the traditional methods of remote printing. While the requirement on users to release their print job only when present at the MFP is expected to result in a reduction in wasted printing (from abandoned and accidental print jobs), it’s a new method for users to learn and get used to, and for some, an extra step that is seen as a loss of previously enjoyed convenience. Acceptance has been generally positive for the most part, and significant or widespread push-back has not been observed. Training was provided at every site during implementation and support resources are made easily available to all users. The rationale behind the Print Strategy – its direct connection to the District 's Environmental Stewardship Action Plan – has arguably gone a long way towards user acceptance of the changes. o The new technical environment implementation has been more complex and resource intensive than anticipated, with a small number of unpredictable but significant challenges initially experienced during the rollout. These challenges are mostly resolved and expected to be overcome in the near future. Information Sheet: Print Strategy – Update • Page 3 Energy-efficient Print/Copier options: The print management software has provided the District the ability to reliably apply and manage energy-efficient print and copy configurations across every printing device, such as black-and white and double-sided printing. Impact: o Little user-impact has resulted - users have the option to select non-default options (colour and/or single-sided output) when needed. Energy, paper and supply costs resulting from the universally applied default settings will be achieved. • Print Usage Data and Reporting: The software provides visibility, reporting and insight on paper, energy and consumable usage associated with all printing and copying taking place in a school or site and across the District, down to the user level if needed. Realtime data and reports are being made readily available to school, site and department administrators on printer usage and the environmental impact. Individual users have access to their own print and copy usage levels. Impact: o Actionable data and intelligence made readily available to managers and individuals on our print/copy costs, consumption levels and trends are expected to provide the greatest driver towards continued and lasting shifts to changes in behaviour related to our environmentally costly dependence on printing and copying in our district. Countless opportunities for energy and paper reduction initiatives and innovations can be construed at the District, school and workgroup level, with metric goals and objectives now available in real time via the software to measure and celebrate the immediate and long-term impacts of such initiatives. Staff are currently working on the rollout of reporting features of the software and the first goal of creating a set of monthly usage reports that will automatically be distributed to Managers/Principals. The reports will highlight top printers, top users, number of copies broken out by color/grayscale, type of print job, and environmental impact. As the print management software has only been installed on district devices for less than a year, actual paper usage and cost comparisons have not been quantified yet: we expect to be able to gain better insight into that once the environment has been in place for at least one full school year cycle to make comparisons – print volumes are heavily influenced by demands from the activities throughout a typical school cycle: start-up forms, report card seasons, school breaks, etc. Next Steps and Conclusion Communication will be the focus of the strategy over the remainder of the year, including: • scheduled reports on printer/copier usage and costs distributed to school and site administrators/managers • self-serve access to reporting tools for administrators and managers • ongoing awareness of support and training resources available to all district printer and copier users • development of internal procedures on printer device requisitions with the goal of maintaining and reducing our footprint. Following the completion of the technical implementation of the new Print Strategy, the District has taken major steps in optimizing its printer and copier environmental footprint. More significantly, through the redesign of our technical printing environment and the introduction of sets of tools that provide levels of visibility into the financial and environmental costs associated with our printing environment, it is now well equipped to plan, action and measure the impact of further initiatives related to printing and copying, towards the goal to "Reduce our impact on the land and implement restorative efforts”.
