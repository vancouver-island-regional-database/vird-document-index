FEBRUARY 2016 Full copy of the Accord is available online: http://www.ladysmith.ca/docs/agreements/2012-05-25-naut’sa-mawt-accord-fully-executed.pdf 2 | NAUT’SA MAWT “WORKING TOGETHER” COMMUNITY ACCORD Background Working Together for a Shared Future Council members of the Stz’uminus First Nation and the Town of Ladysmith first signed a Community Accord in 2007, in which they pledged to strengthen their relationship and work together on matters of common interest. These commitments were renewed in 2012 under an updated Accord, titled the Naut’sa mawt Community Accord (“working together” in the Hul’qumi’num language). Updates to the Accord included: The purpose of the Accord remains the same: to formally recognize and understand the common interests and goals of the two communities so that a strong framework is in place for community leaders to pursue, in partnership, initiatives and opportunities that serve greater good. The two Councils celebrate their successes together in this report. · a new section on implementing joint initiatives; · a commitment to joint education and communications; · a pact to develop further agreements together; and, · language that better reﬂects the respect and strengthened relationship between the two communities. Liquid Waste Management Plan This detailed plan was prepared by the Town to ensure that collection, treatment and discharge of liquid waste (including sewage and stormwater) meets or exceeds federal/provincial environmental standards. This Plan also helps us plan for the future and includes capacity at the Town’s treatment plant for sanitary sewer services for SFN lands. The SFN was invited to sit on the liquid waste management plan committee. The SFN Council received a detailed presentation and the Council endorsed the Plan. This endorsement included support for the relocation of a midden, resulting in significant savings to the project, a good outcome for both the Town and SFN. IR 12 (Oyster Bay) and IR 13 (Kulleet Bay & Shell Beach) water/sewer services agreement These detailed agreements will set out the terms for extending the Town’s water and sewer services to SFN lands at the same level of service provided within the Town. Holland Lake/Stocking Lake pipeline agreement These lakes provide drinking water for the Town, the Diamond Area, Saltair and will provide drinking water for SFN. This pipeline will connect these two sources of drinking water and it is proposed to be located on Crown land where SFN has an active woodlot. Watershed management Landowners, government and service providers are committed to protecting the watershed. The Town and SFN are stakeholders. Operational services The Town and SFN are both interested in sharing resources through service arrangements, such as building inspection services or water meter reading Boundary extension The proposed boundary extension is for privately owned forest land which is adjacent to the Town and traditional lands of interest to the SFN. Harbour clean-up This initiative involves many stakeholders with a long-term aim to restore the health of Ladysmith Harbour. DL 651 partnership This partnership focuses on marina development within the water lot adjacent to the Ladysmith Community Marina. Uniform bylaws for environmental protection Both communities care about the environment. Through this initiative, we will fulfill LWMP commitments. Funding opportunities We are committed to accessing external funding to support our work. Official Community Plan consultation Municipalities may develop Official Community Plans that set a long-term vision for their community, including policies related to planning and land use management. Ladysmith’s current OCP was adopted in 2003. Each amendment to the Town’s OCP is referred to the SFN for comment. South Ladysmith Stz’uminus First Nation (Crown) land development The South Ladysmith Industrial Park is located adjacent to the Peerless Road Recycling Centre. Over one-half of the area is Crown land. The Town and SFN are interested in working together to expand the current land available for new economic investment. Incremental treaty process The SFN and the Town may work together on Crown land development within the Town. Inclusion of First Nation’s heritage/culture The first inhabitants of what is now known as Ladysmith were the people of the SFN. The Town is committed to recognizing SFN history and places in the Hul’qumi’num language as a part of its interpretive signage program. Emergency Preparedness The Cowichan Valley Regional District provides emergency preparedness support for both the Town and SFN. Joint training is available for the staff of both organizations. Land Use Strategy Land development for community, social and economic development purposes is common to both the SFN and the Town. This initiative supports working together to build resilient communities.
