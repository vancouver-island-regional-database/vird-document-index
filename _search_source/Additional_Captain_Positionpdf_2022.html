STAFF REPORT TO COUNCIL Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: File No: Re: Protective Services Department October 7, 2025 2770-01 Additional Paid on Call Captain Position (Fire) RECOMMENDATION: That Council authorize the creation of an additional Paid-on-Call (POC) Captain position within Ladysmith Fire/Rescue. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: When the full time exempt Deputy Fire Chief position was created, it combined some of the duties of the POC Deputy Fire Chief and some duties of the POC Chief Training Officer, plus additional human resource functions. This leaves the fire department short one senior paid on call Duty Officer, which impacts safety and supervision during emergency operations. PREVIOUS COUNCIL DIRECTION: N/A INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND: Ladysmith Fire/Rescue (LFR) responds to a wide range of emergencies, including fires, motor vehicle incidents, rescues, medical aid and public service calls. The department relies on a structured officer team to provide leadership, training, and operational oversight. Currently, the officer complement includes the Fire Chief, Deputy Fire Chief, POC Captain, and four POC Lieutenants. There are four “duty crews” which rotate standby duty coverage, one week at a time. The senior duty officers (chief officers and captain) follow the rotation, and each duty crew is led by a junior officer (lieutenant). Creating a second POC Captain position gives the department the needed senior duty officer coverage and allows us to implement more robust operations and training accountabilities whereby each POC Captain is responsible for two POC Lieutenants, which are then each responsible for one duty crew. Adding one (1) additional Captain position would:   Strengthen the officer team, ensuring sufficient leadership is always available. Improve firefighter safety and supervision during emergency operations.  Support succession planning within the department by creating leadership opportunities. The additional position would be filled from within the existing membership through the department’s officer selection process, ensuring continuity and alignment with the department’s values and standards. ALTERNATIVES: Council can choose to: 1. Deny the request for an additional Captain position. 2. Refer the matter back to staff for further analysis. FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS: There is an expectation this position will be filled within the current membership, resulting in an annual $1,650 increase in pay (2025 rates). There will be a cascading effect that will be filled via regular recruitment for the department.
