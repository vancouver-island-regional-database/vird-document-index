SD68 consultation input: Relocation of French immersion from Nanaimo District Secondary School to John Barsby Secondary due to Woodlands Secondary closure Jennifer Mollins, CPF Nanaimo Ladysmith chapter president November 4, 2015 Vision for French immersion (FI): ● Well managed for ○ Learning excellence ○ Equality and fairness Well managed ● Minimize disruptions, maintain stability ● Commitment to growth ● FI adds education efficiency ● FI attracts resources ○ Out-of-district students (estimate $230K per year) ○ $170K annual federal grant (2015 level) ○ Special grants ● Revive French Advisory Committee Learning excellence ● Better academic results in all subjects, plus French ● Language learning as problem solving technique ● Skills for knowledge based economy and wicked problems ● Proven outcomes - socially, culturally, economically ● Distinct from other district programs (enrichment for all rather than crisis/gap management or merit-based arts/sport) Equality and fairness ● Accessibility ○ Unlimited enrollment ○ Maintain enrollment - support to minimize exits (exits cost $100K in federal grant per year) ○ Access regardless of income, make it easier to enroll ■ Big high school with transit hub ■ Add FI to more elementary schools (walkable) ■ Late French Immersion in an English school Recommendation on NDSS move ● Maintain FI at NDSS ○ FI has invested in location since 1988 ○ Growing with room to grown ○ Broad programming for engaged students ○ Avoid risk of costly attrition ($230K annually in fees) ○ Less disruptive options available (ie, from WLS to JBS, instead of from WLS to NDSS, and NDSS to JBS) ○ Best bus access for large district program Become a member of CPF to support efforts like these.
