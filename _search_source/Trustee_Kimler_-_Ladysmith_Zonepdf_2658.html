Trustee Zone Report 26-Apr-2017 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  Grade 6 Immunizations – Reminder that the 2nd of a 2-dose immunization series will be done at school in the next few months. Students with consent forms received their first dose in the fall & the second, to be administered by public health nurses, will include vaccinations for Hepatitis B, Chickenpox & HPV.  FSA results – Completed by participating grade 4 & 7 students, FSA exams were marked and sent home with students before the Spring Break. Parents are encouraged to look over the results with their child and invited to contact the school if they have any questions about the results or the tests. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  Elemental Run Fund-raiser – Ladysmith Primary is helping to host the second annual “Ladysmith Elemental 5k Run” which takes runners through the picturesque town of Ladysmith and through some of its many parks & trails. Race starts and ends at the Primary School at 10 AM on Sunday, May 7th. The event has been coordinated by the LPS PAC as a fundraiser with all proceeds going toward LPS.  WTK Program – The “Welcome to Kindergarten” program offers resources & strategies for families in support of children and early learning. LPS will be hosting a WTK orientation session on Wed May 10th from 10 until 11:30 am. Registered kindergarten students will participate in a variety of play-based activities & stations. They’ll also get a W2K bag filled with activities, ideas, and books to help prepare kids for their exciting year in Kindergarten. Ecole North Oyster  BCAA Play Here Contest – The ENO PAC Play Space committee entered ENO in this contest, sponsored by the BC Automobile Association. Judges will select the top 10 entries, which will be announced on May 22nd on Global TV. After the announcement, people will vote for their favourite entry. Three grand prize winners will receive up to $100,000 in revitalization services. Consolation prizes of small monetary amounts, may be awarded by BCAA. Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Nutsumaat Syaas – “Working together as One” - The traditional coast Salish house backdrop has been completed and First Nation’s carver John Marston now has a platform on which he will be carving a welcome figure that will – ultimately – grace the foyer for years to come. An opening ceremony and cedar-plank-weave backdrop was completed earlier this month and looks amazing. In addition to the engaging & interactive artistic work by the carver, First Nations elders will also be coming to the school to share their experiences, culture & stories with interested students in an open-ended, authentic and engaging way. In a sense, this project has only just begun. Trustee Kimler
