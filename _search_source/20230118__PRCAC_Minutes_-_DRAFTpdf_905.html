Minutes of the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee Wednesday, January 18, 2023 at 7:00pm Frank Jameson Community Centre COMMITTEE MEMBERS PRESENT: Lynda Baker, Chair Jacqueline Huard Colleen Butcher Bruce Mason Geoff Dean Terri Merritt-Worden Gordon Filewych Councillor Duck Paterson STAFF PRESENT: Chris Barfoot, Lead Kim Cheang, Minute Taker REGRETS: Kelly Daniels Lucy Partington The meeting was called to order at 7:00pm CALL TO ORDER AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT The Chair acknowledged with gratitude that the meeting was taking place on the traditional, unceded territory of the Stz’uminus First Nation. AGENDA 2023-01: That the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee approve the agenda for the meeting as presented. Motion Carried. MINUTES 2023-02: That the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee approve the minutes of the October 19, 2022 meeting as presented. Motion Carried NEW BUSINESS Ladysmith Community Gardens Society (LCGS) Delegation T. Reynen of the Ladysmith Community Gardens Society provided background information about the society and discussed how the garden community functions, some of the challenges it faces, some potential solutions, and issues to consider for the future. Ms. Reynen completed her presentation by highlighting the important, multi-generational role the LCGS plays within the community, as it teaches food awareness, how to grow food and how to preserve it. She noted that there is a growing interest within the community and that there are 17 names on the waiting list for garden plots. Page 1|2 The committee provided the following potential expansion options to the delegation:  Park on Belaire – Ms. Reynen confirmed that discussion has already taken place with Parks, but there is a covenant on the land that restricts its use.  Hospital grounds -the committee noted that this would be an ideal location since it is public property and the grounds are irrigated. Ms. Reynen agreed. D. Paterson offered to touch base with the Mayor.  Strip of alley going up to Arbutus  Gardens at Ladysmith schools are worth pursuing. Invitations for Sport Tourism Event Grant Sub-committee (STEGS) Representative 2023-03: That the Committee select G. Dean as a representative for the Sport Tourism Event Grant Sub-committee (STEGS). Motion Carried OLD BUSINESS Park Implementation Plan – Review Proposed Phases and Community Engagement The Committee provided feedback on the proposed phases based on the community engagement and feedback. PRC staff will provide a presentable package at the February meeting. Civic Recognition Program Council Resolution CS 2022-073 That Council refer the “Civic Recognition” program to the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee to develop a draft policy for Council’s consideration. The Committee provided feedback regarding the award categories, including a clear inclusion of youth. PRC staff will return with descriptions for each of the awards, application details, examples of how the selection committee could be structured, and recognition and ceremony options.
