During the 2011-2012 school year, Trustees codified and approved new Board policies that are currently available on the District website. Once the new Board policies were developed, it was necessary to convert the remaining former Board policies into Administrative Procedures. A number of the Administrative Procedures are also now available on the District website. Over the course of the 2012-2013 and 2013-2014 school years, a joint senior management/Trustee Board Policies and Administrative Procedures Steering Committee undertook the work of deciding which of the former Board Policies would be: a. deleted; b. converted to an Administrative Procedure without significant revision; c. converted to an Administrative Procedure with significant revision; or d. converted to a Board procedure. Trustees Welch, Allen and Neary are to be commended for their work on this extensive project. To date, and as is reflected in the attached list, the following have been undertaken: 1) All Board policies have been developed; 2) 105 former Board policies have been converted to Administrative Procedures 3) 38 former Board policies have been identified as requiring significant revision prior to conversion to Administrative Procedures; and 4) 12 former Board policies have been identified as requiring revision prior to conversion as Board procedures. With respect to next steps, District departments will undertake the revision of the remaining former policies that have yet to be converted to Administrative Procedures or Board Procedures. TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Learning Services RE: Adjustment to Local School Calendar: Local Calendar Consultation Background The Board approved the 2014-15 Local School Calendar in the spring of 2014. As outlined in School Calendar Regulation 314/12, the Board may approve amendments to the local school calendar. Proposal 1) Due to the strike, the Ministry of Education has given school districts some flexibility this year by moving the first semester exams into the first week of February (from the last week of January). This allows the school district to balance the number of instructional days in each semester. 2) By moving the September PD day from Monday, September 29 to Monday, February 24 it will create more instructional time when students first return to school in September, and create further balancing of the number of instructional days in both semesters. This change to the Local School Calendar requires a 30-day notice period as stipulated within School Calendar Regulation 314/12. Both the NDTA and CUPE executive have been made aware of the above recommendations and are supportive. At this time, the consultation period has lapsed and no significant concerns were raised regarding the recommendations. Recommendation That the Board of Education approves the Local School Calendar adjustments by moving the end of Semester 1 to Friday, February 6; and the Monday, September 29 PD day to Monday, February 24. Page 40 of 61 14.4 THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: November 26, 2014 TO: The Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent – Department of Learning Services SUBJECT: Board Motion R14/06/25-43 (Elementary Hockey Academy) BACKGROUND On June 25, 2014 Mr. Jamie Davis, President Nanaimo Minor Hockey Association (NMHA), made a presentation to the Board of Education, School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith). The Nanaimo Minor Hockey Association asked the district to consider offering a hockey academy for Grades 6 and 7 students. They indicated that the current secondary hockey academy at Woodlands has been a big success. Subsequently, at the June 25 Board meeting, the following Motion was passed: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) refers the matter of the Elementary Hockey Academy to staff with a report back to the Board in the fall of 2014. INFORMATION UPDATE In consultation with Mr. Jamie Davis, President, NMHA, it has been determined that, while NMHA is interested in developing a community hockey academy it has no interest in developing an academy to be “contained” within or associated with an existing elementary school. The NMHA plan is to develop a community hockey academy that will attract hockey players district-wide, who are in Grades six and seven. While the academy is directed at elite (rep) players, no player that is interested in the academy will be turned away. NHMA intends to offer these sessions on Mondays and Wednesdays from 2:15 to 3:15 pm, and Thursdays from 2 to 3pm at the Nanaimo Ice Centre (NIC) 2, beginning January 5, 2015. These dates and times were selected by NHMA to take advantage of the available ice time. NMHA has indicated that it has a list of 24 families (representing 10 Nanaimo-Ladysmith public schools) who will be interested in this opportunity. There is no further action required by the Board, at this time.
