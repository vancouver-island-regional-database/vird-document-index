CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Audited Financial Statements for 2010-11 Mr. Turin said that the past school year there were unbudgeted and increased benefit costs experienced during the year that resulted in an overall increase of about 1.4 percent or $1.3 million in benefit costs over the previous year. In January the district froze $1 million in spending to ensure the district would end the year with a surplus that could be used to reduce the impact of reductions in the 2011-12 year. Late in the budget year, the district unexpectedly received $500,000 in additional funding from the Ministry of Education. The financial statements show that the district ended 2010-11 with a $1.2 million surplus on a $125.6 million budget. Of that surplus, $575,000 was restricted as had been planned to balance the 2011-12 budget.
