TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Chair Charlene McKay Name of School(s) Visited: Gabriola Elementary:  Highlights: o Invited to participate as a guest judge alongside Trustee Barron for a Spaghetti Bridge Building contest. o Important to highlight that this cross-grade curricular project was driven by Dr. Hoban, school principal. His leadership and support provided an opportunity for students in all grades and abilities to test theories and work together. This may have been a STEM learning opportunity, but it also was a community building activity that brought together staff, students, and parents.  Related to school goals: STEM Activity: Learning as a school across all grades within a set criteria to create bridges made from spaghetti. Criteria included building across a set distance (gap); determine load capacity of bridge; include colour; build using materials as set out according to grade. o All students trialled various types of bridges and support styles. Team work was evident in all projects. Students were engaged not only in sharing their learning and success, but collectively showed their support for each other’s projects during the exciting judging process. Forest Park Elementary:  Toured school with vice principal, Sean Walsh. o Took a tour of the facility, and was welcomed into many classrooms to meet teachers and students. o Vice principal is new to role. Spoke openly about enjoying a positive school culture and having the opportunity to provide new ideas while learning under the mentorship of the principal. o VP shared the hiking program he has been working on with upwards of 90 students and classroom teachers, with an end goal of hiking Mt Benson with all students. o Discussions regarding intervention strategies for literacy, and the underlying belief that all students are capable of success.  Highlights: o Entrepreneur fair was underway in the gym for the upper grades. The creativity of students was readily apparent, and they were well supported by teachers who were overseeing the event. o School culture of support between colleagues, welcoming environment and classrooms. o Readily able to identify the relationships between VP and students during school tour. Students were very open and happy to see and engage with one of their school leaders; those momentary check-ins reinforced the positive culture and relationships between staff and students. John Barsby:  Quick visit and introduction with new principal, Jane Reynolds, and also was able to connect briefly with vice principal, Carol Girard. Administrators were working on time tabling for next year.  Stopped in counselling office and chatted with staff there briefly.  Highlights: o Toured school with vice principal, Jeff Hasenauer. Chair Charlene McKay – Trustee Report o Tour included a visit through the set for Chesapeake Shores, which was filming in the multipurpose room/cafeteria areas of the school.  Impressed with professionalism of all production staff and the relationships between filming staff/coordinators and Jeff.  Great example of a partnership that provides opportunities for students to participate and observe an example of the film industry within their school.  Food trucks were set up on school grounds to provide food for filming crews and students. All proceeds from the food truck sales for the two days of filming will be donated back to the cafeteria program. Events/Meetings:  District Parent Advisory Council: Met with President.  BCSTA Regional Training: Attended 2 days of professional development, with NanaimoLadysmith as the host district. Topics covered included fiscal responsibility and audit practices, and understanding local context of policy, budgeting and strategic planning. Discussions were held regarding school board governance, and committee structures. Regional session included trustees from various districts around Vancouver Island.  Tillicum Lelum and Kw’umut Lelum: Met with representatives from both organizations who provided input and ideas for NLPS long range facility plan.  Nanaimo Chamber of Commerce Business Awards: NLPS was a finalist in the Inclusive Employer category at the Nanaimo Chamber of Commerce Business awards this year. Inclusion has been a district wide focus for the past year, and this nomination is a testament to how our inclusive practices for our employees is an important part of district success.  Budget Meetings with Stakeholders and Public Open House: Attended both the stakeholder invite session and the open house budget session to hear what values our community partners and education stakeholders feel the budget priorities should be.  NLPS Strategic Planning: Two meetings of the board were held for strategic planning. The first to review and brainstorm values and mission, and the second to discuss goals and objectives. Review included Thought Exchange participant comments, and recognizing alignment between public values and board values.  Assistant Superintendent Hiring Process: Attended several meetings during the hiring process for a new assistant superintendent. The process of hiring was thorough, well thought out, and very professional.
