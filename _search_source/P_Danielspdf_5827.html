Kelsey Toye From: Sent: To: Subject: Peter Daniels Thursday, April 28, 2016 10:33 AM Kelsey Toye; Tania Brzovic; Natasha Bob; Scott Kimler;Steve Rae; BillRobinson; Noah Routley Budget Superintendent salary increase — Committee I was reviewing the Report from the Special Business April 25 , 2016. How can the superintendent justify a 19% raise for himself after closing schools and now cutting custodial staff under the guise of "becoming sustainable"?NoSchool District employee should be getting more than a union mandated raise or a cost of living raise. - I have heard that the justification for the raise is to bring the superintendent's salary more inline with other districts in the Province. I don't think this is a very strong argument for; 1 Presumably John Blain new what the salary was when he took the job if it was not high enough perhaps he should have pursued a position in a higher paying district. 2 The cost of living is vastly higher in the lower mainland districts and in Victoria. If you adjust the various superintendent's salary for cost of living I am pretty sure the 2015-2016 $191,802 salary of our superintendent compares very favourably. 3 I don't really see why it matters what other districts pay their superintendents. Our district is underfunded,the education of our kids is being compromised, parents, students and teaching and support staff in our district are contently being required to work with less so how can the Superintendent give himself more? I urge the trustees not to support this proposed salary change.
