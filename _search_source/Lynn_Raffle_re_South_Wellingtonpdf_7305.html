June 12, 2020 Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools, School District 68 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 To: Trustees for the Nanaimo Ladysmith Board of Education RE: South Wellington School My name is Lynn Raffle and I am a resident of the community of South Wellington. I received notification with respect to the South Wellington school from our community organization, South Wellington and Area Community Association requesting community support/input to acquire the school and property for a community amenity/space and recreational facility. By way of this letter I am showing my support for this concept. Previously, I had been advised through our community association that at a school board meeting the demolition of the school had been on the agenda and application to the Province for funding was imminent. The school board agreed to meet with community members; they were invited to attend a meeting on Thursday, April 30, 2020. The information was received on Tuesday, April 28, 2020 at 9:49pm. Not much time for the community to organize and present a case for keeping the building and property in the community. That meeting was cancelled due to an overabundance of community members requesting attendance. A virtual meeting subsequently took place on May 20, 2020; it was relayed to the attendees that the school district had received approval from the Ministry of Education to delay the submission of the building demolition report to the end of June. It is my understanding that the Regional District of Nanaimo has a report forthcoming for a decision on whether to move forward on creating a community amenity at the South Wellington School. Our community association has previously communicated with current and previous Area Directors about the school and property remaining a public property for community use. The community of South Wellington wants this very viable and important building to be retained for use as their community hub. The school and the property are the heart and soul of South Wellington. The community has made it known that we want the School District to ensure that the school be made available to the community and request that the School District work with South Wellington and Area Community Association, the Regional District of Nanaimo and the Cranberry Improvement District to achieve this goal. At the end of the day, I still find it appalling that the school district finds it necessary to apply for government funding to demolish a very useable building which is not in disrepair. The fact is further troubling in our current state of turmoil-- a worldwide pandemic! These decisions should be delayed until such time that people can meet safely to discuss such important business. I request that the Trustees for the Nanaimo Ladysmith Board of Education lend their support to retain the school building and property for current and future residents of South Wellington to enjoy; work with our community organization and the Regional District of Nanaimo to this end. Let this important amenity remain in our community! Thank you for your consideration, Lynn Raffle cc Susan Toth (Co-Chair, South Wellington & Area Community Association) cc Cranberry Improvement District
