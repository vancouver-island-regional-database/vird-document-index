NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS STRATEGIC DIRECTIONS COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: June 10, 2026 Strategic Direc�ons Commitee Margaret Olsen, Assistant Superintendent, Secondary Outdoor Education Background Outdoor Education programs are offered across all secondary schools and alternative programs and serve students in Grades 8–12. Programs are delivered through a range of models, including block-based courses, extended blocks, half-day structures, and integrated programs such as Take a Hike. Across sites, programming emphasizes experiential and place-based learning and is supported by educators with specialized expertise and strong community partnerships. Discussion Approximately 1,500 students participate in Outdoor Education across the district. Participation ranges from specialized cohorts of approximately 25 students to programs exceeding 280 students, reflecting both targeted and large-scale delivery across school contexts. Programs are grounded in experiential, land-based learning. Students regularly engage in activities such as hiking, canoeing, camping, climbing, fishing, and multi-day excursions, with many programs designed to support the progressive development of skills across grade levels. Schools consistently highlight the importance of connecting students to local environments. Access to nearby natural spaces enables frequent and meaningful outdoor learning, while the integration of Indigenous perspectives and landbased teaching supports a deeper understanding of place, culture, and community. Student voice reflects a high level of engagement and value placed on these experiences. Students consistently highlight the opportunity to learn beyond the classroom, build strong relationships with peers and teachers, and challenge themselves through new experiences. Schools report that Outdoor Education is highly popular and, in some cases, among the most requested electives. Students express an appreciation for time outdoors, including opportunities in some programs to disconnect from devices and engage in shared experiences that foster connection and belonging. Students frequently describe Outdoor Education as a meaningful and memorable part of their school experience and express interest in continued participation. Overall, Outdoor Education programs play a vital role in fostering student engagement, well-being, and connection to community and place. Continued development of these programs will help ensure equitable access to meaningful, experiential learning opportunities for students across the district.
