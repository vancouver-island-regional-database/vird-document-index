NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: February 14, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Ladysmith Intermediate and Ladysmith Primary – Seismic Update Recommended Motion: The Business Committee recommends that the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) direct staff to create a report with options to gather funds to support the higher cost option for the Ladysmith Intermediate Seismic Upgrade (Davis Road) and report back to the Board for approval. Background: As the Board is aware, the District received support in the last round of capital response letters to upgrade Ladysmith Intermediate School and expand Ladysmith Primary. This Action Sheet will provide an update on the status of the projects. Discussion: The Board engaged in a significant public consultation process that resulted in the community favouring an option of changing the configuration of the Ladysmith schools to have two K-7 schools (at Ladysmith Primary and a new site – likely Davis Road) with French Immersion returning to the new site. During the consultation the community was also made aware that such a project would involve a contribution from the District. The Minister of Education and Child Care’s approval letter of January 18, 2024, was presented to the Board during the regular Board meeting on January 31, 2024. The Board is now be faced with a major decision. The decision is whether the Board is able to contribute the required amount to fund the preferable option, which District staff anticipate to be as much as $9 million. This represents the difference between the cost of seismically upgrading LIS at the current site without any other upgrades compared to the new build at the Davis Road site. Staff are contemplating three issues: 1. Strategies to lower the cost differential including the potential for part of the new build to be prefabricated; 2. Strategies for the Board to consider to accumulate sufficient funds for the contribution (including the already discussed sale of the LIS lands upon completion of the new build; and 3. Supporting the Board in its advocacy work with the Ministry. Now that the approval letter has been received, staff require direction from the Board with respect to next steps. To create a report that outlines avenues for potential options to gather sufficient funds for the Davis Road option will require time and will also involve proposing certain parcels of land for potential disposition which also involves spending dollar on formal valuations.
