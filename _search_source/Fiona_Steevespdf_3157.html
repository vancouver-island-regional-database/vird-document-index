4975 Aho Rd Ladysmith, BC V9G 1G8 School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Avenue Nanaimo, British Columbia V9R 3K6 April 19, 2013 Dear School Board Staff and Trustees I want to commend Doug Player on his proposed plan for the facilities in the Nanaimo-Ladysmith school district. I appreciated that the plan was logical and well thought out and not emotionally based. While nobody wants a school to close, the proposed facilities plan brings forward opportunities for better programs, more enriched learning, more options for students and a better functioning school system. Putting aside how it affects me and my children personally, I can see how this plan is beneficial on many levels. I also appreciated the fairness of it, especially with regards to the Ladysmith and Cedar areas. I would like to encourage the school board to adopt Doug Player’s plan for the district facilities with the following considerations. The numbers of North Oyster students going to Ladysmith Intermediate (eventually Ladysmith Elementary) seems low and the number of North Oyster students going to the new Cedar Elementary is likely too high. North Oyster School is currently considered a feeder school to Ladysmith Secondary. Given a choice, most North Oyster families that I have talked with would choose to take their children into the Ladysmith elementary schools rather than out to the Cedar Elementary school. While our families likely have more in common with the other rural families of Cedar, the majority feel ties to Ladysmith. I also feel that if they are slated to go to Ladysmith Secondary, going through elementary school with the same group of kids is more beneficial than switching peer groups in the early teenage years. I do not know if there is room in Ladysmith Intermediate to have a large number of North Oyster students attend along with the English-speaking students of Davis Road but perhaps North Oyster students could be accommodated in both Ladysmith Primary and Ladysmith Intermediate. Consideration for this increase in North Oyster students would have to be accommodated in the plan for the new Ladysmith Elementary. I am hopeful that you will take my comments into consideration when preparing the final plan for the facilities in the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District. Sincerely, Fiona Steeves Parent of 1 North Oyster Student and 2 younger children
