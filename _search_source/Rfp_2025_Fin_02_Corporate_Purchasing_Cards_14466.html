Introduction The Town of Ladysmith (Town) is seeking proposals for Corporate Purchasing Cards from qualified financial services providers (Proponent). The overall objective of this RFP is to select a financial institution to assist the Town in establishing the most cost-effective and efficient long-term purchasing card (P-Card) program. The Town desires a P-Card program that will maximize value to the Town through enhanced technology and opportunities, superior customer service, streamlined services, greater financial benefits, and lower total operating costs. This P-Card program must contain a high level of internal controls ensuring compliance with all relevant federal and provincial regulations and align with the Town’s procurement policies and procedures. The Town currently maintains a purchase card (P-Card) program to enable select employees of the Town to purchase and pay for low dollar value goods and/or services. The Town is now seeking to re-procure the services of a Proponent to provide P-Card services, including services described in Schedule ‘A’ – Scope of Services and Schedule ‘A-1’ - Technical & Functional Requirements. The Town is looking for a Proponent whose solution meets or exceeds the Town’s requirements as described in Schedule ‘A’. 2. Required Project Deliverables The Town is inviting Proponents to submit Proposals that provide a solution to establish a purchase card program (the “P-Card” Program) designed for use by the Town to charge low dollar value goods and/or services related to the business activities of the Town. The Town desires a P-Card Program that will maximize value to the Town through enhanced technology, superior customer service, streamlined services, greater financial benefits and lower total operating costs. The Town will not consider a two-card program (purchase card and travel card programs). The Town currently has 46 P-Card users. Request for Proposals – Corporate Purchasing Cards RFP #2025-FIN-02 Page 12 Schedule ‘A’ – Scope of Service The objective of this Request for Proposal (RFP) is for a Purchasing Card program for the Town of Ladysmith (Town) that maintains a high level of service, wide vendor acceptance, and low administrative cost and delivers a reporting and internal approval mechanism that can be accessed via a web-based system. The Town is seeking a business solution that streamlines the purchasing of smaller dollar value items and services while providing a convenient, administratively efficient reporting, approval and financial recording mechanism. To support these objectives, the Town has identified a number of fundamental requirements that will be included in the contract. Proponents are advised that by submitting a Proposal they are agreeing that the Contract to be negotiated will incorporate substantially all of the fundamental requirements, as follows: Canadian Financial Institution The Provider will be a Canadian financial institution or a service provider to a Canadian financial institution. For the purpose of this requirement, “financial institution” means a bank listed in Schedule I, Schedule II or Schedule III of the Bank Act, or a credit union incorporated pursuant to the Credit Union Incorporation Act or a trust company incorporated pursuant to the Financial Institutions Act. Purchasing Card Program The Purchasing Card program provides Town staff with an effective purchasing and payment processing tool that increases the efficiency of the purchasing and payment functions of the Town. Key features of Town’s current Purchasing Card Program: a) corporate liability, corporate pay; b) contingency line of credit; c) per transaction limit of $500 – $1,000 (higher under approved circumstances); d) maximum monthly Card limit (varies by position and job function); e) no cash advances; f) not accepted at cash machines; g) ability to restrict purchases from specified Merchant Category Codes (MCC); h) payment in full processed on payment due date by automatic bank account debit; I) personal benefits and incentives to employees prohibited; and j) immediate cancellation of Cards in event of theft, loss or unauthorized use The Provider is required to offer Town a Purchasing Card program which, at a minimum, offers the same terms and conditions as the Incumbent Provider. Request for Proposals – Corporate Purchasing Cards RFP #2025-FIN-02 Page 13 Core Business Requirements The Provider will provide Purchasing Card Services for a term of three (3) years, with options to extend the Contract by mutual agreement for an additional period of five (5) years. a) The Provider will issue Purchasing Cards, provided they are widely recognized such as MasterCard or Visa. Preference will be given to the Provider whose Card is accepted at the majority of merchants from which the Town purchases. b) Purchasing Cards will be valid (expiry date) for use for a minimum of three years. c) The Provider will issue and deliver by courier: new cards, replacement cards upon expiry, and replacement cards in the event of loss or theft, at no charge to the Town or Cardholders within five (5) working days. d) The Provider will suspend and/or reactivate individual cards at any time and at no charge to the Town or cardholders on request of the Town Card Coordinator. e) Purchasing Cards are not to be structured as a "credit card" but as a "payment card" and will not be subject to the payment of interest applied either at the time of purchase or at the end of a billing month. f) The Purchasing Card program must have the ability of setting prescribed transaction and monthly limits in accordance with company policy. g) The Provider will provide a program structure for the Purchasing Card program that will enable a separate hierarchical account structure to be reported on. h) To meet the Town’s requirement to release a statement of financial information, the Provider will make available within 2 weeks of the fiscal year end all corporate paid transactional data for the calendar year in electronic format suitable for use by the Town. i) The Provider will provide a liability waiver to protect the Town from risks of unauthorized use of Purchasing Cards. j) The Purchasing Card program will incorporate the ability to block purchases of specific merchant category codes (MCC) identified by the Town. k) The Provider will not establish any program specifically related to Purchasing Cards issued to the Town that are designed to offer or afford any personal benefits (e.g. points or reward programs) to a Cardholder for their use of the Card.
