From: To: Subject: Date: Karen Matthews Karen Matthews FW: Safe return Wednesday, September 2, 2020 8:59:13 AM From: Carol Tuck <> Sent: August 21, 2020 5:35 PM To: Charlene Mckay Subject: Safe return Dear Mrs. Mckay, My name is Carol Tuck. I teach grade 4 at Frank Ney Elementary. I have tried to refrain from getting anxious about going back to school all summer as plans for a safe return seem to be changing constantly. As of today, I just watched a youtube from Scott Saywell. As soon as he stated that in elementary schools, classes will be the same, I felt extremely anxious and teary. I am almost 51 with health issues starting to creep up. I love my job and am happy to go back to school with all the safety protocols I have been maintaining all summer. The difference between primary and intermediate numbers in a normal year is one thing but with bigger bodies, it is increasingly more difficult to distance them.  I have not had less than 29 children in my classroom. To get 29 desks, chairs, and supplies in my room, it is always a challenge to try to move desks as it is within the small confines of a room. Let's not forget putting 29 bodies, (which some are adult size) into these chairs, their coats, back packs, lunch boxes etc. It is almost impossible for my students to not have contact just trying to get up to go use the washroom let alone any kind of physical distance. I felt safe working in June by maintaining physical distance within the 2 groups we had. I also want to say how much teaching/learning was accomplished by having these small groups. I love being a life long learner and am hoping that with any new information on the transmission of Covid 19 from children will be quickly put into action and change the restart plan to reflect this.
