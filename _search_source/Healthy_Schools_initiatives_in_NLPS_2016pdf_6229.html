HEALTHY SCHOOLS INITIATIVES IN NLPS November 2016 Jacquie Poulin BE ACTIVE EVERY DAY CHALLENGE • Was approached to partner with the Nanaimo Division of Family Practice to represent the school district on their ‘Healthy Communities’ working group • First ‘big idea’ that came up was the Be Active Every Day challenge • BAED is a province wide challenge put out by the Doctors of BC to all physicians and schools; we focused on Grade 5’s to keep it manageable this year OUR FIRST YEAR • Had 21 of our elementary schools take on this challenge, with a total of 30 classes (we included any class that was interested and had some Grade 5’s in it so split classes could join) • 11 physicians and Public Health were involved in the ‘kick off’ week in doing presentations at all the schools • Over 850 students (and lots of staff) took the challenge to increase their daily physical activity to one hour each day • The promotion focused on the 5 – 2 – 1 – 0 approach AFTER SCHOOL SPORT & ART INITIATIVE • We are in our fifth year of this program/initiative and it just keeps growing • The grant is now approximately $80 000 each year and has been consistently this amount for the past three years – this grant comes through the Ministry of Community, Sport, and Cultural Development • Continue to partner with PacificSport to implement the • programming • Programs are offered in 15 schools in our district; some get art, some get sport, some get both • The criteria for school selection is very clear – low socioeconomic schools, larger Aboriginal population, and larger immigrant family population • The staff are trained in High Five; Fundamental Movement Skills; Run, Jump, Throw, Wheel; First Aid; as well as receiving various in-service sessions on group management and building rapport • Each year we add a new ‘spin’ on things – last year it was adding Recess Guardians and this year it is adding in soccer with the Whitecaps • Artists are brought in to lead the art programs as well • Have also added field trips to the experience the last couple of years – have gone swimming, bowling, and to Mount Washington for both ski/snowboard trips as well as hiking and biathlon adventures • Additional funds have been secured through Canadian Tire Jumpstart to assist schools in receiving some additional sporting and art equipment which is used both by the after school programs as well as by the schools during the regular school day • We have also applied for additional funds through the Disability Enhancement grants to have additional staffing for students who need the support and through the Canucks Autism Network (CAN) to offer sport programs specifically for children on the autism spectrum SWIM TO SURVIVE – GR. 3 & GR. 7 • Partnership with the City of Nanaimo and the Lifesaving Society • Between grants from the Lifesaving Society and funds provided by the city and the school district, provide ‘lifesaving lessons’ for all Grade 3’s and all Grade 7’s in the school district • Fourth year for Grade 3’s and second year for Grade 7’s • Barrier free, absolutely no cost to students • We worked closely with the Lifesaving Society to adapt the last lesson of the Grade 7 set to be done outside at a lake to make it more realistic to the risks Receiving the cheque from Lifesaving Society! Practicing a lake rescue HEALTHY SCHOOLS INQUIRY GRANTS • As a branch of the Network of Innovation and Inquiry and DASH BC, schools are able to apply for an inquiry grant that focuses on a healthy school question • We traditionally have 5 – 7 schools submit inquiry questions each year on topics such as DPA, nutrition, ‘the outdoor classroom’, self-regulation through drumming, etc. • The grant applications have not yet opened this year but do anticipate supporting schools once again with this process REVISED ACTION SCHOOLS • The whole Action Schools BC initiative that came out back around 2007 has undergone a major review last year and is just coming back out this year – will be working with the team at DASH BC to determine how best to bring it back to Nanaimo-Ladysmith
