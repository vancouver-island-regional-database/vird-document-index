SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 1425P MINISTRY OF CHILDREN & FAMILY DEVELOPMENT A. Joint Management Committee The Board of School Trustees and the Ministry of Children and Family Development will create a Joint Management Committee to facilitate the management of programs and services undertaken by the two organizations in the interests of the students of the School District (including Community Link Funds/Programs). The Joint Management Committee will be comprised of the following participants: School District Ministry of Children and Family Development Superintendent/CEO Assistant Superintendent Community Services Manager Contracts and Resource Manager The primary purpose of the Joint Management Committee will be to facilitate the management of programs and services undertaken by the two organizations (including Community Link Funds/Programs). Additionally, the Joint Management Committee will be responsible for: * * * * distribution and allocation of Community Link funding. collaboration around the use of School District funding to support programs and services related to social equity, poverty, and student support. performing a planning function, including short and long term planning. dealing with related issues as they arise. The Joint Management Committee will meet on an "as needed basis" as issues arise but no less than twice per year. The Joint Management Committee will ensure that the Joint Advisory Process is implemented at least once per year to bring all the stakeholders together. -1- SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) PROCEDURE NO. 1425P B. Joint Advisory Committee The Ministry for Children and FamilY Development and the School District agree to the creation of a Joint Advisory Committee which will serve to provide advice, a mechanism for consultation, and to facilitate informed decision-making by the Joint Management Committee. The committee shall be represented by representatives from the Boys & Girls Club, Ministry of Children and Family Development (MCFD), Nanaimo Family Life, Nanaimo Child Development Centre, Nanaimo Youth Services, Tillicum Haus, and Vancouver Island Health Authority (VIHA). Additionally, as issues arise, the Core Committee will ensure appropriate representation from the field. The Joint Advisory Committee will be jointly chaired and administered by a representative from the School District and the Ministry of Children and Family Development (to be determined by the committee). A representative from the School District and the Ministry of Children and Family Development’s Joint Management Committee may attend the Joint Advisory Committee Meetings as ex-officio participants. The Joint Advisory Committee will meet at least once a year C. Protocol Liaison In order to enhance the implementation of this protocol, the Joint Management Committee shall report to School District 68's Education Committee on an annual basis.
