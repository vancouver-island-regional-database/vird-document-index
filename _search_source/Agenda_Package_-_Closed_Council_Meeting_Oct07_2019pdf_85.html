Improve overall service delivery through greater integration of Town departments 10 Page 19 of 32 PROJECT HIGHLIGHT: Increase Water Storage Capacity In the face of climate change and a growing population, Ladysmith is implementing a 50 year water supply strategy. The first step is to increase our ability to store water in the Holland Lake Reservoir for use during drier summers. The project involves raising the two existing dams and dredging portions of the lake bottom. The project will nearly double the Town’s water storage capacity, ensuring that our growing municipality and neighbouring partner communities of Stz’uminus First Nation and the Diamond Improvement District - have a sufficient water supply for decades to come, even when the potential impacts of climate change are taken into consideration. The actual work involved includes raising the existing earth fill embankments to allow for a water level increase in Holland Lake of 1.5 metres, dredging material from the shallow portion of the lake, construction of a new water intake through the East dam, and upgrades to the existing outlet pipe and spillway located on the West dam. The Town’s long-term water supply strategy, developed by Tetra Tech EBA, is based on extensive water modelling to ensure a sustainable supply of clean, safe drinking water for the next 50 years. According to the results of the modelling, the most practical long-term water supply strategy for the Town involves the continued use of existing surface water sources in the Holland Creek and Stocking Creek watersheds, including increased use of Holland Lake as the community continues to grow and as climate change impacts result in changing weather patterns. Increasing storage is the only way the Town will be able to capture the fall/winter precipitation. The other two major water supply system upgrades are the new Water Filtration and a future Holland to Stocking Creek diversion pipeline. 11 Page 20 of 32 COMMUNITY We will employ strategies and actions which maintain a diverse, vibrant and liveable community, ensuring we do not pass undue burden onto future generations. 1. 2. 3. 4. We must act decisively not only to mitigate its impact, but most importantly to create a community that thrives in the face of a changing climate. In fact, our Official Community Plan update is a an opportunity to bring in guidelines that address a great number of climate change issues, including renewable energy options for buildings, charging stations for electric vehicles, and protecting biodiversity. One of the most important steps we can take towards addressing climate change is to significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Another Strategic Plan project to address climate change is enacting in a new Ladysmith Building Bylaw to reflect the new BC Building Code, and to provide encouragement for building (or retrofitting) energy efficient buildings. One project in this Strategic Plan aimed at shrinking our carbon footprint is updating our Official Community Plan to include emission reduction targets and plans on how to meet them. This can cover anything from neighbourhood design to building and linking more bike paths and walking trails –all aimed at promoting recreation and active forms of transportation. Also key for our community in facing climate change is to manage our water supply. The water supply projects in this plan are based on a 50-year strategy and reflect models for expected population growth, temperature increases and changing precipitation patterns. We plan to increase our capacity to store the heavier rainfall in winters to provide adequate water during the drier summer months. We will also be exploring ways to better manage, or capture, storm water runoff in a new Storm Water Strategy. Residents, too, can contribute to water management and conservation, and we will be looking at options to provide incentives for such things as installing water cisterns and permeable landscaping. Climate change is a challenge for all communities, and Council cannot address the issue in isolation. We plan to work closely with our neighbours the Stz’uminus First Nation, with other local governments, with community partners and with our citizens to tackle this common issue before it’s too late for our children and their children. 13 Page 22 of 32 WATERFRONT We will implement our plans for development of the waterfront together with Stz’uminus First Nation, creating opportunities for economic prosperity, environmental restoration and cultural celebration. 1. Centred on the historic Machine Shop, the Arts and Heritage Hub is a key part of Ladysmith's Waterfront Area Plan and is identified as a priority project within the Ladysmith Economic Development Strategy. The Arts and Heritage Hub will build on the cultural and heritage activities currently taking place in and around the Machine Shop and create even more of a gathering place. Imagine walking along a raised boardwalk from the restored Machine Shop building while watching artists showcasing their craft in new studio spaces; Enjoy sipping a beverage from a nearby café as you marvel at the size of a beautifully restored steam locomotive. All of these ideas are wrapped up in the draft concept design plan for the Hub that resulted from several workshop sessions with key community stakeholders. The vision includes new potential commercial uses for the Machine Shop, and the surrounding industrial heritage buildings remain to compliment and further enhance the character and creativity of current activities and structures. The proposed first phase of the Arts and Heritage Hub concept plan is estimated at $4.3 million and the overall vision will be carried out in multiple phases in future years as prioritized by Council. Part of Phase I – the Machine Shop restoration – is already underway and expected to be completed in early 2020. 15 Page 24 of 32 ECONOMY We will promote a progressive environment that facilitates business development and job creation. 1. Rob Johnson By Hand Dear Councillor Johnson: RE: NOTICE OF HEARING – CONSIDERATION OF SANCTIONS Please be advised that Council is concerned that you may have acted in a manner that is unbecoming a member of Town Council by disclosing confidential information contrary to the requirements of s. 117 of the Community Charter. Specifically, it is alleged that you may have disclosed confidential information regarding Council’s intention with respect to a new library in Ladysmith to the Executive Director of the Vancouver Island Regional Library. I am placing this matter on the agenda of a Special Council meeting in closed session. The reason for the meeting is to follow fair process in considering the matter, together with a resolution in relation to your conduct. Procedural fairness calls for providing you with two weeks’ notice of this special meeting. I am therefore proposing a meeting date of September 30, 2019 at 5:00 p.m. Should you wish to have this hearing held at an earlier date, even as early as during the week of September 16 to 20, please advise the Corporate Officer in writing. You are welcome to request a specific date that we would try and accommodate. Enclosed is a copy of the draft Resolution that will be considered at the meeting for discussion, debate and a vote. Note that one of the possible ultimate outcomes of the process described in the Resolution is that Council may be considering imposing sanctions flowing from your conduct, including rescinding your appointment to the Board of the Vancouver Island Regional Library, censuring you and releasing a media release. Council may also subsequently consider progressive sanctioning, including in relation to committee appointments. I wish to expressly notify you that you may retain legal counsel to represent your interests in this matter. Prior to Council voting on the resolution, you will be provided with the opportunity to address Council regarding the contents of the draft form of resolution and draft media release. Following any submissions that you (or your legal counsel) make at the meeting, Council will consider this resolution and media release. Enclosed you will find as supporting material a transcript of a telephone message left for the Chief Administrative Officer by the Executive Director of the Vancouver Island Regional Library.
