Dear Trustees: On March 13, 2014 in response to concerns raised by Trustee Saunders regarding the condition of the play fields at Cedar Secondary School, you asked your staff if they had costed out how much it would be to fix the fields. The response was “No”. In a recent Freedom of Information (FOI) request obtained by Save Cedar Schools it was discovered that the staff had indeed investigated the situation with the fields and received two reports. 1. On March 5, 2013, staff received a report via Newcastle Engineering regarding the cost of a site assessment for drainage for the Cedar Secondary Play Fields stating the assessment would cost $16,985.00 plus HST (note the HST, this dates this report). This indicates that staff knew about the conditions of the Cedar Secondary Play Fields before the plan was even put into public consultation and ignored the issue until parents at the School Construction Committee began to express concern about the fields. Attached as addendum A. 2. On January 13, 2014 Mr. Brent Spohn authorized New Castle Engineering to proceed with the above site assessment of the Cedar Secondary Play fields. Attached as addendum B. 3. On February 21, 2014 Mr. Brent Spohn received a preliminary cost estimate for the Play Field Remediation at Cedar Secondary School which he forwarded to Bassem Tawfik and Gregg Brown at KMBR architects and cc’d Mr. Pete Sabo on. The quote for the play field remediation for all play fields is $754,845.00. Attached as addendum C. Other information from the FOI that you may find interesting: 1. On February 26, 2014 Mr. Brent Spohn received an email from Jan Michael Williams at Unitech Construction Management with an “updated Class C for Discussion” quote for the original plan developed in collaboration at the School Construction Committee. This detailed quote is for $2,659,558.00. This is an “updated quote” which means there must be a preceding quote, however it did not show up in the FOI request. This quote does not include the cost of the fields, does not include jobs being done by district staff members and does not include construction management fees. If one includes these costs into the total cost outlined above the total for the original plan collaboratively developed by the School Construction Committee is much closer to the $5million quoted by community members since this plan was first outlined. Attached as addendum D. 2. On March 2, 2014 Mr. Pete Sabo sent out an email to Cathal Walsh, Bob Esliger, Bassem Tawfik, Gregg Brown, Brian Parker (of Unitech), ccing John Blain, J. Williams of Unitech, L Rowley of Herold Engineering, Les Brown of RB Engineering, Stephen McNicholls of RB Engineering and Scott Lewis of Newcastle Engineering. In this email Mr. Sabo requests input from the recipients of the email on an “alternative” plan for the renovations of Cedar Secondary due to the board motions on February 6th and the costing received from Unitech on February 27. Particularly absent from the list of recipients are any members of the School Construction Committee. Also of not in this email is the request that the School Construction Committee NOT be informed of the latest discussions. Of further note in this email is why particular items were removed from the renovation, due to cost, not educational viability. Attached as addendum E. 3. Once feedback was received and despite some of the feedback from those on the list, a new estimate from Unitech was received by Brent Spohn on March 7 and forwarded to Pete Sabo on that same day. Attached as addendum F. 4. This is of interest for two reasons. a. On March 13, 2014 at the last School Construction Committee meeting, the SCC was not informed of the cost estimate despite parents on the committee asking the staff if they costed the collaborative plan, they were told “No”. b. There was time between when staff received the first quote from Unitech and when the business committee met in order for the School Construction Committee to be assembled to provide feedback on the quote and plan. This exemplifies the complaints outlined by the parent volunteers from the School Construction Committee in our presentation on April 23. c. 5. On January 10, 2014 Mr. Pete Sabo sent an email to Brian Hackwood requesting a quote for a video surveillance system that would cover the entire site, the skate park and the interior of the school in order to address concerns brought up by teachers, administrators and community members regarding the ability to properly supervise the site for elementary aged students. Attached as addendum G. 6. On February 28, 2014 Mr. Brent Spohn sent an email to Mr. Pete Sabo asking for his input on a report he was working on for the March 3 ACE meeting. On the final page of this report (highlighted in green) is the acknowledgement that Cedar Elementary opening capacity is below base plan and that “attached is a future growth plan for increasing enrolment”. However, the plan is not attached. What is in this plan? We also think families in all elementary schools surrounding the proposed Cedar Elementary are entitled to know what this plan is. Attached as addendum H. There is an elementary facility located 2.8km away from the proposed Cedar Elementary that sits in the top third of facilities rankings in the district. It has playfields that are in perfect shape, does not require video surveillance in order to keep the students safely supervised and currently sits on roads that allows students to continue walking and riding their bikes to school and are not unsafe and hazardous for students. This school can be retrofitted to fit the required number of students with less than $500,000. You have decided to keep North Oyster Elementary School open. It will have more students in it than the current proposed Cedar Elementary. It requires upgrading (increase in playground capacity, classrooms that have been vacant for years) as a result. Yet, no AFG money has been allotted to this school. When you looked at this plan as a whole and took into account the cost savings that this plan was supposed to bring to the district, were all these extra costs factored into your decision? As Trustee Allen likes to say, you have played with the cars on the train. Your insistence at keeping this particular car on the train is not an educationally sound decision for this district or the students who will be attending this school. Cedar Elementary is a $3million gamble that you are all hoping will work out be. The facts tell us all that Cedar Elementary is not an “effective utilization of board educational and economic resources” Sincerely, Save Cedar Schools, on behalf of the community of Cedar.
