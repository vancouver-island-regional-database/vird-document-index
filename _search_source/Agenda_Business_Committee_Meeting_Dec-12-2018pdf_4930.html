ISE Tuition revenue is recognized as the educational services are provided to students during the school year, on a monthly basis, which the YTD of 29.74% reflects. Other Revenue consists of multiple sources including the Local Education Agreement, The Conseil Scolaire Francophone contract, cafeteria revenue, a BC Hydro Grant, P-Card rebates, etc. Revenues are received in varying percentages of contract and times throughout the fiscal year. Since the Annual Budget, we have also received additional Revenues that will be included in our actuals but not reflected as Budget until the Annual Amended Budget is presented. Rentals and Leases, at 54.87% of Budget, appears high so will be audited to ensure all current licenses are incorporated within Budget and adjusted accordingly in the Annual Amended Budget. Investment Income, at 58.02% of Budget, is higher than expected due to increased interest rates on investments. The budget will be evaluated and an adjustment may be required. Expenses - Salaries When looking at the various categories of salaries, it is important to recognize that different employee groups work at different times during the year and this is reflected in the percentage of the budget spent to date, at any time during the fiscal year. In the case of Educational Assistants, who are primarily 10-month employees, the expectation is that approximately 30.0% of the annual budget would have been spent by the end of November. School District Teachers are primarily 10-month employees, so it is presumed that approximately 30.0% of the annual budget would have been spent by the end of November. However, we do have a small group of 12 month Teachers that will inflate that percentage slightly. In the case of Principals, Vice-Principals and Other Professionals, who are primarily 12-month employees, the assumption is that approximately 42% of the budget would have been spent by the end of November. The Support Staff employee group is a mixture of 10 and 12-month employees so it is anticipated that the amount of budget expended by the end of November should be between 30% and 42%. Given the mix of 12-month and 10-month employees, it is difficult to project what percentage of the substitute’s salary budget would have been spent at any point in time. Salaries – Educational Assistants The year to date expense for Education Assistants is 31.77% of budget which is greater than the 29.29% cost in November of 2017. This higher than expected expenditure represents the increased number of Special Needs students and the additional staffing allocated to support them. Salaries – Support Staff With the expected percentage of between 30% and 42% of the budget having been spent by the end of November it is reasonable for the Support Staff salaries to be at 38.09% of this year’s budget, as well, it is comparable to the 37.15% of budget consumed at the end of November in 2017. Salaries – Teachers As approximately 30% of the Teachers Salary budget should be spent by the end of November, the year to date expense at 30.40% of the budget is reasonable compared to the 28.83% spent by the end of November in 2017. The 2017 percentage was lower due to the restoration of the class size and composition language and the related shortage of teachers throughout the Province, which caused us to have unfilled positions well into the 2017-18 school year. Salaries – Substitutes The cost for Substitutes are at 17.90% of budget to the end of November, which is less than the cost at the same period in 2017/18 at 21.20%. This decrease demonstrates our challenges around filling absences throughout the District. Benefits The percentage of 27.45% expended this year on benefits to the end of November is less than the 32.83% consumed by November 2017. The reduction is due to the decreased cost seen in 2017-18 and the changes made to the budgeted benefit percentage for each employee group to align accordingly. We will be analyzing benefits prior to the Annual Amended Budget to ensure no further adjustments, per current and forthcoming costs, are needed for 2018-19. Supplies and Services The year to date expenditures for supplies and services to the end of November is at 35.98% of Budget compared to 34.28% at November 2017.
