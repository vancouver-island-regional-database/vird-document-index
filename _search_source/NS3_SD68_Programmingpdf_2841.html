The Nanaimo Science and Sustainability Society Creative Learning Dr. Erin McClelland June 22, 2016 presentation for the school board Nanaimo Science & Sustainability Society The mission of the Nanaimo Science and Sustainability Society (NS3) is to inspire children and families to develop an interest in science and sustainability through hands-on learning. • activities target all STEM disciplines • placed-based education to connect people with local environment and promote environmental stewardship • strong focus on building regional partnerships Nanaimo Science & Sustainability Society NS3 programs: 3 programs currently offered district-wide • Citizen Science (gr. 5) • Trash to Treasure (gr. 6) • Tinkering Around (gr. 2) NS3 goals: • Foster a greater sense of place, community and stewardship among all participants • Support teachers and learners by • providing unique, inquiry-based activities that tie-in to the new curriculum • developing supporting materials for use in conjunction with NS3 activities • Create connections between SD68, VIU and other science organizations Grade 6 Citizen Science: Intertidal Monitoring Project Citizen Science: • Public participation in scientific research • Learn science by BEING a scientist! • Hands-on, inquiry-based learning • Increases environmental stewardship by engaging in authentic scientific research Grade 6 Citizen Science: Intertidal Monitoring Project Species Monitoring • Students participate in long-term monitoring of bivalves in Departure Bay • Research goal: to understand impact of an invasive species – the Varnish Clam • Collaboration with VIU Grade 6 Citizen Science: Intertidal Monitoring Project T C PS Curriculum and competency connections: • make observations and predictions • experience and interpret the local environment • observe, measure and record data using appropriate tools • identify patterns and connections in data • identify social, ethical, and environmental implications of their findings Enrichment Activities: • Pre-research day sampling game • learn about sampling protocol • Post-research video pitch • Use local species to extend lessons on sampling and scientific method; focus on solutions to local issues Grade 6 Citizen Science: Intertidal Monitoring Project Grade 5 Trash to Treasure: Art and Conservation  participate in the clean-up of a local beach or park  learn about local ecology, conservation, and the effects of pollution  work with other grade 6 students from their school to create public art from trash Grade 5 Trash to Treasure: Art and Conservation T C PS
