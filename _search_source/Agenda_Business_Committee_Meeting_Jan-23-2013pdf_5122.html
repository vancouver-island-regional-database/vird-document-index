NEW BUSINESS Financial Forecast Update Mr. Turin provided the Business Committee with an overview of projections for the funding and expenditure levels for the district over the next four years. He said the district currently receives a funding protection grant from the Ministry of Education. This grant is designed to lessen the impact of declining enrolment on school districts, but the Ministry is reducing the funding protection levels each year. The four-year projection shows the impact of the decline in funding protection. If the district had no change in enrolment over the next four years, the Minutes - December 2012 Page 4 of 68 6.1 ... Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held December 11, 2012 3 Ministry revenue would drop by about $2 million in 2013-14; by $1.7 million in 2014-15; by $1.6 million in 2015-16 and $1 million in 2016-17. Mr. Turin also provided the committee with a budget forecast for the next four years. Using the decline in projected revenue, and known increases in costs, it is anticipated that the district will experience budget shortfalls of $4.9 million in 2013-14; $7 million in 2014-15; $8.7 million in 2015-16 and $9.7 million in 2016-17 (accumulated totals). Report from Anti-Vandalism Committee - Trustee Welch Trustee Welch updated the Business Committee on the Anti-Vandalism Committee. She said the committee would like to conduct an awareness campaign that allows students from each secondary school to submit proposals to the AntiVandalism committee in February. These initiatives would begin in April. This initiative is similar to what the committee did in 2010. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY FOR INFORMATION Cooperative Gains Mandate Ministry of Education Mr. Turin advised he had participated in a conference call earlier today with Ministry officials. The Ministry is requesting school districts find savings in the current budget and next year's budget to assist in compensation increases. They are asking districts to find 1.5 percent this year and again next year, in order to free up funding from within existing budgets. For School District 68, this totals about $412,000 for 2012/13 and an additional $824,000 for 2013/14. Districts have been told that the savings must not negatively impact the delivery of educational programming. The Ministry has suggested operational cost reductions, increased efficiencies, service redesign and increases in revenues as ways districts can make savings.
