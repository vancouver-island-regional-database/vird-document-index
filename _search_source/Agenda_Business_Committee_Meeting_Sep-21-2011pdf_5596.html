CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY QUESTION PERIOD Kristin Houvenaeghel I have been a school secretary in this district for almost 20 years and I have worked as a head secretary in a small school (Dufferin) for 5.5 hours a day. Although the job was part time, there is still an expectation that the whole job can be done. All the purchasing, student records, attendance, correspondence, newsletters, attending to sick kids, hungry kids, phone calls, accounting, Master Card, incident report, courier, early warning calls, material distribution, etc. is practically the same for a small school as a large one. It is already a very difficult task getting it all accomplished in 5.5 hours a day. How does a secretary prioritize what jobs can be dropped when all are essential? Chair Murchie said that the secretary should discuss this with her principal. If the office is to be unmanned at South Wellington for 1.5 hours a day and the viceprincipal is at another school site, the only solution I can see is that the principal will have to hire a teacher in charge for Chase River while he leaves that school to attend South Wellington. I am sure the rate of pay for a teacher is more Minutes - June 1, 2011 Page 3 of 20 Agenda Item Number 3.1 3 Draft Minutes of the Special Business Committee Meeting held June 1, 2011 than that of a secretary, so how does that make any financial sense? Chair Murchie said that he could not comment on this scenario, because he doesn’t actually know how the school works, this is just one scenario, and there are probably many others. And will the principal be doing clerical work while he is there, as this goes against our CUPE contract? If not, how will the secretary ever catch up. Chair Murchie said that when we made this decision, there was a lot of discussion and the CUPE Rep pointed out that the budget cut did not match the FTE, it was less. He said that staff advised us that there is extra secretarial time for those sites that needed it. How much does the district receive for the “small school supplement” Isn’t this $$ to help offset the higher cost per student in a small school? Krista Seggie Mr. Turin said that we receive approx. $408,000 throughout the district; it is not a spending authority, it is an allocation, and that it does offset the higher costs per student at the school. Krista Seggie How was it decided that the cuts to clerical would be large cuts at small schools who have already been cut in administration (no on-site principals) What was decided? (the details) Chair Murchie said that the clerical cuts were proportional to both elementary and secondary schools.
