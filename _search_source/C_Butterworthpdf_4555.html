Learn at Home Funding and Development November 2015 Full Day Dance / Academic Program In the next ten minutes… 1.Why we care about Learn at Home. 2.What needs to be funded? 3.What are the consequences of underfunding? 4.What is one thing you feel SD68 should know? Why we care about Learn@Home In 2011/2012 we started a full day dance / academic program for grades 8-12. We used the SD68 L@H Grade 8-12 program. In 2012/2013 and 2013/2014 it got bigger. In 2014/2015 we expanded to include Grades 4-7. We used the SD68 L@H K-7 program. Students do alternating blocks of dance and academics. One of our studios converts into a LearnLab style classroom during the day. What Needs to be Funded? • Administrative and instructional space for teachers • Teacher release time for online curriculum development • Up to date technology for course management and communication • Adequate teacher / student ratio for online support • Travel time and expenses to visit learners Why? • Quality. Online education is not correspondence with a eBook. • Flexible learning is no longer the exception, it is becoming the norm. It should be the curriculum backbone of SD68. What are the consequences of underfunding? This year we recommended to our clients that they no longer use the SD68 Grade 8-12 L@H program. The quality of service delivery in 2014/2015 fell well below our minimum standards.
