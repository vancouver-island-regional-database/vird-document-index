NDSS ISLANDERS LACROSSE ACADEMY History ● The Lacrosse Academy was founded at NDSS in 2015 by Joel Smith ● We are in our 8th season with Joel as our head coach for both the junior and senior teams ● The academy began as an avenue for Vancouver Island players to achieve US Scholarships for field lacrosse NDSS Lacrosse Academy ● Only competitive academy at NDSS ● We travel and play other academy teams in BC and in the USA ● This current season we have gone to Portland, Victoria, Seattle, Palm Springs with upcoming trips to Mercer Island, and Sisters, Oregon ● Travel showcases our players to recruiters and University coaches US Athletic Scholarships ● To date, 30 NDSS players have received Division 1 & 2 scholarships ● Current NDSS lacrosse players are being actively recruited Fundraising & Community Outreach • Parent led fundraising • Business Community support for the academy • School Spirit • Alumni Games • Senior Nights • Volunteering Reasons WHY the Lacrosse Academy should remain at NDSS • Costly • All gear, uniforms, helmets, bags, backpacks, gloves and clothing would have to be rebranded Branding • NDSS Islanders Lacrosse • Coaches and recruiters for US Universities know who we are after years of playing as the NDSS Islanders • Alumni- this is a connection for our current players and recruiters The type of Lacrosse we play • We play FIELD lacrosse • We do not play in a box as was mentioned in the proposal • We do play on an artificial all weather turf field Necessities of the Academy • Sheds • Are required near the field for gear bags, balls and nets • Must be locked and only accessed by the academy • NDSS Track • Need the track and stairs for training and conditioning • Field Lacrosse is a running game • City Bus • Many athletes take the city bus from the North End, they would require 2 buses or extra walking to attend Barsby Lacrosse Academy Students and Families Position • Academy students should not suffer the consequences of inadequate future planning • We are opposed to Scenario 1 and 2; or any option that involves displacement of Academy, Gabriola residents or any students from NDSS Why Scenario 2 Isn't the Answer: The Costs Outweigh Benefits • Educational impacts: • Scholarships placed in jeopardy • Loss of educational options • Loss of extracurricular offerings: • Students would no longer have access to the same programs and activities that NDSS offers • Detrimental to student mental health & wellbeing: • NDSS is a family to academy students • Trying to fit into a new high school as an adolescent is difficult, the ramifications on mental health as a result of this move could be harmful Things to Consider • 44 Athletes in the academy • 16 of the 44 are already in catchment for NDSS • Disruption and the cost would free up a small number of spaces at NDSS • Unfortunately, without prior consultation, it is unknown if all athletes would relocate to Barsby if the lacrosse academy was moved Our Recommendations • Move forward with consideration of Scenarios 4 and 5 that will achieve the same temporary outcome without displacement of students • All future options proposed should be in line with the SD68 Vision, Mission, Values and Goals • Pursue more consultation with the students and families who are affected by the Board / District plans and recommendations Thank You from the NDSS Lacrosse players and parents
