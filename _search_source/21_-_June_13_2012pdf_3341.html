CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public School 2012-2013 Learning Improvement Plan Mr. Hutchinson said the Learning Improvement Fund was established by the provincial government to enable Boards of Education to address learning improvement issues and target complex classes that present challenging working conditions. School District 68 will receive about $1.5 million from the LIF. Of this, $190,976 is allocated to support staffing; $434,200 is required to be held in reserve; and $862,488 is for professional staff. Mr. Hutchinson said that a key feature of the plan is the appointment of instructional coordinators who will be school-based and will be deployed flexibly, in keeping with the RTI model. The coordinators will be members of inter-disciplinary teams that will include speech pathologists, counselors and psychologists. Each of the district’s four assistant superintendents will have a team that can be deployed in schools as 2 Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held June 13, 2012 necessary when challenging classroom situations arise. The district plans to appoint ten instructional coordinators for the 2012-13 school year.
