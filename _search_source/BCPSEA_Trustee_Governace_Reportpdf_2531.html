BCPSEA Trustee Governance Report 20-Dec-2017  BCPSEA Trustees ousted – On July 31st, 2013, the provincial government removed trustees from their governance role at the British Columbia Public Schools Employer’s Assocation (BCPSEA) organization and appointed a public administrator under Section 9.1 of the Public Sector Employer’s Act. It was stated that it was a temporary measure and done in advance of the K-12 bargaining with the BCTF in September. http://bit.ly/2BFC28a (BC Gov News announcement July 31, 2013) & http://bit.ly/2BHUGwg (BC Local News “BCPSEA trustees ousted” Aug 14, 2013)  At each BCPSEA AGM since, trustees have made a motion to write a letter to re-instate elected school trustees to the Board of Directors of the BCPSEA, which went unheeded for many years. http://bit.ly/2BIVssC (BCSTA letter to Minister of Finance, April 26, 2016)  The Public Sector Employee’s Council (PSEC) advised BCPSEA on Sep 5, 2017 that the Minister of Finance is commencing a process to wind down the responsibilities of the Public Administrator and “… transition to a structure that continues to deliver good governance consistent with BCPSEA’s statutory mandate while restoring a more direct role for elected school trustees.” The process includes consultation with 4 of the major public sector employers’ associations (with the BCSTA facilitating the consultation with trustees). This consultation, review & potential changes would be completed by PSEC prior to the next BCPSEA AGM, scheduled for late January 2018. http://bit.ly/2BIlyw6 - BCPSEA NewsLink Sep 5, 2017  The PSEC consultation process regarding BCPSEA governance started in October & concluded in early November. After compiling member input, the BCSTA made 7 recommendation to PSEC and the Minister of Finance regarding the future governance of BCPSEA, but emphasized that the most important consideration was a return of elected trustee representation to the board of BCPSEA. http://bit.ly/2BDJYa7 - BCSTA recommendations (Nov 27, 2017) 1. The new board of BCPSEA be comprised of a majority of elected trustees in combination with appointed government representatives, with consideration of inclusion from the Ministry of Education, PSEC, as well as others as appropriate. 2. All members of the new BCPSEA board be elected (or appointed in the case of government representatives) to staggered, multi-year terms. 3. Trustee members of the board be elected on a regional basis (the same regions as, or similar to, the current BCPSEA trustee advisory committee), but through a process that allows all eligible members the opportunity to vote for all trustee representatives to the board. 4. The chair of the new BCPSEA board to be elected by the members of the board from within its direct membership, but also with consideration of consistency with the other employers’ association. 5. All four public employer association boards be provided with regular mandatory governance and technical training directed at an identified skill and knowledge set appropriate to such board members 6. The board be provided with the opportunity to interact formally with the other public sector employers’ association boards and senior staff on a regular basis. This should include re-establishing regular meetings of the Public Sector Employers’ Council. 7. Establishment of a Technical Advisory Committee comprised of sector and affiliated experts to advise both the association operations and the board as appropriate Trustee Kimler BCPSEA Trustee Governance Report  20-Dec-2017 What’s next for trustees?
