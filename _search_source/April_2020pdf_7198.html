QUESTION PERIOD There was one question: The new AP does not seem to address removal of resources. Is that an oversight, intentional or addressed somewhere else? Point 3 In the general guidelines appears to suggest that teachers cannot select their own resources without having them approved through someone else. This seems cumbersome and fails to recognize teachers’ professionalism to select resources that address their students’ differentiated needs. Can teachers still select their own resources based on these criteria, or do all resources need to be approved by a “recognized source”? Answer: The removal of resources is in the SD 68 Requirements section on pages 4 & 5 of the revised AP, which outlines BC Ratings from the Consumer Protection BC Guidelines. Resource removal should be based on application of this criteria, and ensuring age relevant / appropriate selection. Selection of resources involves many people, including teachers. Purchases of school-based and district-level resources rests with district staff, principals and professional staff, including teachers. Teachers are directly involved in the selection of Learning Resource process. Learning Resources purchased by the district and schools must be vetted through the criteria as set forth in the revised AP 223 – these selections adhere to the Learning Standards of Provincial Curriculum and Focused Education (formerly ERAC). As a Provincial Public School organization, NLPS remains accountable to the public to ensure the use of appropriate, recognized Provincial standards for selection of learning and curriculum resources. This has not changed. 18. ADJOURNMENT The meeting adjourned at 9:09 pm. R20/04/22-16 IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brzovic IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Stanley That the meeting be adjourned.
