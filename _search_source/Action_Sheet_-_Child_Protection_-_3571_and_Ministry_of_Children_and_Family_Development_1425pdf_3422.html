THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 ACTION SHEET DATE: March 28, 2012 TO: The Board of Education FROM: Bob Esliger, District Principal, Student Support Services SUBJECT: New Policy: Child Protection Policy Removal: MCFD Policy 1425 and Procedure 1425P BACKGROUND All of us - families, communities and service providers – share responsibility for the safety and wellbeing of British Columbia’s children. That means we must all work together and, if we suspect that child abuse or neglect is taking place, we must report it to a child welfare worker. The primary responsibility for children’s safety and well-being rests with their parents. Where a parent is unwilling or unable to care for a child or protect the child from harm, child welfare workers are authorized to intervene. In British Columbia, the Ministry of Children and Family Development (MCFD) has lead responsibility for responding to suspected child abuse and neglect. Child welfare workers employed by MCFD are delegated under the Child, Family and Community Service Act (CFCSA) to assess reports, provide support services, investigate as needed and collaborate with other service providers, such as police, school personnel, health practitioners, etc. to help ensure the safety and well-being of children. This Policy, Procedure and Procedural Guide have been designed to support an integrated, collaborative response to child abuse and neglect by:     providing information about reporting suspected child abuse and neglect; providing an overview of relevant law and government policies; clarifying the roles and shared responsibilities of service providers, including their accountability for responding to suspected child abuse and neglect; and ensuring that responses to suspected child abuse and neglect in SD68 are effective, consistent and sensitive to the needs of children. Schools and communities play an integral role in responding to suspected child abuse and neglect. POLICY The Board of Education will support a comprehensive, coordinated and collaborative approach to appropriately address all issues related to child abuse and neglect. All employees in the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District are to exercise their duty to report any disclosures and/or suspected instances of child abuse. The district will provide annual training in the reporting of suspected child abuse and/or neglect. PROCEDURE A school district employee who has reason to believe that a child “needs protection” must make a report to a Child Welfare Worker. If the employee has a concern but is not sure whether the concern amounts to a reason to believe that the child needs protection, the employee should consult with school officials and/or a Child Welfare Worker about whether the indicators observed are cause for concern or amount to reason to believe that the child needs protection. School officials may assist or support staff in consultations or reports but should not hinder any employee from consulting with a Child Welfare Worker about a concern. The specifics regarding what and how to report are contained in the SD68/MCFD Procedural Guide with also includes a record sheet to be completed for each report conducted. These record sheets are maintained at central office in Student Support Services. SD68/MCFD PROCEDURAL GUIDE SD68 has created this local procedural guide in collaboration with the Ministry of Children and Family Development to ensure an effective, collaborative response to child abuse and neglect in our schools and communities. The guide helps to ensure that all adults in SD68 are clear on roles, responsibilities and channels of communication. In case of any inconsistency between the procedural guide and the Child, Family and Community Service Act, the Act takes precedence.
