BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: April 12, 2017 TO: Business Committee FROM: Secretary-Treasurer, Carrie McVeigh SUBJECT: Membership in the BC School Trustees’ Association Recommendation: That the Education Committee recommends the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receive the report dated April 12, 2017, on the costs and benefits of membership in the BC School Trustees Association. Background: By Board motion R16/03/30-16 (motion included at the end, for reference), staff was requested to provide the cost, including associated travel and accommodation costs, and benefits of membership in the BC School Trustees Association. The Board also asked for alternative options for Trustees to consider. The Cost: The cost of NLPS being a member of the BC School Trustees Association is made up of a number of components. First of all is the membership fee itself, which for 2015-2016 was approximately $46,600. In 2016-2017, the membership fee was some $46,900. The cost of attending the BCSTA Annual General Meeting and professional development events must be factored into the total cost of membership. Those events vary by year. For example in early 2015, the focus was training for newly elected school trustees. In 2015 the cost of trustees and senior staff attending BCSTA functions was approximately $16,700; the amount for 2016 was $10,000. The final cost of being a member of the BCSTA is the cost of participating in Vancouver Island School Trustees Association (VISTA) events. VISTA is a branch organization of BCSTA. In 2015, the cost of attending VISTA events was $1,300 while in 2016 the cost was $2,000. In summary, the cost of participating in the BC School Trustees Association was $64,600 in 2015 and $58,900 in 2016. The Benefits: BCSTA’s mission is to provide support to member boards of education and to advocate on behalf of those boards. BCSTA is recognized as the voice of local boards of education and is influential in shaping Government’s education policy. The executive has direct access to the Minister of Education and senior Ministry staff. Examples of recent advocacy efforts are:  response to Government’s plans to change the structure of BCPSEA, resulting in the BCSTA providing the vehicle for local boards of education to have input into the negotiations during teacher and support staff bargaining. BCSTA developed plans to provide the day-to-day services BCPSEA currently provides to individual boards of education in the event Government disbanded BCPSEA;  response to the Ministry’s changes to how capital and seismic projects are funded, resulting in increasing the level of understanding of district surpluses and retaining local decision making control;  response to the Supreme Court ruling on class size and composition, developed the Stability for Students Action Plan, advocating for stability for students, for reaching a bargained resolution to the class size and composition issue and for provincial funding for the implementation of the decision;  along with partner groups, participating in Ministry of Education and Government committees regarding a variety of initiatives affecting K-12 education, as the only voice of local boards of education; and,  ongoing dialogue with the Minister of Education and senior Ministry staff regarding matters affecting K-12 education. Services provided to member school districts include:  regular communication with member boards regarding new and developing matters affecting K-12 education;  Board Chairs’ discussion forum;  access to the joint BCSTA/BCTF group life insurance program;  access to BCSTA developed legal and policy-related publications which are used by districts to develop their own policies;  access to BCSTA’s in-house legal counsel for advice on legal, policy and governance matters at no additional cost to member districts;  professional development services for trustees of member districts; and,  maintains database of individual district policies, accessible to member districts. Alternatives: 1. Cancel NLPS membership in the BC School Trustees Association. The district would be able to reallocate the funds currently spent on membership and related expenses. The District would no longer have access to the benefits of the association unless they were purchased elsewhere or undertaken by district staff. (Note – March 31 is the deadline for a board to notify BCSTA of its decision to withdraw from the association for it to be effective on July 1 in the same year.) 2. Renew NLPS membership in BCSTA and reduce associated costs by: a. not attending any BCSTA or VISTA events; b. reducing the number of trustees and staff who attend BCSTA and VISTA events; or, c. reducing the cost of those attending BCSTA and VISTA events by using shared accommodation, carpooling or walking on the ferry and using public transit. 3. Renew NLPS membership in BCSTA and continue the current practice of staff and trustees attending BCSTA and VISTA events. R16/03/30-16 The Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to investigate and report back on the cost and benefits of a BCSTA membership (i.e., what is the total cost of BCSTA membership - dues, winter conference registration fees, travel, hotel & meal expenses, AGM registration fees, travel, hotel & meal expenses, etc; cost of membership alone; cost of conferences & AGM's; comparison of non-member conference/AGM fees; list of benefits derived from BCSTA membership). Staff should also provide a list of alternate options for Trustees. Report should be delivered for consideration on or before October 26, 2016.
