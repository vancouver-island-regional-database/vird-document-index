Page 8 of 8 REPORT TO PARKS, RECREATION & CULTURE ADVISORY COMMITTEE Report Prepared By: Meeting Date: RE: Shannon Wilson, Staff Liaison to PATG Chris Barfoot, Director, Parks, Recreation & Culture September 21, 2022 2023 COMMUNITY BANNER PROGRAM - THEME RECOMMENDATION RECOMMENDATION: That the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee endorse the recommendation of the Public Art Task Group and recommend that Council: 1. Approve the theme “Connecting Ladysmith Through the Arts” for the 2023 Community - Public Art Banners. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The purpose of the Community Banner Program is beautification and community identity promotion. Community banners add vibrancy and community spirit throughout Ladysmith and will represent Ladysmith’s small town charm, coastal community life and diverse cultural heritage. The Public Art Task Group (PATG) recommends themes for the program and makes its recommendations to Council through the PRCAC as per the group’s Terms of Reference. The Community Banner Program showcases the downtown core, and the Transfer Beach Boulevard. Purpose of PATG and Coordination of the Community Banner Program In November 2020, the PATG was formed. The purpose of the PATG is to assist the Town with: • Providing advice and recommendations to Council on specific Public Art projects, including selection processes, acceptance of grants, gifts, donations, bequests and deaccession. The PATG has two functions: (a) To advise the Town on public art issues and trends relevant to public art initiative in Town. (b) To advise on specific issues, such as donations, deaccession, and commissions of work. The PATG will review matters as outlined within the purpose of the PATG, and make recommendations to Council through the Parks, Recreation & Culture Advisory Committee. In consulation with the PATG, Staff will manage the Community Public Art Banner Program. The PATG will recommend the theme for the Banner Program to Council through PRCAC and the PATG will strike a selection panel to choose and recommend the Public Art Banners to Council through the PRCAC. THEME RECOMMENDATION The PATG recommends a theme that showcases art that connects people, promotes peace, spreads positivity, creates opportunities to be together, focuses on relationships, and celebrates diversity. Ladysmith and Stz’uminus’ natural environment inspires creativity, and community events and gatherings create the connections we all need to feel a sense of belonging. The Public Art Task Group recommends the 2023 banner theme of “Connecting Ladysmith through the Arts” for consideration by the PRCAC.
