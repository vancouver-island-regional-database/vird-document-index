Trustee Zone Report 24-May-2017 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  Author visits – Two authors visited LIS this month. Scott Chantler, an award-winning graphic novelist from Ontario and Nanaimo First Nation author Celestine Aleck.  Community Fun Fair – To be held on June 1st this year. Games, BBQ, pizza, silent auction & lots of fun for the kids with many prizes up for grabs. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  Self-Regulation Conversations – DJ Thompson engaged parents at a number of schools on the topic of self-regulated learning. This conversation aimed at parents and families comes on the heels of similar presentations to PACs.  Ladysmith Public Works Day – Kids & the community got hands-on experience with equipment, displays, demonstrations, bugs & dirt, a fire truck & were treated to balloons, hotdogs, cake & much more at this annual community event. Ecole North Oyster  Young Entrepreneurs Show – Grade 6 & 7 students were “open for business” earlier this month. After working on their business plans, students were selling hand-made products as part of the final test for their entrepreneur project. Product prices range from 50 cents to $4.00  BCAA Play Here Contest Update – The top-ten candidate projects was announced on Global TV on May 22nd and unfortunately, the ENO play space project did not make the top 10. Three Vancouver Island projects did, however, Eric J. Dunn Elementary in Port Alberni, Sahtlam Park in Lake Cowichan and Galiano Community School on Galiano Island. Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  LSS Musical – The LSS annual musical production “We will Rock You”, featuring music from Queen, will be performing at 7PM on May 25, 26 & 27, as well on Jun 1, 2 & 3. Tickets may be purchased in advance at the office or at the door on show days. It costs $8 for student tickets and $10 for the general public. Trustee Kimler
