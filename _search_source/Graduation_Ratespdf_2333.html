Initially, the student encountered social conflict that prompted RCMP involvement and as a result, finished the school year via outreach. Since returning this September, this student has allowed our staff to support him in all facets of life including creating a personalized education plan. Student A now has an 80% attendance rate and is actively making plans for the future. • Student B had poor attendance and a general disconnect from school. They showed a strong interest in the CTC program, both in baking and culinary arts providing a jumping off point to ignite their motivation. Student B was given more flexibility to shadow classes and pursue more career-based education. With persistence, staff developed learning strategies with Student B that allowed more academic success. Student B’s commitment has resulted in an adult dogwood diploma. Student Voice • “I am grateful because each and every staff member have helped me reach my goal of feeling successful and graduating. Every day they pushed me and made me, believe in myself. I am planning to pursue culinary arts at VIU starting January 2019. I am very thankful for everyone at my school who helped me get to this point.” • “I am grateful because they have helped me progress not only as a student but as a person. …I have been more productive in the year and a half I have been here than in all of my time in high school. My favorite part is that the teachers aren’t separate from students, we are all one family.” Aboriginal Student Performance Indicators • Transition rates (Gr 8-12) are higher for Aboriginal than non-Aboriginal students • Dogwood completion rates for Aboriginal boys increasing (50%, 55%, 58%) over past three years • Dogwood completion rates for Aboriginal girls increased from 49% to 68%. • Dogwood completion rate for all Aboriginal students 62%, a 10% increase from 2016/17. • FSA: decreased participation rates, Grades 4 and 7 meeting/exceeding are mid to low 50% • SLS results: students generally feel more supported by teachers and adults in schools. Through a combination of her own sheer will, teacher and parent support, and the keen eye of our psychologist, who was able to interpret the medical reports (which pointed towards a rare neurological condition) in her file so we could tailor her in-class support, this student persevered and completed her Dogwood last spring. What’s next? • Continue with • focus schools initiative, StrongStarts, Early intervention for literacy/numeracy • hul’q’umi’num language program, • syeyutsus framework • Continue with Graduation Success Coordinator initiatives focused on improving graduation rates (monthly student meetings, monthly parent meetings, supporting teachers) • Continue with data initiative to identify each student on their path to graduation; use this data to inform program delivery • Instructional leadership initiative • District-wide PLC, Collaborative Inquiry, professional development • Student Success Framework to guide our initiatives Questions • Results indicate that the collective ownership adopted by all schools, partners, families and leadership coupled with our strategies for the success of each student is making a difference.
