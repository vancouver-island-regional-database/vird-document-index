Michael & Regan Shepherd 3550 Monterey Drive Nanaimo, BC V9T 6T2 April 27, 2016 Board of Education School District 68 Dear Members of the Board, Parents from our school attended the budget review meeting on April 251“.We were happy to see that our concerns regarding safety with respect to the proposed cuts to transportation seemed to be in alignment with what was discussed by the board on Monday. Forty one children from our area currently use the bus for Route 1 Trip 2 to and from Pleasant Valley Elementary School. Based on the outdated de?nition from 1986, these riders are de?ned as ‘courtesy’ or ‘ineligible’ riders. If this bus route is cancelled and these children are instead expected to walk to school, they would have to walk down Jingle Pot Road, cross the Nanaimo Parkway as well as walk down Mostar Road and Metral Drive all during rush hour. They would also be faced with a very lengthy walk which could be dark in the winter months and a de?nite challenge in the rain. Our house is exactly 4-kmaway from Pleasant Valley School. As an adult, this trip takes me approximately 1 hour to complete, it would take my grade 3 child and my soon to be kindergarten child considerably longer than this. As parents we feel that this is not reasonable for elementary school children. In terms of safety, the route to the elementary school is very busy and there is a portion of this route that is without sidewalks along J inglepot Rd. Drivers on this section of J inglepot Rd have been clocked at travelling over 80 km per hour here. In terms of ICBC statistics, there have been 80 vehicle accidentsjust at the J inglepot/ Mostar Rd intersection with the Nanaimo Parkway between 2009 and 2013, 36 of those accidents have resulted in casualties (personal injury or fatality). A very similarnumber of crashes and casualties in that same time frame along Mostar Rd (85, 42) as well as Metral Drive (52, 21). Safety stats for these roads were not available past 2013, but these numbers may have increased given the rise in accidents related to distracted driving since then. We would like the de?nition of a courtesy / ineligible rider to be revised to include safety as an element for consideration and hope that it would eliminate this route as well as other routes with similar safety concerns from being cancelled in order to reduce costs. Also to note, the parents from Pleasant Valley School were not informed of the parent input session that took place in early April. We are very disappointed that we missed the chance to have our voice be heard and feel that there may be other affected schools and bus parents who were also not aware of this session. If the plan for transportation cuts is potentially moving forward, we would like the opportunity for a future parent input session to discuss this and would hope to send notices out to all bus parents in advance to allow them a chance to attend. Sincerely, Regan Shepherd (250) 740-7288
