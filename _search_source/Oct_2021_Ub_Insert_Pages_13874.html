Since launching on Family Day last February as a pilot program between the Town of Ladysmith and the Ladysmith Resources Centre Association (LRCA), the highly successful community-based initiative promoting literacy and physical literacy has inspired hundreds of participants. The LRCA is providing funding to support the continuation of the Storybook Walk at Kinsmen Park/ Brown Drive Park until the end of the year. The premise behind a storybook walk is that children and families explore the pages of a book that are installed along a path while at the same time participating in physical activity. The local installation winds its way around the playground and through the nearby forest trail where children’s imaginations can come to life. During COVID-19, the LRCA was able to pivot its Adventures in Early Literacy program outside in a way that children could safely gather. A special thank you to the community members who attended the Big Ideas Fair in downtown Ladysmith on October 2nd. The feedback you shared about the opportunities and challenges we face now and in the future will directly influence the updated Official Community Plan (OCP). During this public engagement phase for the OCP review, the Town is continuing to provide opportunities for the entire community to participate in the visioning process. The Big Ideas Fair was the first in-person engagement as part of the OCP review; we heard from hundreds of people who shared an enthusiasm for making Ladysmith an inclusive place to live, work and play. Looking for more ways to get involved in the OCP review? Visit www.ladysmith.ca/ocp to complete the ‘Oot and Aboot’ visual mapping survey. Recycling Audits Fireworks on Halloween Night The Town of Ladysmith completed two informal curbside audits this summer and found in both cases that soft plastics contributed to the majority of contamination in the recycling stream. Some people love to liven up their Halloween activities with a dazzling fireworks display, however, this is a friendly reminder that you need a CVRD Fireworks Discharge Permit, and a Fireworks Operators Certificate for any large-scale commercial or industrial grade displays. Contaminated recycling refers to items such as plastic bags or wrappings, improperly rinsed containers, and multi-layered packaging such as chip bags, or granola bar wrappers that are being disposed of in curbside collection. But there’s good news! You can take the majority of soft plastics to Peerless Road Recycling Centre and Bottle Junction Depot on Oyster Bay Drive free of charge. Holland Creek Crossing The contractor for the Holland Creek bridge crossing at Colonia Drive has resumed construction. During ongoing construction of the crossing, trail users should expect intermittent closures and be prepared to turn back. We anticipate restricted access to the trail at Colonia Drive as well as on the south side above Crystal Falls. You will continue to be able to enter the trail at Mackie Road but the path right towards the Upper Bridge and the Heart Lake Connector may be closed during ongoing construction. Permits If you want to discharge fireworks on Halloween night in your yard you must have the following: • Fireworks Discharge Permit approved by the CVRD. This can be applied for at Ladysmith City Hall, 410 Esplanade during business hours, Monday to Friday: 8:30 a.m. – 4:00 p.m. Please note that fireworks may be discharged only on October 31 between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 12 midnight in the owner’s yard, with a valid permit. Properly disposing of your waste bags by using the nearest available trash container is helping to keep our public spaces enjoyable for everyone. We appreciate your cooperation. Town Donates Produce to Foodbank - The Town harvested and donated 45 squash plants to the Ladysmith Resources Centre Association Food Bank. The produce was grown in garden beds at Transfer Beach Park and City Hall. The use of these gardens for growing vegetables strengthens our local food security by helping to provide nourishing food to those who need it the most.
