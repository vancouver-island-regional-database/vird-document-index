NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BOARD OF EDUCATION PUBLIC MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: July 2, 2020 Board of Education Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer South Wellington Elementary School Background: In the Spring of 2019, the Board approved the 2020-21 Capital Plan Submission. Included in that submission was a request under the Rural District Program (RDP) for funding to demolish South Wellington Elementary School (the “Facility”). The basis for the request was the District’s experience with the cost of maintaining previously closed facilities that were not needed for future use. For instance, Harewood School, Extension Elementary as well as CRC/Library Services are examples of closed facilities that became significant health and safety concerns as well as potential major financial liabilities to the District. Currently, the Facility is not usable for school purposes. For instance, it needs seismic upgrades, doesn’t have a gym etc. It should be noted that while the building is deteriorating, the building could still be used for other purposes if it has appropriate investments such as capital changes required for the intended purpose and ongoing maintenance/operations were provided. The roof will also need attention and systems are aged. The concern is mainly associated with the District’s inability to maintain a building that does not have a school use. The Facility will degrade overtime without such capital and maintenance investments and the District does not have the resources to support the Facility. In the Spring of 2020, the Ministry of Education approved the request for demolition of the Facility, subject to the District providing a business case to receive final approval. Upon receiving this approval, the Board directed staff to consult with the community with respect to the demolition as part of our business case. The land was not subject to the consultation process. The motion was as follows: Folio: R20/04/22-08 That staff meet with the South Wellington Area Community Association and the Regional District of Nanaimo as part of the business case development. Action Sheet: South Wellington Elementary School Page 2 Since that time District staff have:         Updated the Ministry of Education on the current consultation process; Put the start of the business case on hold; Held a meeting at the South Wellington Elementary School site on April 30th with appropriate social distancing (an update was provided at the May Board meeting); Held an online “Teams” meeting on May 20th with all members of the public welcome (an update was provided at the May Board meeting); Updated the Regional District of Nanaimo (the “RDN”) on the current process and provided the RDN access to assess the facility; Posted and updated a FAQ on our website with all available Facility information; Received feedback from the community; Provided the community access to the facility. Summary of Feedback: In addition to a number of letters sent directly to the Board, the community provided feedback to staff. From the feedback received, there was an understanding that it is a challenge for the District to maintain the Facility. The general consensus was that the community would like the Facility to not be torn down and that it be used for community purposes. The main themes were as follows:      The South Wellington area has limited access to amenities; The local community would like an opportunity to rent the facility; There was insufficient time to consult with the local community; While not included in the consultation process, the community is concerned about the future of the land; and The District should give the community and/or the RDN time to create a plan to operate the Facility while also investing in the Facility. New Information: On June 23, 2020, the RDN reported out the current condition of the facility including the costs of bringing the facility to a point where it would be usable as a community asset. The value of the upgrade is significant. Depending on the nature of the upgrade the hard construction costs would be between $1,050,000 (recommended minimum upgrade) and $3,160,560 (major upgrades) – excluding soft costs. At this time the RDN has expressed interest in keeping the land as a community asset and is generally supportive of the communities’ wish for use of the facility but there is no specific funding available to upgrade the facility. The RDN passed the following motions: 1. That the Regional District of Nanaimo (RDN) advise the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (School District 68) of the RDN’s continued interest in the South Wellington Elementary School being used as a community centre. Action Sheet: South Wellington Elementary School Page 3 2. Should Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools District (School District 68) not remove the South Wellington Elementary School from the site, that RDN work with the South Wellington and Area Community Association and the School District on operating models for community use of the school and site. 3. That the South Wellington Elementary School - Feasibility Study Report be provided to Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (School District 68) and the South Wellington and Area Community Association. We have also become aware that the District’s estimate to address our Selby Street properties may be insufficient to address the liabilities. We have had preliminary discussions with the Ministry of Education and we may be able to transfer the funds that were tentatively available to address South Wellington to the Selby Street properties. Given this potential opportunity to transfer the available funding to another project, and the community’s request for additional time to create a plan to address the capital maintenance deficit at South Wellington Elementary School, staff is making the following recommendations: 1. That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) not proceed with the demolition of South Wellington Elementary School as part of the 2020-2021 Capital Plan; 2. That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) direct staff to consult with the Ministry of Education regarding the reallocation of the capital funds allocated to South Wellington Elementary School in the 2020-2021 Capital Plan be reallocated to the Selby Street properties; Finally, as noted above, if there is not investment in the facility it will continue to be a liability to the District. While we are recommending providing time for the community and/or the RDN to create a plan for the facility, if such a plan that includes financial resources to upgrade the facility does not materialize, it is also staff’s recommendation that the facility be demolished. Therefore, staff is also recommending the following motion: 3. That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) amend the 2021-2022 Capital Plan to include a request for funding to demolish South Wellington Elementary School, subject to the District receiving a plan from an external organization that would provide for funding to upgrade and maintain the facility.
