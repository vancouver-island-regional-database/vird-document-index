THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 INFORMATION SHEET DATE: October 26, 2011 TO: Board of Education FROM: Pete Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations SUBJECT: Report on School Janitorial Services Background The following motion was passed by the Board of Education on May 25, 2011 - Folio R11/05/25-15 That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) directs staff to prepare a report on school janitorial services that address staff; workload; safety and costs and for the report to be presented to the October 2011 Regular Board Meeting. Discussion To date staff have solicited School Districts through the School Plant Officials Association (SPOA) list serve for Districts who have undertaken recent janitorial reviews. Staff requested the name of the firm, a copy of the study (if available) and whether or not the District was pleased with the results. Staff reviewed the responses and prepared a rough scope of work for the District study. Key issues include:       Comparison of workload with six other School Districts and the provincial average (by square meter, by student and by budget). Review the state of cleanliness in schools (by representative sample) to comparison to industry standards. Review organization of janitorial department including management structure, workload, effective management, HR practices, etc. Review selected janitorial runs for work load and cleanliness. Provide opportunity for interviews with Trustee representative, Senior Management representative, District staff, school staff (AO, teacher representative, NDTA, CUPE, DPAC, etc.) and custodial representative. Review cleaning products, janitorial storage, absence control, quality control, cleaning strategies, cleaning processes, and janitorial shift hours. From the information received, staff requested proposals from two firms which were deemed capable of providing the services for the study. Both firms have responded. Staff are currently reviewing the proposals for completeness, conducting reference checks and will then evaluate the proposals to ensure that the best value is achieved. Indications are that staff will be in a position to award a contract in early November and it is expected that such a study will take approximately twelve (12) weeks to complete. Recommendations are expected in time for staff to consider the results in the 2012/13 budget deliberations.
