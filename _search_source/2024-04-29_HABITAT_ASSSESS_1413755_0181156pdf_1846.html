The bluff consists of sandstone bedrock with large pieces of fractured bedrock and sandstone boulders on sand at the bottom of the bluff. Sand seabed transitions to a soft mud and silt bottom approximately 26m from the shoreline. The presence of Beggiatoa is indicative of anoxic seabed conditions as it oxidizes hydrogen sulphide as an energy source.
