Appendix A Accessibility Plan Update May 2026 The Accessibility Committee continued its work towards the three-year plan that was implemented in September 2023. This update highlights work completed during the 2025-2026 school year. Committee and community feedback are helping to shape the revised Accessibility Plan, which will be ﬁnalized and made available on the district site by the end of August 2026. Priority #1: Employment and Service Delivery Practices Work in this area was woven into other priority areas across the year. Speciﬁc areas of consultation included: • Survey for parents and community members regarding playground accessibility • Consultation with principals and vice-principals to support updates to the classroom accessibility matrix Priority #2: The Built Environment The year focused on improving how accessibility needs are identiﬁed, prioritized and addressed. Progress to date: • Rolled out the Built Environment Matrix to support shared decision making and clear priorities • Implemented the e-Base system to improve communication and streamline workﬂow • Worked with a consultant to do a fulsome gap analysis of two school sites, to inform the universal guidelines that will be developed in the coming year • Added accessibility features to projects that did not originally include them • Completed many long-standing accessibility projects, reducing the backlog • Created a clear internal process for workorders related to accessibility needs • Established a partnership with DPAC to develop playground accessibility guidelines • Surveyed parents and caregivers of students with accessibility barriers to better understand playground needs Priority #3: Inclusion, Mental Health and Peer Connections Schools continue to build welcoming spaces where students feel supported and connected. Progress to date: • Installing communication boards on playgrounds across the district to support all learners during play (ﬁrst to be unveiled June 01, 2026) • Revising the Student Accessibility Considerations, with district-wide implementation planned for September 2026 • The committee has identiﬁed an ongoing need to improve student transitions: • • • Between programs Between schools From elementary to high school Priority #4: Information and Communication We are working to make district information easier to ﬁnd, understand, and access. Progress to date: • Launched a new external website with prominent accessibility navigation, an accessibility landing page, and a refreshed accessibility reporting tool • Continued work toward a plain-language style guide for staff communications • For the ﬁrst time, the district will recognize National AccessAbility Week (June 1-8, 2026), by unveiling our ﬁrst communication board at Fairview school.
