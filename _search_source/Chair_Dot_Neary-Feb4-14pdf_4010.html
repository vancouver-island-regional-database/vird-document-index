Chair Dot Neary: February 6, 2014 Re: your reply letter dated January 20, 2014 – 10 Year EFL Plan. In the fall of 2012, the Board concluded a public process that identified enhanced learning facilities as a general objective to be pursued by the Board. The Board then went behind closed doors to produce the 10 Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan. Trustees then buttoned their lips and went out to “consult” with the public. Unless something dramatic happens tonight, the final Plan will be almost a mirror image of the Plan on Day 1. It treats the rural area in between Nanaimo and Ladysmith as a resource to be exploited; close and sell every existing rural elementary school in between Nanaimo and Ladysmith ; close the only rural high school in the District and herd the students onto busses in order to fill empty class rooms in the city. The Plan relies heavily on superficial answers to serious questions. What if there is no Provincial money for a new NDSS - no problem – use the money from the sale of school properties for a down payment and borrow the remainder, right? You still don’t have an idea of what the 21st century mega elementary planned for Cedar will look like, what the 21st century teaching model will involve, or how much the building conversion will cost. Your estimates for how many students will be attending the new school are grossly inflated. You have no plans for a complementary 21st century learning environment at the high school level. I doubt that you plan to measure the impact that the 21 century layout and teaching method will have on the graduates of the mega elementary. This is a real life experiment heavily reliant on gut instinct and blind faith on your part. You voted all of this in on June 26th, 2013 without having answers to the most basic of questions, after only 4 hours of closed door discussions, only 48 hours after the close of the public consultation period. A reasonable person will conclude that the process was never about identifying and evaluating options to the plan. The process was all about keeping people busy by providing them with false hope that the Board was open to ideas from outsiders. Yours truly: Jukka Efraimsson 2085 Grieve Road Nanaimo (Cedar), B.C.
