EMPLOYMENT 05 06 07 HOPE & HEALTH COMMUNITY CAMP The spirit of community and youth empowerment was on full display as hundreds of young people gathered for the 10th Annual Community Camp, hosted by Hope and Health in Stz’uminus First Nation. The day was filled with excitement and connection, celebrating active living through sports and the arts. Youth were treated to a powerful musical performance by local Stz’uminus artist Nate Harris, who energized the crowd with his heartfelt 08 lyrics. Adding to the thrill of the day were special visits from Pacific FC players, who spent time engaging with the youth, signing autographs, and sharing words of encouragement. The event also featured a surprise appearance by Raven, the beloved mascot, much to the delight of the crowd. Organizers expressed gratitude to the many partners and supporters who helped make the event possible, including Hope and Health, Kw’umut Lelum, Helijet, Pacific FC, UBC Thunderbirds, as well as the dedicated mentors, coaches, youth participants, and their families. The annual camp continues to grow each year, fostering community pride, leadership, and inspiration among Indigenous youth across the region. 09 KL Red Road Walking the Red Road: A New Healing Journey with Kw’umut Lelum Kw’umut Lelum is honoured to share the launch of our new culturally grounded support program: Red Road to Recovery — an initiative created by and for Indigenous Peoples walking through the complexities of addiction and recovery. Whether you’re thinking about sobriety for the first time, returning from a healing centre, or looking for culturally safe alternatives to mainstream recovery groups, this program offers a welcoming place to start or continue your journey. What is the Red Road to Recovery? The Red Road is a path of healing — a journey that honours balance, accountability, and the sacredness of life. It is not just about becoming sober; it’s about coming home to yourself, your spirit, and your culture. Our program walks beside you at every step: Readiness Path (Before Treatment) Some relatives need time, support, and encouragement before they’re ready to enter detox or treatment. We provide: • One-on-one support from a trusted helper • Healing circles, peer and community connections • Help navigating detox or treatment options • Cultural and land-based healing (sweats, cedar brushing, Elders) • Gentle preparation for the next step During Treatment Stay connected and reinforce cultural identity during treatment. We can help: • Provide medicines, cultural supplies, check-ins with cultural workers • Help families stay informed and connected 10 Aftercare Path (Post-Treatment) Continued healing support can help you succeed on your path. We offer: • One-on-one support with housing, family, work, and community • Relapse prevention tools rooted in culture • Continued connection to cultural practices and ceremonies Community Connection Mainstream programs like AA or NA can feel unfamiliar or isolating. Our approach builds a circle of care through: • Elder and cultural worker involvement • Support attending recovery meetings or events • Peer mentorship opportunities • Family healing and sober community gatherings Who is this program for? Anyone from the 9 Kw’umut Lelum Member Nations who is thinking about recovery or already walking that path. You do not need to be sober to join. This program welcomes: • Youth, adults, Elders, and two-spirit relatives • All genders and identities • People with or without spiritual or religious beliefs Referrals can come through a Kw’umut Lelum Family Support Worker or you can self-refer if you feel ready to reach out. Our Commitment to You We walk this journey with: • • • • • Respect for your path and your pace Responsibility to support healing with compassion Reciprocity through shared stories and strength Relationship to land, culture, and community Wholeness of mind, body, spirit, and heart If you, or someone you care about, is struggling with addiction or thinking about making a change, you don’t have to do it alone. The Red Road to Recovery is here for you — grounded in Coast Salish snuw’uy’ulh, guided by community, and carried forward with love. To learn more or to join the program, please reach out to Troy Brooks at 250-591-0933 or tbrooks@kwumut.org. 11 STZ’UMINUS FAMILY ADVOCACY WHO WE ARE Stz’uminus Family Advocate and Community Engagement Worker are here to support our members. OUR GOAL The main goal for the Community Engagement Worker and Family Advocate is to reduce the number of children coming in to care by linking families to available resources, programs and services. We want to support Cultural Caregivers for children who are unable to reside in their parental home. What We Do – Family Advocate Role The Family Advocate plays a vital role in supporting the well-being of Stz’uminus families through culturally grounded, community-based services. Key responsibilities include: Community Cupboard (Food Bank): Coordinating monthly food bank services. Updates and schedules are shared via the Stz’uminus Health Centre Facebook page. Family Resource Coordination: Assisting in the organization of family support services and traditional decision-making processes to help families with children at risk. 12 Direct Family Support: Providing culturally appropriate guidance and social support to families and individuals facing high-risk situations. Child Safety and Well-being: Supporting families in creating safe, nurturing, and enriching environments for children. Advocacy and Navigation: Attending meetings alongside Stz’uminus members to offer advocacy and help navigate the child protection system. Emotional and Spiritual Support: Offering compassionate support to families experiencing hardship or crisis. Community Guidance: Advising individuals, families, and the broader community on overcoming social and personal challenges. Referral Services: Connecting members with external services such as financial aid, legal assistance, housing, and medical care, as well as internal health and wellness programs. Your feet need special care! Sam, our Certified Diabetic Foot Care Nurse, provides gentle, professional, and preventative care to keep your feet healthy and prevent complications that can lead to infection or amputation. Services Include: * Comprehensive foot assessments * Nail care (including thickened and fungal nails) * Callus and corn reduction * Wound prevention and education * Early detection of circulation or nerve changes * Personalized foot care plans *Nursing Report for podiatry referrals Why It Matters: Diabetes can affect your circulation and sensation. Routine foot care by a nurse trained in diabetic foot health reduces your risk of serious complications and helps you stay active and independent.
