BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: November 28, 2012 TO: Board of Education FROM: Dave Hutchinson, Superintendent/CEO RE: Superintendent’s Report on the Strategic Plan: Draft One BACKGROUND: High-functioning organizations set goals for their performance that are closely linked to the core business of the organization. The core business of any public education enterprise is strengthening student achievement. With this in mind, Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools has undertaken two significant developments over the past year that will position the Board of Education and Senior administrative team well with respect to placing the improvement of student learning at the center of our work. First, the Board of Education has opted to embrace a policy governance model and abandon the former management Board structure. Second, the Board of Education and Senior administrative team led an extensive round of public consultations in order to arrive at the draft strategic plan. It is important to note that the development of a strategic plan is an expectation of the new governance policies, and is defined in Policy 2.1(1), the “Role of the Board” as follows: The Board is responsible for providing District direction and goals, for developing plans to achieve the goals, for establishing monitoring processes to evaluate progress toward the goals and for reporting District achievements. The Board's focus is on student success and organizational direction. The Board establishes strategic plans designed to achieve District Goals and leads and inspires the District through written policies based on the Board's Values, Vision and Mission. To fulfill its role, the Board: 1. Provides Leadership, Strategic Direction and Enabling Supports for Learning: by establishing District Goals, developing and implementing a Strategic Plan to attain the goals, approving the District operating and capital budget and aligning resources to achieve the plan, and continuously monitoring District progress toward goal achievement. 1 RATIONALE: The purpose of this report is to provide an overview of the intent of the initial draft of the strategic plan, and to present its main recommendations. This will provide the Board of Education with an opportunity to discuss the plan, and recommend revisions in preparation for the development of the final version. RECOMMENDATION: That the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) receives the Strategic Plan: Draft One, and refers it to a Special Board Meeting to be held on Monday, December 3, 2012 at 6 pm. 2
