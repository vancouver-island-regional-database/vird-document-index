School District 68 Trustees. Cedar Ball Hockey Challenge Association is committed to the development of recreation in the RDN Electoral Area A region. Heading into our Seventh Annual event we find ourselves coming full circle with our original goal of resurfacing the North Cedar Intermediate multi purpose court area. This goal faced a challenging set back in 2015 when School District 68 went on strike and the future of NCI lied in the balance with what at the time appeared to be a high probability of closure. Recent events have restored balance in our community and we can now move forward confidently knowing that NCI will remain open as a K-7 elementary school for years to come. Knowing with confidence that NCI is here to stay, the CBHCA feels that it makes great sense to move forward with resurfacing the North Cedar Intermediate multi purpose court area. Clearly this is an area that SD68 will need to address when considering the safely of our students, especially now that the school will have younger students. CBHCA has re connected with Hub City Paving to revisit the original quote they had provided back in 2010, make any necessary amendments and provide us with a current quote. CBHCA also has many resources and volunteers in place for much of the preparation and in combination with Hub City Paving. Provided is a copy and details of the quote from Hub City Paving which has been discounted substantially reflecting their commitment and giving back to the community. CBHCA has and is prepared to contribute moneys in the amount of $20,000 which covers over half the cost without factoring in all the volunteers and man hours that will be contributed on our behalf. Taking all this into account, in the opinion of the Cedar Ball Hockey Challenge Association it is in the best interest of this community and SD68 to take advantage of this tremendous opportunity and move forward with this project. Hub City Paving has a window for completion immediately following the final day of school at the end of June 2016. Any time after this may be challenging and could effect having this project completed in time for school opening in September 2016. Looking forward to hearing back from the board soon. Best regards, Francois Brassens. VP CBHCA.
