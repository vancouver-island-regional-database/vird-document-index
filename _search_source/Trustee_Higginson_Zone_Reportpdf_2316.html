TRUSTEE ZONE REPORT Trustee Stephanie Higginson Zone Name: Nanaimo District Secondary Name of School(s) Visited: Fairview Quarteway NDSS Activities happening at school locations: Fairview I attended the opening of the renovated learning commons at Fairview Elementary. As has been reported on at a previous Board meeting, Fairview received a $60000 grant from the Indigo Love of Reading Grant. There were many highlights about this visit and the project in general that I think are take interesting aways for members of the board to think about. 1. The importance of renovating the physical spaces of the schools as we work on progressing the learning transformation. This is a topic for us to think about locally, in terms of budget allocation, as well as provincially to ensure that capital planning guidelines continue to evolve in order to allow newly built spaces to incorporate the principles of the learning transformation. I would be interested to hear from our staff at some point if they feel that this is being addressed by current capital planning guidelines. 2. The event included many staff members from the Nanaimo Chapters store. I was struck by how committed they were to improving the library at Fairview and the deep connections they had developed with staff and with the students. While I struggle with corporate involvement in schools, in particular for basic supplies such as books, I was struck by the love the staff at our local Chapters felt for this project and the Fairview community. It just seemed to be a really mutually beneficial process. 3. There were so many people involved in making this a success, way to go every single person involved. I was able to attend my first Souper Wednesday this morning. This is a PAC run initiative to provide every student in the school with a warm and nourishing bowl of homemade soup for lunch. It is entirely dependent on volunteers, which are not always easy to get. So, if anyone out there has their criminal record check completed and has time on Wednesday mornings between 9-11 or 11-1, please contact me and I will put you in touch with the person who organizes the volunteers. The feeling this day has in the school is deeply important to the school community and many students and staff have commented to me on the positive impact it has on the school. École Quarterway: As many know, École Quarterway has been fundraising for many years for a nature based playground upgrade. They have experienced a few hurdles over the years that are related to materials guidelines and requirements from the Ministry of Education. Those of us who were on the board last term can also reflect on the experience of École North Oyster Elementary with regards to nature based playground and material requirements. I am wondering if in the future we may want to hear from our staff what the tensions are regarding nature based playgrounds and ministry requirements regarding building materials. As we know, nature based playgrounds are Trustee Stephanie Higginson – Zone Report increasing in demand and also link to the Syeyutsus Framework of land based learning. Additionally, utilizing natural materials rather than plastics and other artificial materials benefits both our students and the planet. My long term thinking is that there may be a link to advocacy work for the board with the MOE on this issue. Nanaimo District Secondary School: On Jan. 10 I was fortunate enough to attend the NDSS Dance Extravaganza. It was so great to see the students performing dances they choreographed as well as the one act play “Terezin”. The dances were all incredible. Students not only demonstrated a high level of physical literacy, they did so in multi-age, cross grade groupings and they connected the dances to other classes and subjects. I want to share with you some of the information regarding the play, Terezin that Ms. Barnum shared with the audience. I believe this is a living breathing example of the direction of the learning transformation. This play came about after two students began working with Ms. Barnum, who teaches both performing arts and social studies. After taking Ms. Barnum’s Genocide Studies class the two students, examined children’s poetry and art from the Theresienstadt Ghetto and examined music created by or influenced by Jewish composers. They wrote this play based on this exploration. More than two dozen students came together to bring this play to life. They have performed in two festivals and have won numerous awards, include best one act play at the Vancouver Island One Act Festival. Way to go students and staff of NLPS. NDSS has so many activities and incredible projects happening. I can’t even begin to touch on them all. However, what I can say, is that all of the numerous events are supported by our incredible staff and each one gives students another place to shine and demonstrate learning that is meaningful to them. So thank you to everyone. Semester two began this week. Grade 7’s from across the NDSS zone are beginning their grade 8 orientations, picking courses or at least understanding what next year will look like for them. It is an exciting time across the district for these students and their families. I would also like us as a board to better understand the programming our grade 8’s are receiving across the district. We have had some fabulous presentations from various secondary schools on ways they are working with the grade 8 students on this transition year and my big picture question is around whether this level of programming happens at every high school.
