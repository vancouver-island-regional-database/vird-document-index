From: Briar Work [mailto:otbriar@outlook.com] Sent: Thursday, February 27, 2014 10:28 AM To: David Hutchinson Cc: District PAC; John Blain Subject: SD 68 Concerns. Dear Mr. Hutchinson, I am emailing in response to the media reports this morning about the potential teachers strike. We have recently moved to BC from Northern Alberta and we have 3 children, one which is attending a Grade 2 class, one which will be entering Kindergarten in the fall, and 1 toddler. We live in Cinnabar Valley and my daughter attends Cinnabar Elementary. I am very discouraged with what I see as major undermining of our education system at both the provincial and district level. I feel that this current government is not doing enough to ensure the adequate education of our children in the public system. On top of the reports that 96% of BC's teachers have rejected the offer from the government indicating a potential strike, our board has recently rolled out "Success for All" learning time for teachers. This is causing early dismissal in our local school Mondays and Fridays at 1:30 beginning in 2014. This is now two days which my children are done school early. The board feels that it can educate our children at the very "minimum" of mandated instructional time. Regular dismissal is at 2:30. My children spend more time out of school than in. As a two parent working family this places undue childcare burden on our family, disrupts the routine for my children (some days are short some not – struggling with childcare schedules) and I feel directly impacts "bum in seats" instructional time. Now with an impending strike, my children’s education will potentially be further interrupted. As a result of this mess, our family is seriously looking into the alternatives of private education for our children. We are advocates of the public system, and in Northern Alberta, I worked directly for the public system as an occupational therapist. I feel that a strong public system and strong community schools strengthens the fabric of our communities and province. If our government and local school board cannot demonstrate these core values to our family, we will be motivated to turn elsewhere to ensure that our children get not just the "minimums" that this government and board feels is acceptable. I am aware that teachers contracts can impact how a board is able to provide professional development to its teachers, however, I feel that these are all issues that can be discussed during contract bargaining instead of passing on the extra hour to our families. I am also aware of a significant shortfall in the local school division’s budget. I understand that difficult decisions are required, however, not being able to listen to the needs of many families within the district during these decisions makes me uneasy. Just meeting the minimums of our children’s instructional time as well as a volatile contract relationship with the teachers gives us the message that the public system in Nanaimo may not be able to meet our family’s needs throughout our children’s education. We are beginning the process of looking into the private options in the Nanaimo region. I am an advocate of our local teachers. I feel that they give their very best at the front lines. We are asking that you be able to take our feedback to the board with regards to its decision making around school hours and education time. Sincerely, Briar Shaw Parent of 3 Occupational Therapist, Nanaimo, BC
