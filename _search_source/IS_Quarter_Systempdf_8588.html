NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATION COMMITTEE PUBLIC MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: May 5, 2021 Education Committee Superintendent, Scott Saywell Secondary Quarter System for 2021/2022 Background During the 2019-2020 school year, our secondary schools were organized in a Semester System. Students would take 4 courses from September through January, then another 4 courses from February through June. In March of 2020 the Ministry of Education moved all students to remote learning. With the exception of children of essential services workers, students in NLPS worked remotely through April and May. In June, students returned part time to school, with the remaining learning continuing remotely. The Ministry of Education developed a 5-Stage Restart Plan. We finished the school year in Stage 2, with students attending school for part time in-class instruction and part time remote learning. In late July of 2020, the MOE updated their 5-Stage Restart Plan, with changes to Stage 2, including the requirement to restrict the number of students within the newly termed “Learning Group.” At the secondary level, the maximum Learning Group size was set at 120. This was a significant challenge for schools on a Semester or Linear system throughout the province. To reduce Learning Group size to 120 or fewer, most schools across the province moved to a Quarter System. In NLPS, this required a completely new build of secondary timetables in late August. Building timetables within a very short timeline, for a system that students and staff were not familiar with, along with the restrictions the come with Learning Groups, was challenging. In best efforts to work within the intent of the newly formed Learning Groups, secondary schools in NLPS organized students within Learning Groups of small numbers wherever possible. Many Learning Group sizes in grades 8, 9, or 10 were limited to 30 or less students. Although this organization aligns with the health and safety guidelines for contacttracing purposes in the event of a COVID-19 exposure at school, the smaller Learning Groups sizes limited the ability for students to be placed in all of the courses they had requested. Information Sheet: Secondary Quarter System for 2021/2022 Page 2 Quarter System 2021-2022 Planning for the 2021-2022 school year typically occurs from February to May of each year. As the planning phase proceeds, the ability to change course becomes more and more difficult. Schools have to make decisions based on the current Ministry of Education Guidelines as they cannot predict what Stage of the Restart Plan will be in effect, or if the Ministry will make changes to the plan over the summer. For these reasons, schools are presently organizing classes in a Quarter System for 2021-2022. This does not mean that schools will be unable to change course if more information concerning the threat of COVID-19 becomes available. Schools will continue to engage their staff, students, and broader community this school year as the province continues to address the challenges of COVID-19.
