BOARD OF EDUCATION Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: January 27, 2016 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Superintendent RE: Adjustment to Local School Calendar: Local Calendar Recommendations Background The Board approved the 2015-16 Local School Calendar in the spring, and took it to consultation for approval. As outlined in School Calendar Regulation 314/12, the Board may approve amendments to the local school calendar. Rationale: The Ministry of Education has recently mandated a curriculum redesign and is also providing two days of curriculum in-service, during the 2015-16 school year, for teachers to prepare for the changes. The Nanaimo-Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) local school calendar for 2015-16, therefore, requires an adjustment. After staff discussion with the Nanaimo District Teachers’ Association, the following has been agreed as the two days designated as non-instructional days and to be devoted to curriculum inservice:  Friday, January 29, 2016 and  Monday, May 16, 2016 In addition, the Ministry has provided for one more non-instructional Curriculum In-service day in each of the following two school years. As a result, the revised Local School Calendars for 2016-17 and for 2017-18 will also need to be amended to reflect the Ministry’s adjustment in the number of instructional hours (5 hours less for each school year), and its adjustment to the minimum hours of instruction. Ministry This change to the Local School Calendar requires a 30-day notice period as stipulated within School Calendar Regulation 314/12. Both the NDTA and CUPE executive have been made aware of this recommendation and are supportive. Recommendation: That the Board of Education approves the current Local School Calendar be amended by adding noninstructional days for the purpose of Curriculum In-service. These non-instructional in-service days (1 day per year on a date to be announced) be added to the Local School Calendar for the 2016-17 and 2017-18 local school years. A 30-day notice period will be provided, as required.
