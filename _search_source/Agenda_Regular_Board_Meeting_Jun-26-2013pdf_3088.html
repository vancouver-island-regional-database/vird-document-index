School Trustees Association. Frank J. Ney Elementary Teacher - Terri Zolob & students Learning in Depth Project Frank J, Ney Grade 2 students and their teacher Terri Zolob gave a presentation about the “Learning in Depth” project they have been involved in since January. Each student is randomly assigned a topic to learn in depth and build personal portfolios on their topic. The topics students chose randomly included ships, space, fish, horses, bears and bridges. The project was designed from work by Kieran Egan about the way that learning in depth can transform schooling. Energy Conservation and Sustainability in our Schools. Jennifer Callaghan, BC Hydro and Chad Dalrymple, SD68 Energy Manager BC Hydro has, once again, been working with the outstanding secondary students of SD 68 in the area of energy conservation. BC Hydro has had the pleasure of connecting with students and teachers from four returning school teams: Dover Bay, Ladysmith, Nanaimo District and Woodlands. The students have all worked with Chad Dalrymple, SD68 Energy Manager and Jennifer Callaghan of BC Hydro. The students told us about the projects they have carried out this year as part of the BC Hydro Energy Ambassadors program. The students gather information about energy and resources used at their schools. The students said that they are seeing increased awareness but there are still areas where changes need to be made. NEW BUSINESS Disposal of Real Property Bylaw No. 2012-02 (Harewood Field) Mr. Turin outlined the bylaws that were required this evening. Four of the properties are part of the land exchange with the City of Nanaimo announced in December. The Harewood playing field; Northfield School; a portion of the Oliver Woods property; and Dickinson Road (Pioneer Park) are being transferred to the city, while the school district is obtaining properties that can be used for future planning. McGirr Elementary School in Nanaimo is 4,174 square metres (44,912 square feet) in overall area, originally constructed in 1990, with a major addition and renovation project completed in 1998. EXISTING HEATING EQUIPMENT The heating boilers installed are three Weil-McLean cast iron, atmospheric appliances, with a total combined input rating of 2400 MBH. Two of the boilers date original construction of the school in 1990, while the third boiler was added as part of the 1998 addition. Improved energy efficient appliances are available today, which will assist in reducing energy consumption costs and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The boiler plant is currently piped with no primary pumps on individual boilers, so when the plant is active water flows through all three boilers. In this instance the non-firing boilers act as reverse heat exchangers whenever their interior temperature is lower than the heating loop temperature, resulting in wasted energy. No. 1 – Existing heating plant consisting of 3 Weil-McLean atmospheric boilers, with no primary pumps No. 2 – Piping in the boiler room is in reasonable condition and can be incorporated into the upgraded plant.
