During job action, Learning Services arranged a full morning in-service opportunity at Beban Park Community Centre. One hundred seventy-eight Education Assistants attended a workshop on Sensory Processing & Self-Regulation delivered by Occupational Therapist Michelle Lantaigne. The information received will inform their practice with all students, and in particular will be helpful for intervening with students on the Autism Spectrum. There were good questions asked, positive feedback received and requests for more district-initiated, job-related, professional development opportunities. Teachers and EAs at both of our District Special Education Classes — Elementary Life Skills and Secondary District Skills — spent a full day taking Non-Violent Crisis Intervention training before the students in those programs attended. At times, some of these students pose a physical risk, so training the entire staff before start-up allows the students to get a best start on their educational programs. To better connect with families and address child vulnerability, Learning Services has made community networking a priority for our StrongStart Facilitators. All of the facilitators attended the launch of the Greater Nanaimo Early Years Partnership Working Group meeting. Each of the facilitators has joined a working group, and will be contributing to reducing barriers for families, increasing cultural engagement or influencing public policy. The facilitators will contribute to their working group over the course of the school year. 5) Hand outs: Department of Learning Focus and Goals Learning Initiatives 2014-15 Leadership Series Calendar Learning Services Learning Initiatives Calendar 2014-15 John Blain Deputy Superintendent of Schools
