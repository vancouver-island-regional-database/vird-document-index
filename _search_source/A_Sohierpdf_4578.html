Presentation to SD 68 School Trustees, Nov 4, 2015 by Audrey Sohier The Impact on Traffic and School Parking in Hammond Bay and North Nanaimo area by the closure of Rutherford School. Hammond Bay Road —the route in and out Hammond Bay Road is an extremely busy major road which is the primary route for residents in this area of Nanaimo. Most residents travel Hammond Bay Rd on a daily basis and a large number make many trips a day. City of Nanaimo statistics state that over 10,000 vehicles travel Hammond Bay Rd between Frank Ney and Rutherford Schools on a typical school weekday. Between 8 and 9 am, 850 cars travel between Frank Ney and Rutherford Schools. That is one car every 4 seconds. The traffic count at school pick up time, 3 to 4pm, is 838. Walking to Frank Ney School from Rutherford catchment is not a viable option. Rutherford‘s western catchment boundary to Frank Ney School is 3.8 kilometers. This is a 45 to 60 minute walk for a child, depending on age and physical ability and not 30 minutes as stated by the school district. It's not uncommon for backpacks to be 7 to 10 pounds in weight and up to 10 to 20% of the child's body weight. Plus in the rain factor and you've got one miserable long walk. It is not safe for young children to walk to school along a very busy road while 850 cars speed by them. That's one every 4 seconds. lt’s llegal in B.C. to leave children under the age of 12 years at home alone because it is not safe. Why would we send them down a busy road where they have to cross 5 intersections during peak traffic times and then walk another third of a kilometer down Williamson Road without a sidewalk. Thirdly, it is extremely unhealthy to breathe in car emissions yet alone car emissions from 850 cars twice a day on a daily basis 5 days a week. Numerous health organizations and the B.C. government have stated as such. Health concerns include respiratory issues, cancer, neurological damage. Emissions from all vehicles are harmful but vehicles in our area are no longer tested for emission problems so we conceivably have cars on the road that emit higher emissions than they should. Even ifa parent can accompany a young child to school these harmful effects are still there. Young children, infants, pregnant women, those with asthma or other respiratory issues and those with weakened immune systems are particularly vulnerable. Car emissions make people sick, very, very sick. It's as simple as that. Rutherford parents love their children and are not willing to put them at risk. Many families chose to buy homes near this school so their children could walk to school. They have invested time, money, work, and careful thought in order to make it a home. They have invested in friendships and in the community. If Rutherford School is closed, they will have to leave this all behind at a considerable financial and personal cost in order to be close to another school. I sincerely wish that everyone seated before me were in this position. Perhaps then you would understand the impact of Rutherford closure on these families. DRIVING TO FRANKNEYSCHOOL (traffic counts and space times are fromCity of Nanaimo) Driving to Frank Ney School would require making a left hand turn East onto Hammond Bay Road during peak traffic times with vehicles spaced 4 seconds apart With 134 students from the present Rutherford catchment going to Frank Ney this represents 90 to 100 vehicles going back and forth. Yes, 90 to 100 cars trying to make a left hand turn onto Hammond Bay. Some Hammond Bay residents say that they often wait up to 10 minutes for a break in traffic in order to make a left hand turn. Each weekday from 8 to 9am 850 cars travel Hammond Bay Road. Add another 200 trips (100 cars going back and forth) carrying Rutherford students to Frank Ney and you get 1050 cars on Hammond Bay during this time. That's an increase of more than 23% without factoring in additional traffic from more development. . Therewill be traffic congestion and delays the like of which we have never seen before. There are 4 roads in the present Rutherford Catchment that access Hammond Bay from the north. (Brickyard Rd. Brigantine Dr., Parkway Dr., and Norasea Rd.) If the 100 cars from Rutherford Catchment were equally dispersed on these 4 roads and based on a 10 minute wait per car only 24 cars an hour would be able to turn left onto Hammond Bay. With a five minute wait 48 cars would be able to turn left onto Hammond Bay. While they are waiting there will be other cars behind them waiting to turn right in order to get to work. Remember that the cars travelling on Hammond Bay are 4 seconds apart at this time. It will truly be a traffic nightmare. The frustration of waiting, the need to get children to school on time and to get to work on time will inevitably result in risk taking when making these left hand turns. Accidents will happen. People including children will get hurt. There is only one other way to get to Frank Ney. It is a convoluted route through twisting neighbourhood streets and not designed for this amount of traffic. The neighborhood residents will be on their way to work, or other locations and traffic at that time will already be heavy. This route is not a solution either. Hammond Bay Road will get busier as more development takes place. Closure of Rutherford School would have a huge negative impact on the Hammond Bay community by increasing traffic significantly during peak travel times; resulting in a longer commute time and unsafe driving conditions The risk of vehicle accidents and personal iniurieswill increase. . The city of Nanaimo adopted a transportation master plan in May 2014. It indicates that no improvements are planned between Rutherford Road and Morningside Road east of Frank Ney. Accessibility and Parking at Frank Ney School. Frank Ney School is accessed by one road —Wil|iamson road which is also access for the subdivision. Imagine the chaos of 250 vehicles all arriving at pretty much the same time to drop off children. The drop off area is small and does not accommodate many cars. The small parking lot accommodates only 37 vehicles. Kindergarten and grade 1 students are very young and need to be escorted by an adult to the door of their classroom so this necessitates parking and parking is very limited. There is additional parking on the roadside on Williamson as well as parking by Harry Wipper Park. This provides another 53 spaces. Parking on the roadside is limited by no parking signs near the crosswalk by the trail area, and by a fire hydrant. On Oct 4”‘ at pick up time 74 vehicles were parked in the parking lot and other parking areas, leaving 16 spaces not parked in. If 134 Rutherford students were added to Frank Ney 6 more parking spaces would be required for teachers and 80 spaces for Rutherford parents. Where are they? We don't see them. I would like to know if a traffic study has been done on driving to Frank Ney and the accessibility and parking at the Frank Ney site. Accessibility and parking at Randerson School. Randerson School's parking lot is overcrowded and full. Parents park in front of the garbage bins, in handicapped spaces, to the side ofthe entrance, behind parked cars and in the traffic pathways. On Nelson Road they park in the yellow curb no park zone by the exit and crosswalk , in front of the fire hydrant and all the way down Nelson to Rutherford road. They park on the other side ofthe school on Nelson as well. There is very little room left for additional cars bringing and picking up more students. I submit to you, ladies and gentlemen, our Trustees, that the issues raised here on Traffic and School Parking and Family Disruption make the Proposal not sustainable for the parents, not sustainable for the Children and not sustainable for the general community.
