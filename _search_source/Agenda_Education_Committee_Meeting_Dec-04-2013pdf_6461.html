LEARNING SERVICES DEPARTMENT Report from Deputy Superintendent, John Blain Mr. Blain reported out on the following items: • • • • • • • • • Enrolment Early Learning 23 Meeting with Ministry of Education District Assessment Pilot Response to Intervention Communications BC Education Plan Organization of Classes Human Resources He responded to many questions from Trustees and committee members regarding the various topics. Learn@Home - Kim Hart Ms. Kim Hart, vice-principal at Learn@Home, provided an update on the district’s Learn@Home 8-12 program. She said that last year students were enrolled in 2,700 courses through Learn@Home 8-12. Some students are full-time in the program, while others may take only one or two courses while taking their remaining courses through their catchment school. She said the success rate for Learn@Home 8-12 students last year was 78%. She said the program supports the district’s goals by meeting students’ unique needs. The program is year-round with a flexible pace, and offers quality teaching and ongoing communication with students and parents. Page 4 of 5 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Education Committee Meeting held November 6, 2013 RTI & Assessment - Bob Esliger, Assistant Superintendent Mr. Bob Esliger, Assistant Superintendent and Ms. Darlene Crane, Instructional Coordinator, gave an update on RTI and assessment initiatives to the Committee members. They said Initiatives that are underway this year support the RTI framework in the district. This includes regular communication to provide information to school principals, teachers and parents. They said that the Learning Services Department is focusing on pulling the pieces together, validating the good things that have been happening in schools and providing professional learning opportunities for teachers this year. They said the district has recently adopted a new assessment policy and an assessment pilot program is underway this year. That pilot will be reviewed by a District Assessment Review Committee.
