VIA EMAIL Ref: 185675 January 21, 2016 To: All Secretary-Treasurers Re: 2015/16 Information Updates to Support 2015/16 and 2016/17 budget planning I am writing to provide districts with information on the Economic Stability Dividend, Next Generation Network (NGN) costs, and on reduced Teachers’ Pension Plan costs. This information will assist districts in preparing amended 2015/16 budgets and with general planning for 2016/17. Economic Stability Dividend The intent is that a special operating grant will be provided to districts mid-May 2016 representing the 2015/16 school year portion of the Economic Stability Dividend (ESD), which is effective May 1, 2016. This is to fund the additional increase of 0.45% for unionized provincial public-sector employees who have reached agreements under the government’s Economic Stability Mandate for collective bargaining. The annualized school year ESD will be added to the total public school operating grant for distribution in 2016/17 and future years, and will be confirmed as part of Budget 2016. Next Generation Network Upgrading the Provincial Learning Network (PLNet) to enable student learning was a shared priority for the sector. I am pleased to report that NGN is already providing significant benefits to districts, with over 1100 sites (out of 1650) transitioned to NGN and 80% of students now able to access the new network. As I indicated in my May 1, 2015 letter to all secretary-treasurers, for the 2015/16 school year NGN incremental operating costs will be charged as an expense to districts and adjusted from operating grants. The amount required from each district will be determined based on enrolment. The anticipated 2015/16 NGN costs were approximately $19.6 million. As the project has progressed and costs refined, the costs were reduced to $17.4 million last May. At January 15, 2016, I can advise that the ministry and Telus have worked closely to reduce costs even further for 2015/16 to approximately $13.7 million. As a result of increased enrolment in September 2015, the amount nominally reserved in the holdback for NGNwill be distributed to districts with unanticipated enrolment changes. Therefore, districts will need to ensure NGN charges are included in their amended budgets for 2015/16. Further information and direction regarding NGN charges starting in 2016/17 will be provided with budget instructions for the 2016/17 school year. Teachers’ Pension Plan Your board of education should now have received notification from the Teachers’ Pension Board of Trustees that for employers, the overall teacher pension contribution rate will be reduced by 1.82 per cent effective July 1, 2016, freeing up an estimated provincial total of $45 million annually that may be used to offset other budget pressures starting in 2016/17. More information on this announcement is available at the TPP website. If you have any questions about these allocations, please contact Rebecca John, Director, at Rebecca.John@gov.bc.ca.
