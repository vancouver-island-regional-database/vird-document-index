UNFINISHED BUSINESS Enrolment Update as of September 30th Mr. Turin gave the Business Committee a preliminary report on student enrolment. He said that enrolment as of September 30th totals13,211 full-time-equivalent students. This is 84 fewer students than what was projected in the budget and 56 fewer students than last year. Mr. Turin said that the lower enrolment does not have a financial impact on the district this year, because the district currently receives funding Page 4 of 9 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held October 9, 2013 protection from the Ministry of Education. He said that, using the most current enrolment information and forecasting forward, the district can expect a budget shortfall next year of more than $4.3 million, with the shortfall expected to increase to close to $5.9 million in 2015-16. IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Brennan IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Curley Folio B13/10/09-04 That the Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) that it urge the Ministry of Education to develop an educational funding program that more realistically reflects the needs of BC students. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Update from the Facilities Department Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations and Mr. Dalrymple, Energy Manager, told the Business Committee that the Wellington Secondary seismic upgrade is now underway. Work is now underway on Blocks B and E. In November, the tenders will go out for the work to the other blocks of the school. They said the building envelope programs at Quarterway, Coal Tyee and Bayview are almost completed. The projects included new windows and new stucco. They said that this past summer, the district carried out an equipment revitalization program. In all, 46 appliances were retired, and schools received Energy Star rated appliances. Mr. Sabo distributed an Action Sheet on the feasibility of providing gender-neutral washrooms in schools. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY Master Planning Process for NDSS Site - News Release Mr. Turin said the Board has retained the services of Brook Pooni Associates Inc. and Cornerstone Architecture & Planning Group to undertake the first phases of the master plan process for the NDSS site, including the district facilities located on that site. One of the key initiatives of the 10-Year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan is the proposed rebuilding of NDSS and relocation of all students in Grades 8 to 12 at Woodlands Secondary into the new facility in the fall of 2017. Page 6 of 9 6.1 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held October 9, 2013 The master plan process will include a comprehensive consultation component, including external stakeholders such as the City of Nanaimo; Vancouver Island University; community interest groups; and the general public. Mr. Turin said the NDSS Master Plan process is expected to take 8-9 months to complete and the first two phases is expected to cost about $47,000.
