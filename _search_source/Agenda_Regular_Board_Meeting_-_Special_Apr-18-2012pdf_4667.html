V9R 3K6 Apr 10, 2012 To the Board of Education, I am concerned that the proposed cut in educational days by extending Spring Break an additional 4 days will negatively affect my children’s education within School District 68. I am surprised that the Board of Education is bringing this proposal back to the table this year when it was previously voted down. The results of last year’s online survey were concerning to me. I read most of the comments and it appeared that the majority of the respondents wanting to extend the break just simply wanted an extra week off. Nobody suggested that the extra week off would be a positive benefit to the students education. I hope the Board takes the results of the survey with a grain of salt as I suspect they will be weighted heavily with students voting in favor of the extended break. I can’t see how the proposed extra 32 min a week will be quality instructional time. It’s just that I’m not sure the children are capable of absorbing the additional information each day. More information is being crammed into fewer instructional days. I believe that two weeks is too long to shut the educational system down for. Some children’s minds will have to be restarted and refreshed after such a long break. I can imagine a struggling math student having even more trouble getting back up to speed when classes resume. I think that the core subjects of Math and English have to come first. If you need to make cuts then you shouldn’t be doing it in a place that may affect these core subjects. Most of the children's extra curricular activities like, gymnastics, music and swimming all continue through the extended break. Normally all of these activities only run during the school year and they have the same breaks as SD68. It appears that the rest of the community isn’t on board with a 2 week spring break. A number of parents are already pulling their children out of class for other extended holidays and sick days. Add this to the amount of non instructional days in a year and I don’t see how we can afford to shorten the school year even further. I am also concerned that there are many low income families that will have to bear additional cost for childcare for the extended break. Hopefully the board thinks about all of these factors when making the decision regarding altering the school calendar. We are not in support of the proposed change. Regards, Sandy and Colin Bollinger 5443 Mildmay Rd Nanaimo BC Arlie J. Clegg, J. Sheridan and C. Local School Calendar Consultation Page 5 of 11 Local School Calendar Consultation School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 2012-13 Local School Calendar Survey Results As part of the consultation process for the proposed Local School Calendar for 2012-13, the district conducted an online survey from March 8 to April 12. The survey asked respondents to identify whether they were parents, students, employees or community members and then asked whether the respondents were in favour of the proposed Local School Calendar. Respondents were also given an opportunity to submit written comments. More than 2,500 responses were received. The results are outlined in the tables and charts below. It should be noted that while online surveys such as this can give a clear indication of people’s opinions, the results cannot be considered to be statistically valid for two reasons:  The survey can be answered by anyone who wishes to do so, and as a result the sample group for the survey does not meet the standards for statistical validity. It is possible for an individual to submit more than one response; however, we are able to determine if an individual has responded numerous times from the same computer, and have removed those responses from the statistics below.  Staff has noticed with this survey and past surveys that the pattern of responses is usually set after the first few hundred responses, and the results do not vary a great deal after that pattern is set. This gives us confidence that the survey results are an accurate reflection of the opinions of those individuals who chose to respond to the survey.
