NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS REGULAR BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: April 22, 2026 Board of Education Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Election By-Law and Appointment of Election Officers Recommendation That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) adopt amended Trustee Elections Bylaw No. 2. That the Board of Education of School District No. 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) pursuant to Section 58(1) and (2) of the Local Government Act, appoint Secretary-Treasurer Mark Walsh as the Chief Electoral Oﬃcer for conducting the 2026 General Local Election with the power to appoint other election oﬃcials as required for the administration and conduct of the 2026 General Local Election and that Karen Matthews be appointed as Deputy Chief Election Oﬃcer for the 2026 General Local Election with the power to appoint other election oﬃcials as required for the administration and conduct of the 2026 General Local Election in the absence of the Chief Election Oﬃcer. Background This action sheet outlines two recommended actions to ensure compliance with legislative requirements related to the next Board of Education election to occur in October of 2026. Discussion By-Law The Board has already passed two readings of the draft By-law and put the draft out for consultation. It should be noted that there was no feedback. The draft was based on the template provided by BCSTA and previous experience in the district. It is drafted in a manner that meets legislative changes and also ensures flexibility in the event of a byelection and will minimize the costs to the district while still ensuring wide access to voting. Appointment of Officers The Board must appoint election officials in advance of the election. The last three elections in the district (the general election and two by-elections) have been conducted by Mark Walsh and Karen Mathews in the recommended roles. The recommendation is intended to leverage that experience as well as save on costs. Action Sheet: Election By-Law and Appointment Page 2 Their respective experience is as follows: Chief Electoral Officer – Mark Walsh, J.D. has served as Chief Electoral Officer three times in SD68 including two by-elections. He also served as the Chief Electoral Officer in SD27’s most recent byelection in 2025. He also served as Deputy Chief Electoral Officer in SD61 while employed there. Deputy Chief Electoral Officer – Karen Mathews has served as Deputy Chief Electoral Officer five times in SD68 including two by-elections and was the Deputy Chief Electoral Officer in SD27’s by-election in 2025.
