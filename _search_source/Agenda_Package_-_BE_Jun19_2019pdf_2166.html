The primary purpose of the strategic plan is to create a blueprint for the district that provides direction to the system, maintains its exemplary qualities and supports the continuous improvement of student learning. An updated Strategic Plan serves to organize and ground the district around a few significant goals and ensures that the necessary resources are in place to successfully implement the goals in a manner that is sustainable over time. The Strategic Plan assists the Superintendent in establishing an Operational Plan that will include strategies and action items aligned to the district’s overall direction. The Operational plan will be put in place by September for the start of the school year and will be periodically reported out to the Board of Education and shared with the public to demonstrate progress against the goals and objectives that are outlined in this plan. In essence, the Operational Plan operationalizes and implements the strategic direction set by the Board of Education through its Strategic Plan. The plan was developed by completing an Environmental Scan, SWOT analysis and extensive discussion with Trustees and Senior Staff, including a number of strategic planning workshops, during the period from January – April 2019. In addition, public input was sought through a Board hosted Open House and Thought Exchange survey. The Board of Education has committed publicly to releasing the draft Strategic Plan for public feedback before finalizing the plan. Board policy changes are consequently required to remove references to the previous Board’s strategic plan; to establish governance expectations through the establishment of a new Strategic Plan Policy; and to clarify the Roles and Responsibilities of the Board of Education and the Superintendent to develop, implement and monitor the Strategic Plan. This strategic plan encompasses the vision and values of the Syeyutsus Reconciliation Framework. The framework will be used as an overarching, foundational principle that will guide the district throughout the life of the plan, irrespective of changes to the plan’s goals and objectives. BACKGROUND: The Five-Year Capital Plan Each year, Boards of Education are required to submit a Five-Year Capital Plan detailing the high priority capital projects needed for their school districts, in accordance with the Capital Plan instructions. School districts’ annual Five-Year Capital Plan submissions are used to inform the selection of priority capital projects for the Ministry’s following fiscal year. They also provide the Ministry insight into future year priorities that are used in longer term capital planning. The Ministry is seeking submissions for consideration for the following capital programs:          Seismic Mitigation Program (SMP) School Expansion Program (EXP) School Replacement Program (REP) Rural districts program (RDP) Building Envelope Program (BEP) School Enhancement Program (SEP) Carbon Neutral Capital Program (CNCP) Bus Replacement Program (BUS) Playground Enhancement Program (PEP) Page 41 of 130 The Annual Five-Year Capital Plan Submission is informed by a Long Range Facilities Plan (LRFP), with school districts assessing capital needs over the next five years and involves prioritizing capital needs and undertaking preliminary assessment of project needs, options, scoping and costing, as well as developing and submitting a Five-Year Capital Plan along with supporting assessment documents, specifically a Project Request Fact Sheet (PRFS). The Ministry will provide each school district a written response to their annual Five-Year Capital Plan submission once the assessment of all submissions are complete and funding for fiscal year 2019-20 is announced by the Ministry. See attached response letter to the 19-20 Capital submission. This written response identifies the next steps for supported projects which may include:     Proceed to design, tender and construction Proceed to development of a Project Definition Report (PDR) Proceed to acquire site Proceed to acquire bus The Ministry also provides school districts with an Annual Facility Grant (AFG) based on an established formula. The AFG is managed separately from the annual Five-Year Capital Plan submissions. As such, AFG is excluded from these Capital Plan instructions. DISCUSSION: Operating Capacity: Previously, class sizes for Kindergarten, Grades 1-7, and Grades 8-12 were set in legislation, and were mandatorily applied to all school districts across the province. Currently, class sizes are negotiated as a working condition for teachers in their local contract with a board of education. As such, operating capacities vary between school districts. Individual school districts must determine the operating capacities of existing schools in order to calculate their capacity utilization. Rural District Program: The intention of the RDP is to target funding for specific types of projects that would directly benefit rural communities but are typically not included under the Ministry’s Major Capital Program or Minor Capital Program. Specifically, RDP will provide funding for the full and partial demolition of board-owned buildings, and for capital projects associated with the consolidation of under-utilized schools. The Ministry may give a higher priority to the full demolition of a surplus board-owned building encumbering a school site that is the subject of a Crown land grant, followed by buildings situated on a property owned outright by the board, and then the partial demolition of buildings that will continue to be operational. Development of a Facilities Plan: The District has a Strategic Education Plan (SEP), a 10-year Enhanced Facilities for Learning Plan, Becoming Sustainable: Facilities Plan Update - September 2, 2015 (latest update April 25, 2018). The District’s plan is equivalent to the Ministry’s LRFP and in addition is integrated with a Strategic direction for district operating, Asset Management planning and strategic partnership development resulting in a targeted 5 year plan based on the development of strategic initiatives.
