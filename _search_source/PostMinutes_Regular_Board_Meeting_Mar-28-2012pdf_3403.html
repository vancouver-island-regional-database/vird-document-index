UNFINISHED BUSINESS Custodial Review Report Cascade Facilities Management Consultants presented the results of a review they conducted of the school district’s custodial services. The district hired the consultants to prepare the review to gain an independent perspective of the service levels, staffing and organization of custodial services. As part of the review, the consultants compared SD68 to six other benchmark districts; received input from departments, schools and CUPE; and made on-site inspections of six schools. They analyzed current custodial staffing and workload, evaluated procedures and reviewed supervision of custodial staff. The report makes a number of recommendations related to department management, organization, staffing and practices. Sproston Steve Sproston, Information Systems Manager, provided an update on the district’s technology infrastructure and projects that are currently underway. He said that In proceeding with the IBM Strategic Road Map, we not only bring SD68 up to date in education technology, but we can, over time, also see a return on the investment in reduced paper, books and other learning resource expenditures. The district is currently working on the network infrastructure as the first steps in the implementation of the plan. Financial Update as of February 29th for the 2011-2012 Budget Year Mr. Turin reported that the school district’s finances for the year are on target and a surplus of $1.7 million is expected at June 30. Mr. Turin reported on several highlights from the Ministry of Education 2012-13 Funding Grant announced this week: • The district will not receive funding for the new category of vulnerable students; however, will continue to receive $2.1 million in Community Link funding. • The allocation of funds for transportation is decreasing by $180,193; however, the district will be protected in decreasing amounts for the next two years. • There is a new funding category called Supplement for the Education Plan in the amount of $270,804 for this year. • Funding protection in the amount of $4 million has been received and is $1.7 million less than funds generated by the previous formula. Mr. Turin recommended several changes to the current budget schedule. Details will be announced once approved by the Board.
