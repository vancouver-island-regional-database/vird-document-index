NANAIMO, BC: Vancouver Island University’s (VIU’s) student-led climate change initiative, Awareness of Climate Change through Education and Research (ACER), is hosting its sixth annual public symposium on Saturday, May 11. The symposium, called Local Solutions to a Global Problem, will start with an update on the science of climate change from Kristy Faccer, Program Manager for the University of Victoria’s Pacific Institute for Climate Solutions (PICS), followed by presentations from Paul Manson, President, DC Grid Development Corporation; Eric Smiley, Viridian Energy; Matthew Beck, Chair VIU HVAC Program; and Rob Lawrence, City of Nanaimo Environmental Planner. The International Panel on Climate Change reports that reducing our reliance on fossil fuels for energy production is the most cost-effective mitigation technique we can implement. This year the ACER symposium will focus on local efforts being made to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and include information tables from vendors and community groups. ACER is VIU public outreach initiative that aims to promote a greater understanding of the science and social implications of climate change. It is an inter-disciplinary project rooted in the belief that making informed decisions on the issue of climate change requires greater public engagement and a deeper understanding of this complex topic. Climate change is a global problem, but one that requires different regional strategies that make use of resources available in different areas. Information In early August of 2019, the Ministry of Health announced a new health fee for international students. Effective September 1, 2019, international students are assessed a health fee of $37.50 per month. The fee will increase to $75 per month on January 1, 2020, when the Medical Services Plan (MSP) premiums for BC residents is eliminated. The new fee applies to all international students, including K-12 students, who have an immigration status as “Student” and a Study Permit with Health Insurance BC (HIBC). The government stated that school districts are not expected to collect or process the fee for eligible students enrolled in public schools; rather, the fee would be issued by Revenue Canada to the address on file with the MSP system. However, NLPS has collected health insurance fees and for various reasons, including legal considerations and practical operational issues, the department will continue to do so. The introduction of the International Student Health Fee ensures that international students continue to have access to health services as the government eliminates MSP premiums for BC residents. The provincial announcement aligns the Board’s goal of safe, caring and healthy learning and working environment that is inclusive of the diversity of our entire learning community. It is essential that our international students enjoy the educational programming in Nanaimo with the same health benefits afforded other students. Page 60 of 63 Background The Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools International Student Education was established in 1998. Since 2004, the District has been purchasing a private comprehensive health insurance package that includes all MSP services plus a host of additional health benefits, for its international students. During this period, the District has not enrolled students in MSP, which was supported by a legal opinion issued through BCSTA in 2012. As a prudent District, Staff had, just prior to the announcement, asked BCSTA for an updated legal opinion which confirmed the 2012 opinion – that international students do not need to be enrolled in MSP. Understandably, the announcement produced a flurry of activity in the school community around two MSP issues 1) enrollment and 2) fees. Some of the questions being raised by various associations including BCASBO, BCSTA and IPSEA, were specifically around mandatory MSP enrollment for eligible international students. NLPS staff sought an independent legal opinion which in summary, advised that international students should be enrolled in MSP and should also have private insurance for coverage during times and for claims that MSP coverage would not apply. Around the same time, BCSTA sought another legal opinion which supported the requirement to enroll all minor international students in MSP. It should be noted that public school districts across the province have varying practices in respect of health insurance for international students. Some districts exclusively use MSP, others use exclusive private health insurance and some enroll international students in both MSP and private health insurance. Further, the announcement was unexpected by many school districts and, no consultation had taken place with members of the International Public Schools community. Thousands of students had already been enrolled in BC schools based on existing fee structures and legal agreements. The International Public Schools Education Association of British Columbia (IPSEA), wrote a letter on August 9, to the Ministers of Health, Finance, Education, and Advanced Education. The letter outlined the associations concerns about the immediate implementation of new MSP Fees and requested a delay in implementation until September 2020. That request was denied. In September, the Ministry of Education made the following statements for clarity on this matter:    Under the Medicare Protections Act and Regulations, anyone living in BC for six months or longer must enroll in the Medical Services Plan. This includes international students who meet the eligibility requirements. There has been no change to his requirement, which was in place prior to the introduction of the new health fee. A new health fee for international students has been announced by the Ministry of Health. Beginning September 1, 2019, international students with a valid study permit of six or more months on file with Health Insurance BC, and who have active MSP coverage will be assessed a health fee of $37.50 per month. This fee will increase to $75 per month on January 1, 2020, when MSP premiums are eliminated. Provisions in the Canada Health Act and the Medicare Protection Act ensure MSP coverage and access to care continues while enrolled and eligible, which is not connected to the payment and status of the health fee. In conclusion, the District serves as the custodian for most of its international students and as a prudent parent, the district will now register all eligible international students in MSP and in private Page 61 of 63 insurance as noted below. For example, minor students on a study permit of 6 months or more are required to enroll in MSP after the three month waiting period; December 1, 2019. Multi-year students currently attending NLPS will be enrolled effective September 1, 2019 (where applicable). Previously, NLPS international students were enrolled exclusively with a private insurance carrier that provided exceptional coverage (services normally covered by MSP and additional benefits) with coverage taking effect immediately. In light of the directives from government, District Principal Hutchins has negotiated two plans at reasonable rates with this private carrier, Guard.Me. Guard.Me International Insurance provides several plan options. As noted, for the last several years the ISE Department has enrolled all students in guard.me@school. The plan provides comprehensive coverage that includes services normally covered by MSP and additional benefits. The other plan, guard.me@bcschool, provides for secondary coverage i.e. services not covered by MSP and, is therefore used in conjunction with MSP.
