3137 Barons Road Nanaimo, BC V9T 5W5 p: f: e: w: 250 756-1237 250 756-0188 ndta@shaw.ca www.ndta68.ca March 31, 2014 Dot Neary Chairperson, Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Ave Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 Dear Ms Neary: Re: Implementation of the Collective Agreement (Article 20.1.3 Recommendations of the Sub-Committee of the TTLC) As you are aware the BC Supreme Court, in its decision dated January 27, 2014, returned working conditions clauses to the Collective Agreement. The purpose of this letter is to draw your attention to specific articles that existed prior to 2002 in order to assist the Board to be in compliance with the Collective Agreement and to facilitate a smooth start to the 2014-15 school year. While the government is appealing the decision of the court, the Board should prepare for the possibility that the appeal could be denied and that the working conditions provisions will then become part of the Collective Agreement. Since the Collective Agreement was renumbered, we will use the numbering as it existed in the 2001-2004 Collective Agreement. The renumbering took place at different times in different locals. Sometimes the reference to working conditions is Article 20 (old) and sometimes the reference is Article D (new). Article 20.1.3 of the Collective Agreement states that: To ensure a fair and equitable distribution of available educational resources within the District, it is agreed that the recommendations of the Sub-Committee of the Trustee-Teacher Liaison Committee referred to in Article 19.2 shall be considered by the Board prior to the implementation of the staff allocation formula for the District. As this Sub-Committee has continued to meet in accordance with the Collective Agreement over the past 12 years, the NDTA now expects that the role of the Sub-Committee include the recommendations as outlined in Article 20.1.3 above. Sincerely, Mike Ball President, NDTA pc.
