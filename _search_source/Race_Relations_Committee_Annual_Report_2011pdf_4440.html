MULTICULTURAL AND RACE RELATIIONS COMMITTEE ANNUAL REPORT TO THE BOARD JUNE 22, 2011 The Multicultural and Race Relations Committee is pleased to report another successful year working to support diversity in School District #68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith). The Committee met in the months of October and November, 2010, and January, February, March, April and May, 2011. Attendees included representatives from stakeholders, parents, students, Central Vancouver Island Multicultural Society and trustees Donna Allen, Chair and Andrea Bonkowski, alternate. The goals developed for the school year were: • • • • Continue to recruit and support student participants continue to work in partnership with CVIMS continue to work with and seek involvement of the Aboriginal community continue to identify and develop action plans for cross-cultural issues ACCOMPLISHMENTS • supported students by nominating the Ladysmith Secondary School STAAR group for the Diversity Championship award which they won (Goal #1) • March meeting -met with NDSS students at their school. The students have great energy and we heard about their projects and suggested ideas for their needs. (Goal #1) • Joel Canfield (CUPE) presented the students and the teacher sponsor with Martin Luther King pins (Goal #1) • April meeting – met with John Barsby students at their school. Once again the students had great energy. The students were all members of the GSA. Those who had been more involved in race relations activities were not available that day. (Goal #1) • Joel Canfield (CUPE) gave the students bookmarks (Stop Homophobia in Schools) and Martin Luther King pins (Goal #1) • The Committee continues to support the Pleasant Valley Clan Initiative which has been a huge success. This year we provided funding for the Clan Mascots. (Goal #1, 3) • Overview of Multicultural and Race Relations Committee write-up for the www.diversity.org website. (Goal #2) • Supported CVIMS on a number of initiatives. Purchased calendars. (Goal #3,4) Page 2 The committee has noted the following: • that students seem to be very interested in supporting global issues as a form of race relations support rather than local issues, • there is a great deal of interest on the part of students in the area of Gay-Straight Alliances instead of STAAR., FUTURE INITIATIVES • review of the Multicultural Policy and Procedure 3808 Thank you to all committee members who continue to support and enhance SD#68 (NanaimoLadysmith) Multicultural and Race Relations Committee.
