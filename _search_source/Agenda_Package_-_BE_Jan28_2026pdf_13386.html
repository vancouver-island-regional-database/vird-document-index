Discussion An SSAC is a development charge that is assessed on developments that create new residential units to ensure there are funds available for amenity needs as neighbourhoods develop. The SSAC is intended to represent approximately 1/3rd the cost of the acquisition of green field (undeveloped land) or brown field (previously developed adjacent to schools) land for expansions. In our district we continue to anticipate the need for a future elementary school to the south of the highway in Lantzville as well as potential expansions at a number of sites dependent on in fill growth. We note that growth has slowed in the district, however, SSACs are intended to be long term in nature. Our district has a balance of $687,290.86. The majority of these funds have been collected in the City of Nanaimo. Moreover, we have split the fund between the north $635,629.99 and the south $51,660.87. We note that in the south we do not project an immediate need for a school site. However, a district cannot apply an SSAC to only a part of a district unless there is a significant geographic separation between communities – which is not the case here. As previously noted, we remain alert for strategic acquisitions to support future growth, but no acquisitions have occurred since the expansion of the Bayview site in 2023. Appendix A: GHG Reduc�on Strategy Report Page 12 of 17 Appendix A NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS MEMORANDUM DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: December 12, 2025 Environment Committee Mary Zuccaro, Energy Manager and Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Reduction Strategy – Buildings Only Purpose To outline practical options for reducing greenhouse gas emissions from our school district buildings and explain what it will take to meet Provincial and Board targets. Why This Matters • CleanBC Goal: Reduce building emissions by 50% below 2010 levels by 2030. • Board Goal: Reduce emissions by 4.5% every year. • These targets are ambitious and will require significant investment and planning What We’ve Done So Far • Optimized building control systems (heating, ventilation) through a Continuous Optimization Program (COP) – already saved 9% in gas usage at 13 sites. Funded through AFG. • Detailed mechanical studies at all sites to identify upgrade opportunities. 100% funded by BC Hydro. • Life Cycle Cost Analysis to understand costs and savings for major retrofits Key Findings • Doing nothing beyond current efforts will not meet any targets. • To meet CleanBC’s 2030 goal, we need to spend about $9.5 million by 2030 (roughly $2.4M per year). • To meet the Board’s goal, we need about $27 million by 2035 and ongoing annual spending. • Full electrification (switching from gas to electric boilers) would cost $24 million and increase operating costs (about $30K per year per elementary school). • Even with electrification, meeting both goals long-term is challenging.
