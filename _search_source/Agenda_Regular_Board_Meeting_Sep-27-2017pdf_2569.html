Burgos, Executive Assistant Meeting No. R13 CALL TO ORDER The Chair called the meeting to order at 6:00 pm and recognized that tonight’s meeting is being held on the traditional territory of the Snuneymuxw people. Today, Canada celebrates National Aboriginal Day. This is a day for all Canadians to recognize and celebrate unique heritage, diverse cultures and outstanding contributions of First Nations, Inuit and Metis peoples. The Canadian Constitution recognizes these three groups as Indigenous Peoples. Although these groups share many similarities, they each have their own distinct heritage, language, cultural practices and spiritual beliefs. In cooperation with Indigenous organizations, the Government of Canada chose June 21, the summer solstice, for National Aboriginal Day. For generations, many Indigenous Peoples and communities have celebrated their culture and heritage on or near this day due to the significance of the summer solstice as the longest day of the year. The workshop by Kerri Isham helped parents who want to have healthy, sex-positive, open and honest communications with children about sex education & included topics on puberty, hygiene, healthy relationships, STD’s, sexting, gender & sexual orientation. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  No-report Ecole North Oyster  Play Space Update– In 2013, North Oyster Elementary amalgamated with Ecole Davis Road to become Ecole North Oyster. The school population went from 80 to over 300 students. Existing play equipment was inadequate and dangerous. Parents formed a committee in an effort to fund-raise and build a new play space. Three years have passed and though progress has been made (plans have been submitted to the facilities department, fund-raising is on-going and SD68 budgeted $15k in support of the project) but the fact remains that kids are still without playground equipment and will be for some time. McGirr recently completed the first inclusive & accessible playground in the district after raising more than $400k from community partners. It took them over 4 years to complete this project http://www.sd68.bc.ca/wp-content/uploads/McGirr-Inclusive-Playground-News-Release.pdf I am reminded of the time that Fairview Elementary won the “library lotto” $60k prize from Indigo Love of Reading fund - http://www.nanaimobulletin.com/news/nanaimo-elementaryschool-wins-library-lottery/ Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Working Together as One / Nutsumaat Syaays– On a more positive note … I have reported on this project before, but because it’s Orange Shirt day and because this school board has made “Reconciliation” one of their strategic board goals, it is worth revisiting. The goal of this project is to work with Stz’uminus First Nation master carver John Marston, to help weave aboriginal understandings into the Ladysmith Secondary School community. This represents, I think, a change in how our society views and works with our native aboriginal peoples and is a first step toward reconciliation. It is a powerful and poignant journey for everyone involved with the project and I’d like to share with you the video, which does a better job narrating the story than I. Regardless, this project is a worthy one and I think something that the district should be very proud of and share its story and success with other districts and with the Ministry of Education. In short – I think we’re doing some really great things here and those involved should be applauded and proud of the good work they are doing.
