APPROVAL OF THE AGENDA IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Curley IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Bard Folio S13/03/13-01 That the Agenda be approved. CARRIED UNANIMOUSLY NEW BUSINESS Presentations - Calendar Consultation Chair Brennan said the purpose of tonight’s meeting was to receive feedback from the public regarding the calendar proposals for the 20132014 school year. The three proposals related to the calendar for next school year are: • Schedule two Success for All Days – one on the first day of school and the other at the end of the first semester. • Start school an hour later on Wednesdays to allow for school staffs to meet as professional learning communities (PLCs). • Extend Spring Break for an additional week, for a budget savings of about $400,000. • Kelly Dunaway, president of the Canadian Union of Public Employees Local 606, said that Minutes of the Special Board Meeting held March 13, 2013 the union does not support the proposed calendar changes and said they will have a negative impact on a number of employees, including education assistants. • NDTA president Justin Green said that while it is worthwhile to have collaboration time such as the Success for All Days, the union has concerns about the cost and time involved. He also said that teachers support and engage in PLCs but cannot support a model which negatively impacts students and their families. Finally, he said that the extra week of spring break will be a hardship for TTOCs and asked the Board to look at its spending priorities and find savings in other areas. • Assistant Superintendent Bob Esliger and Instructional Coordinators Darlene Crane and DJ Thompson outlined the critical need that educators have for collaboration time in order to address the goal to raise achievement levels for all learners, and to close the gap for struggling learners. They said that educators in the district need the opportunity to meet as professional learning communities to have focused conversations about the learning of all students. • Nicole Hewitt presented a signed petition from Park Avenue parents opposing the changes. She stated that parents would pay an extra $575/week for the extended spring break and would have to miss work to adjust to the PLC Wednesday morning. QUESTION PERIOD There were no questions this evening. ADJOURNMENT IT WAS MOVED BY Trustee Curley IT WAS SECONDED BY Trustee Howland Folio S13/03/13-02 That the meeting be adjourned.
