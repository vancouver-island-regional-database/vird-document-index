MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING MADE EFFECTIVE as of this ____ day of March, 2014. BETWEEN: VANCOUVER ISLAND UNIVERSITY (“VIU”) AND: SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) (the “School District”) BACKGROUND Whereas VIU and the School District desire to work together on initiatives of mutual interest and are committed to:    open communication between the parties; a collaborative approach where there is mutual interest; and short and long-term planning that best meets community and educational needs. The purpose of this Memorandum of Understanding (“MOU”) is to establish a foundation for collaborative exchange and cooperation between VIU and the School District in areas of mutual benefit and interest. GOALS It is the intent of the parties to:      Establish and maintain a long-term relationship based upon effective communication and collaboration; Promote and encourage open and constructive dialogue based on mutual trust, honesty, respect and understanding; Determine areas that may be of mutual interest to the parties; Develop a constructive process for meaningful collaboration on areas of mutual interest; and Promote understanding of each other’s governing structures, capacities, priorities, roles, responsibilities and current projects. 1 ESTABLISHMENT OF THE SENIOR WORKING COMMITTEE The parties agree to establish a senior working committee (the “Working Committee”) comprised of senior members of both parties to be appointed by the parties after the execution of this MOU. The Working Committee will provide a forum for the parties to communicate on a regular basis about potential areas of mutual interest and collaboration. The Working Committee will meet at least quarterly and more frequently if desired by the parties. It will not be a decision-making body and will not have any independent authority to make commitments or establish priorities for either party. The Working Committee will maintain records regarding the matters discussed by the Working Committee including any areas identified by the Working Committee as potential areas of mutual interest and collaboration. These records will be made available to the School District’s Superintendent and VIU’s President on a regular basis. COLLABORATION PROCESS The parties agree to adopt a collaboration process that will include the following:      Appoint the members of the Working Committee and agree on meeting logistics, timing, ground rules and protocols; At the Working Committee level: o Identify and share perspectives; o Educate each other – reasons, needs, concerns, priorities and motivations of the parties; o Identify interests and the potential areas of mutual interest; o Define the present situation and desired future situation for areas of mutual interest; o Specify information needs and identify information that is available and information that is needed; o Identify potential stakeholders and interests at stake; o Generate options by brainstorming; o Develop criteria for evaluating options; o Evaluate options; and o Reach agreement within the Working Committee on areas of mutual interest and options to be brought forward to each party for further review and the development of a plan of action, if appropriate; The School District’s Superintendent and VIU’s President will be the primary liaison with the Working Committee on behalf of the parties. The records of the Working Committee will be made available to them, as required, and they will be consulted when and if the Working Committee reaches agreement on areas of mutual interest and options and seeks direction on further steps; and Each of the Parties will follow its own internal procedures to review any proposed areas of mutual interest and potential plans. School District Trustees will meet annually with VIU Senior Administration to discuss areas of common interest and potential collaboration. FINANCIAL CONSIDERATIONS Each party will pay all of its own costs and expenses concerning all activities and matters under this MOU. This MOU does not impose any financial liability or responsibility on either party with respect to the costs or expenses of the other. 2 CONFIDENTIAL AND PROPRIETARY INFORMATION Each party is responsible for identifying if information is confidential or proprietary when it is disclosed to the other party. The receiving party shall maintain such information in confidence and not disclose it to any third party or use it for any unauthorized purpose. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the parties acknowledge and agree that it is essential that the discussions undertaken by the parties pursuant to this MOU including, without limitation, the meetings of, and matters discussed in, the Working Committee, be kept confidential. PUBLICITY All publications and publicity, regardless of media, with respect to this MOU including, without limitation, any discussions of the parties and the Working Committee pursuant to this MOU, and any documents that will become part of the public record, require the express prior written consent of both parties in advance of the information being issued, released or disseminated. RELATIONSHIP The parties are entering into this MOU as independent entities. Nothing in this MOU constitutes any party to be an agent or partner of the other party in any respect and neither party will have any authority whatsoever with respect to the property or business of the other party. This MOU does not preclude either party from seeking or negotiating other similar arrangements with other entities. LICENSING No license or conveyance of any rights to either party under any business contracts, copyrights, or other form of intellectual property is granted or implied by the exchange of any Information between the parties. TERM, RENEWAL, TERMINATION AND AMENDMENT This MOU is effective from the date written above and will continue until March 26, 2019 unless terminated by either party upon 30 days’ written notice to the other. This MOU shall only be amended in writing upon mutual consent. LEGAL EFFECT This MOU specifies general processes for information exchange and cooperation and merely sets out the general basis upon which the parties intend to proceed. Except for the sections titled Financial Considerations, Confidential and Proprietary Information, and Publicity, this MOU is a non-binding agreement between the parties and does not commit the parties to enter into any binding or legal arrangements. The sections titled Financial Considerations, Confidential and Proprietary Information, and Publicity will each be binding upon the parties and will be governed as to their interpretation and effect by the law of the Province of British Columbia, Canada.
