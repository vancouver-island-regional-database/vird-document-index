SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 7001 Role of the Superintendent The Board recognizes the need for one person to be in charge of the management of the District. Therefore, the Board designates the Superintendent as the Chief Executive Officer of the Board and the Chief Education Officer of the District and delegates to the Superintendent responsibility for overall administration of the District. The Superintendent is accountable to the Board for carrying out its objectives and policies for implementing functions as specified in the School Act and Regulations. The Superintendent is also responsible for providing leadership and coordination for the development of educational, administrative, instructional and support services. The Superintendent shall endeavor at all times to improve the standard of instruction throughout the schools of the Nanaimo-Ladysmith School District. The Superintendent makes reports which focus on governance implications and is accountable to the corporate Board for the conduct and operation of the District, and for ensuring compliance with legislative requirements. All Board authority delegated to the staff of the District is delegated through the Superintendent. Specific Areas of Responsibility 1. Educational Leadership The Superintendent shall: 1.1 1.2 1.3 Provide leadership in all matters relating to education in the District. Implement directions established by the Ministry and make the Board aware of any instances where this direction is not consistent with Board direction.
