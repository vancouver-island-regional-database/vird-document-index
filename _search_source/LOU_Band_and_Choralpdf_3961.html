3137 Barons Road Nanaimo, BC V9T 5W5 p: f: e: w: 250 756-1237 250 756-0188 ndta@shaw.ca www.ndta68.ca March 31, 2014 Dot Neary, Chairperson, Board of Education School District 68 (Nanaimo-Ladysmith) 395 Wakesiah Ave Nanaimo, BC V9R 3K6 Dear Ms Neary: Re: Implementation of the Collective Agreement (Letter of Understanding: Hours of Instruction Band/ Choral Classes) As you are aware the BC Supreme Court, in its decision dated January 27, 2014, returned working conditions clauses to the Collective Agreement. The purpose of this letter is to draw your attention to specific articles that existed prior to 2002 in order to assist the Board to be in compliance with the Collective Agreement and to facilitate a smooth start to the 2014-15 school year. While the government is appealing the decision of the court, the Board should prepare for the possibility that the appeal could be denied and that the working conditions provisions will then become part of the Collective Agreement. Since the Collective Agreement was renumbered, we will use the numbering as it existed in the 2001-2004 Collective Agreement. The renumbering took place at different times in different locals. Sometimes the reference to working conditions is Article 20 (old) and sometimes the reference is Article D (new). Please note the following Letter of Understanding: Hours of Instruction Band/Choral Classes (1991): Without prejudice or precedent to the period of time subsequent to the expiration of the 1990-1992 Agreement, the Board and Association agrees that application of Article D.26.3 of the Agreement is not intended to restrict the timetabling of, or assignment of a teacher to, band/choral classes either immediately prior to or immediately following the regular timetable, subject to the following conditions: 1. the band/choral teacher is in agreement; 2. SUPPLEMENTAL STAFFING WILL BE PROVIDED TO THE SCHOOL TO OFFSET POSSIBLE CLASS SIZE IMPACT WITHIN THE REGULAR TIMETABLE; 3. the band/choral teacher will not be assigned an instructional day in excess of 6.5 consecutive hours or an instructional week in excess of 32.5 hours. You will note in para 2 that the Board is required to provide supplemental staffing to a school when a Band/Choral block is outside the regular timetable. This is necessary in order to offset any class size impact within the regular timetable. Currently, the Board does not provide this supplemental staffing to schools. It is the expectation of the NDTA that the Board will plan for and be in compliance with this Letter of Understanding for the 2014-15 school year. Sincerely, Mike Ball President, NDTA pc.
