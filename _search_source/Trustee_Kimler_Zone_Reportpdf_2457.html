Trustee Kimler Zone Report 25-Apr-2018 Ladysmith Zone Ladysmith Secondary School (LSS)  Elder Project Book Launch - Land & Language-Based Learning students interviewed Aboriginal Elders – learning from their experience and hearing about old ways. The project culminated in a book of poetry “The Rivers Where We Sing”, which launched on April 4th at a poetry reading (in conjunction with NDSS students) at the Port Theatre. A PDF version of the book can be found online - http://www.sd68.bc.ca/wp-content/uploads/The-Rivers-Where-We-Sing-LSS.pdf  Vancouver Island Concert Festival - Ladysmith Secondary hosted this festival on April 11-12. Bands from over 30 schools from across the Island participated. The LSS Band had an excellent performance and received an invitation to MusicFest Nationals.  Student Art Gallery Showing - The Waterfront Gallery in Ladysmith hosted a Student Show from April 4th-13th. Many of our students enrolled in Visual Arts had pieces displayed and purchased. Ladysmith Primary School (LPS)  Elemental Run – LPS is preparing for their biggest PAC fundraiser of the year. This 5K walk/run will be held on Saturday, May 12th at 10:00 am.  Spring Activities – LPS students are busy with Swimming Lessons, Environment Week and Fine Arts Week. It is great to see the learning that comes from kids composing and creating a collaborative art project based on the kids book, “Only One You”, Linda Kranz Video: https://youtu.be/989O0O_xc3o École North Oyster (ENO)  The Lorax- Nicole Lewis’ grade 6/7 French Immersion class presented the play “The Lorax” (which contained a positive, pro-environmental “Earth Day” message).  Jump Rope for Heart – The whole school participated in this fun activity that got everyone jumping rope as part of a larger community fundraising effort that benefits the Heart and Stroke Foundation of Canada. Ladysmith Intermediate School (LIS)  Junior Achievement Business Program – Grade 6 classes are involved in this program that focussed on business education. Students are creating a business plan and will be holding an entrepreneurial sale on May 25th to sell their homemade products made from recycled objects.  Earth Day – LIS held cross grade environmental activities for Earth Day. The activities ranged from Recycling Sculpture building, planting wild flowers, outside scavenger hunt, Kahoot (Chromebook environmental trivia game), to writing school-wide Earth Day promises.  DARE & Being TRUE – Grade 5 students are participating in the DARE (Drug & Alcohol Reistance Education) program, while grade 6 students are participating in the “Being TRUE” program (a marijuana & alcohol prevention program that acts as a bridge between DARE 5 & DARE 7). Grade 7 students are participating in the “Am I Good Enough” presentation that helps students transition into high school by promoting discussion about social & self-esteem issues). Trustee Kimler
