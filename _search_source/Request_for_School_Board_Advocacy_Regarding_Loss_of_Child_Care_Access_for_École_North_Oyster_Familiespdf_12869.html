Learn why this is important CAUTION: External Message Ladysmith, BC April 25, 2025 Naomi Bailey, Chair Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (SD68) Dear Chair Bailey, Re: Request for School Board Advocacy Regarding Loss of Child Care Access for École North Oyster Families My name is Lindsay Cote, and I am a parent of a child attending and a child registered to attend École North Oyster Elementary. I am writing to request the Board’s support and advocacy following a sudden and deeply concerning change that will significantly impact my family’s ability to access child care, maintain employment, and stay enrolled at our chosen school. On April 24, 2025, families were notified that the Boys and Girls Clubs of Central Vancouver Island (BGC) will no longer accept children attending École North Oyster into their Ladysmith child care program starting August 31, 2025. This decision — made without consultation or opportunity for accommodations — directly affects 10 children: eight who have consistently attended both École North Oyster and BGC since kindergarten, and two who have attended BGC since infancy and are registered to start at North Oyster in September. While École North Oyster is technically a choice school, it is the only French immersion option available to Ladysmith families through the public system. The School District’s transportation services from Ladysmith to North Oyster clearly recognize the importance of ensuring access. However, without reliable before- and after-school care, families like mine are now facing impossible choices: leaving our employment, transferring schools, or losing access to second-language education altogether. The Ladysmith BGC location is licenced for 94 child care spaces, including infant, toddler, and school-aged care. Removing 10 children who have long been part of the program — without warning, consultation, or transition support — represents a major disruption to families with no clear benefit to the broader service. I respectfully ask the Nanaimo-Ladysmith Board of Education to advocate for fair and continued access to child care for all Ladysmith families, including those attending École North Oyster. Access to a second-language public education should not be limited to those who can independently solve child care gaps. I urge the Board to work with BGC, the Ministry of Education and Child Care, and community partners to find a solution that ensures students are not excluded from essential child care services based on their school choice. Thank you for your urgent attention to this matter. I would welcome the opportunity to be part of any discussions seeking a solution.
