Historical Overview Key Elements of the Plan Ramifications of Changes to the Plan Issues to Consider Options 10 YEAR FACILITIES PLAN DEVELOPMENT HISTORY: 1988 - Government took over the remaining taxing authority of school boards. Financing authority was centralized in Ministry of Finance and Treasury Board (cabinet committee)  School Boards have LITTLE INFLUENCE ON OBTAINING FUNDING FOR CAPTIAL PROJECTS unless government policies are followed  Since 1871 when BC joined Canada, the “right wing” has been in government for 130 years and the “left wing” for 12.5 years  That is 9% for “left wing” and 91% for “right wing’  The current government was re-elected in 2000 which was15 years ago – and could be re-elected for the next15 years!  Research shows “right wing” underspends on public facilities so NDP made up for this by spending record amounts on school construction  From 1990 – 2000 NDP opened a new or renovated school once a month for 10 years – 120 projects!! In some areas they overbuilt  In 1993, Cedar Secondary School was a school that should NOT have been built. It was built for political purposes. There never was enough students in the area to support a secondary school that could provide all the courses needed for graduation. Instead the school struggled with double and triple class configuration and some critical courses were not even offered. The community tried hard to make it work but with declining enrollment the school became too expensive to keep open.  The government elected in 2000 introduced a policy for new or renovated school construction that said a district had to have a utilization rate of 90 to 110%. In other words the district could not have excess space.  In addition another new policy said that no funding was provided to districts for excess space. This cost had to be borne by the school board out of its operating budget.  Currently Nanaimo-Ladysmith has a utilization rate of 87.2%*. Far short to qualify for funding—ergo— the district needed to close schools because of these policies and declining enrollment.  Nanaimo-Ladysmith: Many small schools—built up around mining communities  Total enrollment in 1997 17,437*  Total enrollment in 2013 13,519*  Decline of 3,918 22.5%  No. of schools in 1969 = 50  No. of schools closed since 1969 = 14  Since 2004 the Board has approved 3 long term facilities plans: 2008, 2010, 2014 and currently the 4th public consultation is underway….all in a period of 11 yrs.!!  *Ministry of Education figures Government Policy dictates that the district must have between 90 to 110 % capacity BEFORE ANY FUNDING FOR NEW SCHOOL CONSTRUCTION AND RENOVATIONS CAN BE APPROVED  Nanaimo-Ladysmith current capacity is 87.2% *  Ten year plan priorities and the Five Year Capital Plan calls for the closure of Five Acres, Vast, Woodbank, North Cedar Intermediate, Woodlands Secondary and NDSS in order to consolidate and increase capacity  Key Elements of The Current Ten Year Facilities Plan a) Increase utilization b) Build new elementary school in Ladysmith c) Consolidate K-7 in Cedar (Fine Arts focus) d) Offer full range of secondary courses at Ladysmith and John Barsby e) Build new secondary school at NDSS site  Transfer Woodlands Secondary students to NDSS  Close Woodlands Secondary School f) New School on NDSS site to offer access to VIU students for learning and teaching opportunities and VIU to NDSS g) Rebuild Rotary Bowl and upgrade sports facilities in co-operation with the City and VIU h) Move Facilities Department and Board Office to new locations The District needs to sell excess property and put the funds in Restricted Capital Accounts to apply to the NDSS rebuild. This process has been underway for some years.  Obtain Ministry and community support for the rebuild at NDSS site—projected to about $100 M that will:  build a new secondary school  share with VIU and the City the upgrading of sports facilities, upgraded stadium to host football and other events  obtain funding for school repairs and maintenance that is badly needed  obtain funding to build new elementary school in Ladysmith  development of commercial properties in the NDSS area  If Cedar re-opens as a Secondary School: 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. 6. ISSUES TO CONSIDER: Going out for another community consultation on the facilities plan after three previous consultations is difficult to support.  All previous consultations involved the whole of the school district. This consultation appears to focus only on issues of a minority in Cedar and Ladysmith.  One cannot expect a fair representation from the whole community when the agenda is so narrow.  The approval by the Board of the Ten Year Facilities Plan and the approval of AFG funds in the Five Year Capital Plan has been submitted to the Ministry for their approval. Any changes now in the use of the AFG funds need to be communicated to the Ministry for comment.  When making decisions that affect the whole district the Board would be prudent to consider the results of the recent election.
