NLPS Island Connect Ed Program Review Review Team Don Balcombe, NLPS Margaret Olsen, NLPS Kim Darbyshire, NLPS Special thanks to: Paula Sled, NLPS Laura Tait, NLPS Josh Gillingham, Island Connect Ed Andrea Davidson, Island Connect Ed Justin Mark, Island Connect Ed Introduction This review began in the spring of 2025 and was completed in the fall of 2025. It explores Island ConnectEd (ICE), the Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools (NLPS) online learning school, with a focus on equity, student engagement, outcomes, and sustainability. ICE offers flexible, hybrid learning – online and in person – meeting a wide range of student and family needs. This review explored enrolment trends, achievement data, transitions, stakeholder input, and alignment with district and provincial goals. The result was reliable trends, patterns, and possible recommendations. All data presented is from the 2024/2025 school year. Context Island ConnectEd first opened in 2002 with the K-7 program. In 2006, the Learn @ Home 8-12 school was opened. In fall 2016, both programs were merged and officially became Island ConnectEd K-12. ICE enrolls students who learn at home, who travel out of country, who are dual enrolled with other high schools, and who enjoy working on-site in a self-paced environment.
