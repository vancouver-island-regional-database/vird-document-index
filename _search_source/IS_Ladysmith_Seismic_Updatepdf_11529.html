NANAIMO LADYSMITH PUBLIC SCHOOLS BUSINESS COMMITTEE MEETING INFORMATION SHEET DATE: TO: FROM: SUBJECT: January 17, 2024 Business Committee Mark Walsh, Secretary-Treasurer Ladysmith Intermediate and Ladysmith Primary – Seismic Update Background: As the Board is aware, the District received support in the last round of capital response letters to upgrade Ladysmith Intermediate School and expand Ladysmith Primary. This Information Sheet will provide an update on the status of the projects. Discussion: The Board engaged in a significant public consultation process that resulted in the community favouring an option of changing the configuration of the Ladysmith schools to have two k-7 schools (at Ladysmith Primary and a new site – likely Davis Road) with French Immersion returning to the new site. During the consultation the community was also made aware that such a project would involve a contribution from the District. We are waiting on the formal approval from the Ministry of Education and Child Care to proceed to the next steps of the project. We anticipated this in December but will likely receive the approval to move to the next phase of planning in the next few weeks. At this time the Board will be faced with a major decision. The decision will be whether the Board is able to contribute the required amount to fund the preferable option, which District staff anticipate to be as much as 9 million dollars. This represents the difference between the cost of seismically upgrading LIS at the current site without any other upgrades compared to the new build at the Davis Road site. Staff are contemplating three issues: 1. Strategies to lower the cost differential including the potential for part of the new build to be prefabricated; 2. Strategies for the Board to consider to accumulate sufficient funds for the contribution (including the already discussed sale of the LIS lands upon completion of the new build; and 3. Supporting the Board in its advocacy work with the Ministry. As we have not yet received formal approval to move to next steps there is no action item associated with this item. However, due to significant public interest, staff felt a brief update was in order.
