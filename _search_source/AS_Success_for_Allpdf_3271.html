BOARD OF EDUCATION SCHOOL DISTRICT NO. 68 REGULAR BOARD MEETING ACTION SHEET DATE: November 28, 2012 TO: Board of Education FROM: John Blain, Deputy Superintendent SUBJECT: Calendar Change “Success for All” Background This fall Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools is undertaking a number of exciting initiates that will, ultimately, translate into the improvement of student learning. These initiatives, including the introduction of Response to Intervention (a method of academic intervention) and the development of a strategic plan, will not only require the commitment of all stakeholders but, also a shift in present practice. In order to prepare our education community for this shift, we need to bring our community together by providing a series of workshops/learning opportunities. This will involve the release of all of our employees at the same time: educational assistants, teachers, custodial and clerical staff, school leaders and exempt staff. We will call this series of workshops “Success for All.” Proposal The proposal is to release our education community to attend one full day and one afternoon of workshops during the school day. These activities would be tentatively held on February 1, 2013 for the entire day and March 12, 2013 for the afternoon at central locations. All employees would be expected to attend these in-service based workshops which would deliver current school district initiatives, our district vision and mission, strategic planning and development, and most importantly, structures and strategies to support student learning. In addition, BC Ministry legislative requirements such as Duty to Report, Suicide Protocols, and Safety in the Work Place would have an important place on the agenda. As required, the district consulted parents about the proposed calendar change. Attached is a summary of the responses received during the consultation period. While it is recognized that the additional day and a half that students would not be in school is inconvenient for some families, we believe that the educational gains to be made over the long term are significant and warrant the calendar change for this school year. While we plan to continue to hold such sessions in future years, our goal will be to develop a schedule with the least possible impact on families. Recommendation The Board authorizes the Department of Learning Services to move forward with the 2012-13 school year calendar change to support the new district-wide learning series called “Success for All.”
