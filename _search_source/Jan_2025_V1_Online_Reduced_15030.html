Furry Friends Safe and Cozy As the temperature drops and snow begins to fall, winter can be a magical time for us and our pets. However, the season also brings challenges and risks for our furry companions. Whether you have a playful pup or a cozy cat, here are some essential tips to ensure your pet stays safe, warm, and healthy throughout the winter months. 1. Keep Them Warm Just like us, pets can feel the chill of winter. While some breeds are more tolerant of cold weather, others may need extra protection. Here’s how to keep them warm: -Invest in Winter Gear: Dogs with short coats 07 or low body fat may benefit from sweaters or jackets when going outside. - Provide a Cozy Spot Indoors:Ensure your pet has a warm, draft-free space with a comfortable bed or blanket. -Limit Outdoor Time: Prolonged exposure to cold can lead to hypothermia or frostbite. Monitor their outdoor activities and bring them inside if they show signs of discomfort. 2. Protect Their Paws Winter conditions can be tough on your pet’s paws. Snow, ice, and de-icing chemicals can cause irritation or injury. - Use Paw Balm or Boots: Apply a protective balm or have your dog wear boots to shield their paws from the elements. - Rinse Paws After Walks: Wash off salt, chemicals, or debris with warm water to prevent irritation and ingestion. - Trim Fur Between Toes: Keeping this area clean can reduce the risk of ice buildup. 3. Watch for Signs of Cold-Weather Stress Pets can’t tell us when they’re too cold, so it’s important to look for signs of discomfort: - Shivering - Whining or barking - Lifting paws off the ground - Seeking shelter or trying to burrow If you notice these signs, it’s time to get your pet indoors and warm them up. 4. Be Mindful of Holiday Hazards Winter often brings festive celebrations, but some holiday traditions can be dangerous for pets: - Avoid Toxic Foods: Chocolate, alcohol, and rich holiday meals can be harmful to pets. - Keep Antifreeze Out of Reach: This common winter chemical is highly toxic to pets, so store it securely and clean up spills immediately. starting your engine to avoid accidents. - Avoid Idling in Enclosed Spaces: Never leave your pet in a car with the engine running, as carbon monoxide poisoning is a risk. 7. Plan for Emergencies Winter storms can lead to power outages or other emergencies. Prepare a pet-specific emergency kit with essentials like food, water, medications, and blankets. Final Thoughts Winter can be a wonderful season to enjoy with your pets, as long as you take precautions to keep them safe and comfortable. By following these tips, you’ll ensure your furry friends stay happy and healthy no matter how chilly it gets outside. Image by Macrovector on Freepik COCKROACH PREVENTION Preventing cockroach infestations involves maintaining cleanliness, reducing access to food and water, and sealing potential entry points. Consistency in these measures can significantly reduce the likelihood of a cockroach infestation. Here are some practical tips: Maintain Cleanliness Minimize Clutter •Kitchen Hygiene: Wipe down counters, stoves, and dining areas regularly to remove food debris. •Clean Appliances: Pay attention to areas under and behind appliances like refrigerators and ovens. •Dispose of Trash Promptly: Use sealed garbage bins and empty them regularly. •Mop and Vacuum Floors: Regularly clean floors, especially in areas where food is prepared or consumed. •Declutter: Reduce piles of papers, cardboard boxes, and other materials where cockroaches can hide. •Store Items Off the Floor: Use shelves or storage containers with tight-fitting lids. Eliminate Food Sources •Store Food Properly: Keep food in airtight containers and avoid leaving pet food out overnight. •Wash Dishes Promptly: Don’t leave dirty dishes in the sink for long periods. •Inspect Pantry Items: Check for leaks in food packaging and discard expired goods. Remove Water Sources •Fix Leaks: Repair dripping faucets, leaking pipes, and clogged drains. •Dry Wet Areas: Avoid leaving standing water in sinks, bathtubs, or pet bowls. Seal Entry Points •Inspect for Cracks and Gaps: Seal cracks in walls, floors, and around doors and windows. •Check Door and Window Seals: Install weather stripping or door sweeps. •Screen Vents and Openings: Cover vents and pipes with fine mesh screens Use Preventative Treatments •Apply Boric Acid or Diatomaceous Earth: Use these in cracks and crevices, but keep them away from pets and children. •Install Bait Stations: Use cockroach baits to monitor and eliminate small infestations. •Use Natural Repellents: Essential oils like peppermint or eucalyptus may deter cockroaches. Regular Inspections •Check High-Risk Areas: Inspect dark, warm, and moist places such as under sinks, behind appliances, and in storage spaces. •Act Quickly: If you see signs of cockroaches (droppings, egg cases, or a musty smell), take immediate action Hire Professionals •If you suspect a large infestation or preventative measures are not effective, consult a pest control professional for treatment and advice. 10 SAP COLLECTION Hi friends, We’re excited to announce a new project in Outdoor Ed: collecting sap from our Big Leaf Maples to make syrup! Between November 1, 2024 – January 10, 2025, there have been 28 suspected norovirus cases reported in the Island Health region, associated with the consumption of raw oysters. Additional cases have been reported in the Vancouver Coastal Health and Fraser Health regions. Oysters and shellfish can become contaminated with norovirus in the marine environment where they are grown and harvested. Some oyster harvesting areas along the BC coast have been closed, due to the ongoing public health situation. Clinical Presentation Norovirus symptoms are usually self-limiting. The illness is characterized by sudden onset of abdominal cramping, nausea, vomiting, and/or diarrhea. Symptoms may also include chills, headache, myalgia, and fever. Symptoms appear within 12-48 hours after ingestion and typically last for 24-72 hours. Pregnant people, people who are immunocompromised, young children, and adults over the age of 65 are at increased risk for severe illness. Testing Testing is not recommended for those who are asymptomatic, even with a recent history of raw or undercooked oyster/shellfish consumption. For patients who have eaten raw or undercooked oysters/shellfish AND who have developed gastrointestinal symptoms within 48 hours of ingestion, testing can be offered to support pathogen identification and disease surveillance. Norovirus testing is included in the new infectious diarrhea panel (IDP). This is a PCR test that is automatically run on any stool sample submitted for “culture” or “ova and parasite”. It can detect 14 different viruses, bacteria, and protozoa, including other pathogens associated with the consumption of raw oysters/shellfish, such as Vibrio spp. More information about the IDP can be found at the BC Guideline page here. Treatment and Prevention For most patients, norovirus treatment is supportive. Antibiotics are not recommended for the management of norovirus illness. Norovirus is highly contagious. Individuals with gastrointestinal illness should follow good hand hygiene and avoid preparing and handling food for others until 48 hours after symptom resolution. High touch surfaces in the patient’s environment should be disinfected with chlorine bleach. More information can be found at HealthLink BC here. Patients can reduce their risk of exposure by avoiding the consumption of raw or undercooked oysters/shellfish. Those at high risk of severe illness (e.g., pregnant people, people who are immunocompromised, young children, adults over the age of 65) should always avoid eating raw or undercooked oysters/shellfish. Oysters should be cooked to an internal temperature of 90 degrees Celsius for a minimum of 90 seconds before consumption. Public Reporting Any member of the public can report suspected foodborne gastrointestinal illnesses to their nearest local environmental public health office. A list of these offices and their contact information can be found at the Island Health website here. An environmental health officer will review and investigate all reports.
