SAVE Departure Bay Eco School { Written by Chelsea Slobodan & presented by Ruth Wainwright-Christmas August 2015 Our Vision To become a school with an environmental focus that produces students who are environmentally literate by using a hands on, interdisciplinary, place-based approach to provide students with meaningful and imaginative learning opportunities where students are constantly engaged in the natural world around our facility. Place-based learning Eco club & gardens Seashore Program with DFO NS3 Science Saturday & ongoing collaboration Coho salmon restoration Salmon teacher coordinator Collaborating with The David Suzuki Foundation Trash to Treasure Working with My CDC to begin creating a community garden, outdoor classroom spaces & a recycling centre Over 16,000 of financial support in the form of grants have already been obtained to get the program up and running specific to our location • • • • • • • • • • Offer composting & permaculture workshops to the community Solar panels/selling energy back to the grid Green roofs Create wetland space Selling produce from our market garden Stream keeping Coordinated projects with VIU education & Science programs as well as the Pacific Biological Station Include other schools within & out o district to participate in learning & workshops Researchers & graduate students would be able to use our site and resources for research projects Continue on and press forward with the joint vision of the My CDC Departure Bay Eco School Cilaire • • • • • The Board: ‘’ portable’’ ‘’Cilaire is close to the beach’’. FACTS ‘’Cilaire is perched on the top of a sandy cliff with limited steep access to trails and the water. Only 2 ways to access the beach: at the bottom of the steep staircases of Beach Estates Park there is a mudflat near the ferry terminal and a rough rocky shoreline at the base of the cliff. To access beachfront at Departure Bay Beach the children would have to walk 1.7 km each way down the high traffic and steep winding section of Departure Bay Rd. Will the School Board be responsible for the liability?’’ (Excerpts taken from Taryn Silver’s letter to the board) • • • Easy pedestrian access to the coastal habitats including; Departure Bay beach, salmon habitat in Woodstream Park, forest habitats in Linley Valley & +++ other surrounding parks. Our Catchment area also includes: Stephenson Pt. Beach, Piper’s Lagoon, Departure Creek, Cottle Creek, Cottle Lake -Jesper’s Way Nottingham pond. Large protected Features: Linley Valley, District Lot #56, Planta Park, Woodstream Park, Sugarloaf Mtn., Arbutus Ridges & many city green spaces. Theses are place-based learning initiatives that cannot be transferred to another site as well as the design of the program with over 20 community partners specific to the location of our school.
