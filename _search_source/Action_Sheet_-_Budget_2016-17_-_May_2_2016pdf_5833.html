Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools BOARD OF EDUCATION ACTION SHEET DATE: May 2, 2016 TO: Business Committee FROM: John Blain, Superintendent SUBJECT: 2016-17 Proposed Preliminary Budget Rationale: Staff believe that Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools must stay the course with the Becoming Sustainable plan. The district has been facing a structural deficit as a result of costs downloaded to districts, funding not increasing with the inflation rate and declining enrolment. 2016-17 is the second year of a two-year budget plan which targets reductions away from student learning. Staff have listened to the community and have considered the Board’s concerns. As a result of discussions and presentations on April 20 and 25, staff proposes the following:      The district can be less conservative on benefits rates, due to existing contingencies, to create a savings of $200,000; One-time charge on moving costs for school consolidations to accumulated surplus, which will free up $160,000; Transportation reductions to only affect out-of-catchment trips (Cedar/South Wellington) and transportation from Davis Road to Ladysmith Secondary; The remaining catchment routes will be maintained for another year while the transportation policy review is underway; and The cost savings originally proposed related to transportation will be charged to accumulated surplus ($230,000). The following is a comparison between the April 25 and May 2 Special Business Committee meetings. April 25, 2016 May 2, 2016 Transportation - Reduction (5.7 FTE) Transportation - Reduction (2.7 FTE) Custodial - Reduction of 5.0 FTE Custodial – Reduction of 2.0 FTE Maintenance – Reduction of 1.3 FTE (Grounds) Maintenance – Maintained 1.3 FTE reduction Maintenance – Reduction of 2.0 FTE (Carpenters) Maintenance – Reduction of 1.0 FTE Surplus used to balance budget - $500,000 Surplus used to balance budget - $890,000 Total Reduction - $970,000 and 14.0 FTE Total Reduction - $489,000 and 7.0 FTE To minimize reductions, staff realize that the one-time use of surplus addresses concerns presented from the public and partner groups. Staff still believe it is imperative that the use of surplus should not be used to limit reductions, as it is contrary to becoming sustainable. Using surplus would not address the structural deficit and when moving forward, and would limit the investment opportunities to enhance student learning. Staff recommends that the Board stay the course on becoming sustainable and adopt the 2016-17 budget as presented. This will situate NLPS in the position to focus on growing student opportunities instead of making cuts to the system. Board Motion: The Business Committee recommends to the Board of Education of School District 68 (NanaimoLadysmith) that it adopt the 2016-17 budget as presented on May 2, 2016.
