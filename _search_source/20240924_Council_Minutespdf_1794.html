CALL TO ORDER AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENT Mayor Stone called this Public Hearing and Regular Meeting of Council to order at 6:00 p.m., recognizing with gratitude that it was taking place on the unceded territory of the Stz'uminus First Nation. Mayor Stone also recognized September 30, 2024, as National Truth and Reconciliation Day, and expressed his gratitude to the Stz'uminus First Nation for their collaboration in community development. The Mayor remarked that this would be his last meeting and expressed gratitude to Council and staff, acknowledging their support and hard work throughout his tenure. He also reflected on the success of the UBCM convention held in Vancouver the previous week. Town of Ladysmith Public Hearing and Regular Council Meeting Minutes: September 24, 2024 1 2. PUBLIC HEARING Members of the Public attending: 3 3.1 Outline of Public Hearing Process - Mayor Stone Mayor Stone outlined the Public Hearing process and stated that the public would have the opportunity to provide their comments to Council about Bylaw No. 2185. He advised that staff would introduce the proposed bylaw amendments, followed by public submissions. He reminded the public that the content of submissions would be made public and form a part of the public record for the Hearing, and that the function of Council at a Public Hearing is to listen rather than to debate the merits of the proposed bylaws, although they may ask clarifying questions. He advised that once everyone had an opportunity to be heard, the Public Hearing would be closed and no further submissions or comments could be accepted by members of Council. 3.2 Introduction of Bylaw and Statutory Requirements - Director of Development Services Jake Belobaba, Director of Development Services, introduced “Official Community Plan Bylaw 2022, No. 2200, Amendment Bylaw No. 2185” as the subject of the Public Hearing. Bylaw 2185 applies to all properties in the Town of Ladysmith. Mr. Belobaba confirmed that the purpose of Bylaw No. 2185 is to amend the Official Community Plan by: • Adding a new policy to evaluate subdivision and rezoning applications for sufficient emergency access; • Deleting the Coach House Intensive Residential Development Permit Area and guidelines; • Deleting the High Street Residential Development Permit Area and guidelines; Town of Ladysmith Public Hearing and Regular Council Meeting Minutes: September 24, 2024 2 • Expanding Development Permit Area 4 – Multi-Unit Residential; • Adding an exemption to the requirement to obtain a development permit for single-family, two-unit, secondary suite and coach house developments or R-2-zoned residential developments with four units or less in Development Permit Area 4 – Multi-unit Residential; and • Adding an exemption to the requirement to obtain a development permit for a development that is subject to a Phased Development Agreement pursuant to section 516 of the Local Government Act in the following development permit areas: Development Permit Area 1 – Maritime (DPA 1), Development Permit Area 2 – Downtown (DPA 2), Development Permit Area 3 – Commercial (DPA 3), Development Permit Area 4 – Multi-Unit Residential (DPA 4) and Development Permit Area 5 – Industrial (DPA 5). Mr. Belobaba confirmed the Public Hearing notification and engagement process. Notice of this Public Hearing was printed in the Ladysmith Chronicle on September 19th,2024 and posted on the Town’s website and community notice boards. A copy of the Notice, the proposed Bylaw, and background information was made available at the Front Counter of City Hall and Development Services, and on the Town’s website for the Notice period. Staff in the Development Services office were available to respond to questions prior to the Public Hearing. 3.3 Submissions 3.4 Call for Submissions to Council (Three Times) - Mayor Stone Mayor Stone called for submissions from the public. Ken Chipeniuck, 136 Ray Knight Drive, spoke in favor of the proposal. He observed that Council had recently implemented similar provisions by requiring two access routes to the upper regions of the Holland Creek area but asked why only one section of the National Fire Protection Association standard was included. Mayor Stone called for submissions a second time. Mayor Stone called for submissions a third time and final time. Town of Ladysmith Public Hearing and Regular Council Meeting Minutes: September 24, 2024 3 Mayor Stone asked the Corporate Officer, S. Bouma, if any submissions had been received during the Hearing. The Corporate Officer advised that no submissions had been received. 3.5 Declaration that the Public Hearing for Bylaw No. 2185 is Closed Mayor Stone Hearing no comments and receiving no further submissions, Mayor Stone declared the Public Hearing for Bylaw No. 2185 closed at 6:11 p.m. and stated that no further submissions or comments from the public or interested persons could be accepted by members of Council. 4.
