UNFINISHED BUSINESS Wellington Seismic Update Mr. Dalrymple, Energy Manager, gave the Business Committee an update on the design for F block as part of the Wellington Secondary seismic project. The F block is the central circular section of the school which is being completely stripped as part of the seismic work and then will be rebuilt with a new look featuring contemporary learning spaces such as a learning commons, learning labs and student collaboration spaces. The seismic project at Wellington is being staged over several years so that the school can continue operating while work is underway. By the time all of the work is complete in January 2016, much of the school will be newly renovated. NEW BUSINESS Cedar Playgrounds/Playfields Mr. Sabo, Director of Planning and Operations, gave the Business Committee an update on the planning in process for the outdoor areas at the new Cedar Elementary School. Woodbank Primary and North Cedar Intermediate students will move into the newly renovated Cedar Elementary School in September 2015. Mr. Sabo said that parents had expressed concern about the state of the playfield at the new elementary school. At the March Board Meeting had asked Facilities Department staff to review the possibility of providing an upgraded playfield, and to report back to the Business Committee in June. Page 22 of 43 6.3 Draft Minutes of the Business Committee Meeting held May 14, 2014 Mr. Sabo said that since then, staff have reviewed the budget for the renovation of the school and the AFG program, and have identified some opportunities for additional funds that may be sufficient for the playfield. He said that in order to determine the cost of the playfield and to allow for the possibility of installing the playfield this summer, the district will advertise for tenders this month and will likely be in a position to provide the Board with some options at the June 25 meeting. Forecast 2013-14 as of April 30, 2014 Mr. Turin provided the Business Committee with a financial update for the current school year, as well as a look ahead at the financial picture for the district for the next several years. He said that current projections are for the district to end the school year with about a $2.1 million unrestricted surplus. Mr. Turin is forecasting that the district will be facing an approximate $3.5 million shortfall in 2015-16. He said that current projections forecast the elimination of the Ministry’s funding protection grant after the 2015-16 year. With this grant, NLPS and The Ferns will be able to finance the renovation or building of another classroom or portable/modular building to serve as a licensed infant and toddler childcare- 12 spaces- to enhance the Young Mothers classroom currently at N.D.S.S.. Alternatively and depending on the Learning Outcomes Team’s recommendations of directions for NLPS, a second classroom will also be built along with the first, to serve as a licensed 16 space 3-5 yr early years program. As many of the teen parents do not reach graduation completion before their child(ren) reach the age of 3 years, the model of attaching a 3-5 yr classroom to the infant and toddler classroom can ensure greater success rates. As you are aware, Little Ferns Early Learning Centre and Y.P.P. (Young Parent Program) started operations at John Barsby Community School September of 2007 with a 16 space licensed 3-5 yr classroom and a 12 space infant and toddler classroom housed together in a new modular building. This was created with the same Targeted Major Capital Grant as released by MCFD in 2006.
