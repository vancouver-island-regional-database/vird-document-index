Appendix A Administrative Procedure 603.7 – Unexpected Health Emergencies in Schools AP 603.7 – Unexpected Health Emergencies in Schools Purpose Nanaimo Ladysmith Public Schools is committed to ensuring the health and safety of all students, staff and visitors. This procedure is intended to align with requirements of the Ministry of Education and to provide guidelines for the deployment, maintenance, use and training associated with AEDs and naloxone kits for unexpected health emergencies in schools. Definitions Automated external defibrillator (AED) is a portable device used to administer an electric shock to the heart and restore the heart’s normal rhythm during sudden cardiac arrest. Sudden cardiac arrest (SCA) is a condition that occurs when the electrical impulses of the human heart malfunction causing a disturbance called ventricular fibrillation (VF) in the heart’s electrical rhythm. This erratic and ineffective electrical heart rhythm causes complete cessation of the heart’s normal function of pumping blood, resulting in SCA. Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) is an emergency procedure involving chest compressions and rescue breaths when a person’s heartbeat or breathing has stopped. Naloxone Kit is a kit with the supplies to administer naloxone to someone who appears to be experiencing an opioid overdose. Location 1. All NLPS Schools will have both Naloxone Kits and an AED available for school and community use. 2. Naloxone Kits and AEDs will be clearly marked with non-stigmatizing standardized signage to ensure easy and timely identification for use and will be located in accessible locations to maximize barrier free use for diverse users. Training and Maintenance 1. Each school is required to have an appropriate number of staff members trained in First Aid / CPR. This training is provided by the Occupational Health and Safety department through a certified provider. The First Aid / CPR training certification will include a module on the use of AED’s and administrating Naloxone. 2. All school personnel should be made aware of the First Aid designates at their site. 3. Naloxone Kits and AEDs will be inspected, maintained, and replaced as per the manufacturer’s specifications. 4. Naloxone Kit and AED, inspection, and maintenance shall be the responsibility of the site based Occupational Health & Safety committee or health and safety representative.
