A number of students from several secondary schools attended a rally before the Board meeting and stayed for the meeting. The students said that they need the exam week time to study and that teachers need time to prepare for the second semester. They also said there was a lack of consultation and they believe the changes are not fair to students and staff. Mr. Hutchinson said that as a result of the discontinuation of 12 optional provincial exams this year, the district looked at productive ways to use the instructional time that was formerly used for exams. He said that while the district is making changes to the exam week schedule this year, the exact details are still being finalized and he suggested students talk to their school principals to provide input into the schedule for the week. The November 28th catchment open house (6:00-8:00 pm at the District Board Room) was hosted by Pete Sabo, Brent Spohn and Donna Reimer. No members of the public attended. The following information was provided:  Poster boards with maps  Survey forms  Original catchment action sheet  District catchment map  Details of boundaries changes and key areas that require more definition  City of Nanaimo’s version of school district catchment map  News release on November 21, 2011  The proposed boundary changes that were sent home on November 21st Clarification on specific portions of certain catchment areas was provided:  Chase River to Park Avenue: south-east portion of the catchment  Chase River to Park Avenue: west portion of the catchment  Pleasant Valley to Mountain View: north portion of the catchment In the Park Avenue-Chase River area one portion is commercial development and another is an undeveloped residential area. In the Pleasant Valley-Mountain View area a portion is currently a wooded area. Staff have attempted to follow property boundaries; however, in undeveloped areas it is difficult to foresee how development will proceed. When development does proceed, staff will be able to advise regarding school attendance questions arising out of the boundary changes being approved today. Additional detailed maps were available at the open house to illustrate these boundary changes. Although boundary definition in these areas is more challenging, this is not critical as there is no student population affected. The remaining boundary changes were straight forward and follow existing boundaries and/or natural features. Discussion All the information provided at the open house on November 28th is presented at this Board Meeting. Staff are willing to discuss any details regarding the changes being recommended. Staff face ongoing challenges regarding the mapping of catchment areas and will continue working to further refine catchment areas. Although catchment mapping to date has been accurate for District purposes, it is staff’s intention to move towards more accurate and consistent mapping as cost effective technology and staffing levels allow. Students currently attending Quarterway would not be required to change schools.  The boundary change would also affect the secondary school that students in this area attend, since Mountain View students attend NDSS. You are invited to attend an Open House on Monday, November 28, from 6 to 8 p.m. The Open House will be held at the District Administration Centre, 395 Wakesiah Avenue, in the Board Room (enter from the upper parking lot). You can drop in any time from 6 to 8 p.m. to view the proposed changes in more detail, to ask questions of staff and provide your input. You may also provide input by emailing communications@sd68.bc.ca. The Board of Education will make the final decision on the proposed boundary changes.
