DRAF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1220 Role of the Trustee The Board of Education consists of nine (9) Trustees. Trustees as members of the corporate board are accountable to the public for collective decisions of the Board of Education. A Trustee must serve the community as an elected representative but the primary task of the Trustee is to act as a member of a corporate board. The School Act gives no individual authority to a Trustee. Specific Responsibilities of Individual Trustees 1. Policy and Procedures A Trustee Shall: 1.1 Become familiar with District policy and procedures. 1.2 Participate in and contribute to the development of Board policy and procedures to provide the best possible education for students. 1.3 Monitor the implementation of policy in the School District. 2. Education A Trustee shall: 2.1 Participate in an orientation program for new Trustees. 2.2 Participate in Board/Senior Management development sessions in order to enhance leadership in the School District. 2.3 Share materials and ideas gained with fellow Trustees at the Board meeting immediately following a Trustee development activity. 2.4 Remain current with provincial, national and international education trends. -1 - DRAF SCHOOL DISTRICT 68 (NANAIMO-LADYSMITH) DRAFT POLICY NO. 1220 3. Problem Solving A Trustee shall: 3.1 Refer queries, issues or problems not covered by Board policy to the Board of Education for corporate discussion, decision or delegation. 3.2 Refer queries, issues or problems raised by a parent or other member of the community concerning a teacher or classroom situation, to the teacher. 3.3 Refer queries, issues or problems raised by a parent or other member of the community concerning a principal or a school situation to the principal, and where appropriate inform the Superintendent or designate. 4. Community A Trustee shall: 5. 4.1 Act as a liaison to assigned schools. 4.2 Attend District or school events when invited. 4.3 Strive to develop a respectful learning and working culture within the District and wider community. Code of Conduct A Trustee shall: 5.1 Be familiar with and adhere to the Trustee Code of Conduct. 5.2 Be familiar with and adhere to the Trustee Code of Ethics. 5.3 Report any violation of the Trustee Code of Conduct or Ethics to the Board of Education at an In camera session. 5.4 Exercise authority within defined limits when delegated such responsibility. -2 -
